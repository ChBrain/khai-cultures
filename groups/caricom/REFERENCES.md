## The grouping

CARICOM, the Caribbean Community, is the integration bloc of the small nations of
the Caribbean and its South American shoulder, bound by a shared plantation past, a
shared sea, cricket, the University of the West Indies, and the Caribbean Single
Market and Economy. The membership recorded in the registry is **build-derived**
from the member plays this group casts, not authored by hand. Its fourteen members
are the sovereign full members of the community across islands and mainland and
three colonial tongues; the non-sovereign Montserrat is omitted, and Cuba and the
Dominican Republic, which are not full members, are excluded. It is not a single
state, but a community of sovereign nations.

## The community

CARICOM was established by the Treaty of Chaguaramas in 1973, succeeding the
short-lived West Indies Federation (1958-1962) and earlier free-trade
arrangements. Its modern economic project is the Caribbean Single Market and
Economy (CSME), and its shared institutions include the one West Indies cricket
team and the University of the West Indies. The grouping binds the same nations
cast here and is a matter of public record.
