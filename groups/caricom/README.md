# CARICOM: a khai group

CARICOM, the Caribbean Community, staged as a khai **group** - a play in the
shared `groups/` collection that **references its member cultures** rather than
being a country of its own. The integration bloc of the small nations of the
Caribbean and its South American shoulder, bound by a shared plantation past, by
cricket and the University of the West Indies, by the Caribbean Single Market and
Economy, and by the common cause of small states facing a large world.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this production and answers for it.

## A group, not a culture

Like the Anglosphere, DACH, the Nordics, Benelux, the Visegrad Four, the Baltics,
Iberia, NATO, and the EU, CARICOM is a **group**, not a country. It has **no
`geo.json`** and **no Hofstede pitch**: it is a _referencing_ entry. Its
`Company` casts the member plays, and the registry build derives the group's
`references` from exactly those casts, so the membership can never drift from what
the play actually links.

- **Members:** the fourteen sovereign full members of the community, spanning the
  islands and the South American mainland and three colonial tongues - Antigua and
  Barbuda, the Bahamas, Barbados, Belize, Dominica, Grenada, Guyana, Haiti,
  Jamaica, Saint Kitts and Nevis, Saint Lucia, Saint Vincent and the Grenadines,
  Suriname, and Trinidad and Tobago. The non-sovereign Montserrat is omitted, and
  Cuba and the Dominican Republic, which are not full members, are excluded.
- **Kind:** `group`.
- **The growth story:** like NATO and the EU, CARICOM's plots trace its
  **evolution**, not a single event:
  - [the Plantation World](plot_01_the_plantation_world.md) - the shared sugar-and-slavery colonial past across the islands and the Guianas, staged soberly.
  - [Emancipation and Indenture](plot_02_emancipation_and_indenture.md) - the 1834-38 emancipation, Indian and Asian indenture, and the plural societies.
  - [Independence and Federation](plot_03_independence_and_federation.md) - the failed West Indies Federation, independence from the 1960s, and the founding of CARICOM in 1973.
  - [Small States Together](plot_04_small_states_together.md) - the modern community: the CSME, cricket and UWI, climate vulnerability, and facing the world as one.

## Provenance

CARICOM is the integration bloc of the small nations of the Caribbean and its
South American shoulder, bound by a shared plantation-and-slavery past, by cricket
and the University of the West Indies, by the Caribbean Single Market and Economy,
and by the common vulnerability of small states to hurricanes and a rising sea.
Its members and their histories are matters of public record; the region's
founding in slavery and indenture is staged soberly, from the public record and
without atrocity detail, and treated honestly in each member culture. The staging
and the architecture are original work.
