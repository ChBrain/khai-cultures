# Plot: Small States Together

## Taxonomy

[play_caricom](play_caricom.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

The community grows into its modern work: knitting its small markets into one, carrying its shared institutions into daily life, and standing together against the threats that no member could meet alone. CARICOM becomes the instrument of small states determined to face the world as one.

## Action

Through the decades after its founding, CARICOM deepens. The Caribbean Single Market and Economy, the CSME, sets out to join the small national markets into one space for the free movement of goods, services, capital, and skilled people, so that states too small to matter alone can trade and bargain as a bloc. The shared institutions endure and grow: the one West Indies cricket team, for which [Barbados](../../cultures/barbados/play_barbados.md), [Antigua and Barbuda](../../cultures/antigua_and_barbuda/play_antigua_and_barbuda.md), and the rest play as a single nation on the field, and the University of the West Indies that trains the region's people across its campuses. And the community finds its loudest voice in common danger: against the hurricanes that batter islands like [Dominica](../../cultures/dominica/play_dominica.md), and against the rising sea that threatens every low coast, the small states of CARICOM speak together at the world's tables, demanding to be heard. The community becomes what the federation could not, a cooperation of the small that endures, even as the old strains of distance and divided interest endure with it.

## Stage

- [Barbados](../../cultures/barbados/play_barbados.md): a leader of the community and of its single-market project.
- [Antigua and Barbuda](../../cultures/antigua_and_barbuda/play_antigua_and_barbuda.md): a small twin-island member of the modern community.
- [Dominica](../../cultures/dominica/play_dominica.md): the storm-battered isle, a voice for climate-vulnerable small states.
- [Grenada](../../cultures/grenada/play_grenada.md): a small Windward member of the community.

## Tension

This is the community as it stands: a living bloc of small states, bound by a single market, a shared team, a shared university, and a common stand against hurricanes and a rising sea, but still scattered across islands and a mainland and split among three colonial tongues. The stake set at the founding remains open here. Whether CARICOM can be one community strong enough to face great powers, storms, and the sea together, or whether distance, size, and the pull of larger neighbours keep it a cooperation of the small rather than a union, is the question the modern community lives out every day.
