# Plot: the Five Eyes

## Taxonomy

[play_anglosphere](play_anglosphere.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

The war ends, but the trust it built does not. The signals-intelligence partnership of Britain and America is written down, made permanent, and opened to the dominions, and out of it grows the deepest secret-sharing alliance the world has known, binding the five into one circle of confidence.

## Action

The wartime arrangement becomes the UKUSA Agreement, and through the late 1940s and 1950s it widens to take in [Canada](../../cultures/canada/play_canada.md), [Australia](../../cultures/australia/play_australia.md), and [New Zealand](../../cultures/new_zealand/play_new_zealand.md), forming what comes to be called the Five Eyes: [the United States](../../cultures/usa/play_usa.md), [the United Kingdom](../../cultures/united_kingdom/play_united_kingdom.md), and the three dominions sharing intercepted intelligence with one another and with no one else. Through the Cold War and after, the five divide the listening of the whole earth between them and pool what they hear. Yet in the same decades each member also turns toward its own world: the United States to the Pacific and to its own power, the United Kingdom into and then back out of Europe, [Australia](../../cultures/australia/play_australia.md) and [New Zealand](../../cultures/new_zealand/play_new_zealand.md) toward Asia and the Pacific and their own bicultural reckonings, New Zealand even going nuclear-free against its partners' wishes. The kinship endures, deepened in secret and tested in the open.

## Stage

- [the United States](../../cultures/usa/play_usa.md): the hub of the Five Eyes and the leader the others orbit.
- [the United Kingdom](../../cultures/united_kingdom/play_united_kingdom.md): the partner from the first, the bridge between America and the rest.
- [Canada](../../cultures/canada/play_canada.md): an early member, holding the North American flank with the United States.
- [New Zealand](../../cultures/new_zealand/play_new_zealand.md): the smallest eye, that charted its own nuclear-free course without leaving the circle.

## Tension

The Five Eyes makes the Anglosphere a living alliance and not just a shared past, the five trusting one another with their deepest secrets across the whole Cold War and beyond. But the very decades that bind them in private see each member pulled toward its own region and its own century, until the question stands open: whether a family held by language, law, and intercepted signals can stay one bloc as the Pacific, Europe, and Asia each call its members home, or whether the shared tongue slowly becomes a kinship of memory rather than of common cause.
