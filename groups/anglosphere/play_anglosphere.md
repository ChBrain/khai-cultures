# Play: the Anglosphere

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the house that holds this production and answers for it.

## Name

The Anglosphere: the family of nations born of English settlement and bound by a shared tongue, a common-law tradition, and the closest intelligence alliance in history, the Five Eyes. Its core is the five English-speaking democracies of the wider world: the United Kingdom, the United States, Canada, Australia, and New Zealand.

## Arc

A grouping bound not by a treaty or a border but by a language and the world it carried. From one small island the English tongue and the common law crossed the oceans, and where British settlers went they founded societies in their own image, on lands that were never empty: the thirteen colonies that broke away in 1776 to become the first independent English-speaking power, and the settler dominions of Canada, Australia, and New Zealand that grew to nationhood under a shared Crown. Twice in the twentieth century these nations fought side by side, and in the world wars the dominions found their own nationhood in blood while Britain and America forged a "special relationship" that passed the torch of leadership across the Atlantic. Out of that wartime trust came the deepest secret of all: the signals-intelligence pact that grew into the Five Eyes, the five sharing what they hear with one another and with no one else. What binds them is real and intimate, language, law, markets, kinship, and the habit of standing together; what divides them is that each has its own continent and its own century to answer to, the United States turning to the Pacific, Britain charting a course outside Europe, Australia and New Zealand drawn into Asia. A group that shows how far a shared tongue can carry a family of nations, and where its pull begins to fade.

## Triggers

**The English tongue and the common law**

The shared language and the common-law tradition are the true ground of the grouping: the five think, govern, trade, and argue in the same tongue and the same inherited body of law, a kinship deeper than any treaty.

**The Five Eyes**

Out of the wartime UKUSA agreement grew the Five Eyes, the intelligence-sharing alliance that binds [the United States](../../cultures/usa/play_usa.md), [the United Kingdom](../../cultures/united_kingdom/play_united_kingdom.md), [Canada](../../cultures/canada/play_canada.md), [Australia](../../cultures/australia/play_australia.md), and [New Zealand](../../cultures/new_zealand/play_new_zealand.md) into one trusted circle, sharing what they hear with each other and with no one else.

**The shared Crown and Commonwealth**

Four of the five share a Crown and all but one belong to the Commonwealth, the kinship of the English-speaking peoples carried in a common head of state and a family of nations, with [the United States](../../cultures/usa/play_usa.md) the great exception that broke away and led.

## Stakes

Whether a family bound by language, law, intelligence, and blood rather than by treaty or territory can stay a single bloc as each member answers to its own region and its own century, the United States to the Pacific and to its own power, Britain to its course outside Europe, Australia and New Zealand to Asia and to their own bicultural reckonings, or whether the shared tongue and the old kinship slowly give way to divergent interests and the Anglosphere becomes a memory of common descent rather than a living alliance.
