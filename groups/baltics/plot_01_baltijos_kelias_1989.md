# Plot: the Baltic Way, 1989

## Taxonomy

[play_baltics](play_baltics.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

By 1989 the three occupied republics of [Estonia](../../cultures/estonia/play_estonia.md), [Latvia](../../cultures/latvia/play_latvia.md), and [Lithuania](../../cultures/lithuania/play_lithuania.md) are stirring, their popular fronts pressing for freedom as the Soviet grip weakens. The 23rd of August marks fifty years since the Molotov-Ribbentrop Pact, the secret deal by which Hitler and Stalin handed the three to the USSR, the very crime that erased them from the map.

## Action

On that anniversary, around two million people join hands in a single unbroken chain some 675 kilometres long, running from Tallinn through Riga to Vilnius across all three nations at once. The Baltic Way makes the shared fate visible in one image: three peoples acting as one body, demanding that the world acknowledge the occupation was never lawful. Within two years all three restore their independence, and they go on together into the European Union and NATO.

## Stage

- [Estonia](../../cultures/estonia/play_estonia.md): the northern end of the chain, at Tallinn.
- [Latvia](../../cultures/latvia/play_latvia.md): the centre, at Riga, where the chain crosses the middle nation.
- [Lithuania](../../cultures/lithuania/play_lithuania.md): the southern end, at Vilnius.

## Tension

The chain proves that three small nations can act as one and shame an empire without a shot. But the unity is forged against a single occupier, and once freedom is won and the three are safe inside Europe, the question is whether the family holds when the shared enemy is only a memory, or whether each turns again to its own tongue, faith, and gaze.
