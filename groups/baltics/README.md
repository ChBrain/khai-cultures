# The Baltics: a khai group

The Baltics staged as a khai **group** — a play in the shared `groups/` collection
that **references its member cultures** rather than being a country of its own. The
three states of the eastern Baltic shore, Estonia, Latvia, and Lithuania, bound by
a shared occupation and a sung deliverance.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this production and answers for it.

## A group, not a culture

Like DACH and the Nordics, the Baltics is a **group**, not a country. It has **no
`geo.json`** and **no Hofstede pitch**: it is a _referencing_ entry. Its `Company`
casts the member plays, and the registry build derives the group's `references`
from exactly those casts, so the membership can never drift from what the play
actually links.

- **Members:** [Estonia](../../cultures/estonia/play_estonia.md),
  [Latvia](../../cultures/latvia/play_latvia.md),
  [Lithuania](../../cultures/lithuania/play_lithuania.md).
- **Kind:** `group`.
- **Shared plot:** [the Baltic Way, 1989](plot_01_baltijos_kelias_1989.md), the
  two-million-strong human chain across all three nations.

## Provenance

"The Baltic states" is the standard name for the three; they are bound by a
parallel history (independence c. 1918, Soviet annexation in 1940, restoration in
1991), the Singing Revolutions, the Baltic Way of 1989, and joint accession to the
EU and NATO in 2004, and they cooperate through the Baltic Assembly and Baltic
Council. They are not one language (Estonian is Finnic, not Baltic) or one state.
The staging and the architecture are original work.
