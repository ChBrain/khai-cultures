# The Baltics: References

The provenance behind this group. A group carries no Hofstede data of its own (it
has no single national profile); it is a referencing entry over its member
cultures.

## The grouping

"The Baltic states" is the standard name for the three nations of the eastern
Baltic shore. The membership recorded in the registry is **build-derived** from
the member plays this group casts, not authored by hand.

| ISO | Country   | Member culture                                          |
| --- | --------- | ------------------------------------------------------- |
| EE  | Estonia   | [estonia](../../cultures/estonia/play_estonia.md)       |
| LV  | Latvia    | [latvia](../../cultures/latvia/play_latvia.md)          |
| LT  | Lithuania | [lithuania](../../cultures/lithuania/play_lithuania.md) |

The grouping is bound by a parallel modern history (independence around 1918,
Soviet annexation in 1940, deportations, and the restoration of independence in
1991), the Singing Revolutions, the Baltic Way human chain of 23 August 1989, and
joint accession to the European Union and NATO in 2004, with standing cooperation
through the Baltic Assembly and Baltic Council. It is not a single language
(Estonian is Finnic, not Baltic) or a single state.
