# Play: the Baltics

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the house that holds this production and answers for it.

## Name

The Baltics, the three states on the eastern shore of the Baltic Sea: Estonia (EE), Latvia (LV), and Lithuania (LT), two Baltic tongues and one Finnic, read by the world as one.

## Arc

Three small nations on the same exposed shore that share one fate almost to the year. All three won independence around 1918, all three were swallowed by the Soviet Union in 1940, all three endured the deportations and the long occupation, and all three sang themselves free again in 1989-91, then walked together into the European Union, NATO, and the euro. What binds them is that shared arc of independence, annexation, and deliverance, and the insistence that the occupation was never lawful; what divides them is almost everything else: Latvian and Lithuanian are kin while Estonian is Finnic and looks north to Finland, Lithuania is Catholic and once a great power while the north is Lutheran and was long ruled by German barons. A group that shows a common catastrophe and a common deliverance can bind three peoples a shared language never did.

## Triggers

**The shared fate**

Independence around 1918, Soviet annexation in 1940, the deportations, and the restoration of 1991 befell [Estonia](../../cultures/estonia/play_estonia.md), [Latvia](../../cultures/latvia/play_latvia.md), and [Lithuania](../../cultures/lithuania/play_lithuania.md) almost in step, and carried all three together into the EU and NATO.

**The Singing Revolutions**

In all three, song became the bloodless weapon: mass choirs and festivals turned the longing for freedom into an unanswerable public act.

**The Baltic Way**

The human chain of 1989 joined the three capitals hand to hand, the single image of a people acting as one across three nations.

## Stakes

Whether three nations bound by one occupation and one deliverance stay a family now that the threat which united them wears a new face, or whether Estonia's gaze north and Lithuania's memory of greatness pull the three apart once the shared danger recedes.
