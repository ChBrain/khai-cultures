# Play: ASEAN

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the house that holds this production and answers for it.

## Name

ASEAN, the Association of Southeast Asian Nations: the regional community of eleven Southeast Asian states, founded by five neighbours in Bangkok in 1967 and grown across the whole region, from the mainland river plains to the maritime archipelagos, to the admission of Timor-Leste in 2025.

## Arc

The association that turned a region of quarrels into a community of neighbours. Born in Bangkok in 1967, when five nations just out of confrontation with one another chose talk over war in the shadow of the Cold War, ASEAN grew by patience: Brunei on its independence in 1984, then the very states across the old Cold War line, Vietnam, Laos, Myanmar, and Cambodia, until the founders and their former adversaries sat at one table as ten, and in 2025 an eleventh chair was drawn up for Timor-Leste, the region's youngest nation. What binds the members is the ASEAN Way, decision by consensus and non-interference in each other's affairs, a single market project, and the claim to sit at the centre of Asia's diplomacy; what divides them is almost everything else: eleven states with no shared tongue, faith, or coloniser, monarchies and republics, city-states and archipelagos, speaking to each other in English, the one language none of them owns. Strained by the crises it cannot vote away, the meltdown of 1997 and the agony of Myanmar, and courted by great powers on every side, it endures by the habit of meeting. A group that shows how far neighbours can travel on consensus alone, and where consensus reaches its limit.

## Triggers

**The ASEAN Way**

The association's true constitution is a habit, not a law: decision by consultation and consensus, musyawarah and mufakat, and non-interference in each other's affairs. It lets monarchies and republics, [Vietnam](../../cultures/vietnam/play_vietnam.md) and [Singapore](../../cultures/singapore/play_singapore.md), share one table without asking each other to change, and it is also the limit the association meets in every crisis it cannot vote away.

**The Bangkok Declaration**

The founding act of 1967: five neighbours, [Thailand](../../cultures/thailand/play_thailand.md), [Indonesia](../../cultures/indonesia/play_indonesia.md), [Malaysia](../../cultures/malaysia/play_malaysia.md), [the Philippines](../../cultures/philippines/play_philippines.md), and [Singapore](../../cultures/singapore/play_singapore.md), fresh from confrontation and territorial quarrel, signed a two-page declaration in Bangkok and chose the meeting room over the battlefield, the founding wager the association has ridden ever since.

**The one market**

The ASEAN Economic Community sets out to knit eleven very unequal economies into a single market and production base, from the financial towers of [Singapore](../../cultures/singapore/play_singapore.md) to the young economy of [Timor-Leste](../../cultures/timor_leste/play_timor_leste.md), so that a region once divided by empires trades as one bloc with the giants around it.

**ASEAN centrality**

The claim that the region's own association, not any great power, sits at the centre of Asia's diplomacy: the summits where the great powers come to Southeast Asia's table, hosted in turn by every member from [Indonesia](../../cultures/indonesia/play_indonesia.md) to [Laos](../../cultures/laos/play_laos.md), and the balancing act of eleven states courted by all sides that refuse to choose a side.

## Stakes

Whether eleven neighbours with no shared tongue, faith, or coloniser, bound only by geography and the habit of consensus, can remain one community and hold the centre of their own region, or whether the crises that consensus cannot answer and the pull of rival great powers turn the ASEAN Way from a bridge into a ceiling.
