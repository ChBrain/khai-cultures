# ASEAN: a khai group

ASEAN, the Association of Southeast Asian Nations, staged as a khai **group** - a
play in the shared `groups/` collection that **references its member cultures**
rather than being a country of its own. The regional community of eleven
Southeast Asian states, founded by five neighbours in Bangkok in 1967, grown
across the old Cold War line to ten, and completed by Timor-Leste in 2025, bound
by the ASEAN Way of consensus and non-interference, a single-market project, and
the claim to hold the centre of their own region.

## A group, not a culture

Like the Anglosphere, DACH, the Nordics, Benelux, the Visegrad Four, the Baltics,
Iberia, NATO, the EU, and CARICOM, ASEAN is a **group**, not a country. It has
**no `geo.json`** and **no Hofstede pitch**: it is a _referencing_ entry. Its
`Company` casts the member plays, and the registry build derives the group's
`references` from exactly those casts, so the membership can never drift from
what the play actually links.

- **Members:** all eleven member states, listed with their year of entry - the
  five founders of 1967 (Indonesia, Malaysia, the Philippines, Singapore,
  Thailand), Brunei (1984), Vietnam (1995), Laos and Myanmar (1997), Cambodia
  (1999), and Timor-Leste (2025), the eleventh and newest member.
- **Kind:** `group`.
- **The growth story:** like NATO and the EU, ASEAN's plots trace its
  **evolution**, not a single event:
  - [the Bangkok Declaration](plot_01_the_bangkok_declaration.md) - the 1967 founding by five neighbours fresh from confrontation, and the Bali treaty that set consensus as law.
  - [From Five to Ten](plot_02_from_five_to_ten.md) - Brunei in 1984, then the former adversaries across the old line: Vietnam, Laos, Myanmar, and Cambodia complete the ten in 1999.
  - [the ASEAN Way Tested](plot_03_the_asean_way_tested.md) - the 1997 financial crisis, the 2007 Charter, and the agony of Myanmar, consensus on trial.
  - [the Eleventh Chair](plot_04_the_eleventh_chair.md) - Timor-Leste's 2025 admission, the one-market project, and ASEAN centrality among the great powers.

## Provenance

ASEAN is the regional association of Southeast Asia, founded by the Bangkok
Declaration of 8 August 1967 and grown to eleven member states, working by the
ASEAN Way of consultation, consensus, and non-interference, with English as its
working language and a rotating chair. Its members and their histories are
matters of public record; the confrontations before its founding and the crises
that have tested it, including the situation in Myanmar, are staged soberly, from
the public record and without atrocity detail, and treated honestly in each
member culture. The staging and the architecture are original work.
