## The grouping

ASEAN, the Association of Southeast Asian Nations, is the regional community of
eleven Southeast Asian states, founded in Bangkok on 8 August 1967 and working by
the ASEAN Way: consultation, consensus, and non-interference in members' affairs.
The membership recorded in the registry is **build-derived** from the member
plays this group casts, not authored by hand. Its eleven members are all the
member states of the association as of the admission of Timor-Leste on 26 October
2025, the first enlargement since 1999. It is not a single state, but an
association of sovereign nations with no shared tongue, faith, or coloniser,
whose working language is English.

## The association

ASEAN was founded by the Bangkok Declaration (1967); its members declared a Zone
of Peace, Freedom and Neutrality (1971), signed the Treaty of Amity and
Cooperation at the first summit in Bali (1976), adopted the ASEAN Charter (2007),
and launched the ASEAN Economic Community (2015). Its Secretariat sits in
Jakarta, its chair rotates annually, and its summits anchor the wider ASEAN+3 and
East Asia Summit diplomacy, the practice known as ASEAN centrality. These are
matters of public record.
