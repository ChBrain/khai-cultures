# Plot: from five to ten

## Taxonomy

[play_asean](play_asean.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

Founded as five maritime and mainland neighbours on one side of the Cold War, the association faces the question every club faces: whether to remain the founders' circle or to become the whole region. As the Cold War thaws and ends, the states across the old line, the very adversaries the founding had braced against, knock at the door.

## Action

The widening comes in two movements. First the easy step: on 7 January 1984, [Brunei](../../cultures/brunei/play_brunei.md) joins within a week of its independence, a small sultanate folding itself into the neighbourhood as a founding-like sixth. Then the historic one: with the Cold War over and the Cambodian peace settled, the association crosses its own founding line. [Vietnam](../../cultures/vietnam/play_vietnam.md), the communist state the founders had feared, joins in July 1995; [Laos](../../cultures/laos/play_laos.md) and [Myanmar](../../cultures/myanmar/play_myanmar.md) follow together in July 1997; and [Cambodia](../../cultures/cambodia/play_cambodia.md), delayed two years by its own internal coup, completes the ten in April 1999. For the first time in its modern history, the whole of Southeast Asia, mainland and maritime, communist and capitalist, monarchy and republic, sits in one association of its own making, the ASEAN-10 the founders had only imagined.

## Stage

- [Brunei](../../cultures/brunei/play_brunei.md): the sixth member, joining within days of independence in 1984.
- [Vietnam](../../cultures/vietnam/play_vietnam.md): the first state from across the old line, a former adversary become a member in 1995.
- [Laos](../../cultures/laos/play_laos.md): the mainland's quiet interior, joining in the 1997 pair.
- [Myanmar](../../cultures/myanmar/play_myanmar.md): admitted in 1997 over Western objection, the widening's hardest bet.
- [Cambodia](../../cultures/cambodia/play_cambodia.md): the tenth member in 1999, its delayed entry proving the club watches its door.

## Tension

The widening trades coherence for completeness. Taking in the former adversaries makes the association truly regional, but it also stretches the membership across the widest political gulf in Asia, from open city-state to closed junta, and the admission of Myanmar in particular sets a long fuse: a member whose conduct the association's own rule of non-interference forbids it to police. Whether an ASEAN of the whole region can still act as one, or has widened past what consensus can carry, becomes the standing question of everything that follows.
