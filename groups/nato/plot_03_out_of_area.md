# Plot: Out of Area or Out of Business

## Taxonomy

[play_nato](play_nato.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

In 1989 the Berlin Wall falls; in 1991 the Soviet Union and the Warsaw Pact dissolve. The enemy the alliance was built against simply ceases to exist, and a body designed for one purpose faces a question that armies rarely survive: with the threat gone, why go on at all? The watchword of the decade becomes blunt - out of area or out of business.

## Action

NATO reinvents itself by acting beyond its own borders for the first time. Through the 1990s it intervenes in the Balkan wars, in Bosnia and then over Kosovo, no longer defending its own soil but enforcing a peace abroad. Then on 11 September 2001 the United States is attacked, and the alliance invokes Article 5 for the only time in its history - not to defend Europe from the East, but to stand with [the United States](../../cultures/usa/play_usa.md), its European members joining a long war in Afghanistan far from the North Atlantic. The defensive pact of the Cold War becomes an expeditionary one.

## Stage

- [the United States](../../cultures/usa/play_usa.md): attacked in 2001, the cause of the alliance's only Article 5 invocation.
- [the United Kingdom](../../cultures/united_kingdom/play_united_kingdom.md): foremost among the European allies in the wars that follow.
- [Germany](../../cultures/germany/play_germany.md): sending forces abroad for the first time since the war, a profound break with its past.

## Tension

By finding missions far from home the alliance proves it can outlive its enemy and keep America and Europe bound together. But it stretches its purpose and its forces thin, learns that wars of choice abroad strain the unity that collective defence once made automatic, and never quite settles whether a shield still has meaning when there is no longer a sword raised against it.
