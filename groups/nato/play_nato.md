# Play: NATO

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the house that holds this production and answers for it.

## Name

NATO, the North Atlantic Treaty Organization: a defensive alliance of thirty-two democracies on both shores of the Atlantic, founded in 1949 and bound by Article 5, the pledge that an attack on one is an attack on all.

## Arc

The one alliance that binds North America to Europe across an ocean, and the rare one that has not only survived its founding purpose but outgrown it. Born in 1949 of twelve nations against the Soviet threat, meant in Ismay's quip to keep the Americans in, the Russians out, and the Germans down, it froze the Cold War front for forty years without firing on its enemy, then woke in 1989 to find that enemy gone and had to invent a reason to live. It found new wars far from home, took in the very nations it had once faced across the line, and grew east to Russia's border, until a new Russian war returned it to its first purpose and brought even the old neutrals in. What binds the members is Article 5 and the transatlantic link; what divides them is almost everything else: thirty-two sovereign states that decide only by consensus, with different histories, different enemies, and a perennial quarrel over who pays. A group that shows an alliance can endure by changing what it is for.

## Triggers

**Article 5**

The pledge that an armed attack on one member is an attack on all binds the thirty-two into a single body of collective defence, the heart from which everything else hangs.

**The transatlantic link**

NATO is the one standing alliance that ties North America, [the United States](../../cultures/usa/play_usa.md) and [Canada](../../cultures/canada/play_canada.md), to Europe across the ocean, joining two continents in one defence.

**The open door**

Article 10's policy of enlargement carried the alliance from twelve members to thirty-two, admitting any European democracy able to meet its obligations and willing to be bound by consensus among sovereign equals.

## Stakes

Whether an alliance of thirty-two sovereign democracies, grown vast and spread across an ocean, can keep the unity and the credibility of "an attack on one is an attack on all" as its members differ on who the enemy is and who should pay, or whether the very growth and reach that made it strong make it too large and too varied to act as one.
