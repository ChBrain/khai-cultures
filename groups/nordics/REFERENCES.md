# The Nordics: References

The provenance behind this group. A group carries no Hofstede data of its own (it
has no single national profile); it is a referencing entry over its member
cultures.

## The grouping

The Nordics is the standard name for the five countries of the European north,
together with their associated territories. The membership recorded in the
registry is **build-derived** from the member plays this group casts, not authored
by hand.

| ISO | Country | Member culture                                    |
| --- | ------- | ------------------------------------------------- |
| DK  | Denmark | [denmark](../../cultures/denmark/play_denmark.md) |
| SE  | Sweden  | [sweden](../../cultures/sweden/play_sweden.md)    |
| NO  | Norway  | [norway](../../cultures/norway/play_norway.md)    |
| FI  | Finland | [finland](../../cultures/finland/play_finland.md) |
| IS  | Iceland | [iceland](../../cultures/iceland/play_iceland.md) |

The grouping is bound by a shared social and economic model and a long history of
cooperation, formalised in bodies such as the Nordic Council (1952) and the Nordic
passport union, not by a single language or a political union. Finnish belongs to
a different language family from the Scandinavian tongues, and Icelandic preserves
an older Norse; the group is held by a common temper and model, not a common
speech.
