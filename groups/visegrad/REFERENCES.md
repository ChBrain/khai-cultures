# The Visegrad Four: References

The provenance behind this group. A group carries no Hofstede data of its own (it
has no single national profile); it is a referencing entry over its member
cultures.

## The grouping

The Visegrad Four (V4) is the standard name for the Central European bloc. The
membership recorded in the registry is **build-derived** from the member plays
this group casts, not authored by hand.

| ISO | Country  | Member culture                                       |
| --- | -------- | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| PL  | Poland   | [poland](../../cultures/poland/play_poland.md)       |
| CZ  | Czechia  | [czechia](../../cultures/czechia/play_czechia.md)    |
| SK  | Slovakia | [slovakia](../../cultures/slovakia/play_slovakia.md) |
| HU  | Hungary  | [hungary](../../cultures/hungary/play_hungary.md)    |

The grouping is bound by a shared history under the Habsburg and Soviet empires,
the near-simultaneous revolutions of 1989, and the Visegrad Declaration of 15
February 1991, by which Poland, Czechoslovakia, and Hungary pledged to coordinate
their accession to NATO and the European Union; the three became four after the
1993 split of Czechoslovakia. It is a real instrument of regional cooperation,
not a single language or a single state.
