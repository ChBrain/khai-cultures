# Play: the Visegrad Four

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the house that holds this production and answers for it.

## Name

The Visegrad Four (V4), the Central European bloc of Poland (PL), Czechia (CZ), Slovakia (SK), and Hungary (HU), named after the Hungarian town of Visegrad where their kings once met and their leaders met again.

## Arc

Four nations in the middle of Europe, between the German lands and Russia, that have shared the same masters and the same longing. For centuries they lived under empires not their own, Habsburg then Soviet, and in 1989 they threw off communism within months of one another. Free at last, three of them met in 1991 at Visegrad, the very town where their medieval kings had allied in 1335, and pledged to cross together into the West; when Czechoslovakia split, the three became four. What binds them is that shared history and the return-to-Europe project that carried them into NATO and the European Union together; what divides them is almost everything else: two pairs of Slavic tongues and the kinless Magyar, Catholic and Hussite and secular tempers, and politics that pull them now together, now sharply apart. A group that shows a common road out of empire need not make a common mind once the gate is passed.

## Triggers

**The shared empires**

Centuries under powers not their own, Habsburg and then Soviet, gave [Poland](../../cultures/poland/play_poland.md), [Czechia](../../cultures/czechia/play_czechia.md), [Slovakia](../../cultures/slovakia/play_slovakia.md), and [Hungary](../../cultures/hungary/play_hungary.md) a common Central European fate.

**The return to Europe**

Freed in 1989 within months of each other, the four coordinated their way into NATO and the European Union together, the bloc built to leave the East and rejoin the West.

**The Visegrad pledge**

The 1991 declaration that named the group revived the memory of the 1335 royal congress at the same town, binding the four to a shared road even as their politics diverge.

## Stakes

Whether a bloc forged to enter Europe still coheres now that all four are inside, or whether Poland, Czechia, Slovakia, and Hungary drift apart once the common goal that united them is reached, four very different nations who discover, in their resemblance, how unlike they are.
