# The Visegrad Four: a khai group

The Visegrad Four (V4) staged as a khai **group** — a play in the shared `groups/`
collection that **references its member cultures** rather than being a country of
its own. The Central European bloc of Poland, Czechia, Slovakia, and Hungary,
bound by a shared history under empire and the project of returning to Europe.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this production and answers for it.

## A group, not a culture

Like DACH and the Nordics, the Visegrad Four is a **group**, not a country. It has
**no `geo.json`** and **no Hofstede pitch**: it is a _referencing_ entry. Its
`Company` casts the member plays, and the registry build derives the group's
`references` from exactly those casts, so the membership can never drift from what
the play actually links.

- **Members:** [Poland](../../cultures/poland/play_poland.md),
  [Czechia](../../cultures/czechia/play_czechia.md),
  [Slovakia](../../cultures/slovakia/play_slovakia.md),
  [Hungary](../../cultures/hungary/play_hungary.md).
- **Kind:** `group`.
- **Shared plot:** [the Visegrad Declaration, 1991](plot_01_deklaracia_1991.md),
  the founding pledge that revived the 1335 royal congress and grew from three
  members to four after the Velvet Divorce.

## Provenance

The Visegrad Group was founded by the declaration of 15 February 1991 at Visegrad,
Hungary, by Poland, Czechoslovakia, and Hungary, becoming four members when
Czechoslovakia split in 1993. It is a real, standing instrument of regional
cooperation, named for the 1335 Congress of Visegrad. The staging and the
architecture are original work.
