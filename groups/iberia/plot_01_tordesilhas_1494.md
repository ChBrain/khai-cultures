# Plot: the Treaty of Tordesillas, 1494

## Taxonomy

[play_iberia](play_iberia.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

At the close of the 15th century the two crowns of the peninsula are racing onto the ocean. [Portugal](../../cultures/portugal/play_portugal.md) has crept down the coast of Africa for decades, and [Spain](../../cultures/spain/play_spain.md), fresh from taking Granada, has just sent Columbus west and come back claiming a new world. Two Iberian neighbours suddenly need to decide who owns what across waters neither has finished crossing.

## Action

In 1494, with the Pope's blessing, Spain and Portugal sign the Treaty of Tordesillas and draw a meridian down the unmapped globe: lands to the west fall to Castile, lands to the east to Portugal. Two small kingdoms on one peninsula carve the world in two between them, the line that gives Portugal Brazil and Spain most of the Americas, and that sets the bounds of the two great seaborne empires. For a moment Iberia is the centre of the world, dividing it like an apple.

## Stage

- [Spain](../../cultures/spain/play_spain.md): the crown of Castile and Aragon, taking the western hemisphere.
- [Portugal](../../cultures/portugal/play_portugal.md): the seafaring kingdom, taking the eastern route and Brazil.

## Tension

The treaty binds the two nations into a single Iberian moment of world mastery, partners in dividing the earth. But it also fixes them as rivals with separate empires and separate destinies, and the grandeur it grants is the very height from which both will later fall, leaving two nations on one peninsula to remember, differently, when they owned the world.
