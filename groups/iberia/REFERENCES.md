# Iberia: References

The provenance behind this group. A group carries no Hofstede data of its own (it
has no single national profile); it is a referencing entry over its member
cultures.

## The grouping

Iberia is the standard name for the peninsula south of the Pyrenees and its two
nations. The membership recorded in the registry is **build-derived** from the
member plays this group casts, not authored by hand.

| ISO | Country  | Member culture                                       |
| --- | -------- | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| ES  | Spain    | [spain](../../cultures/spain/play_spain.md)          |
| PT  | Portugal | [portugal](../../cultures/portugal/play_portugal.md) |

The grouping is bound by a shared geography and history: Roman Hispania, Moorish
al-Andalus and the Reconquista, the age of discovery and the Treaty of Tordesillas
(1494), the Iberian Union of 1580-1640, and parallel returns to democracy and to
the European Communities in 1986. The two languages are close kin (Ibero-Romance),
but Iberia is not one tongue or one state.
