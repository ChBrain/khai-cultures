# Iberia: a khai group

Iberia staged as a khai **group** — a play in the shared `groups/` collection that
**references its member cultures** rather than being a country of its own. The two
nations of the Iberian Peninsula, Spain and Portugal, bound by one ground and one
history and divided by Portugal's long insistence on not being Spain.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this production and answers for it.

## A group, not a culture

Like DACH and the Nordics, Iberia is a **group**, not a country. It has **no
`geo.json`** and **no Hofstede pitch**: it is a _referencing_ entry. Its `Company`
casts the member plays, and the registry build derives the group's `references`
from exactly those casts, so the membership can never drift from what the play
actually links.

- **Members:** [Spain](../../cultures/spain/play_spain.md),
  [Portugal](../../cultures/portugal/play_portugal.md).
- **Kind:** `group`.
- **Shared plot:** [the Treaty of Tordesillas, 1494](plot_01_tordesilhas_1494.md),
  the line down the globe by which the two crowns divided the unexplored world.

## Provenance

Iberia is the standard name for the peninsula south of the Pyrenees and, by
extension, for its two nations. They are bound by a shared geography and history,
Roman Hispania, al-Andalus, the Reconquista, the age of discovery and the Treaty
of Tordesillas (1494), the Iberian Union of 1580-1640, and parallel 20th-century
paths into democracy and the European Communities (1986). They remain two distinct
states and two languages. The staging and the architecture are original work.
