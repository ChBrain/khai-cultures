# Play: Iberia

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the house that holds this production and answers for it.

## Name

Iberia, the peninsula south of the Pyrenees between the Atlantic and the Mediterranean: Spain (ES) and Portugal (PT), two nations on one near-island of Europe.

## Arc

Two nations who share a peninsula, a long history, and a pair of close-kin tongues, and who once, between them, divided the whole world. Together they were Roman Hispania and Moorish al-Andalus, together they fought the centuries of the Reconquista, and at the height of the age of discovery they drew a line down an unmapped globe and took a hemisphere each. For sixty years they even wore one crown. Both then lost their empires, fell under long dictatorships, and returned to democracy and to Europe in the same year. What binds them is the peninsula and that shared past of faith, frontier, and sail; what divides them is Portugal's whole identity as the land that is not Castile, the Atlantic gaze against the Mediterranean, and two rival empires that never merged. A group that shows how one ground and one history can still hold two nations who define themselves by their difference.

## Triggers

**The shared peninsula**

Roman Hispania, Moorish al-Andalus, and the long Reconquista shaped [Spain](../../cultures/spain/play_spain.md) and [Portugal](../../cultures/portugal/play_portugal.md) on the same ground before they were two nations.

**The age of discovery**

The two seaborne crowns opened the ocean routes and, at Tordesillas, divided the unexplored world between them, building rival empires across three continents.

**The parallel paths**

Both lost their empires, endured long 20th-century dictatorships, and returned together to democracy and to Europe in 1986, walking the same road a step apart.

## Stakes

Whether two nations who share a peninsula and a history but built rival empires can be read as one Iberia, or whether Portugal's long turn away from Castile, and the Atlantic set against the Mediterranean, keep Spain and Portugal forever two.
