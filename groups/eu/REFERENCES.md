# The European Union: References

The provenance behind this group. A group carries no Hofstede data of its own (it
has no single national profile); it is a referencing entry over its member
cultures.

## The grouping

The European Union is the union of twenty-seven European democracies, grown out of
the European Coal and Steel Community (1951) and the European Economic Community
founded by the Treaty of Rome (1957), and given its present form by the Maastricht
Treaty (1992). The membership recorded in the registry is **build-derived** from
the member plays this group casts, not authored by hand. It binds the members in a
single market with four freedoms, a common body of law and shared institutions,
and (for most) the euro. It is not a single language (it has twenty-four official
ones) or a single state.

## The accession waves

| Year | Joined                                                                                               |
| ---- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1958 | Belgium, France, Germany, Italy, Luxembourg, the Netherlands (the six founders, Treaty of Rome 1957) |
| 1973 | Denmark, Ireland (and the United Kingdom, which left in 2020)                                        |
| 1981 | Greece                                                                                               |
| 1986 | Portugal, Spain                                                                                      |
| 1995 | Austria, Finland, Sweden                                                                             |
| 2004 | Cyprus, Czechia, Estonia, Hungary, Latvia, Lithuania, Malta, Poland, Slovakia, Slovenia              |
| 2007 | Bulgaria, Romania                                                                                    |
| 2013 | Croatia                                                                                              |

The United Kingdom was a member from 1973 until its withdrawal in 2020 (Brexit),
and so is not one of the present twenty-seven; it appears in the growth story as
the first state ever to leave.
