# Plot: Maastricht and the Euro

## Taxonomy

[play_eu](play_eu.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

At the end of the 1980s the Cold War ends and [Germany](../../cultures/germany/play_germany.md) reunites, and the community decides to match the moment by deepening as well as widening - to bind a bigger Germany and a freer continent into something stronger than a common market.

## Action

In 1992 the members sign the Maastricht Treaty, which turns the European Economic Community into the European Union: it creates a shared European citizenship, sets the path to a single currency, and reaches beyond trade into foreign policy and justice. The euro arrives as money in 2002, carried in the pockets of most members; the Schengen agreement erases the border posts so that people cross the continent without showing a passport. The community becomes a Union with a currency, a citizenship, and open internal borders - a leap in integration without precedent among sovereign states.

## Stage

- [Germany](../../cultures/germany/play_germany.md) and [France](../../cultures/france/play_france.md): the axis that drives the deepening, binding a reunited Germany into the Union.
- [Luxembourg](../../cultures/luxembourg/play_luxembourg.md) and [the Netherlands](../../cultures/netherlands/play_netherlands.md): small founders at the heart of the single currency and open borders.

## Tension

By deepening into a Union with one money and one space, the members tie their fates together as never before, and "ever-closer union" becomes concrete in the coins and the open road. But a shared currency without a shared treasury binds the members so tightly that one nation's debt becomes everyone's crisis, as the eurozone will learn - the deepening that strengthens them also leaves them unable to let go.
