# Play: the European Union

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the house that holds this production and answers for it.

## Name

The European Union, the union of twenty-seven European democracies that pool part of their sovereignty in a single market and a shared body of law, from the six founders of 1957 to Croatia in 2013.

## Arc

Out of the ruins of a continent that had torn itself apart twice in thirty years, six nations bound their coal and steel together so that war between them would become not just unthinkable but impossible, then opened a common market, and then went where no sovereign states had gone before: pooling their power into a Union with its own citizenship, currency, court, and parliament. It widened from six members to twenty-eight and deepened from a market into a Union, reuniting a continent the Cold War had split. What binds the members is the single market and its four freedoms, a common law above the states, and the founding promise of an ever-closer union; what divides them is almost everything else: twenty-seven sovereign states and twenty-four official languages in a permanent argument over how much closer, strained by the euro, by migration, by the rule of law, and shaken by the first member ever to walk out. A group that shows nations can pool their sovereignty without dissolving into one.

## Triggers

**The single market**

The four freedoms - the free movement of goods, services, capital, and people - bind the members' economies into one market, the core that everything else is built around.

**The shared law and institutions**

A common body of law stands above the member states, made and judged by shared institutions - the Commission, the Parliament, the Court of Justice - with a single currency, the euro, for most of them.

**Ever-closer union**

The founding aspiration and the open door carried the Union from six members to twenty-eight and from a common market to a Union with its own citizenship, and keep alive the perennial argument over how much closer it should go.

## Stakes

Whether twenty-seven sovereign democracies speaking twenty-four languages can keep pooling their sovereignty and acting as one in a hard world, or whether the strains - the euro, migration, the rule of law, and the example of the one that left - pull the promise of ever-closer union apart, leaving a market where a union was meant to be.
