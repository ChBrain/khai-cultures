# The Americas: References

The provenance behind this group. A group carries no Hofstede data of its own (it
has no single national profile); it is a referencing entry over its member
cultures.

## The grouping

The Americas is the Western Hemisphere: the two continents of North and South
America, joined by the Central American isthmus and ringed by the islands of the
Caribbean, the New World of the European voyages. Its membership recorded in the
registry is **build-derived** from the member plays this group casts, not authored
by hand. Its 35 members are the sovereign states of the hemisphere, from the United
States and Canada through Central America and the Caribbean to the republics of
South America. It is not a single state but a continent of nations, gathered loosely
under the hemispheric ideal embodied in the Organization of American States, where
every state of the Americas takes a seat.

## The hemisphere

The hemispheric ideal of a family of American republics, Pan-Americanism, found its
institution in the Organization of American States, the regional body that seats
every state of the Americas and is a matter of public record. It binds the same
nations cast here under one hemispheric framework, however unequal in power and
wealth its members remain.

## Historical sources

The growth-and-change story is drawn from the public historical record and
credited here, never claimed; the staging is original work. The conquest, the
demographic catastrophe of the indigenous peoples, the transatlantic slave trade,
and the modern record of intervention are framed soberly, on the public record,
without atrocity detail and without taking a side.
