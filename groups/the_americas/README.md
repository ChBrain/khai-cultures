# The Americas: a khai group

The Americas staged as a khai **group** - a play in the shared `groups/`
collection that **references its member cultures** rather than being a country of
its own. The Western Hemisphere: two continents joined by the Central American
isthmus and ringed by the Caribbean, the New World from its first peoples to its
republics, gathered loosely under the hemispheric ideal of the Organization of
American States.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this production and answers for it.

## A group, not a culture

Like the Anglosphere, DACH, the Nordics, Benelux, the Visegrad Four, the Baltics,
Iberia, NATO, and the EU, the Americas is a **group**, not a country. It has **no
`geo.json`** and **no Hofstede pitch**: it is a _referencing_ entry. Its `Company`
casts the member plays, and the registry build derives the group's `references`
from exactly those casts, so the membership can never drift from what the play
actually links.

- **Members:** the 35 sovereign states of the Western Hemisphere - North America,
  Central America, the Caribbean, and South America - from the United States and
  Canada to Brazil, Argentina, and the islands of the Antilles.
- **Kind:** `group`.
- **The growth story:** like NATO and the EU, the Americas' plots trace its
  **evolution**, not a single event:
  - [the First Peoples and the Encounter](plot_01_the_first_peoples_and_the_encounter.md) - the indigenous civilizations and nations, the conquest from 1492, and the demographic catastrophe and survival of the first peoples.
  - [the Colonial New World](plot_02_the_colonial_new_world.md) - the three colonial worlds, the silver, sugar, and the enslaved, and the plural societies born of the mixing.
  - [the Age of Independence](plot_03_the_age_of_independence.md) - 1776, Haiti 1804, the Latin American wars of 1810-1825, Brazil's imperial path, and the Caribbean decolonization of the twentieth century.
  - [the Hemisphere](plot_04_the_hemisphere.md) - the modern Americas, Pan-Americanism and the OAS, the northern power, migration, and the shared and divided continent.

## Provenance

The Americas is the Western Hemisphere: two continents and the Caribbean, the New
World of the European voyages, home first to its indigenous peoples and remade by
conquest, slavery, and immigration into a hemisphere of settler and creole-mestizo
republics. Its members and their histories are matters of public record. The
conquest, the demographic catastrophe of the first peoples, the enslavement of
millions of Africans, and the later record of intervention are staged soberly, on
the public record, without atrocity detail and without taking a side; the
dispossession and slavery at the root of these nations are treated honestly in the
staging and in each member culture. The staging and the architecture are original
work.
