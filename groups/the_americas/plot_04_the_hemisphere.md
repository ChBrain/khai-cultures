# Plot: the Hemisphere

## Taxonomy

[play_the_americas](play_the_americas.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

With the New World free, the question becomes what its many nations are to one another. Across the modern era an idea takes hold that the republics of the Americas share a hemisphere and a destiny, and out of it grows an institution where every American state takes a seat, even as the great inequalities and the weight of the northern power keep the hemisphere divided.

## Action

The modern Americas live the tension between the ideal of one hemisphere and the reality of many. Pan-Americanism, the notion that the republics of the New World form a single family, gathers them from the nineteenth century onward into a hemispheric conversation, and in the twentieth it is given a home: the Organization of American States, where every state from [Canada](../../cultures/canada/play_canada.md) to Chile takes its seat. Yet the conversation is unequal. [The United States](../../cultures/usa/play_usa.md) rises to be the colossus of the north, its power shaping every neighbor's century, and the long history of its intervention in the affairs of Latin America and the Caribbean is part of the public record. The hemisphere is bound together by migration, above all the great northward movement across the border between [the United States](../../cultures/usa/play_usa.md) and [Mexico](../../cultures/mexico/play_mexico.md), and by the canal at [Panama](../../cultures/panama/play_panama.md) that joins its oceans and its trade. [Brazil](../../cultures/brazil/play_brazil.md) rises as a power of the south. The Americas remain at once a shared continent and a divided one: one in geography and in the New World's common history, divided by language, by wealth, and by the asymmetry of power that runs from north to south.

## Stage

- [the United States](../../cultures/usa/play_usa.md): the colossus of the north, whose power shapes the whole hemisphere.
- [Mexico](../../cultures/mexico/play_mexico.md): the great northern neighbor, the border, and the migration that binds it to the north.
- [Brazil](../../cultures/brazil/play_brazil.md): the rising power of the south.
- [Panama](../../cultures/panama/play_panama.md): the canal that joins the oceans and the hemisphere's trade.
- [Canada](../../cultures/canada/play_canada.md): the northern member, full partner of the hemisphere's institutions.
- [Colombia](../../cultures/colombia/play_colombia.md): a southern republic long entangled with the northern power.

## Tension

The hemisphere holds an ideal of unity, the family of American republics gathered in the Organization of American States, against the hard facts of language, wealth, and power that pull it apart. The shadow of the colossus of the north and the long record of intervention, set down here soberly and without taking a side, weigh on every effort at hemispheric union, so that the Americas remain one New World in name and many worlds in fact, bound and divided by the same long history.
