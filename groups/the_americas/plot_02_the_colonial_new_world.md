# Plot: the Colonial New World

## Taxonomy

[play_the_americas](play_the_americas.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

After the conquest the New World is parceled among the powers of Europe, and three distinct colonial worlds take shape across the hemisphere: the vast Iberian realms of Spain and Portugal, the British seaboard and islands, and the smaller French and Dutch holdings. Each builds its wealth on the same twin foundations: the silver and sugar of the land, and the labor of the enslaved.

## Action

For three centuries the Americas are colonies. The Spanish hold the bulk of the continent, from the silver mountains of [Bolivia](../../cultures/bolivia/play_bolivia.md) to the sugar islands of [Cuba](../../cultures/cuba/play_cuba.md); Portugal builds an empire in [Brazil](../../cultures/brazil/play_brazil.md) on sugar and gold; Britain plants the seaboard colonies that would become [the United States](../../cultures/usa/play_usa.md) and seizes islands like [Barbados](../../cultures/barbados/play_barbados.md), the first great sugar colony; France and the Netherlands hold the rest, from the richest of all sugar colonies on Hispaniola to the plantation coast of [Suriname](../../cultures/suriname/play_suriname.md). Across all three worlds the engine is the same: the mines pour out silver, the plantations pour out sugar, and the labor is supplied by the largest forced migration in history, the transatlantic trade that carries millions of enslaved Africans to the New World. From this violent economy grow societies plural at the root, where European masters, indigenous survivors, and enslaved Africans live entangled lives, and where new peoples - creole, mestizo, mulatto - are born of the mixing. The colonial Americas are unequal and unfree, but they are already the meeting place of three worlds, and the plural societies of the hemisphere are forged here.

## Stage

- [Brazil](../../cultures/brazil/play_brazil.md): the Portuguese empire of sugar and gold, with the largest enslaved population of all.
- [Cuba](../../cultures/cuba/play_cuba.md): the great Spanish sugar island of the Antilles.
- [Haiti](../../cultures/haiti/play_haiti.md): the French colony of Saint-Domingue, the richest and most brutal of the sugar lands.
- [Barbados](../../cultures/barbados/play_barbados.md): the early British sugar island, the model for the plantation world.
- [the United States](../../cultures/usa/play_usa.md): the British seaboard colonies, plural and unequal from the start.
- [Suriname](../../cultures/suriname/play_suriname.md): the Dutch plantation coast of South America.

## Tension

The colonial New World is built on conquest and on the largest forced migration in history, the enslavement of millions of Africans alongside the dispossession of the first peoples, recorded here soberly and on the public record. Yet from that injustice grew the plural, mixed societies that define the hemisphere, so that the colonial centuries are at once the deepest of its wounds and the crucible in which its peoples were made.
