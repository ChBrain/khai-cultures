# Play: Benelux

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the house that holds this production and answers for it.

## Name

Benelux, the union of the Low Countries: **Be**lgium (BE), the **Ne**therlands (NL), and **Lux**embourg (LU), three small kingdoms and a grand duchy at the delta where the Rhine, the Scheldt, and the Meuse meet the North Sea.

## Arc

Three small countries on the flat, wet edge of the continent that turned their smallness into a method. Squeezed between France and Germany and fought over for centuries, they learned to live by trade, water, and compromise, and after the last war they did something new: while still in exile they agreed to drop the borders between them, built the first modern customs union, and so drew the blueprint that France and Germany would enlarge into the European project. What binds them is the union and the trading delta; what divides them is almost everything else: three crowns, and a tangle of tongues - Dutch, French, German, and Luxembourgish - with no single language of their own. A group that shows how the smallest states, by uniting first, taught a continent to follow.

## Triggers

**The customs union**

The agreement to drop the borders between [Belgium](../../cultures/belgium/play_belgium.md), [the Netherlands](../../cultures/netherlands/play_netherlands.md), and [Luxembourg](../../cultures/luxembourg/play_luxembourg.md) became the first working model of the integration that built the European Union.

**The trading delta**

The great ports and rivers - Rotterdam, Antwerp, the Rhine and the Scheldt - knit the three into one merchant economy facing the sea and the world.

**The small-state compromise**

Three small lands wedged between France and Germany made a habit of negotiation and host between them the seats of Europe - Brussels, Luxembourg, The Hague - turning their exposed position into a vocation.

## Stakes

Whether the original union of small lands still means something of its own now that the Europe it prototyped has grown up around it, or whether Belgium, the Netherlands, and Luxembourg are simply three neighbours who once, by going first, showed everyone else the way.
