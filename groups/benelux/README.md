# Benelux: a khai group

Benelux (**Be**lgium, the **Ne**therlands, **Lux**embourg) staged as a khai
**group** — a play in the shared `groups/` collection that **references its member
cultures** rather than being a country of its own. The union of the Low Countries:
three small states that built the first modern customs union and so prototyped
European integration.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this production and answers for it.

## A group, not a culture

Like DACH and the Nordics, Benelux is a **group**, not a country. It has **no
`geo.json`** (no ISO of its own) and **no Hofstede pitch** (no single profile): it
is a _referencing_ entry. Its `Company` casts the member plays, and the registry
build derives the group's `references` from exactly those casts, so the membership
can never drift from what the play actually links.

- **Members:** [Belgium](../../cultures/belgium/play_belgium.md),
  [the Netherlands](../../cultures/netherlands/play_netherlands.md),
  [Luxembourg](../../cultures/luxembourg/play_luxembourg.md).
- **Kind:** `group` (the registry stamps it, so the website can tell it from a
  culture and resolve its members on the map).
- **Shared plot:** [the Customs Union](plot_01_douane_unie.md), the 1944-1958
  founding that became the blueprint for the European Union.

## Provenance

Benelux is the standard name for the economic union of the three Low Countries,
formalised in the customs convention of 1944 (in force 1948) and the Benelux
Treaty of 1958, renewed in 2008. It is a real treaty union, the oldest in
modern Europe. The staging and the architecture are original work.
