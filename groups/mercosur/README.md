# Mercosur: a khai group

Mercosur, the Southern Common Market, staged as a khai **group** - a play in the
shared `groups/` collection that **references its member cultures** rather than
being a country of its own. The economic union of the Southern Cone, born of rivals
reconciled: two giants once set against each other joined to two small but proud
nations the great rivers bind to them, and carried up into the Andes - Argentina,
Brazil, Paraguay, Uruguay, and Bolivia.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house

## A group, not a culture

Like the Anglosphere, DACH, the Nordics, Benelux, the Visegrad Four, the Baltics,
Iberia, NATO, and the EU, Mercosur is a **group**, not a country. It has **no
`geo.json`** and **no Hofstede pitch**: it is a _referencing_ entry. Its `Company`
casts the member plays, and the registry build derives the group's `references`
from exactly those casts, so the membership can never drift from what the play
actually links.

- **Members:** the four founders of 1991 carried up into the Andes - Argentina,
  Brazil, Paraguay, and Uruguay, with Bolivia by full accession. Venezuela joined
  but is currently suspended, so it is not cast here.
- **Kind:** `group`.
- **The growth story:** like NATO and the EU, Mercosur's plots trace its
  **evolution**, not a single event:
  - [the Rival Giants](plot_01_the_rival_giants.md) - the long Argentina-Brazil rivalry over the Plata and the south.
  - [Democracy and Reconciliation](plot_02_democracy_and_reconciliation.md) - the return of democracy in the 1980s and the rapprochement that made a bloc possible.
  - [the Treaty of Asuncion](plot_03_the_treaty_of_asuncion.md) - 1991, the founding of Mercosur by the four, the common market in name and law.
  - [the Southern Market](plot_04_the_southern_market.md) - the modern bloc: Bolivia's accession into the Andes, trade and tension, and the river basin today.

## Provenance

Mercosur is the economic union of the Southern Cone, founded by the Treaty of
Asuncion in 1991 and bound by the great Rio de la Plata basin, by the trade in
beef, soy, grain, and protein, and by a shared Iberian-creole inheritance of
Spanish and Portuguese. Its members and their histories are matters of public
record; the Southern Cone dictatorships, where the staging touches them, are named
soberly and without taking any side, treated honestly here and in each member
culture. The staging and the architecture are original work.
