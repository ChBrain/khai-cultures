# Plot: the Treaty of Asuncion

## Taxonomy

[play_mercosur](play_mercosur.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

In the Paraguayan capital, four nations of the Southern Cone gather to set their names to a single document. The reconciliation of the giants is ready to become an institution: a treaty to build a common market of the south.

## Action

In 1991, in the city of Asuncion, [Paraguay](../../cultures/paraguay/play_paraguay.md), [Argentina](../../cultures/argentina/play_argentina.md), [Brazil](../../cultures/brazil/play_brazil.md), and [Uruguay](../../cultures/uruguay/play_uruguay.md) sign the Treaty of Asuncion, founding Mercosur, the Southern Common Market. The four pledge the free movement of goods, services, and factors of production among them, a common external tariff, and the coordination of their policies, the building blocks of a true common market. The giant and the rival-giant join the two small but proud nations that the great rivers bind to them, so that the bloc is born as four, the two large powers balanced against two smaller members who hold a real voice in the union. The treaty turns a decade of bilateral rapprochement into a four-nation institution and gives the Southern Cone its common market in name and in law.

## Stage

- [Paraguay](../../cultures/paraguay/play_paraguay.md): host of the founding treaty and a small founder of the bloc.
- [Argentina](../../cultures/argentina/play_argentina.md): the southern giant and a founder.
- [Brazil](../../cultures/brazil/play_brazil.md): the northern giant, largest of the founders.
- [Uruguay](../../cultures/uruguay/play_uruguay.md): the small founder at the mouth of the Plata.

## Tension

The founding joins two giants and two small nations in a single market, and the asymmetry between them is present from the first signature: the two large economies dwarf the two small ones, and the question of whether the smaller members can hold a true and equal voice, rather than orbit the giants, is built into the bloc at its birth. The treaty creates the form of a common market; whether the union behind that form can be made real is the work it leaves to the years that follow.
