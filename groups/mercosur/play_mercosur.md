# Play: Mercosur

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the house that holds this production and answers for it.

## Name

Mercosur, the Southern Common Market: the economic union of the Southern Cone, born in 1991 when two giants long set against each other joined two small but proud neighbors to build a common market of the south. Its core is the four founders carried up into the Andes: Argentina, Brazil, Paraguay, Uruguay, and Bolivia.

## Arc

The Southern Common Market, the economic union of the Southern Cone. For more than a century its two giants, Argentina and Brazil, were rivals for mastery of the south and the great Rio de la Plata basin, twice nearly at war and long arming against each other. The return of democracy to both in the 1980s turned rivalry into reconciliation, and in 1991 Argentina, Brazil, Paraguay, and Uruguay signed the Treaty of Asuncion to build a common market, the giant and the rival-giant joined to two small but proud nations that the great rivers bind to them. Bolivia later acceded as a full member, carrying the bloc up into the Andes; Venezuela joined but was suspended, so it is not cast among the members here. Bound by the river basin and its trade, by beef, soy, and the world's grain and protein, by a shared Iberian-creole inheritance of Spanish and Portuguese, and by the dream of a Southern union that can bargain with the world; divided by the old asymmetry between the giants and the small, by recurring economic crises, and by the perennial argument over how open the market should be. A group that shows how far a shared river basin and a common trade can carry a union of rivals reconciled, and where the old imbalance and the cycle of crises begin to strain it.

## Triggers

**The Rio de la Plata basin and its rivers**

The great Rio de la Plata basin and the rivers that feed it, the Parana, the Paraguay, and the Uruguay, are the true ground of the grouping: the waters bind [Argentina](../../cultures/argentina/play_argentina.md), [Brazil](../../cultures/brazil/play_brazil.md), [Paraguay](../../cultures/paraguay/play_paraguay.md), and [Uruguay](../../cultures/uruguay/play_uruguay.md) into one watershed long before any treaty, the highways of the south down which trade and history have always run.

**Beef, soy, and the world's grain and protein**

The bloc is one of the planet's great breadbaskets and feedlots: beef from the pampas, soy and grain from the vast interior, the world's protein and bread carried out through the river ports. This shared vocation as supplier to the world is what gives the common market its weight, and the argument over how to sell it together its edge.

**Democracy's return and the Southern Cone union**

The bloc was made possible only when democracy returned to the south in the 1980s, turning a century of rivalry between the giants into reconciliation, and out of that rapprochement came the dream of a Southern union that could bargain with the world as one, joining [Argentina](../../cultures/argentina/play_argentina.md) and [Brazil](../../cultures/brazil/play_brazil.md) to [Paraguay](../../cultures/paraguay/play_paraguay.md), [Uruguay](../../cultures/uruguay/play_uruguay.md), and later [Bolivia](../../cultures/bolivia/play_bolivia.md).

## Stakes

Whether two giants that were once rivals and the smaller nations bound to them by the great rivers can hold a true common market of the south, widened now into the Andes, or whether the old asymmetry between the giants and the small, the cycle of recurring crises, and the endless argument over how open the market should be keep Mercosur a treaty more than a union.
