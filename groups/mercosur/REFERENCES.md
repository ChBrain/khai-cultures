## The grouping

Mercosur, the Southern Common Market, is the economic union of the Southern Cone,
founded by the Treaty of Asuncion in 1991 and bound by the great Rio de la Plata
basin, the trade in grain and protein, and a shared Iberian-creole inheritance. The
membership recorded in the registry is **build-derived** from the member plays this
group casts, not authored by hand. Its members are Argentina, Brazil, Paraguay,
Uruguay, and Bolivia. It is not a single state, and it joins two large economies to
smaller members that the river basin binds to them.

## The founding and the bloc

Mercosur was created by the Treaty of Asuncion, signed in 1991 by Argentina,
Brazil, Paraguay, and Uruguay, establishing a common market with the free movement
of goods and a common external tariff. Bolivia, long an associate, completed
accession as a full member; Venezuela joined but was later suspended and is not
cast here. The bloc and its history are matters of public record.
