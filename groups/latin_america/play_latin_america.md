# Play: Latin America

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the house that holds this production and answers for it.

## Name

Latin America: the family of nations of the Americas whose tongues descend from Latin, born of the Iberian conquest and the long synthesis of indigenous, European, and African peoples into a Catholic, creole, mestizo world. Its span is the Spanish-, Portuguese-, and French- or Creole-speaking republics from Mexico to the southern cone, the Caribbean, and the great mass of Brazil, deliberately apart from the English- and Dutch-speaking Americas around them.

## Arc

A grouping bound not by one government but by a shared root and a shared wound. From 1492 the Spanish and Portuguese crowns crossed the Atlantic and laid an empire over the lands of the Aztec, the Maya, the Inca, and a thousand peoples more, and across three centuries the conquest, the Church, the encomienda, and the slave trade ground indigenous, European, and African into one Catholic, creole, mestizo world. Then the world broke: Haiti rose first in 1804, the only republic born of a successful slave revolt; and from 1810 to 1825 the creole armies of Hidalgo, Bolivar, and San Martin shattered the Spanish empire into a dozen republics, while Brazil walked to independence in 1822 by an imperial path of its own. Bolivar dreamed of one great American union and could not hold even Gran Colombia together; the liberators left behind borders they could not dissolve. The century that followed was the century of the caudillo, the oligarchy, the Church, and a vast inequality, and the long, unfinished struggle for democracy and dignity. Yet the region found one voice in its art: the magical realism of the Boom, the nueva cancion, a literature and a music that spoke for a continent. What binds Latin America is real, Latin tongues, Catholicism, a mixed people, and the long shadow of the United States to the north; what divides it is geography, race and class, and every border the liberators could not erase. A group that shows how far a shared origin can carry twenty nations, and where the dream of unity meets the weight of the past.

## Triggers

**The Latin tongues and the Catholic Church**

Spanish across most of the mainland, Portuguese over the great mass of Brazil, French and Creole in Haiti, all descended from Latin, and over them all the Catholic faith carried by the conquest: a shared tongue-family and a shared Church are the deepest ground of the grouping, a kinship older than any republic.

**Mestizaje and the mixed people**

Out of the conquest came a new people: indigenous, European, and African ground together over centuries into the mestizo, creole, mulatto, and a hundred named mixtures, so that Latin America is bound less by one blood than by the very fact of mixture, the synthesis that made a continent.

**Bolivar's dream of unity**

[Simon Bolivar](../../cultures/venezuela/play_venezuela.md) dreamed of one great union of the Spanish American republics and could not hold even Gran Colombia together; the longing for a single Latin America, voiced from [Venezuela](../../cultures/venezuela/play_venezuela.md) and [Colombia](../../cultures/colombia/play_colombia.md) to the southern cone, outlived the man and remains the region's great unfinished idea.

**The northern shadow**

Over the whole grouping falls the long shadow of the United States to the north: a century and more of trade, influence, and intervention that the southern republics have answered with resistance, accommodation, and a shared sense of a continent apart, a common other against which Latin America has often defined itself.

## Stakes

Whether twenty nations that share a tongue-family, a faith, a mixed people, and a dream of unity Bolivar could never realize can act as one Latin America, or remain a constellation of separate republics bound by sentiment and divided by every border and inequality the colonial past bequeathed.
