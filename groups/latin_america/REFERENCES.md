# Latin America: References

The provenance behind this group. A group carries no Hofstede data of its own (it
has no single national profile); it is a referencing entry over its member
cultures.

## The grouping

Latin America is the family of nations of the Americas whose languages descend from
Latin, bound by the Iberian and French conquest, the Catholic faith, a mixed
indigenous, European, and African people, and the long shadow of the United States.
The membership recorded in the registry is **build-derived** from the member plays
this group casts, not authored by hand. Its twenty members are the Spanish-,
Portuguese-, and French- or Creole-speaking republics, and it deliberately excludes
the English- and Dutch-speaking Belize, Guyana, Suriname, and the Anglophone
Caribbean. It is not a single state.

## Latin tongues and the Catholic faith

The grouping is bound by a family of languages descended from Latin, Spanish across
most of the mainland, Portuguese over Brazil, and French and Creole in Haiti, and
by the Catholic Church carried by the conquest. The tongue-family and the faith are
matters of public record and the deepest ground of the grouping.
