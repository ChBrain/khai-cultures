# Latin America: a khai group

Latin America staged as a khai **group** - a play in the shared `groups/`
collection that **references its member cultures** rather than being a country of
its own. The family of twenty nations of the Iberian-and-French Americas, born of
the conquest and bound by Latin tongues, the Catholic faith, a mestizo people, and
the long shadow of the United States to the north: Mexico and Central America, the
Spanish and French Caribbean, Brazil, and the republics of the Andes and the
southern cone.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this production and answers for it.

## A group, not a culture

Like DACH, the Nordics, Benelux, the Visegrad Four, the Baltics, Iberia, NATO, the
EU, and the Anglosphere, Latin America is a **group**, not a country. It has **no
`geo.json`** and **no Hofstede pitch**: it is a _referencing_ entry. Its `Company`
casts the member plays, and the registry build derives the group's `references`
from exactly those casts, so the membership can never drift from what the play
actually links.

- **Members:** the twenty Spanish-, Portuguese-, and French- or Creole-speaking
  nations - Mexico, Guatemala, Honduras, El Salvador, Nicaragua, Costa Rica,
  Panama, Cuba, the Dominican Republic, Haiti, Brazil, Argentina, Colombia, Chile,
  Peru, Venezuela, Ecuador, Bolivia, Paraguay, and Uruguay. This deliberately
  excludes the English- and Dutch-speaking Belize, Guyana, Suriname, and the
  Anglophone Caribbean.
- **Kind:** `group`.
- **The growth story:** like NATO and the EU, Latin America's plots trace its
  **evolution**, not a single event:
  - [the Conquest and the Colony](plot_01_the_conquest_and_the_colony.md) - the Iberian conquest from 1492 and the Catholic, mestizo, African colonial order of the viceroyalties and Brazil.
  - [the Wars of Independence](plot_02_the_wars_of_independence.md) - Hidalgo, Bolivar, and San Martin and the creole republics of 1810 to 1825, with Haiti the forerunner of 1804 and Brazil's imperial independence of 1822.
  - [Caudillos and the Long Century](plot_03_caudillos_and_the_long_century.md) - the long nineteenth and twentieth centuries of oligarchies, caudillos, revolutions, dictatorships, and the return of democracy.
  - [the Continental Voice](plot_04_the_continental_voice.md) - magical realism and the Boom, music and faith, the diaspora, and the shared, unfinished modern Latin America.

## Provenance

Latin America is the family of nations of the Americas whose tongues descend from
Latin, born of the Spanish, Portuguese, and French conquest and the long synthesis
of indigenous, European, and African peoples into a Catholic, creole, mestizo
world. Its members and their histories are matters of public record; the conquest,
slavery, the dictatorships, and the interventions that mark the region are staged
honestly and soberly, without atrocity detail and without taking a side, and each
member culture answers for its own past in full. The staging and the architecture
are original work.
