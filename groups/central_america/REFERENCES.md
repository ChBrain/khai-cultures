# Central America: References

The provenance behind this group. A group carries no Hofstede data of its own (it
has no single national profile); it is a referencing entry over its member
cultures.

## The grouping

Central America is the isthmus between the continents, the heir of the southern
Maya world and the Spanish Captaincy General of Guatemala, of Morazan's broken
Federal Republic of Central America, and of a century of intervention and war, now
bound by the Central American Integration System (SICA). The membership recorded in
the registry is **build-derived** from the member plays this group casts, not
authored by hand. Its eight members are Guatemala, Belize, Honduras, El Salvador,
Nicaragua, Costa Rica, Panama, and the Dominican Republic. It is not a single
state, and the Dominican Republic joins from across the Caribbean as a full SICA
member rather than as a state of the isthmus itself.

## SICA

The Central American Integration System (SICA) is the regional body that binds the
isthmus together through shared institutions, founded out of the late-twentieth-
century peace. Its full members are the seven isthmus states cast here together
with the Dominican Republic, which is why this group reaches across the Caribbean
to that island nation. SICA's membership is a matter of public record.
