# Plot: Bananas, Intervention and the Wars

## Taxonomy

[play_central_america](play_central_america.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

The small republics, sovereign but weak, enter a century in which their economies, their politics, and their borders are shaped from outside. Fruit and coffee draw foreign companies and foreign powers onto the isthmus, and the long century closes in conflict.

## Action

Across the late nineteenth and twentieth centuries the isthmus becomes bound to the world market and to outside power. Foreign fruit companies plant vast banana plantations on the hot coastal plains of [Honduras](../../cultures/honduras/play_honduras.md) and beyond, and their weight on small economies and small governments gives the era its name, the banana republics. Foreign intervention follows the foreign capital, and outside powers act repeatedly in the politics of the isthmus over the decades. In the later twentieth century several of the republics, among them [Guatemala](../../cultures/guatemala/play_guatemala.md), [Nicaragua](../../cultures/nicaragua/play_nicaragua.md), and [El Salvador](../../cultures/el_salvador/play_el_salvador.md), pass through long and costly civil wars and armed conflicts, a generation of upheaval recorded here as a matter of public history. These are the years that scar the isthmus and that the later peace and integration must answer for.

## Stage

- [Honduras](../../cultures/honduras/play_honduras.md): the archetypal banana republic, economy and politics shaped by the fruit trade.
- [Guatemala](../../cultures/guatemala/play_guatemala.md): site of a long internal conflict in the later twentieth century.
- [Nicaragua](../../cultures/nicaragua/play_nicaragua.md): scene of revolution and a long armed conflict drawing in outside powers.
- [El Salvador](../../cultures/el_salvador/play_el_salvador.md): the small, crowded land that endured a protracted civil war.

## Tension

This is the hardest century of the grouping's story: an era of economic dependence, foreign intervention, and civil wars that cost the isthmus dearly. It is staged soberly and on the public record, without atrocity detail and without taking any side, because the point here is the shared scar, not the verdict. The wars are the common wound that makes the later reach for peace and union urgent, and the reason a new system of integration carries so much weight.
