# Play: Central America

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the house that holds this production and answers for it.

## Name

Central America: the slender land bridge between the continents, the isthmus of volcanoes and rainforest that joins North and South America. Its core is the seven small states of the isthmus, Guatemala, Belize, Honduras, El Salvador, Nicaragua, Costa Rica, and Panama, joined across the water by the Dominican Republic as a full member of the Central American Integration System (SICA).

## Arc

A grouping bound by a narrow land and a long, unfinished dream of union. Central America is the isthmus between the continents, the slender bridge of volcanoes and rainforest that joins North and South America and carries the great roads of trade and migration upon it. It was the heart of the southern Maya world, a land of city-states and stone, before Spain conquered it and ruled it as the Captaincy General of Guatemala, a single colonial province from Chiapas to Costa Rica. When the isthmus won its independence in 1821 it tried to keep that unity: for a brief generation it held together as the Federal Republic of Central America under Francisco Morazan's dream of a single isthmian nation, a dream that fractured into small, quarrelsome republics by 1841. The nineteenth and twentieth centuries brought the banana republics, foreign companies and foreign intervention, and the long civil wars of the late twentieth century, before the peace and a new attempt at union: the Central American Integration System (SICA), which binds the seven isthmus states and, as a full member, the Dominican Republic across the Caribbean. SICA includes the Dominican Republic as a full member, which is why that island nation joins this group though it sits in the Caribbean and not on the isthmus itself. Bound by the shared Maya-and-colonial past, by coffee and bananas and volcanoes, by Morazan's unrealized dream, and by the great northward migration; divided by old rivalries, by the canal that made Panama a world apart, and by the borders the federation could not erase.

## Triggers

**The Maya isthmus**

The land itself is the first bond: the southern reach of the Maya world, a bridge of volcanoes and rainforest between the continents, where the same stone cities, the same maize, and the same shared deep past underlie [Guatemala](../../cultures/guatemala/play_guatemala.md), [Honduras](../../cultures/honduras/play_honduras.md), [El Salvador](../../cultures/el_salvador/play_el_salvador.md), and [Belize](../../cultures/belize/play_belize.md) before any border was drawn.

**Morazan's federal dream**

The deepest tie is the unrealized dream of a single isthmian nation: the Federal Republic of Central America under Francisco Morazan, born of one independence in 1821, that held the isthmus together for a generation before fracturing into the small republics of [Guatemala](../../cultures/guatemala/play_guatemala.md), [Honduras](../../cultures/honduras/play_honduras.md), [Nicaragua](../../cultures/nicaragua/play_nicaragua.md), [El Salvador](../../cultures/el_salvador/play_el_salvador.md), and [Costa Rica](../../cultures/costa_rica/play_costa_rica.md), a memory of unity that every later attempt at union answers to.

**The land bridge of volcanoes and coffee**

The isthmus is bound by what its land yields and what crosses it: coffee on the highland slopes, bananas on the hot coastal plains, a chain of volcanoes down its spine, and the great roads of trade and migration that run its length toward the north, a shared geography that ties the small nations to one another and to the world.

**The system of integration**

After the wars came the peace and a new attempt at union: the Central American Integration System (SICA), which binds the seven isthmus states and, as a full member, [the Dominican Republic](../../cultures/dominican_republic/play_dominican_republic.md) across the water, reaching for in institutions what Morazan's federation could not hold by arms.

## Stakes

Whether the small nations of the isthmus, heirs to Morazan's broken federation and scarred by a century of intervention and war, can finally be one Central America through SICA, even reaching across the sea to [the Dominican Republic](../../cultures/dominican_republic/play_dominican_republic.md), or whether old rivalries, the world-apart canal, and the pull of the north keep the isthmus a row of separate republics.
