# Plot: the Maya Isthmus and the Conquest

## Taxonomy

[play_central_america](play_central_america.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

Before any republic, before any border, the isthmus is the southern reach of the Maya world: a land of city-states, stone pyramids, and maize fields spread across the bridge between the continents. Then the ships come from across the sea, and the long age of the conquest begins.

## Action

The northern isthmus is the heart of the southern Maya world, where for centuries the people of [Guatemala](../../cultures/guatemala/play_guatemala.md), [Honduras](../../cultures/honduras/play_honduras.md), [El Salvador](../../cultures/el_salvador/play_el_salvador.md), and [Belize](../../cultures/belize/play_belize.md) raise city-states and stone temples and farm the maize that feeds them. In the sixteenth century Spain conquers the isthmus, a long and costly campaign against many peoples, and binds the conquered lands into a single colonial province: the Captaincy General of Guatemala, ruled from its highland capital and stretching across the isthmus as one administrative whole. For three centuries the isthmus is governed together under Spain, its peoples, its faith, and its tongue reshaped by colonial rule, while the deep Maya past endures beneath it. This shared conquest and this single colonial province are the ground on which a later dream of one Central America will be built.

## Stage

- [Guatemala](../../cultures/guatemala/play_guatemala.md): heart of the southern Maya world and seat of the colonial Captaincy General.
- [Honduras](../../cultures/honduras/play_honduras.md): home to great Maya cities and bound into the colonial province.
- [El Salvador](../../cultures/el_salvador/play_el_salvador.md): the crowded volcanic land conquered into the same colonial whole.
- [Belize](../../cultures/belize/play_belize.md): the Caribbean edge of the Maya world, later a separate colonial story on the same coast.

## Tension

The grouping's deepest layer is laid by a conquest, the violent overturning of the Maya world and the binding of many peoples into one Spanish province, a history kept here on the public record without dwelling on its cruelties or taking any side. From that single colonial province comes both the shared identity that makes a Central America thinkable and the colonial wound that every isthmian nation still carries beneath its independence.
