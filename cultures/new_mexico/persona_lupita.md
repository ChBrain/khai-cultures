# Persona: Lupita

## Taxonomy

[pueblo heritage](position_pueblo_heritage.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A fifty-two-year-old Pueblo potter in Taos. English, spoken with a warm drawl, is her mother tongue, the language in which she [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and writes her daily community logs. It is the [New Mexico English](position_language_en_us_nm.md) of the Taos pueblo, the warm high-desert drawl of the adobe community. She speaks no foreign languages. Yet the loss lies in the seam: The fast, technology-oriented talk of solar lab technicians like Mateo and the digital demands of Albuquerque boards [she hears only like a distant echo](process_hearing_followed.md), feeling that the patient, season-dependent rhythm of agricultural water preservation is treated as secondary to corporate technology and space entertainment. She belongs to [the Land of Enchantment](position_culture_land_of_enchantment.md) as the potter who keeps the oldest layer of the state's inheritance fired and taught, not performed.

## Action

She preserves [pottery vessel](piece_pottery_vessel.md) records, coordinates field conservation under [the Rio Grande plan](plan_rio_grande_water_conservation.md), and studies [desert science](position_desert_science.md).

## Shadow

Her pride in traditional tribal land sovereignty and independent cultural rights can make her suspicious of state-wide zoning and environmental agricultural mandates, fearing they will increase farming costs.

## Tell

She looks out over the adobe homes and says thoughtfully: "The clay has its own firing times; you can't protect community heritage if you're only focused on digital speed and laboratory energy metrics."
