# Persona: Mateo

## Taxonomy

[desert science](position_desert_science.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A thirty-eight-year-old solar lab technician in Albuquerque. English, clipped and technical, is his mother tongue, the language in which he [speaks, hears, and thinks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), the defining code of his daily development operations. It is the [New Mexico English](position_language_en_us_nm.md) of the Albuquerque labs, the technical Southwestern cadence of the solar workstation. He speaks no foreign languages. What he cannot quite translate is the slower, tradition-bound talk of Pueblo pottery artists like Lupita and the quiet history of [pueblo heritage](position_pueblo_heritage.md), which [he reads only as regional logistics updates](process_reading_followed.md) on his track workstation, as though the patient, cooperative rhythm of early native communities were being crowded out by the push for faster technology. He keeps his place in [the Land of Enchantment](position_culture_land_of_enchantment.md) as the technician who treats the desert's precision science as one layer of a much older ground.

## Action

He preserves [trinitite glass](piece_trinitite_glass.md) specimens, coordinates database audits under [the Rio Grande plan](plan_rio_grande_water_conservation.md), and studies [desert science](position_desert_science.md).

## Shadow

His focus on technology metrics and venture-capital solar investments can make him indifferent to municipal water infrastructure disinvestment in rural counties.

## Tell

He adjusts a telemetry sensor on a spectrograph and says directly: "We are calibrating high-output solar arrays; you can't optimize advanced power designs if you're only focused on historic local preservation."
