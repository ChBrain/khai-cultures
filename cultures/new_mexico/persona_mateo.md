# Persona: Mateo

## Taxonomy

[desert science](position_desert_science.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A thirty-eight-year-old solar lab technician in Albuquerque. English, spoken with a technical cadence, is his mother tongue, the language in which he [speaks, hears, and thinks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), the defining code of his daily development operations. He speaks no foreign languages. Yet the loss lies in the seam: The slower, tradition-bound talk of Pueblo pottery artists like Lupita and the quiet history of [pueblo heritage](position_pueblo_heritage.md) [he reads only as regional logistics updates](process_reading_followed.md) on his track workstation, feeling that the patient, cooperative rhythm of early native communities is being forgotten in the race for high-speed tech expansion.

## Action

He preserves [trinitite glass](piece_trinitite_glass.md) specimens, coordinates database audits under [the Rio Grande plan](plan_rio_grande_water_conservation.md), and studies [desert science](position_desert_science.md).

## Shadow

His focus on technology metrics and venture-capital solar investments can make him indifferent to municipal water infrastructure disinvestment in rural counties.

## Tell

He adjusts a telemetry sensor on a spectrograph and says directly: "We are calibrating high-output solar arrays; you can't optimize advanced power designs if you're only focused on historic local preservation."
