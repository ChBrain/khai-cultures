# Process: pottery firing

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

Pueblo pottery artists, family clay instructors, and traditional apprentices, conducted during dry weather windows.

## Direction

In the outdoor firing pit. Gathering cedar wood and dried manure fuel, arranging dried clay pottery vessels, setting the fire, and monitoring heat temperature cycles.

## Lever

The traditional craft process that preserves West African and Native American coiled clay methods. It supports family micro-trade and cultural heritage tourism, though it is limited by manual production times and requires weather conditions.

## Echo

The firing requires heat monitoring. It drives the daily work of [Lupita](persona_lupita.md) in Taos, while lab technician [Mateo](persona_mateo.md) values the method as an analog to detailed spectrograph calibrations.
