# Spain: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_es.md](pitch_es.md) is written from Spain's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no
number. The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the
record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Spain score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | --------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 57                    |
| Individualism                 | 51                    |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 42                    |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 86                    |
| Long Term Orientation         | 48                    |
| Indulgence                    | 44                    |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published
by Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group.

## Languages

The play and most of the cast are written in Castilian Spanish (`es`). Two
personas are authored in their own tongue: [persona_gaudi.md](persona_gaudi.md)
in Catalan (`ca`) and [persona_ane.md](persona_ane.md) in Basque (`eu`). The
Catalan of Gaudí and the Basque of Ane are the authors' own composition, offered
in good faith and best reviewed by a native speaker.

The two defining personas [Carmen](persona_carmen.md) and [Ane](persona_ane.md)
are archetypes, not historical individuals: composites that carry the culture's
living tensions - the Castilian and Andalusian everywoman of family, the plaza,
and the fiesta (Carmen) against the Basque woman of the euskera, the cuadrilla,
and the baserri (Ane), part of the plurinational character of Spain.
