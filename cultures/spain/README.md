# Spain: a khai culture

Spain staged as a full khai play, ISO ES. A plurinational land of warmth and
family, one and many at once, held together across Castilian, Catalan, and
Basque. Its history is the plot line, its people the cast, the roles its society
encodes, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a
single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## Three tongues

The play and most of the cast are in Castilian Spanish, reflecting Spain's
plurinational character; two personas are written in their own tongue, each with
its own declared `language:`:

- **Castilian Spanish** (`es`) - Miguel de Cervantes, Carmen
- **Catalan** (`ca`) - Antoni Gaudí
- **Basque** (`eu`) - Ane

The play and the shared instances are in Castilian (the nation's common tongue);
Gaudí is authored in Catalan and Ane in Basque, each routed to its own language
detector.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_spain.md](play_spain.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_es.md](pitch_es.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Spain's
  profile (warm, family-first, present-living, plurinational) and naming no
  dimension.
- **Plots (history):** 1492 (the fall of Granada and the start of empire), the
  Siglo de Oro (Cervantes and the Golden Age), the Civil War (1936-1939), and the
  Transition to democracy - each casting the elements it needs.
- **Personas:** Miguel de Cervantes and Antoni Gaudí as the historic cast, plus
  two defining archetypes, Carmen (the Castilian and Andalusian everywoman of
  family and fiesta) and Ane (the Basque woman of the euskera and the cuadrilla).
  Each is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** las autonomías (the plurinational state, centre and periphery),
  la familia (family and close personal bonds, la confianza), el catolicismo (the
  deep Catholic cultural inheritance).
- **Places:** Madrid (the central capital, the seat of the state), Granada (the
  Alhambra, Al-Andalus, the fall of 1492).
- **Processes:** la sobremesa, la fiesta.
- **Pieces:** la guitarra (the Spanish guitar and flamenco), el jamón (Iberian
  cured ham, the heart of the table).
