# China: a khai culture

China staged as a full khai play, ISO CN, language zh (Mandarin, written in
simplified characters). A civilization that has endured for millennia without a
true break, set as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the
cast, the roles its writing and thought encode, its places, its practices, and
its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_china.md](play_china.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_zh.md](pitch_zh.md), the Hofstede layer, written from China's
  profile (a strong collective and family orientation, a marked respect for
  hierarchy, a long-term and persevering outlook, and a restrained, composed
  emotional register) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the founding of the civilization (the Yellow and Yangtze
  rivers, the oracle-bone characters, the early dynasties and the Mandate of
  Heaven), the Great Unification (the unifying of the script and the empire, and
  Confucian thought made the spine of the state), the flourishing and the brush
  (the golden age of poetry and calligraphy, and the dynastic cycle), and the
  modern age and the renewal (the end of empire, reform and opening, and the
  sober return to global stature). Each casts the elements it needs, and the
  dynastic cycle and the modern era are staged soberly and take no side.
- **Personas:** Confucius, Du Fu, Wang Xizhi, and Lu Xun as the historic cast,
  plus one archetype, the common people (laobaixing), the enduring base of the
  civilization. Each is wired to the language engine by the relationship it holds
  to its mother tongue.
- **Positions:** the Mandate of Heaven (the dynastic cycle and the rule that
  power rests on the people's welfare), filial piety (the root of the human bonds
  that join family to state), and the three teachings (the Confucian, Daoist, and
  Buddhist streams that together temper the Chinese disposition).
- **Places:** the Great Wall (the civilization's emblem of endurance) and the
  Forbidden City (imperial order rendered in stone).
- **Processes:** calligraphy (writing raised into an art of cultivating the self)
  and the Spring Festival (the yearly reunion that gathers the scattered family).
- **Pieces:** Chinese characters (the one writing that binds many spoken tongues
  into one civilization) and porcelain (the craft that carried the land's name to
  the world).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the dynastic cycle, periods of division and conflict, and the modern era
are treated soberly and from within China's own self-understanding, taking no
side; the staging and the architecture are original work.
