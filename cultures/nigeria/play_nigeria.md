# Play: Nigeria

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the house that holds this production and answers for it.

## Name

Nigeria, the big country at the bend of West Africa, where the River Niger and the River Benue meet and run down to the Atlantic in the great delta of the south. ISO NG, language en, but English is only the lingua franca that lets the house talk to itself; under it run Yoruba, Igbo, Hausa, and over two hundred and fifty more tongues, with Naija Pidgin as the street language that everybody, abi, understands. The most populous nation on the whole continent, the giant of Africa, one country sewn from many nations, loud, deep, and very much alive.

## Arc

This is a land that did not begin in 1914 when the British joined the north and the south and called it one Nigeria. Long before the white man came, the great peoples here had built their own greatness: the Yoruba with Ile-Ife where, they say, the world itself was created, and Oyo whose Alaafin ruled an empire; the Edo of Benin who cast the famous bronzes that now sit in faraway museums, taken and never freely given; the Igbo of the east who ran their towns by the gathering of the people, the elders and the age-grades, a republican spirit where, as they say, Igbo enwe eze, the Igbo have no king; the Hausa city-states and, after the jihad of Usman dan Fodio, the Sokoto Caliphate and the great walled emirate of Kano on the trans-Saharan trade; and the Kanuri of Borno, the Ijaw of the creeks, the Tiv, the Nupe, the Efik, and the rest of the more than two hundred and fifty peoples. The British amalgamated them, ruled, and left, and at independence in 1960 the new country had to learn to be one. It went through a sober and sorrowful chapter, a civil war that the nation will never forget and grieves with dignity, and came out the other side determined that what remains must remain together. Since then the giant has roared in a way nobody can ignore: Nollywood, the second-biggest film machine on earth; Afrobeats carrying the sound of Lagos to every corner of the globe, on the road that Fela's Afrobeat first cut; the literature of Achebe and Soyinka and Adichie read in classrooms across the world; jollof rice that we will fight you over; football that fills the streets; the markets that never sleep; the owambe parties in their matching aso-ebi; faith, both the church and the mosque, lived loud and deep. Under it all runs one nerve: a people of enormous energy and bigger swagger, warm and generous to a fault, who hustle hard, laugh harder, suffer and still smile, and who hold, against every doubting, that naija no dey carry last.

## Triggers

**[Plot 1: the kingdoms before](plot_01_the_kingdoms.md)**

Long before any Nigeria, the great peoples build their own glory: [Ile-Ife](place_ile_ife.md), the cradle where the Yoruba say the world began, the Benin that casts [the Benin bronzes](piece_the_benin_bronzes.md), the Igbo towns that govern by the gathering of the people, and the northern emirates of the Sokoto Caliphate, all of them rich in [the peoples](position_the_peoples.md) that one Nigeria will later have to hold.

**[Plot 2: the amalgamation (1914)](plot_02_amalgamation.md)**

The British join the northern and southern protectorates into one Nigeria and rule it from [Lagos](place_lagos.md), drawing a single border around many nations who never asked to be one, and seeding [the peoples](position_the_peoples.md) with both a shared country and a long argument over how to share it.

**[Plot 3: independence and the war](plot_03_independence_and_war.md)**

In 1960 Nigeria takes its freedom and must learn to be one nation of many, a job that [Chinua Achebe](persona_achebe.md) chronicles; the young country passes through a sober chapter of civil war that the nation grieves with dignity, and resolves that the union, once kept, must hold.

**[Plot 4: the giant roars](plot_04_the_giant_roars.md)**

From [Lagos](place_lagos.md) the giant of Africa makes itself heard the world over: Nollywood and Afrobeats on the road [Fela Anikulapo Kuti](persona_fela.md) cut, the literature of [Wole Soyinka](persona_soyinka.md) and [Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie](persona_adichie.md), the hustle and the owambe and the football, the whole country proving that naija no dey carry last.

## Stakes

Whether a country sewn from over two hundred and fifty peoples, one of the youngest and fastest-growing nations on earth, can hold its many together as one without losing the warmth, the faith, and the swagger that make it itself; whether [the peoples](position_the_peoples.md) can stay one Nigeria while each remains fully what it is; whether the giant can turn its enormous energy and its hustle into the greatness its own people already feel in their bones; and whether, having grieved its sober chapter with dignity, it can carry the union forward so that, as the country swears of itself, naija truly no dey ever carry last.
