# Plot: the giant roars

## Taxonomy

[play_nigeria](play_nigeria.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

Having come through its sober chapter and held itself together, the giant of Africa, the most populous nation on the continent and one of the youngest and fastest-growing on earth, turns its enormous energy outward. The world, which once came only to take, now comes to listen, to watch, and to dance.

## Action

From [Lagos](place_lagos.md), the country's roaring engine, Nigeria makes itself heard the whole world over. Nollywood grows into the second-largest film industry on earth by sheer volume, telling Nigerian stories to all of Africa and beyond. Afrobeats, grown out of the Afrobeat road that [Fela Anikulapo Kuti](persona_fela.md) cut, fills the charts and the clubs of every continent. The literature of [Wole Soyinka](persona_soyinka.md), crowned with the first African Nobel, and of [Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie](persona_adichie.md), read everywhere, carries the country's voice into the world's classrooms. The Super Eagles bring the whole nation onto the street in green. The markets and the tech hustle never sleep, [Mama Market](persona_mama_market.md) and her millions turning over the real economy kobo by kobo. And the jollof is defended like a national border. The giant roars, and proves to itself and to everyone that naija no dey carry last.

## Stage

- [Fela Anikulapo Kuti](persona_fela.md): the root of the sound, whose Afrobeat cut the road the whole world now dances down.
- [Wole Soyinka](persona_soyinka.md): the Nobel crown of the country's letters, the conscience that would not be silenced.
- [Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie](persona_adichie.md): the new voice carrying Nigerian stories to readers everywhere.
- [Mama Market](persona_mama_market.md): the hustle multiplied a million times, the engine room under the headlines.
- [Lagos](place_lagos.md): the roaring city from which the giant's culture, money, and noise pour out to the world.

## Tension

The giant roars loud and real, and the world cannot ignore it, yet the swagger lives beside the daily grind, and the country aches at the gap between the greatness it knows it carries and the ordinary table it has not yet fully reached. The triumph is true and earned, and the work is not finished, the two held together in the same proud, restless, unbeatable spirit that swears, against every doubting, naija no dey carry last.
