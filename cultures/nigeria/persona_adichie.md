# Persona: Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie

## Taxonomy

[the extended family](position_the_extended_family.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The brilliant daughter of Nsukka, the Igbo storyteller who carried the Nigerian novel into a new century and onto the lips and shelves of the whole world, writing the inner lives of women inside the great web of family, tradition, and faith with a clear eye and a warm heart. Sharp, elegant, and fearless, she gave the world the warning of the single story, that to know a people by only one telling is to cheat them, and reminded it that her Nigeria is many stories at once. Her mother tongue is Igbo, which she [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [hears](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) at her people's table, threading it through her English so the reader hears Nsukka in the prose; [Nigerian English](position_language_en_ng.md) she [writes drafted and exact](process_writing_drafted.md) and reads the world's books to [follow them and answer back](process_reading_followed.md). She belongs to the generation raised in English yet rooted in Igbo, and she names the gap herself, for a child schooled in the colonizer's tongue can lose the grandmother's, and the deepest endearments and the oldest proverbs sit, in the end, only in Igbo, the gap at the limit of the tongue waiting just where the new language cannot hold what the old one carried whole. She belongs to [Naija](position_culture_naija.md) as the daughter who honours the extended family's warmth while asking, plainly, what it costs the women held inside it.

## Action

In [the giant roars](plot_04_the_giant_roars.md) she is the new face of the country's letters before the world, the writer whose novels and whose voice carried Nigeria to readers and stages everywhere and made the next generation proud to tell their own stories. She carries [the extended family](position_the_extended_family.md) by setting her people inside the wide Nigerian web of kin, the aunties and cousins and in-laws and elders, honouring its warmth and shelter while asking, plainly, what it costs the women inside it.

## Shadow

The writer who both loves the tradition and questions it walks a narrow road, and the shadow is the heat that comes from saying the unsaid, the cost of the daughter who will not simply keep quiet and please the elders. To honour the family and still hold it to account is to risk being called disloyal by the very house one is trying to mend, and that strain is the country's own argument made personal.

## Tell

She tells the world that there is never only one story of any people, then proves it by writing a Nigeria of so many stories, the war and the love and the hair salon and the kitchen all at once, that no single telling could ever stand for it again.
