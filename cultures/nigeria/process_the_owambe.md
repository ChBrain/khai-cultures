# Process: the owambe

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

A family throwing the big party, the wedding, the burial, the chieftaincy, the milestone birthday, that the whole community will talk about for weeks. It takes a celebrant and a reason, the aso-ebi cloth chosen so the family and friends all wear the same colour and pattern and show they are one body, the canopies and the live band or the DJ, the small chops and the big pots of jollof, the spraying of money on the dancers, and a crowd that swells far beyond whoever was actually invited, because in Nigeria nobody comes to dance alone.

## Direction

Toward joy shared loud and wide: the owambe is the country celebrating itself, the family showing the community its togetherness and its blessing, the place where status and gratitude and sheer enjoyment are poured out without holding back. It is generosity made visible, the host feeding everyone who comes, and belonging made wearable, the sea of matching aso-ebi declaring across a whole hall that these people are one. To throw a good owambe is to honour your family before the eyes of all.

## Lever

It is driven by the deep Nigerian love of celebration and by the truth that joy, like sorrow, belongs to the whole community and not to one house alone. It holds against the grind of the daily hustle, giving the people their bursting release, and against distance, gathering the scattered kin back home; against it stand the cost, which can stretch a family thin, and the pressure to outdo the last party, the owambe that must always be bigger than the one before.

## Echo

The owambe makes visible what [the extended family](position_the_extended_family.md) lives for, the sharing of every joy with the whole web of kin and community, nobody dancing alone. To a celebration like this [Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie](persona_adichie.md) might bring her clear eye, loving the warmth of the gathering even as she weighs, gently, all that it costs the family that throws it.
