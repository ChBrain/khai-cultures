# Position: Nigerian English

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The authority of the shared official tongue of Nigeria, the English inherited from the colonizer and kept as the one language of the schools, the offices, the law and the printed page. It is nobody's mother tongue among the many peoples and that is its whole office: the neutral ground on which Igbo and Yoruba and Hausa and the rest can meet, the language chosen precisely because it belonged to none of them by birth and so could carry the business of them all. Beside it runs Naija Pidgin, the broken English that belongs to no single people and so to all at once, the everyday shared voice the whole giant speaks.

## Orders

What counts as the public standard of the land, the drafted English of the statute and the ledger, the school and the bank, the novel set before the world. It orders which words go on the record and cross to the wider reading, the high and deliberate page a writer bends until it can hold the weight of another tongue, and it sets the register in which a nation of many mother tongues does its shared and official work with one public voice.

## Loses

Ground to the mother tongues that carry the marrow, for the proverb that is the palm-oil with which words are eaten, the deepest endearment and the oldest cosmology live in Igbo or Yoruba or Hausa and never translate whole, the masquerade and the drumbeat staying in the end in the tongue they were born to. It loses too to Naija Pidgin, the people's shared speech the offices look down on, the tongue a trader greets in and a musician tells the truth in, so that the standard English holds the receipts while the real life of the street runs in the broken one.

## Drives

It is bent and seasoned to carry Igbo thought by [Chinua Achebe](persona_achebe.md), who made it hold the rhythm of his people; threaded with Nsukka and written drafted and exact by [Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie](persona_adichie.md); written in a high deliberate hand that sets the Yoruba gods on the world stage by [Wole Soyinka](persona_soyinka.md); followed on the page and then mostly thrown away for the people's Pidgin by [Fela Anikulapo Kuti](persona_fela.md); and caught only haltingly off the forms and the banks by [Mama Market](persona_mama_market.md), who built a whole life on a tongue the offices scorned. So the shared official tongue shows who took it up to be read and who keeps the heart in a language of their own.
