# Persona: Mama Market

## Taxonomy

[the extended family](position_the_extended_family.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The market woman, the engine room of the Nigerian economy and the backbone of the Nigerian home, the trader in her stall at Balogun or Onitsha or Kano who is up before the cock crows and home after dark, calling you my customer and my dear, ready to bargain till you both laugh. Tough as old leather and warm as fresh bread, she feeds not only her own children but a whole compound of relatives, pays the school fees, settles the in-laws, and bankrolls the church harvest or the mosque, all from what she turns over kobo by kobo on her table. She is the Lagos hustle in a wrapper and headtie, the proof that naija no dey carry last because she will not let it. Her mother tongue she [speaks and thinks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) in fully, whether Yoruba, Igbo, or Hausa, and she [hears](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) it in the songs and the prayers of her home. Pidgin she [carries](process_speaking_carried.md) as the tongue of trade, switching to it in a breath to greet a customer from another people; the proper English of forms and banks she [follows only haltingly, catching what she can](process_reading_caught.md), having had little schooling and given all she could to her children's. She built a whole life and lifted a whole family on a tongue the offices look down on, and there is the gap at the limit of the tongue, for the wisdom and the figures she carries flawlessly in her own language go unwritten in the language of the receipts and the ledgers.

## Action

In [the giant roars](plot_04_the_giant_roars.md) she is the hustle that makes the giant move, the small trader multiplied a million times whose daily turning of goods is the real engine under the headlines, the entrepreneurial spirit that refuses to sit and wait. She carries [the extended family](position_the_extended_family.md) on her own two shoulders, the one who feeds the many, holding the lineage together with the money she makes and the food she cooks, so that nobody in her house ever truly goes hungry or alone.

## Shadow

The one who carries everybody can be ground down by the carrying, the many hands held out, the relatives who see her stall as a bank, the burden of the breadwinner that never lets her rest. And the very world that runs on her hustle can overlook her, the offices and the big men who eat from the economy she powers yet treat her, with her little schooling and her market tongue, as if she were nobody, until the day the market closes and the city remembers who feeds it.

## Tell

She will price you down, dash you small extra for being a good customer, send you off with a blessing and a laugh, and still, out of that same little profit, pay one more child through school, carrying her whole family on a market table the bankers never even see.
