# Persona: Fela Anikulapo Kuti

## Taxonomy

[the peoples](position_the_peoples.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The abami eda, the strange one, the Black President, the Yoruba genius from Abeokuta who took highlife and jazz and the deep funk of the Yoruba talking drum and forged a brand-new music, Afrobeat, the long, hot, hypnotic groove out of which the whole Afrobeats of today eventually grew. Saxophone in hand, defiant to the bone, he played all night at his shrine and turned the dance floor into a place to tell the truth. He sang mostly not in the polished English of the schools but in Naija Pidgin, the broken English that belongs to no single people and so belongs to all of them at once, the one tongue the whole giant shares. Yoruba he [speaks and thinks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) as his birthright, and the talking drum that talks in Yoruba tone he [carries into his horns](process_speaking_carried.md); Pidgin he [borrowed and made his own weapon](process_speaking_borrowed.md), and the English of his London music college he [followed on the page](process_reading_followed.md) only to throw most of it away. He chose the people's Pidgin over the master's grammar on purpose, knowing the proper English would have shut out the very people he sang for, and there is the gap at the limit of the tongue, for the truth that the trumpet and the talking drum carry runs deeper than any grammar can write down.

## Action

In [the giant roars](plot_04_the_giant_roars.md) he is the root from which the giant's sound grew, the man whose Afrobeat cut the road that Afrobeats now travels to every corner of the earth, and the artist who made music a way for ordinary Nigerians to speak. He carries [the peoples](position_the_peoples.md) by singing in the one Pidgin tongue they all understand, gathering Yoruba, Igbo, Hausa, and all the rest onto a single dance floor where, for the length of a song, the many are one.

## Shadow

The truth-teller who will not bend pays a heavy price, and the shadow is the long collision between the free spirit and the powers that want him quiet, the raids, the harassment, the cost borne by him and those around him. And there is the shadow of his own contradictions, the great gift and the hard living tangled together, the brilliance and the recklessness that the country remembers honestly, loving the music without pretending the man was a saint.

## Tell

He names himself Anikulapo, he who carries death in his pouch, and plays a thirty-minute song that says in plain Pidgin what no newspaper dared print, turning a saxophone into the loudest mouth in the land.
