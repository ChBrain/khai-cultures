# Process: the naming ceremony

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

A family welcoming a new baby, gathering the whole kindred on the appointed day, the seventh or eighth after the birth among many peoples, to give the child its name before the eyes of all. It takes a newborn, the grandparents and uncles and aunties and cousins and in-laws, the elder or the cleric to bless and to name, and the symbolic tastes, the honey and the salt and the water and the kola, each carrying a prayer for the kind of life the child will lead.

## Direction

Toward belonging and blessing: the moment a new person is folded into the family, the lineage, and the community, never to stand alone. The name itself carries the prayer, the circumstance, and the hope, often a whole sentence about God or the family's gratitude, so that a Nigerian carries the story of his welcome in his very name. It is the family declaring that this child is ours, that the line continues, and that the wide web of kin will hold the new one from the first week of life.

## Lever

It is driven by the deep truth that in Nigeria a person is born not as one but as a thread in the cloth of the family, and by the joy a new birth brings to a whole kindred. It holds against distance and against the thinning of the old ways, gathering even scattered relatives back for the day; against it stand the cost and the strain of the great gathering, and the pull of a busier world that would shrink the ceremony down.

## Echo

The naming ceremony makes audible what [the extended family](position_the_extended_family.md) holds dearest, that no one in Nigeria is ever just one person, but a child of the whole house from the very first week. Into a gathering like this [Mama Market](persona_mama_market.md) brings the food she cooked and the cloth she bought, welcoming yet another of her kindred into the family she carries.
