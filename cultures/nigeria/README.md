# Nigeria: a khai culture

Nigeria staged as a full khai play, ISO NG, language en. The giant of Africa,
the most populous nation on the continent, one country sewn from over two hundred
and fifty peoples, as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the
cast, the roles its languages and faith encode, its places, its practices, and
its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch. English is only the lingua
franca that lets the house talk to itself; under it run Yoruba, Igbo, Hausa, and
many more, with Naija Pidgin as the street tongue everybody, abi, understands.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the house that holds
this production and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its Estate; a
culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_nigeria.md](play_nigeria.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Nigeria's profile (a warm collectivism where a person is a whole lineage, a
  respect for the elder and the big man, a deep and everyday faith, and the
  hustle and swagger of a giant) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the kingdoms before, the amalgamation of 1914,
  independence and the war, and the giant roars, each casting the elements it
  needs, and the sober chapter of the civil war grieved with dignity and without
  taking a side.
- **Personas:** Chinua Achebe, Wole Soyinka, Fela Anikulapo Kuti, and Chimamanda
  Ngozi Adichie as the historic cast, plus one archetype, Mama Market, the trader
  who feeds a whole compound on what she turns over kobo by kobo. Each is wired to
  the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** the peoples (one country of many nations, fully your own people
  and fully of the one country), the extended family (a person is never one
  person but a whole lineage), faith (God in the matter of the day, the church and
  the mosque side by side).
- **Places:** Lagos (the roaring, sleepless megacity, the engine of the giant),
  Ile-Ife (the ancient Yoruba cradle of creation).
- **Processes:** the naming ceremony (the new child folded into the family from
  the first week), the owambe (joy shared loud and wide in matching aso-ebi).
- **Pieces:** jollof rice (the party dish the whole giant agrees on and defends
  to the last breath), the Benin bronzes (the ancient masterworks carried off and
  not freely given, and the homecoming the country still seeks).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the civil war is grieved soberly on the public record, with no atrocity
detail and no political side, and recent politics and security are out of scope;
the staging and the architecture are original work.
