# Nigeria: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical and cultural
sources behind the plots, personas, places, pieces, and processes.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md) is written from Nigeria's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record without
entering the prose. Nigeria is commonly published as part of a West Africa
grouping in the model; the figures below are approximate, as published.

| Dimension                     | Nigeria score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ----------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 80                      |
| Individualism                 | 30                      |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 60                      |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 55                      |
| Long Term Orientation         | 13                      |
| Indulgence                    | 84                      |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; figures for Nigeria are often given
within a West Africa regional grouping.

The defining persona [Mama Market](persona_mama_market.md) is an archetype, not a
historical individual: a composite of the market women who are the engine room of
the economy and the backbone of the home, carrying a whole compound of kin on what
they turn over kobo by kobo.
