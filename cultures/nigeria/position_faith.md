# Position: faith

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The deep, everyday conviction that God is not far away in the sky but right here in the matter of the day. Nigeria is one of the most religious lands on earth, and it carries two great faiths together under one roof: the church, mostly in the south, where Pentecostal worship pours out on a Sunday in song and dance and the loud Amen, and the mosque, mostly in the north, where the call to prayer marks the hours and the faithful turn to Mecca five times a day. Under both, and older than both, runs the long memory of the ancestral way, the Yoruba orisa and Ifa divination, the reverence for the ancestors, that has not vanished but woven itself into how people pray. To greet a Nigerian is often to hear God in the very greeting; to ask how business goes is to be told we thank God; to part is to hear, go well, God dey.

## Orders

That God is in everything and over everything, so no day starts without prayer and no plan is laid without saying, by His grace; that the church and the mosque are pillars of the community and not only of the soul, schools, gathering places, and the spine of the week; that faith is to be lived loud and shared, worn openly and spoken freely, never a private and embarrassed thing; and that across the two great religions Nigerians are first one people, neighbours who greet one another's feasts, the Christian wishing the Muslim well at Sallah and the Muslim wishing the Christian well at Christmas.

## Loses

Ground when the deep trust in God becomes a reason to wait where one should work, the everything-go-better-by-God that folds its hands; when faith is used to milk the faithful rather than to feed them; and ground, most of all, when the two great religions, given to a people to bless them, are set against each other instead of standing together, so that what should join the giant is twisted to divide it.

## Drives

Lives in [Wole Soyinka](persona_soyinka.md), who reached past the imported faiths to the Yoruba gods, to Ogun the god of iron and the road, and made of the old cosmology a living drama. It drives Nigeria to carry God into every corner of the day, the church and the mosque and the shrine all part of one believing people, and to hold its faiths together as a blessing rather than let them be turned into a wound.
