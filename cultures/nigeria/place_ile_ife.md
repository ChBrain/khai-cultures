# Place: Ile-Ife

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

## Shown

The ancient Yoruba city in the southwest that the people honour as the cradle of creation, the place where, they say, the god Oduduwa first set foot and the world itself began, and from which the crowns of the Yoruba kings are said to descend. The seat of the Ooni, one of the most revered traditional rulers in the land, a town heavy with shrines, groves, and history, and the home of the famous Ife bronze and terracotta heads, cast with a lifelike grace that stunned the world when it first saw them and proved a high art flourishing here centuries before any colonizer came.

## Offers

A place where the country touches its own ancient depth, the proof that this land carried great civilizations, sacred kingship, and master art long before the line on the map, the spiritual home of one of the giant's great peoples, and the standing reminder that Nigeria did not begin in 1914 but reaches back into the deep memory of the peoples who made it.

## Withheld

The simple pride of an unbroken story. Ile-Ife also carries the ache of the bronzes scattered far from home, of an ancient glory the wider world long refused to credit, and of the gap between the deep, sacred greatness of the cradle and a present that does not always honour or keep it as it should.
