# Place: Lagos

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Shown

The roaring, sleepless, never-finished megacity by the lagoon, the largest in all of Africa, where millions hustle from before dawn to after dark. The yellow danfo buses and the okada bikes weaving through the go-slow, the conductors shouting their routes, the markets of Balogun and Computer Village spilling over, the high towers of Victoria Island looking across the water at the crowded mainland, the beat of Afrobeats pouring out of every speaker, and the studios where Nollywood turns out its films. The city that does not wait for anyone, where if you can make it here you can make it anywhere, and where every one of the giant's peoples rubs shoulders in one heaving, laughing, striving crowd.

## Offers

A place where the whole giant comes together and the hustle is a way of life, the front door through which Nigeria meets the world and the world meets Nigeria, the stage on which Nollywood, Afrobeats, and the tech and the markets are made, and the living proof that the energy of the country, pressed into one crowded city, can make anything happen for the one who is ready to chase it.

## Withheld

The ease its swagger advertises. Lagos carries the cost beneath the hustle, the go-slow that eats the hours, the crowding and the daily struggle behind the bright lights, the hard truth that the city promises everything and makes you sweat for every kobo of it, and the gap between the giant's glitter and the grind of the ordinary person fighting through the traffic to earn the day.
