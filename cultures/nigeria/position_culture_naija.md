# Position: Naija

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The belonging to a giant sewn from more than two hundred and fifty peoples, [the peoples](position_the_peoples.md) the British amalgamated into one border in 1914 and never quite finished becoming one. It carries [the extended family](position_the_extended_family.md) that makes a person never one person but a whole lineage walking about, the naming ceremony and the owambe and the aso-ebi that show the house is one body. It carries [faith](position_faith.md) lived loud in the church and the mosque and the older ancestral way underneath both, God greeted in the very greeting. And it carries the swagger that came out of the sober chapter of civil war grieved with dignity: Nollywood, Afrobeats on the road Fela cut, the literature of Achebe and Soyinka and Adichie, jollof rice worth fighting over, and the hustle of the market woman who feeds a whole compound from what she turns over on her table.

## Orders

That to be Nigerian is to be two things at once and proud of both, fully your own people and fully of the one country, with no contradiction between them. That the elder is greeted on the knees or with both hands, that the visitor is fed before he is asked his business, and that no joy and no sorrow is private, every birth and marriage and burial belonging to the whole kindred that gathers to mark it. That God is named in every plan and every parting. And that whatever the day brings, naija no dey carry last, so the hustle continues and the smile does not quit.

## Loses

Ground when a person is reduced to only his tribe and the country forgotten, when the question of who gets what is asked along the lines of blood instead of the common good. Ground when the duty owed to the family hardens into a weight that grinds the one who rose, and when respect for the elder and the big man shades into a silence where the junior swallows a truth that should be spoken. Ground, too, when the deep trust in God becomes a reason to wait where one should work, and when the giant's own swagger curdles into an ache at the gap between the greatness it knows in its bones and the daily grind that does not always reach the ordinary table.

## Drives

Lives in the market woman who carries a whole compound on a market table the bankers never see, in the musician who sang the one Pidgin tongue that Yoruba, Igbo, and Hausa all understand at once, and in the writers who told the world their people had a deep, dignified world before any colonizer arrived and who still question the very family and faith that hold them. It drives Nigeria to keep being one country of many nations, warm, loud, and generous to a fault, carrying one another so that nobody is ever truly alone, and to prove, with its whole chest, that the giant of Africa does not carry last.
