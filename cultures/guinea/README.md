# Guinea: a khai culture

Guinea staged as a full khai play, ISO GN, working language French (the prose is
in a Guinean French voice). A small country that carries a great deal, as a
theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its
languages and faith encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects,
all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_guinea.md](play_guinea.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_fr.md](pitch_fr.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Guinea's
  profile (a strongly communal society of hierarchy, respect for age and the
  spoken word, restraint, and a deep sense of honour and dignity) and naming no
  dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Mali empire (the Mandinka heartland and the jeli's
  epic), the Fouta Djallon state (the Fula highlands and their Islamic learning),
  the No of 1958 (the lone refusal of the French Community, treated soberly as
  national pride), and the sound of Guinea (Bembeya Jazz National, Mory Kanté, the
  kora and balafon carried to the world).
- **Personas:** Mory Kanté, Camara Laye, Sékou Touré (handled soberly), and
  Karamoko Alfa of the Fouta as the historic cast, plus one archetype, le jeli
  (the Mandinka griot, keeper of the spoken word). Each is wired to the language
  engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** the word of the jeli (the spoken memory of the Mande and the
  sacredness of the given word), the learning of the Fouta (the scholarly Islam of
  the Fula highlands), the water tower (Guinea as source of West Africa's rivers
  and the richest bauxite ground on earth).
- **Places:** Conakry (the coastal capital where the republic was born and the
  national sound went out), the Fouta Djallon (the highland water tower and seat of
  faith).
- **Processes:** the jeli's evening (the griot's recitation of the Mandinka epic),
  the djembe circle (the drum-and-dance gathering that binds the community).
- **Pieces:** the Sosso Bala (the sacred ancestral balafon of the Mande, kept
  since Soundiata), the kora (the griots' harp-lute, carried from the fireside to
  the world's stages).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the independence of 1958 and the harder passages of the country's history
are treated soberly on the public record, taking no political side; the staging
and the architecture are original work.
