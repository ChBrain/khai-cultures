# Laos: a khai culture

Laos staged as a full khai play, ISO LA, language lo. A land held together by the
wat, the river, and the unhurried heart of sticky rice, set out as a theatre: its
history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its language and faith
encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single
pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house that
holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its Estate; a
culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_laos.md](play_laos.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_lo.md](pitch_lo.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Laos'
  profile (a calm, unhurried, communal temper, respect for elders and the monkhood,
  a preference for harmony over confrontation, and the letting-go of "bo pen nyang")
  and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the founding of Lan Xang (1353), the golden age and That
  Luang, division and upheaval, and Laos today. The fragmentation of the kingdom,
  the colonial period, and the upheaval of the twentieth century are acknowledged
  soberly and with dignity, taking no side and dwelling on no atrocity.
- **Personas:** King Fa Ngum (founder of Lan Xang) and King Setthathirath (builder
  of That Luang) as the historic royal cast, Pangkham (poet of the Sin Xay epic)
  and Maha Sila Viravong (the scholar who preserved the Lao language and
  literature) as the cultural cast, plus the Novice, an archetype, the village boy
  ordained in the wat. Each is wired to the language engine by the
  language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** the Dhamma (Theravada Buddhism and the rhythm of the wat,
  almsgiving, and ordination), Lan Xang (the heritage of the Kingdom of a Million
  Elephants, the Lao language, script, and literature), and bo pen nyang (the
  relaxed grace of letting go).
- **Places:** Luang Prabang (the old royal capital of the wats and the morning
  almsgiving) and Vientiane (the present capital and That Luang).
- **Processes:** tak bat (the morning almsgiving) and the baci, or sou khwan (the
  calling-back of the soul, the binding of cotton threads).
- **Pieces:** the khene (the bamboo mouth organ, the national sound) and the
  sticky-rice basket (the heart of the Lao table).
- **Plan:** [plan_the_middle_way.md](plan_the_middle_way.md), the open mandate of
  keeping the calm, the faith, the river, the language, and the people through a
  changing world.

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the fragmentation of Lan Xang, the colonial period, and the upheaval of
the twentieth century are treated soberly on the public record, taking no side; the
staging and the architecture are original work.
