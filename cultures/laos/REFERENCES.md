# Laos: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind the
plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_lo.md](pitch_lo.md) is written from Laos' profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
Published Hofstede data for Laos is limited; the scores below are approximate and
read alongside neighbouring mainland Southeast Asian profiles. They are recorded
here so the data is on the record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Laos score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | -------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 70                   |
| Individualism                 | 20                   |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 35                   |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 50                   |
| Long Term Orientation         | 55                   |
| Indulgence                    | 45                   |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; where Laos is not separately
published, the profile is read alongside neighbouring mainland Southeast Asian
cultures.

## Language

The play and the whole cast are written in Lao (`lo`), which is written in the Lao
script as scriptio-continua, with no spaces between words. The Lao prose is the
author's own composition, offered in good faith and best reviewed by a native
speaker.

## Historical sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn
from the public historical record. They are credited here and never claimed; the
staging is original work. The fragmentation of Lan Xang, the colonial period, and
the upheaval of the twentieth century are staged soberly on the public record,
taking no side and dwelling on no atrocity.

The defining persona [the Novice](persona_the_novice.md) is an archetype, not a
historical individual: a composite of the village boy ordained in the wat, who
carries the living rhythm of Theravada Buddhism, almsgiving, and the passing-on of
the Dhamma to the next generation.
