# Persona: the Yapese stone holder

## Taxonomy

[the stone wonders](position_the_stone_wonders.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The Yapese keeper of the rai, the great limestone discs of stone money, who holds not the stone itself - which may stand right where it is at the village edge for generations, untouched - but the remembered story of who owns it, what it was given for, and the danger of the voyage that carried it home. Keeper of memory more than of any object, he carries the worth of a thing that is worth only what the people still recall of it. Yapese is his tongue, and the ledger of the stones: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [hears](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) in it the family lines of ownership and the named histories each disc's value rests on, an account kept whole in spoken memory and nowhere else. [English](position_language_en_fm.md) he [carries borrowed and partial](process_speaking_borrowed.md), enough for the office and the visitor's question, [following](process_reading_followed.md) what the wider world asks of him, but no rai's history was ever set down in it. The worth of a stone lives in a Yapese telling - the names, the deeds, the price paid - that has no home outside the tongue that holds it, so a disc explained in English comes down to a curiosity of size while its true worth, which is its story, stays shut up in Yapese; the gap at the limit of the tongue is the difference between a stone and its meaning.

## Action

In [the Stone Builders](plot_01_the_stone_builders.md) he keeps the rai and the histories that give each disc its worth, carrying [the stone wonders](position_the_stone_wonders.md) as a living account rather than a yard full of curiosities. He holds the proof that value can rest in memory and in relation, and that a wonder is only as alive as the telling that still goes with it.

## Shadow

The memory he keeps carries its shadow: a worth held only in the heads of the ones who remember it, so a history let go is a fortune wiped clean away, and a generation that lets the tellings fall quiet turns the discs from money back into mute rock; the frailty of a worth with no home but the living tongue.

## Tell

He can stand before a stone he has never once laid a hand on and name every hand it has passed through and every story that gave it weight - and to a stranger who sees only a great rock, he says the value was never in the stone.
