# Persona: the Yapese stone holder

## Taxonomy

[the stone wonders](position_the_stone_wonders.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The Yapese keeper of the rai, the great limestone discs of stone money, who holds not the stone itself - which may stand untouched at the village edge for generations - but the remembered history of who owns it, what it was given for, and the danger of the voyage that brought it home. Custodian of memory more than of object, he carries the worth of a thing that is worth only what the community recalls of it. Yapese is his tongue and the ledger of the stones: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [hears](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) in it the genealogies of ownership and the named histories on which each disc's value rests, an account kept entirely in spoken memory. English he [carries borrowed and partial](process_speaking_borrowed.md), enough for the office and the visitor's question, [following](process_reading_followed.md) what the wider world requires, but no rai's history was ever written in it. The worth of a stone lives in a Yapese telling - the names, the deeds, the price - that has no register outside the tongue that holds it, so a disc explained in English becomes a curiosity of size while its true value, which is its story, stays locked in Yapese; the gap at the limit of the tongue is the difference between a stone and its meaning.

## Action

In [the Stone Builders](plot_01_the_stone_builders.md) he keeps the rai and the histories that give each disc its worth, carrying [the stone wonders](position_the_stone_wonders.md) as a living account rather than a quarry of curiosities. He holds the proof that value can rest in memory and relation, and that a wonder is only as alive as the telling that still attends it.

## Shadow

The memory he keeps carries its shadow: a worth held only in the heads of those who remember it, so a history forgotten is a fortune erased, and a generation that lets the tellings lapse turns the discs from money into mute rock; the fragility of a value with no register but the living tongue.

## Tell

He can stand before a stone he has never touched and name every hand it has passed through and every story that gave it weight - and to a stranger who sees only a great rock, he says the value was never in the stone.
