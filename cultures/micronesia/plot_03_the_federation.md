# Plot: the Federation

## Taxonomy

[play_micronesia](play_micronesia.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

In the years after the war, the United Nations charge laid on the Trust Territory comes due: the islands are to govern themselves. Across the scattered districts the islanders begin to talk over what kind of country, if any, they might make - and whether four very different island peoples, speaking four tongues, ought to stand together or apart. A constitutional convention gathers, and [Tosiwo Nakayama](persona_tosiwo_nakayama.md) is called to chair it.

## Action

Out of long bargaining the four districts of Yap, Chuuk, Pohnpei, and Kosrae frame a constitution and join as the Federated States of Micronesia, a federation that leaves most of the power with the states and lays only a light shared roof above them. The new nation then talks out and signs the Compact of Free Association with the United States: it takes full sovereignty over its own affairs while the bigger power carries its defense and sends aid, and its citizens gain the right to live and work freely in the United States. A country is made not from one people but from four who chose to be one, its union written down in the English that bridges its four tongues, its independence held in partnership rather than standing all alone.

## Stage

- [Tosiwo Nakayama](persona_tosiwo_nakayama.md): the architect and first president who draws the four states into one and steers the bargaining through.
- [Nan Madol](place_nan_madol.md): the ancient seat on Pohnpei, the deep memory of island self-rule standing behind the new nation.
- [four states, one nation](position_four_states_one_nation.md): the proposition that four unlike peoples can choose to hold together as one federation.

## Tension

The islanders make themselves a sovereign nation by their own constitution and their own choice, four proud and separate peoples agreeing to stand as one Micronesia - a federation built by patience and by everybody coming around to it, not laid down on them by any power. Yet the union is held by a frame kept light on purpose, just to win the states over, and the independence it claims rests on a Compact that ties the new country to a far bigger one and opens a door its people will begin to go out through - so the nation is born both made and leaning, one and four at the same time.
