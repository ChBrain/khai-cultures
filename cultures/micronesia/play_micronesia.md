# Play: the Federated States of Micronesia

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the house that holds this staging and answers for it.

## Name

The Federated States of Micronesia, a chain of more than six hundred islands strung across the western Pacific just north of the equator, four states gathered into one federation: Yap, Chuuk, Pohnpei, and Kosrae. ISO FM, language en, the English of the schools and the capitol the only tongue the whole nation shares, while each state keeps its own. A country whose land is small, whose sea is vast, and whose unity is a thing made rather than inherited.

## Arc

A federation unlike most: not one people who grew into four provinces, but four very different island peoples - each with its own language and its own ways - who chose to hold together as one. Yap of the great stone money discs, Chuuk of the wide lagoon, Pohnpei of the basalt city on the reef, and Kosrae the green high island at the end of the chain. The land carries an ancient wonder twice over: Nan Madol, the city of stacked basalt raised on the reef of Pohnpei, and the rai of Yap, the giant limestone discs whose worth lives not in the stone but in the story carried with it. Across the open water moved the master navigators, reading the star paths and the swells without instrument, settling islands a thousand miles apart by knowledge alone. Then came the long foreign hand: Spain, then Germany, then Japan, and after a great war the United States held the islands as a Trust Territory under the United Nations. In the lagoon of Chuuk, once the great Japanese naval anchorage, a sunken fleet still rests beneath the divers. Out of the Trust the four states framed a federation and signed a Compact of Free Association with the United States, holding their own sovereignty while leaning on a larger power for defense and aid. Under it all run the matrilineal clans, the deference and the sakau circle, and the slow, gathering threat of a warming sea. One nerve holds the whole: a plural, clan-rooted, navigator-proud people who keep four tongues and one ocean, and ask whether four can stay one.

## Triggers

**[Plot 1: the Stone Builders](plot_01_the_stone_builders.md)**

Across centuries the island peoples raise [the stone wonders](position_the_stone_wonders.md) - the basalt city of Nan Madol on the reef of Pohnpei and the great limestone discs of Yap - and the master navigators settle the scattered islands by star and swell, led by figures like [the Nahnmwarki](persona_the_nahnmwarki.md) and [the Yapese stone holder](persona_the_yapese_stone_holder.md).

**[Plot 2: the Trust Territory](plot_02_the_trust_territory.md)**

Spain, Germany, and then Japan rule the islands in turn; war fills [Chuuk Lagoon](place_chuuk_lagoon.md) with a great naval base and then a sunken fleet, and afterward the United States holds the islands as a United Nations Trust Territory, where [the Chuukese clan mother](persona_the_chuukese_clan_mother.md) keeps the clan through the foreign hand.

**[Plot 3: the Federation](plot_03_the_federation.md)**

Out of the Trust Territory [Tosiwo Nakayama](persona_tosiwo_nakayama.md) and his fellow leaders draw the four states into [one federation](position_four_states_one_nation.md), frame a constitution, and sign the Compact of Free Association that makes the islands a sovereign nation in partnership with the United States.

**[Plot 4: the Wave-Finders](plot_04_the_wave_finders.md)**

[Mau Piailug](persona_mau_piailug.md) revives the dying art of voyaging and teaches it to the world, carrying [the navigating heritage](position_the_navigators_and_the_compact.md) into the present as the young nation faces a scattering diaspora and a rising sea.

## Stakes

Whether a federation of four distinct island cultures, each proud and apart and speaking its own tongue, can hold together as one Micronesia rather than drift back into four - whether it can steward the Compact that ties it to the United States without losing its own sovereignty or its young to the migration the Compact opens, whether it can carry its ancient navigating heritage and its stone wonders forward as living things rather than relics, and whether a low chain of islands can hold its ground at all against the warming, rising sea that threatens to take the land out from under the nation.
