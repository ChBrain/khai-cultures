# Play: the Federated States of Micronesia

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the house that holds this staging and answers for it.

## Name

The Federated States of Micronesia, more than six hundred islands strung across the western Pacific just north of the line, four states sitting down together as one: Yap, Chuuk, Pohnpei, and Kosrae. ISO FM, language en, and the English of the school and the capitol is the one tongue the whole nation shares, while each state holds tight to its own. Small island, big ocean. The land here is little, the sea is plenty, and the staying-together is not something the people were born into but something they chose, and choose again.

## Arc

This is not the usual kind of federation. Not one people who spread out into four provinces, but four very different island peoples, each one carrying its own language and its own ways, who agreed to hold together as one. Yap with its great stone money discs, Chuuk with its wide lagoon, Pohnpei with its basalt city out on the reef, and Kosrae the green high island at the far end of the chain. The land carries an old wonder, and not once but twice over: Nan Madol, the city of stacked basalt raised out on the reef of Pohnpei, and the rai of Yap, the big limestone discs whose worth is not in the stone at all but in the story that travels with it. Across all that open water the master navigators came and went, reading the star paths and the swells with no instrument in hand, settling islands a thousand miles apart by knowing alone. Then the foreign hand came, and stayed long: Spain, then Germany, then Japan, and after a big war the United States held the islands as a Trust Territory under the United Nations. In the Chuuk lagoon, once the great Japanese anchorage, a sunken fleet still rests quiet beneath the divers. Out of the Trust the four states drew up a federation and signed a Compact of Free Association with the United States, keeping their own sovereignty while leaning on a bigger power for defense and for aid. Underneath all of it run the matrilineal clans, the respect that is owed and given, the sakau bowl passed around the circle, and the slow rising of a warming sea. One nerve holds the whole thing together: a plural, clan-rooted, navigator-proud people who keep four tongues and one ocean, and who keep asking whether four can stay one.

## Triggers

**[Plot 1: the Stone Builders](plot_01_the_stone_builders.md)**

Over many generations the island people raise [the stone wonders](position_the_stone_wonders.md) - the basalt city of Nan Madol out on the reef of Pohnpei and the great limestone discs of Yap - and the master navigators go out and settle the scattered islands by star and by swell, with men like [the Nahnmwarki](persona_the_nahnmwarki.md) and [the Yapese stone holder](persona_the_yapese_stone_holder.md) standing at the head of it.

**[Plot 2: the Trust Territory](plot_02_the_trust_territory.md)**

Spain, Germany, and then Japan rule the islands each in their turn; war fills [Chuuk Lagoon](place_chuuk_lagoon.md) with a great naval base and then leaves a sunken fleet on the bottom, and afterward the United States holds the islands as a United Nations Trust Territory, while [the Chuukese clan mother](persona_the_chuukese_clan_mother.md) keeps the clan whole right through the foreign hand.

**[Plot 3: the Federation](plot_03_the_federation.md)**

Out of the Trust Territory [Tosiwo Nakayama](persona_tosiwo_nakayama.md) and the leaders alongside him draw the four states into [one federation](position_four_states_one_nation.md), frame a constitution, and sign the Compact of Free Association that makes the islands a sovereign nation standing in partnership with the United States.

**[Plot 4: the Wave-Finders](plot_04_the_wave_finders.md)**

[Mau Piailug](persona_mau_piailug.md) brings the dying art of voyaging back to life and teaches it out to the world, carrying [the navigating heritage](position_the_navigators_and_the_compact.md) into the present day as the young nation watches its young people scatter abroad and the sea come up around it.

## Stakes

Whether four distinct island cultures, each one proud and apart and speaking its own tongue, can keep holding together as one Micronesia and not drift back into four - whether the nation can carry the Compact that ties it to the United States without letting go of its own sovereignty, or losing its young people through the door that same Compact opens, whether it can carry its old navigating heritage and its stone wonders forward as living things and not as relics behind glass, and whether a low chain of islands can hold its ground at all against the warming, rising sea that means to take the very land out from under the nation.
