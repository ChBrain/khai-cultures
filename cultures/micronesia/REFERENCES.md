# the Federated States of Micronesia: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md) is written from a Micronesian cultural
profile in the spirit of Geert Hofstede's model of national culture and names no
dimension and carries no number. The Federated States of Micronesia is not among
the individually scored countries in the published Hofstede data; the profile used
here is an approximate reading drawn from the regional and Pacific-island pattern
and from the islands' own self-understanding, recorded here so the basis is on the
record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Micronesian profile (approx., regional reading) |
| ----------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | high                                            |
| Individualism                 | low (strongly collectivist, clan-rooted)        |
| Motivation toward Achievement | low (modest, group-first, cooperative)          |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | moderate                                        |
| Long Term Orientation         | moderate                                        |
| Indulgence                    | moderate                                        |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the six-dimensions model of national
culture), read against the broader Pacific-island pattern. The Federated States of
Micronesia carries no individual published country score; the values above are an
approximate, regional reading and are not claimed as official Hofstede figures.

## Historical sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn
from the public historical record. They are credited here and never claimed; the
staging is original work. The long succession of foreign rule and the war that
reached the islands - Spanish, German, and Japanese administration, the Second
World War in Chuuk Lagoon, and the US Trust Territory of the Pacific Islands - is
staged soberly, on the public record and from within the islands' own
self-understanding, taking no side and dwelling on no atrocity.

The three defining archetypes, [the Nahnmwarki](persona_the_nahnmwarki.md), [the
Yapese stone holder](persona_the_yapese_stone_holder.md), and [the Chuukese clan
mother](persona_the_chuukese_clan_mother.md), are not historical individuals but
composites: the Pohnpeian paramount chief and the sakau circle, the Yapese keeper
of the rai and its memory, and the senior woman of a Chuukese matrilineal clan,
each carrying a home-state tongue (Pohnpeian, Yapese, Chuukese) over the English
that bridges the four states.
