# Process: wayfinding

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

A master navigator setting a voyaging canoe onto the open ocean with no instrument and no chart: the star compass held in his head, the rising and setting points of the stars memorized around the whole horizon, the canoe pointed at a sea that holds no road. It takes the canoe, the crew, the wind, and a navigator who has spent a lifetime learning the sky and the water - and the voyage begins by reading what cannot be written down.

## Direction

Toward the far island over the curve of the world: wayfinding is the art of finding a speck of land across a thousand miles of empty water by knowledge alone. The navigator holds his course by the star paths at night, by the sun and the set of the swells by day, by the flight of birds and the colour of the water near land, keeping in his mind a moving picture of where he is on a sea that gives no marks. It is the knowledge that peopled the scattered islands and made of the vast ocean a single sea-world.

## Lever

It is driven by the islands' ancient mastery of the open sea and by a knowledge so complete it could cross the Pacific without a single instrument. It holds against the emptiness of the ocean and the smallness of the target; against it stand the long decline that nearly killed it, the engines and charts that made it seem needless, and the rule that kept it within the island until one navigator chose to give it away.

## Echo

Wayfinding makes audible what [the navigators and the Compact](position_the_navigators_and_the_compact.md) carry: that this is a people of the open sea, reaching the world by knowledge of the water. Down these star paths [Mau Piailug](persona_mau_piailug.md) sailed without instrument and then gave the art away, so the knowledge that almost died with his island lives on across the ocean it was made to cross.
