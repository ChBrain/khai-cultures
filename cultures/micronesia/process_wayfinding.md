# Process: wayfinding

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

A master navigator setting a voyaging canoe out onto the open ocean with no instrument and no chart: the star compass held in his head, the rising and the setting points of the stars learned by heart all around the whole horizon, the canoe pointed at a sea that holds no road at all. It takes the canoe, the crew, the wind, and a navigator who has spent a whole lifetime learning the sky and the water - and the voyage begins by reading what cannot be written down.

## Direction

Toward the far island, away over the curve of the world: wayfinding is the art of finding a speck of land across a thousand miles of empty water by knowing alone. The navigator holds his course by the star paths in the night, by the sun and the set of the swells in the day, by the flight of the birds and the colour of the water near land, keeping in his head a moving picture of where he is on a sea that gives no marks at all. It is the knowledge that peopled the scattered islands and made one single sea-world out of the vast ocean.

## Lever

It is driven by the islands' old mastery of the open sea and by a knowledge so complete it could cross the whole Pacific without one single instrument. It holds against the emptiness of the ocean and the smallness of the target; against it stand the long fading that near killed it off, the engines and the charts that made it look like a thing no longer needed, and the rule that kept it home on the island until one navigator chose to give it away.

## Echo

Wayfinding makes you hear what [the navigators and the Compact](position_the_navigators_and_the_compact.md) carry: that this is a people of the open sea, reaching the world by their knowledge of the water. Down these very star paths [Mau Piailug](persona_mau_piailug.md) sailed with no instrument and then gave the art away, so the knowledge that near died with his island lives on now across the ocean it was made to cross.
