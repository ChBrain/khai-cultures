# Persona: Tosiwo Nakayama

## Taxonomy

[four states, one nation](position_four_states_one_nation.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The son of a Japanese trader and a Chuukese mother, who came to be the chief architect of the federation and its first president, the man who took four unlike island peoples out from under a foreign trusteeship and drew them into one nation. Patient, a peacemaker, and quietly stubborn under it, he chaired the constitutional convention, held the bargaining together when the states wanted to pull apart, and gave Micronesia its first shape as a country. Chuukese is the tongue of his mother and his clan: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [hears](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) it as the language of home, of the island that claims him as its own. [English](position_language_en_fm.md) he [carries as his working tongue](process_speaking_carried.md), the language he [wrote and drafted](process_writing_drafted.md) the federation's founding charter in and [followed](process_reading_followed.md) through all the long American legalese of the Trust and the Compact, the one speech the four states held in common across their four tongues. He built one nation out of four peoples in a borrowed language not one of them was born to, drawing up the bond of Yapese and Chuukese and Pohnpeian and Kosraean in the English of the trustee - so the union itself rests on a tongue carried rather than inherited, and the gap at the limit of the tongue runs straight through the very paper that made the country one.

## Action

In [the Federation](plot_03_the_federation.md) he draws the four states into one, chairing the convention that frames the constitution and steering the bargaining that makes [four states, one nation](position_four_states_one_nation.md) hold. By holding the union together when it could so easily have come apart into four, he gives the Federated States of Micronesia the shape it has carried ever since - a federation made by patience and by everybody coming around to it.

## Shadow

The unity he built by give-and-take carries its shadow: a federal frame kept so light, just to win the states over, that it can struggle to hold them, the bargain forever needing to be struck again; and the founder's faith that everybody coming around will alone keep four proud peoples together, when the pull back toward island and tongue never quite lies down to rest.

## Tell

He holds the convention together not by laying down the law but by listening each state out, patient, until a shared word can be found, the navigator's patience turned to politics - and writes the bond of four mother tongues in the one tongue not one of them owned.
