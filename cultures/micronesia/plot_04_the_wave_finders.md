# Plot: the Wave-Finders

## Taxonomy

[play_micronesia](play_micronesia.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

By the late twentieth century the ancient art of voyaging is all but gone, the master navigators few and old, the knowledge of the star paths and the swells dying with them as engines and charts replace the canoe. On the tiny atoll of Satawal, one of the last who hold the whole knowledge whole, [Mau Piailug](persona_mau_piailug.md), faces the choice of letting it die with his island or breaking the old rule and giving it away.

## Action

Mau Piailug sails a voyaging canoe across thousands of miles of open ocean by knowledge alone, without instrument, and teaches the wayfinding to outsiders across the Pacific, sparking a revival of traditional voyaging that spreads from island to island. The young nation grows up around this rekindled pride: a Micronesia making its own way in the world, its people moving freely under the Compact to the United States until a great share of them live abroad, sending money home and keeping ties across the water. And over the low islands rises the newest and gravest threat - a warming, rising sea that no navigator's knowledge can steer around, bearing down on a people who read the ocean for a thousand years.

## Stage

- [Mau Piailug](persona_mau_piailug.md): the master navigator who revives the voyaging and gives the knowledge back to the sea.
- [Chuuk Lagoon](place_chuuk_lagoon.md): the lagoon now drawing divers to its ghost fleet, the islands' past turned to a present livelihood.
- [the navigators and the Compact](position_the_navigators_and_the_compact.md): the two ties to the wider sea, the ancient knowledge and the modern treaty, carried into the present.

## Tension

The nation carries its oldest mastery into the present as a living thing - the wayfinding revived, the pride in the open sea rekindled, the canoe sailing again - and meets the modern world on the Compact's open road. Yet the same openness scatters its people abroad faster than the islands can hold them, and the rising sea threatens the very ground beneath the nation: a people who could always find their way across the ocean now face a water that will not stay where it was, and must navigate a future no star path charts.
