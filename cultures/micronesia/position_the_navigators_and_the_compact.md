# Position: the navigators and the Compact

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The two ties that bind these islands to the wider sea, one old and one new. The first is the wayfinding heritage: the master navigators who read the star paths, the swells, the flight of the birds, and the shape of the water to cross a thousand miles of open ocean with no instrument, the knowledge that peopled the scattered islands and made one single sea-world of them. The second is the Compact of Free Association, the treaty by which the Federated States of Micronesia holds its own sovereignty while the United States carries its defense and sends aid, and through which its citizens may live and work freely in the bigger country. One reach goes outward by the nation's own knowledge; the other is a lifeline held to a far bigger power - and both are ways a small island people has always met a vast ocean.

## Orders

That the nation belongs to the open sea and not only to its little land, and that the navigator's knowledge is a national treasure to be kept alive; that a small people may lean on a bigger power without ceasing to be its own, holding sovereignty and partnership both at once; and that the ties outward - of voyaging and of treaty - are the means by which an island nation reaches a world far bigger than itself.

## Loses

Ground when the lifeline turns into a leash - when leaning on the Compact hollows out the nation's own footing and the door it opens drains the young away faster than the country can hold them; and ground, too, when the old navigating knowledge is let die as a quaint thing of the past, so a people who once read the whole ocean come to know it only as far as the next port and the airline schedule.

## Drives

Lives in the master navigator who would not let the wayfinding die and gave it back to the Pacific and to the world. It drives the Federated States of Micronesia to keep both its reaches open - the old knowledge of the sea and the modern partnership across it - and to meet the wide ocean as its own people always have.
