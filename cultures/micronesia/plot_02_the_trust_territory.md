# Plot: the Trust Territory

## Taxonomy

[play_micronesia](play_micronesia.md)

## Owner

## Cue

From the sixteenth century onward the foreign hands arrive in turn. Spain claims the islands and rules them lightly from afar, then sells them to Germany, which works them for copra and phosphate. After the First World War Japan takes the islands under a League of Nations mandate, settles them heavily, and builds across them - and turns the great lagoon of Chuuk into a forward naval anchorage of the Pacific.

## Action

In the Second World War the islands become a theatre of the wider conflict, and the Japanese base in [Chuuk Lagoon](place_chuuk_lagoon.md) is struck and broken, leaving a fleet of ships and aircraft on the lagoon floor where they rest to this day. After the war the United States holds the islands as the Trust Territory of the Pacific Islands under the United Nations, charged with their welfare and their eventual self-government. Through the whole long passage of foreign administrations - Spanish, German, Japanese, and American - the deeper order of the islands endures beneath the changing flag: the clans, the titles, the matrilineal lines, and the languages of the four peoples, carried on at home while the governors come and go.

## Stage

- [the Chuukese clan mother](persona_the_chuukese_clan_mother.md): the keeper of the matrilineal clan, holding the people's deepest belonging through each foreign rule.
- [Chuuk Lagoon](place_chuuk_lagoon.md): the great anchorage of the war, and afterward the sunken ghost fleet beneath the divers.

## Tension

The islands pass through a long succession of foreign powers, each leaving its mark - a church, a road, a wreck on the lagoon floor, a border drawn on someone else's map - and the war reaches even these distant atolls with a cost soberly remembered. Yet beneath every administration the clans and the tongues hold, the people remaining themselves while the flag above them keeps changing; and out of the last trusteeship, charged with leading the islands to self-rule, the path opens toward a nation the islanders will frame for themselves.
