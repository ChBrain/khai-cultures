# Plot: the Trust Territory

## Taxonomy

[play_micronesia](play_micronesia.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

From the sixteenth century onward the foreign hands arrive, one after another. Spain claims the islands and rules them with a light hand from far off, then sells them on to Germany, which works them for copra and phosphate. After the First World War Japan takes the islands under a League of Nations mandate, settles them heavy, and builds all across them - and turns the great lagoon of Chuuk into a forward naval anchorage of the Pacific.

## Action

In the Second World War the islands become a theatre of the wider war, and the Japanese base in [Chuuk Lagoon](place_chuuk_lagoon.md) is struck and broken, leaving a fleet of ships and aircraft down on the lagoon floor where they rest to this day. After the war the United States holds the islands as the Trust Territory of the Pacific Islands under the United Nations, charged with their welfare and with bringing them in time to govern themselves. Through the whole long passage of foreign administrations - Spanish, German, Japanese, and American - the deeper order of the islands holds on beneath the changing flag: the clans, the titles, the matrilineal lines, and the languages of the four peoples, all carried on at home while the governors come and go.

## Stage

- [the Chuukese clan mother](persona_the_chuukese_clan_mother.md): the keeper of the matrilineal clan, holding the people's deepest belonging through each foreign rule in turn.
- [Chuuk Lagoon](place_chuuk_lagoon.md): the great anchorage of the war, and afterward the sunken ghost fleet down beneath the divers.

## Tension

The islands pass through a long line of foreign powers, each one leaving its mark - a church, a road, a wreck on the lagoon floor, a border drawn on someone else's map - and the war reaches even these far-off atolls with a cost remembered soberly. Yet beneath every administration the clans and the tongues hold, the people staying their own selves while the flag above them keeps on changing; and out of that last trusteeship, charged with leading the islands toward governing themselves, the way opens toward a nation the islanders will frame for themselves.
