# Plan: four states, one Micronesia

## Taxonomy

The open mandate the nation set itself when four island peoples chose to stand as one: the patient work of holding the federation together that [four states, one nation](position_four_states_one_nation.md) keeps forever apart-and-together, of carrying the Compact that ties the islands to the United States without losing their footing or their young, of carrying the old mastery of [the navigators and the Compact](position_the_navigators_and_the_compact.md) and the inheritance of [the stone wonders](position_the_stone_wonders.md) forward as living things, and of facing a rising sea that threatens the very ground the nation stands on.

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Direction

Hold the union whole - keep four proud and separate peoples choosing to be one Micronesia even when island and tongue pull them back toward four; carry the Compact as a partnership and not a leash, drawing its support while keeping the nation's own sovereignty and holding its people at home against the door it opens; keep the heritage alive - the wayfinding, the stone wonders, the clans, and the four tongues all carried as living inheritance and not as relics; and meet the rising sea, the gravest threat of all, with every voice the small nation can raise in a world whose warming it had no hand in causing.

## Orders

- To [the Nahnmwarki](persona_the_nahnmwarki.md): keep the order, the titles, and the sakau alive as a living thread back to the builders, not a museum of rank, and let the deep custom hold the young as the wider world levels around it.
- To the navigator like [Mau Piailug](persona_mau_piailug.md): give the wayfinding away rather than let it die, and teach the next generation to find their way on a sea that is no longer the one their fathers read.
- To the nation: hold the four as one and the Compact as a partnership, and carry the islands' case for their own survival to a world that has got to hear it.

## Implementation

A young federation of four island states, sovereign by its own constitution and tied to the United States by the Compact, that has held its union together for decades and brought its oldest mastery of the sea back to life - and that now faces the hard arithmetic of a thin land and a vast ocean: a people scattering abroad through the Compact's open door, a federal frame forever needing to be held together by everybody coming around to it, an economy leaning on aid and on the wrecks and reefs the world comes out to see, and a rising sea that no navigator's knowledge can steer around.
