# the Federated States of Micronesia: a khai culture

The Federated States of Micronesia staged as a full khai play, ISO FM, language
en. A federation of four island peoples held as one nation by English and a
shared ocean, as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast,
the roles its languages encode, its places, its practices, and its defining
objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_micronesia.md](play_micronesia.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md), the Hofstede layer, written from the
  Micronesian profile (a plural, clan-rooted, group-first people with deep
  deference to rank and age, unhurried and modest, settling by consensus) and
  naming no dimension. English is the bridge across the four state tongues.
- **Plots (history):** the Stone Builders, the Trust Territory, the Federation,
  and the Wave-Finders - each casting the elements it needs, and each staging the
  long foreign rule and the war soberly and on the public record.
- **Personas:** Tosiwo Nakayama, the architect and first president of the
  federation, and Mau Piailug, the Satawalese master navigator who revived
  voyaging, as the historic cast, plus three defining archetypes - the Nahnmwarki
  (the Pohnpeian paramount chief and the sakau circle), the Yapese stone holder
  (keeper of the rai and its remembered history), and the Chuukese clan mother
  (the matrilineal line beneath the nation). Each is wired to the language engine
  by the language-crossing it holds, its home-state tongue carried over English.
- **Positions:** four states, one nation (four unlike peoples choosing one
  federation, bridged by English), the stone wonders (Nan Madol and the rai, the
  ancient achievement), the navigators and the Compact (the wayfinding heritage
  and the free-association tie to the United States).
- **Places:** Nan Madol (the basalt city on the reef of Pohnpei), Chuuk Lagoon
  (the great anchorage, the war, and the diver's ghost fleet).
- **Processes:** the sakau circle (the Pohnpeian kava rite of the nahs and its
  order of rank), wayfinding (navigation by star path and swell across the open
  ocean).
- **Pieces:** the rai (the giant Yapese stone money whose value lives in memory),
  the outrigger canoe (the voyaging craft on which the sea-world was settled).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the long succession of foreign rule - Spanish, German, Japanese, and the
US Trust Territory - and the war in Chuuk Lagoon are treated soberly on the public
record, from within the islands' own self-understanding; the staging and the
architecture are original work.
