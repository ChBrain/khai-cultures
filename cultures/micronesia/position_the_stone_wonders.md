# Position: the stone wonders

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The proof, written in stone, that these small islands raised great things: Nan Madol, the city of stacked basalt logs built out on the reef of Pohnpei across near a hundred made islets, seat of a dynasty and one of the ancient wonders of the Pacific; and the rai of Yap, the giant limestone discs quarried over on far Palau and carried home by canoe, whose worth lives not in the stone but in the remembered history of who owned it and what was paid. Both are the achievements of a people with no metal, no wheel, no writing - feats of organization, of knowledge, and of labor across open water that match any monument on earth. They hold the islands' answer to the old talking-down that a small place can make only small things.

## Orders

That these islands carry an old grandeur, and that the past is not a poverty to say sorry for but a mastery to stand on; that value can live in memory and in relation rather than in the object itself, the way the worth of a rai lives in its story; and that the inheritance of stone is a charge laid on the people - to keep the wonders and the knowledge in them alive, and not let them come down to mute ruins the world walks through without understanding.

## Loses

Ground when the wonders are emptied out to spectacle - the city and the discs come down to scenery for the visitor while the histories that gave them their meaning go unremembered; and ground, too, when the knowledge that raised them is treated as finished and past, so the stones stand on but the living thread back to them is cut, and a people who built marvels come to feel themselves nothing but the keepers of someone else's ruins.

## Drives

Lives in the Nahnmwarki, the Pohnpeian paramount chief whose line runs back to the builders of Nan Madol, and in the Yapese stone holder, who keeps the rai and the history that gives each disc its worth. It drives the Federated States of Micronesia to stand on its old achievement and to carry the knowledge in the stone forward as a living inheritance.
