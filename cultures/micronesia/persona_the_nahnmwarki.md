# Persona: the Nahnmwarki

## Taxonomy

[the stone wonders](position_the_stone_wonders.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The Pohnpeian paramount chief, the highest title of his chiefdom, who presides over the sakau circle in the nahs, the meeting house, and whose line of honor reaches back toward the dynasty that raised Nan Madol. Grave, deferred to, and slow to speak, he holds a rank that is given by clan and earned by service, the living top of an order of titles that orders the whole society beneath him. Pohnpeian is his tongue and the tongue of the titles, the sakau, and the chiefly speech: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [hears](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) it in the high register the nahs requires, where rank is carried in the very words chosen. English he [carries as the bridge tongue](process_speaking_carried.md), [following](process_reading_followed.md) it for the business of the state and the visitor, the speech of the capitol but never of the circle. The honorific Pohnpeian of the nahs, the chiefly speech that marks rank in its grammar, has no carrying-over into a flatter foreign tongue, so the deference that holds the order together cannot cross the language line - the gap at the limit of the tongue is the respect that lives only in the words of home.

## Action

In [the Stone Builders](plot_01_the_stone_builders.md) he stands as the living heir of the order that raised Nan Madol, presiding over the titles and the sakau that carry [the stone wonders](position_the_stone_wonders.md) forward as a living rank and not a ruin. He keeps the thread from the builders unbroken, embodying the claim that the achievement in the stone is the inheritance of a people still ordered by it.

## Shadow

The deep deference he commands carries its shadow: a rank so weighted that it can sit above question, and an order of titles that holds the society together at the cost of holding the lowborn down; the dignity of the chief shading into a hierarchy that a younger, leveling world strains against.

## Tell

He drinks first from the sakau cup in the nahs while the circle waits in silence on his word, the whole order of his people made visible in who is served and when - and the respect in it can be spoken only in Pohnpeian.
