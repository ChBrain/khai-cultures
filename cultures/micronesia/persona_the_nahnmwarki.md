# Persona: the Nahnmwarki

## Taxonomy

[the stone wonders](position_the_stone_wonders.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The Pohnpeian paramount chief, the highest title of his chiefdom, who sits at the head of the sakau circle in the nahs, the meeting house, and whose line of honor runs back toward the dynasty that raised Nan Madol. Grave, given his respect, and slow to open his mouth, he holds a rank his clan gives him and his service earns, the living top of an order of titles that sets every person below him in their place. Pohnpeian is his tongue, and the tongue of the titles, the sakau, and the chiefly speech: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [hears](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) it in the high register the nahs asks for, where the very words a man chooses carry his rank. [English](position_language_en_fm.md) he [carries as the bridge tongue](process_speaking_carried.md), [following](process_reading_followed.md) it for the business of the state and for the visitor, the speech of the capitol but never of the circle. The honorific Pohnpeian of the nahs, the chiefly speech that marks rank right down in its grammar, has no way of carrying over into a flatter foreign tongue, so the respect that holds the whole order together cannot cross the language line - the gap at the limit of the tongue is the respect that lives only in the words of home. He belongs to [Micronesian culture](position_culture_micronesian_culture.md) as the living rank descended from the builders of Nan Madol.

## Action

In [the Stone Builders](plot_01_the_stone_builders.md) he stands as the living heir of the order that raised Nan Madol, sitting at the head of the titles and the sakau that carry [the stone wonders](position_the_stone_wonders.md) forward as a living rank and not a ruin. He keeps the thread from the builders unbroken, the living proof that the achievement in the stone is the inheritance of a people still ordered by it.

## Shadow

The deep respect he is owed carries its shadow: a rank so weighted it can sit clean above any question, and an order of titles that holds the society together but does it by holding the lowborn down; the dignity of the chief shading off into a hierarchy a younger, leveling world pushes against.

## Tell

He drinks first from the sakau cup in the nahs while the whole circle waits quiet on his word, the whole order of his people there to see in who is served, and when - and the respect in it can be spoken only in Pohnpeian.
