# Process: sakau

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

The people of Pohnpei gathering in the nahs, the meeting house, around the flat stone where the sakau is made: the root of the kava plant pounded with measured blows, mixed with water and the inner bark of the hibiscus, and strained into a cup of slick, earthen liquid. It takes a host, a stone, the root, and the rank of those who gather - and the circle forms in order, the cup carried first to the highest title.

## Direction

Toward order, respect, and the quiet binding of the community: the sakau circle is where rank is shown and honored, where disputes are settled and bonds renewed, where the chief is deferred to and the day's tension drained into the slow calm the drink brings. It is not merely refreshment but the rite in which the society arranges itself, each served in the order of their standing, the whole company brought low and patient together.

## Lever

It is driven by the deep value the islands place on rank, deference, and the held-together group, and by a drink whose slow, settling calm suits the unhurried time of the circle. It holds against discord and the heat of dispute, draining them into the patience of the bowl; against it stand the cost of the deference it enacts, the order it keeps fixing the high above the low even as it binds them.

## Echo

The sakau circle makes audible what [the stone wonders](position_the_stone_wonders.md) carry: a society ordered by rank and held together by rite, the same order whose line runs back toward the builders of Nan Madol. Around this stone [the Nahnmwarki](persona_the_nahnmwarki.md) is served first while the circle waits in silence, the whole order of the people made visible in the passing of the cup.
