# Process: sakau

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

The people of Pohnpei gathering in the nahs, the meeting house, around the flat stone where the sakau is made: the root of the kava plant pounded with steady, measured blows, mixed with water and the inner bark of the hibiscus, and strained out into a cup of slick, earthen liquid. It takes a host, a stone, the root, and the rank of the ones who gather - and the circle forms up in order, the cup carried first to the highest title.

## Direction

Toward order, toward respect, and toward the quiet binding-up of the community: the sakau circle is where rank is shown and given its honor, where the troubles between people are talked out and settled and bonds made new again, where the chief is given his respect and the day's tension drains off into the slow calm the drink brings. It is not just refreshment but the rite where the society sets itself in order, each one served by his standing, the whole company brought low and patient together.

## Lever

It is driven by the deep value the islands set on rank, on respect, and on the group held together, and by a drink whose slow, settling calm suits the unhurried time of the circle. It holds against discord and the heat of a quarrel, draining them down into the patience of the bowl; against it stands the cost of the deference it enacts, the order it keeps fixing the high above the low even as it binds them together.

## Echo

The sakau circle makes you hear what [the stone wonders](position_the_stone_wonders.md) carry: a society ordered by rank and held together by rite, the same order whose line runs back toward the builders of Nan Madol. Around this stone [the Nahnmwarki](persona_the_nahnmwarki.md) is served first while the whole circle waits quiet, the whole order of the people there to see in the passing of the cup.
