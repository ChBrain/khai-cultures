# Position: Micronesian English

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The authority of English as the one shared tongue of the Federated States of Micronesia, the official language of the whole union and the only speech that runs from one end of the chain to the other above the four unlike languages of the states - the Yapese of Yap, the Chuukese of the lagoon, the Pohnpeian of the reef city, and the Kosraean of the green high island. It is the tongue of the Compact and the constitution, of the school, the office, the court, and the paper that makes a thing official, inherited from the American trusteeship that held the islands after the war and left its language behind as the medium of the state. No island people is born to it - each is cradled in a tongue of its own home - yet all must cross into it to be one nation, so the union rests on a speech carried rather than inherited. Micronesian English, with its own turns and its own borrowed words, is neither better nor worse than the English of elsewhere: it is the English of these islands, the tool of the trustee turned into the common ground of a country that stands on its own.

## Orders

It orders what counts as the official word of the nation: the language of the Compact and the constitution, the register of the office and the record, the speech in which a law is set down, a title is filed, a bond between the four states is written, and a lesson is given in the school. It commands which tongue passes into the writing that holds authority and ties the four peoples together under one flag when the four mother tongues cannot speak across to one another. It is in this one shared language, the tongue not one state was born to, that four unlike island peoples drew up their charter and made themselves a single country, so that the very paper that binds the federation is written in a speech held in common precisely because it belongs to no one clan or island first.

## Loses

It loses ground to the mother tongues of home and clan, the languages in which the people actually pray, count, reckon their kin, and quarrel - the Pohnpeian of the nahs and the sakau circle, the Satawalese of the star compass and the swell, the Yapese ledger of the stone money, the Chuukese thread of the matrilineal line. A flat foreign tongue cannot carry the honorifics and the kinship distinctions those languages hold: the chiefly register that marks a man's rank down in its grammar, the kin terms that name who belongs to whom and whose the land is, the names and deeds that give a rai disc its worth. Set down in English, the respect and the belonging that hold the whole order together lose the very distinctions they live by, so what the state writes in the shared tongue is always a little less than what the home tongue knows. It loses too against the older English of the books and the wider world, held up as the high standard against which the English of these islands is measured, as if it were only a departure and not a whole speech of its own.

## Drives

It drives the nation to speak with one official voice above the sharing of its tongues, without any people holding it as the language of the cradle. It is carried as tool and as bond by those who, born to other tongues, had to cross into it: [Tosiwo Nakayama](persona_tosiwo_nakayama.md), who drafted the founding charter in it and built one nation out of four peoples in a borrowed language not one of them owned, so that the union itself rests on a tongue carried rather than inherited; [the Nahnmwarki](persona_the_nahnmwarki.md), who carries it as the bridge tongue of the capitol and the visitor but never of the sakau circle, where the respect can be spoken only in Pohnpeian; and [Mau Piailug](persona_mau_piailug.md) and [the Yapese stone holder](persona_the_yapese_stone_holder.md), who carry it borrowed and partial, just enough for the office and the outsider, while the deep knowing - the star path and the swell, the histories that give a stone its worth - stays shut up in the home tongue. Beside them stands the one the tongue least reaches: [the Chuukese clan mother](persona_the_chuukese_clan_mother.md), who reads it only haltingly and hears far more of it than she will ever speak, and watches the kinship she keeps pass off into English forms where its terms have no words. So the shared language shows who carries it to hold the nation and treat with the world, and who, before it or beside it, keeps first the tongue of their own.
