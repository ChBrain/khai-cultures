# Place: Nan Madol

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Shown

The city of stone raised on the reef off the southeast shore of Pohnpei: nearly a hundred artificial islets built up in the shallow water and walled and floored with great prismatic basalt logs, stacked log on log like cordwood, divided by tidal canals you cross by canoe. Seat of the Saudeleur dynasty and centre of power and ritual for centuries, it is the largest ancient monument of the Pacific islands and one of the wonders of the sea-world, now silent and half-drowned, the walls standing in the mangroves where the dynasty once ruled.

## Offers

A place where the islands show what they once raised - a city on the sea built without metal or the wheel, proof that a small ocean people commanded the organization, knowledge, and labor to make a wonder; a seat of ancient power that anchors Pohnpei's place in the nation and the islands' claim to a grandeur of their own, and a site the whole world now counts among its heritage.

## Withheld

The full reading of itself. Nan Madol keeps its mysteries - how the great stones were moved and stacked, the whole story of the dynasty that fell - and it withholds the ease of a living monument: the city is a ruin, the line to its builders thinned, and it stands as both the proof of the achievement and the warning that a wonder unkept becomes stone the world walks through without understanding.
