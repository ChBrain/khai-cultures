# Place: Nan Madol

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Shown

The city of stone raised out on the reef off the southeast shore of Pohnpei: near a hundred made islets built up out of the shallow water and walled and floored with great prismatic basalt logs, stacked log on log like cordwood, cut through by tidal canals you cross by canoe. Seat of the Saudeleur dynasty and the centre of power and ritual for centuries on end, it is the largest ancient monument in all the Pacific islands and one of the wonders of the sea-world, gone silent now and half-drowned, the walls standing on in the mangroves where the dynasty once ruled.

## Offers

A place where the islands show what they once raised - a city out on the sea built with no metal and no wheel, proof that a small ocean people had the organization, the knowledge, and the labor in hand to make a wonder; a seat of ancient power that anchors Pohnpei's place in the nation and the islands' claim to a grandeur all their own, and a site the whole world now counts among its heritage.

## Withheld

The full reading of itself. Nan Madol keeps its mysteries close - how the great stones were ever moved and stacked, the whole story of the dynasty that fell - and it withholds the ease of a living monument: the city is a ruin now, the line back to its builders worn thin, and it stands as both the proof of the achievement and the warning that a wonder left unkept comes down to stone the world walks through without understanding.
