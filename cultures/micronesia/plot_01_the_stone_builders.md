# Plot: the Stone Builders

## Owner

## Cue

Over many generations the master navigators go out and settle the scattered islands of the western Pacific, reading the star paths and the swells to cross a thousand miles of open water and people atolls and high islands a whole sea apart. On Pohnpei a dynasty rises up, and on Yap a people sail off to far quarries for a stone they will turn into money - and out of small islands with no metal, no wheel, no writing, great works begin to take their shape.

## Action

Out on the reef off Pohnpei the builders raise Nan Madol, a city of near a hundred made islets walled and floored with stacked basalt logs, seat of the Saudeleur dynasty and one of the ancient wonders of the Pacific, hauled and fitted by knowledge and labor alone. On Yap the people quarry great limestone discs over on distant Palau, carry them home by canoe across the open ocean at the risk of their lives, and make of them the rai, a money whose worth lives not in the stone but in the remembered history of its making and its owners. The achievement runs two ways: feats of building and feats of memory, both raised up by a seafaring people whose mastery of the open water was the ground under all of it.

## Stage

- [the Nahnmwarki](persona_the_nahnmwarki.md): the Pohnpeian paramount chief whose line of title runs back toward the builders of Nan Madol.
- [the Yapese stone holder](persona_the_yapese_stone_holder.md): the keeper of the rai and the histories that give each disc its worth.
- [Nan Madol](place_nan_madol.md): the basalt city raised out on the reef, the great monument of the ancient achievement.

## Tension

The islands prove that a small and far-off people can raise wonders to match any on earth - a city of stone out on the sea and a money made of memory - by knowledge, by organization, and by a mastery of the open ocean. Yet the wonders carry the question of their own keeping: the city falls to ruin and the dynasty fades away, the knowledge that raised them must be handed on or else lost, and a people who built marvels must decide whether to stand on that inheritance as a living mastery or come down to nothing but the keepers of someone else's stone.
