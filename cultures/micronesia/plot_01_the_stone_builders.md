# Plot: the Stone Builders

## Owner

## Cue

Over many centuries the master navigators settle the scattered islands of the western Pacific, reading the star paths and the swells to cross a thousand miles of open water and people atolls and high islands a sea apart. On Pohnpei a dynasty rises, and on Yap a people sail to far quarries for a stone they will turn into money - and out of small islands without metal, the wheel, or writing, great works begin to take shape.

## Action

On the reef off Pohnpei the builders raise Nan Madol, a city of nearly a hundred artificial islets walled and floored with stacked basalt logs, seat of the Saudeleur dynasty and one of the ancient wonders of the Pacific, hauled and fitted by knowledge and labor alone. On Yap the people quarry great limestone discs on distant Palau, carry them home by canoe across open ocean at the risk of their lives, and make of them the rai, a money whose worth lives not in the stone but in the remembered history of its making and its owners. The achievement is twofold: feats of building and feats of memory, both raised by a seafaring people whose mastery of the open water was the foundation of all of it.

## Stage

- [the Nahnmwarki](persona_the_nahnmwarki.md): the Pohnpeian paramount chief whose line of title reaches back toward the builders of Nan Madol.
- [the Yapese stone holder](persona_the_yapese_stone_holder.md): the keeper of the rai and the histories that give each disc its worth.
- [Nan Madol](place_nan_madol.md): the basalt city raised on the reef, the great monument of the ancient achievement.

## Tension

The islands prove that a small and isolated people can raise wonders to match any on earth - a city of stone on the sea and a money made of memory - by knowledge, organization, and a mastery of the open ocean. Yet the wonders carry the question of their own keeping: the city falls to ruin and the dynasty fades, the knowledge that raised them must be passed on or lost, and a people who built marvels must decide whether to stand on that inheritance as living mastery or become only the keepers of someone else's stone.
