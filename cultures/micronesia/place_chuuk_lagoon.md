# Place: Chuuk Lagoon

## Owner

## Shown

The great lagoon of Chuuk, once known as Truk, a wide ring of reef enclosing a scatter of high islands and a sheltered anchorage broad enough to hold a fleet. Home waters of the Chuukese people and their villages, it became under Japanese rule one of the most important naval bases of the Pacific, and in the war it was struck and broken, leaving ships and aircraft on the lagoon floor. Today the warm clear water draws divers from across the world to the ghost fleet resting in the depths, the wrecks grown over with coral above the living villages of the lagoon.

## Offers

A place where the islands' three faces meet in one water: the deep home of a clan-rooted Chuukese people, the mark of the foreign powers and the war that reached even here, and a present livelihood as the wrecks beneath bring the wider world to the lagoon. It offers the nation both a sober memory and a living resource, the past lying quiet under the keels of the boats that now make their way across it.

## Withheld

The simple peace of home waters. Chuuk Lagoon carries the cost beneath its beauty - a fleet of the war dead resting in its depths, a foreign struggle that filled and then broke its anchorage, and the hard truth that the wonder the divers come for is a grave; it withholds any reading of itself that forgets the lives the lagoon floor holds.
