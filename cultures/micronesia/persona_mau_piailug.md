# Persona: Mau Piailug

## Taxonomy

[the navigators and the Compact](position_the_navigators_and_the_compact.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The master navigator from the tiny atoll of Satawal who refused to let the ancient wayfinding die, who carried in his head the star paths, the swells, and the signs of the open ocean, and who sailed a canoe by knowledge alone across thousands of miles of empty water. Generous against the grain of his own tradition, he broke the rule that the knowledge stay within the island and taught it to outsiders, reviving voyaging across the whole Pacific and giving the world back an art it had almost lost. Satawalese is his mother tongue and the tongue of the navigation itself: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md), and [hears](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) the sea in it, for the star compass and the names of the swells are carried in the words of his island and nowhere else. English he [carries borrowed and partial](process_speaking_borrowed.md), enough to teach across the language line, [following](process_reading_followed.md) what he must of the wider world's speech, the bridge tongue laid over the deep knowledge held in his own. He taught a star compass that has no full word in English and a feel for the swell that no foreign tongue can name, so the deepest of his knowing stayed locked in Satawalese even as he gave it away - the gap at the limit of the tongue is the part of the ocean that cannot cross from his language into another.

## Action

In [the Wave-Finders](plot_04_the_wave_finders.md) he revives the dying art of voyaging and teaches it beyond his island, carrying [the navigating heritage](position_the_navigators_and_the_compact.md) into the present. By giving the knowledge away rather than letting it die with him, he turns a vanishing inheritance into a living one, and proves to a modern, scattering nation that its oldest mastery is not a relic but a way still open.

## Shadow

The gift he gave by breaking the old rule carries its shadow: a knowledge that lived by staying within the island now spread thin across the world, honored everywhere and rooted in fewer places; and the question whether an art kept alive by outsiders' wonder can stay the deep, lived knowing it was, or become a thing demonstrated for the visitor.

## Tell

He steers a canoe across an ocean with no chart and no instrument, holding the whole star path in his head - and gives away, against the law of his own fathers, the knowledge meant to stay home, because he would rather it live in strangers than die with him.
