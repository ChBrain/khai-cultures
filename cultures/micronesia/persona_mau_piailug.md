# Persona: Mau Piailug

## Taxonomy

[the navigators and the Compact](position_the_navigators_and_the_compact.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The master navigator from the tiny atoll of Satawal who would not let the old wayfinding die, who carried in his own head the star paths, the swells, and all the signs of the open ocean, and who sailed a canoe across thousands of miles of empty water by knowing alone. Open-handed against the grain of his own tradition, he broke the rule that the knowledge stay home on the island and taught it out to outsiders, bringing voyaging back to life across the whole Pacific and giving the world back an art it had all but lost. Satawalese is his mother tongue, and the tongue of the navigation itself: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md), and [hears](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) the sea in it, for the star compass and the names of the swells live in the words of his island and nowhere else at all. [English](position_language_en_fm.md) he [carries borrowed and partial](process_speaking_borrowed.md), just enough to teach across the language line, [following](process_reading_followed.md) what he must of the wider world's speech, the bridge tongue laid over the deep knowing held in his own. He taught a star compass that has no whole word in English and a feel for the swell that no foreign tongue can name, so the deepest of his knowing stayed shut up in Satawalese even as he gave it away - the gap at the limit of the tongue is the part of the ocean that cannot cross from his language into another. He belongs to [Micronesian culture](position_culture_micronesian_culture.md) as the navigator's knowledge carried out to the whole Pacific and the world.

## Action

In [the Wave-Finders](plot_04_the_wave_finders.md) he brings the dying art of voyaging back to life and teaches it out beyond his island, carrying [the navigating heritage](position_the_navigators_and_the_compact.md) into the present. By giving the knowledge away rather than letting it die with him, he turns a vanishing inheritance into a living one, and shows a modern, scattering nation that its oldest mastery is no relic but a way still open to it.

## Shadow

The gift he gave by breaking the old rule carries its shadow: a knowledge that lived by staying home on the island now spread thin across the world, honored everywhere and rooted in fewer and fewer places; and the question whether an art kept alive by outsiders' wonder can stay the deep, lived knowing it once was, or come down to a thing put on for the visitor.

## Tell

He steers a canoe clean across an ocean with no chart and no instrument, the whole star path held in his head - and gives away, against the law of his own fathers, the knowledge meant to stay home, because he would rather it live on in strangers than die with him.
