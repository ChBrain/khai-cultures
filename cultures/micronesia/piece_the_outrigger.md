# Piece: the outrigger canoe

## Taxonomy

Parent group: pieces

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Place

On the beach and on the open sea: the voyaging canoe of the islands, a hull cut and lashed from a single great tree, balanced by an outrigger float on a frame to the side, rigged with a woven sail and steered by paddle and the navigator's knowledge. From the small craft of the lagoon to the great ocean-going canoes that carried families across a thousand miles of open water, it is the vessel on which the whole sea-world was settled and held together.

## Load Bearing

It stands for the islands' mastery of the open ocean and the reach of a small-land, great-sea people. The outrigger is what made the navigators' knowledge usable - the craft light enough to be built without metal, stable enough to cross the deep ocean, and seaworthy enough to find and settle islands a sea apart. On it the wayfinding rode, the clans moved, the rai were carried home, and the scattered islands became one people of the water. It is the made thing that turned a vast and dividing ocean into a road. Without this canoe the navigator's star compass would have had nothing to steer, and the islands would have stayed apart.

## Apparent

A slender hull and a float on outstretched booms, wood lashed with cord and rigged with a triangular sail. Plain and spare on the sand, it looks too light for the deep ocean; its weight is in what it crossed and carried, and in the knowledge of the one who sails it, who reads the sea the hull rides on.
