# Piece: the outrigger canoe

## Taxonomy

Parent group: pieces

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Place

On the beach and out on the open sea: the voyaging canoe of the islands, a hull cut and lashed from a single great tree, kept level by an outrigger float on a frame run out to the side, rigged with a woven sail and steered by paddle and the navigator's knowledge. From the small craft of the lagoon to the great ocean-going canoes that carried whole families across a thousand miles of open water, this is the vessel the whole sea-world was settled on and held together by.

## Load Bearing

It stands for the islands' mastery of the open ocean and the reach of a small-land, big-sea people. The outrigger is the thing that made the navigators' knowledge of any use - the craft light enough to build without any metal, steady enough to cross the deep ocean, and seaworthy enough to find and settle islands a whole sea apart. On it the wayfinding rode, the clans moved, the rai came home, and the scattered islands became one people of the water. It is the made thing that turned a vast and dividing ocean into a road you could travel. Without this canoe the navigator's star compass would have had nothing under it to steer, and the islands would have stayed apart.

## Apparent

A slender hull and a float out on its booms, wood lashed with cord and rigged with a triangular sail. Plain and spare up on the sand, it looks too light by far for the deep ocean; its real weight is in what it crossed and carried, and in the knowledge of the one who sails it, who reads the sea the hull rides on.
