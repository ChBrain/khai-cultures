# Schleswig-Holstein: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the
pitch (kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources
behind the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_sh.md](pitch_sh.md) is written from Germany's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture — Schleswig-Holstein is a German Land, so
the national scores are the base, inflected by the region's coastal, border
character — and it names no dimension and carries no number. The scores and
their source are recorded here so the data is on the record without entering the
prose.

| Dimension                     | Germany score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ----------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 35                      |
| Individualism                 | 67                      |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 66                      |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 65                      |
| Long Term Orientation         | 83                      |
| Indulgence                    | 40                      |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published
by Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group. No regional sub-score is
claimed; the national profile is the documented base.

The two defining personas, [Okke](persona_okke.md) and [Merle](persona_merle.md),
are archetypes, not historical individuals: composites that carry the
generational tension between the older coast (the dyke-and-sea life, Low German)
and the younger, connected generation (wind energy, the open border).
