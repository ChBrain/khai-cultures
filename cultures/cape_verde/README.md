# Cabo Verde: a khai culture

Cape Verde staged as a full khai play, ISO CV, language pt (Portuguese is the
official tongue, but Kriolu, the Cape Verdean creole, is the mother tongue of the
whole people). A nation of ten volcanic islands and one creole people scattered
across the sea, as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the
cast, the roles its language and feeling encode, its places, its practices, and
its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_cape_verde.md](play_cape_verde.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_pt.md](pitch_pt.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Cape
  Verde's profile (a warm, collective, hospitable people with a gentle, present
  enjoyment of life and a patient endurance of a hard land) and naming no
  dimension. It is written in a Cape Verdean register, not a Lisbon one, warmly
  coloured by Kriolu.
- **Plots (history):** the settlement and the birth of the creole people, the
  drought and the great emigration, independence (1975), and the morna going out
  to the world with the diaspora - each casting the elements it needs, and the
  colonial period, the trade of that time, and independence all staged soberly
  and taking no political side.
- **Personas:** Cesaria Evora (the barefoot diva), Eugenio Tavares (the poet who
  gave Kriolu the dignity of art), B.Leza (the composer who shaped the modern
  morna), and Amilcar Cabral (handled soberly, the name linked to the road to
  nationhood) as the historic cast, plus one archetype, the sodade singer (the
  one who stays on the island, keeps the home and the language, and sings the
  morna for those gone over the sea). Each is wired to the language engine by the
  language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** sodade (the bittersweet ache of distance and loss, the heart of
  the morna), morabeza (the Cape Verdean warmth and hospitality), and Kriolu (the
  mother tongue, the soul of a people born of the islands).
- **Places:** Mindelo (the musical heart on Sao Vicente, the Porto Grande of
  departures and the carnival), Fogo (the volcano island of the coffee and the
  wine).
- **Processes:** the morna (carrying loss by singing it), cachupa (the national
  dish, the open table made of little).
- **Pieces:** the cavaquinho (the small four-string voice of the morna), grogue
  (the sugarcane spirit of welcome).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the colonial period, the trade of that time, the recurring droughts and
famine, and the road to independence are treated soberly and with dignity, taking
no political side; the staging and the architecture are original work.
