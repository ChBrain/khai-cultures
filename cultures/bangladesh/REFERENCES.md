# Bangladesh: References

The provenance behind this culture: the cultural-profile source that tunes the
pitch (kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical and
heritage sources behind the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Cultural profile behind the pitch

The pitch [pitch_bn.md](pitch_bn.md) is written from a qualitative reading of
Bangladesh's cultural profile and names no dimension and carries no number. The
profile is recorded here in words so it is on the record without entering the
prose.

| Trait                  | Bangladesh profile (qualitative)                                                |
| ---------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Group orientation      | Strongly collective: family, neighbourhood, and the wider kin group come first. |
| Authority              | Hierarchical in form, yet held with an old wariness of distant power.           |
| Expression             | Warm, emotional, and outwardly expressed in song, poetry, and long talk.        |
| Attachment to language | Exceptional: the mother tongue is central to identity, a cause died for.        |
| Resilience             | High: a deep adaptive endurance born of living with rivers and the monsoon.     |

Source: drawn in qualitative terms from widely published descriptions of Bengali
and Bangladeshi culture (the centrality of the Bengali language, collective family
life, and monsoon-delta resilience). No proprietary numeric scores are reproduced;
the reading is the author's own and is offered as the pitch's invisible hand.

## Historical and heritage sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn
from the public historical and cultural record. They are credited here and never
claimed; the staging is original work. The Language Movement of 1952 and the
founding of 1971 are staged soberly on the public record, with no graphic detail,
no numbers, and no political side; the focus stays on the enduring culture, the
language, the rivers, the faith, and the people.
