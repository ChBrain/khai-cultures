# Bangladesh: a khai culture

Bangladesh staged as a full khai play, ISO BD, language bn. The land of rivers
and the great delta, a nation born of its mother tongue, staged as a theatre: its
history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its language and faith
encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single
pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_bangladesh.md](play_bangladesh.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_bn.md](pitch_bn.md), the tonal layer, written from
  Bangladesh's profile (warm and family-first, expressive and emotional, deeply
  attached to the mother tongue, and shaped by a hard-won resilience born of
  living with rivers and the monsoon) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Bengal Renaissance, the Language Movement of 1952
  (Ekushey), the Independence of 1971, and the delta nation that built itself
  after. The 1971 founding is acknowledged soberly and briefly, with dignity, as
  the nation's birth and the price of the mother tongue and freedom, with no
  graphic detail and no political side; the focus stays on the enduring culture.
- **Personas:** Rabindranath Tagore (the poet whose song became the anthem), Kazi
  Nazrul Islam (the rebel poet, the national poet), Lalon Shah (the Baul mystic
  and songmaker), and Sheikh Mujibur Rahman (the founding figure, treated soberly
  on the public record), plus one archetype, the boatman (majhi), who carries the
  river-borne life of the delta. Each is wired to the language engine by the
  language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** the mother tongue (Bengali at the heart of national identity, and
  Ekushey as the founding spirit), the land of rivers (the delta and the
  resilience of living with water), the syncretic faith (the Sufi-Baul traditions
  alongside Islam, and a deep tolerance).
- **Places:** Dhaka (the capital, the Shaheed Minar, the heart of language and
  culture), the Sundarbans (the great mangrove forest, the Royal Bengal tiger, and
  the delta's shield against the storm).
- **Processes:** the Ekushey procession (the barefoot dawn walk to the Shaheed
  Minar), the boat journey (the river-borne rhythm of delta life).
- **Pieces:** jamdani (the handwoven muslin heritage), the nakshi kantha (the
  village embroidered quilt, the quiet art of Bengali women).

## Provenance

The cultural profile behind the pitch and the public-domain historical and
heritage sources are recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The profile never
enters the prose; the pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain
are credited, never claimed; the Language Movement, the 1971 founding, and the
nation's price are treated soberly on the public record, with no atrocity detail
and no political side; the staging and the architecture are original work.
