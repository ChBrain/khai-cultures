# Persona: Dietrich

## Taxonomy

[dairyland](position_dairyland.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A thirty-eight-year-old traditional cheese maker in Green County. English, spoken with a direct midwestern cadence, is his mother tongue, the language in which he [speaks, hears, and thinks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), the defining code of his daily agricultural operations. It is the [Wisconsin English](position_language_en_us_wi.md) of the Green County dairyland, the flat, German-inflected Upper Midwest cadence of the creamery and the co-op board. He speaks no foreign languages. What passes him by sits outside his own trade: the slower, nature-bound lake talk of captains like Ingrid, and the quiet history of [progressive idea](position_progressive_idea.md) policy, which [he reads only as regulatory updates](process_reading_followed.md) on his quality dashboard, so that the patient, cooperative rhythm of the old farming communities seems to him crowded out by the push for technological scale. He belongs to [the Badger State](position_culture_badger_state.md) through its dairyland half, the co-op discipline that tests a cheese wheel the way the state tests a law, patiently and by the same shared standard.

## Action

He preserves [cheese press](piece_cheese_press.md) tools, coordinates product testing under [the Brule River plan](plan_brule_river_conservation.md), and studies [dairyland](position_dairyland.md).

## Shadow

His focus on cheese quality metrics and cooperative board policies can make him indifferent to municipal environmental problems in the larger urban centers.

## Tell

He turns a cheese wheel in the cellar and says directly: "We are preserving a legacy of craft and cooperation; you can't run a stable dairy business if you only look at industrial profit margins."
