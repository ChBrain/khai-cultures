## Name

Wisconsin, the Badger State in the East North Central region of the United States. ISO US-WI, language en.

## Arc

A land of agricultural labor and civic reform, defined by the shores of Lake Michigan and Lake Superior. From the development of dairy cooperatives and the industrial brewing boom in Milwaukee, to the implementation of the progressive Wisconsin Idea linking university research with state laws. A culture that balances the dairyland craftsmanship of its rural counties with the industrial transport of its lake ports, even as it plans the watershed conservation of the Brule River.

## Triggers

**[Plot 1: The Dairy Cooperative Movement 1890](plot_01_dairy_cooperative.md)**

Dairy farmers organize cooperative creameries and cheese factories, standardizing quality and establishing the state's dominant dairy brand.

**[Plot 2: The Milwaukee Brewing Boom 1880](plot_02_brewing_boom.md)**

German immigrant families build massive industrial breweries in Milwaukee, expanding national markets for regional lager beer.

**[Plot 3: Progressive Era Reforms 1901](plot_03_progressive_reforms.md)**

Governor Robert La Follette implements the "Wisconsin Idea," utilizing university research to draft pioneering labor safety and tax laws.

## Stakes

Whether a state defined by intensive dairy farming and industrial ports can successfully manage forest runoff to protect the wild trout waters of the Brule River Basin.
