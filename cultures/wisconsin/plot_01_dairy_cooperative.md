# Plot: The Dairy Cooperative Movement 1890

## Taxonomy

[play_wisconsin](play_wisconsin.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

In the 1890s, dairy farmers across the state establish cooperative creameries, standardizing milk inspections and processing.

## Action

The movement establishes [dairyland](position_dairyland.md) cooperative networks, developing the [cheese press](piece_cheese_press.md) as a symbol of agricultural quality. Dietrich curates historic cheese-making logs in Green County, while Ingrid coordinates lake transport for dairy product distribution.

## Stage

- [dairyland](position_dairyland.md) : the agricultural work, cooperative solidarity, and dairy craftsmanship of rural counties.
- [cheese press](piece_cheese_press.md) : the manufacturing press used to standardize cheese quality.
- [cheese making](process_cheese_making.md) : the traditional culturing and fermentation process of regional cheese makers.

## Tension

Independent creamery owners oppose the cooperatives, leading to intense price wars and competition over local milk supplies.
