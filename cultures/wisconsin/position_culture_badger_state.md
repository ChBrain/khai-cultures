# Position: the Badger State

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

Belonging to a state built on two kinds of craft raised to a civic virtue, the patient labor of the creamery and the deliberate reform of the statehouse. It carries [dairyland](position_dairyland.md), the cooperative discipline of the cheese factory and the family farm that standardized a whole state's produce, and it carries the [progressive idea](position_progressive_idea.md), the Wisconsin Idea that put university research directly into the state's labor and tax laws. It speaks [Wisconsin English](position_language_en_us_wi.md), the flat, German-inflected cadence of the Upper Midwest creamery and the co-op board alike. What it hands to whoever grows up in it is a habit of trusting the cooperative and the expert study over either the lone operator or the untested hunch, on the farm as much as in the legislature.

## Orders

That a cheese wheel and a piece of drafted legislation both answer to the same standard, tested, aged, and judged fit before it is sent out into the world; that the co-op board and the university seminar be trusted the way a neighbor is trusted, as the ordinary way problems get worked out rather than an intrusion on private judgment; that the lake ports and the dairy counties treat each other's slower season, the ice-out and the growing season alike, as the same state's business rather than someone else's problem; and that reform, once studied and adopted, be carried through steadily rather than abandoned at the first hard year.

## Loses

Ground where the rural dairy counties and the industrial lake ports stop reading each other as one state, the creamery's quality metrics indifferent to the port's environmental problems and the shipping schedule blind to the small farm's margins; ground where the progressive faith in expert study curdles into an urban confidence that state-wide mandates always know better than local zoning; and ground in the forest runoff and lake pollution that intensive dairy farming and industrial shipping both leave behind, a debt the Brule River and the waters beyond it are still owed.

## Drives

Drives Wisconsinites to hold the creamery and the statehouse as one inheritance, judging a dairy cooperative's standard and a legislative reform by the same patient, unhurried test of whether it actually holds up once it leaves the cellar or the seminar room. It carries the memory of a state that once let its own university write its labor and tax law into every later argument over how change ought to be made, careful, tested, and cooperative rather than sudden, and it presses the state to remember that the same watershed feeding its dairy pastures and its lake trade is owed the same conservation it asks its farmers and its shippers to practice on the land.
