# Syria: a khai culture

Syria staged as a full khai play, ISO SY, language ar. The cradle of cities and
of the first alphabets, the land of jasmine and the word, set out as a theatre:
its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its language and
craft encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by
a single pitch. The whole staging stands on enduring ground: ancient Damascus
and Aleppo, the first letters, the Umayyad heritage, Arabic poetry and learning,
the famous crafts, the cuisine, and the hospitality.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_syria.md](play_syria.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_ar.md](pitch_ar.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Syria's
  profile (a warm, people-first culture of strong family and group ties, an old
  wariness of the distant and the new, a deep regard for the spoken word and for
  hospitality, and a long-memoried patience) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the cradle and the first alphabet (Ugarit, Ebla, Mari, and
  Palmyra), the Umayyad age and the Great Mosque, the age of the word and the
  height of poetry, and the modern renaissance - each casting the elements it
  needs, all kept on enduring cultural and historical ground.
- **Personas:** al-Mutanabbi (the towering classical poet), Nizar Qabbani (the
  beloved modern Damascene poet), Zenobia (the queen of Palmyra), and Saadallah
  Wannous (the playwright) as the real cast, plus one archetype, the Damascene
  inlayer (the master craftsman who passes the craft from hand to hand). Each is
  wired to the language engine by the relationship to the mother tongue it holds.
- **Positions:** the living ancient cities (the land as a cradle of cities still
  inhabited), the word (the high place of poetry and the Arabic language), the
  craft tradition (the inherited mastery of the hand).
- **Places:** Damascus (the oldest living city, the Great Mosque, the souq, and
  the jasmine) and Aleppo (the ancient citadel, the covered souqs, and the musical
  tradition of the maqam).
- **Processes:** wood inlay (the craft passed from master to apprentice), the souq
  and hospitality (the open door, the offered coffee, the welcomed guest).
- **Pieces:** Damascus brocade (the silk woven with gold that carried the city's
  name into other tongues), the oud (the lord of instruments in Arabic music).
