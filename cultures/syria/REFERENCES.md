# Syria: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical and cultural
sources behind the plots, personas, places, and pieces. The whole culture is kept
on enduring ground and takes no political side.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_ar.md](pitch_ar.md) is written from Syria's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record without
entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Syria score (approx.) |

| Individualism                 | 35                    |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; figures for Syria are estimates and
where unpublished are read from the broader Arab-region profile, which is high on
Power Distance and collectivist (low Individualism).

## Historical and cultural sources

The plots, the real personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn from
the public historical and cultural record. They are credited here and never
claimed; the staging is original work. The whole culture is staged on enduring
ground - ancient cities, the first alphabets, the Umayyad heritage, Arabic poetry
and learning, the crafts, the cuisine, and hospitality - and takes no political
side.

The one archetype, the [Damascene inlayer](persona_almutaim.md), is not a
historical individual but a composite: the master craftsman of the old souq who
inlays wood with shell and brass and passes the craft from hand to hand, carrying
the inherited mastery the culture holds.
