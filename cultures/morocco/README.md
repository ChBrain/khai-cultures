# Morocco: a khai culture

Morocco staged as a full khai play, ISO MA, language ar. A land where an Amazigh
root and an Arab branch meet, between the Atlas and the desert, as a theatre: its
history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its languages and
faith encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a
single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_morocco.md](play_morocco.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_ar.md](pitch_ar.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Morocco's profile (a respect for established institutions and elders, a strong
  pull toward family and community, a measured pace that values patience and
  craft over haste, and a warm, indirect register) and naming no dimension. The
  Arabic is the readable matrix, carrying a Moroccan voice and Darija color
  (wakha, bzzaf, daba, zwin, the high-poured tea).
- **Plots (history):** the coming of Islam, the building of Fez and the
  Qarawiyyin, the age of travel and al-Andalus, and the Kingdom and independence -
  each casting the elements it needs.
- **Personas:** Ibn Battuta (the traveler of Tangier), Fatima al-Fihri (founder
  of the Qarawiyyin), Abu Madyan al-Ghawth (the Amazigh shepherd who became the
  master of the Sufi way), and Mohammed Ben Brahim (the poet of Marrakech) as the
  historic cast, plus one archetype, the tea-pourer (every Moroccan who lifts the
  pot for a guest). Each is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing
  it holds, between Tamazight, Arabic, and Darija.
- **Positions:** the Amazigh root (the deepest layer, honored as living, not
  past), hospitality (the guest as the guest of God), and the craft (mastery of
  the hand, made slowly).
- **Places:** Fez (the city of learning, the Qarawiyyin, and the craft of the
  medina) and Marrakech (the red city, the Koutoubia, and the square Jemaa
  el-Fnaa with its storytellers and Gnawa).
- **Processes:** atay (the mint tea poured high, the pretext of sitting together)
  and the moussem (the festival that gathers the tribes around a saint, the
  harvest, the music, and the horsemanship).
- **Pieces:** the tagine (the shared clay pot cooked slowly) and zellij (the
  hand-cut mosaic, the visible proof of patient craft).
