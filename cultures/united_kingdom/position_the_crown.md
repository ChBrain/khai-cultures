# Position: the Crown

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The power of the keystone, the one figure under whom four nations stand together. The monarch reigns but does not rule; the Crown is the continuity that outlasts every government, the symbol in whose name the law is kept and the union is held.

## Orders

That sovereignty rest formally in the Crown and really in the people through [Westminster](position_westminster.md), the monarch above party and beneath the constitution at once, dignified and dutiful, binding the union by ceremony and consent rather than command.

## Loses

Standing, where the Crown grows remote, costly, or scandalous and forgets that its only ground is the affection and consent of the governed. It loses the moment it sets itself above the nations it exists to join.

## Drives

It is carried by the generation that holds the Crown and its continuity dear, and it is the long thread that ties the four nations into one realm across the centuries.
