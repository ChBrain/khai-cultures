# Piece: the double-decker bus

## Taxonomy

Parent group: pieces

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Place

On the streets of [London](place_london.md) and towns across the country, the red double-decker bus, two storeys tall, carrying everyone together up and down the same stairs.

## Load Bearing

It stands for the plain, shared fabric of British public life: a red icon known the world over, but in truth an ordinary thing, the bus that everyone rides and no one owns. It carries the same spirit as the queue and the NHS, the common provision that levels rank for the length of a journey. Without this everyday red emblem, the British faith in shared public things would want its homeliest token.

## Apparent

A tall red bus with two decks and a stair between them, as much a postcard as a means of getting to work. Nothing about it is grand; it belongs to the daily round, not the parade, even as the world takes it for a symbol.
