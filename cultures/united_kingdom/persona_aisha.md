# Persona: Aisha

## Taxonomy

[class](position_class.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A young woman in her twenties, born in [London](place_london.md) to a family that came from elsewhere, at home in the modern, multicultural Britain the union became. She moves between worlds, a heritage language at the family table and English everywhere else. English [she wears as a matter of course](process_speaking_worn.md), the [common tongue](position_the_common_tongue.md) already on her lip before she reaches for it, and she [reads and writes it](process_reading_followed.md) through every public hour of her day. The heritage language of her parents she keeps for the family table, [catching and speaking it there](process_hearing_caught.md) but never schooled in it: there is the gap of the new Britain, the shared English carries her whole public life while the language of home thins to the kitchen, and she crosses between two worlds in a single afternoon.

## Action

She takes the British understatement and irony as her own idiom, joins [the queue](process_queuing.md) and the round of [a cup of tea](process_tea.md) without thinking, and treats the four-nation union as the ordinary backdrop of a country that has always been made of more than one people. She works the question of [class](position_class.md) as something to be navigated, not simply inherited.

## Shadow

Her ease can hide how recently the welcome was grudging, and how the old grain of class and belonging still asks her, more than others, to prove she is at home. The shared public tongue can quietly press the language of home to the margins.

## Tell

She switches register without a seam, the heritage word at home and the dry British understatement in the street, fluent in both and fully claiming both as hers.
