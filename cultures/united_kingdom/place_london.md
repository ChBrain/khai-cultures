## Shown

The capital on the Thames and the seat of the union, a vast and layered city where the old stones of power stand among the towers of finance and the streets of a hundred peoples. Not one city but many at once, the gathering point of the whole United Kingdom.

## Holds

- [the Acts of Union 1707](plot_02_acts_of_union_1707.md): here the united parliament of Great Britain takes its seat.
- [the Finest Hour 1940](plot_03_finest_hour_1940.md): here the capital takes the Blitz and does not break.
- [the founding of the NHS 1948](plot_04_nhs_1948.md): from here the union-wide health service is founded and run.

## Offers

The place where the union is governed and where [Westminster](position_westminster.md), the Crown, and the institutions of the British state sit together, and where the four nations and the wider world meet in one city.

## Withheld

The claim to be the whole of Britain. London concentrates the union's power and wealth, yet it withholds the lives of the nations beyond it, which the parent culture holds open for England, Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland to fill.
