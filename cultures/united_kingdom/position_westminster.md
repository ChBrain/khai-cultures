# Position: Westminster

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The power of parliamentary sovereignty, the doctrine that the parliament in [London](place_london.md) is the supreme lawmaking authority of the union. Called the mother of parliaments, it is the institution from which the model of representative government spread across much of the world.

## Orders

That the law be made by an elected commons and a debating chamber, the government drawn from and answerable to parliament, power changing hands by vote and custom rather than by force, and the union of the nations governed under one sovereign legislature.

## Loses

Ground, where the centre is felt to ignore the nations it governs, and the sovereignty of the one parliament strains against the claims of Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland to decide their own affairs. It loses when the mother of parliaments stops listening to her children.

## Drives

It is carried by the wartime leadership that held it through the war, and it traces its root to [Magna Carta 1215](plot_01_magna_carta_1215.md), where the crown was first bound beneath the law.
