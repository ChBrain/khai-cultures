# Process: the queue

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

Anyone who arrives and finds another already waiting, and takes their place at the back without being asked. It needs no sign and no marshal: two people and a wait are enough for an orderly line to form itself.

## Direction

Toward fair order without authority: each takes their turn in the order they came, the first served first, the line policed by nothing but a shared sense of fairness and the quiet disapproval of anyone who jumps it. It is not mere patience but fairness made visible.

## Lever

The faith in fair play drives it, and the conviction that taking one's turn is the decent thing. Against it stand haste and self-importance, and the queue meets both with a level glance and an unmoving line.

## Echo

The queue makes the British sense of fairness audible as a habit of the body. To wait one's turn is to keep faith with [class](position_class.md) levelled for a moment into a single line, the order of arrival the only rank that holds, as [Margaret](persona_margaret.md) takes her place without being told.
