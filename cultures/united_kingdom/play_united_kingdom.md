# Play: the United Kingdom

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the house that holds this production and answers for it.

## Name

The United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland, an island state off the north-west coast of Europe, a union of four nations under one crown and one parliament. ISO GB, language en. This is the union-wide parent culture; England, Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland follow as its constituent nation cultures.

## Arc

An island that turned an offshore kingdom into a union of nations and then into a global power, and that learned to govern itself by binding the crown to the law rather than by overthrowing it. From Magna Carta to the union of the parliaments, from standing alone in 1940 to building a welfare state for all, the British story is one of reform without revolution, of continuity carried through change. Under it runs a temperament of understatement and irony, a faith in fair play and the orderly queue, and an island self-sufficiency that looks outward to the world even as it keeps its own counsel.

## Triggers

**[Plot 1: Magna Carta, 1215](plot_01_magna_carta_1215.md)**

At Runnymede the barons force King John to set his seal to a charter that binds even the king beneath the law; [Westminster](position_westminster.md) and the rule of law trace their root to this moment.

**[Plot 2: The Acts of Union, 1707](plot_02_acts_of_union_1707.md)**

The parliaments of England and Scotland join to make the kingdom of Great Britain, and [Westminster](position_westminster.md) in [London](place_london.md) becomes the single seat of the new union state.

**[Plot 3: The Finest Hour, 1940](plot_03_finest_hour_1940.md)**

With Europe fallen, the island stands alone under the Blitz; [Winston Churchill](persona_churchill.md) gives the resolve words, and [London](place_london.md) takes the bombing and does not break.

**[Plot 4: The Founding of the NHS, 1948](plot_04_nhs_1948.md)**

Out of the war comes a welfare settlement, and from [London](place_london.md) the union builds a health service free to all at the point of use, an institution shared across the four nations.

## Stakes

Whether a union of four nations, held together by law and crown rather than by force, can keep its faith in fair play, reserve, and the common good as the world it once ruled changes around it and the bonds of the union are tested from within.
