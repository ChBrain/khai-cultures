# Plot: Magna Carta 1215

## Taxonomy

[play_united_kingdom](play_united_kingdom.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

King John has taxed and ruled at his own will until the barons will bear it no longer. In the meadow at Runnymede, near [London](place_london.md), they bring an army and a charter and will not leave without his seal.

## Action

John sets his seal to the Great Charter, conceding that even the king is bound by the law and that no free man may be imprisoned or stripped of his rights but by lawful judgement. The charter is broken and reissued within years, yet the principle outlives the quarrel: it becomes the root from which the rule of law and, in time, [Westminster](position_westminster.md) draw their authority.

## Stage

- [London](place_london.md): the kingdom's seat, beside which the charter is sealed at Runnymede.
- [Westminster](position_westminster.md): the parliamentary tradition that will trace its root to this binding of the crown.

## Tension

The charter binds the king, but it is wrung from him by force and serves the barons before the people. The promise that no one stands above the law is set down centuries before most can claim it, and the long work of the union will be to make the principle real for all, not only for the great.
