# Plan: the union

## Taxonomy

A mandate: the deliberate, ongoing effort to hold four nations in one state, the union the Acts of 1707 began and that every generation since has had to re-make or watch fray.

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Direction

To keep England, Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland one kingdom by consent rather than compulsion: bound by a shared Crown, a common Parliament, the [common tongue](position_language_en_gb.md), and common goods like the NHS, against the centrifugal pull of each nation and the strains of leaving Europe.

## Orders

- To Westminster: hold the union by consent, devolve real power, and govern for all four nations and not one.
- To the nations: find common cause enough to stay, or settle a parting by agreement.
- To the Crown and the shared institutions: be the things all four still hold together when much else pulls apart.

## Implementation

The Acts of Union and the shared Crown and Parliament; devolution to Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland; the NHS and the welfare state as union-wide goods; and the strains of the 2014 referendum and the Brexit vote.

## Targets

- [x] Four nations were bound into one multinational state and held together for three centuries.
- [x] Devolution gave each nation its own voice while keeping the union whole.
- [W] Brexit and the independence questions strained the union as never before; consent no longer feels assured.
- [ ] Hold the union by consent and common cause, or let it part in peace, but never keep it by mere inertia.
