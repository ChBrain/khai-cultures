# Process: a cup of tea

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

Anyone who fills the kettle at the first sign of trouble or comfort, and offers a cup before a word is said. It asks nothing but a kettle and the wish to put someone at ease, and it answers nearly every occasion.

## Direction

Toward steadiness and quiet care: the making and offering of tea as the British answer to crisis, grief, welcome, and the ordinary pause, a small ritual that buys a moment of calm and says what reserve leaves unspoken.

## Lever

The instinct to keep calm and carry on drives it, and the wish to comfort without making a fuss. Against it stands every drama and panic, and the cup of tea meets them with the level remark that we shall manage and a brew set down on the table.

## Echo

The cup of tea makes British understatement into a kindness one can hold. It is care offered without display, the way [Margaret](persona_margaret.md) reaches for the kettle before she reaches for words, turning reserve into a quiet act of looking after one another.
