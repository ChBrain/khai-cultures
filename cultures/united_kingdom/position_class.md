# Position: class

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The power of an unwritten order of rank that runs through the whole of British life: the long grain of class, read in accent, schooling, and manner, sorting people by where they are taken to belong before they have said a word.

## Orders

That one knows one's place and reads everyone else's, the codes of speech and dress and bearing doing the work that no law sets down. It shapes opportunity and ease quietly, an order felt everywhere and admitted nowhere.

## Loses

Ground, where mobility, money, and a more mixed Britain blur the old lines, and where the pretence that class no longer matters meets the stubborn fact that it still does. It loses its grip as the codes that signalled rank lose their certainty.

## Drives

It is carried by the stage that set lord and labourer before the same audience, and it is worked daily by whoever navigates rather than inherits their place in it.
