# United Kingdom: a khai culture

The United Kingdom staged as a full khai play, ISO GB, language en. A union of
four nations as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast,
the roles its public life encodes, its places, its practices, and its defining
objects, all tuned by a single pitch. This is the union-wide parent culture;
England, Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland follow as its constituent nation
cultures.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_united_kingdom.md](play_united_kingdom.md), the culture
  itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_uk.md](pitch_uk.md), the Hofstede layer, written from the
  UK's profile (understated, ironic, fair play, reform over revolution) and
  naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** Magna Carta (1215), the Acts of Union (1707), the Finest
  Hour (1940), and the founding of the NHS (1948) - each casting the elements it
  needs, all union-wide rather than nation-specific.
- **Personas:** Winston Churchill and William Shakespeare as the historic cast,
  plus two defining archetypes, Margaret (older, the post-war welfare generation,
  the stiff upper lip) and Aisha (younger, modern multicultural Britain). Each is
  wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** the Crown (the union's keystone), Westminster (parliamentary
  sovereignty, the mother of parliaments), class (the British class system).
- **Places:** London (the capital and seat of the union), Dover (the white
  cliffs, the island's edge).
- **Processes:** the queue, a cup of tea.
- **Pieces:** the Union Jack (the union of the nations' crosses), the
  double-decker bus.
