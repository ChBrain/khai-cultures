# Persona: Margaret

## Taxonomy

[the Crown](position_the_crown.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A woman in her late seventies, of the post-war generation that grew up with rationing and the new welfare state. Thrifty, courteous, and undemonstrative, she keeps strong feeling behind a level voice and meets trouble with the remark that it could be worse. She [speaks, hears, and thinks in](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) her mother tongue plainly and understated, keeping strong feeling behind a level voice and meeting trouble with the remark that it could be worse. There is the gap: her [common tongue](position_language_en_gb.md) says least about what matters most, the louder, more confessional speech of a newer Britain strikes her as laying it on too thick, and a whole [class](position_class.md) and post-war generation are written into the very flatness of her voice.

## Action

She takes her place in [the queue](process_queuing.md) without being told, sets the kettle going at every crisis and comfort with [a cup of tea](process_tea.md), and holds the [the Crown](position_the_crown.md) and the NHS dear as the things that held the country together. She keeps calm and carries on, and minds her place without resentment.

## Shadow

Her reserve can curdle into a stiffness that mistakes silence for strength, and her sense of place can make her wary of those who do not know theirs. The stiff upper lip that carried a generation can also leave much unsaid that ought to be spoken.

## Tell

She waves off thanks and praise alike, makes light of what she has endured, and reaches for the kettle before she reaches for words.
