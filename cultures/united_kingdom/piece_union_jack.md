# Piece: the Union Jack

## Taxonomy

Parent group: pieces

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Place

Flown over public buildings and stitched onto everything from bunting to luggage, the red, white, and blue flag whose crosses are the crosses of the nations laid one over another.

## Load Bearing

It stands for the union made visible: the flag is literally the combined crosses of the nations joined under one crown, the design itself a picture of four peoples held in one device. It dates from the unions of the crowns and parliaments, and without this single overlaid emblem the idea of the union would lack its plainest image. It carries both the pride of the union and the long argument over what each nation gives up to it.

## Apparent

A single flag of overlaid crosses on a blue field, red and white over blue, seen on a flagpole as readily as on a souvenir. Nothing about it hides its making: the joins of the crosses are there to be read by anyone who looks.

## Yearbook

- Formed by laying the crosses of the nations one over another as the crowns and parliaments joined.
- Flown over the institutions of the union and worn as the readiest sign of Britishness.
- Read by some as the emblem of the union and by others as a question still open about it.
