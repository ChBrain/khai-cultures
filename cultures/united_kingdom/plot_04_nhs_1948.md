# Plot: the founding of the NHS 1948

## Taxonomy

[play_united_kingdom](play_united_kingdom.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

The war is won but the country is exhausted and in debt. A wartime promise of a fairer peace presses on the nation: that the sacrifice of all should be answered by care for all.

## Action

On the fifth of July the National Health Service opens, run from [London](place_london.md) and reaching every corner of the four nations: medical care free at the point of use, paid for by all and owed to all alike. It is the keystone of the post-war welfare settlement and the clearest act of [Westminster](position_westminster.md)'s belief that the union exists to look after its own, regardless of means.

## Stage

- [London](place_london.md): the seat from which the union-wide service is founded and run.
- [Westminster](position_westminster.md): the parliament whose post-war settlement makes care a common right.

## Tension

A health service free to all is a bold claim on a country with little money, and its promise is forever costlier than its purse. It becomes the institution the British hold dearest and defend most fiercely, the proof that the union can share its burdens as well as its history.
