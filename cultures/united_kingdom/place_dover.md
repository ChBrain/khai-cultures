# Place: Dover

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Shown

The port at the narrowest of the Channel, beneath the white cliffs that face the continent across twenty-odd miles of water. The island's edge and its gateway at once: the first sight of Britain from the sea and the last of it to those who leave.

## Holds

- [the Finest Hour 1940](plot_03_finest_hour_1940.md): the front line of the island in the war, the cliffs facing an occupied continent across the narrow water.

## Offers

The image of the island state itself: the moat of sea that made the United Kingdom both apart from Europe and reachable by it, the edge where self-sufficiency and an outward look meet.

## Withheld

The comfort of an open border. Dover marks the line where the island ends, holding back the easy continuity with the continent and standing for the sense of a country that is of Europe and yet set apart from it.
