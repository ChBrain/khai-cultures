# Position: the Lone Star State

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

Membership in a state that was once its own republic and has never quite let that memory go: the rugged individualism and endurance of [the frontier legacy](position_frontier.md), the proud self-reliance of [Texan independence](position_independence.md), and a single star that stands for a people who consider themselves apart even while belonging to the Union. It carries [Texan English](position_language_en_us_tx.md) as its natural voice, a wide horizon that runs from the cattle range to the oil derrick to the launch pad, and a readiness to bet everything on the next venture, whether cattle, oil, or silicon.

## Orders

That one meets hardship with resilience rather than complaint, that private initiative and personal risk-taking are honored over waiting for help from outside, and that state and local self-governance are defended before federal authority is asked to step in. It orders a pride in Texan identity carried openly, in speech, in symbol, and in the stubborn insistence that Texas can do for itself what other states ask Washington to do for them.

## Loses

Ground when the suspicion of outside regulation curdles into unchecked pollution, environmental degradation, or corporate exploitation with no one left to answer for it, and credibility when the isolation it prizes, in its power grid, in its infrastructure, buckles under crisis and needs the very federal help it swore off. It frays where the frontier's tolerance for speculation rewards the fast venture over the durable one.

## Drives

It drives Texans to meet the open range, the oil boom, and the space frontier with the same rugged self-reliance, certain that risk met head-on is the surest road to opportunity. It carries the rancher's handshake and the software engineer's independent grid alike, pushing the state to answer its own crises, from drought to blackout, with its own hands first, and to wear its lone star as a promise that Texas stands on its own footing even inside a larger union.
