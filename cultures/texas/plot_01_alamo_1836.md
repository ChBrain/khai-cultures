# Plot: The Texas Revolution 1836

## Cue

In the spring of 1836, Texan settlers rebel against the centralized government of Mexican President Santa Anna, culminating in the siege of the Alamo.

## Action

The defense of [the Alamo](place_alamo.md) and the subsequent victory at San Jacinto establish [Texan independence](position_independence.md) as a foundational myth. The struggle cements the Lone Star identity. Carter retells the stories of the defenders to visitors, honoring their rugged spirit.

## Stage

- [Texan independence](position_independence.md) : the sovereign spirit and pride of the former republic.
- [the Alamo](place_alamo.md) : the historic mission that serves as a monument to sacrifice and liberty.
- [cowboy boots](piece_cowboy_boots.md) : the traditional footwear that inherits the rugged leather-craft of the frontier.

## Tension

The conflict deepens divisions over land rights, resulting in the displacement of Tejano and Native American populations who had long inhabited the region.
