# Persona: Elena

## Taxonomy

[Texan independence](position_independence.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A thirty-four-year-old software engineer in Austin. Spanish, spoken in her family home in San Antonio, is her mother tongue, the language in which she [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and shares her memories. English [she wears as an overgarment](process_speaking_worn.md) for her technical work, coding, and pitching projects in the Austin tech scene. It is the [Texan English](position_language_en_us_tx.md) she carries as a borrowed frontier tongue over her native Spanish, never her own first tongue. What she can't quite hear over the hum of her own work is the old, slow ranching stories of Carter and the traditional frontier values, which reach her [only like a distant echo](process_hearing_followed.md), her focus locked instead on the digital demands of the independent power grid and the global silicon market. She answers to [the Lone Star State](position_culture_lone_star_state.md), which shapes her drive to build an independent grid, and she carries [la mexicanidad](../mexico/position_culture_mexicanidad.md) of her family home alongside it, never quite one or the other.

## Action

She coordinates grid software for [the Texas power grid plan](plan_texas_power_grid.md), conducts tests in [Houston Space Center](place_houston_space_center.md), and studies [the frontier legacy](position_frontier.md).

## Shadow

Her focus on technical independence can lead her to ignore the social and economic disparities affecting communities that cannot afford private microgrid installations.

## Tell

She looks at her laptop screen and says confidently: "We have the tech and the sun to run our own grid, we just need to build it with enough resilience to survive the next big freeze."
