# Persona: Elena

## Taxonomy

[Texan independence](position_independence.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A thirty-four-year-old software engineer in Austin. Spanish, spoken in her family home in San Antonio, is her mother tongue, the language in which she [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and shares her memories. English [she wears as an overgarment](process_speaking_worn.md) for her technical work, coding, and pitching projects in the Austin tech scene. It is the [Texan English](position_language_en_us_tx.md) she carries as a borrowed frontier tongue over her native Spanish, never her own first tongue. Yet the loss lies in the seam: The old, slow ranching stories of Carter and the traditional frontier values [she hears only like a distant echo](process_hearing_followed.md), as her focus remains locked on the digital demands of the independent power grid and the global silicon market.

## Action

She coordinates grid software for [the Texas power grid plan](plan_texas_power_grid.md), conducts tests in [Houston Space Center](place_houston_space_center.md), and studies [the frontier legacy](position_frontier.md).

## Shadow

Her focus on technical independence can lead her to ignore the social and economic disparities affecting communities that cannot afford private microgrid installations.

## Tell

She looks at her laptop screen and says confidently: "We have the tech and the sun to run our own grid, we just need to build it with enough resilience to survive the next big freeze."
