# Persona: Chris Patten

## Taxonomy

[one country, two systems](position_one_country_two_systems.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The last governor of a colony he was sent to hand back rather than to keep, a career British politician and former chairman of the Conservative Party who arrived in Hong Kong in 1992 already marked as the man who would preside over the end of a hundred and fifty years of empire. English was the only tongue he ever needed to govern in: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in [British English](../united_kingdom/position_language_en_gb.md) as his native ground, the language of Whitehall, of the dispatch box, and of every negotiation he conducted with Beijing and with the colony's own legislature; and he picked up, over his five years in Government House, no more than a scattering of [Cantonese](position_language_yue_hk.md), [caught by ear](process_hearing_caught.md) at walkabouts and street stalls, enough to charm a crowd with a greeting but never enough to argue a point in it. Unlike the city he governed, he did not stay to become one of its own: he holds [British culture](../united_kingdom/position_culture_britishness.md), the home he returned to and the one he never stopped answering to even while he sat in Government House.

## Action

In [the handover](plot_03_handover.md) he is the last governor who must actually hand the colony over, pushing through democratic reforms to the Legislative Council in his final years against fierce objection from Beijing, then standing at the ceremony in the rain in 1997 to watch the flag come down on the empire's last great Asian possession. By spending his governorship arguing for the widest possible autonomy and the furthest possible democracy the colony could carry into the new arrangement, he tries to load as much of the promise as he can into [one country, two systems](position_one_country_two_systems.md) before the negotiating leverage of empire runs out for good.

## Shadow

The reforms he pushed through in his last years, expanding the electorate for the Legislative Council against Beijing's explicit objection, earned him a string of insults in the Chinese state press and left the mainland resolved to dismantle the council he had reformed the moment the handover was complete, so that much of what he tried to entrench did not survive the transition he oversaw. He left the city he governed for five years without ever quite becoming of it, and returned to a British political life that had moved on without him.

## Tell

At the handover ceremony in the rain on the night of 30 June 1997, he stood on the deck of the royal yacht Britannia holding his youngest daughter as the last Union flag over Hong Kong was lowered, visibly close to tears in front of the cameras of the entire world.
