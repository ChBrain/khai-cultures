# Position: the Lion Rock spirit

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The grassroots creed of the tenement city, named for the crag that stands over the crowded roofs of Kowloon: that people who arrived with nothing can climb by their own grit, thrift, and cunning, side by side, asking no one for pity. It holds the memory of the refugee decades, when whole families lived in a single room and turned a borrowed sewing machine or a street stall into a living, and it carries the faith that hard work and a quick wit are enough to make a life where none was owed.

## Orders

That a person work, save, and endure without complaint, treating hardship as the ordinary weather of life and self-reliance as the first virtue; that neighbours in the same cramped estate look out for one another and share the climb, since none of them had anyone else. It orders a respect for the striver over the born-lucky, and a suspicion of anyone who expects the world to provide what they have not earned.

## Loses

Ground when the ladder it promised is pulled up: when property and rent rise so high that no amount of grit lets the young buy the flat their grandparents' toil built, the creed curdles into a cruelty that blames the ones who cannot climb. It loses ground when the memory of the struggle fades in the generations born into comfort, and when "work harder" becomes an excuse for a city to ignore how steep it has made its own slope.

## Drives

Drives the city up from the refugee shacks and rooftop schools to the factories and then the towers, powering the long climb from a port of the destitute to one of the richest cities on earth. It lives wherever someone builds a livelihood out of almost nothing and refuses to be pitied, and it drives the Hongkonger to prize self-reliance, endurance, and the dignity of the self-made - even as the city it built grows steep enough to test whether the promise beneath the Lion Rock still holds.
