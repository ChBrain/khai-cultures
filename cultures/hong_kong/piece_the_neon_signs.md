# Piece: the neon signs

## Taxonomy

Parent group: pieces

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Place

Overhanging the narrow streets of [Kowloon](place_kowloon.md), Mong Kok, Yau Ma Tei, Tsim Sha Tsui, and the older commercial blocks of the Island, thousands of signboards once hung out over the traffic on iron brackets: restaurants, pawnshops, mahjong parlours, tailors, each sign a wall of glowing tubes bent into Chinese characters beside their English name, stacked one above another until the whole street seemed to be lit by its own advertising rather than by lamps.

## Load Bearing

It stands for the bilingual, mercantile, crowded street the city built for itself: every sign an act of small commerce lit up and hung out over the public way for anyone passing to read, in Chinese first and English beside it, the two tongues of the place sharing the same glowing board without either one giving way. As the streets narrowed under their own advertising, the forest of neon carried the ordinary hum of shopkeepers competing for the same passing crowd, and it fits a city that turned even its need to be noticed into a shared, glowing skyline of its own, hung not over the harbour but over the pavement. Now that safety rules and cheaper LED boards are taking the old tubes down one by one, what is being lost is not only light but a whole visible layer of the small businesses that once spoke, each in its own glow, from over the street.

## Apparent

A crowded overhang of glass tubing bent into strokes of Chinese and Latin letters, red, blue, green, pink, buzzing and flickering above the traffic and the crowd below. In itself each sign is only a shopkeeper's advertisement; the weight is in the accumulation, street after street of them stacked and competing, so that the ordinary business of eating, betting, and getting a suit made is what, seen from below at night, made the city's skyline glow.

## Yearbook

- Risen through the mid-twentieth century as shopkeepers competed for attention on narrow, crowded commercial streets.
- Become an internationally recognised image of the city, photographed and filmed as shorthand for its dense, bilingual, nocturnal energy.
- Now dwindling under safety regulations and the switch to cheaper LED lighting, with old signs taken down faster than new ones are approved.
