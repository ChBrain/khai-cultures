# Place: Victoria Harbour

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Shown

The deep-water channel between Hong Kong Island and the Kowloon peninsula, one of the finest natural harbours on earth: the skyline of towers massed on the Island's edge, the small green-and-white ferries crossing back and forth, the container ships and junks sharing the same water, and the reclaimed waterfront pushing further out into the channel with every decade. This is the water the free port was built on, and the water on whose edge, in 1997, the flag was lowered on one order and raised on another.

## Holds

- [the cession and the free port](plot_01_cession.md): the barren rock ceded island by island and lease by lease, turned into the free harbour where the trade of China met the world on this water.
- [the handover (1997)](plot_03_handover.md): the colony returned to China at a ceremony on the harbour's edge, the last governor sailing out past the skyline he had governed.

## Offers

A place where the whole arc of the city can be read from the water itself - the free port that started it, the skyline that grew from it, and the handover that changed who answered for it. It offers the view every visitor is given and every Hongkonger carries: two shores facing each other across a crossing that made a barren rock into a byword for a certain kind of nimble, crowded, glittering city.

## Withheld

The illusion that the harbour was always this shape. The water withholds how much of the shore is not shore at all but land made by reclaiming the channel piece by piece, narrowing the very thing that made the city, and it withholds how easily a free port drawn to one flag can be handed, by treaty and by ceremony, to another, the ships and the skyline going on as if the water itself had not just changed hands.
