# Hong Kong: a khai culture

Hong Kong staged as a full khai play, ISO HK, working language en. A barren rock
turned into the harbour where East meets West, as a theatre: its history as the
plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its languages and communities
encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a
single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_hong_kong.md](play_hong_kong.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Hong
  Kong's profile (a strong pragmatism and long-term orientation, a sharp
  restraint, and a striving, hierarchical, deal-making cast).
- **Plots:** the cession and the free port, the refugees and the factories, the
  handover of 1997, and the long contested testing of its autonomy.
- **Personas:** the refugee industrialist and the young Hongkonger in
  generational tension, with Bruce Lee, Louis Cha (Jin Yong), Anita Mui, and the
  last governor Chris Patten.
- **Positions:** the Hongkonger (the culture), the Lion Rock spirit, one country
  two systems, the entrepot, and the languages - Cantonese and Hong Kong English.
- **Places:** Victoria Harbour and Kowloon.
- **Processes:** yum cha and the races.
- **Pieces:** the Star Ferry and the neon signs.
