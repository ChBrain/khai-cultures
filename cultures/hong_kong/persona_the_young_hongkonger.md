# Persona: the young Hongkonger

## Taxonomy

[the Hongkonger](position_culture_hongkonger.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

Born in the years around or after the handover, growing up inside a promise whose fifty years were already running before she understood what was being promised, she is the generation asked to inherit a home whose terms keep changing under her feet. Schooled in both official tongues and fluent in the unofficial third, she [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in [Cantonese](position_language_yue_hk.md) as the language of her home, her friends, and her own mind; she keeps [Hong Kong English](position_language_en_hk.md) [worn](process_speaking_worn.md) close at hand for the office, the university seminar, and a world she expects to have to leave for or persuade; and she has [Putonghua](../china/position_language_zh_cn.md), [caught by ear](process_hearing_caught.md) from television and the classroom and [followed on the page](process_reading_followed.md) in the textbook and the street sign, close enough to get by in without ever quite calling it her own. She holds the Hongkonger not as an inheritance taken for granted but as an identity she has had to argue for, in the street and to herself, long before she was old enough to vote on what it meant.

## Action

In [the contested autonomy](plot_04_the_contested_autonomy.md) she comes of age inside the unresolved question of what the city is and what it is still allowed to be, carrying a placard or a degree or a one-way ticket out, depending on the year and the mood of the streets. By living the whole of her adult life inside the test of the fifty-year promise, she turns the abstract bargain of [one country, two systems](position_one_country_two_systems.md) into a daily, personal reckoning of how much of the home she was given is still hers to keep.

## Shadow

The question she was born into does not resolve itself by her growing older, and the vigilance it demands, over what changed this year, what she can still say, whether to stay or to go, wears at a life that was owed the ordinary business of just living it. Friends leave for Vancouver or London or Taipei and the group chat empties out a little more each year, and the ones who stay carry a grief for a city that has not died but keeps not quite being the one they grew up in.

## Tell

She keeps a folder of old photographs and voice notes from friends who have since emigrated, updated every time another name changes its time zone, a whole city's worth of goodbyes filed under people she still calls, technically, her closest friends.
