# the Republic of the Congo: a khai culture

The Republic of the Congo (Congo-Brazzaville) staged as a full khai play, ISO CG,
language fr. A small nation large in style, as a theatre: its history as the plot
line, its people as the cast, the roles its language and faith encode, its places,
its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch. The prose is
written in a Congolese (Brazzaville) French voice, warmed by Lingala and Kituba.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_republic_of_congo.md](play_republic_of_congo.md), the culture
  itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_fr.md](pitch_fr.md), the Hofstede layer, written from the
  Congolese profile (a strong pull toward the family and the group, respect for
  elders and hierarchy, a present- and ambiance-loving warmth, and dignity sought
  through style) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the kingdoms of Kongo and Loango, Brazzaville on the river,
  the golden age of the rumba, and la SAPE and literary Brazzaville - each casting
  the elements it needs. Recent and past conflict are out of scope.
- **Personas:** the writers Sony Labou Tansi and Tchicaya U Tam'si, the rumba
  great Pamelo Mounk'a, and the founding sapeur Stervos Niarcos as the historic
  cast, plus one archetype, le sapeur (the everyday man of the quarter who makes
  elegance his dignity). Each is wired to the language engine by the
  language-crossing it holds, between French and Lingala or Kikongo.
- **Positions:** le fleuve (the Congo River and the twin capitals facing each
  other across the water), la rumba (the shared cradle of the great music), and la
  SAPE (elegance as art, philosophy, and dignity).
- **Places:** Brazzaville (the capital on the right bank, facing Kinshasa, city of
  letters and elegance) and Pointe-Noire (the ocean city and the old Loango
  coast).
- **Processes:** la descente des sapeurs (the Sunday parade of elegance) and la
  nuit de rumba (the shared night of music and dance).
- **Pieces:** le costume du sapeur (the suit as dignity sewn by hand) and la
  guitare de la rumba (the soul of the great music).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain cultural sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the nation is staged from within its own self-understanding, taking no
political side and dwelling on no conflict; the staging and the architecture are
original work.
