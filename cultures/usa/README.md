# the United States: a khai culture

The United States staged as a full khai play, ISO US, language en. A nation
founded on a creed and peopled by the world, as a theatre: its history as the
plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its language and faith encode, its
places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_usa.md](play_usa.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md), the Hofstede layer, written from the
  United States' profile (intense individualism and self-reliance, optimism and
  indulgence, a present- and achievement-focused drive, openness and bigness, and
  a deep suspicion of authority) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Founding (1776), the frontier and Manifest Destiny,
  the Civil War (1861-1865), and the American Century - each casting the elements
  it needs, and each staging the gap between the creed and the reality honestly.
- **Personas:** Thomas Jefferson, Abraham Lincoln, and Martin Luther King Jr. as
  the historic cast, plus two defining archetypes, Hank (older, heartland,
  self-reliant, the frontier grown old) and Maya (younger, the immigrant drawn by
  the promise whose mother tongue thins into English). Each is wired to the
  language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** the creed (the propositional nation held by an idea, and the
  defining gap between that creed and the reality of slavery and race), the
  frontier (rugged individualism and the self-made man of the moving West),
  exceptionalism (the city on a hill with an errand to the world).
- **Places:** Washington, D.C. (the creed in marble and the seat of power), New
  York City (the immigrant gateway, the melting pot, and the marketplace).
- **Processes:** the road trip (the frontier turned into a highway, freedom of
  movement and reinvention), Thanksgiving (the national feast that binds the many
  into one).
- **Pieces:** the Stars and Stripes (the near-sacred civic emblem of a nation
  held by an idea), blue jeans (the frontier workwear become the world's most-worn
  garment of American freedom).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; slavery, race, and the cost of expansion to Native peoples are treated
soberly on the public record; the staging and the architecture are original work.
