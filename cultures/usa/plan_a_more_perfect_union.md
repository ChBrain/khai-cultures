# Plan: a more perfect union

## Taxonomy

The open mandate the nation set itself in the preamble to its Constitution: the endless work of closing the gap [the creed](position_the_creed.md) holds open between the promise and the reality, of holding the many of e pluribus unum together across deep division, of keeping the second chance and the fair shake of [the frontier](position_the_frontier.md) real for each new arrival, and of carrying the burden and the temptation of [exceptionalism](position_exceptionalism.md) without losing the republic back home.

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Direction

Pay down the debt of the creed - to make equality real in fact and not just on paper, across race and across the widening gulf between rich and poor; keep the union whole, holding the many together when faction and division pull it apart; renew the promise for the newcomer, keeping the golden door open and the Dream worth the crossing; and carry the nation's power in the world as a charge to be answered, not a free hand, resisting the temptation to mistake might for right.

## Orders

- To the newcomer like [Maya](persona_maya.md): claim the promise, give it your all, become American, and remake the country once more out of many.
- To the citizen like [Hank](persona_hank.md): hold your own ground and your liberty, and remember the country you owe and that owes you.
- To the nation: keep paying the debt of the creed and keep the union whole, never mistaking the words for the deed nor saying it for doing it.

## Implementation

A republic two and a half centuries old, the world's oldest written constitution still on the books, that has closed the gap in great strides - abolition, the vote extended, the civil rights won - and left it open still, in the persistence of racial and economic inequality, in deep partisan division, in the strain of a power that must lead the world without lording it over the world, and in the standing question of whether a nation held by an idea can keep making the idea new.

## Targets

- [x] Founded on a written creed and a federal union, the first nation born of an idea.
- [x] Slavery abolished and the creed written into law by the Civil War and its amendments.
- [x] The civil rights movement forced the promise of equality into the nation's law and conscience.
- [W] Racial and economic inequality persist beneath the equality the creed proclaims.
- [W] Deep division strains the union the many are meant to hold together.
- [ ] Renew the promise and the fair shake for each new wave the world sends to its shores.
- [ ] Carry the nation's power in the world as a charge, not a free hand, without losing the republic back home.
