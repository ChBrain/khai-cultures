# Plot: the frontier and Manifest Destiny

## Cue

Through the nineteenth century the young republic looks West, to a continent its people believe is theirs to take. Settlers, traders, and dreamers push past the mountains in wagons, drawn by free land and a fresh start, and politicians give the hunger a name: Manifest Destiny, the conviction that the nation is fated to span the continent from sea to sea.

## Action

Wave after wave moves West - the Louisiana Purchase, the Oregon Trail, the gold rushes, the homesteads, the railroad driving its iron line across the plains. A country of self-made men is forged on the moving edge, where a person can begin again and answer to no one. And the same expansion falls with terrible cost on the Native peoples who already lived on the land: treaties broken, nations removed along trails of death, and ways of life destroyed to make the open country open. The frontier builds the American myth and exacts its price in full from those it displaced.

## Tension

The West makes the country what it is - restless, individual, sure that no fence is final and no station fixed - and the freedom and the fresh start are real. Yet the open land was never empty, and the promise given to the settler was taken from the peoples already there. The myth of the self-made man rests on a continent that was not made but seized, and the glory and the grievous cost are bound together in the same march.
