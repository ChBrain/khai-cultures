# Play: the United States

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the house that holds this staging and answers for it.

## Name

The United States of America, a continental republic spanning North America from the Atlantic to the Pacific, between Canada and Mexico, fifty states bound into one federal union. ISO US, language en, with no official tongue on the books. A nation that holds itself together not by blood or soil but by an idea, and bets the whole house on it.

## Arc

A nation unlike the old ones: not a people who slowly became a state, but a state proclaimed on an idea and then peopled by the whole world. It declared in 1776 that all men are created equal and broke from an empire to build a republic on that creed - and it built that republic while holding millions in slavery and clearing a continent of the peoples who already lived on it. The gap between the creed and the practice is the engine that drives the whole story. The nation lit out West across the continent on the promise of free land and a fresh start, made itself a country of self-made men who pulled themselves up by their own bootstraps, and then tore itself apart in a civil war over slavery, redeeming the creed in blood and binding the wound only partly. It rose in a single century to the richest and strongest power on earth, the city on a hill the whole world watched, while its own Black citizens marched to hold it to the words it had written down. Drawing the world to its shores, it turned a hundred mother tongues into English inside a generation, and made its own English the language the whole earth has to learn. Under it all runs one nerve: a restless, hopeful, contradicted people who reckon the promise is real and figure the country is never finished, only perfected, with the next chance always out past the horizon.

## Triggers

**[Plot 1: the Founding (1776)](plot_01_founding_1776.md)**

In 1776 a generation of colonists declares independence and writes [the creed](position_the_creed.md) into being - "all men are created equal" - then frames a Constitution that opens "We the People," led by men like [Thomas Jefferson](persona_jefferson.md), founding a republic on an idea and going for broke on it, while leaving slavery in place.

**[Plot 2: the frontier and Manifest Destiny](plot_02_the_frontier.md)**

Through the nineteenth century the nation moves West across the continent, [the frontier](position_the_frontier.md) making a country of self-made men on the promise of free land and a fresh start - bought at a grievous cost to the Native peoples who already lived there.

**[Plot 3: the Civil War (1861-1865)](plot_03_civil_war.md)**

In 1861 the union splits over slavery and goes to war with itself; [Abraham Lincoln](persona_lincoln.md) holds the union together, frees the enslaved, and redeems [the creed](position_the_creed.md) in blood, squaring up at last to the nation's original sin.

**[Plot 4: the American Century](plot_04_american_century.md)**

In the twentieth century the United States rises to the world's leading power, its reach an [exceptionalism](position_exceptionalism.md) felt the whole world over, while at home [Martin Luther King Jr.](persona_mlk.md) marches to hold the nation to its own creed and give the excluded a fair shake.

## Stakes

Whether a nation held together by an idea rather than by blood or soil can keep closing the gap between [the creed](position_the_creed.md) it proclaimed and the reality it has lived - in race, in inequality, in the cost it once exacted - can hold [e pluribus unum](position_the_creed.md) together across deep division, can renew the promise and the fair shake for each new wave the world sends to its shores, and can carry the burden of leading the world without losing the republic back home, or whether the contradiction at its heart finally pulls it apart.
