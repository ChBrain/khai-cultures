# Persona: Maya

## Taxonomy

[exceptionalism](position_exceptionalism.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A young woman of the first or second generation, drawn or carried here by the promise of the shining city, working and studying to make the new life her parents crossed the world for. Hopeful, hard-driving, grateful, and torn, she is the newcomer the country was built to receive, the e pluribus unum still happening. Her heritage Spanish she still has and feels slipping: she [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [hears](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) the mother tongue at her parents' table, but [reads it only haltingly](process_reading_caught.md), catching what she can. English she [carries as her working tongue](process_speaking_carried.md) and [reads and follows without effort](process_reading_followed.md), the language of school, of work, of the dream, and the tongue her own children will think in and answer her in. The Dream she came for is paid in the mother tongue: English swallows the home language within a generation, so her parents' Spanish thins in her mouth and will be gone from her children's, the melting pot doing its quiet work - the price of belonging exacted at the limit of the tongue, where the old language is the coin you spend to buy the new country.

## Action

In [the American Century](plot_04_american_century.md) she is the proof of [exceptionalism](position_exceptionalism.md), the world arriving at the city on a hill to be remade and to remake it, the largest and rising heritage of a Latino future the country is becoming. She carries the promise forward by claiming it - making herself American, and making America once more out of many.

## Shadow

The melting pot that lets her in can also erase what she brought: the home language lost not by force but by the slow pull of belonging, until a grandchild cannot speak to a grandmother, and the gift of the many is quietly reduced to the one. The promise that takes everyone can ask, in payment, that they cease to be what they were.

## Tell

She answers her mother in English and is answered in Spanish, the table running in two tongues at once - and she dreams, when she dreams of arriving, in the language she is losing.
