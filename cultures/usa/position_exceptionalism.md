# Position: exceptionalism

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The sense of a chosen nation with an errand to the world: the city on a hill, watched by all, a fresh start for mankind and a light to the nations. It runs from the Puritans who came to build a holy commonwealth, through the Manifest Destiny that read the continent as the nation's God-given right, to the superpower that won the World Wars, reached the moon, and sent its movies, music, money, and creed into every corner of the earth. Bound up in it is the country's deep, restless religiosity and the promise that draws the world to its shores - and the temptation to mistake power for virtue and the mission for a free hand to remake others.

## Orders

That the nation has a purpose beyond itself, a calling to carry liberty and the promise to the world and to lead by example; that it must be better than the old nations, a refuge and a model; and that its strength, its reach, and its faith are no accident but a charge to be answered.

## Loses

Ground when the sense of mission curdles into the conviction that the nation can do no wrong, that being chosen gives it a pass for what it does abroad and at home; ground, too, when the city on a hill stops keeping its own house in order, so the watching world sees the gap between the sermon and the street, and the light it claimed to be dims in others' eyes.

## Drives

Lives in the presidency that called the union "the last best hope of earth" and asked whether a nation so conceived could endure, and in every newcomer drawn from across the world by the promise of the shining city. It drives the United States to see itself as a nation with an errand, and to be judged - by the world and by itself - against the full height of its own claim.
