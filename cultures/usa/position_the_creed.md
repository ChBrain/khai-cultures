# Position: the creed

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The proposition that the nation is held together not by blood, soil, or king, but by an idea: that all men are created equal and endowed with unalienable rights, and that just government rests on consent. It is the only nation born of a written creed and peopled afterward from all the world, e pluribus unum, one out of many. And it is a creed founded in contradiction: proclaimed by men who held others in slavery, written for "all" while the enslaved were owned and counted as fractions, the gap between the words and the practice present from the first day.

## Orders

That the country is defined by an idea anyone can adopt, not by ancestry, so the immigrant and the native-born are equally American; that the creed is a standard the nation can be measured and found wanting against, and held to; and that the work of the republic is the endless task of closing the distance between what was written and what is lived.

## Loses

Ground when the gap is denied rather than closed - when the creed becomes a comfort rather than a demand, recited to flatter the nation instead of to indict it; and ground, too, to the cynicism that, seeing the founders' hypocrisy, throws out the promise with the men, and forgets that the same words that excused them also armed those who came to claim them.

## Drives

Lives in [Thomas Jefferson](persona_jefferson.md), who wrote the creed of universal liberty while holding others enslaved, the contradiction embodied, and in [Martin Luther King Jr.](persona_mlk.md), who held the nation to its own words and called the Declaration a promissory note still unpaid. It drives the United States to keep measuring itself against the promise it made and to keep paying the debt down.
