# Plot: the Civil War (1861-1865)

## Taxonomy

[play_usa](play_usa.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

By 1860 the contradiction written into the Founding can no longer hold. A nation half slave and half free is pulled apart over whether slavery may spread, and over whether the union is a compact that states may walk out of. When [Abraham Lincoln](persona_lincoln.md) is elected, the slaveholding South secedes and forms a confederacy, and the country goes to war with itself.

## Action

For four years, from 1861 to 1865, the bloodiest war in the nation's history is fought on its own soil, and over six hundred thousand die. Midway through, Lincoln issues the Emancipation Proclamation and turns the war into a war for freedom; at Gettysburg he calls for a new birth of freedom. The union prevails, slavery is abolished, and the Constitution is amended to make the enslaved free and equal under law. The creed is redeemed in blood and the nation squares up at last to its original sin - though the war's end only opens the long, unfinished job of making the freedom real.

## Stage

- [Abraham Lincoln](persona_lincoln.md): the president who holds the union together and turns the war into the creed's redemption.
- [the creed](position_the_creed.md): the promise of equality, paid down in blood and written at last into law.
- [exceptionalism](position_exceptionalism.md): the union as the last best hope of earth, tested by whether it can endure.
- [Washington, D.C.](place_washington.md): the capital held against the rebellion and the seat from which the union is preserved.

## Tension

The nation finally pays the first great instalment on the promise of 1776, ending slavery and proving that a republic conceived in liberty can endure. Yet it is paid in a slaughter of its own people, and the victory binds the wound only partly: the freed are freed on paper before they are free in life, and the rest of the debt - of equality made real - is left standing on the books for the century to come.
