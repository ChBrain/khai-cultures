# the United States: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md) is written from the United States' profile on
Geert Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no
number. The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record
without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | United States score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ----------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 40                            |
| Individualism                 | 91                            |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 62                            |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 46                            |
| Long Term Orientation         | 26                            |
| Indulgence                    | 68                            |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; the United States' Individualism
score is among the highest in the published data.

## Historical sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn
from the public historical record. They are credited here and never claimed; the
staging is original work. The contradiction at the heart of the story - slavery,
the Civil War, segregation, the Black freedom struggle, and the cost of westward
expansion to Native American peoples - is staged soberly on the public record.

The two defining personas, [Hank](persona_hank.md) and [Maya](persona_maya.md),
are archetypes, not historical individuals: composites that carry the tension
between the self-reliant heir of the frontier (the heartland, the porch, the gun,
the church, the open country) and the newcomer drawn by the promise (the immigrant
Dream and the mother tongue swallowed by English within a generation).
