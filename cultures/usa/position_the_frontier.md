# Position: the frontier

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The moving edge of the West and the cult of the self-made man it bred: the conviction that beyond the next ridge lies free land and a fresh start, that a person can light out, pull themselves up, reinvent themselves, and answer to no one. From the first colonists to the pioneers in their wagons, the open continent taught self-reliance, restlessness, and a suspicion of the settled and the powerful - and it taught the world's highest individualism. The frontier promised a new beginning to anyone game enough to go, and exacted that promise from the Native peoples whose ground it crossed.

## Orders

That a person makes themselves by their own bootstraps and owes their station to no one, so independence is the first virtue and dependence the first shame; that the answer to a closed door is to move on and start over somewhere new; and that the country is a land of self-made men where grit and nerve, not birth, decide what a life becomes.

## Loses

Ground when self-reliance hardens into a refusal to see what the country owes and was built on - the land taken, the labor stolen - and when the myth of the man who made it all on his own hides the help, the luck, and the others; ground, too, when the restless start-over curdles into rootlessness, and the freedom to move becomes the freedom to walk out on every bond behind.

## Drives

Lives in [Hank](persona_hank.md), the heartland man who needs no one, holds his own ground, and meets the world on his own terms. It drives the United States toward the open road and the second chance, the faith that no fence is final and no one is born your better, the restless individualism that is the country's glory and its loneliness both - go West, start over, give it a shot.
