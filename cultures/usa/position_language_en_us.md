# Position: American English

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

American English pronounces the r in every position, car, hard, teacher, where the tongue that gave it its name long ago dropped that same sound at the end of a syllable, and it turns nouns into verbs on the spot, to google, to text, to parent, to gift, a productivity for coining new verbs that most languages restrict far more tightly. It also compounds and clips words fast into shorthand, incentivize, onboarding, deliverable, building new vocabulary faster than the grammar around it changes at all.

## Orders

It forces the r to be pronounced wherever it is written, car and better and further, a rule the flattened national broadcast standard, General American, imposes on every regional accent that reaches the airwaves, smoothing away the local vowel underneath. And, like all English, it forces strict word order in place of case endings, and collapses every degree of address into the one flat you, so distance and closeness must be built by name and title rather than by the pronoun itself.

## Loses

It loses regional accent under the pressure of General American, the flattened, rhotic standard that broadcasting and national schooling reward, treating the non-rhotic drawl of the old South or the dropped r of the Northeast as a deviation rather than a variant just as old. And a noun turned into a verb overnight, to adult, to input, rarely arrives with the settled, agreed usage an older verb carries, so the new coinage can grate on an ear that has not yet adopted it, even while it fills a real gap in the sentence.

## Drives

It trains whoever speaks it to reach first for the r wherever the spelling puts one, so its absence in another English marks that speaker as a visitor to this tongue rather than a native of it. It also trains the mind to treat any noun as a verb waiting to happen, gifting a friend, parenting a child, incentivizing a team, so the speaker never quite stops listening for the next noun the language can put to work as an action.
