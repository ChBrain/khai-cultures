# Persona: Martin Luther King Jr.

## Taxonomy

[the creed](position_the_creed.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The Baptist preacher who led the civil rights movement, who answered the nation's broken promise with nonviolence and conscience, marched on Washington, dreamed aloud that his children would be judged by their character, and was assassinated for it. Eloquent, courageous, and morally relentless, he held the country to the very creed it had written down and would not let it forget the debt or duck the bill. [American English](position_language_en_us.md) he has whole and turns into a weapon of conscience: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in his mother tongue, and he [drafts](process_writing_drafted.md) it - from a Birmingham jail cell, on the page - into argument no one could answer. The cadence of the Black church and the King James Bible he [carries as his own](process_speaking_carried.md), having [read scripture and the prophets deeply](process_reading_followed.md), and he fuses it with the words of the founders. He held America to its own creed in the borrowed English of the men who enslaved his forebears, the tongue the founders wrote in and the tongue slavery had imposed, and demanded payment of the promise in the nation's own words - calling the Declaration a promissory note come due - so that the gap at the limit of the tongue became the gap between a written promise and an unpaid debt. He belongs to [the American way](position_culture_american_way.md) as the voice that held the nation to the very words it wrote down and would not let it forget the debt.

## Action

In [the American Century](plot_04_american_century.md) he claims [the creed](position_the_creed.md) at home while the nation leads abroad, marching to make the words of 1776 true for those the country had always shut out. He turns the Declaration into a bill the nation must finally make good on, and forces the gap between the promise and the practice out into the open for all the watching world to see.

## Shadow

The promise he held the country to is one it can hear and applaud without ever footing the bill - the dream quoted, carved in stone, and emptied out; the prophet honored with a holiday while the debt he named goes on unpaid, the comfort of the words mistaken once more for the doing of the deed.

## Tell

He stands on the steps where Lincoln sits in marble and reads the founders' own words back to the nation as an unpaid bill - meeting hatred with love and the billy club with conscience, and asking only that the country be what it said it was.
