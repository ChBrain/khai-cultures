# Persona: Thomas Jefferson

## Taxonomy

[the creed](position_the_creed.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

Planter, polymath, and author of the Declaration of Independence, who set down the words on which the nation rests - "all men are created equal" - and who held more than six hundred human beings in slavery across his life: the contradiction at the country's heart, embodied in one man. Learned, restless, idealistic, and evasive, he handed the world the creed of universal liberty and never once freed himself of the practice that gave it the lie. English he has whole and shaping: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md), and [writes](process_writing_drafted.md) in his mother tongue with such force that he drafted the nation's founding scripture in it - the very English of the empire he was breaking from. The French of the Enlightenment he [carries almost as his own](process_speaking_carried.md), reads its philosophers and [follows them on the page](process_reading_followed.md), and brings their ideas home to the new republic. He wrote the creed of liberty for all men in the tongue of the king he defied, while the people he enslaved - whose freedom that creed denied - spoke African tongues he never wrote down and never learned, and so the gap at the limit of the tongue runs straight through his own house.

## Action

In [the Founding (1776)](plot_01_founding_1776.md) he writes [the creed](position_the_creed.md) into being and proclaims the equality the nation will forever be measured against. By setting the promise down in plain words, he hands the United States the standard it will spend its whole history struggling to meet - and, by living against it, the gap it will spend that history struggling to close.

## Shadow

The mind that can write a perfect creed and live a contradicting life is the nation's own shadow: the temptation to mistake the noble word for the deed, to recite the promise as if saying it were the same as keeping it, and to let the beauty of what was written paper over the ugliness of what was done.

## Tell

He writes "all men are created equal" in a house run by the men he owns, and means the words even so - leaving the country a promise larger than himself, and a debt his own life makes plain.
