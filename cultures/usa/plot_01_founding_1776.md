# Plot: the Founding (1776)

## Taxonomy

[play_usa](play_usa.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

By the 1770s thirteen British colonies along the Atlantic seaboard chafe under a distant crown that taxes them without so much as a by-your-leave. In Philadelphia a Continental Congress gathers, and a committee asks [Thomas Jefferson](persona_jefferson.md) to set down, in plain words, the reasons a free people may break from their king.

## Action

On the fourth of July, 1776, the Congress adopts the Declaration of Independence, proclaiming that all men are created equal and that government rests on the consent of the governed - and then goes for broke, winning a long revolution to make the words true. A decade later the framers gather in the same city to write a Constitution that opens "We the People" and binds the states into one federal union. A republic is founded on an idea, the first nation born of a written creed - and slavery is left standing inside it, baked into the very compromises that made the union possible.

## Stage

- [Thomas Jefferson](persona_jefferson.md): the author who sets down the creed of equality the nation will be measured against.
- [the creed](position_the_creed.md): the proposition, proclaimed at the Founding, that the nation is held by an idea.
- [Washington, D.C.](place_washington.md): the capital later raised to house the institutions the framers designed.

## Tension

The nation is born of the noblest words yet written into a state's founding - and born already in contradiction, proclaiming liberty for all while holding millions enslaved. The same document that arms every later claim of justice was written by men who lived against it, so the country starts out with both the promise and the gap, and the whole story after is the long struggle to close it.
