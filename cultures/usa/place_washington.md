# Place: Washington, D.C.

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Shown

The capital raised on the Potomac to be the seat of the republic, a city of white marble and broad avenues built to house the government the framers drew up. The Capitol, the White House, and the long green Mall lined with monuments - the obelisk to Washington, the temple to Jefferson, and the great seated Lincoln before whom King would stand. The creed set in stone, and the machinery of power humming beneath it.

## Holds

- [the Civil War (1861-1865)](plot_03_civil_war.md): the capital held against the rebellion, the seat from which the union is preserved.
- [the American Century](plot_04_american_century.md): the seat of the superpower and the ground of the March on Washington, where King speaks before the Lincoln Memorial.

## Offers

A place where the nation governs itself and shows itself its own ideals in marble; a stage where the creed is both enshrined and fought over, where laws are made, presidents inaugurated, and citizens march to hold the government to the very words carved on its walls.

## Withheld

The innocence of mere power. Washington carries the gap between the creed in its marble and the practice out on its streets - a capital that long denied its own residents a vote, that housed the slaveholding republic, and that reminds the nation the words are only as good as the keeping of them.
