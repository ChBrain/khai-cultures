# Pitch: the American tone

## Taxonomy

The key in which the staging of [the United States](play_usa.md) is played. It changes nothing of what happens - not the Founding, not the frontier, not the Civil War, not the American Century. It sets only how it sounds, the register in which the whole show is given.

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Tenor

Played big, bright, and forward: a people who back the individual above all, who figure anyone can pull themselves up and make themselves anew, and who reckon the future will beat the past hands down. Optimistic, outgoing, openly emotional, generous with praise and quick to say what they feel, they give the bold and the self-starting a fair shake, compete out in the open and keep score, and trust the deal over the title and the person over the office. Authority is held loose and questioned freely; the boss goes by a first name, and a stranger is just a friend you have not met yet. Under all the cheer runs a tireless drive to win, to do, to get ahead and cash in the reward now - because everyone knows there is no such thing as a free lunch.

## Undertow

Under the optimism runs a loneliness in the self-reliance, the cost of a country that asks each person to make it on their own and figures the one who does not has only himself to blame. There is restlessness with no rest in it, the present chased so hard the long view is lost over the horizon, and a faith in the fresh start so deep it can flat refuse to reckon with the past. And under the confidence runs the suspicion of authority gone brittle, the bigness gone to bluster, and the open competition curdled into a winner-take-all where the ones who came up short are left behind.

## Nerve

It strikes the chord of the second chance and the open road: the conviction that you are not your birth, that the door is open, that hard work pays off and tomorrow can be made over. It plays on the pride of building something from nothing, the thrill of the new and the big and the free, the American Dream that says go for broke and the country will give you a shot, and the deep belief that no one is your better and no future is set in stone. It plays, too, on the ache underneath - of being measured by what you make of yourself, alone.

## Echo

What is left is the image of a people forever reinventing themselves: hopeful, individual, loud, and unfinished, suspicious of every master and dead sure that the country and the self are both still being built. A nation that runs on the promise that you can begin again, and that for all the gaps between what it says and what it does, it reckons the promise is real and worth the chase.
