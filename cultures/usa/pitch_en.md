# Pitch: the American tone

## Taxonomy

The key in which the staging of [the United States](play_usa.md) is played. It changes nothing of what happens - not the Founding, not the frontier, not the Civil War, not the American Century. It sets only how it sounds, the register in which all of it is given.

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Tenor

Played big, bright, and forward: a people who believe in the individual above all, that anyone can make themselves anew, and that the future will be better than the past. Optimistic, outgoing, openly emotional, generous with praise and quick to say what they feel, they reward the bold and the self-starting, compete openly and keep score, and trust the deal over the title and the person over the office. Authority is held lightly and questioned freely; the boss is a first name, and a stranger is a friend not yet met. Under the cheer runs a tireless drive to win, to do, to get ahead and enjoy the reward now.

## Undertow

Under the optimism runs a loneliness in the self-reliance, the cost of a country that asks each person to make it alone and treats the one who does not as having only themselves to blame. There is restlessness without rest, the present chased so hard the long view is lost, and a faith in the fresh start so deep it can refuse to reckon with the past. And under the confidence runs the suspicion of authority turned brittle, the bigness turned to bluster, and the open competition curdled into a winner-take-all where the losers are left behind.

## Nerve

It strikes the chord of the second chance and the open road: the conviction that you are not your birth, that the door is open, that effort is rewarded and tomorrow can be remade. It plays on the pride of building something from nothing, the thrill of the new and the big and the free, and the deep American belief that no one is your better and no future is fixed. It plays, too, on the ache underneath - of being measured by what you make of yourself, alone.

## Echo

What is left is the image of a people forever reinventing themselves: hopeful, individual, loud, and unfinished, suspicious of every master and sure that the country and the self are both still being made. A nation that runs on the promise that you can begin again, and that for all the gaps between what it says and what it does, it believes the promise is real and worth the chase.
