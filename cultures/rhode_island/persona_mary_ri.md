# Persona: Mary

## Taxonomy

[charter liberty](position_charter_liberty.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A fifty-two-year-old marine biologist in Providence. English, spoken with a warm drawl, is her mother tongue, the language in which she [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and writes her daily estuary reports. It is the [Rhode Island English](position_language_en_us_ri.md) of Providence, the warm, non-rhotic bay drawl of the estuary labs. She speaks no foreign languages. Yet the loss lies in the seam: The fast, technology-oriented talk of boat builders like William and the digital demands of harbor boards [she hears only like a distant echo](process_hearing_followed.md), feeling that the patient, law-protecting rhythm of municipal charter preservation is treated as secondary to corporate yacht sales and coastal tourism. She belongs to [the Ocean State](position_culture_ocean_state.md), and carries its charter liberty and its bay stewardship both, the same patient rhythm she brings to every estuary report.

## Action

She preserves [quahog shell](piece_quahog_shell.md) records, coordinates field conservation under [the Narragansett Bay plan](plan_narragansett_bay_estuary_conservation.md), and studies [bay stewardship](position_bay_stewardship.md).

## Shadow

Her pride in traditional town meetings and local property rights can make her suspicious of state-wide zoning and environmental housing mandates, fearing they will restrict family property use.

## Tell

She looks at a historic colonial charter and says thoughtfully: "The charter has its own precedents; you can't protect community stability if you're only focused on digital speed and yacht sales metrics."
