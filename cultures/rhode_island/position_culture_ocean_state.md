# Position: the Ocean State

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

Belonging to the smallest state in the union and the one most thoroughly shaped by its own coastline, every town within reach of the tidal salt estuaries of Narragansett Bay, so that being a Rhode Islander means carrying two inheritances that were never meant to sit easy together. It carries [charter liberty](position_charter_liberty.md), the religious tolerance and legal independence Roger Williams won in 1663 when he secured a royal charter founded on the separation of church and state, and it carries [bay stewardship](position_bay_stewardship.md), the sailboat craftsmanship and estuary conservation of the boatyards and the clam flats. It speaks [Rhode Island English](position_language_en_us_ri.md), the non-rhotic bay drawl that drops its r and lends one back where the spelling has none. What it hands to whoever grows up in it is a careful, courteous, exacting temperament, a respect for the town meeting and the historic record, and a pride in family labor and technical competence that Newport's Gilded Age mansions and Narragansett Bay's working boatyards both still answer to.

## Orders

That one respect local town regulation and the historic commission before any state-wide mandate, arguing a position out in the open meeting the way the charter itself was argued into being; that a person's word, a shellfish harvest limit, a boatyard safety check, be honored as a promise kept to the community and not a rule endured for its own sake; that craft and conservation be treated as the same discipline, the same care that shapes a sailboat hull owed also to the clam beds it sails over; and that family heritage and hands-on competence count for more, in the end, than a passing fashion in yacht sales or resort development.

## Loses

Ground at its own geographic seam, between the rapidly expanding coastal resort towns and the working-class urban corridors that never shared in the same boom, a divide that turns every zoning hearing and watershed regulation into a contest between state-led development and local protective instinct. It loses ground, too, when the patient rhythm of charter preservation and municipal self-determination is read as mere delay by those chasing corporate yacht sales and tourism revenue, and when heavy coastal wastewater runoff closes the shellfish beds that generations of boatwrights and clammers alike have depended on, fracturing a fishing network built on trust between neighbors.

## Drives

Whoever it raises to measure a town by whether it keeps its own charter and its own water clean, not by how fast it can grow. It drives the boatbuilder to read a sailboat hull the way the town moderator reads a bylaw, as a thing built to outlast the builder, and it drives the marine biologist and the shipwright alike to treat the bay itself as a covenant owed to the next generation of clammers and sailors. And it drives Rhode Island to keep testing whether an estuary state can execute its water plans fast enough to protect the breeding beds that fed it long before the first summer cottage went up on the shore.
