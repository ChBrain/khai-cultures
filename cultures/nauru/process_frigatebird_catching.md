# Process: Frigatebird Catching

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

Frigatebird catching begins with a single bird lured within reach off the reef, then tamed to the hand over weeks of patient handling until it will return on its own. It is not initiated for food alone, though a tamed frigatebird does help its keeper fish; it is initiated, just as much, to prove a skill the tribes still measure standing by.

## Direction

Toward custom and daily life at once: the catching and keeping of a frigatebird is a food practice and a custom in the same motion, taught hand to hand rather than written down. It directs a young catcher toward patience above all, since no shortcut tames a wild bird faster than time will.

## Lever

The lever is patience itself, applied to a wild animal that has no reason to trust a human hand until it is given one, again and again, without hurry. Nothing replaces the time this takes, which is exactly why the finished bird still marks a catcher's standing rather than merely their effort.

## Echo

Frigatebird catching echoes into [the Twelve Tribes](position_the_twelve_tribes.md): a custom this old, held this closely by hand, is one of the clearest surviving marks of tribal order the island has left. It echoes too into [the Frigatebird Catcher](persona_the_frigatebird_catcher.md), whose skill with the bird carries the custom forward exactly as it was carried to them.
