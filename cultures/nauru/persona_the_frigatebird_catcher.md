# Persona: The Frigatebird Catcher

## Taxonomy

[the Twelve Tribes](position_the_twelve_tribes.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The catcher and keeper of the tamed frigatebird, a skill still practised and still a mark of standing among the tribes. Frigatebird catching is patient work: luring the bird within reach, taming it by hand over weeks, and keeping it afterward as a companion that also fishes. Nauruan is the catcher's mother tongue: they [speak](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), [think](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md), and [hear](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) in it, the tongue the whole practice is named and taught in. English reaches the catcher only [caught](process_reading_caught.md) secondhand, from a sign or a visitor's question, never as the language the skill itself is passed down in. The custom of frigatebird catching, and the Nauruan it is taught in, both stay largely off the page, so the practice survives person to person or not at all, in a way an English record could never quite substitute for. The catcher belongs to [Nauruan Culture](position_culture_nauruan_culture.md), the frigatebird and the custom around it being one of the clearest surviving marks of that culture.

## Action

In [The Island Before](plot_01_the_island_before.md) the catcher trains and keeps a frigatebird as a mark of standing, embodying [the Twelve Tribes](position_the_twelve_tribes.md). The catcher's skill outlasts every administration that has passed over the island since.

## Shadow

A skill taught only hand to hand can vanish in a single generation that does not take it up, and the same insistence on doing it the old way, patiently, without shortcuts, can look to an outsider like stubbornness rather than what it actually is, which is the only way the skill has ever survived.

## Tell

The catcher trains a frigatebird the same unhurried way it has always been trained, in Nauruan, from an elder who learned it the same way, and treats the bird's return to the hand as proof that some things this island does have not yet needed English to explain them.
