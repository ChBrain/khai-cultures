# Place: Topside

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Shown

Topside is Nauru's interior, and most of it is bare coral pinnacle now, stripped of the soil and forest the phosphate mining took with the rock. It is the scarred heart of the island, the clearest single place where the boom that made Nauru rich can be seen still costing the republic something, decades after the mining that caused it.

## Holds

- [The Phosphate Republic](plot_03_the_phosphate_republic.md): the ground where the cantilever worked and the boom's rock was pulled up.
- [After the Money](plot_04_after_the_money.md): the same ground, mined through, left as the debt the boom did not mention at the time.

## Offers

Topside offers, bluntly, the plainest evidence of what the phosphate boom actually cost: a moonscape of pinnacles where the rock was extracted rather than the polished figures the trust fund once promised. It also offers the republic its clearest site for rehabilitation, whatever comes after the phosphate has to start on this ground or nowhere.

## Withheld

Topside offers no peace about the mining, and no easy answer either. It sits as the unresolved account between what the boom paid the republic and what the land itself lost, and the republic's plans for what comes after phosphate all have to reckon with this same scarred ground before they can reckon with anything else.
