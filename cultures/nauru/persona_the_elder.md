# Persona: The Elder

## Taxonomy

[the Twelve Tribes](position_the_twelve_tribes.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The keeper of custom for one of the twelve tribes, holding the genealogy, the land right, and the standing that decides who may speak for whom at a gathering. Nauruan is the elder's mother tongue: they [speak](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), [think](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md), and [hear](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) in it, the tongue that carries custom, not a translation of it. What the elder rarely does is write it down; Nauruan custom has always been held in memory and in speech passed hand to hand, and the language itself has stayed thin on the page even as it stays full in the mouth. English only reaches the elder [caught](process_hearing_caught.md) secondhand, at a council meeting or from a form that needs a signature, never as the tongue the custom itself is judged in. That imbalance, a mother tongue spoken every day and almost never written, against an official tongue, [Nauruan English](position_language_en_nr.md), that is barely spoken at home yet holds every record, is the elder's daily fact of life. The elder belongs to [Nauruan Culture](position_culture_nauruan_culture.md), custom and family being what that culture rests on.

## Action

In [The Island Before](plot_01_the_island_before.md) the elder holds the custom and the faith of the tribes, embodying [the Twelve Tribes](position_the_twelve_tribes.md). The elder keeps standing, land right, and custom in order across a tribe too small to survive a serious quarrel over any of the three.

## Shadow

An elder who holds custom entirely by memory can also lose it that way, one generation's silence away from a gap nothing written can fill, and the same authority that keeps custom from drifting can also keep it from ever being questioned or renewed.

## Tell

The elder answers a dispute over land or standing in Nauruan, from memory, while the paperwork that follows gets written in English by someone else, a small daily reminder of which tongue the custom lives in and which tongue merely records it.
