# Nauru: a khai culture

Nauru staged as a full khai play, ISO NR, written entirely in English: Nauruan and
English are co-official, and English is in practice the tongue this republic
writes in, even where Nauruan is still what most households speak. The world's
smallest republic, an island of twelve tribes, staged as a theatre: its history as
the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its language and faith encode, its
places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house that
holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its Estate;
a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_nauru.md](play_nauru.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_nauru.md](pitch_nauru.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Nauru's profile (a close-knit and tribal island life, faith filled and plain
  spoken, the tight community of the small island, and a deep resilience) and
  naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the island before (the settlement, the twelve tribes,
  frigatebird catching and the lagoon, and faith), occupations and war (the German
  and Australian administration, the Second World War, the Japanese occupation and
  the deportation to Chuuk, all staged soberly), the phosphate republic
  (independence in 1968, DeRoburt and the phosphate rights struggle, and the rich
  years), and after the money (the bust, the devastated Topside, reinvention, Angam
  survival, and modern Nauru), each casting the elements it needs, and each staging
  the phosphate devastation, the wartime deportation, and the modern
  processing-centre era soberly and neutrally.
- **Personas:** Hammer DeRoburt and Bernard Dowiyogo as the historic cast, plus
  three defining archetypes: the miner (the phosphate miner of the cantilever and
  Topside), the frigatebird catcher (the catcher and keeper of the tamed
  frigatebird), and the elder (keeper of the twelve tribes' custom). Each is wired
  to the language engine by the language crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** the twelve tribes (the clans and the star, faith and custom),
  phosphate and the price (the boom, the bust, and the devastated land), the
  smallest republic (sovereignty and reinvention), Nauruan English (the co-official
  variety Nauru writes and governs in, and the unwritten mother tongue behind it).
- **Places:** Topside (the mined out interior of coral pinnacles, the scarred heart
  of the island), Buada Lagoon (the green inland lagoon, the ring of life and the
  milkfish ponds).
- **Processes:** frigatebird catching (the catching and keeping of the
  frigatebird), Angam Day (the day celebrating the people reaching survival in
  number).
- **Pieces:** the frigatebird (the tamed fishing frigatebird), the milkfish (raised
  in the Buada ponds, food of the inland).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public record historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources on the public record are credited, never

processing-centre era are treated soberly and neutrally and take no side; the
