# Nauru: a khai culture

Nauru staged as a full khai play, ISO NR, the prose in dorerin Naoero and only the
headings in English. The world's smallest republic, an island of twelve tribes,
staged as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the
roles its language and faith encode, its places, its practices, and its defining
objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house that
holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its Estate;
a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_nauru.md](play_nauru.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_nau.md](pitch_nau.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Nauru's
  profile (a close-knit and tribal island life, faith-filled and plain-spoken, the
  tight community of the small island, and a deep resilience) and naming no
  dimension.
- **Plots (history):** eko oe aiwo (the settlement, the twelve tribes, frigatebird
  fishing and the lagoon, and faith), te kowaki ma te buaka (the German and
  Australian administration, the Second World War, the Japanese occupation and the
  deportation to Truk, all staged soberly), te republic ma te phosphate
  (independence in 1968, DeRoburt and the phosphate-rights struggle, and the rich
  years), and boe ma eko (the bust, the devastated Topside, reinvention, Angam
  survival, and modern Nauru) - each casting the elements it needs, and each
  staging the phosphate devastation, the wartime deportation, and the modern
  processing-centre era soberly and neutrally.
- **Personas:** Hammer DeRoburt and Bernard Dowiyogo as the historic cast, plus
  three defining archetypes, te tia mining (the phosphate miner of the cantilever
  and the Topside), te tia itsibwij (the frigatebird catcher and keeper), and te eo
  (the tribal elder, keeper of the twelve tribes' custom). Each is wired to the
  language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** eko oe aiwo (the twelve tribes, the clans and the star, faith and
  custom), phosphate ma boe (phosphate and the price, the boom, the bust, and the
  devastated land), te republic ororo (the smallest republic, sovereignty and
  reinvention).
- **Places:** Topside (the mined-out interior of coral pinnacles, the scarred heart
  of the island), Buada Lagoon (the green inland lagoon, the ring of life and the
  ibija fishponds).
- **Processes:** itsibwij (the catching and keeping of the frigatebird), Angam Day
  (the day celebrating the people reaching survival in number).
- **Pieces:** te itsibwij (the tamed fishing frigatebird), ibija (the milkfish
  raised in the Buada ponds, food of the inland).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-record historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources on the public record are credited, never
claimed; the wartime deportation, the phosphate devastation, and the modern
processing-centre era are treated soberly and neutrally and take no side; the
staging and the architecture are original work.
