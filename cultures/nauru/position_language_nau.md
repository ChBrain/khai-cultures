# Position: dorerin Naoero

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

Dorerin Naoero, the tongue of Nauru, holds five vowel qualities, each of which can be spoken short or long with the length alone changing a word's meaning, and it carries a set of possessive classifiers that mark whether a thing is held as one's own by nature or only carried, a fine-grained distinction few outsiders' languages bother to make.

## Orders

It orders the speaker to choose the right possessive classifier for every object named, one word shape for what is inseparably one's own and another for what is merely carried or acquired, and it orders the vowel's length to be held precisely, since a short vowel and a long vowel are two different words wearing the same letters.

## Loses

It has worn down from a far larger system that once sorted nearly everything spoken of into dozens of separate classes, and it loses ground daily to English, the tongue the phosphate trade and the administration left behind, so that new things are named in the borrowed tongue before this one ever gets to name them itself.

## Drives

It drives the very small number who still hold it to carry two tongues at once, this one for the vowel length and the classifier that mark a thing as truly one's own, and English for the record and the modern word; and it marks whoever grows up with it as keeping, in a single long or short vowel, a distinction the rest of the world no longer bothers to hear.
