# Nauru: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public record historical sources behind the
plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_nauru.md](pitch_nauru.md) is written from Nauru's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
Where Nauru is not separately published, the values are read from the regional and
collectivist profile of the Pacific; the scores and their source are recorded here
so the data is on the record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Nauru profile (approx.)   |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | moderate to high          |
| Individualism                 | low (strongly collective) |
| Motivation toward Achievement | moderate                  |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | moderate                  |
| Long Term Orientation         | low (tradition honouring) |
| Indulgence                    | moderate                  |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the six dimensions model of national
culture and The Culture Factor Group). Nauru is not published as a separate
country; the profile is read as approximate from the regional and collectivist
Pacific data and is qualitative, not numeric.

from the public historical record. They are credited here and never claimed; the
staging is original work. The wartime deportation to Chuuk, the phosphate
devastation, and the modern processing centre era are staged soberly, on the public
record, with no atrocity detail and no side taken.

The three defining personas, [the miner](persona_the_miner.md),
[the frigatebird catcher](persona_the_frigatebird_catcher.md), and
