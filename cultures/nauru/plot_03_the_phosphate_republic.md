# Plot: The Phosphate Republic

## Taxonomy

[play_nauru](play_nauru.md)

## Owner

## Cue

Independence arrived on 31 January 1968, and Hammer DeRoburt, who had spent years pressing the case that Nauruans, not a foreign commission, should own the phosphate under their own island, became the new republic's first president. Within two years the phosphate operation itself was bought back from the commissioners who had run it, and for a stretch of the 1970s and 1980s the royalties made Nauru's citizens among the highest earning people on earth, per head, of any nation.

## Action

DeRoburt led the independence negotiations to their conclusion and then turned the same argument, that the rock belonged to Nauru, into the purchase of the phosphate operation itself in 1970, folding it into a locally owned corporation. The miner working the cantilever above Topside kept extracting the rock at the same pace as before, only now the money it earned stayed on the island rather than sailing off with a foreign commissioner's ledger. A trust fund was built alongside the mining to bank the royalties against the day the phosphate ran out, and for a time the republic could point to new housing, free health care, and one of the highest per capita incomes anywhere as proof the fight had been worth it.

## Stage

- [Hammer DeRoburt](persona_hammer_deroburt.md): the founding president who won independence and then won the phosphate rights themselves back for Nauru.
- [the Miner](persona_the_miner.md): works the cantilever over Topside through the boom that phosphate ownership built.
- [Topside](place_topside.md): the interior being mined out even as its rock funds the republic's rich years.

## Tension

The very ownership DeRoburt fought for meant the rate of extraction, and the rate of the interior's ruin, was now a decision Nauru made about itself rather than one imposed on it, and the trust fund built to outlast the mining was a bet that this generation would manage what it had won better than any foreign commissioner had. Whether that bet paid off is the question the years right after this one would answer.
