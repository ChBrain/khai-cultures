# Plot: The Island Before

## Cue

Long before a foreign ship anchored off the reef, twelve tribes held the island between them, and the flag of the republic they would eventually found still carries their number as a twelve pointed star. Life ran on the reef and the coconut, on frigatebirds caught and kept for their fishing, and on faith and custom woven so tightly into the day to day that there was no separate word for it.

## Action

The twelve tribes divided the small island's land and its lagoon among themselves, and marriage between them, not conquest, is what wove the tribes into one people. Young men proved themselves by catching a frigatebird off the reef and taming it to hand, a skill passed down and displayed rather than merely told about. Inland, milkfish were raised in shallow ponds off Buada Lagoon, a second food source to set against the sea's uncertainty. Custom governed land, marriage, and standing between tribes, and it, along with an inherited faith, held the island together long before any outside power took an interest in it.

## Stage

## Tension

The twelve tribes' order rested on custom, faith, and a skill like frigatebird catching that had to be earned and shown, not simply inherited, and that order was the whole of the island's life before any other one arrived. What holds that order together against later disruption, and what of the custom and the language survives once new authorities and new money enter the picture, is the question this island quietly carried from its own beginning.
