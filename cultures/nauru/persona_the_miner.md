# Persona: The Miner

## Taxonomy

[Phosphate and the Price](position_phosphate_and_the_price.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The phosphate miner who worked the cantilever over Topside, digging the rock that built the republic's wealth and left the interior behind as bare coral pinnacles. Nauruan is the miner's mother tongue: they [speak](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), [think](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md), and [hear](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) in it, the language of home. English arrived with the mine itself: the cantilever, the company, and the mining terms were all [borrowed](process_speaking_borrowed.md) whole from the language of the operation, so that the miner names their own daily work in a tongue that is not the one they were raised in. Nauruan holds the miner's life outside the pit; English holds the company, the pay, and the land title, and it was in that gap that Nauruan lost ground fastest, since a company town runs on the company's tongue whatever language its workers grew up speaking. The miner belongs to [Nauruan Culture](position_culture_nauruan_culture.md), Topside and the mining being as much a part of that culture's memory now as the twelve tribes who once owned the land it sits on.

## Action

In [The Phosphate Republic](plot_03_the_phosphate_republic.md) the miner works the cantilever over Topside through the boom years, embodying [Phosphate and the Price](position_phosphate_and_the_price.md). Every tonne the miner sends down the cantilever is both the republic's income and the interior's ruin, in the same shovel load.

## Shadow

A miner who takes pride in the work, in the skill of the cantilever and the tonnage moved, can look away from what the same work is doing to the ground underneath it, and a company town's English can make the whole operation sound like ordinary business rather than the slow spending of an island's only irreplaceable asset.

## Tell

The miner talks about the cantilever and the yield in the company's English, then goes home and talks about the same day's work in Nauruan, and the two accounts of it, the ledger's and the household's, rarely agree about what the mining actually cost.
