# Plot: the Copperbelt

## Owner

## Cue

In the early decades of the twentieth century the red metal is found in great seams beneath the northern bush. The line of rail comes up the spine of the country, the shafts go down, and where there had been woodland a string of mining towns begins to rise, Ndola, Kitwe, Mufulira, Chingola, the belt of copper that will give the country its name in the world's markets and its weight in its own economy.

## Action

Men come from every corner of the land to work the mines, a Bemba and a Tonga and a Luvale side by side at the same shaft, learning one another's ways and a shared working life in the new towns. The copper becomes the country's backbone, the metal that pays for the schools and the roads, soberly so, for the country learns too the hard lesson of leaning on a single price set far away. Out of the crowded, restless townships comes a new sound: the young men plug in guitars and make Zamrock, the raw, electric music of urban Zambia, and play the kalindula that carries the older rhythms into the modern town. [Rikki Ililonga](persona_rikki.md) and his generation give the Copperbelt its voice, the sound of a people remade by the mine and the town.

## Tension

The Copperbelt makes the country modern and brings its peoples together in one working life, the first great mixing that prepares the nation. Yet it also ties the whole country to one red metal and one price set far away, so the floor can move under it when the price falls, and the very wealth that built the towns leaves them exposed. The strength and the weakness are the same seam.
