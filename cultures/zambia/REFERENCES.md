# Zambia: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain sources behind the plots,
personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md) is written from Zambia's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record without
entering the prose. Where a country-specific score is not published, the regional
profile for Southern / sub-Saharan Africa is used as the guide.

| Dimension                     | Zambia score (approx., regional guide) |
| ----------------------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 60                                     |
| Individualism                 | 25                                     |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 40                                     |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 50                                     |
| Long Term Orientation         | 30                                     |
| Indulgence                    | 40                                     |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate and, where
Zambia is not separately published, are guided by the regional profile for
sub-Saharan / Southern Africa; the low Individualism (a strongly group-first,
collective society) is the load-bearing reading behind the pitch.

The one defining persona, [Bamaayo](persona_bamaayo.md), is an archetype, not a
historical individual: a composite of the mother at the cooking fire who keeps the
creed of the human being first alive at the shared plate, carrying the tension
between the warmth she gives and the unseen burden it lays on her.
