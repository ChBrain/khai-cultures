# Persona: Kenneth Kaunda

## Taxonomy

[One Zambia, One Nation](position_culture_one_zambia_one_nation.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The schoolteacher's son who became the founding president, "KK," the man who led the country to independence and then gave it the idea to hold itself together by: One Zambia, One Nation, and the gentle creed of Humanism, the human being first, before colour, before tribe, before money. Earnest, warm, given to tears and to song, he carried a white handkerchief and a guitar as readily as the burdens of office, and he set the temper of the young nation toward mercy and the round, soft way. A Bemba speaker among seventy tongues, he [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) and [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) in his mother tongue at home and in the heart, yet he led in [English](position_language_en_zm.md) and [carried it as the bridge tongue](process_speaking_carried.md), the neutral ground on which a Bemba and a Tonga and a Lozi could meet as equals, and he [followed on the page](process_reading_followed.md) the wide reading that shaped his creed. He chose the tongue that no group owned precisely so that no group's tongue would rule, making a bridge of English while the seventy mother tongues went on living in the home and the village, each one a country English could not enter, and so the gap at the limit of the tongue is the very gap he turned into the nation's bridge: the place where no one's first words are everyone's, kanshi, made the level ground of all.

## Action

In [One Zambia, One Nation (1964)](plot_03_one_zambia_one_nation_1964.md) he makes the motto the law of the young country and builds a government drawn from across the peoples, choosing the idea and the bridge tongue over any one tribe or tongue. By holding the nation with a creed of warmth rather than the rule of the largest people, he hands Zambia the choice it will have to keep making in every generation.

## Shadow

The gentle creed and the soft way can become the country's own shadow: the patience that waits too long, the mercy that lets the wrong slide, the fine words about the human being first spoken over an empty plate. The temper that keeps the peace can also keep the hard thing unsaid, and the leader loved for his warmth can be slow to answer what warmth alone cannot fix.

## Tell

He weeps openly and sings to a hall full of cabinet ministers, and means it; and he gives a country of seventy tongues one idea to live by, choosing the tongue that no one owned so that no one's tongue would rule.
