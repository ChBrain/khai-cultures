# Zambia: a khai culture

Zambia staged as a full khai play, ISO ZM, language en. A country of more than
seventy peoples who chose, at independence, to be one nation in equal dignity, as
a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its
languages and creed encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects,
all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_zambia.md](play_zambia.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Zambia's
  profile (a warm, group-first society of high collectivism, considerable respect
  for authority and elders, a patient and unhurried temper, and a gentleness slow
  to quarrel) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the great rivers, the Copperbelt, One Zambia, One Nation
  (1964), and the peace held - each casting the elements it needs, and each
  staging the country's making and its costs soberly.
- **Personas:** Kenneth Kaunda the founding president, Litunga Lewanika the Lozi
  river king, the novelist Dominic Mulaisho, and the Zamrock musician Rikki
  Ililonga as the historic cast, plus one defining archetype, Bamaayo (the mother
  at the cooking fire who keeps the shared plate). Each is wired to the language
  engine by the language-crossing it holds, the seventy mother tongues and the
  bridge tongue of English.
- **Positions:** One Zambia, One Nation (the country held by one idea and a
  neutral bridge tongue rather than one tribe), Humanism (the founding creed of
  the human being first), the bush (the wild country and the great rivers held as
  a trust).
- **Places:** Lusaka (the capital where the peoples meet and the nation is
  governed), Mosi-oa-Tunya (the falls on the Zambezi, the country's wonder and the
  home of the walking safari).
- **Processes:** the Kuomboka (the great Lozi ceremony of the royal barge and the
  flood), the shared plate (the daily rite of eating nshima together).
- **Pieces:** nshima (the maize-meal staple the whole land shares), copper (the
  red metal that built the country and taught it its hardest lesson).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain sources are recorded in
[REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the pitch is its
invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never claimed; the
country's making, the leaning on a single metal, and the strains of holding many
peoples as one are treated soberly on the public record; the staging and the
architecture are original work.
