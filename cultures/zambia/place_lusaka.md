# Place: Lusaka

## Owner

## Shown

The capital up on the central plateau, the meeting place of the whole country, where a Bemba and a Tonga and a Lozi and a Nyanja live side by side on the same streets and the seventy tongues are heard in one market in a single morning. The seat of parliament and of government, the wide Cairo Road and the busy minibus ranks, the Independence stadium and the Cathedral, the town that grew from a railway siding into the place where the one nation is governed and the choice of 1964 is kept. Where the country comes to be one.

## Offers

A place where the many peoples meet and become one in daily life, the most mixed and the most national of the country's towns; the stage where the nation governs itself, settles its quarrels the round way, and passes power on peacefully, and a market where the seventy tongues trade and joke together under the one bridge tongue.

## Withheld

The ease the capital seems to promise. Lusaka carries the strain beneath the welcome, the crowding and the hard living at the edges of the growing town, the lean years when the copper price fell, and the truth that the peace and the coming-together it stands for are not a thing the country was simply given but a thing it has to keep choosing and keep working at, kanshi, season after season.
