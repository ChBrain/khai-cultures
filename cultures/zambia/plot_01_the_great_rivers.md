# Plot: the great rivers

[play_zambia](play_zambia.md)

## Owner

## Cue

Long before any border was drawn on the plateau, the peoples are already there, settled along the great rivers that run through the high heart of the land. The Bemba in the north, the Tonga of the southern plateau, the Nyanja and Chewa to the east, the Kaonde, the Lunda and the Luvale of the north-west, and on the Zambezi floodplain in the west the Lozi, who learned to live with a river that swells each year and swallows the plain.

## Action

Each people raises its own way of living and its own line of chiefs. On the floodplain the Lozi build a kingdom, Barotseland, around a Litunga, a king who reads the flood and moves his whole capital with it when the waters rise, the royal barge carrying him from the low country to the high in the great ceremony that still marks the year. Under a Litunga like [Litunga Lewanika](persona_lewanika.md), the river kingdom is ordered and held as a trust. Across the rest of the land the many peoples trade and marry and quarrel and make peace along the rivers, each speaking its own tongue, each keeping its own ceremony, the deep human ground out of which one country would much later be made.

## Stage

- [Litunga Lewanika](persona_lewanika.md): the river king who held the Lozi floodplain kingdom and read the flood as a trust.
- [the bush](position_the_bush.md): the wild country and the great rivers the peoples settled along and lived by.
- [Mosi-oa-Tunya](place_mosi_oa_tunya.md): the falls on the Zambezi, the great thunder the peoples knew long before any map.

## Tension

The land begins as many peoples and many kingdoms, not one, each rooted in its own river country and its own tongue. That deep plurality is the country's first gift and its first problem at once: the warmth and the richness of seventy ways of living, and the fault line that, when the one nation is later made, will have to be held together rather than wished away.
