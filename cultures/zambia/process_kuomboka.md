# Process: the Kuomboka

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

The Litunga of the Lozi and his people, when the Zambezi rises each year and the flood begins to swallow the plain. The great drums sound, the royal barge, the Nalikwanda, black and white with its towering paddlers, is made ready, and the whole nation of the floodplain moves from the low country to the high ground, the king carried at the head in the splendour of the ceremony as his line has done for centuries.

## Direction

Toward the keeping of the river kingdom and its rhythm: the Kuomboka, "to get out of the water," is the moving of the king and his people to high ground ahead of the flood, the oldest and grandest of the country's ceremonies and the living sign that the land and its yearly flood are read and honoured as a trust. It is not only a journey but a remembering, the people moving with the water as their kings have always done, the order of the river kept whole.

## Lever

It is driven by the deep bond between the Lozi and their flooding plain, the need to live with a river that gives the soil and takes the ground in the same season, and by the dignity of a kingship that orders its year by the water. It holds against the flood itself and the long centuries; against it stand the pull of the modern world that would make a mere show of it, and the strain of holding a proud old ceremony whole inside the one young nation.

## Echo

The Kuomboka makes visible what [the bush](position_the_bush.md) holds: that the land and the great rivers are a trust lived with and handed on, not a thing spent. At its head rides [Litunga Lewanika](persona_lewanika.md), the river king borne in the royal barge from the flood to the high ground, the people moving with the water.
