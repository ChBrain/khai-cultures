# Process: the shared plate

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

A household at mealtime, anyone with a pot of nshima and a relish to go with it: the maize meal stirred thick and turned out warm, the relish of greens or beans or fish or meat set in the middle, the hands washed, and everyone, family and visitor alike, gathering round to break a lump of nshima, dip it, and eat together. It takes only a pot and an open door, and whoever is there when the food is ready is fed.

## Direction

Toward warmth and belonging: the daily rite of eating together that makes the creed of the human being first into a plain habit of the hands. The nshima is shared from the common dish, the visitor is never let leave unfed, and there is always, somehow, a little more on the plate for one more person. It is the village warmth carried into every Zambian home, the small, repeated act in which the country is one people three times a day.

## Lever

It is driven by the deep sense that a person is a person through other people, that food is meant to be shared and not hoarded, and by the plain truth that to feed a guest is to honour them. It holds against hunger and the lean years, when the plate is small and shared smaller still; against it stand the hard times that test even the open hand, and the unseen burden borne by the one who cooks and stretches the pot to feed the rest.

## Echo

The shared plate makes audible what [Humanism](position_humanism.md) asks: that the human being comes first and the warmth of the village is the spirit of the nation. Over this dish [Bamaayo](persona_bamaayo.md) feeds whoever comes, stretching one pot to a growing table and letting no visitor go without.
