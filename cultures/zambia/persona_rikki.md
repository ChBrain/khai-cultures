# Persona: Rikki Ililonga

## Taxonomy

[Humanism](position_humanism.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The guitarist and singer who helped make Zamrock, the raw, electric sound that rose out of the Copperbelt townships in the years after independence, a founder of the band WITCH and a voice of the young, urban Zambia finding itself in the new towns. Restless, soulful, and modern, he plugged the guitar into the older rhythms and made a music that was Zambian and of the wide world at once, carrying the warmth and the ache of ordinary life into the dance halls. He [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) and [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) in his mother tongue, sings across it and the [bridge tongue](position_language_en_zm.md) both, and [borrowed the English of rock and soul](process_speaking_borrowed.md) from the records that crossed the ocean, [catching by ear](process_reading_caught.md) the idiom of a music made far away and answering it in his own. He sang the Zambian feeling in tongues partly borrowed, the home speech and the English of the records braided into one sound, and what would not cross cleanly from the one tongue to the other he carried in the music instead of the words, the gap at the limit of the tongue closed not by translation but by the guitar, where the thing that could not be said was played. He belongs to [One Zambia, One Nation](position_culture_one_zambia_one_nation.md) as the townships' answer to it, a sound made by Bemba and Tonga and Lozi hands together in the mining towns the founding idea had drawn into one working life.

## Action

In [the Copperbelt](plot_02_the_copperbelt.md) he gives the mining towns their Zamrock voice, the sound of a people remade by the mine and the town. By carrying the warmth and ache of ordinary Zambian life into a new and modern music, he keeps the human being at the centre of it, the creed of Humanism turned into a beat the towns could dance to.

## Shadow

The borrowed sound can drown the home note it was meant to carry: the pull of the music made far away so strong that the local rhythm and the mother tongue thin into it, the modern beat winning the young while the older song goes quiet. The reach for the wide world's idiom can leave the village voice unheard at home.

## Tell

He plugs the guitar into the rhythms his grandparents knew and makes a sound at once Zambian and of the whole world, and what the words of either tongue cannot quite say he leaves to the strings.
