# Plan: the peace we keep

## Taxonomy

The open mandate the country set itself at independence and has kept choosing since: to hold [One Zambia, One Nation](position_one_zambia_one_nation.md) whole across more than seventy peoples, to keep the gentle creed of [Humanism](position_humanism.md) and the human being first when times are hard, and to keep [the bush](position_the_bush.md) and the great rivers that feed the country as a trust handed on whole. It is the long work of staying one and staying at peace, season after season, kanshi.

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Direction

Keep the nation one: hold the seventy peoples together in equal dignity under the bridge tongue, give the office on merit and not on the home village, and never let the old tribe-feeling back in by the side door. Keep the human being first: carry the warmth and the shared plate into hard times and broaden what the country leans on beyond the one red metal. Keep the bush: hold the rivers and the wild country as a trust, show the falls and the walking safari with pride, and share what they bring with the people who live closest to them. Keep the peace: settle quarrels the round way and pass power on without blood, generation to generation.

## Orders

- To the elder like [Kenneth Kaunda](persona_kaunda.md): keep the motto the law of the land and the cabinet open to all the peoples, choosing the idea over any one tribe.
- To the mother like [Bamaayo](persona_bamaayo.md): keep the shared plate and the warmth alive at the fire, and let the burden of the open hand be seen and shared.
- To the nation: keep choosing to be one, season after season, never mistaking the patience that keeps the peace for the patience that leaves the hard thing unsaid.

## Implementation

A country more than sixty years independent that has done the rare thing, holding seventy peoples as one and keeping the peace while the region around it was torn apart, passing power on peacefully and keeping the door open to those fleeing trouble. It has kept the falls and the great game lands as a pride and a trust, and learned, through the lean years, the hard lesson of leaning on one metal. The work left open is to broaden the base, to keep the old tribe-feeling from the side door, and to hand the kept peace on whole to a generation that did not have to win it.
