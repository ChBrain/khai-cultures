# Persona: Litunga Lewanika

## Taxonomy

[the bush](position_the_bush.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The great Litunga of the Lozi, the river king of the western floodplain, who ruled Barotseland in the years the outside world came pressing in, and who is remembered for ordering and strengthening his kingdom and for the splendour of the Kuomboka, the ceremony in which the king is borne in the royal barge from the flooding plain to the high ground, the paddles rising and falling to the royal drums. A statesman of the river, shrewd and dignified, he held the floodplain and its yearly flood as a kingdom's trust. The Lozi tongue, Silozi, he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) as the very voice of his kingship, the language of the court and the barge and the praise-singers; the tongues that came with the outsiders, [English](position_language_en_zm.md) among them, he met through others, [following only what was carried across to him](process_reading_deciphered.md) by interpreter and clerk. He ruled in the tongue of the river while the language of the newcomers reached him only secondhand, so that the words that would decide his kingdom's place in the world arrived already turned by another mouth, and there the gap at the limit of the tongue ran like the flood line itself, between what was said on his side of the water and what could be carried across to the other. He belongs to [One Zambia, One Nation](position_culture_one_zambia_one_nation.md) as the kingdom that had to choose to be one people among many rather than a nation apart, the Lozi crown folded into a wider country it did not rule alone.

## Action

In [the great rivers](plot_01_the_great_rivers.md) he holds the Lozi floodplain kingdom and reads the flood as a trust, the river king whose people move with the water and mark the year by the Kuomboka. By keeping the kingdom ordered and its life bound to the river, he carries into the one nation the proof that the land and its rhythms are a thing held and handed on, not spent.

## Shadow

The dignity of the old kingdom can harden into the pull of the separate part against the whole: the river kingdom that remembers it was a kingdom first and a province after, the proud particular that can strain against [One Zambia, One Nation](position_culture_one_zambia_one_nation.md). The trust held so well for one people can become the wall that keeps the one nation from being wholly one.

## Tell

He is borne from the flood in the royal barge to the royal drums, the whole people moving with the water as their kings have for centuries; and the river orders his year more surely than any calendar the outside world could bring.
