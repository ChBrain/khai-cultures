# Position: the bush

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The wild country itself, the woodland and the floodplain and the great rivers that thread them, the heart of Southern Africa where the Zambezi runs and the falls thunder. It is the land the peoples settled along and still live by, the source of the fish and the game and the flood that brings the soil, and it is the country's great open inheritance: the South Luangwa and the wide game lands where, on Zambian ground, the walking safari was born, the visitor brought out of the vehicle and led on foot through the bush by a guide and a scout, to meet the country at the pace of the animals that live there. The bush is wealth and danger both, beauty and livelihood at once, and the country has learned to hold it as something to be kept rather than spent.

## Orders

That the land and the rivers are a trust and not just a resource, to be lived with and handed on whole; that the falls and the game lands are the country's pride and its welcome to the world, to be shown with care and not sold off cheap; and that the people who live closest to the bush, the fisherman on the river and the villager at the park's edge, are owed their share of what the bush gives.

## Loses

Ground when the trust is spent for the quick return, the river overfished and the woodland cut for charcoal faster than it grows; and ground, too, when the bush is fenced off only for the visitor and the foreign lens, so that the very people who have lived with it longest are shut out of the wealth it draws.

## Drives

Lives in [Litunga Lewanika](persona_lewanika.md), the river king whose people read the flood and move with it, who held the floodplain and its life as a kingdom's trust. It drives Zambia to keep the great rivers and the wild country whole, to show the falls and the walking safari to the world with pride, and to remember that the bush is a thing kept, not a thing used up.
