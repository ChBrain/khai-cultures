# Position: Zambian English

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The authority of English as Zambia's one official tongue, the neutral bridge that crosses more than seventy mother tongues without belonging wholly to any of them. It holds among the Bemba and the Tonga, the Lozi of the flood plain and the Nyanja and Chewa of the east, the ground on which a people who share no single first language can meet as equals. Carried in on the line of rail and the colonial school, it was taken up at independence as the tongue that no group owned and so every group could share, the working speech of the town and the clinic, the record and the radio, while the deepest things are still said in the village tongue, the proverb, and the lullaby.

## Orders

What the young nation conducts its shared life across: the statute and the school lesson, the newspaper and the broadcast, the business done between one people and another. It orders the level ground where a Bemba and a Tonga and a Lozi meet with none made to speak inside another's language, and it gives a country of seventy tongues a single working speech in which to address itself and the world.

## Loses

Ground to the mother tongues that live in the home and the heart, each one a country English cannot enter, in which the people pray and mourn and sing; and ground most of all when it becomes the language of the school and of advancement, so that the grandchildren come to answer the grandmother in English while she holds the old tongue at the pot, and the deep speech thins toward silence across a single table.

## Drives

It drives a nation of many peoples to hold its common life on ground that belongs to none of them and so erases none. It is carried, not born, by [Kenneth Kaunda](persona_kaunda.md), who chose the tongue no group owned so that no group's tongue would rule; drafted onto the page by [Dominic Mulaisho](persona_mulaisho.md), who gave the one nation its literature in English; borrowed from the records that crossed the ocean by [Rikki Ililonga](persona_rikki.md), who braided it into the sound of Zamrock; caught by ear off the radio and the grandchildren by [Bamaayo](persona_bamaayo.md), who keeps the home in the village tongue while the bridge tongue rises around her; and it reached [Litunga Lewanika](persona_lewanika.md) only secondhand, turned by interpreter and clerk, the river king who ruled in the tongue of the flood while the newcomers' speech came to him across the water. So the bridge tongue shows who carries it and whom it merely reaches, and binds the seventy peoples without erasing the mother tongues they were born to.
