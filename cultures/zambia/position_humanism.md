# Position: Humanism

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The gentle creed the founding president gave the young country, that the human being comes first, before colour, before tribe, before money: that a society is to be measured by how it treats the person, and that the warmth and sharing of the old village, where no one ate while a neighbour went hungry, should be the spirit of the modern nation too. It draws on the deep African sense that a person is a person through other people, that you greet before you ask and share before you keep, and it sets this above the cold arithmetic of profit. Whatever became of it in policy and in the hard years, as a temper of the country it stayed: a leaning toward mercy, toward the round and soft way of doing things, toward keeping the dignity of the small person whole.

## Orders

That the person is never to be used merely as a tool or a number, so the old, the poor, the sick and the stranger are owed care as a matter of course; that wealth and rank do not raise a man above his fellows in worth; and that quarrels are settled the human way, talked round and round until nobody is shamed, because keeping the relationship whole matters more than winning the point.

## Loses

Ground when the soft way becomes the slack way, the patience that will not confront the thing that needs confronting, the mercy that shades into letting the wrong slide; and ground, too, when the fine words about the human being first are spoken over an empty plate, the creed honoured in the speech while the person it names goes wanting.

## Drives

Lives in the music that carried the warmth and the ache of ordinary Zambian life into the towns, and in the cooking fire kept by whoever feeds anyone who comes. It drives Zambia toward gentleness and the shared plate, the conviction that a country is built first of all out of how it treats the human being in front of it.
