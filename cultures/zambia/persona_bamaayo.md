# Persona: Bamaayo

## Taxonomy

[Humanism](position_humanism.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The mother at the cooking fire, bamaayo, the woman who turns the maize meal in the pot and feeds whoever comes, the one in whom the warmth and the sharing of the country are kept alive day by day. Patient, generous, and quietly in charge of the home, she cooks the nshima and the relish, scolds and laughs and welcomes, and lives the creed that the human being comes first by the plainest means there is: there is always a little more on the plate for one more person. She is the Humanism of the speeches made true at the hearth. Her mother tongue she [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md), and [hears](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) as the whole air she lives in, the language of the home and the proverb and the lullaby; the [bridge tongue](position_language_en_zm.md) she [catches by ear](process_reading_caught.md) from the radio and the grandchildren and uses as she must in the town and the clinic. She keeps the home in the mother tongue while her grandchildren answer her more and more in English, the bridge tongue rising in the house even as she holds the old one at the pot, so that across her own table runs the gap at the limit of the tongue, where she speaks the warmth in the language of the village and is answered, lovingly, in the language of the nation. She belongs to [One Zambia, One Nation](position_culture_one_zambia_one_nation.md) at the root, the plate she stretches for one more mouth the same idea the founders put into a motto, lived long before it was ever written down.

## Action

In [the peace held](plot_04_the_peace_held.md) she keeps the creed of warmth alive at the cooking fire, feeding whoever comes and holding the home together through the lean years and the good. By living Humanism as a matter of course, sharing the plate however small, she carries the gentleness that keeps the wider peace into the smallest room of the country.

## Shadow

The endless giving can become the burden borne unseen: the warmth that asks everything of the woman at the fire and thanks her least, the creed of the human being first that leans hardest on the one who feeds the rest. The shared plate is kept whole at the cost of the hands that fill it, and the cost is easy not to count.

## Tell

She stretches one pot of nshima to feed a table that keeps growing and never lets a visitor leave unfed, mwabombeni; and she speaks her warmth in the village tongue to grandchildren who answer her, smiling, in the tongue of the nation.
