# Plot: the peace held

## Cue

In the decades after independence the region around Zambia is hard country. Wars and upheavals trouble several of its eight neighbours, refugees cross the borders, and the world's quarrels press in. At home the copper price falls and the lean years come, and the young nation is tested as to whether the choice it made in 1964 will hold under the strain.

## Action

The peace holds, soberly and against the odds. While neighbours are torn apart, Zambia stays at peace within its own borders and keeps the door open to those fleeing trouble around it. When the hard times come, the country settles its quarrels the round, soft way, and in time learns to pass power from one hand to the next without blood, the rarest and most precious thing in a young nation. The writers and the singers record the coming-together and its costs honestly: [Dominic Mulaisho](persona_mulaisho.md) sets the nation's character and its strains into the country's own books, and the people come to guard the peace itself as the thing most worth keeping. The shared plate, the kept temper, the patience that will not rush to the quarrel, all do their quiet work.

## Stage

- [Dominic Mulaisho](persona_mulaisho.md): the writer who records the nation's character and its costs in its own books.
- [One Zambia, One Nation](position_culture_one_zambia_one_nation.md): the founding idea, tested by hard years and kept.
- [Lusaka](place_lusaka.md): the capital where power is passed on without blood and the peace is held.

## Tension

The country reaches its quiet height not in conquest or wealth but in a thing easy to overlook: a peace kept while the region burned. Yet the same patience that keeps the peace can wait too long, and the same warmth that holds the nation can let the hard thing go unsaid. The standing question is whether the kept peace, won by gentleness and choice, can be handed on whole to a generation that did not have to fight for it.
