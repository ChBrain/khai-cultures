# the Philippines: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_tl.md](pitch_tl.md) is written from the Philippines' profile on
Geert Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no
number. The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record
without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Philippines score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | --------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 94                          |
| Individualism                 | 32                          |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 64                          |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 44                          |
| Long Term Orientation         | 27                          |
| Indulgence                    | 42                          |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; the Philippines' high Power Distance
and strongly collectivist (low Individualism) orientation are among the defining
features of the published profile.

## Historical sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn
from the public historical record. They are credited here and never claimed; the
staging is original work. The colonial periods, the revolution, and the upheavals
of the twentieth century are staged soberly on the public record, never
graphically and never taking a political side; the focus is kept on the enduring
culture, the language, faith and family, the islands, and the people.

The one defining archetype, [the OFW](persona_ofw.md), is not a historical
individual but a composite: the overseas Filipino worker who carries the family
in the heart across the whole world and smiles through the cost of the leaving,
the figure in whom the modern tension between family and the distance it must
cross is held.
