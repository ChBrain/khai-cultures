# the Philippines: a khai culture

The Philippines staged as a full khai play, ISO PH, language tl. An archipelago of
thousands of islands held as one not by blood or soil but by kapwa, family, and
faith, set out as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast,
the roles its language and faith encode, its places, its practices, and its
defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_philippines.md](play_philippines.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_tl.md](pitch_tl.md), the Hofstede layer, written from the
  Philippines' profile (a deeply collectivist orientation toward family and
  barangay, a strong sense of hierarchy and face, an easy indulgence and warmth,
  and a resilience that smiles through hardship) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the islands (the pre-colonial archipelago), the colonial
  era (the long period of rule from the west, handled soberly), the awakening
  (the rise of national consciousness and the revolution), and independence (the
  modern republic and the global Filipino diaspora) - each casting the elements
  it needs, and each staged with dignity and no political side.
- **Personas:** Jose Rizal (the writer who woke the nation), Andres Bonifacio
  (the son of Tondo who carried the cause to the common people), Francisco
  Balagtas (the great Tagalog poet of Florante at Laura), and Levi Celerio (the
  National Artist who set the people's faith and feeling to song) as the historic
  cast, plus one defining archetype, the OFW (the overseas worker who leaves so
  there will be something to send home). Each is wired to the language engine by
  the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** kapwa (the shared identity that holds the islands as one),
  ang pamilya (the family and the barangay as first home), ang pananampalataya
  (the faith, the fiesta, and the resilience it gives).
- **Places:** Intramuros (the walled city of Manila, the seat of power and
  church), the Banaue Rice Terraces (the indigenous mastery carved into the
  mountains over a thousand years).
- **Processes:** bayanihan (communal cooperation, the whole community sharing the
  burden), the fiesta (the shared feast and devotion of the town).
- **Pieces:** the jeepney (the resourceful, colourful people's vehicle of the
  street), the barong tagalog (the sheer national shirt of dignity worn at every
  honoured occasion).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the colonial periods, the revolution, and the upheavals of the twentieth
century are treated soberly on the public record, never graphically and never
taking a side; the staging and the architecture are original work.
