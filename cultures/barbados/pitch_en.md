# Pitch: the Bajan tone

## Taxonomy

The key in which the staging of [Barbados](play_barbados.md) is played. It changes nothing of what happens - not the settling, not the sugar and the code, not the road to independence, not the republic. It sets only how it sounds, the register in which all of it is given.

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Tenor

Played orderly, courteous, and dry: a people who keep their forms and keep their tempers, who value manners, propriety, and the steady running of things, and who say much in little. Reserved and understated where their neighbours are loud, British-inflected in their bearing and their schooling, they take pride in being correct, in church on Sunday and cricket on Saturday, in a thing well kept and a word well chosen. Under the politeness runs a quick, wry humour, the Bajan wit that turns a sharp observation gently, never raising the voice to make the point. They are settled where others are restless, holding their place with a quiet, earned dignity.

## Undertow

Under the orderliness runs the unhealed thing the order was built on: the plantation Barbados pioneered for the world, the bondage beneath the tidy parishes and the great houses, the cost that bought the calm. There is a propriety that can become a politeness too careful to name the wound, an English form so well kept it can stand in the way of reckoning with what the form once served. And under the modest pride runs the smallness of an island that exported its wealth for centuries and must now make its own, the quiet question of whether the order it kept was ever truly its own to keep.

## Nerve

It strikes the chord of dignity earned and order held: the conviction that a people can keep their bearing through anything, that manners and steadiness are themselves a kind of resistance, that a small island can carry itself with more composure than empires. It plays on the pride of a thing done properly, the love of the game and the harvest and the parish, the deep Bajan belief in being correct and being free both. It plays, too, on the ache underneath - of an order whose foundation is not yet fully faced.

## Echo

What is left is the image of a people composed and courteous, dry of wit and steady of hand, who took the mould the mother country pressed on them and made it their own, and who now govern as a republic the ground where the plantation began. A nation that runs on quiet dignity and good order, and that carries, beneath the calm, the long work of reckoning with the very thing that order was first raised to serve.
