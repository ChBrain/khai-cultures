# Persona: Garfield Sobers

## Taxonomy

[cricket and Crop Over](position_cricket_and_crop_over.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

Sir Garfield Sobers, the Bajan boy from a Bridgetown neighbourhood who became the greatest all-round cricketer the game has known, master of bat and ball alike, captain of the West Indies, knighted on his own island, the proof that a small place could best the empire at the empire's own game. Graceful, complete, and quietly assured, he carried the island's mastery onto the world's fields and gave a colony, and then a nation, a hero the whole earth acknowledged. English was his mother tongue and the Bajan dialect his everyday speech: in it he [spoke](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [heard](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) and [thought](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md), the English of an island that had held the tongue for three centuries, now carried out into the cricketing world as Bajan as the cut shot. He learned the master's game and the master's language and turned both to the island's glory, beating the empire at the sport it brought and being honoured by it - and where his Bajan English reached its limit lay the African homeland tongues the cane had taken from those who built the island, the speech lost beneath the dialect he played and spoke so well, the gap at the limit of the tongue standing in what the plantation had swallowed long before the boundary was first chalked.

## Action

In [the Republic and the Field](plot_04_the_republic_and_the_field.md) he is the island's cricketing mastery made flesh, the all-rounder who carried Barbados onto the world's fields and beat the empire at its game, the pride of a nation that loved cricket as a religion. He carries [cricket and Crop Over](position_cricket_and_crop_over.md) as the proof that a small island, given the master's game, could make of it the finest the world had seen.

## Shadow

The hero on the field can let a nation rest its pride on the game alone, the cricketing glory a comfort that stands in for the harder reckonings a young republic still owes. The shadow is the island's temptation to be content with mastery at play, to celebrate the boundary and the knighthood and leave the foundation beneath the field unfaced.

## Tell

He beat the empire at the game it carried ashore and was knighted on his own island for it - the Bajan boy from the neighbourhood who made the master's sport the small island's finest gift to the world.
