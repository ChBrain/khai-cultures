# Persona: Garfield Sobers

## Owner

## Projection

Sir Garfield Sobers, the Bajan boy from a Bridgetown neighbourhood who became the greatest all-round cricketer the game has known, master of bat and ball alike, captain of the West Indies, knighted on his own island, the proof that a small place could best the empire at the empire's own game. He learnt the strokes in the road with a soft ball and a sour-apple bat like every Bridgetown boy, born with an extra finger pon each hand and never once let it trouble him. Graceful, complete, and quietly assured, the man never boasted; he let the bat answer and went home for his cou-cou. He carried the island's mastery onto the world's fields and gave a colony, and then a nation, a hero the whole earth acknowledged. English was his mother tongue and the [Bajan](position_language_en_bb.md) dialect his everyday speech: in it he [spoke](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [heard](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) and [thought](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md), the English of an island that had held the tongue for three centuries, now carried out into the cricketing world as Bajan as the cut shot. He learned the master's game and the master's language and turned both to the island's glory, beating the empire at the sport it brought and being honoured by it - and where his Bajan English reached its limit lay the African homeland tongues the cane had taken from those who built the island, the speech lost beneath the dialect he played and spoke so well, the gap at the limit of the tongue standing in what the plantation had swallowed long before the boundary was first chalked.

## Action

In [the Republic and the Field](plot_04_the_republic_and_the_field.md) he is the island's cricketing mastery made flesh, the all-rounder who carried Barbados onto the world's fields and beat the empire at its game, the pride of a nation that loved cricket as a religion. From the same small island came the three Ws, Worrell, Weekes, and Walcott, all three born within a few miles and a few years of one another, and out of that same nursery Sobers rose the greatest of all. He carries [cricket and Crop Over](position_cricket_and_crop_over.md) as the proof that a likkle island, given the master's game, could make of it the finest the world had seen.

## Shadow

The hero on the field can let a nation rest its pride on the game alone, the cricketing glory a comfort that stands in for the harder reckonings a young republic still owes. The shadow is the island's temptation to be content with mastery at play, to celebrate the boundary and the knighthood and leave the foundation beneath the field unfaced.

## Tell

He beat the empire at the game it carried ashore and was knighted on his own island for it - the Bajan boy from the neighbourhood, six sixes in one over and the whole island saying only, soft and proud, the boy could play, who made the master's sport the small island's finest gift to the world.
