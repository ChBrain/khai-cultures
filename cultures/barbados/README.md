# Barbados: a khai culture

Barbados staged as a full khai play, ISO BB, language en. An island settled clean
and held longer than any other colony of the crown, made the cradle of the New
World plantation and then a Black-majority republic governing the very ground it
once was, as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the
roles its language and order encode, its places, its practices, and its defining
objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_barbados.md](play_barbados.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Barbados' profile (orderly and proper, courteous and reserved, dry of wit and
  British-inflected, steady where its neighbours are loud) and naming no
  dimension.
- **Plots (history):** Little England (the empty island, the English settling, the
  oldest colony), the Sugar and the Code (the sugar revolution, the plantation
  model and the 1661 slave code, Bussa's rising of 1816), the Road to Independence
  (Adams and labour, Barrow and independence in 1966), and the Republic and the
  Field (cricket and Sobers, Crop Over, the 2021 republic) - each casting the
  elements it needs, and each staging the gap between the order and its foundation
  honestly.
- **Personas:** Bussa, Grantley Adams, Errol Barrow, and Garfield Sobers as the
  historic cast, plus one defining archetype, the cane cutter (the enslaved then
  freed sugar worker whose labour built the island and whose endurance outlasted
  the system). Each is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it
  holds.
- **Positions:** the cradle of the plantation (the model of Caribbean
  sugar-slavery first set here, and the resistance to it), Little England made its
  own (the orderly British-modelled society reclaimed as a Black republic), cricket
  and Crop Over (the national passions of the game and the harvest reclaimed as
  joy).
- **Places:** Bridgetown (the capital, the Parliament, and the historic Garrison,
  a UNESCO World Heritage site), the Cane Country (the plantation parishes of
  rolling cane, great houses, and chattel houses).
- **Processes:** Crop Over (the sugar-harvest festival climaxing in Grand
  Kadooment), cricket (the Test at the Kensington Oval, the national religion).
- **Pieces:** flying fish and cou-cou (the national dish and emblem), rum (the
  spirit born on the island from the cane).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; slavery, the plantation model the island pioneered, the slave code, and
the resistance to it are treated soberly on the public record, with no atrocity
detail and no side taken; the staging and the architecture are original work.
