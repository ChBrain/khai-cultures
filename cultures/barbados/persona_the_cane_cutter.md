# Persona: the cane cutter

## Taxonomy

[the cradle of the plantation](position_the_cradle_of_the_plantation.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The worker in the cane: enslaved on the great estate and then, after emancipation, still bent over the same rows for a wage that barely freed, the body on which the island's wealth was raised across three centuries. Enduring, skilled, and unbroken in dignity, this is the labour the plantation consumed and the endurance that outlasted it, the truer inheritance of the cradle. Out of season the cutting done, the same hands raised the chattel house pon its blocks, light enough to take up and shift to the next estate when the work moved, the little wooden house that owned nothing of the ground it stood pon and stood firm regardless. The mother tongue, for the African-born among them, was the language carried from a homeland across the water: in it they [spoke](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thought](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) and named the world they had been torn from, and from many such tongues thrown together in the cane the enslaved [forged a creole carried](process_speaking_carried.md) among themselves, the speech of the field passed mouth to mouth. The English of the estate they [deciphered](process_reading_deciphered.md) from order and ledger that counted them as property, a tongue learned under the whip of the work and never freely chosen. Generations cut the cane in tongues the masters would not hear, until the African languages thinned and the [Bajan creole](position_language_en_bb.md) that grew from them became the island's own - so the gap at the limit of the tongue is the homeland speech the cane swallowed, surviving only in the rhythm of the dialect that replaced it.

## Action

In [the Sugar and the Code](plot_02_the_sugar_and_the_code.md) the cane cutter is the labour the plantation model was built to use, enslaved under the 1661 code and worked still after freedom came, the foundation of all the wealth the island sent abroad. By enduring and surviving, this worker carries [the cradle of the plantation](position_the_cradle_of_the_plantation.md) as the dignity that outlasted the system, the people the order was raised on and could not break.

## Shadow

The endurance that survives can be praised in a way that hides the cost, the suffering turned into a tale of patient strength that asks nothing further of the present. The shadow is the island's temptation to admire the resilience and forget the wound, to let the dignity of the worker excuse the long reckoning still owed to the labour the cane consumed.

## Tell

After freedom came, the same hands cut the same cane for a wage that scarcely fed them, and still they kept their bearing - the body the wealth was built pon, owning nothing of the wealth it built, and asked how it keeping, would answer only, soft and dry, getting by.
