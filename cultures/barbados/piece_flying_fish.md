# Piece: flying fish and cou-cou

## Taxonomy

Parent group: pieces

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Place

On the Sunday table and in the rum shop, at the fish fry of Oistins and on the plate the whole island claims as its own: flying fish, seasoned and steamed or fried, served with cou-cou, the smooth golden meal of cornmeal and okra stirred firm, the acknowledged national dish of Barbados. The flying fish leaps in the island's own waters, on its coins and its emblems, the creature by which Barbados knows itself, the Land of the Flying Fish.

## Load Bearing

It stands for the island made from what it had: the fish from its own sea, the cou-cou of cornmeal and okra grown from the food the enslaved were given and the cooking they made of it, the African hand and the island's waters meeting on one plate. In a nation that took the master's forms and made them its own, the national dish is the everyday proof of it, the meal cooked in every parish that is wholly Barbadian, owing nothing to the mother country and everything to the island and the people who built it. Without this plate the island would have no taste it could call entirely its own.

## Apparent

A steamed or fried fish beside a mound of firm golden cornmeal-and-okra cou-cou, a green seasoning and a gravy, set on a Sunday plate. In itself it is only a meal of fish and meal; its weight is in its being the island's own, the food the enslaved made into a cuisine and the nation lifted into its emblem.
