# Plot: the Republic and the Field

## Taxonomy

[play_barbados](play_barbados.md)

## Owner

## Cue

The independent nation settles into itself: a stable, orderly Caribbean democracy that keeps its English forms and its quiet dignity, and pours its passion into the game and the harvest. Cricket becomes the island's religion, the field at the Kensington Oval the ground where a small nation stands at the top of a world sport, and the sugar harvest is danced each summer into the festival of Crop Over.

## Action

Modern Barbados makes its name at play and at festival: it gives the cricketing world Garfield Sobers, the finest all-rounder the game has known, the Bajan boy who beats the empire at the sport it brought ashore and is knighted on his own island. It turns the plantation's harvest into Crop Over, the weeks of calypso and fete climaxing in the costumed street dance of Grand Kadooment, the labour of the cane reclaimed by its descendants as joy. And on the thirtieth of November, 2021, fifty-five years to the day after independence, the island sets aside the British crown and becomes a republic, a Black-majority nation governing as its own the very ground where the plantation began.

## Stage

- [Garfield Sobers](persona_garfield_sobers.md): the greatest all-round cricketer the game has known, the island's mastery carried onto the world's fields.
- [Bridgetown](place_bridgetown.md): the capital where the republic is proclaimed in 2021 and the Kensington Oval where the island worships cricket.
- [cricket and Crop Over](position_cricket_and_crop_over.md): the national passions in which modern Barbados is most itself, the game and the harvest reclaimed as joy.

## Tension

The island reaches a new dignity, mastering the master's game and reclaiming the plantation's harvest as celebration, and setting aside the crown at last to govern itself entire as a republic. Yet the republic stands on the cradle of the plantation, and the joy of the field and the festival, real and earned, must not stand in for the reckoning still owed: whether the young republic can face the foundation beneath its order and turn its pride and its industry into prosperity shared across the island.
