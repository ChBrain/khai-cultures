# Persona: Bussa

## Taxonomy

[the cradle of the plantation](position_the_cradle_of_the_plantation.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

An African-born ranger held in bondage on a Barbadian estate, who in 1816 led the largest rebellion the island had ever seen and gave his name to the cause of freedom, now a national hero of Barbados. Resolute, organised, and unbroken, he carried within him a homeland the cane had not erased and a refusal the order could not contain. His mother tongue was an African one carried across the water from his birth, the language of the home he was taken from: in it he [spoke](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thought](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) and [heard](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) the world named as he had first learned to name it, and in it, and in the broken creole the enslaved forged among themselves, the rising was [carried](process_speaking_carried.md) from estate to estate by mouth, never written down. The English of the masters he had only as much as bondage forced on him, [deciphered](process_reading_deciphered.md) from command and law that were never meant to free him, a tongue he wore but never owned. He plotted liberty in a language his masters could not hear and was denied the one they wrote their power in, so the gap at the limit of the tongue was the very ground he fought across - the freedom he sought spoken in a tongue carried from a home he would not see again.

## Action

In [the Sugar and the Code](plot_02_the_sugar_and_the_code.md) he rises against the plantation system the island pioneered and the slave code that bound it, leading the enslaved of 1816 in the great refusal that bears his name. He sets against [the cradle of the plantation](position_the_cradle_of_the_plantation.md) the truth that no code could hold forever, and gives the island a hero of its own resistance.

## Shadow

The rising that fails in arms can be remembered as defeat and not as the seed of freedom it was, the rebel reduced to a single date while the long daily endurance behind him goes unnamed. The shadow is the island's temptation to honour the hero and forget the many, to mark the rebellion and not the bondage it rose against, and to let the monument stand in the place of the reckoning.

## Tell

He led men to their freedom in a tongue carried from across the sea, and is honoured now by a nation that speaks the masters' English he was never freely given - his name the Bajan word for the cause itself.
