# Persona: Grantley Adams

## Taxonomy

[Little England made its own](position_little_england_made_its_own.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The lawyer and labour leader who founded the Barbados Labour Party, championed the working people of the island in the wake of the unrest of the 1930s, and became its first premier, the man who worked the colonial institution from within to lift those it had left out. Eloquent, constitutional, and shrewd, schooled at Oxford and steeped in the English forms, he could stand in the parish church on a Sunday more correct than the rector, then take the same correctness into the courtroom and turn the mother country's own law and parliament toward the people who filled them. He did not break the table; he sat at it and would not be moved. English was his mother tongue, the English of an island that had kept it longer than any other colony, and the [Bajan](position_language_en_bb.md) dialect his everyday speech: in it he [spoke](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thought](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) and [wrote](process_writing_mother_tongue.md), and he had it whole and shaping, the legal and parliamentary English of the empire turned to argue the island's case before the empire itself. He mastered the master's tongue so completely he could beat the colonial power with its own law and its own words, and where his eloquence reached its end stood the homeland speech of the enslaved who had built the island, the African tongues the cane had swallowed before his English could be the island's own - so the gap at the limit of the tongue lay behind him, in the languages lost to make the English he wielded so well. He belongs to [Bajan culture](position_culture_bajan_culture.md) as the reformer who claimed the colony's own institutions for the people who filled them.

## Action

In [the Road to Independence](plot_03_the_road_to_independence.md) he leads the labour movement and the constitutional advance that lifted the Barbadian people within the colonial frame, the first premier who made the island's institutions answer to its majority. He carries [Little England made its own](position_little_england_made_its_own.md) by claiming the English form for the people who filled it, the reformer who worked the system rather than smashed it.

## Shadow

The reformer who works within the institution can come to defend the form for its own sake, to mistake the orderly advance for the whole of freedom and to grow cautious where boldness was needed - the federalist whose grand design failed and who was overtaken by a younger, sharper claim to nationhood. The shadow is the island's own: the propriety that can stand in the way of the deeper break.

## Tell

He argued the island's freedom in the precise English of the empire that held it, beating the colonial power at its own law - and was in the end pressed past, gentle but firm, by a younger man who would take the freedom whole and not wait pon permission.
