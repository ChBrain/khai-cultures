# Barbados: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md) is written from Barbados' cultural profile and
names no dimension and carries no number. National-level Hofstede scores for
Barbados are not part of the principal published six-dimension dataset, so the
pitch is written from the island's well-documented cultural disposition - orderly,
proper, courteous, reserved, dry-witted, and British-inflected, with a strong
respect for institutions and good order - read in the spirit of Hofstede's model
rather than from a published national score. Where regional Caribbean estimates are
cited in the literature, they are treated as approximate and indicative only, and
no number enters the prose.

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the six-dimensions model of national
culture and the Country Comparison tool). The model and method are credited; any
figures are approximate and do not enter the staging.

## Historical sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn
from the public historical record. They are credited here and never claimed; the
staging is original work. The hard centre of the story - the plantation model the
island pioneered, the 1661 slave code, slavery, and the resistance and dignity of
the enslaved - is staged soberly on the public record, from within the nation's own
self-understanding, with no atrocity detail and no side taken.
