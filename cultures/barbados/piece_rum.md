# Piece: rum

## Taxonomy

Parent group: pieces

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Place

In the rum shop on every corner, on the world's bars, and in the bottle the island sends abroad: rum, the spirit distilled from the sugar cane, born in Barbados where the plantation first turned the molasses of the harvest into liquor. The home of the oldest commercial rum distillery still running, the island claims the spirit as its own invention, the drink that came out of the cane the enslaved cut and that the world now pours.

## Load Bearing

It stands for the whole of the island's story in a single glass: the sugar that made Barbados the cradle of the plantation, the molasses left from the crushing turned into a spirit, the wealth and the trade and the bondage all distilled into the drink the island gave the world. Rum is the plantation's by-product become the island's pride, the rum shop the centre of Bajan social life, the spirit poured at every fete and Crop Over and gathering. It carries both the joy of the island and the memory of the cane it came from, the celebration and the cost in the same glass. Without this spirit the island would lack the very emblem of what the sugar made.

## Apparent

A clear or amber spirit in a plain bottle, distilled from cane molasses and aged in oak or poured young, drunk neat, with water, or in the island's punches. In itself it is only liquor from sugar; its weight is in its birth on this island from the cane the plantation worked, the drink that holds the whole story of the place.
