# Play: Barbados

## Estate

## Name

Barbados, the easternmost island of the Caribbean, a coral outcrop standing alone in the Atlantic beyond the arc of the Lesser Antilles, twenty-one miles long and fourteen wide. ISO BB, language en, the English of the empire turned in the Bajan mouth to a dialect all its own. England's oldest continuous colony and longest-held possession, called Little England for the order it kept and the mother country it mirrored, and since 2021 a republic with a Black majority governing the very plantation it once was.

## Arc

An island found empty of its first peoples when the English came ashore in 1627, with no surviving indigenous nation to meet them, and so settled clean and held without a break longer than any other colony of the crown. Within a generation the sugar revolution remade it: cane covered the parishes, the great estates rose, and Barbados became the first true plantation society of the New World and the model the whole Caribbean would copy, its 1661 slave code the template for the codes that followed across the islands and into the mainland colonies. The wealth was built on enslaved Africans worked in the cane, and the order that earned the island its English name was raised on that foundation. Against it the enslaved rose, most famously in Bussa's rebellion of 1816, the largest the island had seen, and the long pressure of resistance and dignity never let up. Emancipation came, and then a century in which Barbadians made the English mould their own: a stable, orderly, deeply Anglican society of churches and cricket and parish life, schooled to the mother country yet steadily claiming itself. Labour leaders like Grantley Adams and then Errol Barrow carried it to independence in 1966, and the island that had been Little England became a nation that loved cricket as a religion, gave the game Garfield Sobers and the world its finest all-rounder, danced its sugar harvest into the festival of Crop Over with the tuk band beating up the road, sat down to cou-cou and flying fish, invented rum where the cane was crushed, sent Rihanna out from Bridgetown to the whole earth, and in 2021 set aside the crown to become a republic. Under it all runs one nerve: an orderly, courteous, dry-witted people who kept the form England gave them and turned it to their own ends, who will tell you not too bad when they mean a great deal more, carrying with quiet pride the burden of having been where the plantation began.

## Stakes

Whether Little England, now a Black-majority republic standing pon the very plantation it pioneered for the world, can reckon soberly with the gap between the order it kept and the bondage that order was raised on - can say the hard thing plain instead of saying not too bad and changing the subject - can hold its hard-won stability and its cricketing pride without mistaking the form for the freedom, and can turn its industry and its joy into prosperity shared across the island, or whether the wealth that once flowed off it leaves the cradle of the plantation carrying the cost without the reward.
