# Place: Mosi-oa-Tunya

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Shown

The great falls on the Zambezi in the country's northwest, Mosi-oa-Tunya, the smoke that thunders, where the wide river drops into a gorge and throws up a column of spray seen for miles and a roar heard long before the water. One of the largest sheets of falling water on earth and one of the natural wonders of the world, the river marking the northern edge of the land. The baobab and the mopane on the dry plateau, the highveld stretching to the horizon, and beyond the spray the great Zambezi running on toward the sea.

## Offers

A place where the country shows the world a wonder that needs no telling, the smoke that thunders that names itself; the great river that bounds the land and waters it; a meeting-place of the nation's pride in its own ground, belonging to every people on the plateau in common, the natural counterpart to the stone city as the country's two great wonders, the one built and the one given.

## Withheld

The smallness of a mere sight. Mosi-oa-Tunya keeps the awe that humbles, the sound of the falling water that drowns the visitor's voice and puts the brief span of a life against the long age of the river; and it keeps, beneath the spray that draws the world, the truth that the wonder belongs to the land and its peoples first, a gift to be honoured and not merely sold.
