# Place: Great Zimbabwe

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

## Shown

The great stone city on the southern plateau, dzimba dzemabwe, the houses of stone from which the whole nation takes its name. The Hill Complex rising on its granite outcrop, and below it the Great Enclosure, its high curved outer wall and its conical tower dry-laid from dressed granite without a drop of mortar, the largest such structure in Africa south of the Sahara. Among the ruins that are no ruins, the soapstone birds that once stood upon the walls, the figures lifted onto the nation's flag. The proof of the builders, standing on the highveld for the centuries to see.

## Offers

A place where the nation can lay its hand on its own past, a heritage site and a place of memory where the descendants of the builders walk the walls their forebears raised; the source of the country's name, its flag's bird, and its deepest claim that the land had builders of its own; a wonder that belongs to all its peoples in common, under the one name they all carry.

## Withheld

The full reading of its mystery. Great Zimbabwe keeps much of its own story, the exact ways and meanings of those who built it, and it carries, too, the charge of its keeping: a heritage that asks each generation to tend it and not let it fall to the grass again, the proof of the builders standing only as long as the living choose to keep it standing.
