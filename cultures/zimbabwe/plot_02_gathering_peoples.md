# Plot: the gathering of the peoples

## Taxonomy

## Owner

## Cue

After the time of the great stone city, the southern plateau is held by the Shona in their many groupings, farming the highveld, herding cattle, calling their ancestors through the mbira, and carrying the heritage of the builders. The wide land between the Zambezi and the Limpopo is home, too, to the Tonga along the great river, the Venda in the south, and others, each with their own tongue and their own ground.

## Action

In the nineteenth century, in the wake of the great upheavals and migrations to the south, the Ndebele come up onto the plateau under their kings and settle in the west and the southwest, around what would become Bulawayo, bringing isiNdebele and their own way of life onto the same high land. So the country comes to be peopled by two great nations, Shona and Ndebele, held in equal dignity, alongside the Tonga, the Venda, and the rest, all sharing one plateau and, in time, one name. Through it all runs the ancestral voice rooted in the soil, honoured across the land in revered figures such as the spirit medium remembered as a grandmother of the nation. The peoples gather, distinct and yet bound, onto the one ground.

## Stage

- [hunhu](position_culture_hunhu.md): the conviction that binds the distinct peoples into one company under one name.
- [Mbuya Nehanda](persona_nehanda.md): the enduring image of the ancestral voice rooted in the soil, honoured across the land.
- [Mosi-oa-Tunya](place_mosi_oa_tunya.md): the great falls on the Zambezi, the country's northern edge and a wonder shared by all its peoples.

## Tension

The plateau becomes a land of many peoples, Shona and Ndebele foremost and the Tonga, Venda, and others besides, each holding their own tongue and ground, and the richness of the country is in that plurality held together. Yet to hold many peoples as one company in equal dignity is the standing work of every generation, the promise of hunhu that a person is a person through other people stretched to hold a whole nation, distinct and bound at once.
