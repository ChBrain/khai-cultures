# Persona: Oliver Mtukudzi

## Taxonomy

[hunhu](position_culture_hunhu.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

Tuku, the beloved singer and guitarist whose warm, husky voice and Tuku Music style, drawn from the mbira and the rhythms of the highveld, made him the conscience and the comfort of a whole country and a son of the soil known far past it. Gentle, wise, and humble, he sang of love and loss, of the family and the community, of hunhu and how a person carries themselves toward others, and the nation took his songs as elders' counsel set to a guitar. He sings most often in chiShona, his mother tongue, which he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md), and pours into song until it became the nation's own feeling [written down in melody](process_writing_mother_tongue.md). [English](position_language_en_zw.md) he [carries as the bridge tongue](process_speaking_carried.md) that took his music to the world, and the languages of the wider continent he [followed by ear](process_hearing_followed.md) on his travels and wove into his sound. He sang the hardest truths so gently that the whole country could bear to hear them, knowing that some things sit deepest in the mother tongue and that the kindest counsel is the one that leaves the listener room, so that what he left unsaid in his soft voice was its own message, the gap at the limit of the tongue where a song means more than its words.

## Action

In [the stone and the mbira go out](plot_04_stone_and_mbira.md) he carries the [ancestral voice](position_ancestral_voice.md) and the spirit of hunhu out into the world, his Tuku Music sounding from the highveld to stages across the earth. He makes the country's warmth and conscience audible, singing the community to itself and to all who would listen, the elder's word become the nation's song.

## Shadow

The beloved voice that consoles can also let a people feel that to have sung the trouble is to have answered it, the gentle song mistaken for the doing of the deed it gently asks. To love the singer and let the counsel slide, to take the comfort and skip the conscience, is to keep the melody and lose the message held inside it.

## Tell

He sings the gravest matter in the softest voice and leaves you more comforted than scolded, so that a whole nation took an album as it would take the quiet word of a trusted elder, and called him, with love, simply Tuku.
