# Position: Zimbabwean English

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The authority of English as the bridge tongue of a nation of many peoples, the working speech that crosses between the chiShona of the plateau and the isiNdebele of the west, the Tonga of the Zambezi and the Venda of the south, without belonging wholly to any of them. Carried in by the settler and the mission school, it was kept at independence as the neutral ground on which Shona and Ndebele and all the country's peoples could meet as one, the tongue of the courtroom and the parliament, the novel and the newspaper, while the deepest things are still said in the mother tongue of the clan praises, the ancestors' names, and the mbira songs.

## Orders

What the nation conducts its shared public life in across its peoples: the statute and the ruling, the school lesson and the broadcast, the literature it sends out to the world. It orders the common ground where communities of different mother tongues meet on equal terms, none made to speak inside another's language, and it carries the country's name into the letters of the wider world.

## Loses

Ground to the mother tongues that live in the home and the shrine, the chiShona and the isiNdebele in which the people pray, mourn, praise, and call the ancestors, and which the working tongue never wholly holds; ground to the ancestral voice of the mbira and the medium, older than any newcomer's speech and needing none of it; and ground when it becomes the language of the school and of advancement, thinning the deep tongues it was meant only to join.

## Drives

It drives a nation of two great peoples and many smaller to hold its shared life on ground that erases none of them. It is carried, not born, by [Oliver Mtukudzi](persona_mtukudzi.md), who sang most often in chiShona and carried English as the bridge that took his music to the world; by [Nicholas Mukomberanwa](persona_mukomberanwa.md), who thought in chiShona and let the world's galleries speak of his stone in English; and by [Yvonne Vera](persona_vera.md), who drafted a prose all her own in the bridge tongue while the cadences of isiNdebele and chiShona charged every line. It reached [Sekuru](persona_sekuru.md) only at the edge of the ear, the old mbira player who lets the world's languages pass and keeps a voice older than all of them; and it never reached [Mbuya Nehanda](persona_nehanda.md) at all, the ancestral chiShona voice who neither sought nor needed the newcomers' tongue. So the bridge tongue shows who carries it, whom it merely reaches, and the deep tongue it never touched, and binds the country's peoples without erasing the mother tongues they were born to.
