# Persona: Mbuya Nehanda

## Taxonomy

[the ancestral voice](position_ancestral_voice.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The revered spirit medium of the late nineteenth century, the woman through whom the ancestral spirit Nehanda was held to speak, honoured to this day as a grandmother of the nation, mbuya, and a cultural and spiritual icon of the deepest kind. Held in awe and tenderness, spoken of softly, she stands in the country's memory not as a figure of any faction but as the very voice of the land and the ancestors, the dignity of a people who believe the dead counsel the living. Her tongue is chiShona, and she carries it as the ancestors' own: she [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in the mother tongue, and through her the lineage [hears its own voice answered](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md), the medium become the mouth of the spirit. The languages of the newcomers she neither sought nor needed, for her authority was rooted wholly in the soil and the speech of her own people. She is remembered above all as a voice, not a pen, the ancestral word spoken aloud and passed mouth to ear down the generations, so that where her language ends and the spirit's begins is the very mystery she holds, the gap at the limit of the tongue where the living strain to hear the dead.

## Action

In [the gathering of the peoples](plot_02_gathering_peoples.md) she stands as the enduring image of the ancestral voice rooted in the land, the spirit medium honoured across the country as a grandmother of the nation. She carries the conviction that the land itself has a voice and a memory, that the ancestors hold the living to account, and that the soil and the speech of a people are one inheritance.

## Shadow

The icon so revered can be quoted and carved and set on every wall until the awe wears smooth, the grandmother of the nation honoured in name while the ancestral voice she stood for is no longer truly listened to. To venerate the medium and ignore the message, to keep the image and lose the counsel, is to honour the word and forget the keeping of it.

## Tell

She is remembered not for anything she built but for what spoke through her, a single woman become the voice of the land and the ancestors, held so dear that the nation calls her grandmother and lowers its voice when it says her name.
