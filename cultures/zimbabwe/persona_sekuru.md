# Persona: Sekuru

## Taxonomy

[the ancestral voice](position_ancestral_voice.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The old mbira player of the village, the grandfather, sekuru, who keeps the spirit songs in his hands and his memory, who is called to play the bira through the night when the family must reach its ancestors, and who teaches the keys to a grandchild before his own hands stiffen. Patient, unhurried, and gravely cheerful, he is the keeper of the ancestral voice in one man, the unbroken line sitting cross-legged behind his gourd. His tongue is chiShona, which he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md), and [hears the ancestors answer in](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md), and the old spirit songs he carries are themselves a speech, [a tongue passed mouth to ear and hand to hand](process_speaking_carried.md) down the generations. The world's languages crowding in on the young, [English](position_language_en_zw.md) among them, he [follows only at the edge](process_hearing_followed.md) and lets pass, for his work is with a voice older than any of them. He plays a music that is itself a language, the metal keys speaking what no spoken word can to the vadzimu, and when the medium falls into trance and the ancestor speaks back, the player and the family strain at the very edge of hearing, the gap at the limit of the tongue bridged for one night by the buzzing of the keys.

## Action

In [the houses of stone](plot_01_great_zimbabwe.md) he stands for the unbroken ancestral voice that runs from the builders of the stone city down to the living, the keeper of the [ancestral voice](position_ancestral_voice.md) who carries the oldest music into the present night. He proves the line has never broken: the same spirits the forebears called are called still, by the same keys, in the same tongue.

## Shadow

The old player can take the spirit songs to the grave if no young hand will learn them, the ancestral voice falling silent not by force but by neglect, a grandchild who never sat to learn the keys and so cannot call the family's dead. To honour the elder and not learn his music, to love the sound and not take up the gourd, is to let the line break in one's own hands.

## Tell

He sets a grandchild's small fingers on the metal keys and waits, unhurried, through every wrong note, knowing the ancestors can wait a season longer but not forever, and that the whole inheritance passes in this one quiet lesson or passes not at all.
