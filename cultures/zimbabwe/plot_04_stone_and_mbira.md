# Plot: the stone and the mbira go out

## Taxonomy

[play_zimbabwe](play_zimbabwe.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

In the modern age the country's oldest voices find new ears. The working of stone, the calling of the ancestors through the mbira, and the songs born of that ancestral music do not stay on the plateau but begin to carry, through galleries and recordings and stages, far past the land that made them.

## Action

The Shona stone sculptors take up the chisel where the dry-laid granite left off, and the new Zimbabwean sculpture, its brooding ancestor-heads and bound families drawn from serpentine and springstone, stands in galleries across the world as the country's name carved anew. The mbira dzavadzimu, once heard only at the bira through the night, sounds out into the world through the chimurenga music it gave birth to and through the songs of masters like Thomas Mapfumo and Oliver Mtukudzi, whose Tuku Music carries the highveld's warmth and conscience to stages on every continent. The ancestral voice and the houses of stone, the country's deepest inheritances, go out and tell the world who Zimbabwe is.

## Tension

The country's oldest voices, the stone and the mbira, become its gift to the world and the proof of its living art, the ancestors' work carried far by modern hands. Yet what travels can also thin: the sacred mbira taken only as entertainment, the spirit-figures crowded by quick copies for quick sale, so that the going-out asks the country to keep the depth alongside the reach, the bira behind the concert and the spirit behind the stone.
