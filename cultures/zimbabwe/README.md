# Zimbabwe: a khai culture

Zimbabwe staged as a full khai play, ISO ZW, language en (the bridge tongue
honouring both nations, with Shona and Ndebele woven in). A nation named for its
houses of stone, as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the
cast, the convictions its heritage and its faith encode, its places, its
practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_zimbabwe.md](play_zimbabwe.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Zimbabwe's profile (deep communalism and the extended family, respect for age
  and the ancestors, patience and endurance, a long view shaped by hard seasons)
  and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the houses of stone, the gathering of the peoples, the
  taking of the name, and the stone and the mbira going out to the world - each
  casting the elements it needs, and each staged soberly and with dignity.
- **Personas:** Mbuya Nehanda (the revered spirit medium and cultural-spiritual
  icon), Oliver Mtukudzi (Tuku, the beloved musician), Nicholas Mukomberanwa (the
  master stone sculptor), and Yvonne Vera (the novelist of Bulawayo) as the
  historic cast, plus one archetype, Sekuru (the old mbira player who passes the
  ancestral voice to the young). Each is wired to the language engine by the
  language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** hunhu / ubuntu (a person is a person through other people, the
  living and the dead one company), the houses of stone (dzimba dzemabwe, the
  granite heritage from which the nation takes its name), the ancestral voice (the
  mbira dzavadzimu and the spirits it calls).
- **Places:** Great Zimbabwe (the stone city, the source of the name and the
  bird), Mosi-oa-Tunya (the smoke that thunders, the great falls on the Zambezi).
- **Processes:** the bira (the all-night mbira ceremony that calls the
  ancestors), cooking sadza (the daily meal that gathers the household as one).
- **Pieces:** the Zimbabwe Bird (the soapstone bird lifted from the stone city
  onto the nation's emblem), Shona stone sculpture (the country's oldest art made
  new and carried to the world).
