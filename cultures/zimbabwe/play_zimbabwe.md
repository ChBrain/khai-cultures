# Play: Zimbabwe

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the house that holds this staging and answers for it.

## Name

Zimbabwe, a high plateau country in southern Africa, landlocked between the great Zambezi in the north and the Limpopo in the south, with Harare on the highveld for its capital. ISO ZW. The name itself is taken from the stone: dzimba dzemabwe, the houses of stone, those mortarless granite walls our forebears raised at Great Zimbabwe. We are a nation that wears the name of a ruin that is no ruin at all but a standing proof, a country of many tongues and many peoples who answer, in the end, to the one stone city that gave us all our name.

## Arc

We are old before we are new. Long before any line was drawn on a map, the ancestors of the Shona built a city of stone on the southern plateau, dry-laid granite without mortar, the largest such structure south of the Sahara, and on its walls and its altars they set the soapstone birds that watch over us still. The gold of the plateau moved down to the coast and the wealth of the city rose, and then, in its own time, the court moved on and the walls were left to the grass and the lichen, holding their secret. Onto this same high land came the Ndebele under their kings, an offshoot of the great migrations from the south, settling in the west and the southwest, so that the country was peopled by two great nations, Shona and Ndebele, with the Tonga along the Zambezi and the Venda in the south and others besides, each holding their own and yet bound to one ground. The land knew the carving of stone and the calling of the ancestors through the mbira dzavadzimu, the thumb-piano whose voice the spirits answer; it knew sadza in the pot and the long highveld stretching to the horizon; it knew Mosi-oa-Tunya, the smoke that thunders, where the Zambezi falls into its gorge. When the country came at last to take its own name, it reached past every borrowed word and lifted up the name of the stone city: Zimbabwe, after dzimba dzemabwe, so that the nation declares its descent from the builders every time it says who it is. Under it all runs one nerve, hunhu, ubuntu, that a person is a person through other people, that the living and the dead are one company, and that the stone, the carving, and the mbira are the same voice saying the same thing: we were here, we are here, and the ancestors are not finished with us.

## Triggers

**[Plot 1: the houses of stone](plot_01_great_zimbabwe.md)**

Between roughly the eleventh and the fifteenth centuries our forebears raise [the houses of stone](position_houses_of_stone.md) at [Great Zimbabwe](place_great_zimbabwe.md), the dry-laid granite city whose name the whole nation now carries, and set the soapstone birds upon its walls.

**[Plot 2: the gathering of the peoples](plot_02_gathering_peoples.md)**

Across the centuries the high plateau is peopled by two great nations and many smaller, the Shona long settled and the Ndebele coming up under their kings to the west, so that the land of [hunhu](position_hunhu.md) is shared in equal dignity by Shona and Ndebele together.

**[Plot 3: the taking of the name](plot_03_taking_the_name.md)**

In 1980 the country, coming into its own, reaches past every borrowed word and lifts up the name of the stone city, declaring itself Zimbabwe after dzimba dzemabwe and binding its [houses of stone](position_houses_of_stone.md) into its very name.

**[Plot 4: the stone and the mbira go out](plot_04_stone_and_mbira.md)**

In the modern age the [ancestral voice](position_ancestral_voice.md) carries to the world, the Shona stone sculptors making the country a name in stone again and the mbira of [Oliver Mtukudzi](persona_mtukudzi.md) and his fellow players sounding far past the plateau.

## Stakes

Whether a nation named for the houses of stone can keep faith with the builders, holding Shona and Ndebele and all its peoples in equal dignity under the one name; whether [hunhu](position_hunhu.md) can hold a person a person through other people when the world pulls each one to stand alone; whether the [ancestral voice](position_ancestral_voice.md) of the mbira and the sculptor's stone keeps sounding in the young as it sounded in the old; and whether, through every hard season the land has known, the country can restand its inheritance the way the forebears laid the granite, stone upon stone and no mortar, so that it holds.
