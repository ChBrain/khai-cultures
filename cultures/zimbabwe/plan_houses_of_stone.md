# Plan: the houses of stone, restanding

## Taxonomy

The standing charge a nation takes on when it names itself for [the houses of stone](position_houses_of_stone.md): to keep the granite of the builders standing and tended; to hold Shona and Ndebele and all its peoples in the equal dignity of [hunhu](position_culture_hunhu.md), one company under one name; to keep the [ancestral voice](position_ancestral_voice.md) of the mbira sounding in the young as it sounded in the old; and to carry the country's stone and song out to the world without thinning the depth from which they came.

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Direction

Tend the inheritance the way the forebears laid the granite, stone upon stone and no mortar, so that it holds: keep Great Zimbabwe and the country's heritage standing and known; hold the many peoples as one in equal dignity, the western city no less than the capital; pass the mbira and the stone hand to hand to the young so the ancestral voice is not orphaned; and let the country's art and music carry far while the bira behind the concert and the spirit behind the stone are kept whole.

## Orders

- To the elder like [Sekuru](persona_sekuru.md): pass the keys and the spirit songs to the young hands before they stiffen, so the line is not broken in your keeping.
- To the artist like [Nicholas Mukomberanwa](persona_mukomberanwa.md): carry the country's stone out to the world and keep the spirit in it, the depth alongside the reach.
- To the nation: keep the houses of stone standing and hold all its peoples in equal dignity under the one name, never mistaking the keeping of the name for the keeping of its charge.

## Implementation

A nation old in its heritage and steady in its peoples, that has named itself for its builders, lifted their bird onto its flag, and carried its stone sculpture and its mbira music to the galleries and stages of the world, and that holds open still the standing work of every generation: the tending of the ancient stone, the holding of the many peoples as one in equal dignity, and the passing of the ancestral voice unbroken to the young.
