# Position: the houses of stone

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The proof in granite, dzimba dzemabwe, the houses of stone from which the whole nation takes its name. It holds that our forebears built greatly, dry-laying granite without a drop of mortar into walls that have stood the centuries, the Great Enclosure with its towering curved wall and conical tower, the largest stone structure in Africa south of the Sahara. It is the standing answer to anyone who would say the land had no builders: a city of stone, raised by the ancestors of the Shona, that gave its name to a country and set the soapstone bird upon its emblem. The stone is heritage made visible, the past you can lay your hand on.

## Orders

That the nation descends from builders and says so every time it speaks its name, so the past is not a thing lost but a foundation walked upon; that what was laid stone upon stone without mortar was laid to hold, and asks of each generation that it tend and not let fall; and that the heritage of the stone belongs to all the peoples who share the land, Shona and Ndebele and every other, as the common inheritance under the one name.

## Loses

Ground when the stone is left to the grass and the lichen, its keeping neglected and its lesson forgotten; ground, too, when its meaning is narrowed to one people or one telling, when the whole point of dzimba dzemabwe is that it is the foundation the entire nation stands on, and the proof that what the ancestors built, the living are charged to keep standing.

## Drives

Lives in [Nicholas Mukomberanwa](persona_mukomberanwa.md), who took the country's name in stone back into the world through his hands, and stands behind [the Zimbabwe Bird](piece_zimbabwe_bird.md) lifted from the city's walls onto the nation's flag. It drives Zimbabwe to carry the builders' name with pride and the builders' charge with care, restanding its inheritance the way the granite was first laid, to hold.
