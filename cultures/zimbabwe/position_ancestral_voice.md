# Position: the ancestral voice

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The conviction that the ancestors, the vadzimu, are not gone but present, and that they speak and are spoken to through the voice of the mbira dzavadzimu, the thumb-piano of the spirits. It holds that the metal keys of the mbira, struck over its gourd resonator with the small shells buzzing, call the spirits of the lineage to the bira through the long night, that the ancestors answer through the medium who falls into trance, and that the same voice runs on through the chimurenga music born of the mbira and through the songs of those who, like Mtukudzi and Mapfumo, carried the old voice into the modern world. It is the line unbroken: the dead and the living singing back and forth across one drum.

## Orders

That the past is a presence and not a memory, the ancestors consulted, thanked, and asked at every turn of the family's life; that music is no mere pleasure but a calling, the mbira a sacred instrument that gathers the spirits and not a toy; and that the voice must be passed hand to hand, the young learning the keys from the old so the line is not broken and the ancestors are not left without a tongue.

## Loses

Ground when the voice is cut off from the young, when the mbira falls silent in a household and a generation grows up with no one to call the spirits; ground, too, when the music is taken only as entertainment, stripped of the bira and the lineage, so the keys still sound but the ancestors are no longer in the room.

## Drives

Lives in the revered spirit medium through whom the ancestral voice was held to speak, and in the player who knows the spirit songs by heart and calls the family's dead by name. It drives Zimbabwe to keep the mbira sounding, to hold the bira through the night, and to trust that as long as the keys are struck, the ancestral voice will answer.
