# Process: the bira

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

A family with a matter to lay before its ancestors: an illness to be understood, a guidance to be sought, a debt of thanks to be paid to the vadzimu. The relatives gather at the homestead as night falls, the mbira players are called, the women take up the hosho rattles and the songs, and the long all-night ceremony begins, beer brewed, a place prepared, the family settling in to call its dead.

## Direction

Toward the ancestors and the unbroken line: the bira is the night the living reach the dead, the mbira dzavadzimu sounding hour upon hour until the spirit answers, descending upon the medium who falls into trance and speaks for the ancestor among the gathered family. It is not performance but communion, the household made whole across the line of death, the vadzimu consulted, thanked, and asked, the family knit to the generations that made it.

## Lever

It is driven by the conviction that the ancestors are present and answer when called, and by the sacred power of the mbira to call them, the metal keys and the buzzing shells the very voice that the spirits hear. It holds against the long night, the patience it asks, and the world that would crowd it out; against it stand the silencing of the mbira where the young no longer learn it, and the loss of the elder who alone knows the family's songs.

## Echo

The bira makes audible what holds the country together below all else, the faith that a person is a person through other people, the living among them and the dead among them too, all one company across the night. It is the ancestral voice answered in the dark, the proof renewed each time that the line has not broken and the vadzimu are not finished with the living.
