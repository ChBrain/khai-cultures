# Persona: Nanny of the Maroons

## Taxonomy

[the resistance](position_the_resistance.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The Windward Maroon leader of the early eighteenth century, a woman of African birth who led the free people of the hills against the British and is now a National Hero of Jamaica, her face on the currency and her name on the land. Fierce, cunning, and revered, a leader and an obeah-woman in the eyes of her people, she held the high ground of the interior, struck at the plantations, freed the enslaved, and fought the empire to a treaty that left the Maroons free. The tongue she carried from Africa was her own and shaping: an Akan mother tongue she [spoke](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), [thought](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md), and [heard](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) from her first day, the Twi of her homeland, and in the hills it became Kromanti, the Maroon ritual tongue of council and ceremony she [carried as her own](process_speaking_carried.md) and passed to her people. The [English of the planters](position_language_en_jm.md) she met only as the speech of the enemy, [caught by ear](process_hearing_followed.md) from across the lines and never truly hers, the tongue of the treaty she signed but not of the heart she kept. She led a free people in the language Africa gave her, in the hills the empire could not climb, and English remained the tongue of Babylon at the foot of the mountain - so the gap at the limit of the tongue was the line she defended, the speech of the free kept apart from the speech of those who came to enslave them. She holds [out of many, one people](position_culture_out_of_many_one_people.md) at its root, for the free nation the Maroons founded in the hills is the first proof that a people thrown together against their will could still stand as one.

## Action

In [the Maroons and the Cane](plot_01_the_maroons_and_the_cane.md) she raises [the resistance](position_the_resistance.md) into a free nation in the hills, fighting the British to a standstill and to a treaty, and showing the island that bondage could be refused and freedom held by arms. She makes the Maroon hills the first free ground, and her name the proof that a people are not what is done to them but what they do in answer.

## Shadow

The freedom she won was a freedom apart, bought by treaty with the empire, and the same treaty that kept the Maroons free is said to have bound them to return others who fled - the resistance set against itself, the price of survival in a corner of the hills. The pride of those who got free can shadow the cost paid by those left behind, and the honour of the hero can be made to forget the hardness of the bargain.

## Tell

She stands on a hilltop the redcoats cannot take, reading the land like a book the enemy cannot open, and answers the empire not in its tongue but in her own - keeping a free people free in the speech Africa carried to the mountain.
