# Position: out of many, one people

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The national motto and the truth it names: a people made of many roots - African above all, and Taino, European, Indian, Chinese, Lebanese, Maroon - bound into one nation that is plural to its core and still one. It is carried not by blood or by a single church but by a shared way of being: the food, the faith, the riddim, and most of all the Patois, the tongue every Jamaican knows, that no school taught and no master gave, the language the many speak as one. And it is a unity won out of the gap between the roots, the long work of making one people out of those the plantation threw together against their will.

## Orders

That the nation is defined by its mix and not by any one strand, so the many origins are all equally Jamaican; that Patois is the common ground, the tongue that binds the yard and the dancehall and the diaspora across the world; and that the work of the nation is to keep the many as one, to let no root be ranked above another, and to carry the whole of the mix as the island's pride.

## Loses

Ground when the motto becomes a slogan on a coat of arms rather than a lived thing - when colour and class still sort the island quietly beneath the words, when the lightest are favoured and the Patois is shamed as broken speech instead of honoured as a tongue; and ground, too, to the violence and division that split community from community, so the one people fractures into the warring corners the motto was meant to bind.

## Drives

Lives in [Louise Bennett](persona_louise_bennett.md), Miss Lou, who raised the Patois of all the people into letters and pride and showed the island its own face and its own voice, the many speaking as one. It drives Jamaica to hold its many roots together, to honour the tongue that binds them, and to make the motto true in the street and not only on the seal.
