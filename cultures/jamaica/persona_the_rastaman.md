# Persona: the Rastaman

## Taxonomy

[reggae and Rastafari](position_reggae_and_rastafari.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The keeper of the Rastafari faith and the livity from which the music grew: a man of the yard with locks uncut, who lives by an ital way, chants Nyabinghi to the drum, and holds himself a child of the Most High and a stranger in Babylon. Devout, dignified, and defiant, he answers a hard world with praise and a steady refusal to bow, and from his faith and his drum came the conscience at the root of reggae. The [Patois of the yard](position_language_en_jm.md) is his mother tongue, and he has bent it to his faith: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md), and [hears](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) in it, and out of it he has shaped Iyaric, the Rasta talk of I-and-I and overstanding, a tongue of his own making. The King James scripture he [reads and deciphers](process_reading_deciphered.md) word by word, turning its lines to his own light, and the proper English of Babylon he [carries](process_speaking_carried.md) only as far as he must. He reworks the master's English into a speech of his own, refusing the words that wound and remaking the rest, so the gap at the limit of the tongue becomes the very place he plants his freedom: where the borrowed language failed him, he built a tongue Babylon cannot fully read. He too holds [out of many, one people](position_culture_out_of_many_one_people.md), for the livity he keeps binds African root and yard-born faith into the one nation the motto names.

## Action

In [Independence and Reggae](plot_03_independence_and_reggae.md) he keeps [reggae and Rastafari](position_reggae_and_rastafari.md) at its living root, the faith and the drum and the livity from which the world's reggae sprang. He carries the conscience of the music - the praise, the protest, the dignity of the downpressed - and gives the island's sound the soul that the world hears beneath the riddim.

## Shadow

The faith he keeps with his whole life is sold to the world as a flavour: the locks a fashion, the herb a holiday, the livity a beach mood, the deep refusal of Babylon flattened into an easy calm for tourists. The world that loves the look can miss the witness, and the dignity he built can be worn by others as a costume emptied of its cost.

## Tell

He greets you with I-and-I and blessings in a tongue he remade from the master's own, locks down his back and the drum at his side, at peace with the Most High and at war with no man but Babylon - free in a speech he built where the borrowed one let him down.
