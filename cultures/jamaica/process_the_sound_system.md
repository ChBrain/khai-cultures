# Process: the Sound System

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

A selector with a stack of giant speakers, a crate of records, and a corner of the street: the sound system crew sets up the towers of bass at a yard or a lawn, the deejay takes the mic to ride the riddim, and the dance is on. It takes only the speakers, the tunes, and a crowd, and the whole neighbourhood comes out to feel the bass in the chest.

## Direction

Toward the music made the people's own: the street dance where the sound is not bought from afar but built, cut, and toasted right there in the yard. The selector spins, the deejay chats over the riddim, the crowd answers, and the rivalry of system against system - the sound clash - drives the music ever forward. It is not just a party but the island's own engine of sound, the place where ska became rocksteady, rocksteady became reggae, and the deejay toasting on the mic became dancehall.

## Lever

It is driven by the deep Jamaican faith that the music belongs to the street and not to the studio alone, and by the speaker stack that put the power of the sound in the hands of the yard. It holds against poverty and the want of a stage, turning a corner and a crate of records into a national art; against it stand the noise and the rivalry that can tip from clash into conflict, the bass that can wake a war as well as a dance.

## Echo

The sound system makes audible what drives [reggae and Rastafari](position_reggae_and_rastafari.md): that the island's reach begins in its own streets, the music built from the bottom up and carried out to the world. On these speakers the riddim that [Bob Marley](persona_bob_marley.md) carried across the earth was first cut and clashed and chanted in the yards of Kingston, the sound made the people's before it was made the world's.
