# Persona: Bob Marley

## Taxonomy

[reggae and Rastafari](position_reggae_and_rastafari.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The reggae prophet of Trench Town, the son of a poor Kingston yard who carried the island and its faith to the whole earth, the voice through which a likkle nation spoke to the world. Devout, magnetic, and unbreakable, a Rastaman who turned the riddim into a message and the message into a movement, he sang of one love and of standing up for your rights until the planet sang it back to him. The [Patois of the yard](position_language_en_jm.md) was his mother tongue and his instrument: he [spoke](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), [thought](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md), and [heard](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) in it, and he [wrote](process_writing_mother_tongue.md) his songs out of its cadence, the speech of Trench Town raised into psalm. [The world's standard English](../usa/position_language_en_us.md) he [carried](process_speaking_carried.md) when the world's stages required it, and the [King James scripture](../england/position_language_en_gb_eng.md) the Rastaman reads he [followed deeply on the page](process_reading_followed.md), folding Zion and Babylon into the riddim. He sang the gospel of the downpressed in the Patois his island made, and the world learned to chant his Jamaican words by heart even where they could not parse them - so the gap at the limit of the tongue closed into music, the one place where a small island's own speech needed no translation to be understood by all the earth.

## Action

In [Independence and Reggae](plot_03_independence_and_reggae.md) he carries [reggae and Rastafari](position_reggae_and_rastafari.md) out of the Kingston yards and across the whole world, making a newly free island the voice of the oppressed everywhere and its riddim the planet's. He turns the island's faith and its hardship into a song the earth cannot stop singing, and makes a small nation a cultural superpower by the power of its sound.

## Shadow

The prophet of one love came of an island that could not hold its own peace, was nearly shot in the violence of its politics, and is now sold the world over as a face on a T-shirt, the message of the downpressed flattened into a beach-side mellow. The reach that carried his word can hollow it, the riddim that warned and witnessed reduced to a holiday mood, and the cost behind the calm forgotten in the brand.

## Tell

He sings a song made in a Trench Town yard, in the Patois of the poorest corner of a small island, and stadiums on every continent chant it back word for word - a likkle nation's own tongue understood across the whole earth without a single line translated.
