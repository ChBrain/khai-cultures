# Place: Kingston

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Shown

The capital on the great natural harbour of the south coast, the largest English-speaking city of the Caribbean, hard against the Blue Mountains and spread between the wealthy hills uptown and the dense, poor yards downtown. Trench Town and the inner-city neighbourhoods where reggae was born, the studios where the sound was cut, the dancehalls and the street corners, and the seat of the government of a free nation. The engine of the island's music, and the place where its riddim and its hardship live side by side.

## Offers

A place where a small island's outsized voice is made: where the sound system rules the street, where the studios turned the speech and the suffering of the poorest yards into the world's reggae, and where the nation governs itself and shows the world the riddim it gave it. The downtown yard that fed the planet a sound, and the uptown hills that hold the island's wealth.

## Withheld

The ease of the brand the world buys. Kingston carries the gap between the global fame of the music born in its yards and the hard life of the streets that made it - the inequality between the hills and the ghetto, the violence that has shadowed its corners, and the youth with no work in the very city the world dances to. The capital that gave the earth one love knows the cost of the streets that sang it.
