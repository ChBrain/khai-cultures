# Plot: Emancipation and Garvey

## Taxonomy

[play_jamaica](play_jamaica.md)

## Owner

## Cue

The bondage at the heart of the cane economy cannot hold forever. Rebellion after rebellion shakes the plantations, and pressure at home and on the island pushes the empire toward the end of slavery. Across the British Caribbean the day of freedom is set, and in Jamaica the freed people walk out of bondage into a world that has not yet decided to give them a way to live.

## Action

In 1838 full emancipation comes to Jamaica, and the enslaved are free at last - but freedom proves only the start of the work, for the land, the wealth, and the power stay in the old hands, and the freed are left to build a life on the margins of the estate that once owned them. Out of this unfinished freedom, decades on, rises [Marcus Garvey](persona_marcus_garvey.md), born at St. Ann's Bay, who turns the will to be free into a vision for a whole scattered people: look to Africa, look to yourselves, rise up and stand. He builds a movement that crosses oceans and lights a fire in the diaspora, and at home he plants the seed from which Rastafari and the island's faith will grow. Emancipation freed the body; Garvey calls the people to free the mind, and sets the island on the long road to nationhood.

## Stage

- [Cockpit Country](place_cockpit_country.md): the free Maroon ground, the older proof that the island had always answered back.
- [the resistance](position_the_resistance.md): the refusal carried forward from the hills into a vision for the whole diaspora.

## Tension

Emancipation is the promise kept on paper and withheld in fact - the chains struck off while the land, the wealth, and the say stay with the old masters, so the gap between a freedom proclaimed and a freedom lived runs straight through the day of jubilee. Garvey names that gap and answers it, telling a freed but still-bound people that the work is not done until the mind is free and the people stand, and so the road from emancipation to nationhood is the long closing of the distance between being let go and being truly free.
