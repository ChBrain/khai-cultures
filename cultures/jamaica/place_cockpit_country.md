# Place: Cockpit Country

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Shown

The wild limestone interior of the northwest, a vast maze of conical hills and deep sinkholes, of cockpits and blind valleys, cloaked in dense forest and threaded with hidden caves and underground rivers. A land that defeats the road and the map, where the only way through is the way you already know. Here the Leeward Maroons made their stronghold, and to this day the free Maroon town of Accompong keeps its ground in the hills the empire could never take.

## Holds

## Offers

A place where freedom was held by the land itself: a country so broken and so dense that a free people could vanish into it, strike from it, and keep it against an empire. The fastness that made the treaty possible, the hills that turned the will to be free into a place to be free, and the living Maroon ground where that freedom is kept to this day.

## Withheld

The reach of the wider nation. Cockpit Country holds the gap between the freedom won in the hills and the bondage that ground on below them - a free corner inside a colony of cane, a refuge bought by the very wildness that walled it off. And it carries still the question of its keeping: a unique and threatened land, guarding its waters and its forest and its Maroon heritage against the pressures that would open it up.
