# Jamaica: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-record sources behind the plots,
personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md) is written from Jamaica's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record without
entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Jamaica score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ----------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 45                      |
| Individualism                 | 39                      |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 68                      |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 13                      |
| Long Term Orientation         | n/a                     |
| Indulgence                    | n/a                     |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; Jamaica is noted in the published
data for a very low Uncertainty Avoidance, and not all dimensions are published for
every country.

## Historical and cultural sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, the processes, and the
pieces are drawn from the public record. They are credited here and never claimed;
the staging is original work. The hard root of the story - the enslavement that
built the cane economy and the Maroon wars - is staged soberly as the gap between
promise and reality, with resistance and pride at the centre, never in atrocity
detail.

The one defining archetype, [the Rastaman](persona_the_rastaman.md), is not a
historical individual but a composite: the keeper of the Rastafari faith and the
livity from which reggae grew, carrying the tension between the spirit of the yard
and the brand the world buys.
