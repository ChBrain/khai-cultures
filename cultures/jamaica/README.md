# Jamaica: a khai culture

Jamaica staged as a full khai play, ISO JM, language en. A small island that
became a global cultural superpower, likkle but tallawah, as a theatre: its
history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its language and faith
encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single
pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_jamaica.md](play_jamaica.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Jamaica's profile (bold and proverb-rich, devout and defiant, present- and
  enjoyment-focused, a pride that bows to no master) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Maroons and the Cane, Emancipation and Garvey,
  Independence and Reggae, and Likkle but Tallawah - each casting the elements it
  needs, and each staging the gap between the promise and the reality honestly,
  with resistance and pride at the centre.
- **Personas:** Nanny of the Maroons, Marcus Garvey, Bob Marley, and Louise
  Bennett (Miss Lou) as the historic cast, plus one defining archetype, the
  Rastaman (the keeper of the faith and the livity from which reggae grew). Each is
  wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds, the English and
  Patois mother tongue against the ancestral African tongue carried to the island.
- **Positions:** out of many, one people (the plural-but-one nation and the Patois
  that binds it), the resistance (Maroon and Pan-African pride, from Nanny to
  Garvey), reggae and Rastafari (the faith and the sound carried to the world).
- **Places:** Kingston (the capital, Trench Town, the engine of the music) and
  Cockpit Country (the wild limestone interior, the Maroon redoubt).
- **Processes:** the sound system (the street dance of the speaker stack, root of
  dancehall) and Jonkonnu (the Christmas masquerade that carried the African root
  through the plantation).
- **Pieces:** jerk (the pimento-smoked cooking of the Maroons, the island's
  most-loved export) and the Nyabinghi drum (the Rastafari hand drum, the heartbeat
  of reggae).
