# Plot: Likkle but Tallawah

## Taxonomy

[play_jamaica](play_jamaica.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

A free nation, its music gone round the world, sets about the harder work of building a country at home and carrying its name abroad. The diaspora grows in London, New York, and Toronto, the brand of the island travels far ahead of its size, and a proverb names the whole enterprise: wi likkle but wi tallawah - we are small, but we are strong.

## Action

Modern Jamaica makes its smallness no measure of its reach. [Louise Bennett](persona_louise_bennett.md), Miss Lou, raises the Patois of all the people into letters and onto the air, giving the nation pride in its own tongue and its own face. On the track the island becomes the fastest nation on earth, its sprinters - Usain Bolt at their head - sweeping the world's stadiums and stamping the gold and green on every finish line. The diaspora carries the food, the music, and the speech of the island into the great cities of the world, and dancehall sends a new Jamaican sound out after reggae. A nation of fewer than three million holds a place in the world's imagination far beyond its size - in music, in sport, in style - and gathers its many roots and its scattered children under one motto: out of many, one people.

## Stage

- [Louise Bennett](persona_louise_bennett.md): Miss Lou, who raises the people's tongue into pride and letters.
- [Kingston](place_kingston.md): the capital from which the music, the speech, and the brand go out to the world.
- [out of many, one people](position_out_of_many_one_people.md): the motto that holds the many roots and the diaspora as one.

## Tension

The island carries a reach the whole world feels and a struggle the world does not always see - the fastest nation on earth and the brand on every shelf set against the youth with no work, the violence on the corner, and the gap between the fame abroad and the fight at home. Likkle but tallawah is both the boast and the burden: the pride of a small nation that stands tall before the world, and the open question of whether it can turn that reach into a way up for its own, and hold its many as one.
