# Piece: the Nyabinghi drum

## Taxonomy

Parent group: pieces

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Place

At the Rastafari grounation and the Nyabinghi chant, in the yard and at the assembly: the hand drums of the faith, played in a set of three - the deep bass, the steady fundeh, and the lead repeater that rides above them - struck with the hand to carry the chant through the night. From the Rasta gathering where the riddim was first beaten to the heartbeat under all the island's music and the reggae the world plays.

## Load Bearing

It stands for the African heart kept beating through everything done to it, the root from which the island's sound grew. The Nyabinghi drumming carried the African drum tradition through the plantation and into Rastafari, and from the steady pulse of the bass and the fundeh and the talk of the repeater came the very feel of reggae - the one-drop, the deep downbeat, the riddim that the whole world would learn. It is the spiritual root beneath the riddim, the praise of the Most High beaten on skin and wood, the drum that turned suffering into chant and chant into the heartbeat of a global music. Without this pulse the island's sound would have no heart, and reggae no root in the faith that bore it.

## Apparent

A set of three hand drums of skin stretched over wood, beaten with the palms and fingers, deep and resonant and plain. In itself it is only a drum; its weight is in the chant it carries and the faith it serves - the Nyabinghi praise, the African pulse kept alive - and in the hands that beat it through the night to lift the spirit and call the riddim into being.
