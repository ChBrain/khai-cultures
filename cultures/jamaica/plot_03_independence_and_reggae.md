# Plot: Independence and Reggae

## Taxonomy

[play_jamaica](play_jamaica.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

The long road from emancipation runs at last toward nationhood. The labour risings of the nineteen-thirties give birth to the parties and the unions, the call for self-rule grows, and in the yards of Kingston a new faith and a new sound are stirring among the poor. The gold, green, and black is readied, and the island stands at the door of its own freedom.

## Action

On the sixth of August, 1962, Jamaica wins its independence, and a free nation raises its own flag over its own land. In the same years, in the poorest corners of Kingston, Rastafari rises and the music quickens - ska giving way to rocksteady and then to reggae, the riddim of the downpressed. Out of a Trench Town yard comes [Bob Marley](persona_bob_marley.md), a Rastaman with a guitar and a message, and he carries [the Rastaman](persona_the_rastaman.md)'s faith and the island's hardship into a song the whole world will sing. Reggae goes out from a tiny new nation to every continent, the voice of the oppressed everywhere, one love and stand up for your rights chanted back from stadiums on every shore. A small island, free at last, becomes a cultural superpower by the power of its sound.

## Stage

- [Bob Marley](persona_bob_marley.md): the reggae prophet who carries the island and its faith to the whole earth.
- [the Rastaman](persona_the_rastaman.md): the keeper of the livity and the drum from which the music grew.
- [Kingston](place_kingston.md): the capital and its yards, the engine of the music and the home of the sound.

## Tension

A nation wins its freedom and finds its voice in the same breath - and the voice is loudest from the poorest yards, the music of the downpressed rising out of the very corners independence had not yet lifted. The gap between the global fame of the sound and the local hardship of the yard that made it is the tension at the heart of the triumph: the island the whole world dances to is also the island still climbing, and reggae carries both the joy of the free nation and the witness of the streets it came from.
