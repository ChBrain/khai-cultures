# Play: Jamaica

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the house that holds this staging and answers for it.

## Name

Jamaica, the third-largest island of the Greater Antilles, a mountainous tropical land in the Caribbean Sea south of Cuba, its capital Kingston on the southern coast. ISO JM, language en, with Jamaican Patois the tongue of the heart and the yard. A small nation, likkle but tallawah, whose reach in music and sport runs far beyond its size.

## Arc

A small island whose story is far larger than its shores. First the home of the Taino, then a Spanish and after that an English colony built on sugar and the labour of the enslaved brought from Africa - the gap between the wealth of the plantation and the bondage that made it is where the whole story begins. Into the wild limestone hills of the interior went the Maroons, who fought the British to a standstill and won a treaty, led by Nanny, a woman the nation now keeps as a hero. Emancipation came in 1838, and freedom proved only the start of the work. Out of the island rose Marcus Garvey, the Pan-Africanist prophet who told a scattered people to look to Africa and to themselves, and out of his teaching grew Rastafari, a faith that turned suffering into livity and praise. Independence came in 1962, the gold and green and black raised over a free nation, and then the island did the thing no one foresaw: it sang. Reggae, carried to the world by Bob Marley, made a tiny country the voice of the oppressed everywhere, and the sprinters, Usain Bolt and the rest, made it the fastest nation on earth. Patois, jerk, the sound system, "out of many, one people" - a small island of outsized cultural and athletic reach. Under it all runs one nerve: a proud, devout, defiant people, likkle but tallawah, who took everything taken from them and made of it a voice the whole earth now sings.

## Triggers

**[Plot 1: the Maroons and the Cane](plot_01_the_maroons_and_the_cane.md)**

The Taino meet the Spanish and then the English, who build a colony of sugar on the labour of the enslaved; into the hills go the Maroons, and [Nanny of the Maroons](persona_nanny.md) leads [the resistance](position_the_resistance.md) that fights the British to a treaty.

**[Plot 2: Emancipation and Garvey](plot_02_emancipation_and_garvey.md)**

Emancipation comes in 1838 and freedom proves only the beginning; [Marcus Garvey](persona_marcus_garvey.md) raises [the resistance](position_the_resistance.md) into a Pan-African vision, telling a scattered people to look to Africa and to rise, and sets the island on the road to nationhood.

**[Plot 3: Independence and Reggae](plot_03_independence_and_reggae.md)**

In 1962 the island wins independence, Rastafari rises in the yards of Kingston, and [Bob Marley](persona_bob_marley.md) carries [reggae and Rastafari](position_reggae_and_rastafari.md) out of Trench Town and across the whole earth, the voice of a small island heard everywhere.

**[Plot 4: Likkle but Tallawah](plot_04_likkle_but_tallawah.md)**

Modern Jamaica makes its size no measure of its reach: [Louise Bennett](persona_louise_bennett.md) raises the Patois into letters and pride, the sprinters make it the fastest nation on earth, and [out of many, one people](position_culture_out_of_many_one_people.md) holds the many roots as one across the island and its diaspora.

## Stakes

Whether a small island that became a global cultural superpower can turn that reach into opportunity at home - into work, into schooling, into a way up for the youth - and can heal the inequality and the violence that shadow the swagger, can hold the many roots of [out of many, one people](position_culture_out_of_many_one_people.md) together as one, and can keep the pride and the defiance of a people likkle but tallawah without letting the gap between the global fame and the local struggle pull it apart.
