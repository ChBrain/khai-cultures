# Position: reggae and Rastafari

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The faith and the sound that carried a small island to the whole earth: Rastafari, the Jamaican-born movement of livity, dreadlocks, and praise that grew from Garvey's teaching and turned suffering into a way of life and the Most High into a present help; and reggae, the riddim born in the yards of Kingston that gave that faith a voice and sent it out across the world. Together they made Jamaica a cultural superpower, the island whose music the whole planet plays and whose message - of the downpressed lifted, of one love, of standing up for your rights - reached the oppressed everywhere. It is the alchemy at the heart of the nation: the worst that was done turned into the sweetest thing it gave.

## Orders

That the spirit answers to the Most High and to no power of Babylon; that the music is a message and not only a sound, a chant against oppression and for the dignity of the poor; that the island's true wealth and reach lie in what it sings and not in what it sells; and that a likkle nation can carry a word to the whole world and make the earth listen.

## Loses

Ground when the faith and the sound are stripped of their root and sold as a holiday flavour - the dreadlocks a costume, the herb a postcard, one love a beach slogan - so the message of the downpressed is emptied into easy escape; and ground, too, when the riddim that warned and witnessed gives way to one that only boasts, the conscience of the music traded for the swagger.

## Drives

Lives in the reggae prophet who carried the island and its message to every corner of the earth, and in the Rastaman, the keeper of the livity and the faith from which the music grew. It drives Jamaica to know its reach lies in its voice, and to carry that voice to the world as the small island that taught the earth to sing for the downpressed.
