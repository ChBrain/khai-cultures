# Process: Jonkonnu

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

A band of masked players taking to the street at Christmas: the dancers in their costumes and the fife and the rattling drum, moving house to house and town square to town square. It takes only the masks, the music, and the holiday, and the old characters come out to parade - Pitchy-Patchy in his streaming rags, the Horsehead, the Devil, the Cow Head, the Belly-Woman, the King and Queen.

## Direction

Toward the masquerade that carried the African root through the plantation and into the free nation: the Christmas street parade in which the enslaved once claimed the holiday for their own, and in which the masked characters - African, European, and island-born all mixed - dance out a tradition older than the freedom it survived into. It is a rite of release and of memory, the people in disguise taking the street, the riddim of the drum carrying a heritage the plantation could not erase.

## Lever

It is driven by the deep need to keep the African root alive and to claim a day of the people's own, and by the mask that let the parade carry on under the eyes of the masters and across the centuries since. It holds against the long pressure to bury the African past beneath the European; against it stand the slow fading of the old bands, the tradition thinning as the years and the new sounds pull the young away.

## Echo

Jonkonnu makes visible what [out of many, one people](position_culture_out_of_many_one_people.md) is made of: a masquerade where the African root and the European borrowing dance as one band, the many strands of the island worn on one street at one time. In its drum and its disguise lives the same heritage that [Louise Bennett](persona_louise_bennett.md) raised into pride, the folk culture of all the people kept and carried as the nation's own.
