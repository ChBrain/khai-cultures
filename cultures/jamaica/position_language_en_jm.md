# Position: Jamaican English

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The authority of the island's English tongue, the whole span of it, from the Patois of the yard where the heart beats to the Standard English of the office, the statute and the school. It is one language stretched across a continuum: the creole the enslaved built from the master's words and their own African cadence, the tongue of the market, the riddim and the proverb, at one end; and the proper English the empire imposed and the free nation kept for its law, its ledger and its address to the world, at the other. It is the tongue in which a small island, likkle but tallawah, speaks to itself and to the whole earth, having taken the language forced upon it and made of it a voice no one else could have made.

## Orders

That the nation carry its public life, its law and its lesson in the standard English it holds in common, so a people of many roots can meet on one ground and answer the wider world in a tongue the world can read. And, beneath that, that the Patois of the yard be honoured as the true speech of the heart and not shamed as broken English, so the island keeps the tongue it lives in as well as the tongue it writes in, and does not teach its young to apologise for the way they truly speak.

## Loses

Ground where the standard tongue of the school still marks the Patois of the yard as wrong, correcting the mother tongue out of the children's mouths even as the stage and the song crown it. It loses, too, where the proper English is worn as a badge of rank over those who speak the creole, the one tongue set above the other instead of beside it. And it loses at the far edge where the African tongues of the crossing were drowned altogether, the names and languages the slave ships took, so that a people call home to Africa in a borrowed language and that is the gap at the limit of the tongue, the home speech that the middle passage silenced.

## Drives

It drives a small island to carry a reach far larger than its shores in a tongue it made its own. It is the mother tongue, the Patois of the yard, of [Bob Marley](persona_bob_marley.md), who raised the speech of Trench Town into psalm the whole earth chants back untranslated; of [Louise Bennett](persona_louise_bennett.md), who carried that same Patois over the line from shame into pride and put it at last onto the printed page; and of [the Rastaman](persona_the_rastaman.md), who remade it into Iyaric, a speech of I-and-I that Babylon cannot fully read. [Marcus Garvey](persona_marcus_garvey.md) had the tongue whole and turned it into thunder, preaching the redemption of Africa in the very English the empire imposed, the language of the masters made the trumpet of the mastered. It reached [Nanny of the Maroons](persona_nanny.md) only across the lines, the speech of the enemy caught by ear and never truly hers, the tongue of the treaty she signed but not of the Akan heart she kept free in the hills. So the island's English shows who carries it and whom it merely reached, and binds the many roots as one people without drowning the yard tongue they were born to.
