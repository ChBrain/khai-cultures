# Plan: Out of Many, One People

## Taxonomy

The open mandate the nation set itself in the motto on its coat of arms: the endless work of turning the island's outsized cultural and athletic reach into opportunity at home, of healing the inequality and the violence that shadow the swagger that [reggae and Rastafari](position_reggae_and_rastafari.md) carried to the world, of holding the many roots of [out of many, one people](position_culture_out_of_many_one_people.md) together as one, and of keeping the pride and the refusal of [the resistance](position_the_resistance.md) alive without letting them curdle into mere grievance or pose.

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Direction

Turn the reach into a road home - to make the global fame of the music, the sport, and the brand pay in work, in schooling, and in a way up for the youth of the yards that made it; heal the gap, closing the distance between the island the world dances to and the island still climbing, and answering the violence and the inequality that shadow the swagger; hold the many as one, ranking no root above another and honouring the Patois that binds them; and keep the diaspora bound in, the scattered children of the island held as part of the one people across the world.

## Orders

- To the keeper of the faith like [the Rastaman](persona_the_rastaman.md): hold the conscience of the music and the livity, that the message of the downpressed is not emptied into a brand.
- To the keeper of the tongue like [Louise Bennett](persona_louise_bennett.md): carry the pride of the Patois from the stage into the school, that the tongue that binds the many is honoured and not corrected away.
- To the nation: turn the reach into opportunity at home, heal the gap between the fame abroad and the fight at home, and make the motto true in the street.

## Implementation

A free nation of fewer than three million, a cultural and athletic superpower far beyond its size, that has given the world reggae, the sprinters, the Patois, and the brand, and that carries still the unfinished work at home - the inequality between the hills and the yards, the violence on the corners, the youth with no work in the very places the world's music came from, and the standing question of whether a small island can turn its global reach into a living for its own.
