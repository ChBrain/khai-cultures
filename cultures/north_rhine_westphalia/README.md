# North Rhine-Westphalia (Nordrhein-Westfalen): a khai culture

North Rhine-Westphalia staged as a full khai play, ISO DE-NW, language de. The most populous state of Germany: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its industrial coal history and Rhenish joy of life encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house that holds this culture and answers for it.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_north_rhine_westphalia.md](play_north_rhine_westphalia.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_north_rhine_westphalia.md](pitch_north_rhine_westphalia.md), the North Rhine-Westphalia key.
- **Plots (history):** the industrialization of the Ruhr Valley, the creation of the state in 1946 (Operation Marriage), and the structural transition from coal mining.
- **Personas:** Jupp (traditional retired coal miner archetype) and Luisa (modern creative/student class), written in German.
- **Positions:** die Kumpelmentalität (miner solidarity), der Strukturwandel (structural change), der rheinische Frohsinn (Rhenish joie de vivre).
- **Places:** der Kölner Dom (Cologne Cathedral), die Zeche Zollverein (Zollverein Coal Mine).
- **Processes:** der Karneval (Rhenish carnival).
- **Pieces:** das Grubengold (piece of coal), der Karnevalsorden (carnival medal).
- **Plan:** der Kohleausstieg (coal phase-out plan).
