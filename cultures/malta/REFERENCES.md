# Malta: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_mt.md](pitch_mt.md) is written from Malta's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no
number. The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the
record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Malta score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | --------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 56                    |
| Individualism                 | 59                    |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 47                    |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 96                    |
| Long Term Orientation         | 47                    |
| Indulgence                    | 66                    |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published
by Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; the data for Malta is limited
and less firmly established than for larger countries, and its very high
Uncertainty Avoidance is the most marked figure.

The two defining personas, [Ċettina](persona_cettina.md) and
[Mark](persona_mark.md), are archetypes, not historical individuals: composites
that carry the tension between the older generation (the parish, the festa, the
band club, the deep Catholic faith, and the memory of the wartime siege) and the
younger one (the EU-era boom and the pull of English on the only Semitic language
written in the Latin alphabet).
