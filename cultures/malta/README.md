# Malta: a khai culture

Malta staged as a full khai play, ISO MT, language mt. A tiny island fortress at
the heart of the Mediterranean, as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its
people as the cast, the roles its language and faith encode, its places, its
practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_malta.md](play_malta.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_mt.md](pitch_mt.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Malta's
  profile (warm and gregarious, family- and parish-bound, intensely Catholic,
  anxious for certainty, proud and resilient) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** St Paul's shipwreck (around 60 AD), the Great Siege of
  1565, the Maltese awakening and the language, and the George Cross and
  independence - each casting the elements it needs.
- **Personas:** Jean de Valette, St Paul, and Dun Karm as the historic cast, plus
  two defining archetypes, Ċettina (older, the festa, the parish, and the memory
  of the wartime siege) and Mark (younger, the EU-era professional whose Maltese
  thins under English). Each is wired to the language engine by the
  language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** il-Malti (the only Semitic language of the EU, written in the
  Latin alphabet and layered with Italian and English), il-fortizza (the island
  bastion of the Great Siege, the wartime siege, and the George Cross), il-fidi
  (the deep Catholic faith brought by St Paul and held for two millennia).
- **Places:** Valletta (the Knights' fortress-capital above the Grand Harbour),
  Mdina (the silent walled former capital tied to St Paul).
- **Processes:** il-festa (the village patron-saint feast and the rival band
  clubs), ir-regatta (the traditional rowing race in the Grand Harbour on the
  days of victory).
- **Pieces:** il-luzzu (the brightly painted fishing boat with the Eye on its
  prow), il-ġebla (the honey-coloured limestone Malta is built from).
