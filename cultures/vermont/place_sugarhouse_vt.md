# Place: the Vermont sugarhouse

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Shown

The active wood-frame sugarhouse barn in Addison County, featuring a metal steam cupola, sap holding tanks, a wood-fired evaporator, and stacks of firewood.

## Holds

- the maple syrup history in the plot [The Maple Sugar Industry Standard 1893](plot_02_maple_sugar.md) : the steam cupola.

## Offers

The syrup boiling demonstrations and sap quality tests where [Emma](persona_emma.md) coordinates wood-fire levels.

## Withheld

The fuel intensive carbon footprints. While celebrated today as a natural forest harvest tradition, wood-fired sugarhouses historically consumed vast quantities of local timber resources, contributing to regional deforestation during the nineteenth century, details downplayed in modern tourism flyers.
