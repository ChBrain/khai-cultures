# Vermont: a khai culture

Vermont staged as a full khai play, ISO US-VT, language en. The Green Mountain State: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its Green Mountain Boys, Maple Sugar Standard, and Ben & Jerry's Launch encodes, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house that holds this culture and answers for it.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_vermont.md](play_vermont.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_vermont.md](pitch_vermont.md), the Vermont key.
- **Plots (history):** the Green Mountain Boys of the 1770s, the Maple Sugar Standard of 1893, and the Ben & Jerry's Launch of 1978.
- **Personas:** Ira (historic site coordinator) and Emma (maple sugar cooperative coordinator), written in English.
- **Positions:** green mountain liberty (independence/town meeting debate/local property rights), maple craftsmanship (maple sap boiling/dairy farming pride/local agricultural cooperatives).
- **Places:** Green Mountain cabin, Vermont sugarhouse.
- **Processes:** maple evaporation (boiling maple sap to syrup), ice cream churning (churning cream and sugar).
- **Pieces:** a sugarhouse evaporator, an ice cream tub.
- **Plan:** the Lake Champlain watershed plan.
