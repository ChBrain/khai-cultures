# Persona: the spice grower

## Taxonomy

[the Spice Isle](position_the_spice_isle.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The nutmeg and mace grower of the hill estates, who tend a few acres up the wet green slope the same way their mother and grandmother did, gathering the fallen nutmegs off the ground, peeling the scarlet mace off the shell with the thumb, and carrying the crop down to the station to be graded and dried, the whole hillside smelling of nutmeg and cinnamon and the cocoa drying on the trays. Patient, God-fearing, and weathered like an old board, rooted to the land and slow to complain, they are the figure the Spice Isle stand on and the one who plant back the grove after every storm. The Grenadian dialect of the hill and the yard is their tongue first: they [speak](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [hear](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) it among the trees and at the station, the quick sweet creole of the island, and the standard English of school and church and the radio they [carry alongside it](process_speaking_carried.md), reaching for the one or the other as the moment ask. Back behind the dialect lie the ancestral African tongues their forebears carried across the water into bondage on these same estates and were never let to keep, lost in the generations of the sugar and the unfree, so that the gap at the limit of the tongue is the silence where those first languages should be, the names of the old country worn away by the crossing and the cane, leaving the creole that grow up in their place to carry what it could.

## Action

In [the Last Stand and the Spice Isle](plot_01_the_last_stand_and_the_spice_isle.md) they embody [the Spice Isle](position_the_spice_isle.md), tending the nutmeg and the mace on the hills the Kalinago held and the enslaved worked, and growing the spice that name the nation. They carry the oldest layer of the island forward by minding it season on season and planting the grove back again after every storm.

## Shadow

The patience that endure can slide into a resignation that ask too little, the grower taking the low price and the hard season as just the way things go: the danger that the one the island stand on is the one the island overlook the most, the spice prized up while the hands that raise it are left to make do with what little they get.

## Tell

They climb the wet hill at first light to gather the fallen nutmegs, peel the scarlet mace off the shell with a thumb worn smooth by the work, and after the storm done strip the grove they clear the ground and plant back again, because the spice always come back, and so do they.
