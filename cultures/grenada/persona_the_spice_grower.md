# Persona: the spice grower

## Taxonomy

[the Spice Isle](position_the_spice_isle.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The nutmeg and mace grower of the hill estates, who tends a few acres up the wet green slope as their parents and grandparents did, gathers the fallen nutmegs, peels the scarlet mace from the shell, and carries the crop down to the station to be graded and dried. Patient, devout, and weathered, rooted to the land and slow to complain, they are the figure on whom the Spice Isle stands and the one who rebuilds the grove after every storm. The Grenadian dialect of the hill and the yard is their tongue first: they [speak](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [hear](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) it among the trees and at the station, the quick sweet creole of the island, and the standard English of school and church and the radio they [carry alongside it](process_speaking_carried.md), reaching for the one or the other as the moment asks. Behind the dialect lie the ancestral African tongues their forebears carried across the water into bondage on these same estates and were never let to keep, lost in the generations of the sugar and the unfree, so that the gap at the limit of the tongue is the silence where those first languages should be, the names of the old country worn away by the crossing and the cane, leaving the creole that grew up in their place to carry what it could.

## Action

In [the Last Stand and the Spice Isle](plot_01_the_last_stand_and_the_spice_isle.md) they embody [the Spice Isle](position_the_spice_isle.md), tending the nutmeg and the mace on the hills the Kalinago held and the enslaved worked, and growing the spice that names the nation. They carry the oldest layer of the island forward by tending it season on season and planting the grove again after every storm.

## Shadow

The patience that endures can shade into a resignation that asks too little, the grower taking the low price and the hard season as simply the way of things: the danger that the one on whom the island stands is the one the island most overlooks, the spice prized while the hands that raise it are left to make do.

## Tell

They climb the wet hill at first light to gather the fallen nutmegs, peel the scarlet mace from the shell with a thumb worn smooth by the work, and after the storm has stripped the grove they clear the ground and plant again, because the spice always comes back and so do they.
