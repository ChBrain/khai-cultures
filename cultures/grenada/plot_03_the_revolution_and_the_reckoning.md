# Plot: the Revolution and the Reckoning

## Cue

In 1974 the island gains its independence and takes its place as a small new nation in the world. Within a few years a movement led by [Maurice Bishop](persona_maurice_bishop.md) takes the country onto a different road.

## Action

From 1979 to 1983 the island lives its revolution, a chapter the nation now carries soberly and on the public record. The years come to their end in 1983, and an intervention follows in that year, named here without taking a side and without dwelling on the hurt. The nation comes through the chapter and takes up the long work that comes after, the reckoning, the memory and the reconciliation by which a people choose to carry a costly chapter together rather than bury it. The years 1979 to 1983 enter the nation's history as a time to be held with truth, and the island sets itself to heal the memory as one people.

## Stage

- [Maurice Bishop](persona_maurice_bishop.md): the central figure of the revolution, carried soberly as part of the nation's history.
- [St. George's](place_st_georges.md): the capital and seat of the nation where the years of the revolution are centred and remembered.
- [the revolution and the reckoning](position_the_revolution_and_the_reckoning.md): the chapter of 1979 to 1983 and the work of memory and reconciliation that follows.

## Tension

The most tender chapter in the island's story asks the hardest thing of it: to carry the years 1979 to 1983 on the public record, with truth and without rancour, neither burying them in silence nor reopening them to divide. The nation that came through the chapter must now do the long work of reckoning, holding the memory in a way that draws the people together as one rather than setting them against one another again.
