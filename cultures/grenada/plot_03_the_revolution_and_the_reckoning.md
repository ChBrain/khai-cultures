# Plot: the Revolution and the Reckoning

## Cue

In 1974 the island gain its independence and take its place as a small new nation in the world. Within a few short years a movement led by [Maurice Bishop](persona_maurice_bishop.md) take the country onto a different road.

## Action

From 1979 to 1983 the island live its revolution, a chapter the nation now carry soft and sober and on the public record. The years come to their end in 1983, and an intervention follow in that same year, named here without taking any side and without dwelling on the hurt. The nation come through the chapter and take up the long work that come after, the reckoning, the memory and the reconciliation by which a people choose to carry a costly chapter together instead of burying it. The years 1979 to 1983 enter the nation history as a time to be held with truth, and the island set itself to mend the memory as one people.

## Stage

- [Maurice Bishop](persona_maurice_bishop.md): the central figure of the revolution, carried soft and sober as part of the nation history.
- [St. George's](place_st_georges.md): the capital and seat of the nation where the years of the revolution are centred and remembered.
- [the revolution and the reckoning](position_the_revolution_and_the_reckoning.md): the chapter of 1979 to 1983 and the work of memory and reconciliation that come after.

## Tension

The most tender chapter in the island story ask the hardest thing of it: to carry the years 1979 to 1983 on the public record, with truth and without rancour, neither burying them down in silence nor opening them back up to divide. The nation that come through the chapter must now do the long work of reckoning, holding the memory in such a way that it draw the people together as one instead of setting them one against the other again.
