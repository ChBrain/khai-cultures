# Process: Spicemas

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

The whole island come August, when Spicemas, the Grenadian carnival, take over the streets: the bands and the mas players, the calypso and the soca pouring out the trucks, the costumes sewn up for months, and before day clean the J'ouvert, when the jab jab come out slick with old engine oil and molasses, horns on their head, dragging chains and chanting through the dark, and the shortknee in their bright cloth and powder dancing down the road. It take the island and the diaspora coming home, and the streets of St. George's fill up from the small hours of the morning.

## Direction

Toward release and belonging: the big spice-island fete that let the nation loose and bind it back together in the same days of mas. The jab jab, the oil-covered devil black and shining in the dark, carry the memory of the enslaved who once worked the cane, turning the old hurt into a fierce celebration the people own for themself; the calypso talk the year's truth in song, and the whole island, home and come back from abroad, dance as one.

## Lever

It is driven by the need of a small, God-fearing, hard-working people for a season of release and a rite that gather the diaspora home, and by the deep island love of music, costume, and the road. It hold against the year's labour and the scattering of Grenadians across the world; against it stand the cost of the fete and the weight the jab jab carry, the celebration grow straight up out of the memory of bondage.

## Echo

Spicemas make audible what drive [the Spice Isle](position_the_spice_isle.md): a small green nation that know itself by its spice and its music and gather as one to celebrate it. Through the J'ouvert dark [the spice grower](persona_the_spice_grower.md) come down from the hills to play the jab jab in the streets, the island loose and made one for the season of mas.
