# Process: Spicemas

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

The whole island in August, when Spicemas, the Grenadian carnival, takes over the streets: the bands and the mas players, the calypso and the soca pouring from the trucks, the costumes sewn for months, and before dawn the J'ouvert, when the Jab Jab come out slicked in old engine oil and molasses, horns on their heads, dragging chains and chanting through the dark. It takes the island and the diaspora coming home, and the streets of St. George's fill from the small hours.

## Direction

Toward release and belonging: the great spice-island fete that lets the nation loose and binds it together in the same days of mas. The Jab Jab, the devil-figure black with oil, carries the memory of the enslaved who once worked the cane, turning the old hurt into a fierce, owned celebration; the calypso speaks the year's truths in song, and the whole island, at home and returned from abroad, dances as one.

## Lever

It is driven by the need of a small, devout, hard-working people for a season of release and a rite that gathers the diaspora home, and by the deep island love of music, costume, and the road. It holds against the year's labour and the scattering of Grenadians across the world; against it stand the cost of the fete and the weight the Jab Jab carries, the celebration grown straight out of the memory of bondage.

## Echo

Spicemas makes audible what drives [the Spice Isle](position_the_spice_isle.md): a small green nation that knows itself by its spice and its music and gathers as one to celebrate it. Through the J'ouvert dark [the spice grower](persona_the_spice_grower.md) comes down from the hills to play the Jab Jab in the streets, the island loosed and made one for the season of mas.
