# Piece: oil-down

## Taxonomy

Parent group: pieces

## Owner

## Place

In the big pot over the fire, at the beach lime, the family Sunday, and the village fete: the national dish, breadfruit and salted meat and dumplings stewing down slow in coconut milk with callaloo, turmeric, and the island own spice, cooking down and down until the milk cook away and the oil settle to the bottom of the pot, and that is where the name come from. The one-pot that feed a whole crowd, make up in the hills, down by the shore, and anywhere at all that Grenadians gather.

## Load Bearing

It stand for the island plenty make out of little and share out to all. Build on the breadfruit the poor man could grow in his yard and the salted meat that keep without ice, layer up into one pot and spice with the nutmeg the hills give up, the oil-down is the food of a people who make plenty out of small means and make it together, everybody putting something in the pot and everybody eating from it. It carry the island resourcefulness, its love of the gathering, and its spice, the Spice Isle right there on a plate. Without this one-pot the island table would have no dish the whole nation call its own.

## Apparent

A heavy iron pot pack up with breadfruit, dumplings, salted pigtail or saltfish, callaloo and ground provisions, simmering down in coconut milk gone golden with turmeric and sweet with grated nutmeg, until the liquid cook away and a sheen of oil gather at the bottom. Plain country food in the pot; its weight is in the gathering it feed and the island it stand for.
