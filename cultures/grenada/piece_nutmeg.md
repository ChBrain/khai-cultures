# Piece: nutmeg

## Taxonomy

Parent group: pieces

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Place

On the hill estate and in the kitchen, on the flag and in the world's spice racks: the hard brown seed in its lacy scarlet coat of mace, grown in the wet groves of the Spice Isle, dried on the racks at the station, grated into the rum punch and the oil-down, and shipped abroad as the island's signature. The one spice that names a nation and rides on its banner, Grenada long among the world's great growers of it.

## Load Bearing

It stands for the island itself, the green land and the people who tend it, the resilience that comes back after the storm. Grenada is the only nation on earth that carries a nutmeg on its flag, the spice set in the red disc at the hoist, and the seed is the nation's name made visible, [the Spice Isle](position_the_spice_isle.md) grown into an emblem. It carries the whole story in one seed: the colonial estates and the enslaved who worked them, the smallholders' island that came after, the groves that Hurricane Ivan stripped and the slow replanting that brought them back. Without this seed the Spice Isle would have no signature and no flag to fly.

## Apparent

A hard, oval brown seed the size of a thumbnail, wrapped while fresh in a lacy red web of mace inside a fleshy yellow fruit, drying to a deep aroma that warms when it is grated. In itself it is only a seed and a spice; its weight is in what the island has made of it, the name it gives the nation and the place it holds on the flag.

## Yearbook

- Grown on the wet hill estates from colonial times, making the island one of the world's leading sources of nutmeg and mace.
- Set on the national flag in the red disc at the hoist, the only nutmeg on any nation's banner.
- Stripped to bare ground by Hurricane Ivan in 2004 and replanted grove by grove, the slow comeback the very figure of the island's resilience.
