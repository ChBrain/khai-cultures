# Position: Grenadian English

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The authority of English as Grenada's tongue, the language of the school, the court, the church and the record, and with it the sweet Grenadian dialect that runs over every inch of the island, the quick creole of the yard and the market. It is the tongue the nation does its business in and the tongue it lives in, the standard English of office and the broad island speech braided together, the one carrying the law and the other carrying the life.

## Orders

What the island sets down and conducts its public life in: the statute, the lesson, the newspaper and the record, in the standard English of office; and, alongside it, the Grenadian dialect that orders the talk of the yard, the market and the fete. It orders that the nation's business be done in the one and the nation's living be done in the other, and that a small people across three islands hold themselves together in a speech they all share.

## Loses

Ground where the standard tongue is held up as correct and the dialect put down as broken, so that the island speech the people actually live in is made to feel a lesser thing in its own home. It loses, too, the older layer beneath the creole, the ancestral African tongues the enslaved carried across the water and were never let to keep, worn away by the crossing and the cane, leaving the creole to carry what it could in their place.

## Drives

It drives a small nation across three islands to hold itself together in one shared tongue. It is the mother tongue of [T.A. Marryshow](persona_marryshow.md), who argued the federation dream in it; of [Maurice Bishop](persona_maurice_bishop.md), who turned it to the platform; and of [Merle Collins](persona_merle_collins.md), who bent it on the page to hold the Grenadian voice. It is the first tongue too of [the spice grower](persona_the_spice_grower.md), who speaks the broad island creole among the trees and carries the standard English alongside it. [Julien Fedon](persona_julien_fedon.md) borrowed only as much of the empire's English as the colony forced on him, rising his people instead in the French-Creole the master could not command. So the tongue shows who was raised in it and who merely borrowed it, and binds the island's people without unsaying the speech of the yard.
