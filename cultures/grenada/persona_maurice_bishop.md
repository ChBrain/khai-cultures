## Projection

The lawyer and leader who headed the revolution of 1979 to 1983, a central figure of the island's modern history, carried here soberly and on the public record as part of the nation's story. A compelling speaker who drew the young and the hopeful and who died in 1983 at the chapter's end, he is held in the nation's memory as a figure of that time, named without taking a side and without dwelling on the hurt. English he has whole and turns to the platform, the tongue of the courtroom he trained in and the crowd he addressed: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in his mother tongue, and he [drafts it](process_writing_drafted.md) into the addresses of those years. The Grenadian dialect of the street and the market he [carried as his own](process_speaking_carried.md), and could meet the people in the speech they shared. He spoke of a different road for the island in the English of the law and the platform, and yet what the years 1979 to 1983 would come to mean to the nation went beyond what any speech could fix or settle, so that the gap at the limit of the tongue is the silence that still falls when the years are named, the part of the memory that no eloquence has yet been able to put into peace.
