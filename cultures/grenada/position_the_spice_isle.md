# Position: the Spice Isle

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The conviction that the island is its land and what the land give: the wet volcanic hills that grow the nutmeg and the mace, the cocoa and the cinnamon and the clove, the spice that name the nation and ride on its flag, the smell of it hanging in the air. It is the oldest layer of the story, the green held first by the Kalinago who hunt and fish and farm it, take into the colonial estates where sugar and then spice were worked by the enslaved, and grown at last into a smallholders island where the grower tend a few acres up the hill. The Spice Isle is the land that outlive the empires that fight over it and the storms that level it, and grow the same trees back again.

## Orders

That the island be known by its land first, that the nutmeg and the mace are not a crop alone but the nation name and signature; that the grower and the estate, the rainforest and the cocoa, are the ground the whole story stand on; and that the resilience of the spice, coming back after the storm, is the resilience of the people who tend it.

## Loses

Ground when the spice is taken for granted or sold too cheap, when the world price the nutmeg low and the grower cannot live off the hill; and ground to the storm itself, to the hurricane that strip the trees in one day and take years to grow back, so that the island that is its land is always one storm away from starting all over again.

## Drives

Lives in the spice grower, who tend the nutmeg and the mace on the hill estates the same way their mother and grandmother did and plant back the grove after every storm. It drive Grenada to know itself by its land and its spice, to carry the nutmeg on its flag, and to come back green from every blow the world and the weather deal it.
