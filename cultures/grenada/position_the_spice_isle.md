# Position: the Spice Isle

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The conviction that the island is its land and what the land gives: the wet volcanic hills that grow the nutmeg and the mace, the cocoa and the cinnamon, the spice that names the nation and rides on its flag. It is the oldest layer of the story, the green held first by the Kalinago who hunted and fished and farmed it, taken into the colonial estates where sugar and then spice were worked by the enslaved, and grown at last into a smallholders' island where the grower tends a few acres up the hill. The Spice Isle is the land that survives the empires that fought over it and the storms that level it, and grows the same trees again.

## Orders

That the island be understood by its land first, that the nutmeg and the mace are not a crop alone but the nation's name and signature; that the grower and the estate, the rainforest and the cocoa, are the ground the whole story stands on; and that the resilience of the spice, coming back after the storm, is the resilience of the people who tend it.

## Loses

Ground when the spice is taken for granted or sold too cheap, when the world prices the nutmeg low and the grower cannot live by the hill; and ground to the storm itself, to the hurricane that strips the trees in a day and takes years to grow back, so that the island that is its land is always one storm from beginning again.

## Drives

Lives in [the spice grower](persona_the_spice_grower.md), who tends the nutmeg and the mace on the hill estates as their parents and grandparents did and rebuilds the grove after every storm. It drives Grenada to know itself by its land and its spice, to carry the nutmeg on its flag, and to come back green from every blow the world and the weather deal it.
