# Process: the Big Drum

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

The people of Carriacou, the sister isle, at the wedding, the tombstone feast, the launching of a boat, or the calling of the ancestors: the Big Drum Nation Dance, three drums and the chac-chac, the women in their long skirts dancing the nation dances, each tracing back to an African nation, the Cromanti, the Igbo, the Manding, the Kongo, the names of the old country kept alive in song and step.

## Direction

Toward remembrance and continuity: the rite by which Carriacou keeps faith with its African heritage and calls the ancestors to the living. The Big Drum holds the line back across the water to the nations from which the enslaved were taken, naming them, dancing them, and feeding the dead, so that a small island people keep alive what the crossing and the cane tried to take away.

## Lever

It is driven by the deep need of a people severed from their origin to keep the thread to the ancestors unbroken, and by the devotion of Carriacou to its old ways. It holds against the long erasure of slavery and the pull of the modern world that would let the nation dances fade; against it stand the passing of the elders who carry the songs and the steps, and the small numbers who still keep them.

## Echo

The Big Drum makes audible what [the Spice Isle](position_the_spice_isle.md) holds in its oldest layer: the African heritage of the people the estates were built on, kept alive in song and dance. Through its nation dances [the spice grower](persona_the_spice_grower.md) hears named the ancestral nations their own forebears were taken from, the languages the cane took recalled where the drum still beats.
