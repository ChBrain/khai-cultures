# Process: the Big Drum

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

The people of Carriacou, the sister isle of the boat-builders, at the wedding, the tombstone feast, the launching of a new sloop they build with their own hands on the beach, or the calling of the ancestors: the Big Drum Nation Dance, three drums and the chac-chac, the women in their long skirts dancing the nation dances, each one tracing back to an African nation, the Cromanti, the Igbo, the Manding, the Kongo, the names of the old country kept alive in song and step.

## Direction

Toward remembrance and continuity: the rite by which Carriacou keep faith with its African heritage and call the ancestors to the living. The Big Drum hold the line back across the water to the nations the enslaved were taken from, naming them, dancing them, and feeding the dead, so that a small island people keep alive what the crossing and the cane try to take away.

## Lever

It is driven by the deep need of a people cut off from their origin to keep the thread to the ancestors from breaking, and by the devotion of Carriacou to its old old ways. It hold against the long erasure of slavery and the pull of the modern world that would let the nation dances fade away; against it stand the passing of the old heads who carry the songs and the steps, and the small few who still keep them.

## Echo

The Big Drum make audible what [the Spice Isle](position_the_spice_isle.md) hold in its oldest layer: the African heritage of the people the estates were built on, kept alive in song and dance. Through its nation dances [the spice grower](persona_the_spice_grower.md) hear named the ancestral nations their own forebears were taken from, the languages the cane take away called back where the drum still beat.
