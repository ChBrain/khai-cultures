# Place: the Spice Country

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Shown

The wet green interior and the nutmeg coast: Gouyave, the fishing and spice town on the west shore where the nutmeg station stands and the fallen crop is graded and dried, the hill estates climbing the volcanic slopes in groves of nutmeg, mace, cocoa, and cinnamon, and high in the centre the Grand Etang rainforest around its crater lake, wreathed in cloud and loud with the mona monkeys and the rush of waterfalls. The land that grows the spice and names the nation, the green heart of the Spice Isle.

## Offers

A place where the island is most itself, the land that gives the nutmeg and the mace, the cocoa and the cinnamon, the ground the grower tends and the rainforest that holds the island's water and its wild; the green country in which the Spice Isle is grown season on season and replanted after every storm.

## Withheld

The promise of a harvest secure. The Spice Country carries the exposure beneath its abundance, the groves a single hurricane can strip to bare ground and the years it takes the nutmeg to bear again, the low price the world can set on the spice, and the long patience the land demands of those who live by it, a green country generous and fragile at once.
