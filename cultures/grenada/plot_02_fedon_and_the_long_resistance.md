# Plot: Fedon and the Long Resistance

## Taxonomy

[play_grenada](play_grenada.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

Under British rule the free-coloured of the island chafe at the limits set on them and the enslaved labour the estates without end. On the high estate at Belvidere, [Julien Fedon](persona_julien_fedon.md) gathers those willing to rise.

## Action

In 1795 Fedon raises the free-coloured and the enslaved against the British and holds much of the island for more than a year before the rising is put down, the boldest act of refusal in the island's story. The long struggle against bondage runs on past it to emancipation, when the enslaved are at last set free and the smallholders' island begins to take shape. Generations on, T.A. Marryshow turns the old spirit of refusal into a vision, founding a newspaper and arguing on every platform that the islands of the Caribbean, each too small alone, must stand together as one West Indian people. From the rebellion to the federation dream, the island sets down its long tradition of resistance.

## Stage

- [Julien Fedon](persona_julien_fedon.md): the leader who raises the rebellion of 1795 and sets the founding act of refusal.
- [T.A. Marryshow](persona_marryshow.md): the statesman who turns refusal into the federation dream and the vision of one Caribbean people.
- [St. George's](place_st_georges.md): the colonial capital and harbour against which the rising is set and from which the island is governed and argued over.

## Tension

The island finds in refusal both its courage and its burden: the rebellion of 1795 proves that an unfree people and a small place can take up arms for themselves, and yet the rising is put down and its cost is heavy. The same tradition runs on into the federation dream, nobler and gentler, and it too outruns the means of the small islands, so the long resistance carries the island forward on a spirit larger than it has yet been able to fulfil.
