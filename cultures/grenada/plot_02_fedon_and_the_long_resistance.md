# Plot: Fedon and the Long Resistance

## Taxonomy

[play_grenada](play_grenada.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

Under British rule the free-coloured of the island chafe at the limits set on them and the enslaved labour the estates day in day out with no end in sight. Up on the high estate at Belvidere, [Julien Fedon](persona_julien_fedon.md) gather those who are willing to rise.

## Action

In 1795 Fedon rise the free-coloured and the enslaved against the British and hold much of the island for better than a year before the rising is put down, the boldest act of refusal in the whole island story. The long struggle against bondage run on past it to emancipation, when the enslaved are at last set free and the smallholders island start to take shape. Generations on, T.A. Marryshow turn the old spirit of refusal into a vision, founding a newspaper and arguing on every platform that the islands of the Caribbean, each one too small on its own, must stand together as one West Indian people. From the rebellion to the federation dream, the island set down its long tradition of resistance.

## Stage

- [Julien Fedon](persona_julien_fedon.md): the leader who rise the rebellion of 1795 and set the founding act of refusal.
- [T.A. Marryshow](persona_marryshow.md): the statesman who turn refusal into the federation dream and the vision of one Caribbean people.
- [St. George's](place_st_georges.md): the colonial capital and harbour the rising is set against and the island is governed and argued over from.

## Tension

The island find in refusal both its courage and its burden: the rebellion of 1795 prove that an unfree people and a small place can take up arms for their own self, and yet the rising is put down and its cost is heavy, heavy. The same tradition run on into the federation dream, nobler and gentler, and it too run on ahead of the means of the small islands, so the long resistance carry the island forward on a spirit bigger than it has yet been able to fulfil.
