# Plot: the Last Stand and the Spice Isle

## Taxonomy

[play_grenada](play_grenada.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

The Kalinago hold the green island they have hunted, fished, and farmed for generations, and the wide world arrives by sea. France comes first to take the land and name it, and the Kalinago resist to the end.

## Action

At Sauteurs in the north, the last of the Kalinago, cornered by the French, leap from the high cliff into the sea rather than yield, and the place keeps the memory in its name. France works the island with sugar and the enslaved brought across the water to cut it, then loses it to Britain, and the two empires trade the small island back and forth while the estates grow rich on cane and the unfree. In the wet volcanic hills a different crop takes hold, the nutmeg and the mace climbing the slopes, and the island finds the spice that will name it. From the last stand of its first people to the rise of the Spice Isle, the ground of the whole story is laid.

## Stage

- [the spice grower](persona_the_spice_grower.md): the figure on whom the Spice Isle stands, tending the nutmeg and the mace the hills give up.
- [the Spice Country](place_the_spice_country.md): the wet hills and rainforest where the Kalinago held the land and the spice now grows.
- [the Spice Isle](position_the_spice_isle.md): the island known by its land and the spice that names it, the oldest layer of the story.

## Tension

The island that names itself by the sweetness of its spice is also the island of the last stand at Sauteurs and the estates worked by the enslaved, the green ground holding both the gift and the cost. The same hills that give the nutmeg carried the cane and the unfree who cut it, so the Spice Isle is sweet and sober at once, and the whole story after grows from that doubled ground.
