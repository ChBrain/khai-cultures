# Plot: the Last Stand and the Spice Isle

## Taxonomy

## Owner

## Cue

The Kalinago hold the green island they done hunt, fish, and farm for generations upon generations, and then the wide world come in off the sea. France come first to take the land and name it, and the Kalinago stand up and resist to the very end.

## Action

At Sauteurs in the north, the last of the Kalinago, hem in by the French, leap off the high cliff into the sea sooner than bow down, and the place keep that memory right there in its name. France work the island with sugar and the enslaved brought across the water to cut it, then lose it to Britain, and the two empires pass the small island back and forth while the estates grow fat on cane and the unfree. Up in the wet volcanic hills a different crop take hold, the nutmeg and the mace climbing the slopes, the smell of it on the breeze, and the island find the spice that would name it. From the last stand of its first people to the rise of the Spice Isle, the ground of the whole story is laid down.

## Stage

- [the spice grower](persona_the_spice_grower.md): the figure the Spice Isle stand on, tending the nutmeg and the mace the hills give up.
- [the Spice Country](place_the_spice_country.md): the wet hills and rainforest where the Kalinago held the land and the spice grow today.
- [the Spice Isle](position_the_spice_isle.md): the island known by its land and the spice that name it, the oldest layer of the story.

## Tension

The island that name itself by the sweetness of its spice is also the island of the last stand at Sauteurs and the estates worked by the enslaved, the green ground holding both the gift and the cost. The same hills that give the nutmeg bear the cane and the unfree who cut it, so the Spice Isle is sweet and sober at the one same time, and the whole story after grow up from that doubled ground.
