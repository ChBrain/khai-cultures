# Grenada: a khai culture

Grenada staged as a full khai play, ISO GD, language en. The Spice Isle, fought
over by empires and fought for by its own people, as a theatre: its history as the
plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its language and faith encode, its
places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_grenada.md](play_grenada.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Grenada's regional profile (warm and family-minded, devout, soft-spoken and
  unhurried, close-knit and resilient, with the sweetness of the spice over an
  unhealed chapter of memory) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Last Stand and the Spice Isle, Fedon and the Long
  Resistance, the Revolution and the Reckoning, and the Resilient Spice Isle -
  each casting the elements it needs, and each staging the island's hardest
  chapters soberly and on the public record.
- **Personas:** Julien Fedon, T.A. Marryshow, Maurice Bishop, and Merle Collins
  as the historic cast, plus one defining archetype, the spice grower (the nutmeg
  and mace grower of the hill estates on whom the Spice Isle stands). Each is
  wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** the Spice Isle (the island known by its land and the spice that
  names it), the rebel tradition (from Fedon's rebellion to the federation dream,
  resistance and the West Indian idea), the revolution and the reckoning (the
  years 1979 to 1983 and the work of memory and reconciliation).
- **Places:** St. George's (the picturesque capital, the Carenage and Fort
  George), the Spice Country (Gouyave, the nutmeg estates, and the Grand Etang
  rainforest).
- **Processes:** Spicemas (the August carnival of J'ouvert and the Jab Jab), the
  Big Drum (the Big Drum Nation Dance of African heritage on Carriacou).
- **Pieces:** nutmeg (the spice on the flag, the island's signature), the
  oil-down (the national one-pot of breadfruit, salted meat, and coconut milk).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the last stand at Sauteurs, slavery, Fedon's rebellion, and the
revolution of 1979 to 1983 and the 1983 intervention are treated soberly on the
public record, with no side taken and the memory and reconciliation in view; the
staging and the architecture are original work.
