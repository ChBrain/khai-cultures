# Persona: Merle Collins

## Taxonomy

[the revolution and the reckoning](position_the_revolution_and_the_reckoning.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The poet and novelist who gave the people their voice and the memory of the revolution its language, the writer who set the island's ordinary lives and its hardest years down on the page. Watchful, lyrical, and unflinching, she wrote the village and the woman, the dialect and the grief, and made literature out of the memory the nation carries. English she has whole and turns to art, the tongue of her schooling and her books: she [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in her mother tongue, and she [drafts it](process_writing_drafted.md) into the poems and the novels that hold the island's story. The Grenadian dialect of the yard and the market she [carries as her own](process_speaking_carried.md) and writes it onto the page beside the standard English, [catching what the ear hears](process_reading_caught.md) and setting the spoken island down in print. She writes the memory of the revolution and the speech of the people in an English she bends to hold the Grenadian voice, and yet some of what those years cost the island lives below any words, in the silence the survivors keep, so that the gap at the limit of the tongue is the part of the grief that the page can circle and honour but never wholly say.

## Action

In [the Resilient Spice Isle](plot_04_the_resilient_spice_isle.md) she gives [the revolution and the reckoning](position_the_revolution_and_the_reckoning.md) its literature and the modern island its voice, writing the memory of the hard years and the ordinary life that goes on around it. She carries the work of memory into art, holding the chapter where it can be looked at and mourned rather than buried.

## Shadow

The voice that honours the memory can also be asked to carry it alone, the nation content to let the poet remember so the rest need not: the danger that the literature becomes the only reckoning, the grief left on the page while the public silence holds, the art mistaken for the healing it can only begin.

## Tell

She writes the island's women and villages and its hardest years in an English bent to hold the Grenadian voice, giving the nation a language for the memory it carries and a place to mourn what the years took.
