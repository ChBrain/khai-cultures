# Persona: Merle Collins

## Taxonomy

[the revolution and the reckoning](position_the_revolution_and_the_reckoning.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The poet and novelist who give the people their voice and give the memory of the revolution its language, the writer who set the island ordinary lives and its hardest years down on the page. Watchful, lyrical, and never flinching, she write the village and the woman, the dialect and the grief, and make literature out of the memory the nation carry. [Grenadian English](position_language_en_gd.md) she has whole and turn to art, the tongue of her schooling and her books: she [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in her mother tongue, and she [drafts it](process_writing_drafted.md) into the poems and the novels that hold the island story. The Grenadian dialect of the yard and the market she [carries as her own](process_speaking_carried.md) and write it right onto the page beside the standard English, [catching what the ear hears](process_reading_caught.md) and setting the spoken island down in print. She write the memory of the revolution and the speech of the people in an English she bend to hold the Grenadian voice, and yet some of what those years cost the island live below any words, in the silence the survivors keep, so that the gap at the limit of the tongue is the part of the grief the page can circle round and honour but never wholly say.

## Action

In [the Resilient Spice Isle](plot_04_the_resilient_spice_isle.md) she give [the revolution and the reckoning](position_the_revolution_and_the_reckoning.md) its literature and give the modern island its voice, writing the memory of the hard years and the ordinary life that go on all around it. She carry the work of memory into art, holding the chapter where it can be looked at and mourned proper instead of buried.

## Shadow

The voice that honour the memory can also be asked to carry it alone, the nation content to let the poet do the remembering so the rest don't have to: the danger that the literature come to be the only reckoning, the grief left on the page while the public silence hold, the art mistaken for the healing it can only begin.

## Tell

She write the island women and villages and its hardest years in an English bend to hold the Grenadian voice, giving the nation a language for the memory it carry and a place to mourn what the years take away.
