# Persona: Julien Fedon

## Taxonomy

[the rebel tradition](position_the_rebel_tradition.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A free-coloured planter of the hills who in 1795 raised the free-coloured and the enslaved against British rule in the rebellion that bears his name, the boldest act of refusal in the island's story and the spark from which the long rebel tradition runs. Determined, organising, and willing to risk everything, he led from the high estate at Belvidere and held much of the island for more than a year before the rising was put down. French and the French-Creole of the island he [carries almost as his own](process_speaking_carried.md), the tongue of the planters who had named the land and of the enslaved who worked it, and in it he [speaks to his people](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [hears them answer](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md), rallying the field and the estate in the language they share. The English of the empire he was rising against he [borrowed](process_speaking_borrowed.md) only as far as the world of the British colony forced him to, and his rebellion was in part a refusal of the tongue along with the rule. He raised the island in the French-Creole the enslaved spoke and the British could not own, while behind that creole lay the African tongues the enslaved had carried across the water and never written down, so that the gap at the limit of the tongue was the very line he rose to cross, the field set free to speak and to fight in a language the master could not command.

## Action

In [Fedon and the Long Resistance](plot_02_fedon_and_the_long_resistance.md) he raises the rebellion that sets down [the rebel tradition](position_the_rebel_tradition.md), arming the free-coloured and the enslaved and holding the hills against the crown. By rising when rising seemed impossible, he gives the island the founding act of its long refusal, the proof that a small place and an unfree people could take up arms for themselves.

## Shadow

The rising that frees can also consume, and refusal raised to arms can leave wounds it cannot itself heal: the danger that the rebel tradition becomes a memory of strife alone, the spark prized over the vision, the willingness to risk everything mistaken for a plan for what comes after the risk is taken.

## Tell

He holds the high estate above the spice hills and calls the field and the free-coloured to him in the creole the master cannot command, raising a small island against an empire and setting a tradition of refusal the nation has carried ever since.
