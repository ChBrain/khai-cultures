# Persona: Julien Fedon

## Taxonomy

[the rebel tradition](position_the_rebel_tradition.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A free-coloured planter of the hills who in 1795 rise the free-coloured and the enslaved against British rule in the rebellion that bear his name, the boldest act of refusal in the whole island story and the spark the long rebel tradition run from. Hard-set, good at organising, and ready to risk every last thing, he lead from the high estate up at Belvidere and hold much of the island better than a year before the rising was put down. French and the French-Creole of the island he [carries almost as his own](process_speaking_carried.md), the tongue of the planters who name the land and of the enslaved who work it, and in it he [speaks to his people](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [hears them answer](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md), rallying the field and the estate in the one language they share. The English of the empire he was rising against he [borrowed](process_speaking_borrowed.md) only as far as the world of the British colony force him to, and his rebellion was part refusal of the tongue right along with the rule. He raise the island in the French-Creole the enslaved talk and the British could never own, while back behind that creole lie the African tongues the enslaved had carried across the water and never write down, so that the gap at the limit of the tongue was the very line he rose to cross, the field set free to talk and to fight in a language the master could not command.

## Action

In [Fedon and the Long Resistance](plot_02_fedon_and_the_long_resistance.md) he rise the rebellion that set down [the rebel tradition](position_the_rebel_tradition.md), arming the free-coloured and the enslaved and holding the hills against the crown. By rising up when rising up look like it could not be done, he give the island the founding act of its long refusal, the proof that a small place and an unfree people could take up arms for their own self.

## Shadow

The rising that free can also eat you up, and refusal raised to arms can leave wounds it cannot mend by itself: the danger that the rebel tradition come to mean a memory of strife alone, the spark prized over the vision, the readiness to risk every last thing mistaken for a plan for what come after the risk is taken.

## Tell

He hold the high estate up above the spice hills and call the field and the free-coloured to him in the creole the master cannot command, raising a small island against a whole empire and setting down a tradition of refusal the nation carry from that day to this.
