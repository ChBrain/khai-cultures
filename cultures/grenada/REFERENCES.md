# Grenada: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md) is written from Grenada's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
Grenada is not always scored individually in the published data; the profile here
draws on the broadly studied West Indian and Caribbean regional pattern. The
approximate values and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record
without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Caribbean / West Indian profile (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ----------------------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | high                                      |
| Individualism                 | low to moderate (collectivist, family)    |
| Motivation toward Achievement | moderate                                  |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | low to moderate                           |
| Long Term Orientation         | low (present-focused)                     |
| Indulgence                    | high                                      |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the six-dimensions model of national
culture and the Country Comparison tool). Where Grenada is not scored on its own,
the profile follows the broadly published West Indian and Caribbean regional
pattern; values are approximate and qualitative, used only to set the pitch.

staging is original work. The island's hardest chapters - the last stand of the
Kalinago at Sauteurs, slavery and emancipation, Fedon's rebellion, and the
revolution of 1979 to 1983 and the 1983 intervention - are staged soberly on the
public record, with no side taken, no atrocity detail, and the memory and
reconciliation in view.

The one defining archetype, [the spice grower](persona_the_spice_grower.md), is
not a historical individual but a composite: the nutmeg and mace grower of the
hill estates who carries the Spice Isle on their land, speaks the Grenadian
dialect, and stands in the line of the enslaved whose ancestral African tongues
the crossing and the cane took away.

The revolution of 1979 to 1983 and the 1983 intervention are named here from
within the nation's own self-understanding and on the public record, taking no
political side and giving no atrocity detail, with the work of memory and
reconciliation kept in view.
