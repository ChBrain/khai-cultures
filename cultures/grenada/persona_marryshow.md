# Persona: T.A. Marryshow

## Taxonomy

[the rebel tradition](position_the_rebel_tradition.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

Theophilus Albert Marryshow, journalist, orator, and statesman, the man they call the father of the West Indian federation, who turn the island's old spirit of refusal into a vision of the Caribbean as one people, and teach a small island to count itself part of a region. Sweet-mouthed, far-seeing, and never tiring, he found a newspaper and build a whole political life on the conviction that the islands, each one too small on its own, must stand together. English he has whole and shaping, the tongue of the colonial press and the public platform he make his own: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in his mother tongue, and he [writes it drafted](process_writing_drafted.md) into the editorials and the addresses that carry the federation dream right across the islands. The wider library of British letters and Caribbean argument he [read and followed](process_reading_followed.md), and bring home to the cause. He make the case for a West Indian people in the English the empire had force on them, turning the coloniser own tongue into the very instrument of the islands coming together, and yet the federation he argue for was a belonging the English language by itself could never supply, so that the gap at the limit of the tongue was the distance between a region that share a language and a region not yet one people.

## Action

In [Fedon and the Long Resistance](plot_02_fedon_and_the_long_resistance.md) he carry [the rebel tradition](position_the_rebel_tradition.md) from the rising into the vision, turning refusal into the federation dream and teaching the island to imagine a bigger Caribbean belonging. He give the long tradition of resistance its statesman and its idea, the proof that a small island could think beyond its own shoreline.

## Shadow

The vision of the one people can run on ahead of the people themself, and the dream of federation can come to be a thing more argued than built: the danger that the bigger belonging stay an idea up on the platform, that the sweet talk is mistaken for the union, and that the small islands praise the dream and stay apart all the same.

## Tell

He build a newspaper and a public life on one single conviction, that the islands are one people, and argue the case in the coloniser English until a small island learn to count itself part of a region and a Caribbean.
