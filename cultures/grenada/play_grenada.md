## Name

Grenada, the southernmost of the Windward Islands, a small mountainous nation down here in the eastern Caribbean made of three inhabited islands, Grenada herself with her sister isles Carriacou and Petite Martinique, sitting north of Trinidad and south of St. Vincent. ISO GD, language en, and the Grenadian dialect running sweet over every inch of her. The Spice Isle, the one nation on God earth that carry a nutmeg right there on its flag, a small small place that has lived a big big history.

## Arc

A small green island the wide world done fight over, and that fight back, in she own turn, for sheself. The Kalinago hold it first and make their last stand at Sauteurs, leaping off the cliff sooner than bow down, and the place still carry that memory right there in its name. France take it and name it, Britain take it from France, and the two big empires pass the island back and forth like a hot coal while sugar and the enslaved they bring across to cut it fill up the estates. In 1795 Julien Fedon rise the free-coloured and the enslaved against the British in the rebellion that bear his name, and a long tradition of standing up was set down, running on through emancipation to T.A. Marryshow, the father of the West Indian federation, who teach a small island to count itself one with its whole region. The island find the spice that would name it, nutmeg and mace climbing up the wet hills, the smell of it on the breeze, and put that nutmeg right there on its flag. Independence come in 1974, and then the chapter the nation still carry soft and sober, the revolution of 1979 to 1983 and the intervention of 1983, named on the public record and held now in the work of memory and reconciliation. Hurricane Ivan tear the island to pieces in 2004 and the island build back, and through all of it run Spicemas and the calypso and the soca, the Big Drum beating on Carriacou, and the oil-down cooking down slow in the one big pot. Under all of it run one nerve: a warm, God-fearing, hardback people, soft-spoken and proud, the sweetness of the spice carried over a chapter of memory not yet wholly mend.
