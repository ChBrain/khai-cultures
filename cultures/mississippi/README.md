# Mississippi: a khai culture

Mississippi staged as a full khai play, ISO US-MS, language en. The Hospitality State: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its Delta Blues birth, cotton plantation boom, and Freedom Summer campaign encodes, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house that holds this culture and answers for it.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_mississippi.md](play_mississippi.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_mississippi.md](pitch_mississippi.md), the Mississippi key.
- **Plots (history):** the Delta Blues Birth of the 1900s, the Cotton Plantation Boom of the 1830s, and the Freedom Summer Campaign of 1964.
- **Personas:** Charles (blues guitarist) and Ida (civil rights archivist), written in English.
- **Positions:** blues legacy (musical expression/historic struggle), civil rights solidarity (voter equality/justice pride).
- **Places:** Dockery Plantation, Freedom School.
- **Processes:** cotton picking (traditional harvest picking), slide guitar playing (delta blues slide playing).
- **Pieces:** a cotton boll, a resonator guitar.
- **Plan:** the Mississippi Delta flood plan.
