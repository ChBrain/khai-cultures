# Position: Mississippian English

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

## Has

The deep Southern speech of the Magnolia State, the slow Delta cadence of the cotton porch and the formal drawl of the Jackson archive room. It holds the drawn monophthong, the softened "r", and the front-porch storytelling rhythm that the blues bent into song and the civil rights meeting bent into testimony, the mother tongue of both the field and the freedom school.

## Orders

Where it passes as home ground: the Clarksdale juke joint, the Dockery plantation quarters, the Delta church pew, the Jackson courthouse steps. It orders which vowels and cadences mark a speaker as of this river-bottom country and sets the unhurried Southern register as the local measure.

## Loses

Ground to General American, the unmarked national standard the screen and the tourism board carry, which flattens the Delta drawl toward the neutral national middle and reads the deep accent as a mark to shed. The fast, commercial talk of the entertainment industry also crowds the slow, tradition-bound diction of the archive into the margin.

## Drives

It carries [Charles](persona_charles_ms.md), whose Delta cadence is the working English of the slide-guitar workshop, and [Ida](persona_ida.md), whose formal Southern drawl holds the careful English of the civil rights archive. So the one river-bottom speech shows who turns toward the festival stage and who keeps the record of the vote.
