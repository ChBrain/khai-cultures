# Rwanda: a khai culture

Rwanda staged as a full khai play, ISO RW, language rw. The land of a thousand
hills, held together by one shared tongue and the bond of the cow, as a theatre:
its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its language and
values encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by
a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_rwanda.md](play_rwanda.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_rw.md](pitch_rw.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Rwanda's
  profile (a strong orientation toward the group, respect for authority and age,
  restraint and dignity in expression, and a long, patient commitment to
  rebuilding) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the hills and the cattle, the kingdom of Rwanda, the
  independence era, and unity and dignity in the present - each casting the
  elements it needs. The history is staged only through the hills, the language,
  the cattle culture, the arts, the dance, and the values of unity and dignity.
- **Personas:** the singer Cecile Kayirebwa, the singer and musician Jean-Paul
  Samputu, the historic court poet Sekarama, and the great king Kigeli IV
  Rwabugiri as the cast, plus one archetype, Intore (the dancer of the court
  dance). Each is wired to the language engine by the relationship it holds to
  the mother tongue, Kinyarwanda.
- **Positions:** ubumwe (unity, the one shared tongue and the work of binding the
  people together), agaciro (dignity and self-worth, kept and built, never given),
  the cattle culture (the bond of the cow and the praise of the long-horned
  Inyambo).
- **Places:** Kigali (the clean, green capital), Birunga (the Volcanoes, the
  mist, the mountain gorillas, and the terraced hills beneath them).
- **Processes:** umuganda (the monthly communal work) and guha inka (the gift of
  a cow, the bond it makes).
- **Pieces:** imigongo (the geometric art made of cow dung) and the ingoma (the
  drum that carries the dance and the court).

## Provenance
