# Plan: the nation of Salone

## Taxonomy

The open mandate the country set itself when it came to itself as one nation: to braid its two stories - the people of the land with [the Societies](position_the_societies.md) that raise them, and the returned Africans of [the Settlement](position_the_settlement.md) with their Krio welcome - into one Salone without either being made the lesser; to keep the lamp of [the Learning](position_the_learning.md) lit for the generations coming up; and to make the green hills and the diamonds a blessing for the whole house.

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Direction

Hold the rope together - keep the rooted and the returned as one country, neither swallowing the other, the welcome of the town of the free shared by all and the deep authority of the societies honoured alongside it; keep the lamp of the learning lit, the schoolhouse open and the gown a ladder and not a wall; feed the many at one table the way the Awujoh does, so nobody, near or far, sits down hungry; and pray the riches of the land - the diamonds in the riverbeds, the palm and the rice and the green mountains - down into a blessing for the whole of Salone and not only for a few.

## Orders

- To the rooted son like [Bai Bureh](persona_bai_bureh.md): hold the law and the honour of the people of the land, and braid it into the wider Salone rather than against it.
- To the gathered people of [the Settlement](position_the_settlement.md): keep the door open and the table set, for "stranger no de tan stranger", and let the Krio welcome bind and not divide.
- To the schooled like [Samuel Ajayi Crowther](persona_crowther.md): give the learning back to the people, the gown in the service of the town, and never let the book become a wall.
- To the whole nation: go far by going together, and make the land's riches a blessing for the whole house.

## Implementation

A country free and one since 1961, its two stories braided, Krio carrying the whole of Salone as its lingua franca, its music gone out across the coast and into the wider world, its Leone Stars cheered by all. The welcome holds and the table is still set; the work that stands open is to keep the rope from fraying as each pulls his own end, to keep the lamp of the Athens of West Africa lit when the brightest are drawn abroad, and to bring the blessing of the diamonds and the green hills home to the whole people.
