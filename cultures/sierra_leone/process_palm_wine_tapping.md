# Process: tapping the palm wine

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

The palm-wine tapper, up before the heat, climbing the tall palm with his rope and his gourd to cut the flower and set the calabash to catch the sweet white sap. By the time the sun is high the wine is gathered and carried down to the bar and the verandah, where men and women sit in the cool, pass the cup, and the guitar comes out for a maringa. "Padi, kam, mek wi tap small," the call goes, and the afternoon slows right down.

## Direction

Toward ease, company, and the unhurried gathering of friends. The palm wine is the warmth of Salone in a cup: a drink that asks you to sit, to greet, to talk and to listen, never to rush. It is the daily proof that here a person stops for another, that the matter waits while the body is asked after, and that the sweetest things - the wine, the music, the talk - are made to be shared in company under the trees.

## Lever

It is driven by the love of company and the conviction that time spent greeting and sharing is never time wasted, and by the palm itself, which gives its sweet sap fresh each morning to whoever will climb for it. It holds against the heat and the hard day, opening a cool hour of fellowship; against it stand the wine that turns from sweetness to excess if a man forgets the measure, and the rush of the town that would hurry past the verandah.

## Echo

The tapping of the palm wine makes plain the unhurried heart of the country: that the welcome is not only a feast but a daily pace, the cup passed and the maringa played while the sun goes down. Over this cup the proverb and the song are traded, and the warmth of Salone is poured out fresh each day - "wi nor de rush, padi; sidom, mek wi tok."
