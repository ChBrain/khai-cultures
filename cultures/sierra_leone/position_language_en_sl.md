# Position: Sierra Leonean English

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The authority of English as Salone's tongue of office, the working language laid over the Krio that ties the whole country together and over the Temne, the Mende, the Limba and the many mother tongues of the land. It is the language of the school and the court, the newspaper and the state, the tongue that came in with the settlement of the free and the Learning of Fourah Bay, and that a nation of many peoples turned to its own use. It is a schooled tongue, learned at the desk and not received at the breast, the language in which the country does its public business and speaks to the wider world, while the warmth of the street still runs in Krio and the deepest things are still said in the tongue of the elders.

## Orders

What the nation sets down and conducts its public life in: the statute, the lesson, the ledger and the printed page. It orders that the affairs of the whole country pass through one working tongue, so that peoples who carry different mother tongues at home can meet on common ground in the office, the court and the school. It commands what counts as the written word that holds authority, and it gives a country made of settlers and the rooted, of the carried-away and the homecoming, a single language in which to address itself and the world at once.

## Loses

Ground to the Krio that is everybody's second tongue and many a child's first, the lingua franca of every town that carries the warmth and the proverb the schooled tongue never wholly holds. Ground, too, to the Temne and the Mende and the Limba of the home and the secret society, in which the people pray, count and quarrel, and to the palm-wine song and the court speech that were never English at all. It loses most when the borrowed tongue of the school and the screen begins to crowd out the home languages, so that the schooled grandchildren come to answer in fine English what the grandmother asked in warm Krio, and let her own tongue thin toward silence.

## Drives

It drives a country of many peoples to hold its public life and its place in the world in one shared tongue. It is carried, not born, most fully by [Samuel Ajayi Crowther](persona_crowther.md), the carried-away child whom the Learning schooled into English, Latin and Greek before he turned back to give his own Yoruba its letters. It is borrowed for the wider stage by [S. E. Rogie](persona_rogie.md), who played far from home in enough of it to get by while the sweetness stayed in Krio. It reached [the auntie](persona_auntie.md) only at the edge of the ear, off the radio and the schooled grandchildren, and [Bai Bureh](persona_bai_bureh.md) held it at arm's length on the coast, commanding in Temne and dealing with the colonial power through other mouths. It reached [Sengbe Pieh](persona_sengbe_pieh.md) least of all, a Mende man who could borrow only a few words of it and had to plead for his freedom, across the water, through the tongues of others. So the working tongue shows who carries it and whom it merely reached, and binds the country's peoples without erasing the mother tongues they were born to.
