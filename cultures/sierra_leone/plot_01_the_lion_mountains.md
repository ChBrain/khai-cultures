# Plot: the Lion Mountains

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

Long before any sail came over the water, the people of the land live by the green mountains and the heavy rain - the Temne by the coast and the rivers, the Mende in the south and the east, the Limba in the north, and many more, each in their towns with their farms and their secret societies. When the Portuguese sailors come down the coast and see the ridges rising straight out of the sea, they name the land Sierra Lyoa, the lion mountains, and the name holds.

## Action

For long ages the towns live by the law of the elders and the schools of the societies, the Poro and the Bondo raising each child into a grown person who knows their place and their people. Then the coast is drawn into the great trade across the water, and from the river forts, among them the works on Bunce Island, many souls are carried away to the far side of the ocean. It is a wound this telling holds with a sober and steady hand, naming it plainly and dwelling on no man's pain. The land keeps its name, the lion mountains, and keeps its people; and the carrying-away sets the table for the answer that is to come, when a home is made on that same coast for those the trade had taken.

## Tension

The land is whole and named and peopled, its towns held by the elders and the societies, older than any settlement on the coast. Yet the same waters that brought the sailors who named it bring the trade that carries its people away. The story opens with both the strength of the land and the wound the sea brings, and the whole telling after is the long answer to that wound - the making of a home for the carried-away on the very coast they were taken from.
