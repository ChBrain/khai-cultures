# Plot: the Founding of Freetown

## Owner

## Cue

In answer to the carrying-away, a settlement is founded on the coast as a home for people the trade had taken. The first companies of returnees come ashore, and after them, decade upon decade, Africans freed from the ships are landed at the same harbour, at the foot of the great Cotton Tree. They come from a hundred places and a hundred peoples, with no one home left to go back to but this new one.

## Action

The settlement is named Freetown, the town of the free, and from its gathering of many peoples a new people grows: the Krio, who take the languages and ways they all carried and weave them into one warm tongue and one common life. They build the tall board houses with their fretwork verandahs, they cook the Krio dishes, they hold the Awujoh feast that sets a plate for the family living and the family gone. Among the souls the wider world comes to know is [Sengbe Pieh](persona_sengbe_pieh.md), the man carried off who fought his way back across the water - the homecoming the whole settlement was made for, told in one life. The carried-away are gathered, the homeless given a home, and a new people stands on the old coast.

## Tension

The wound of the trade is answered by the making of a home: those scattered are gathered, and a people who had been carried off build a new life on the coast. Yet the new settlement and the people of the land must learn to be one Salone, the returned and the rooted sharing a single country, and the long work after the founding is to braid the two stories into one rope without either being made the lesser.
