# Piece: the gumbe drum

## Taxonomy

Parent group: pieces

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Place

In the yard, the bar and the street procession, wherever Salone gathers to make music: the square gumbe drum, a wooden frame with a skin stretched across it, played seated with the hands, the beat that carries the gumbe music and the maringa. From the verandahs of Freetown out to the towns of the coast, it is the pulse under the palm-wine guitar, the drum that calls people up to dance.

## Load Bearing

It stands for the music in which the many of Salone become one. The gumbe began among the returned and gathered people and grew into a sound the whole country shares, the beat under the maringa that S.E. Rogie made the song of the nation. In a land of many peoples and many tongues, the drum is a speech everyone understands: it needs no translation, it gathers Temne, Mende, Limba and Krio into one rhythm, and it carries the welcome of the country in a beat you cannot help but move to. Without this drum the country's gathering would have no pulse to dance to.

## Apparent

A square or rectangular wooden frame with an animal skin stretched and tuned across the top, played with the bare hands by a drummer sitting astride or behind it. Plain enough to look at, a box and a skin; its whole weight is in the beat it carries and the people it calls together, the maringa it drives and the dancing it raises.
