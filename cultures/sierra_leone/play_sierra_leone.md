# Play: Sierra Leone

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the house that holds this production and answers for it.

## Name

Sierra Leone, the small green country on the West African coast, between Guinea and Liberia, where the rain falls heavy and the mountains rise straight up out of the sea. The Portuguese sailors called it Sierra Lyoa, the lion mountains, for the way the ridges roar above the harbour, and the name stuck. ISO SL, language en, with Krio the lingua franca that ties the whole country together, plus Temne, Mende, Limba and the rest of the people's own tongues. Salone, the people call it, with plenty love. A land that took in those the world had sold away and made them a home, and where, as the old people say, "if yu wan go kwik, go wan; if yu wan go far, go togeda."

## Arc

A country with two stories braided into one rope. There were the people already on the land long before any ship came - the Temne by the coast, the Mende in the south and east, the Limba in the north, the Susu, the Loko, the Kono, the Sherbro and the rest - each with their towns, their farms, their secret societies that raised a child into a person, the Poro for the men and the Bondo for the women. And then there were those who came back: the liberated and the returned Africans, brought ashore at the foot of the great Cotton Tree to a settlement called Freetown, founded as a home for people the trade had carried off, and from that gathering of many places a new people grew, the Krio, with their own warm tongue, their board houses and their Awujoh feasts. From Freetown the learning spread, Fourah Bay College making the place the Athens of West Africa, schooling teachers and bishops and clerks for half the coast. The land gave diamonds out of the riverbeds and palm wine out of the trees, and the people gave the world gumbe and maringa, S.E. Rogie's sweet guitar and Ebenezer Calender's bands. The country came to itself as a nation in 1961, took its place among the free, and sends out its Leone Stars to play football for the whole of Salone. Under it all runs one nerve: a people who know what it is to be carried away and to come home, who carry their welcome like a lamp, and who hold that nobody is a stranger for long, for "stranger no de tan stranger" - a guest soon becomes one of us.

## Stakes

Whether a country built as a home for the carried-away can hold its two stories - the people of the land with [the Societies](position_the_societies.md) that raise them, and the returned Africans of [the Settlement](position_the_settlement.md) with their Krio welcome - into one Salone without one swallowing the other; whether it can keep the lamp of [the Learning](position_the_learning.md) lit and feed the many at one table the way the Awujoh feeds them; and whether the green mountains and the diamonds in the riverbeds become a blessing for all the people, the way the old people pray, and not a thing that pulls the rope apart.
