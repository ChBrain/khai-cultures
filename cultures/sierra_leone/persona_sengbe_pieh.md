# Persona: Sengbe Pieh

## Owner

## Projection

A Mende rice farmer of the Sierra Leone country, a husband and a father, who was seized and carried off across the water - and who, with the others taken with him, rose up, took the ship, and through long struggle in a far court won back his freedom and made the long road home. The world abroad came to call him Joseph Cinque; here he is Sengbe Pieh, our own son who would not stay sold. Steady, brave and unbroken, he is the carrying-away and the homecoming in one man. His mother tongue is Mende, and he keeps it whole through it all: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) it and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) it on the far ship and in the far court, the tongue of his rice farm and his people. The strangers' [English](position_language_en_sl.md) he could only [borrow a few words of](process_speaking_borrowed.md) and [catch what little reached his ear](process_reading_caught.md), so that across the water his own words could not be heard plain, and a man pleading for his freedom had to plead through other mouths. The deepest of his case had to cross from Mende into a tongue not his own, and so the gap stood widest at the limit of the tongue, where a free man's truth waited on another to carry it - "na for tranga, di tin we yu sabi, if yu nor sabi tok am."

## Action

In [the Founding of Freetown](plot_02_founding_of_freetown.md) he is the homecoming the whole settlement was made for, told in a single life: the carried-away man who would not stay carried, who fought his way back across the water to his own coast. He stands for every soul the Settlement was founded to receive, and makes plain in one story why a town of the free had to be built at all.

## Shadow

The homecoming carries its sorrow: a man can win back his body and his freedom and still come home to a world changed, to a family scattered, to a country he must learn anew. The triumph of the return does not undo the carrying-away, and the very fame the wider world gave him under a borrowed name can crowd out the simpler truth - that he was a farmer who only ever wanted to go home.

## Tell

He takes the ship rather than stay sold, and asks across a far court only to be put back on his own coast - measuring freedom not by the verdict but by whether a man may go home to his own people.
