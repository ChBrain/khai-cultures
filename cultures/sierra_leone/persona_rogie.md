# Persona: S.E. Rogie

## Taxonomy

[the Settlement](position_the_settlement.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The sweet-voiced guitarist of the palm-wine sound, who took the easy, lilting maringa of the bars and the verandahs and made it the song of the whole country and a name known beyond the coast. Gentle, smiling and unhurried, with that soft guitar in his hands, he is the warmth of Salone turned into music - the welcome you can dance to, the proverb set to a tune. He sang in the Krio that ties the country together, the lingua franca of every town, so that all of Salone could sing along: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) and [carries his songs](process_speaking_carried.md) in the Krio that began in the town of the free and grew into everybody's tongue. When the wider world came calling and he played far from home, the English of the record men and the foreign stages he [borrowed enough of to get by](process_speaking_borrowed.md), but the sweetness was always in the home tongue. A maringa carried in Krio could be felt across a hall where not one soul knew the words, and there at the limit of the tongue the music crossed where the speech could not - "di sweetness de pan di sound, nor to di word."

## Action

In [the Nation Comes to Itself](plot_04_the_nation_comes.md) he is the song of the young Salone: the palm-wine guitar and the maringa become the music of a country come to itself, hummed across the coast and carried into the wider world. He carries [the Settlement](position_the_settlement.md) forward in sound, the gathered Krio welcome made into a tune the whole nation, of every people, could sing as one.

## Shadow

The sweetness of the palm-wine sound can be taken for mere ease, the warmth mistaken for having nothing to say, so that a music made to gather a country is heard abroad as only a pleasant tune. And the artist who must go far to be heard can be claimed more by the foreign stage than by the home that made him, the maringa praised everywhere but at the verandah where it was born.

## Tell

He plays the welcome of his country soft enough to dance to and deep enough to carry a whole people, holding that the surest way to gather Salone is not to argue it together but to give it one tune to sing.
