# Plot: the Athens of West Africa

## Owner

## Cue

With the settlement standing and the Krio growing into a people, the love of the book takes root. Schools rise in Freetown, and in time Fourah Bay College is founded - the oldest college of its grade in West Africa - and the children of the carried-away sit down to Latin, Greek, scripture and law.

## Action

Out of Fourah Bay and the Freetown schools go teachers, clerks, lawyers, doctors and bishops, not only into Salone but across the whole coast, so that for a long season Sierra Leone is the schoolhouse of the region and earns its proud name, the Athens of West Africa. Among the first fruits is [Samuel Ajayi Crowther](persona_crowther.md), a boy freed from a slave ship and landed at Freetown, schooled at Fourah Bay, who rises to become the first African bishop and a maker of the written word in his own people's tongue. The lamp of learning lit in the town of the free shines out over half of West Africa, and the carried-away repay the world that took them by teaching it.

## Tension

The settlement made to receive the carried-away becomes the place that gives learning back to the whole region, the deepest answer yet to the wound it was founded upon. Yet the gown can become a wall as well as a ladder: the schooled coast must not hold itself above the people of the land and their societies, and the lamp of the Athens must be kept lit for the generations behind, lest the old name outlast the open schoolhouse.
