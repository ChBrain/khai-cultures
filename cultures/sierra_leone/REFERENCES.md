# Sierra Leone: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md) is written from Sierra Leone's profile on
Geert Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no
number. Hofstede Insights does not publish a full six-dimension score set for
Sierra Leone individually; the pitch is written from the West African regional
profile in the model, which is recorded here so the data is on the record without
entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | West Africa (regional) score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 77                                     |
| Individualism                 | 20                                     |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 46                                     |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 54                                     |
| Long Term Orientation         | 9                                      |
| Indulgence                    | 78                                     |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group for the West Africa region; the high
Power Distance and low Individualism mark a strongly group-bound, hierarchical, and
community-centred culture.

from the public historical record. They are credited here and never claimed; the
staging is original work. The memory of the trade across the water and of Bunce
Island is staged soberly, with dignity, with no atrocity detail and taking no
political side; the civil-war period of the 1990s is out of scope.
