# Piece: the Cotton Tree

## Taxonomy

Parent group: pieces

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Place

At the very heart of Freetown, in the middle of the old town where the roads meet: a great silk-cotton tree, huge and ancient, its grey trunk wide as a house and its branches spread over the streets. The spot beneath which the first returned and liberated Africans are said to have gathered and given thanks on first coming ashore, and ever after the meeting-place, the landmark, the heart of the town of the free.

## Load Bearing

It stands for the founding of the home itself, the place where the carried-away first gathered free. In a country made from many peoples, the Cotton Tree is the one thing every son and daughter of the capital can point to and call the centre - the spot the homecoming began, the gathering-place of a people made from the scattered. It carries the whole meaning of the Settlement in one living thing: the door opened, the thanks given, the home made on the old coast for those the world had sold away. Without this tree the town of the free would have no heart to gather round.

## Apparent

A silk-cotton tree, of the kind that grows tall across the West African forest, its trunk buttressed and grey, its crown full of birds and its branches reaching over the streets of the town. In itself it is a tree like the great trees of the bush; its whole weight is in what the country has gathered beneath it, the thanks given in its shade and the homecoming it has stood over since the beginning.
