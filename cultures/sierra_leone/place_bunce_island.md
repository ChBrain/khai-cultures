# Place: Bunce Island

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Shown

A small island up the Sierra Leone River, an hour or so above the harbour, where the ruins of an old river fort stand quiet among the trees and the tide. Once a station of the trade across the water, from which many souls were carried away, it is today a place of memory, visited soberly, its broken walls left standing as a witness. The country holds it not as a thing to dwell upon in pain but as a place to remember plainly, with dignity, and to keep faith with those who passed through it.

## Offers

A place to remember and to keep faith: the country looks at the island steadily, names the carrying-away plainly, and honours the memory of those taken without making a spectacle of their suffering. It anchors the whole story, for it is the wound that Freetown, the town of the free, was founded to answer - the question to which the welcome is the long reply.

## Withheld

The comfort of forgetting, and equally the indignity of dwelling on the worst. Bunce Island withholds both the easy peace of a country that turns its back on its memory and the harm of a telling that lingers on pain - it asks instead for sober remembrance, a steady eye, and the keeping of a promise: that the carried-away are remembered, and that the home built for them stays open.
