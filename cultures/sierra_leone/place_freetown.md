# Place: Freetown

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

## Shown

The town of the free, spread along the great harbour beneath the green mountains, where the rain comes down in sheets and the ridges climb straight up behind the streets. The old Cotton Tree standing at the heart of the town, the spot the returned and the liberated first gathered; the tall board houses with their fretwork verandahs and their shutters; Fourah Bay College up on the hill; the markets loud with Krio, the smell of fried fish and palm oil, the gumbe and the maringa drifting from the bars. The capital and the cradle, where Salone was made into a home for the carried-away.

## Offers

A place where the homeless were given a home and the scattered were gathered into one people; the seat of the welcome and the learning both, where the door stays open and the table stays set, and where Krio binds Temne, Mende, Limba and returnee into one Salone. The cradle of a people made from many, and the proof that "stranger no de tan stranger."

## Withheld

The ease of forgetting why it was built. Freetown carries the long memory of the carrying-away that the whole settlement was meant to answer, and the standing work of holding its two stories - the people of the land and the returned - into one rope without either being made the lesser. The town of the free reminds the country that the welcome was won, not given, and must be kept.
