# Position: Salone

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The belonging to Salone, a country with two stories braided into one rope, the peoples of the land, Temne, Mende, Limba and the rest, who raise a child into a person through [the Societies](position_the_societies.md), and the liberated and returned Africans gathered at Freetown who grew, from many places, into the Krio people of [the Settlement](position_the_settlement.md). It carries [the Learning](position_the_learning.md), the lamp that Fourah Bay College lit and carried the length of the coast, and it speaks [Sierra Leonean English](position_language_en_sl.md) alongside the Krio that ties the whole country together. It hands down, unchosen, a welcome carried like a lamp and the conviction that nobody is a stranger for long.

## Orders

That the guest be fed and asked after before he is asked his business, for stranger no de tan stranger; that the elder and the society that raised you be honoured, since a person is made by the community around them and not by themselves alone; that the lamp of learning, once lit at Freetown, be kept burning and handed on rather than hoarded; and that the two stories of the country, the rooted and the returned, sit at one table rather than looking past one another.

## Loses

Ground where the people of the land and the Krio of the Settlement look sideways at one another instead of into the same rope, the authority of the chief and the society held too tight against the schooled coast, or the schooled coast grown proud over the unlettered elder. It loses ground when the diamonds in the riverbeds and the green hills become a thing fought over rather than a blessing shared, and when the Learning that made Freetown the Athens of West Africa sends its brightest abroad and never gets them fully home.

## Drives

Drives Salone to feed the many at one table the way the Awujoh feeds them, and to raise every child, of the land or of the Settlement, into a person the community can answer for. It lives in the chief who answers for his whole people, in the freed man who fought his way home across the water, in the bishop who gave his own mother tongue its letters, and in the guitarist whose soft song gathered a whole country into one tune. It drives the nation to keep its two stories one rope and its lamp of learning lit, so that the green mountains and the diamonds become a blessing for all its people and not a thing that pulls it apart.
