# Sierra Leone: a khai culture

Sierra Leone, affectionately Salone, staged as a full khai play, ISO SL, language
en (written in Sierra Leonean English, warmly coloured by Krio). A country built
as a home for returned and liberated Africans, woven together with the deep-rooted
peoples of the land, as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the
cast, the roles its languages and welcome encode, its places, its practices, and
its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the house that holds
this production and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its Estate; a
culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_sierra_leone.md](play_sierra_leone.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Sierra
  Leone's profile (a strongly group-bound, kin-and-community culture, with respect
  for elders and hierarchy, and a warm, unhurried, welcoming register) and naming
  no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Lion Mountains (the people of the land and the sober
  memory of the trade), the Founding of Freetown (the home for the carried-away
  and the rise of the Krio), the Athens of West Africa (Fourah Bay and the love of
  learning), and the Nation Comes to Itself (independence in 1961, the music, the
  diamonds, and Leone Stars).
- **Personas:** Sengbe Pieh, Bai Bureh, Samuel Ajayi Crowther, and S.E. Rogie as
  the historic cast, plus Auntie Yeama, the Krio auntie and keeper of the Awujoh
  table, as the defining archetype. Each is wired to the language engine by the
  language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** the Settlement (the home made for the carried-away and the Krio
  welcome), the Societies (the secret societies and deep institutions of the
  people of the land, treated with dignity), the Learning (the love of the book
  that made Freetown the Athens of West Africa).
- **Places:** Freetown (the town of the free, the Cotton Tree and the board
  houses), Bunce Island (the river fort, the memory of the trade, held soberly).
- **Processes:** the Awujoh feast (the table that feeds the family living and
  gone), tapping the palm wine (the daily, unhurried gathering of friends).
- **Pieces:** the Cotton Tree (the heart of the town of the free), the gumbe drum
  (the beat in which the many of Salone become one).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the trade across the water and the memory of Bunce Island are treated
soberly on the public record, with no atrocity detail and no political side; the
staging and the architecture are original work.
