# Position: the Settlement

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The idea that gave Freetown its name: a place founded as a home for people the trade had carried away, where the liberated and the returned could come ashore and be free. Out of that gathering - returnees from across the water and Africans set free from the ships and landed at the foot of the Cotton Tree - a new people grew, the Krio, who took the many places they came from and made one warm tongue, one cuisine, one way of building the board houses with their fretwork verandahs. The Settlement is the conviction that a home can be made for the homeless, that those scattered can be gathered, and that "we own people" can be a thing you join, not only a thing you are born into.

## Orders

That the door stays open and the table stays set, for "stranger no de tan stranger" - a guest soon becomes one of us; that a people made from many places hold their welcome as their pride and their work; and that the Krio tongue and the Krio way bind the whole of Salone together as the lingua franca, the common ground on which Temne and Mende and Limba and returnee can all meet and call one another padi.

## Loses

Ground when the welcome turns into a hierarchy, when the returned and the people of the land begin to measure who is the truer Salone son, and the gathering meant to make one people hardens into two looking sideways at each other; ground, too, when the open door is taken for granted and the long memory of why it was opened - the carrying-away that the whole settlement was meant to answer - is allowed to fade into a story nobody tells.

## Drives

Lives in [Sengbe Pieh](persona_sengbe_pieh.md), the carried-away man who fought his way home and stands for every soul the Settlement was made to receive, and in [S.E. Rogie](persona_rogie.md), whose sweet palm-wine guitar became the song the gathered people of the town of the free could all sing. It drives Sierra Leone to be the home for the homecoming, the country that knows what it is to be sold away and to be brought back, and that holds its door open as the proof of who it is.
