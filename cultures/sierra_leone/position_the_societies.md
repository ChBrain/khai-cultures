# Position: the Societies

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The deep institutions of the people of the land - the Temne, the Mende, the Limba, the Susu, the Loko, the Sherbro and the rest - the secret societies that take a child and raise it into a grown person who knows the law, the lore and the duties of the community. The Poro for the men and the Bondo, also called the Sande, for the women: these are the schools of the old way, where the young are taught what it means to belong, to carry oneself, to honour the elders, and to hold the knowledge that is not spoken in the open. They are held with dignity and with privacy, guarded from idle eyes, the backbone of identity in the towns and villages, older than any settlement on the coast.

## Orders

That a person is not born complete but is made a person by the community and its societies, so belonging and initiation come before the standing of any single soul; that the elders carry the law and the young owe them honour, for those who came before know the road; and that there is knowledge meant for the inside, kept with respect and not paraded, the quiet authority that holds a people together.

## Loses

Ground when the old way and the new way are set against each other as enemies rather than woven together, when the schooled coast looks down on the societies as backward, or the societies close so tight against the wider Salone that the two stories of the country drift apart; ground, too, when the honour owed to elders and the knowledge kept inside are forgotten by the young who go to the town and never carry the lore home.

## Drives

Lives in every warrior chief who stood for the law and the authority of the people of the land, and in every elder woman who carries the rites that raise the young and feed the family living and gone. It drives Sierra Leone to hold that a person is made by the people around them, that the elders are heard, and that the deep knowledge of the land is honoured alongside the learning of the coast.
