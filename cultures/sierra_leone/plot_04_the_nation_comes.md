# Plot: the Nation Comes to Itself

## Taxonomy

[play_sierra_leone](play_sierra_leone.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

The two stories - the people of the land with their societies, and the returned Africans of the settlement with their Krio welcome - are by now bound into one country. In 1961 Sierra Leone takes its place among the free nations of the world, one Salone, the green mountains and the diamonds in the riverbeds its dower.

## Action

The young nation comes to itself with a flag of green, white and blue - the green of the mountains, the blue of the sea, the white for the hope between them. Krio carries the whole country as its lingua franca, the tongue in which Temne, Mende, Limba and returnee all call one another padi. The music pours out: the gumbe drum and the maringa, the sweet palm-wine guitar of [S.E. Rogie](persona_rogie.md) and the bands of Ebenezer Calender, songs the whole coast hums. The Leone Stars carry the country's name onto the football pitch, and the whole of Salone roars them on as one. The diamonds and the palm and the rice are the land's gift, and the people pray, in the old way, that the riches under the ground bless the whole house and not only a few.

## Tension

The country stands free and one, its two stories braided, its music carried across the coast, its Leone Stars cheered by all. Yet the rope held together by trust must be tended: the people of the land and the returned must keep neither swallowing the other, the lamp of the learning must stay lit, and the blessing of the diamonds and the green hills must be made to reach the whole of Salone, the way the old people pray over the Awujoh table - "if yu wan go far, go togeda."
