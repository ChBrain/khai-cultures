# Position: the Learning

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The bookishness that made Freetown the Athens of West Africa: the conviction that schooling lifts a person and a people, and the long pride in being lettered. From the early schools of the settlement grew Fourah Bay College, the oldest university-grade college in West Africa, and from it teachers, clerks, lawyers, doctors and bishops went out across the whole coast, so that for a long season Salone was the schoolhouse of the region. The Learning is the love of the book and the degree, the respect paid to the educated man, the church and the mosque both raising readers, and the faith that the gown and the pen are a road up and a road out into the wider world.

## Orders

That schooling is a high calling and the lettered person is honoured, so a family will give all it has to put a child through the books; that knowledge is a thing Salone gives to the whole coast and not only to itself, a lamp it was the first to light in the region; and that the educated owe their learning back to the people, as teachers, clerks and leaders, the gown in the service of the town.

## Loses

Ground when the learning becomes a wall instead of a ladder, when the schooled coast holds itself above the people of the land and their societies, and the book is used to look down rather than to lift; ground, too, when the Athens of West Africa rests on its old name while the schoolhouse runs short, the brightest go abroad and never come home, and the lamp that lit the region is left to gutter.

## Drives

Lives in every liberated African who was schooled at Fourah Bay and rose to become a bishop or a teacher across the coast, the very proof of what the Learning could make of a carried-away child. It drives Sierra Leone to hold the book in honour, to send its teachers across the coast, and to keep the lamp of the Athens of West Africa lit for the generations coming up behind.
