# Kazakhstan: a khai culture

Kazakhstan staged as a full khai play, ISO KZ, language kk (written in Kazakh
Cyrillic). A nomadic people of the boundless steppe staged as a theatre: its
history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its language and
faith encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by
a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_kazakhstan.md](play_kazakhstan.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_kk.md](pitch_kk.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Kazakhstan's profile (a hospitable, kin-centred, patient and enduring people of
  the wide steppe, holding elders and the spoken word in high honour) and naming
  no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Kazakh Khanate and the three juz, Abai and the
  flowering of Kazakh letters, the trials of the twentieth century, and
  independence and the reach for the stars - each casting the elements it needs,
  and each staging the nation's history soberly and with dignity.
- **Personas:** Abai Qunanbaiuly (the poet who raised the Kazakh word), Al-Farabi
  (the philosopher born at Farab, a heritage figure), Kurmangazy Sagyrbaiuly (the
  great kuy composer), and Mukhtar Auezov (the writer who kept the nation's
  memory) as the historic cast, plus one defining archetype, the Eagle Hunter
  (berkutshi), who carries the nomadic steppe into the present. Each is wired to
  the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** the way of the steppe (the nomadic life and worldview), Tengri
  and faith (the older sky-reverence woven with Islam), the mother tongue (the
  oral word and the akyn tradition raised into letters).
- **Places:** the steppe (the boundless home of the nomad) and Turkistan (the
  Khoja Ahmed Yasawi mausoleum, the spiritual heart).
- **Processes:** Nauryz (the spring new-year of renewal and reconciliation) and
  eagle hunting (the saiat tradition that binds man, horse, and bird).
- **Pieces:** the dombra (the two-stringed soul of the kuy) and the yurt (kiiz uy,
  the round felt home of the steppe).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the hardships of the twentieth century are treated soberly on the public
record, with no detail and no political side; the staging and the architecture
are original work.
