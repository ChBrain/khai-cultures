# Persona: Chloe

## Taxonomy

[Willamette eco-living](position_willamette_eco_living.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A thirty-six-year-old specialty coffee roaster in Portland. English, clipped to a direct West Coast cadence, is her mother tongue, the language in which she [speaks, hears, and thinks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), the defining code of her professional life. It is the [Oregon English](position_language_en_us_or.md) of Portland, the direct West Coast cadence of the coffee houses. She speaks no foreign languages. What she misses is the slower register: the tradition-bound agricultural talk of winemakers like Thomas and the historic pioneer narratives of [Oregon Trail](position_oregon_trail.md), which [she reads only as trade metrics](process_reading_followed.md) on her terminal, as the hard, physical reality of the land gets reduced, in her feed, to marketing slogans for urban consumers. Her claim on [Oregon culture](position_culture_oregon_culture.md) is Willamette eco-living, driven forward even where she reads the trail-worn half of that same culture only as trade metrics.

## Action

She preserves [pinot noir bottle](piece_pinot_noir.md) designs, coordinates resources under [the salmon passage plan](plan_salmon_passage_restoration.md), and studies [Oregon Trail](position_oregon_trail.md).

## Shadow

Her focus on localism and specialty markets can make her elitist, overlooking the immediate material struggles of rural timber workers outside the wealthy metropolitan districts.

## Tell

She looks at a micro-roasting machine and says directly: "We are sourcing directly from organic farms; you can't run a sustainable coffee business if you're only focused on industrial volume."
