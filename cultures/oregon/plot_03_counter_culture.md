## Cue

In the 1970s, specialty coffee houses, microbreweries, and organic food cooperatives establish a thriving localist economy in Portland.

## Action

The movement establishes [Willamette eco-living](position_willamette_eco_living.md) principles, prioritizing environmental stewardship and local trade. The [pinot noir bottle](piece_pinot_noir.md) represents this artisanal production. Chloe develops micro-roasting techniques, while Thomas implements biodynamic farming methods in the valley.

## Stage

- [Willamette eco-living](position_willamette_eco_living.md) : the localist, environmentalist, and sustainability culture of the valley.
- [pinot noir bottle](piece_pinot_noir.md) : the glass wine bottle representing local biodynamic agriculture.
- [organic winemaking](process_organic_winemaking.md) : the low-intervention fermentation process that replaced conventional farming.

## Tension

The rapid growth of the localist brand attracts heavy urban migration and real estate investment, leading to gentrification and the displacement of low-income residents.
