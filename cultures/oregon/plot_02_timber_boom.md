# Plot: Columbia River Timber Boom 1880

## Taxonomy

[play_oregon](play_oregon.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

In the 1880s, industrial sawmills expand along the Columbia River, transforming logging into the state's dominant resource industry.

## Action

The timber boom establishes major shipping and logging centers, clearing old-growth forests to supply global construction. The [timber logging](process_timber_logging.md) process shapes regional river transport. Chloe documents the logging heritage in Portland museums, while Thomas coordinates land conservation projects.

## Stage

- [the Columbia River Gorge](place_columbia_river_gorge.md) : the scenic river gorge that served as a transport corridor for logs.
- [timber logging](process_timber_logging.md) : the industrial timber cutting and transport operations in the forests.
- [the salmon passage plan](plan_salmon_passage_restoration.md) : the recovery plan developed to mitigate the impact of timber dams.

## Tension

High-volume logging and log-driving dams destroy native river habitats, causing a severe, long-term decline in wild salmon runs.
