# Persona: Thomas

## Taxonomy

[Oregon Trail](position_oregon_trail.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A fifty-eight-year-old winemaker in the Willamette Valley. English, flat and Northwestern, is his mother tongue, the language in which he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and writes his agricultural logs. It is the [Oregon English](position_language_en_us_or.md) of the Willamette Valley, the flat Northwestern speech of the vineyards. [French](../france/position_language_fr_fr.md) [he wears as an overgarment](process_speaking_worn.md) in his daily blending reviews, keeping alive the language of his training in Burgundy. What he cannot keep pace with is the tempo of it: the start-up talk of specialty roasters like Chloe and the digital demands of Portland marketing boards reach him [only like a distant echo](process_hearing_followed.md), and he suspects the patient, season-dependent rhythm of farming is being crowded out by the state's urban counter-culture economy. His claim on [Oregon culture](position_culture_oregon_culture.md) is Oregon Trail self-reliance, the ground he farms by, wary of the eco-living half of that same culture managed from Portland.

## Action

He manages [organic winemaking](process_organic_winemaking.md) harvests, utilizes resources under [the salmon passage plan](plan_salmon_passage_restoration.md), and studies [Willamette eco-living](position_willamette_eco_living.md).

## Shadow

His pride in pioneer independence and agricultural self-reliance can make him suspicious of state-wide water restrictions and municipal environmental plans, fearing they will increase farming costs and restrict vineyard expansion.

## Tell

He looks at a barrel of fermenting wine and says thoughtfully: "A vineyard requires patience and soil; you can't run agricultural exports if you only understand coffee house marketing and software releases."
