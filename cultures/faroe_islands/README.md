# Faroe Islands: a khai culture

Føroyar staged as a full khai play, ISO FO, declared language fo (Faroese). A
green archipelago in the North Atlantic between Norway, Iceland, and Scotland, as
a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its
language and sea encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all
tuned by a single pitch. The Faroese prose is machine-composed and flagged for
native-speaker review, on the same footing as the house's other assisted-language
cultures.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_faroe_islands.md](play_faroe_islands.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_faroe_islands.md](pitch_faroe_islands.md), the Hofstede layer,
  written from the Faroese profile.
- **Plots:** the Norse settlement and the Løgting, the language awakening, and the
  wartime occupation and home rule.
- **Personas:** the skipper and the young Faroese in generational tension, with
  Tróndur í Gøtu of the sagas, V.U. Hammershaimb who gave Faroese its written
  form, and Nólsoyar Páll the national forerunner.
- **Positions:** the Faroese (the culture), the sea, and the Faroese language.
- **Places:** Tórshavn and the villages.
- **Processes:** the chain dance and the grind.
- **Pieces:** the grass roof and the wool sweater.
