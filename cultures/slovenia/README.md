# Slovenia: a khai culture

Slovenia staged as a full khai play, ISO SI, language sl. A small Alpine nation,
long under others, as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the
cast, the roles its language and faith encode, its places, its practices, and its
defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_slovenia.md](play_slovenia.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_sl.md](pitch_sl.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Slovenia's
  profile (modest and consensus-seeking, anxious for security and order,
  hardworking and thrifty, close to nature and the mountains, understated, poised
  between Central Europe and the South) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** Trubar's books (1550), Prešeren and the Spring of Nations
  (1848), the centuries under the Habsburgs and then Yugoslavia, and Independence
  and the Ten-Day War (1991) - each casting the elements it needs.
- **Personas:** Primož Trubar, France Prešeren, and Anton Janša as the historic
  cast, plus two defining archetypes, Marija (older, diligent and thrifty, the
  garden and the bees, the mother tongue and its dual) and Luka (younger, the
  EU-native Western-facing skier and climber whose Slovene thins between English
  and German). Each is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it
  holds.
- **Positions:** slovenščina (the small South Slavic tongue with the rare dual,
  born as a literary language from Trubar's printing), sončna stran Alp (the sunny
  side of the Alps, the Alpine, North- and West-facing Central-European identity),
  pridnost (diligence and smallness, the bee-like industriousness and the quiet
  pride of "our little Slovenia").
- **Places:** Ljubljana (the Habsburg-Baroque capital, Plečnik's green city),
  Triglav (the highest peak, on the flag and the coat of arms, the national
  symbol).
- **Processes:** čebelarstvo (beekeeping, the Carniolan grey bee, Janša's legacy,
  World Bee Day), v gore (going to the mountains, hiking and climbing Triglav once
  in a lifetime).
- **Pieces:** panjska končnica (the unique painted beehive front-panel),
  kozolec (the distinctive Slovene wooden hayrack of the rural landscape).
