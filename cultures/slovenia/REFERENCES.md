# Slovenia: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_sl.md](pitch_sl.md) is written from Slovenia's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no
number. The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the
record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Slovenia score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------ |
| Power Distance                | 71                       |
| Individualism                 | 27                       |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 19                       |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 88                       |
| Long Term Orientation         | 49                       |
| Indulgence                    | 48                       |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published
by Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; Slovenia's very low Motivation
toward Achievement and very high Uncertainty Avoidance are notable in the
published data.

The two defining personas, [Marija](persona_marija.md) and [Luka](persona_luka.md),
are archetypes, not historical individuals: composites that carry the tension
between the older generation (diligence and thrift, the garden and the bees, the
Yugoslav decades, the mother tongue and its dual) and the younger one (the
EU-native, Western-facing life and the pull of English and German on a small
language whose dual and dialects thin).
