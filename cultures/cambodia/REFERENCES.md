# Cambodia: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical and cultural
sources behind the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_km.md](pitch_km.md) is written from Cambodia's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record without
entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Cambodia score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------ |
| Power Distance                | 78                       |
| Individualism                 | 20                       |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 10                       |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 30                       |
| Long Term Orientation         | 47                       |
| Indulgence                    | 38                       |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; some dimensions for Cambodia are
estimated, and the high Power Distance and strongly collective profile are the
load-bearing values behind the pitch.

## Historical and cultural sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn
from the public historical and cultural record. They are credited here and never
claimed; the staging is original work. The great sorrow the nation endured in the
twentieth century is staged soberly and with dignity, with no atrocity detail, no
numbers, and taking no political side; the arc emphasizes the enduring
civilization, the survival, and the renewal of the Khmer people.
