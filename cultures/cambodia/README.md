# Cambodia: a khai culture

Cambodia staged as a full khai play, ISO KH, language km. The heirs of Angkor, a
people of the wat and the rice, as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its
people as the cast, the roles its language and faith encode, its places, its
practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_cambodia.md](play_cambodia.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_km.md](pitch_km.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Cambodia's profile (a respect for hierarchy and age, a collective bond to family
  and village, restraint and the saving smile, and a deep, quiet endurance) and
  naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the rise of Angkor, the golden age of Angkor, decline and
  the return of independence, and sorrow and renewal in the twentieth century -
  each casting the elements it needs, and each staging the enduring civilization,
  the survival, and the renewal of the Khmer people.
- **Personas:** Jayavarman VII (the great Angkor king and builder), Suryavarman II
  (the builder of Angkor Wat), Chuon Nath (the scholar-monk who gave Khmer its
  dictionary and guarded its language), and Norodom Buppha Devi (the royal dancer
  who restored Khmer classical dance) as the historic cast, plus the rice farmer,
  the archetype of village life. Each is wired to the language engine by the
  language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** Theravada Buddhism (the national faith and the backbone of Khmer
  life, with its earlier Hindu-Buddhist heritage), the Angkor legacy (the pride
  and the burden of a glorious past), and the village and the wat (the small unit
  at the heart of Khmer life).
- **Places:** Angkor (the ancient capital, the temples in stone, near Siem Reap)
  and Phnom Penh (the modern capital at the meeting of the rivers).
- **Processes:** Khmer New Year, Choul Chnam Thmey (the yearly renewal that binds
  the people to village, wat, and ancestors), and the apsara dance (the royal
  classical dance that brings the carvings of Angkor back to life).
- **Pieces:** the krama (the checked cotton scarf, the everyday emblem of the
  ordinary Khmer) and prahok (the fermented fish at the backbone of the cuisine,
  the rhythm of the Tonle Sap on the plate).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical and
cultural sources are recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never

atrocity detail and taking no side; the staging and the architecture are original
