# Turkey: a khai culture

Turkey staged as a full khai play, ISO TR, language tr. A steppe people astride
two continents, between a secular republic and a devout heartland, as a theatre:
its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its language and
faith encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a
single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_turkey.md](play_turkey.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_tr.md](pitch_tr.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Turkey's
  profile (hierarchical and family- and honour-bound, warmly hospitable, anxious
  for certainty, proud, poised between tradition and modernity and between East
  and West) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** Anatolia (Manzikert, 1071, to Yunus Emre), 1453 and the
  Ottomans, the Republic (1923 and the 1928 alphabet revolution), and modern
  Turkey's secular-versus-religious fault line - each casting the elements it
  needs.
- **Personas:** Fatih Sultan Mehmet, Atatürk, and Yunus Emre as the historic
  cast, plus two defining archetypes, Ayşe (older, Anatolian faith, tea, the evil
  eye, the family, the children gone to Germany) and Can (younger, between
  Istanbul-modern and Anatolian-traditional, between the diaspora and Turkey).
  Each is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** iki kıta (between two continents, the bridge on the Bosphorus),
  laiklik ve din (Atatürk's secular republic against the faith of the heartland -
  the deepest modern fault line), Türk dili (the steppe-Turkic tongue and the 1928
  switch from Arabic script and Ottoman Turkish to Latin-script purified Turkish).
- **Places:** İstanbul (the city on two continents, Hagia Sophia and the Bosphorus),
  Ankara (Atatürk's republican capital in the Anatolian heartland).
- **Processes:** çay (the endless tea and Turkish-coffee culture of hospitality),
  hamam (the Ottoman bath and its cleansing, social ritual).
- **Pieces:** nazar boncuğu (the blue evil-eye bead, spanning secular and
  religious Turkey alike), halı (the knotted Turkish carpet, from nomadic craft to
  imperial art).
