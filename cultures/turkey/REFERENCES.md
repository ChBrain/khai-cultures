# Turkey: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_tr.md](pitch_tr.md) is written from Turkey's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no
number. The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the
record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Turkey score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ---------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 66                     |
| Individualism                 | 37                     |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 45                     |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 85                     |
| Long Term Orientation         | 46                     |
| Indulgence                    | 49                     |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published
by Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; Turkey's high Uncertainty
Avoidance and its hierarchical, collectivist profile are notable in the published
data.

The two defining personas, [Ayşe](persona_ayse.md) and [Can](persona_can.md),
are archetypes, not historical individuals: composites that carry the tension
between the older generation (Anatolian faith, tea, the evil eye, the family, the
mother tongue, the children emigrated to Germany) and the younger one (between
Istanbul-modern and Anatolian-traditional, between the diaspora and Turkey, and
the pull of German and global English on the language).
