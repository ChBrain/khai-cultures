# Mongolia: a khai culture

Mongolia staged as a full khai play, ISO MN, language mn (written in Cyrillic).
A nation of the great steppe under the eternal blue sky, staged as a theatre: its
history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its language, faith,
and way of life encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all
tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_mongolia.md](play_mongolia.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_mn.md](pitch_mn.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Mongolia's profile (a collective, kin- and community-bound society, patient and
  enduring in the face of a hard climate, with the wide-space solitude of a vast
  land and few people) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Great Mongol State (1206), the spread of Tibetan
  Buddhism, the restoration of independence, and democracy (1990) - each casting
  the elements it needs, and each staged soberly and with dignity, taking no
  political side.
- **Personas:** Chinggis Khaan, Zanabazar (the artist-lama), and Damdin
  Sukhbaatar as the historic cast, plus the modern literary figure Dashdorjiin
  Natsagdorj (founder of modern Mongolian literature), and one defining archetype,
  the herder (malchin), the living witness of the nomadic life. Each is wired to
  the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** the eternal blue sky (Tengrism woven with Tibetan Buddhism and
  shamanism), the nomadic life (herding, the five snouts of livestock, the
  seasonal move), and the legacy of Chinggis (unity, honour, and being master of
  one's own land), all carried soberly as heritage.
- **Places:** Kharkhorin (Karakorum, the imperial capital and the ground of
  Erdene Zuu), Ulaanbaatar (the modern capital where each new age is decided).
- **Processes:** Naadam (the three manly games of wrestling, archery, and horse
  racing) and the migration (the seasonal nomadic move of the ger).
- **Pieces:** the horse-head fiddle (morin khuur, the voice of the steppe) and the
  ger (the felt home of the nomad).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never

are treated soberly and with dignity, on the public record, taking no political
side; the staging and the architecture are original work.
