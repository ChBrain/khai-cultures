# Belgium: a khai culture

Belgium staged as a full khai play, ISO BE, languages nl, fr, de. A country invented
as a neutral buffer and held across a language border, staged as a theatre: its
history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its languages encode, its
places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house that
holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its Estate; a
culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_belgium.md](play_belgium.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_be.md](pitch_be.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Belgium's
  profile (a strong need for rules and certainty answered by endless compromise,
  individualist and long-term, fond of the absurd and the understated) and naming no
  dimension.
- **Plots (history):** independence in 1830, the Yser front of 1914-18, the fixing
  of the language border in 1962-63, and the federalisation of the state - each
  casting the elements it needs.
- **Personas:** Albert I (the Roi-Chevalier) and Hendrik Conscience (who taught
  Flanders to read) as the historic cast, plus three archetypes, Julien (a Walloon of
  the de-industrialised south), Wout (a young, self-confident Fleming), and Naïma (a
  multilingual Bruxelloise, the bridge). Each is wired to the language engine by the
  language-crossing it holds, and together they stage a country with no common tongue.
- **Positions:** la frontière linguistique (the language border), le compromis à la
  belge (the genius for compromise), la Couronne (the Crown above the divide).
- **Places:** Bruxelles (the bilingual capital and EU seat), l'Yser (the last free
  front of the Great War).
- **Processes:** le carnaval (Binche and the Gilles), la table (the convivial Belgian
  table of frites, mussels, and beer).
- **Pieces:** les frites (the one national thing both communities claim), la pipe de
  Magritte (surrealism, and the gap between the word and the thing).
- **Plan:** tenir le pays ensemble, the standing mandate to hold a country without a
  common language together by compromise, the Crown, and federalism.
