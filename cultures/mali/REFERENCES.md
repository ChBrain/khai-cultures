# Mali: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical and cultural
sources behind the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_fr.md](pitch_fr.md) is written from Mali's (West African)
profile on Geert Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and
carries no number. The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on
the record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Mali / West Africa score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ---------------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 80                                 |
| Individualism                 | 15                                 |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 46                                 |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 50                                 |
| Long Term Orientation         | 20                                 |
| Indulgence                    | 40                                 |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate and, where Mali
itself is not separately published, drawn from the West Africa regional cluster as
published by Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; Mali is a strongly
collective and hierarchy-respecting society with a low Individualism score.

claimed; the staging is original work. The great empires, the oral epic, the

The one archetype persona, [the jeli](persona_le_jeli.md), is not a historical
individual but a composite that carries the role the Manden assigned to the griot
caste: keeper of genealogy, of the Sundiata epic, and of the given word.

The peoples named in the staging - the Bambara (Mande), the Songhai, the Fulani
(Peul), the Tuareg, and the Dogon of the Bandiagara cliffs - and the joking
kinship (sinankunya / cousinage à plaisanterie) are drawn from the public
ethnographic record and treated with respect.
