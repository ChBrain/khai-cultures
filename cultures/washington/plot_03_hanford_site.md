# Plot: The Hanford Site and Manhattan Project 1943

## Cue

In 1943, the federal government establishes the secret Hanford Site along the Columbia River to produce plutonium for the Manhattan Project.

## Action

The project establishes a legacy of scientific research and nuclear waste cleanup, prompting the development of [the salmon recovery plan](plan_salmon_recovery.md) to protect surrounding river habitats. Alan's tech firm analyzes ecological data from the site, while Sarah's Yakima farm is located downwind from the historic reactor zones.

## Stage

- [the salmon recovery plan](plan_salmon_recovery.md) : the environmental initiative designed to protect native fish from river pollution.
- [the Yakima Valley](place_yakima_valley.md) : the agricultural valley adjacent to the Hanford security zones.
- [Cascadia](position_cascadia.md) : the environmentalist values mobilized to clean up and protect the Columbia River basin.

## Tension

The rapid construction of the reactors forces the evacuation of local farming towns and releases toxic radioactive isotopes into the surrounding air and water.
