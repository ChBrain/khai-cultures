# Plot: The Oregon Treaty 1846

[play_washington](play_washington.md)

## Owner

## Cue

In June 1846, Great Britain and the United States sign the Treaty of Oregon, establishing the 49th parallel border.

## Action

The treaty establishes [Cascadia](position_cascadia.md) regional trade patterns, securing the Pacific Northwest for settlement and agricultural development. The [apple crate](piece_apple_crate.md) becomes a symbol of the agricultural trade that grew in the fertile valleys. Sarah coordinates apple shipments along these historic trade corridors, while Alan researches early boundary survey data.

## Stage

- [Cascadia](position_cascadia.md) : the regional environmental and cultural identity focused on the forests and rivers.
- [apple crate](piece_apple_crate.md) : the wooden crate representing the state's commercial fruit agriculture.
- [apple picking](process_apple_picking.md) : the manual agricultural harvest that developed in the valleys.

## Tension

The boundary settlement ignores the sovereignty and territorial rights of the native Coast Salish and inland Columbia River tribes, leading to decades of forced relocation.
