# Position: the Evergreen State

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

Belonging to a state set at the edge of a continent and the edge of a technology, drawn by a treaty line agreed far away and shaped ever since by its role as a gateway to the Pacific Rim. It carries [Cascadia](position_cascadia.md), the bioregional environmentalism of the sound, the rivers, and the salmon runs the state is still trying to restore, and it carries the [tech frontier](position_tech_frontier.md), the commercial ambition of the aerospace bays and the software campuses that made Washington a name in the world's markets. It speaks [Washington English](position_language_en_us_wa.md), the rapid, flat cadence common to the orchard and the office alike. What it hands to whoever grows up in it, whether they end up in an orchard row or a server room, is a working assumption that a wild coastline and a global industry are meant to sit inside the same state without either one having to give way.

## Orders

That a rivet set in an assembly bay and a fish counted at a dam are both taken seriously as measures of the state's health; that isolation from the rest of the country be treated not as a disadvantage but as room to build something first, whether an aerospace industry, a software giant, or a coffee culture that spread outward from Seattle to the world; that the wilderness of the Cascades and the Sound be defended as part of the state's identity and not merely its scenery; and that whoever works the land, the water, or the cloud be measured by the same plain, unhurried competence.

## Loses

Ground where the fast, well-paid talk of the tech corridor treats the slower, season-bound work of the orchard and the fishery as a rounding error, reading the physical truth of a river or a harvest as nothing more than a data point on a dashboard; ground in the housing costs and displacement that a booming tech economy visits on the working-class neighborhoods it grew up alongside; and ground at the Hanford Site itself, where the atomic-age legacy of the Columbia River leaves a cleanup that will outlast the generation that built the reactors.

## Drives

Drives the people of Washington to hold the orchard and the assembly bay, the fishery and the server farm, as one economy rather than two rival claims on what the state is really worth, and to keep pressing at the seam between them until the person who reads the land and the person who reads the code understand that they are describing the same place. It pushes the state to treat its isolation as an advantage, a room in which an aerospace industry, a software empire, and a coffee trade could each be built first and exported to the world, while never quite letting it forget that the salmon it is trying to bring back were here before any of it.
