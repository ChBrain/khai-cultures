# Persona: Sarah

## Taxonomy

[Cascadia](position_cascadia.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A fifty-two-year-old orchard manager in the Yakima Valley. English, spoken with a slow, flat Northwestern cadence, is her mother tongue, the language in which she [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and writes her harvest logs. It is the [Washington English](position_language_en_us_wa.md) of the Yakima Valley, the slow, flat Northwestern cadence of the orchard country. Spanish [she wears as an overgarment](process_speaking_worn.md) in her daily orchard work, coordinating with seasonal agricultural crews. Yet the loss lies in the seam: The fast, start-up talk of software developers like Alan and the digital demands of the Redmond campuses [she hears only like a distant echo](process_hearing_followed.md), feeling that the patient, season-dependent rhythm of agriculture is being forgotten in the state's tech economy. She belongs to [the Evergreen State](position_culture_evergreen_state.md) through its Cascadia half, the conviction that the orchard's season-bound labor is worth as much as the tech corridor's quarterly release.

## Action

She manages [apple picking](process_apple_picking.md), utilizes resources under [the salmon recovery plan](plan_salmon_recovery.md), and studies [tech frontier](position_tech_frontier.md).

## Shadow

Her suspicion of urban-led environmental mandates can make her resistant to irrigation and pesticide regulations, fearing they will increase farming costs and make local orchards uncompetitive.

## Tell

She looks at a bin of freshly picked apples and says thoughtfully: "An orchard requires seasons and soil; you can't run agricultural exports if you only understand cloud servers and software releases."
