# Latvia: a khai culture

Latvia staged as a full khai play, ISO LV, language lv. A small Baltic nation,
long under foreign lords, as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people
as the cast, the roles its language and song encode, its places, its practices,
and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_latvia.md](play_latvia.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_lv.md](pitch_lv.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Latvia's
  profile (extreme modesty and reserve, stoic restraint and understatement, a love
  of nature, a song-carrying soul, quiet melancholy and stubborn resilience) and
  naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the German centuries (the Baltic-German barons since the
  Livonian crusade), the National Awakening (the Young Latvians and the dainas),
  the occupations (1918 independence, the Soviet and Nazi occupations, the
  deportations and Russification), and the Singing Revolution (the Baltic Way and
  the 1991 barricades) - each casting the elements it needs.
- **Personas:** Krišjānis Barons, Krišjānis Valdemārs, and Vaira Vīķe-Freiberga as
  the historic cast, plus two defining archetypes, Marta (older, a lifelong Song
  Festival singer in a half-Russian Rīga) and Roberts (younger, the post-2004
  emigrant whose Latvian thins between English and the Russian of his home city).
  Each is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** latviešu valoda (the peasant tongue under German lords, raised by
  the Young Latvians), dziesma (song - the ~1.2 million dainas, the Song Festival,
  the nation that sang itself free), starp kaimiņiem (between great neighbours -
  the peasant nation under foreign lords and the sharp Russian-minority question).
- **Places:** Rīga (the great Hanseatic and Art Nouveau capital, German- and
  Russian-layered, the most cosmopolitan and most Russified city), Latgale (the
  Catholic, Latgalian-speaking, Russian-mixed eastern borderland, the "other"
  Latvia).
- **Processes:** Dziesmu svētki (the five-yearly Song Festival of massed choirs,
  UNESCO heritage), Jāņi (the pagan-rooted Midsummer of bonfires, oak wreaths,
  cheese, and song).
- **Pieces:** Lielvārdes josta (the woven Lielvārde belt whose ancient signs read
  like a coded "writing" of the nation), rudzu maize (the dark rye sourdough, the
  near-sacred staple of the table).
