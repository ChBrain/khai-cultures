# Persona: Arthur

## Taxonomy

[coastal stewardship](position_coastal_stewardship.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A thirty-eight-year-old lobster boat captain in Penobscot Bay. English, spoken with a maritime cadence, is his mother tongue, the language in which he [speaks, hears, and thinks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), the defining code of his daily trapping checks. What he speaks is [Maine English](position_language_en_us_me.md), the down east coastal cadence in which his whole working day is set. He speaks no foreign languages. Yet you can't get there from here without crossing some ground first: the slower, tradition-bound talk of forest conservationists like Eleanor and the quiet history of [forest independence](position_forest_independence.md) [he reads only as regional resource updates](process_reading_followed.md) on his workstation, while the patient, self-reliant woodcraft of early interior towns gets crowded out by the push for bigger seafood exports. He belongs to [the Pine Tree State](position_culture_pine_tree_state.md) as the captain who carries its coastal half, reading the harbor the way Eleanor reads the woods.

## Action

He preserves [lobster buoy](piece_lobster_buoy.md) markers, coordinates harbor safety programs under [the lobster plan](plan_gulf_of_maine_fishery_management.md), and studies [coastal stewardship](position_coastal_stewardship.md).

## Shadow

His intense focus on harvest metrics and commercial seafood trade can make him indifferent to municipal environmental zoning problems in interior agricultural counties.

## Tell

He adjusts a marine telemetry sensor on his boat and says directly: "We are calibrating high-volume commercial lobster traps; you can't run a profitable fishery if you're only focused on historic land preservation."
