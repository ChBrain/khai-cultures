# Place: Portland Head Light

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Shown

The historic stone lighthouse situated on a rocky cliff in Cape Elizabeth, featuring white tower walls, a red-roofed keeper's house, and crashing waves.

## Holds

- the political and maritime history in the plot [The Separation of 1820](plot_01_separation.md) : the light tower.

## Offers

The museum exhibitions and harbor navigation briefings where [Eleanor](persona_eleanor.md) audits historic shipping logs.

## Withheld

The labor exploitation. While celebrated as a beacon of safety, the lighthouse's early construction and maintenance relied on low-wage local stonecutters working under extremely hazardous winter conditions, details omitted from modern tourist guides.
