# Venezuela: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-record sources behind the plots,
personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_es.md](pitch_es.md) is written from Venezuela's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record without
entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Venezuela score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 81                        |
| Individualism                 | 12                        |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 73                        |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 76                        |
| Long Term Orientation         | 16                        |
| Indulgence                    | 100                       |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; Venezuela's Collectivism (low
Individualism) and Indulgence scores are among the most pronounced in the
published data, tuning the warm, familiar, and exuberant tone of the pitch.

original work. Venezuela is staged from within its own self-understanding, taking
no side; the recent economic crisis and the great migration are treated soberly as
the gap between promise and reality, without political blame and without detail of
suffering.

The defining persona [el llanero](persona_el_llanero.md) is an archetype, not a
historical individual: a composite that carries the soul of the Orinoco plains -
the free horseman, the singer of coplas, and the brava independence at the root of
the country's nineteenth century. The cultural framing - the joropo, the cuatro,
the arepa, baseball, the warmth of the Caribbean, and the longing of the diaspora
to return - is drawn from Venezuela's own widely shared self-understanding.
