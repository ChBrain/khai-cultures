# Venezuela: a khai culture

Venezuela staged as a full khai play, ISO VE, language es. The cradle of a
continent's independence, blessed and cursed by oil, and carried today in a vast
diaspora, as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the
roles its language and soul encode, its places, its practices, and its defining
objects, all tuned by a single pitch. The prose body is written in Spanish; only
the structural headings are in English.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_venezuela.md](play_venezuela.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_es.md](pitch_es.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Venezuela's profile (warm and Caribbean, exuberant and humorous, familiar and
  hospitable, present-focused and affectionate) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the cradle of independence (Miranda and Bolivar,
  1811-1821), the republic of the caudillos (the nineteenth century of the llano
  and civilizacion y barbarie), oil Venezuela (the twentieth-century boom and the
  democracy after 1958), and the crisis and the diaspora (the present wound),
  each casting the elements it needs and each staging the gap between the promise
  and the reality soberly.
- **Personas:** Simon Bolivar, Francisco de Miranda, Romulo Gallegos, and Simon
  Diaz as the historic cast, plus one defining archetype, el llanero (the free
  horseman of the plains who carries the soul of the llano). Each is wired to the
  language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** el bolivarianismo (the cradle of the Liberator and Bolivar's
  enduring shadow), el alma llanera (the plains soul, the joropo, and the old
  tension of civilizacion y barbarie), and el petroestado (oil as blessing and
  curse, the rentier nation).
- **Places:** Caracas (Bolivar's birthplace under the Avila, the capital) and los
  Llanos (the vast Orinoco plains, the llanero heartland).
- **Processes:** el joropo (the harp, cuatro, and maracas music and dance of the
  plains) and el beisbol (the national passion and the winter-league fervor).
- **Pieces:** la arepa (the maize cake, the daily bread carried abroad as a
  portable homeland) and el cuatro (the four-string guitar that drives the joropo
  and the parranda).
- **Plan:** [plan_la_venezuela_posible.md](plan_la_venezuela_posible.md), the
  possible Venezuela - to rebuild beyond oil, reconcile a divided people, and
  bring the diaspora home.

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-record sources are recorded
in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the pitch is
its invisible hand. Sources on the public record are credited, never claimed;
Venezuela is staged from within its own self-understanding, taking no side, and
the recent economic crisis and the great migration are treated soberly as the gap
between promise and reality, without political blame. The staging and the
architecture are original work.
