# Lithuania: a khai culture

Lithuania staged as a full khai play, ISO LT, language lt. An archaic Baltic
nation, once vast, as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the
cast, the roles its language and faith encode, its places, its practices, and its
defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_lithuania.md](play_lithuania.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_lt.md](pitch_lt.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Lithuania's
  profile (proud but restrained, modest and serious, devout, deeply rooted,
  patient, a melancholic resilience) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** Žalgiris/Grunwald (1410), the Tsarist press ban and the
  book-smugglers (1864-1904), the Soviet occupation, deportations, and the Hill of
  Crosses, and March 11 (1990) - each casting the elements it needs.
- **Personas:** Vytautas the Great, Jonas Basanavičius, and Vytautas Landsbergis
  as the historic cast, plus two defining archetypes, Ona (older, rural Catholic
  faith, the Hill of Crosses, the deportations remembered) and Mantas (younger, the
  post-2004 emigrant whose archaic Lithuanian thins toward English in his
  children). Each is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** lietuvių kalba (the most archaic living Indo-European tongue, the
  press ban and the knygnešiai who saved it), Didžioji Kunigaikštystė (the Grand
  Duchy, once Europe's largest state, and the memory of lost greatness), katalikybė
  (Europe's last pagans, baptized only in 1387, faith as resistance over a pagan
  substrate).
- **Places:** Vilnius (the baroque capital, the Grand Duchy's seat, the lost
  "Jerusalem of the North," the 1991 TV-tower defence), Kryžių kalnas (the Hill of
  Crosses bulldozed by the Soviets and replanted overnight).
- **Processes:** Joninės (the pagan-rooted Midsummer of fires, wreaths, and the
  fern flower), krepšinis (basketball, the "second religion" of a small nation).
- **Pieces:** Vytis (the white mounted knight of the coat of arms, the Grand
  Duchy's emblem and continuity), gintaras (Baltic amber, "Lithuanian gold," the
  sea and the Jūratė and Kastytis legend).
