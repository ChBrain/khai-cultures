# Lithuania: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_lt.md](pitch_lt.md) is written from Lithuania's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no
number. The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the
record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Lithuania score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 42                        |
| Individualism                 | 60                        |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 19                        |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 65                        |
| Long Term Orientation         | 82                        |
| Indulgence                    | 16                        |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published
by Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; Lithuania's very low Motivation
toward Achievement and Indulgence scores are notable in the published data.

The two defining personas, [Ona](persona_ona.md) and [Mantas](persona_mantas.md),
are archetypes, not historical individuals: composites that carry the tension
between the older generation (rural Catholic faith, the parish, the Hill of
Crosses, the deportations remembered, the mother tongue) and the younger one (the
post-2004 emigration and the pull of global English on the most archaic of living
languages).
