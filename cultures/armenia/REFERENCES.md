# Armenia: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical and cultural
sources behind the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_hy.md](pitch_hy.md) is written from Armenia's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record without
entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Armenia score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ----------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 85                      |
| Individualism                 | 22                      |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 50                      |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 88                      |
| Long Term Orientation         | 61                      |
| Indulgence                    | 25                      |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; where a country's published values
vary across editions, the values used are indicative of a collectivist, high
power distance, and strongly uncertainty-avoiding profile.

from the public historical and cultural record. They are credited here and never
claimed; the staging is original work. The great sorrow of the early twentieth
century and the diaspora it scattered are staged soberly and briefly, with
restraint and dignity, with no detail and taking no political side.

The one archetypal persona, the [khachkar carver](persona_vardpet.md), is not a
historical individual but a composite: the anonymous master who, century after
century, keeps the nation's faith and memory in carved tufa where no two stones
are alike.
