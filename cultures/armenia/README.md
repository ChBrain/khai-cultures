# Armenia: a khai culture

Armenia staged as a full khai play, ISO AM, language hy. An ancient highland
nation kept not by border or army but by faith, the letter, and the mountain it
cannot reach, staged as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as
the cast, the roles its language and faith encode, its places, its practices, and
its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_armenia.md](play_armenia.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_hy.md](pitch_hy.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Armenia's profile (a warm, close, family- and group-centered people, deep
  respect for elders, faith, and memory, and a long endurance carried with
  dignity) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the adoption of Christianity (301), the creation of the
  alphabet (405), the monasteries and the manuscripts, and the sorrow and the
  renewal - each casting the elements it needs.
- **Personas:** Mesrop Mashtots (the creator of the Armenian alphabet), Tigranes
  the Great (the ancient king, heritage memory), Komitas (the priest-musician who
  saved the folk song), and Hovhannes Tumanyan (the people's poet) as the
  historic cast, plus one archetype, the khachkar carver (the anonymous master
  who keeps the nation's memory in stone). Each is wired to the language engine by
  the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** faith (the Church as the nation's spine when there was no state),
  the language and the letter (the alphabet as the nation's first homeland), and
  resilience (the endurance that keeps what loss could not erase).
- **Places:** Echmiadzin (the cathedral and spiritual heart, before Mount Ararat),
  Yerevan (one of the world's oldest cities, the reborn capital and the
  Matenadaran).
- **Processes:** baking lavash (the oldest bread and the table that shares it),
  the matagh and the table (faith become a shared meal).
- **Pieces:** the khachkar (the cross-stone, a prayer in carved tufa), the duduk
  (the apricot-wood reed whose voice carries the nation's grief and hope).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical and
cultural sources are recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never
enters the prose; the pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain
are credited, never claimed; the great sorrow of the early twentieth century and
the diaspora it scattered are treated soberly and briefly, with restraint and
dignity, taking no political side; the focus is kept on the enduring culture -
faith, the alphabet and language, Mount Ararat, the music, and the manuscripts.
The staging and the architecture are original work.
