# Plot: The Great Chicago Fire 1871

## Taxonomy

[play_illinois](play_illinois.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

In October 1871, a devastating fire sweeps through Chicago, destroying four square miles of the city center and leaving 100,000 homeless.

## Action

The destruction prompts a massive rebuilding boom that establishes [metropolis](position_metropolis.md) as a laboratory for architectural innovation, leading to the invention of the steel-framed skyscraper. The [blueprint](piece_architectural_blueprint.md) becomes the tool of this rebuilding. Robert designs modern high-rises in the Loop, drawing inspiration from these early structural pioneers.

## Stage

- [metropolis](position_metropolis.md) : the ambition, engineering, and architectural drive of the city.
- [blueprint](piece_architectural_blueprint.md) : the technical drawings that guided the rebuilding and early skyscraper designs.
- [the Chicago Loop](place_chicago_loop.md) : the commercial center that was rebuilt with steel-framed high-rises.

## Tension

The rapid reconstruction prioritizes commercial interests and corporate offices, displacing working-class immigrant neighborhoods to the city's periphery.
