# Illinois: a khai culture

Illinois staged as a full khai play, ISO US-IL, language en. The Prairie State: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its agricultural, architectural, and transport life encodes, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house that holds this culture and answers for it.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_illinois.md](play_illinois.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_illinois.md](pitch_illinois.md), the Illinois key.
- **Plots (history):** the Great Chicago Fire and rebuilding of 1871, Lincoln's rise in Springfield, and the transcontinental railroad hub.
- **Personas:** Robert (Chicago architect) and Mary (central Illinois corn farmer), written in English.
- **Positions:** prairie grit (heartland resilience), metropolis (urban ambition/architecture).
- **Places:** Lincoln's home (Springfield), the Chicago Loop.
- **Processes:** skyscraper construction, corn harvesting.
- **Pieces:** an architectural blueprint, a railroad spike.
- **Plan:** the Chicago River reversal and canal flow plan.
