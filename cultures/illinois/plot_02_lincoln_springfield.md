# Plot: Lincoln's Rise in Springfield 1860

## Taxonomy

[play_illinois](play_illinois.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

In the decades leading to 1860, Abraham Lincoln practices law and serves in the legislature in Springfield, culminating in his election as President.

## Action

Lincoln's political career in Springfield establishes [prairie grit](position_prairie_grit.md) as a national symbol of heartland resilience and defense of the Union. His historic home becomes a monument. Mary retells Lincoln's stories to visitors, highlighting the agrarian values that shaped his early politics.

## Stage

- [prairie grit](position_prairie_grit.md) : the resilience, pragmatism, and hard work of the rural heartland.
- [Lincoln's home](place_springfield_home.md) : the historic Springfield residence where Lincoln lived before his presidency.
- [rail spike](piece_rail_spike.md) : the steel spike representing the expansion of the Illinois central railroad that Lincoln represented as a lawyer.

## Tension

Lincoln's rise and the polarization over slavery highlight deep regional divisions between the northern industrial counties and southern-leaning rural districts of the state.
