# Plot: the peoples and the lake

[play_malawi](play_malawi.md)

## Owner

## Cue

Down the long ages, peoples move into the land along the western shore of the great lake, the inland sea that runs nearly the whole length of the country. The Chewa settle the central plain and raise the Maravi from whom the country would one day take its name, and around the lake and the highlands gather the Tumbuka and the Yao, the Lomwe and the Sena, and, coming down from the south, the Ngoni.

## Action

The peoples settle into the lake and the land, the fishing life on the shore and the farming life on the plain, each with its own tongue and its own dance, the Gule Wamkulu of the Chewa and the vimbuza of the north among them. The dugout bwato is pushed out onto the water before dawn, the nsima is turned in the pot at the family fire, and the long custom of the shared plate and the open hand takes root. No single people rules all the rest; the land fills with many peoples living side by side along the lake that feeds them all.

## Stage

- [Mount Mulanje](place_mulanje.md): the standing highland over the southern country where the peoples settle and farm.

## Tension

A land of many peoples gathers along one lake, each keeping its own tongue and its own way, and the long question of the country is set from the start: whether the many can be held as one. The lake feeds them all and the custom of the open hand binds them, but the many tongues and many peoples are a richness that will one day have to be made, deliberately and with care, into a single house where none is a guest.
