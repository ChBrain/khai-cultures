# Play: Malawi

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the house that holds this production and answers for it.

## Name

Malawi, a long and slender country in south-eastern Africa, landlocked between Tanzania, Mozambique, and Zambia, lying along the western shore of the great lake that bears its name. The capital is Lilongwe, set on the central plain; the old commercial town is Blantyre, down in the south. ISO MW, language en, the neutral bridge tongue spoken across a land that carries Chichewa and Tumbuka and Yao and many more besides. A small country by the map, but the one the whole continent has learned to call the Warm Heart of Africa.

## Arc

A country that the map makes small and the welcome makes large. Its spine is the lake, the inland sea the missionaries called the Lake of Stars, brimming with more kinds of fish than any lake on earth, and along its shore the fishing life goes on as it has for centuries, the dugout bwato pushed out before dawn and the nets drawn in at first light. Around the lake the peoples gathered and settled, the Chewa and the Tumbuka, the Yao and the Lomwe, the Ngoni and the Sena and more, each with its own tongue and its own dance, and Malawi has made its name on holding them all in one house with no people made to feel a guest. In the south rise the highlands, Mulanje the island-in-the-sky and the cool green Zomba Plateau, with the tea estates spread below and the Shire River running down out of the lake. The missionaries came and named the lake for its stars, and an early voice spoke up for the dignity of his own people and is remembered soberly as a father of the nation. The country took its own name and its own flag, and the masked spirit-dancers of the Gule Wamkulu, the Great Dance of the Chewa, were honoured by the whole world as a treasure to keep. Under it all runs one nerve: a people who reckon that warmth costs nothing and is worth everything, who would rather share the last of the nsima off one plate than eat alone with plenty, and who hold their north and their south, their many tongues and their one welcome, as the truest wealth a poor country can have.

## Triggers

**[Plot 1: the peoples and the lake](plot_01_the_lake.md)**

Down the long ages the peoples gather on the shore of the great lake, the Chewa and those who came after, settling into the fishing life and the farming life around the inland sea, and [the Lake of Stars](position_lake_of_stars.md) becomes the spine on which the country grows.

**[Plot 2: the Lake of Stars named](plot_02_lake_of_stars.md)**

In the nineteenth century the missionaries reach the lake by way of [Mount Mulanje](place_mulanje.md) and the highlands, name its night water the Lake of Stars, and plant the missions and the tea estates that knit the south, while the older order of [the many peoples](position_the_peoples.md) meets the wider world.

**[Plot 3: the early voice](plot_03_chilembwe.md)**

In 1915 [John Chilembwe](persona_chilembwe.md), a mission-trained pastor of the south, speaks up for the dignity of his own people and is remembered soberly as an early father of the nation, the first to say plainly that [the many peoples](position_the_peoples.md) are no one's lesser.

**[Plot 4: the Warm Heart](plot_04_the_nation.md)**

In 1964 the country takes its own name and its flag and governs itself from [Lilongwe](place_lilongwe.md), and Malawi makes its name in the world as the Warm Heart of Africa, its musicians and its masked dancers carrying [the Warm Heart](position_warm_heart.md) far past its borders.

## Stakes

Whether a country the map keeps poor and small can keep the welcome that made it the Warm Heart of Africa, can hold its north and its south and its many tongues in one house with no people made a guest, can keep the fishing life and the lake that feeds it alive as the waters thin and the people grow, and can carry its dances and its songs and its open hand into the world without letting hardship close the heart that is the one wealth it has always had.
