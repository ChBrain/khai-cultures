# Piece: the bwato

## Taxonomy

Parent group: pieces

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Place

Drawn up on every beach along the great lake and out on its dark water before the dawn: the dugout canoe, hollowed by hand from a single trunk, low and narrow, paddled or poled out onto the inland sea by the fishermen of the shore. It rests nose-up on the sand by day among the drying racks and the mending nets, and goes out under the last stars by night, its lamp one of the lights that gave the Lake of Stars its name.

## Load Bearing

It stands for the fishing life that feeds the lakeshore and for the lake itself as the spine of the country: the bwato is how a landlocked people goes to sea, the craft in which the catch that feeds the shore is won. In a country with no coast, the dugout on the lake is the boat the whole nation lives by, carrying the chambo and the usipa home to the racks and the pots, and carrying with it the oldest way of life in the land. Without it the lake is only water; the bwato is how the country takes the inland sea into its hands.

## Apparent

A single log, hollowed and shaped, low in the water and balanced by the skill of the man who paddles it, a lamp set in it for the night fishing. In itself it is a tree made into a boat by patient hand; its whole weight is in the lake it goes out onto, the catch it brings in, and the centuries of fishermen who have pushed the same shape off the same beaches before first light.
