# Persona: Wambali Mkandawire

## Taxonomy

[the Warm Heart](position_warm_heart.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A singer of the north whose voice carried the warmth of Malawi out into the world, weaving the harmonies of the lakeshore and the church choir with jazz and the soul of the wider continent, a man who sang home to those who had left it and carried the country's gentleness in his throat. Soulful, devout, and generous on the stage, he sang as a Malawian sings, to gather people in and not to set himself above them. His Tumbuka was the first music of his mouth, the tongue in which he [spoke](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), [thought](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md), and [sang the songs of home](process_writing_mother_tongue.md); Chichewa he [carried](process_speaking_carried.md) as the everyday speech of the wider country, and English, [the bridge tongue](position_language_en_mw.md), he [followed](process_reading_followed.md) on the world's stages where his music travelled. He sang the Warm Heart in his mother tongue and let the melody carry what the words could not be translated to mean, so that a listener who had no Tumbuka still felt the warmth, though the exact home in the song stayed behind at the gap at the limit of the tongue.

## Action

In [the Warm Heart](plot_04_the_nation.md) he gives the modern nation its singing voice, carrying [the Warm Heart](position_warm_heart.md) of Africa out onto the world's stages and home again to every Malawian far from the lake. He carries the country forward in song, proving that the warmth of a small country can fill a hall anywhere on earth.

## Shadow

The singer who must leave home to be heard is the country's own quiet sorrow: that the warmth which fills the world's stages is grown in a country too poor to keep all its gifted at home, and that the song of belonging is often sung loudest by those who had to go.

## Tell

He opens his mouth and the lakeshore comes out, the church choir and the jazz club and the warmth of a whole country in one voice, gathering a room of strangers into a home.
