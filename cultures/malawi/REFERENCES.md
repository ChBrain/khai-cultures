# Malawi: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical and cultural
sources behind the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md) is written from Malawi's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record without
entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Malawi score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ---------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 70                     |
| Individualism                 | 30                     |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 40                     |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 50                     |
| Long Term Orientation         | 34                     |
| Indulgence                    | 30                     |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Malawi is not always listed
individually in the published data; the figures here are approximate and drawn
from the regional (East and Southern African) profiles published by Hofstede
Insights / The Culture Factor Group, used only to tune the register of the pitch.

the public historical and cultural record. They are credited here and never
claimed; the staging is original work. The country's encounters and its early

the many peoples are treated with respect.

The writers and singer cast here, [Jack Mapanje](persona_mapanje.md),
[Legson Kayira](persona_kayira.md), and [Wambali Mkandawire](persona_wambali.md),
are real figures of the country's letters and music, credited from the public
record. The one archetype, [the lake fisherman](persona_fisherman.md), is not a
historical individual but a composite that carries the fishing life of the lake
and the oldest way of being in the land.
