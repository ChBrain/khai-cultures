# Persona: Legson Kayira

## Taxonomy

[the Lake of Stars](position_lake_of_stars.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A boy of the northern lakeshore who set out on foot with a few coins, a Bible, and a copy of Pilgrim's Progress, and walked some thousands of miles up the length of the continent to reach a school, and who came home a writer the world had read, the very picture of a Malawian determination that hardship cannot wear down. Patient, faithful, and quietly stubborn, he carried the gentleness of his people and a will of iron beneath it, the warmth and the grit that a poor shore breeds together. His Tumbuka he had from the cradle, the tongue in which he [spoke](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thought](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) the home he was walking away from to walk back toward; English he [carried](process_speaking_carried.md) as the road tongue of the school he was walking to, and learned to [write his story](process_writing_drafted.md) and [read the wide world](process_reading_followed.md) in it. He told the tale of a Tumbuka boy of the lake in the language of the far-off schools that took him in, the only tongue in which that journey could be read where books are kept, and the first warmth of the home he left, said in the only words that fit it, stayed behind at the gap at the edge of the tongue.

## Action

In [the Warm Heart](plot_04_the_nation.md) he becomes the figure of what the lakeshore can send into the world, a son of [the Lake of Stars](position_lake_of_stars.md) who walked out of a poor village and wrote his way around the earth. He carries the country's name forward as living proof that the gentle and the determined are one people, and that warmth and an unbending will come off the same shore.

## Shadow

The boy who must walk a continent to reach a desk is the country's own ache: that a poor shore can grow the finest will and have nowhere near to spend it, that the road out is long for those born far from the school, and that the patience which carries a man so far can ask too much of those who never get the chance to set off.

## Tell

He walked the length of Africa toward a school with a Bible and one borrowed book, and came back a writer, the warmth of his people and the iron in his patience proved to be the same metal.
