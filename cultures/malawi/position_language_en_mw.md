# Position: the bridge tongue

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

English lets a speaker address anyone, high or low, with the same single word "you", carrying no mark of rank the way many of the country's own tongues carry in a change of pronoun or verb form; and it lets speakers of unrelated mother tongues meet without either one bending to the other's noun classes, since English sorts its nouns by nothing but singular and plural, not by the many noun classes that organize a Bantu sentence and demand agreement across every adjective and verb.

## Orders

It forces a fixed word order, subject before verb before object, to show who is doing what, since it has almost no case ending left to do that work; it forces every noun to take an article, a or the, marking whether the thing spoken of is known already or new to the hearing; and it forces the same plain "you" onto every hearer, so that a speaker addressing an elder or a stranger cannot fall back on the layered respect a home tongue would require.

## Loses

It cannot easily carry the noun class concord that gives a Bantu sentence its music, where a single class marker echoes across noun, adjective, and verb alike; it flattens the respect distinctions that Chichewa and Tumbuka build into the pronoun and the verb ending, so that a plain "you" must do the work that a home tongue splits into several forms; and it cannot hold, without long paraphrase, the exact shape of a proverb built on a home tongue's own grammar, so that something of its weight goes missing in the carrying over.

## Drives

It drives whoever holds it to listen for order rather than ending to find the doer and the done to, and to reach for an article on every noun out of habit. It habituates the speaker to a flat mode of address that treats every hearer alike in the grammar, even where a home tongue would first weigh age and standing, and it trains the mind to build a sentence around a fixed slot for subject and verb rather than around the noun class a home tongue would lead with.
