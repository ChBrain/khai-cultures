# Position: the bridge tongue

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The English of Malawi, the neutral bridge that belongs to no one people and so can be shared by every people. In a land that carries Chichewa and Tumbuka and Yao and many more besides, no one mother tongue was made to lord it over the rest; English, the tongue of the mission and the school and the office, became the common ground on which north and south, Chewa and Tumbuka and Yao and Lomwe, could meet as equals. It is nobody's first water and everybody's second, the tongue in which the country writes itself to the world and holds its many peoples in one house with none made a guest for the tongue of home.

## Orders

That the bridge tongue be shared so that no one mother tongue is set above the others, and every people meets on it as an equal; that it carry a Malawian voice out to the world, the poem and the song and the story, without leaving the home tongue behind; and that it stay a bridge between the peoples and never harden into a fence that shuts out the one who never reached the school.

## Loses

Ground when the bridge becomes a wall, kept for the schooled and set against the fisherman who catches only a word of it by ear; ground when the young leave the mother tongue for the English of the town and the deep home words thin, until a grandchild cannot answer a grandmother; and ground when the exact weight of a Chichewa proverb or the warmth of a Tumbuka song will not cross over, and the truest thing a Malawian has to say stays waiting at the limit of the tongue.

## Drives

Carried by [John Chilembwe](persona_chilembwe.md), whose Chichewa was the speech of his pulpit and his people while English was the tongue that could be heard where it mattered; carried by [Jack Mapanje](persona_mapanje.md), who poured the village proverb into the world's tongue so a Malawian voice could travel out; and carried by [Legson Kayira](persona_kayira.md), the Tumbuka boy who learned to write his own journey in it. It is followed by [Wambali Mkandawire](persona_wambali.md), who met it on the world's stages where his Tumbuka song travelled, and caught by ear by [the lake fisherman](persona_fisherman.md), who has no need of it out on the water and takes only a word off the radio. So the one bridge tongue, first water to no one, shows a people who share a second tongue precisely so that no first tongue rules the rest.
