# Place: Lilongwe

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Shown

The capital set on the central plain, on the banks of the Lilongwe River, a city of two halves: the Old Town with its market and its minibus ranks and the smell of roasting maize, and the planned Capital Hill where the government sits among the wide avenues and the jacaranda. The seat from which a country of many peoples governs itself, neither the lakeshore nor the highland but the central ground between them, the place the young nation chose to plant its flag and its parliament when it took its own name.

## Holds

- [the early voice](plot_03_chilembwe.md): the country's early stirring toward its own dignity, the ground from which the nation that governs from here would grow.
- [the Warm Heart](plot_04_the_nation.md): the seat of the self-governing nation, the capital from which Malawi took its name and made it in the world.

## Offers

A place where a country of many peoples meets on common central ground, where the Chichewa of the centre and the tongues of north and south mingle in one market, and the bridge tongue does its quiet work in the offices on the hill. A capital that holds the nation together not by grandeur but by being the meeting-place of all its parts.

## Withheld

The lake and the highland that are the country's true faces. Lilongwe is the plain centre, the seat and the meeting-ground, but the soul of the country is out on the water and up on the mountain, and the capital carries the knowing that the nation's heart lies in the shore and the highland it governs from the middle.
