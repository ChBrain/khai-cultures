# Persona: the lake fisherman

## Taxonomy

[the Lake of Stars](position_lake_of_stars.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A man of the lakeshore, asodzi, who pushes his dugout bwato out onto the great water before the light, sets his nets for the chambo and the usipa and the kampango, and comes in at dawn to the women waiting to dry the catch on the racks in the sun. Weathered, patient, and devout, he reads the water and the wind the way his father taught him, takes what the lake gives, and shares the surplus down the beach as a matter of course. His mother tongue, Chichewa or Tumbuka or Yao by the stretch of shore he was born on, is the only tongue the lake has ever asked of him: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md), and [hears](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) the water and the weather in it, the words for every wind and every fish handed down in it. The English of the town and the school, [the bridge tongue](position_language_en_mw.md), he [catches by ear](process_reading_caught.md), a word here and there off the radio or a notice, and seldom needs out on the water. He names the lake and its fish and its moods in the tongue his people have always named them in, the warmth and the work of a whole life said in one mother tongue, and the day the lake thins and the young ones leave for the town and the English of it, the old names will be left waiting at the gap at the edge of the tongue. He belongs to [the Warm Heart](position_culture_the_warm_heart.md) as the oldest hand at sharing the catch down the beach as a matter of course.

## Action

In [the peoples and the lake](plot_01_the_lake.md) he is the oldest figure in the country, the man on the water who has fished the inland sea since before there was a name for the nation, the living root of [the Lake of Stars](position_lake_of_stars.md). He carries the country forward by carrying it on his back from the lake, the catch that feeds the shore and the way of life that holds it.

## Shadow

The fisherman whose lake is asked to feed more each year is the country's own warning: that the warmth that shares the catch down the beach cannot share what is no longer there, that the great water has limits the growing shore is reaching, and that the oldest way of life in the land is the one most easily lost.

## Tell

He pushes the bwato out under the last stars and reads a lake's worth of weather in one glance at the water, taking what it gives and sharing what is left, the same way his grandfather did.
