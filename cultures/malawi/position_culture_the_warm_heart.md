# Position: the Warm Heart

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

Membership in the country the whole continent calls by a nickname it earned rather than chose, the Warm Heart of Africa: the conviction that the greeting comes before the business, that a stranger is fed before he is questioned, and that warmth is the one wealth a poor country can always afford. Moni is the first word and the chitenje is spread for the guest; you ask after a man's home and his people before you ask anything of him. It holds [the many peoples](position_the_peoples.md), the Chewa and Tumbuka and Yao and Ngoni and more, each with its own tongue, gathered in one house with none made a guest in it; and it holds [the Lake of Stars](position_lake_of_stars.md), the inland sea that is larder and road and beauty at once, the oldest rhythm the country lives by. It is the belief that no one who has eaten at your fire is a stranger after, and that to share the last of the plate is not a sacrifice but the plain way of being a person among people, umunthu, the dignity that comes of belonging to others.

## Orders

That a guest is welcomed and fed before anything is asked of him, and that the open hand is the measure of a person; that the elder and the chief are honoured as a matter of course and the soft answer is the strong one; that every people and every tongue be held in equal dignity, north and south alike, with none set above; and that warmth given freely, north or south, to kin or to stranger, is the true wealth of the country and the thing it will not let hardship take.

## Loses

Ground when the gentleness is leaned on too hard and becomes a deference that swallows the grievance rather than speak it, when patience curdles into a resignation that bears the hungry season in silence rather than name it; and ground, too, when the open hand is taken for weakness by those who would mistake a warm heart for a soft head. It loses ground as well when north and south are set against each other over the little there is, and when the lake that feeds the country is asked to feed more mouths than its waters can easily bear.

## Drives

Lives in the pastor who first said plainly that his people were no one's lesser, in the poet who carried the village proverb into the world's tongue without losing its weight, in the boy who walked a continent to reach a school and came home to prove that warmth and an iron will are the same metal, in the fisherman who reads the lake's moods and shares its catch down the shore, and in the musician who carried the warmth of the lakeshore into song and home again to every Malawian far from the lake. It drives Malawi to greet first and feed first, to hold its many peoples and many tongues in one house, to hold the head high in a poor country, and to keep the heart open against every hard year, because the welcome is the one thing no map can take away.
