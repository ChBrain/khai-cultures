# Process: the Gule Wamkulu

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

The Nyau, the ancient masked society of the Chewa, called to dance at the great moments of the village: at a funeral, at the end of mourning, at an initiation, at the installation of a chief. The drums are sounded, the masked spirit-dancers come out from the bush and the burial grounds in their masks of wood and cloth and animal skin, each figure a being from the world of the spirits and the ancestors, and the village gathers in a wide ring to receive them. It is treated with the dignity of a sacred thing, for the dancers are not men playing a part but the spirits come among the living.

## Direction

Toward the keeping of the unseen order and the binding of the village to its ancestors and its dead. The Great Dance carries the teaching of the society, the honouring of the departed, and the marking of the great passages of a life, the boundary between the living and the dead walked openly for a day. It is the Chewa's deepest custom and one of the country's great treasures, honoured by the whole world as a masterpiece to be kept.

## Lever

It is driven by the conviction that the ancestors are present and the spirits must be received with respect, and by the secret keeping of the Nyau society, which holds the knowledge of the masks and the dance close and hands it down with care. It holds against the passing of time and the pull of the new; against it stands only the forgetting that would let a sacred thing dwindle into a show, which the keeping of the society guards against.

## Echo

The Gule Wamkulu makes audible the deep ground beneath the country's gentleness: that the warmth of the living rests on the honouring of the dead, and that a people who welcome the stranger so openly keep, at their centre, a sacred thing held close and handed down. It is the Great Dance of the Chewa, a treasure of the whole country and of the world, carried into each new generation with the same respect.
