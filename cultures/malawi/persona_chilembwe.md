# Persona: John Chilembwe

## Taxonomy

[the many peoples](position_the_peoples.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A mission-trained pastor of the southern highlands who founded his own church and school and spoke up, soberly and with conviction, for the dignity of his own people at a time when few would, and who is remembered today as an early father of the nation, his face on the kwacha. Devout, learned, and quiet-spoken, he held that a man of this soil was no one's lesser and that his people deserved to be taught, to be heard, and to be treated as men. His English he had whole from the mission, and he [wrote in his mother tongue](process_writing_mother_tongue.md) and [followed the printed page](process_reading_followed.md) with the ease of a man schooled in books, the bridge tongue his to use against the very order that taught it to him. Chichewa was the speech of his pulpit and his people, the tongue in which he [spoke](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thought](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) the things closest to him; English, [the bridge tongue](position_language_en_mw.md), he [carried](process_speaking_carried.md) as the language that could be heard where it mattered. He pleaded his people's cause in the tongue the powerful would read, knowing the words that could be heard at all were not the ones his people spoke at home, and so even his protest ran up against the gap at the edge of the tongue.

## Action

In [the early voice](plot_03_chilembwe.md) he stands up in 1915 for the dignity of his own people and pays for it with his life, the first to say plainly that [the many peoples](position_the_peoples.md) of this land are no man's property and no man's lesser. By saying it sober and clear when it cost everything, he hands the country that would later take its own name an early father and a plain word to grow from.

## Shadow

The cause that must be paid for in a man's life is the country's own warning: that the gentleness which makes the Warm Heart can be leaned on too long and too hard, that patience asked of the patient becomes a burden, and that even the softest people reach a place past which dignity will not be made to bend.

## Tell

A pastor with a school and a church, he asked only that his people be counted as men, and the asking of it, plainly and at the cost of his life, made him a father of a nation that did not yet have a name.
