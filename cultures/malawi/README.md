# Malawi: a khai culture

Malawi staged as a full khai play, ISO MW, language en. The Warm Heart of Africa
staged as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the
roles its warmth and its tongues encode, its places, its practices, and its
defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_malawi.md](play_malawi.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Malawi's profile (a warm and welcoming collectivism, respect for elders and
  authority, patience and modesty, and a gentle, unhurried register) and naming
  no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the peoples and the lake, the Lake of Stars named, the
  early voice, and the Warm Heart, each casting the elements it needs, and each
  staging the country's encounters soberly and with dignity.
- **Personas:** John Chilembwe, Jack Mapanje, Legson Kayira, and Wambali
  Mkandawire as the real cast, plus one archetype, the lake fisherman (the man on
  the water who is the oldest figure in the land). Each is wired to the language
  engine by the language-crossing it holds, with both Chichewa and Tumbuka colour
  and English the neutral bridge.
- **Positions:** the Warm Heart (the famed friendliness and the open hand), the
  many peoples (north and south and many tongues held in equal dignity), the Lake
  of Stars (the great lake and the fishing life it feeds).
- **Places:** Lilongwe (the central capital and meeting-ground), Mount Mulanje
  (the island-in-the-sky of the southern highlands).
- **Processes:** sharing the nsima (the shared plate and the open hand), the Gule
  Wamkulu (the Great Dance of the Chewa, the masked spirit-dancers, treated with
  dignity).
- **Pieces:** nsima (the maize staple at the centre of every shared plate), the
  bwato (the dugout canoe of the lake fishing life).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical and cultural
sources are recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the
prose; the pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited,
never claimed; the Gule Wamkulu and the country's many peoples are treated with
respect, the early national figure is handled soberly, and the staging and the
architecture are original work.
