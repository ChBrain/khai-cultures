# Position: the Lake of Stars

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The great lake that is the spine of the country and the heart of its life, the vast freshwater inland sea the missionaries called the Lake of Stars for the lamps of the night fishermen scattered like stars on its dark water, known too as Lake Nyasa and Lake Malawi. It runs nearly the whole length of the country, holds more kinds of fish than any lake on earth, the bright cichlids the world calls mbuna among them, and feeds the shore with the chambo and the usipa and the kampango drawn in each dawn. Along its beaches the fishing life goes on as it has for centuries, the dugout bwato pushed out before light, the nets drawn in at first sun, the catch dried on racks by the women and shared down the beach. The lake is larder, road, and beauty all at once, the inland sea of a landlocked land.

## Orders

That the lake is the country's living larder and must be read and respected, its winds and its fish and its moods learned and handed down; that what it gives is taken and the surplus shared down the shore as a matter of course; and that the fishing life, the bwato and the net and the dawn launch, is a way of being and not only a way of eating, the oldest rhythm in the land.

## Loses

Ground when the lake is asked to feed more mouths than its waters easily can, when the fish thin and the nets come in lighter year on year, and when the young leave the shore for the town and let the old names for the winds and the fish go unlearned; ground, too, when the larder is taken for bottomless and the limits of even an inland sea are forgotten.

## Drives

Lives in the lake fisherman, who pushes the bwato out under the last stars and reads a lake's worth of weather in one glance, and in a son of the northern shore who walked off it to the world and wrote his way home. It drives Malawi along its lake, the spine that feeds it and names it, the Lake of Stars that is the one sea of a country with no coast.
