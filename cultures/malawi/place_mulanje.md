# Place: Mount Mulanje

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Shown

The great massif of the southern highlands, the island-in-the-sky that rises sheer from the tea estates of the plain, its peaks the highest in the country and the highest in all of central Africa, often wrapped in cloud so that it seems to float above the green below. Its cedar forests, its waterfalls, and its bare grey granite domes have stood over the south long enough to gather their own legends, the home in the old tales of the spirits and the place a man does not climb without respect. Below it spread the tea estates and the cool of the Zomba Plateau, and the Shire River runs down out of the lake through the country it overlooks.

## Holds

- [the peoples and the lake](plot_01_the_lake.md): the highland over the southern country, the standing landmark above the land where the peoples settled and farmed.
- [the Lake of Stars named](plot_02_lake_of_stars.md): the gateway of the highlands by which the missionaries came up to the lake and planted the missions and the tea of the south.

## Offers

A place of awe and respect in a gentle country, the high ground that gives the south its weather and its rivers and its tea, and a landmark that gathers the legends of the people who live in its shadow. A mountain that offers the country a sense of its own deep age and the standing presence the old tales give it.

## Withheld

The easy familiarity of the lowland. Mulanje keeps its cloud and its distance, a mountain treated with respect and not made small, and it holds the knowing that even the warmest country keeps places where awe and a little fear are the proper feeling, and where a man climbs softly or not at all.
