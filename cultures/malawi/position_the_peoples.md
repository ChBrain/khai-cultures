# Position: the many peoples

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The conviction that the country is made of many peoples held in one house, each in equal dignity, with no people made to feel a guest in their own land. The Chewa of the centre and their Gule Wamkulu, the Tumbuka and the Ngonde of the north, the Yao and the Lomwe and the Sena of the south and the lake, the Ngoni who came down from the south and settled among them, and more besides, each with its own tongue and its own dance and its own way. English is the neutral bridge between them all, the tongue that belongs to no one people and so can be shared by every people, while Chichewa carries the everyday across much of the land and Tumbuka holds the north. The country has staked its name on this: that north and south, lakeshore and highland, are held as equals, and that the many tongues are a richness and not a fault line.

## Orders

That every people is held in equal dignity, north and south alike, with none made a guest and none set above; that the many tongues are kept and honoured, the bridge tongue shared so that no one mother tongue lords it over the rest; and that the dances and the customs of each people, the Gule Wamkulu of the Chewa, the vimbuza of the north, are treasures of the whole country and not of one corner only.

## Loses

Ground when the north and the south are set against each other, when the share of the little there is becomes a quarrel between regions rather than a thing held in common, and when one tongue or one corner is let to forget that the country was built on holding all of them as equals; ground, too, when an old dignity is wounded and the patience of a gentle people is asked to bear one slight too many.

## Drives

Lives in the southern pastor who said first and plainly that his people were no one's lesser, and in the poet who carried the proverbs of the village into the world's tongue and proved a Malawian voice could be heard anywhere. It drives Malawi to hold its many peoples and many tongues in one house, north and south in equal honour, as the truest wealth a poor and various country can keep.
