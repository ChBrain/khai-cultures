# Process: sharing the nsima

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

Any household at the hour of the meal, and any household that hears a visitor at the gate: the amayi at the pot turning the thick maize porridge with the wooden mthiko until it is smooth and firm, the relish, the ndiwo, set beside it, a little dried fish, beans, or greens, and the family and whoever has come gathering round the one plate. A bowl of water is brought to wash the right hand, and the eating begins, all hands to the same nsima, no one served apart.

## Direction

Toward belonging and the open hand: nsima is not just the food but the meaning of being fed, and to share it from one plate is to say there are no strangers here. The visitor who arrives at the meal does not need to be asked; a portion is already his, and to let a guest go unfed is the one true shame. It is the daily enactment of the Warm Heart, the warmth made a thing you can taste, the dignity of a people who will not eat alone with plenty.

## Lever

It is driven by the deep conviction that the plate is shared and the guest is fed, and by the staple itself, nsima, which a Malawian will say he has not truly eaten until he has eaten, however much else has passed his lips. It holds against the hungry season and the lean year, when the relish is thin and the nsima stretched; against it stands only the hardship that empties the pot, and even then the little there is is shared first with the one who has none.

## Echo

Sharing the nsima makes audible what the country lives by: that warmth is a thing you do daily and not only feel, that the open hand and the shared plate are the plain way of being a person among people. It is the Warm Heart of Africa served at every table, the welcome you can roll in your fingers and dip in the relish, three times a day.
