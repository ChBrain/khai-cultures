# Process: Jounen Kweyol

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

The whole island on the last Friday of October, in the Independence season: the schools and offices put aside the everyday and turn to Creole Day, everybody in the madras and the wob dwiyet, the traditional dress, the radio and the talk turning over wholesale to Kweyol, the bakwa and the cassava and the bush rum laid out for all who pass. It takes only a community willing to wear, speak, cook, and dance its own Creole for one good day, and Dominica does it together, yard to yard.

## Direction

Toward pride in the Creole self: the day the island lifts her Kweyol tongue, her food, her dress, and her music out of the everyday and holds them up high as the heart of who she is. It is the festival of the Creole identity that men like the cultural nationalists made the nation's pride, the yard's own way of life claimed open and worn with honor, the speaking of Kweyol turned from a thing of the kitchen into a thing to celebrate.

## Lever

It is driven by the long work of turning Creole from something the island was taught to be ashamed of into something she wears with pride, and by the need of a small nation to know and keep her own. It holds against the pull of the metropolitan and the modern, the English and the imported, that would thin the Kweyol away; and against it stands the risk of the day becoming costume alone, the living tongue worn once a year and let slip the rest of the time.

## Echo

Jounen Kweyol makes audible what [the Creole nation's voice](position_the_creole_nations_voice.md) holds: that the Kweyol tongue and the Creole way are not some lesser thing to hide but the very identity of the nation. In her madras and her Kweyol the island wears the pride that [E.O. LeBlanc](persona_eo_leblanc.md) made the work of the nation, the yard's own speech lifted up for all to claim. Sa ki ta'w se ta'w, what is yours is yours, and on this day everybody know it.
