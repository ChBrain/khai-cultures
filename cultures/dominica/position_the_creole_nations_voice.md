# Position: the Creole nation's voice

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The Kweyol-Catholic people the island made of itself, and the voice it found in letters and statecraft. Out of the meeting of French and African and Kalinago on the plantation rose a new people with a new tongue, Kweyol, the French-based Creole spoken in every yard, and a faith that gave the nation its motto and its calendar. That people learned to speak for itself: in the cultural nationalism that lifted Creole from shame into pride, in the stateswoman who carried the small island's word into the world, and in the novelist who took the island's light and loss into the literature of the age. The Creole nation's voice is Dominica claiming its own speech, its own faith, and its own seat at the table.

## Orders

That the Kweyol tongue and the Creole way are not a lesser thing to be hidden but the very identity of the nation, to be spoken with pride and kept alive; that a small island has the right to its own voice in its own affairs and in the world's, and need answer to no larger power for being itself; and that the dignity of the Creole nation rests on its language, its faith, and its leaders speaking plainly and for their own.

## Loses

Ground when the Creole is treated as backward beside the metropolitan French or English, the Kweyol pushed to the yard and out of the school and the office; and ground, too, when the small nation's voice is drowned by the larger powers around it, or when its own leaders speak for the office rather than for the people whose plain Kweyol first gave the nation its tongue.

## Drives

Lives in [Dame Eugenia Charles](persona_eugenia_charles.md), the first woman to lead a Caribbean nation, who gave the island a stateswoman's voice; in [Jean Rhys](persona_jean_rhys.md), who carried the island into the world's letters; and in [E.O. LeBlanc](persona_eo_leblanc.md), who made Creole pride the work of statecraft. It drives Dominica to speak as itself - in Kweyol, in faith, and in its own name - and to claim its place, however small, among the nations.
