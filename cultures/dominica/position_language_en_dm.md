# Position: Dominican English

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The authority of English as it is spoken and written on the island: the tongue of law and school and record, the language Dominica does her governing in, official in law though Kweyol is what lives in nearly every yard. It is not the Creole of the kitchen and the market, the French-based Kweyol the island made of herself, but the English of the assembly, the courtroom, the classroom and the page, the tongue in which the small nation writes her statutes, argues her case before the world, and sets down in ink what the yard says by mouth.

## Orders

What passes on the island for the language of governance and letters: the statute, the contract, the diploma and the law are said in it, and it is by it that the one the school has kept is measured. It orders the way into the office, the court and the exam, sorts to the side of learning the one who has it whole and to the side of the yard the one who has only the Creole, and makes well-held English, on an island whose truest speech is Kweyol, a key not every hand holds.

## Loses

Ground to the Kweyol of the yards and the markets, the French Creole the island actually speaks, that English never drove out of the home nor the song nor the Jounen Kweyol pride; ground, too, to the near-emptied Kalinago tongue that named these rivers before any flag, kept now only in the old names the land still answers to. It loses as well to the very people it governs, whenever the English of law and record stands above the line and the Creole that is the nation's own voice stays below it, honored in costume and kept out of the statute.

## Drives

It carries the island into governance and the world's letters. [E.O. LeBlanc](persona_eo_leblanc.md) has it whole as the tongue of the assembly and the law, and raised the Kweyol nation's pride from inside its English machinery; [Dame Eugenia Charles](persona_eugenia_charles.md) carried a small island's word into the rooms of the world's powers in it, the borrowed English of the empire that once ruled her; [Jean Rhys](persona_jean_rhys.md) made it into art, turning the metropole's own English back on itself from the margin; and [the Kalinago](persona_the_kalinago.md) carry it as the working tongue of the documents and the maps that drew the lines of their territory, holding their own first language only in fragments beneath it. So the island's official tongue shows at once what it opens to the nation and what it leaves below the line, between the English the world will hear and the Kweyol the island actually speaks.
