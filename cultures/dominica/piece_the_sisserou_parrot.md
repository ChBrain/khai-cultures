# Piece: the Sisserou parrot

## Taxonomy

Parent group: pieces

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Place

On the national flag, the coat of arms, and the heart of the island's high rainforest: the Sisserou, the imperial Amazon, a large purple-and-green parrot found in the wild nowhere on earth but Dominica, perched in green and purple at the center of the flag. The only national flag in the world to carry the colour purple, and it carries it for this bird, the rarest and grandest of the island's endemic life.

## Load Bearing

It ties to [the Nature Isle](position_the_nature_isle.md), the wild land and the rare life that is found nowhere else. The Sisserou is the island made into an emblem: a bird that exists only here, on the high slopes of the rainforest, raised to the flag as the sign of all the endemic green the Nature Isle keeps and the others lost. To put the parrot at the center of the flag is to say that the island's identity is its wild - that what makes Dominica itself is the rare life its forest shelters, and that to lose the bird would be to lose a piece of the nation. Without this parrot the Nature Isle would have no living face on its own flag.

## Apparent

A large parrot of deep purple breast and green back and wings, perched and rendered in the colours of the flag. In itself one rare and threatened bird among the forest canopy; its weight is in being found nowhere else on earth, and in standing on the flag for the whole wild inheritance the island stakes its name on.
