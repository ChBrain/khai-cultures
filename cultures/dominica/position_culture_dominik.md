# Position: Dominik

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The belonging to Dominik, the small, wild island her own people call by a name older than any flag flown over her: not merely "Dominica" of the maps and the passport, but the green, steep, unconquered ground itself. She carries [the Nature Isle](position_the_nature_isle.md), the rainforest and the three hundred and sixty-five rivers that gave the nation her body and her name; she carries [Neg Mawon](position_neg_mawon.md), the dignity of the Maroon who chose the hard freedom of the bush over the soft chains of the estate; and she carries [the Creole nation's voice](position_the_creole_nations_voice.md), the Kweyol tongue and Catholic faith that a scattered people forged into one on the plantation. She carries the Kalinago's endurance as her oldest root, the last Carib ground left anywhere in the sea that once bore their name, and the motto that holds all of it together: Apres Bondie c'est la te, after God, the Earth.

## Orders

That the land be kept as a trust and not a thing used up, that Kweyol be spoken with pride in the yard and not hidden from the school or the office, and that freedom, once kept by the Maroon in the bush, never be handed back or taken for granted. It orders that a small people speak for themselves in their own tongue and their own name before any larger power, and that every storm be answered the same way: bend, but pa kase, do not break.

## Loses

Ground where the green is traded for short money and the rainforest sold hard to the visitor while the people who live in the wild go unspoken for; where the unbowed neck of Neg Mawon, cut loose from its old purpose, hardens into a bare mistrust of every common effort; and where the Kweyol tongue that is the nation's truest voice stays below the line of law and record even as her own leaders speak the empire's English in the world's rooms. It loses ground, too, in the pull that has always carried her gifted away to colder, richer places, so the island's voice is sometimes heard everywhere but at home.

## Drives

Drives Dominik to rebuild stronger after every storm rather than be emptied by it, setting herself the boldest charge in the region: to become the world's first climate-resilient nation. It drives her people to hold the wild against the market and the wind, to claim their Kweyol and their faith as pride rather than shame, and to stand small and unbowed among far larger powers, keeping their freedom and their ground as something fought for, not granted, apres Bondie c'est la te.
