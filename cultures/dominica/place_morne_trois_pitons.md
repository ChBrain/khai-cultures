# Place: Morne Trois Pitons

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Shown

The rainforest national park at the island's volcanic heart, named for the three-pointed peak that rises above it, a UNESCO World Heritage Site of dense green mountain forest, hot springs, and waterfalls. Within it lie the Valley of Desolation, a smoking floor of fumaroles and sulphur where the green gives way to bare rock, and beyond it the Boiling Lake, a flooded fumarole of grey, churning water, the second largest of its kind on earth. The rivers run off the slopes in their hundreds, and the wild closes over every trail.

## Holds

- [the Last Refuge](plot_01_the_last_refuge.md): the steep, wet rainforest interior that made the island the refuge of the Kalinago and the place the empires could not wholly reach.
- [the Nature Isle and the Storm](plot_04_the_nature_isle_and_the_storm.md): the heart of the eco-tourism the nation stakes its future on, stripped bare by Hurricane Maria and regrowing.

## Offers

A place where the island shows itself what it is - the wild made visible, the volcano breathing in the Boiling Lake, the rainforest standing where its neighbors cut theirs down; the draw that brings the world to the Nature Isle, and the green larder and watershed that feed and water the island's life.

## Withheld

The ease of the wild it displays. Morne Trois Pitons carries the cost beneath the beauty - the boiling ground and the smoking valley that remind the island it lives on a volcano, the steep forest that gives little to the farmer, and the green that the hurricane can strip to grey rock in a single night, so that the Nature Isle's heart is its glory and its hazard at once.
