# Place: Morne Trois Pitons

## Shown

The rainforest national park at the island's volcanic heart, Morne Twa Piton, named for the three-pointed peak that rises above it, a UNESCO World Heritage Site of dense green mountain forest, hot springs, and waterfalls. Within it lie the Valley of Desolation, a smoking floor of fumaroles and sulphur where the green gives way to bare rock, and beyond it the Boiling Lake, a flooded fumarole of grey, churning water, the second largest of its kind on God's earth. The rivers run off the slopes in their hundreds, and the wild closes over every trail before you can blink.

## Holds

## Offers

A place where the island shows herself what she is - the wild made plain to the eye, the volcano breathing in the Boiling Lake, the rainforest standing tall where the neighbors cut theirs down long ago; the draw that brings the world to the Nature Isle, and the green larder and watershed that feed and water the island's life.

## Withheld

The ease of the wild it displays. Morne Trois Pitons carries the cost beneath the beauty - the boiling ground and the smoking valley that remind the island she lives on a volcano, the steep forest that gives little to the farmer, and the green the hurricane can strip to grey rock in a single night, so the Nature Isle's heart is her glory and her hazard all in one.
