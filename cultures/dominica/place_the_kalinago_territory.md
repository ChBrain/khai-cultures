# Place: the Kalinago Territory

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Shown

The stretch of rugged east-coast land set aside as the homeland of the surviving Kalinago, the only Carib territory left anywhere in the whole Caribbean. Eight hamlets along a wild Atlantic shore of black rock and heavy surf, with the steep forest rising behind, the gardens and the larouma stands among the trees, and the karbet, the traditional meeting house, looking out over the water. Here the larouma basket is still woven, the old names of the rivers and plants are still spoken soft, and the island's first people keep their own ground the way they always done.

## Holds

- [Neg Mawon](plot_02_neg_mawon.md): the east-coast homeland whose surviving first people shared the island as the new Creole nation took shape out of African, French, and Kalinago.
- [the Nature Isle and the Storm](plot_04_the_nature_isle_and_the_storm.md): the homeland of the island's first people, struck by Hurricane Maria like all the island and rebuilding with it.

## Offers

A place where the island keeps her oldest self - the living homeland of the Kalinago, where a people the rest of the Caribbean lost still hold their territory, their craft, and the surviving fragments of their tongue; the proof that the Nature Isle kept what the cane fields could not, and the heart of the nation's inheritance from before any flag flew here.

## Withheld

The comfort of a past safely kept. The Kalinago Territory carries the same hard present as every poor yard on the island - the thin, steep land, the wild and dangerous Atlantic shore, the storms that beat it like all the rest, and the drift of the young away - so it is no museum but a living place facing tomorrow, its dignity easy to admire from outside and harder to carry from within.
