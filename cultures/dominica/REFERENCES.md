# Dominica: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md) is written from a Caribbean cultural profile
on Geert Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries
no number. Dominica is not individually published in Hofstede's country data; the
figures below are the regional West Africa / Caribbean approximations commonly used
as the nearest published proxy, recorded here so the data is on the record without
entering the prose. They are indicative only.

| Dimension                     | Caribbean proxy score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 77                              |
| Individualism                 | 20                              |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 46                              |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 54                              |
| Long Term Orientation         | 16                              |
| Indulgence                    | 78                              |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the six-dimensions model of national
culture and the Country Comparison tool). Scores are approximate proxies, as
published by Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group for the West African and
Caribbean region rather than for Dominica itself, which is not separately scored;
they are used here only as the nearest available indication.

## Historical sources

from the public historical record. They are credited here and never claimed; the
staging is original work. The harder chapters - slavery, the Maroon wars and their
suppression, and the long survival of the Kalinago - are staged soberly on the
public record, without atrocity detail, with the surviving Kalinago presented with
dignity as a living people and the nation seen from within its own
self-understanding.

The one defining archetype, [the Kalinago](persona_the_kalinago.md), is not a
single historical individual but a dignified composite of the surviving Kalinago
people of the Kalinago Territory: the island's first people, the last Caribs to
hold their own ground, carrying the craft, the inheritance, and the surviving
fragments of a tongue an empire all but silenced. Kweyol, the French-based Creole
woven through the English prose, is the living speech of the island, set here in
its everyday cultural reality - the yard, the festival, the faith, and the motto -
and never as caricature.
