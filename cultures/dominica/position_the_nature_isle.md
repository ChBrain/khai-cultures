# Position: the Nature Isle

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The land herself as the island's first identity: the wild volcanic rainforest, the steep green mountains, the three hundred and sixty-five rivers, one for every day, the boiling fumaroles of the Valley of Desolation and the Boiling Lake, the second largest of its kind on God's earth. Where her neighbors flattened their forests for cane and sugar, Dominica stayed too rugged to be wholly tamed, and so she kept the green the others lost. The Nature Isle is the island's pride, her draw, her larder, and her trust - the body that gives the nation her name, her rivers, her motto, and her very breath.

## Orders

That the land is a gift held in trust, not a thing to be used up, so her rainforest is to be guarded and not cleared; that Dominica's wealth and worth are measured in green - in forest, river, and the rare life found nowhere else - rather than in the cane and concrete of richer islands; and that to be the wild one, the unconquered one, the last that kept her forest, is the island's honor and her calling. Apres Bondie c'est la te, and the te is held in trust.

## Loses

Ground when the green is taken for granted or traded away - cleared for short money, fouled for a quick crop, or simply left undefended before the next storm comes; and ground, too, when the beauty is sold so hard to the visitor that the people who live in the wild are forgotten, the Nature Isle made a postcard while the hardship of living on the steep, wet, storm-struck slope goes unspoken.

## Drives

Lives in [the Kalinago](persona_the_kalinago.md), the people who knew the forest before any empire and who keep the last of it as their own. It drives Dominica to hold the wild against the market and the wind, to set herself the charge of becoming the world's first climate-resilient nation, and to keep faith with the oldest words she has - that apres Bondie c'est la te, after God comes the Earth.
