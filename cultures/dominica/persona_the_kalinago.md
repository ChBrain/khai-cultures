# Persona: the Kalinago

## Taxonomy

[the Nature Isle](position_the_nature_isle.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The surviving Kalinago of the Kalinago Territory on the rugged east coast, the people Columbus called Carib, the last of their kind anywhere in the whole Caribbean to hold their own ground. Where their kin were lost on the other islands, the steep rainforest of this one sheltered them, and they remain - weaving the larouma basket, fishing the wild Atlantic shore where the surf comes in heavy, keeping a name and an inheritance the rest of the region lost. Rooted, dignified, and patient, they are the island's first people and her living memory, the proof that the wild kept what the cane fields could not. The Kalinago tongue they hold as the deep inheritance: an elder still [speaks the fragments of the mother tongue](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) that survive, and [hears them carried](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) in the old names of the rivers and the plants, the words the land itself still answers to. English, the tongue of law and school, they [carry as the working tongue](process_speaking_carried.md) and [read and follow](process_reading_followed.md) in the documents and the maps that drew the lines of their territory, and Kweyol they [follow on the ear](process_hearing_followed.md) from the yards beyond their own. They keep the last of a tongue that once named this whole sea, most of it gone now with the people who spoke it, so that the gap at the limit of the tongue is, for them, the whole near-emptied space of a language an empire all but silenced, where the old words survive only in the names of the wild that outlived the speakers.

## Action

In [the Last Refuge](plot_01_the_last_refuge.md) they are the island's first people, holding their forest forts while the empires fight over the coast, and surviving where their kin did not, so that Dominica becomes the last refuge of the Carib world. They carry [the Nature Isle](position_the_nature_isle.md) as its oldest keepers, the people who knew the rainforest before any flag was planted on it, and who hold the only Kalinago Territory left in the Caribbean.

## Shadow

The dignity of survival can be mistaken for a thing already finished and safe, the Kalinago made a postcard of the past while the living people face the same hard slope, the same thin land, and the same drift away as every poor yard on the island - kept as a memory and not met as a present, their territory admired and their future left to them alone.

## Tell

They weave the larouma basket the way it was woven before any ship came, fingers keeping a knowledge older than the nation, and call the rivers by names from a tongue the rivers still answer to though the people who made it are nearly gone. The land remembers, even where the speakers do not.
