# Plan: Apres Bondie C'est La Te

## Taxonomy

The open mandate the nation set herself in her motto, the Kweyol words right there on her coat of arms: Apres Bondie C'est La Te, after God, the Earth. It names the charge the island lives by - to guard [the Nature Isle](position_the_nature_isle.md), the rainforest and the three hundred and sixty-five rivers that are the nation's body and trust; to keep the dignity of the unowned that [Neg Mawon](position_neg_mawon.md) carries; and to speak and live as herself in [the Creole nation's voice](position_the_creole_nations_voice.md). After God, nothing stands above the Earth - so the land is the first duty, and the keeping of her the whole work.

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Direction

Protect the rainforest and the rivers as the trust the motto names, guarding the wild against the market and the clearing; keep the only surviving Kalinago people rooted on their own ground and the homeland a living place, not a museum; rebuild stronger after each storm rather than be emptied by it, and carry the bold charge of becoming the world's first climate-resilient nation through to fact; and keep the Kweyol nation speaking, singing, and governing as herself, holding her small voice strong among far larger powers.

## Orders

- To [the Kalinago](persona_the_kalinago.md): hold the last refuge and the oldest knowledge, keep the territory a living homeland, and carry the surviving tongue and craft forward where the rest of the Caribbean done lost theirs.
- To the leader like [E.O. LeBlanc](persona_eo_leblanc.md): keep the Kweyol nation proud and speaking for herself, and let the work of statecraft serve the steep land and the people on it, not the office.
- To the nation: guard the Earth that comes after God, rebuild stronger after every storm, and keep faith with the wild and the Creole self that make the island herself.

## Implementation

A small, poor, sovereign nation that has already weathered empire and storm and set herself the boldest environmental charge in the region: to be the world's first climate-resilient country. She has kept her rainforest where her neighbors cleared theirs, held the last Kalinago Territory in the whole Caribbean, lifted Kweyol from shame into pride, and after Hurricane Maria struck the green island bare she began the rebuild and the green again - with the work far from done, the slopes still steep, the young still leaving, and the sky still warming. Piti piti, the bird builds the nest.
