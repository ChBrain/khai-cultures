# Process: Mas Domnik

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

The island in the days before Lent, when Mas Domnik, "the real mas," takes the streets: the bands and the revellers come out behind the music, the sensay masquerade shaggy in its layered fibre and horned mask, the sleeping johnnies and the old characters, and the jing ping band with its boom-boom, the accordion, the shak-shak, and the drum driving the lapo kabwit beat hard. It takes only the town and the crowd and the old costumes, and the whole island pours down into the road.

## Direction

Toward release and belonging: the carnival that lets the island be wholly herself in the road, the African drum and the Creole masquerade and the homemade costume carrying a freedom the imported, polished carnivals of other islands done lost long ago. Mas Domnik prides herself on being the real mas, the people's own and not the show's, the old characters and the jing ping kept alive, the whole community in the street as one body.

## Lever

It is driven by the deep need of a Creole people to come out, to mask, to drum, and to be free in the road together, and by the pride of keeping the old, raw, homemade carnival when others done turn theirs into a spectacle. It holds against the year's hardship and the pull toward the slick and the bought; and against it stands the same drift, the imported costume and the recorded music edging out the sensay and the living jing ping band.

## Echo

Mas Domnik makes visible what [Neg Mawon](position_neg_mawon.md) carries: the spirit of the free in the road, the masked and the drumming claiming a freedom no master gives. Behind the sensay and the jing ping the island dances the unbowed dignity that [Chief Jacko](persona_chief_jacko.md) held in the forest, the free of the people loose in the street for one good day, and not a soul to say them no.
