# Persona: Chief Jacko

## Taxonomy

[Neg Mawon](position_neg_mawon.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

Jacko, the Maroon chief who made the forested heights his fortress and led the longest resistance the island ever knew, holding out in the bush against the British for years, honored today as a national hero. Liberte oben lanmo, freedom or death, and he chose the hard freedom of the mountains over the chains of the estate, gathered the free of the forest around him, and held a ground that the steep rainforest herself defended. Resolute, unbowed, and rooted in the wild like the gwo bwa, the big trees, he is the spirit of Neg Mawon made one man. The tongue he was born to never reached the page: an African mother tongue carried across the water, he [speaks it](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks in it](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) among his own, and [hears it carried](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) in the speech and the drum of the forest camps. The new Creole rising on the island he [follows by ear](process_hearing_followed.md) and [borrows where he must](process_speaking_borrowed.md), the half-shared tongue of the many peoples thrown together. [English](position_language_en_dm.md) was the language of the men he fought, and he held the forest against it; English was never his, no master's tongue ever set in his mouth, and so the gap at the limit of the tongue is the one he refused to cross at all - the freedom of the bush kept in a speech the empire could not own, while the African words he carried went unwritten and were lost to the page even as the man was kept by memory. He belongs to [Dominik](position_culture_dominik.md) as the man who proved the wild island could shelter the free the same as she sheltered her first people.

## Action

In [Neg Mawon](plot_02_neg_mawon.md) he leads the Maroons of the free forest in the island's longest war of resistance, holding his mountain ground against the soldiers until the day comes when the chains are struck from the whole island. He carries [Neg Mawon](position_neg_mawon.md) as its first and fiercest keeper, the chief who proved the wild island could shelter the free same as she sheltered her first people, and who gave the nation its root in the refusal to be owned.

## Shadow

The hero of the unowned can be flattened into a statue and a road sign, the long hunger and the harder cost of his freedom smoothed into a tidy pride, and the spirit of refusal he carried turned, cut loose from its purpose, into a bare resistance to every common order - the unbowed neck honored in stone and forgotten in the living.

## Tell

He holds a mountain against an empire with little more than the bush and the will not to be a slave, and he does it in a tongue the empire could neither read nor command - free in the forest, in his own words, to the last. Mon ka kembe, the mountain holds.
