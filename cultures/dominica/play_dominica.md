# Play: Dominica

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the house that holds this staging and answers for it.

## Name

The Commonwealth of Dominica, the "Nature Isle of the Caribbean," Dominik to her own, a small volcanic island in the Lesser Antilles, set between the two French departements of Guadeloupe to the north and Martinique to the south. ISO DM, language en in law, but ask anybody Sa ka fet and the answer comes back in Kweyol, the French-based Creole that lives in nearly every yard. A rugged green island of rainforest and rivers, three hundred and sixty-five of them, one for every day, and the last place in the whole Caribbean where the Kalinago, the people Columbus called Carib, still hold their own ground.

## Arc

An island so wild and so steep that the empires could not easily take it: the mountains stood, the rainforest closed over the trails, and the Kalinago held out in their forts of forest while France and Britain fought over the coast for a century and more. It is the last refuge of the Kalinago, who keep the only Carib territory left anywhere in the Caribbean, and the land of the Neg Mawon, the Maroons of the free forest who waged the longest resistance the island ever saw under chiefs like Jacko. The volcano shows itself in the Boiling Lake and the smoking valley of Morne Trois Pitons; the rainforest pours down in three hundred and sixty-five rivers, one for every day, the sisserou parrot calling from the high green where it lives nowhere else on God's earth. Out of slavery and the plantation rose a Kweyol-Catholic and African people, who made a French Creole tongue, a music of bele and jing ping and the lapo kabwit drum, and a faith that gave the nation its motto: Apres Bondie C'est La Te, after God, the Earth. It won independence in 1978, sent Dame Eugenia Charles to be the first woman to lead a Caribbean nation, grew its bananas on the steep green slopes, and sang its Kweyol back into pride through men like E.O. LeBlanc and the World Creole Music Festival. Then in 2017 Hurricane Maria tore the green island bare in a single night, and the nation set itself the boldest charge in the region: to rebuild as the world's first climate-resilient country. Under it all runs one nerve: a small, poor, proud people who hold the wild, and mean to keep it, and themselves, against the storm. Bend, but pa kase, do not break.

## Triggers

**[Plot 1: the Last Refuge](plot_01_the_last_refuge.md)**

On an island too steep and too forested to conquer easy, [the Kalinago](persona_the_kalinago.md) hold their ground while France and Britain fight over the coast for a century, and [the Nature Isle](position_the_nature_isle.md) herself, the rainforest of [Morne Trois Pitons](place_morne_trois_pitons.md), becomes the last refuge of the only Carib people left in the whole Caribbean.

**[Plot 2: Neg Mawon](plot_02_neg_mawon.md)**

Out of the plantation rise the Neg Mawon, the Maroons of the free forest, and under [Chief Jacko](persona_chief_jacko.md) they wage [Neg Mawon](position_neg_mawon.md), the longest forest resistance the island ever knew, the bush itself their fort, until emancipation comes and the free of the forest pass into the free of the nation.

**[Plot 3: the Creole Nation](plot_03_the_creole_nation.md)**

The island lifts its Kweyol-Catholic identity into a nation: [E.O. LeBlanc](persona_eo_leblanc.md) makes Creole pride the work of statecraft, taking the yard's own tongue from shame to honor, [Dame Eugenia Charles](persona_eugenia_charles.md) leads it to and through independence in 1978, and [Jean Rhys](persona_jean_rhys.md) carries the island's voice into the world's letters, all giving body to [the Creole nation's voice](position_the_creole_nations_voice.md).

**[Plot 4: the Nature Isle and the Storm](plot_04_the_nature_isle_and_the_storm.md)**

Modern Dominica stakes its future on [the Nature Isle](position_the_nature_isle.md), the eco-tourism of [Morne Trois Pitons](place_morne_trois_pitons.md) and the homeland of [the Kalinago Territory](place_the_kalinago_territory.md), until Hurricane Maria strips the green island bare in 2017 and the nation, refusing to lie down, sets itself to rebuild as the world's first climate-resilient country.

## Stakes

Whether the wild Nature Isle can hold what makes her herself - protect the rainforest and the three hundred and sixty-five rivers that are her body and her name, keep the only surviving Kalinago people rooted on their own ground, rebuild stronger after each storm rather than be emptied by it, and carry her small, poor, proud Creole nation and her Kweyol tongue forward into a world that scarcely knows the island is there - or whether the storms, the leaving, and the long odds of a tiny state finally wear the Nature Isle down. Apres Bondie c'est la te, and the te, the land, is the whole wager.
