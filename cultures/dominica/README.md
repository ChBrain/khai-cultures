# Dominica: a khai culture

Dominica, the Nature Isle of the Caribbean, staged as a full khai play, ISO DM,
language en (with Kweyol in nearly every yard), as a theatre: its history as the
plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its language and faith encode, its
places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.
This is the Commonwealth of Dominica, the volcanic rainforest island between
Guadeloupe and Martinique, and not the Dominican Republic.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_dominica.md](play_dominica.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Dominica's profile (a warm, devout, family- and community-rooted people, patient
  and resilient, plainspoken and proud of their wild island) and naming no
  dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Last Refuge (the Kalinago and the contested
  France-Britain isle), Neg Mawon (the Maroon resistance under Jacko, slavery and
  emancipation), the Creole Nation (independence in 1978, LeBlanc, Eugenia Charles,
  Jean Rhys), and the Nature Isle and the Storm (eco-tourism, Hurricane Maria, and
  the goal of becoming the world's first climate-resilient nation) - each casting
  the elements it needs, and each staging slavery, the Maroon wars, and the
  surviving Kalinago soberly and with dignity.
- **Personas:** Chief Jacko (the Maroon hero of the forest resistance), Dame
  Eugenia Charles (the Caribbean's first woman Prime Minister), Jean Rhys (the
  novelist of "Wide Sargasso Sea"), and E.O. LeBlanc (the cultural nationalist
  premier) as the historic cast, plus one defining archetype, the Kalinago (the
  surviving first people of the Kalinago Territory). Each is wired to the language
  engine by the language-crossing it holds, between English, Kweyol, and the older
  tongues.
- **Positions:** the Nature Isle (the wild volcanic rainforest and the land
  itself), Neg Mawon (the Maroon resistance and the dignity of the unowned), and
  the Creole nation's voice (the Kweyol-Catholic nation speaking for itself in
  letters and statecraft).
- **Places:** Morne Trois Pitons (the rainforest national park of the Boiling
  Lake, UNESCO) and the Kalinago Territory (the east-coast homeland of the
  surviving Kalinago).
- **Processes:** Jounen Kweyol (Creole Day of language, food, and dress in the
  Independence season) and Mas Domnik (Dominica's "real mas," the carnival of
  sensay and jing ping).
- **Pieces:** the larouma basket (the Kalinago waterproof basketry) and the
  Sisserou parrot (the endemic parrot on the national flag).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; slavery, the Maroon wars, and the survival of the Kalinago are treated
soberly on the public record and with dignity; the staging and the architecture
are original work.
