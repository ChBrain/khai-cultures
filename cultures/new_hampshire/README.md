# New Hampshire: a khai culture

New Hampshire staged as a full khai play, ISO US-NH, language en. The Granite State: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its first presidential primary, Mount Washington cog railway, and maple syrup harvest boom encodes, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house that holds this culture and answers for it.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_new_hampshire.md](play_new_hampshire.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_new_hampshire.md](pitch_new_hampshire.md), the New Hampshire key.
- **Plots (history):** the First presidential Primary of 1916, the Mount Washington Cog Railway Opening of 1869, and the Maple Syrup Harvest Boom of the 1890s.
- **Personas:** Robert (cog railway engineer) and Alice (maple syrup producer), written in English.
- **Positions:** granite independence (primary elections/town meeting debate/local liberties), alpine precision (mountain engineering/railway safety/maple harvest craftsmanship).
- **Places:** Mount Washington Cog, Sugarhouse.
- **Processes:** maple sap boiling (traditional wood-fired evaporation), granite quarrying (stone cutting and splitting).
- **Pieces:** a maple bucket, a railway cog.
- **Plan:** the White Mountain forest preservation plan.
