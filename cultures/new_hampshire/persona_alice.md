# Persona: Alice

## Taxonomy

[granite independence](position_granite_independence.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A maple producer in Plymouth, fifty-two and no stranger to a slow boil. English, in a warm drawl, is her mother tongue, the language she [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and writes her daily production logs in. It is the [New Hampshire English](position_language_en_us_nh.md) of the Plymouth sugarhouse, the warm Yankee drawl of the town meeting. She speaks no foreign languages. Where her patience thins is with the railway crowd: the fast, technology-oriented talk of engineers like Robert and the digital demands of tourism boards, she [hears only as distant noise](process_hearing_followed.md), sensing that the patient, law-protecting rhythm of municipal moderator debates counts for less next to corporate railroads and resort entertainment. She carries [Live Free or Die](position_culture_live_free_or_die.md) in her own key - defending the town meeting's plain right to answer for itself, batch by graded batch.

## Action

She preserves [maple bucket](piece_maple_bucket.md) records, coordinates field conservation under [the White Mountain plan](plan_white_mountain_forest_preservation.md), and studies [alpine precision](position_alpine_precision.md).

## Shadow

Her pride in traditional town meetings and local property rights can make her suspicious of state-wide zoning and environmental agricultural mandates, fearing they will restrict family property use.

## Tell

She looks at a historic town moderation ledger and says thoughtfully: "The town meeting has its own precedents; you can't protect community stability if you're only focused on digital speed and tourism profit metrics."
