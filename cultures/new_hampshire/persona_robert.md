# Persona: Robert

## Taxonomy

[alpine precision](position_alpine_precision.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A railway engineer in Fayetteville, thirty-eight and inclined to check a gauge twice before he trusts it once. English, in a New England cadence, is his mother tongue, the language he [speaks, hears, and thinks in](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), the code his daily mechanical checks run on. It is the [New Hampshire English](position_language_en_us_nh.md) of the Mount Washington cog, the clipped cadence of the mountain railway. He speaks no foreign languages. Where it thins is with the maple producers: the slower, tradition-bound talk of Alice and the quiet history of [granite independence](position_granite_independence.md), he [reads only as routine town updates](process_reading_followed.md) on his workstation, sensing that the patient, self-reliant voting rhythm of early town moderator meetings counts for less against the pull of alpine tourism. He carries [Live Free or Die](position_culture_live_free_or_die.md) in his own key - holding a mountain railway to the same exacting tolerance the town meeting holds its own debates.

## Action

He preserves [railway cog](piece_railway_cog.md) gears, coordinates track safety programs under [the White Mountain plan](plan_white_mountain_forest_preservation.md), and studies [alpine precision](position_alpine_precision.md).

## Shadow

His intense focus on mechanical performance metrics and passenger transport can make him indifferent to municipal environmental zoning problems in rural agricultural counties.

## Tell

He adjusts a safety valve on a steam locomotive and says directly: "We are calibrating high-pressure railway cogs; you can't run a safe mountain railroad if you're only focused on historic local preservation."
