# Bolivia: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_es.md](pitch_es.md) is written from Bolivia's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no
number. The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the
record without entering the prose.

staging is original work. The hardest passages of the story - the silver of
Potosi and the mita, the loss of the coast in the War of the Pacific, and the
plurinational turn - are staged soberly on the public record, without atrocity
detail and without taking sides, as the gap between promise and reality.

The defining persona [la chola pacena](persona_la_chola_pacena.md) is an
archetype, not a historical individual: a composite that carries the culture's
living tension - the Aymara urban woman of La Paz and El Alto, in pollera and
bowler hat, who passed from an excluded figure to an icon of Bolivian identity,
the indigenous majority made urban and visible.

The cultural framing - the coca leaf as sacred and contested, the charango, the
Oruro Carnival, the ch'alla and the devotion to the Pachamama alongside the
saints - is drawn from the public ethnographic and cultural record and staged
with respect; the staging is original work.
