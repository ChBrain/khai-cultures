# Bolivia: a khai culture

Bolivia staged as a full khai play, ISO BO, language es. The most indigenous
nation of the Americas, plurinational and high in the Andes, one and many at
once. Its history is the plot line, its people the cast, the roles its languages
and faith encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned
by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_bolivia.md](play_bolivia.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_es.md](pitch_es.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Bolivia's profile (reserved, communal, present at altitude, devout to the
  Pachamama and the saints alike, resilient) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** Tiwanaku and the Andean world (the pre-Columbian Aymara
  and Quechua heartland, Tiwanaku and the Inca), the Cerro Rico (the conquest,
  Potosi's silver and the mita, and the great rebellions of Katari and Sisa),
  independence and the Republic (Juana Azurduy, 1825, and the loss of the sea in
  the War of the Pacific), and plurinational Bolivia (tin and the 1952
  revolution, the miners, and the plurinational state) - each casting the
  elements it needs and each staging the gap between promise and reality soberly.
- **Personas:** Tupac Katari, Bartolina Sisa, Juana Azurduy, and Domitila Barrios
  as the historic cast, plus one defining archetype, la chola pacena (the Aymara
  urban woman of La Paz in pollera and bowler hat, an icon of Bolivian identity).
  Each is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds, between
  Aymara or Quechua and the Spanish carried over them.
- **Positions:** lo indigena (the indigenous majority, Aymara and Quechua, and
  the Pachamama), la veta y la mita (the mine and forced labor, Potosi and the
  miners), el mar perdido (the landlocked wound and the claim to the sea).
- **Places:** Potosi (the Cerro Rico, the silver mountain that minted an empire),
  La Paz (the highest seat of government, with El Alto the great Aymara metropolis
  above it).
- **Processes:** el Carnaval de Oruro (the syncretic Diablada pilgrimage-carnival),
  la ch'alla (the libation and offering to the Pachamama).
- **Pieces:** la hoja de coca (the sacred and contested Andean coca leaf), el
  charango (the small Andean stringed instrument).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the legacy of Potosi and the mita, the loss of the sea, and the
plurinational turn are treated soberly on the public record and without taking
sides; the staging and the architecture are original work.
