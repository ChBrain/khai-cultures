# Persona: Ramon

## Taxonomy

[southwest frontier](position_southwest_frontier.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A forty-two-year-old copper geologist in Bisbee. [Arizonan English](position_language_en_us_az.md), carried in a direct Western cadence, is his mother tongue, the language he [speaks, hears, and thinks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), the working code of his safety briefings. He speaks no foreign languages. Here is where it thins out for him: the slower, tradition-bound Navajo talk of weavers like Yazhi and the quiet water histories of [desert resilience](position_desert_resilience.md) [reach him only as hydrological parameters](process_reading_followed.md) on his workstation, and he senses the patient, resource-conserving rhythm of the desert getting lost in the mining industry's own hurry. He belongs to [Arizona culture](position_culture_arizona_culture.md) through the mining and engineering half of its character, the ambition that reads the rock for what it can yield.

## Action

He preserves [copper ore](piece_copper_ore.md) samples, coordinates safety programs under [the groundwater plan](plan_groundwater_management.md), and studies [southwest frontier](position_southwest_frontier.md).

## Shadow

His focus on copper mining metrics and geological development can make him indifferent to the municipal water conservation demands of growing desert cities.

## Tell

He looks at a core sample and says directly: "We are mapping the mineral foundations of modern technology; you can't run industrial infrastructure if you're only focused on historic surface preservation."
