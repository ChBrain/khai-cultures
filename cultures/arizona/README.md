# Arizona: a khai culture

Arizona staged as a full khai play, ISO US-AZ, language en. The Grand Canyon State: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its park protections, water allocations, and native military code contributions encodes, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house that holds this culture and answers for it.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_arizona.md](play_arizona.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_arizona.md](pitch_arizona.md), the Arizona key.
- **Plots (history):** the Grand Canyon National Park Act of 1919, the Central Arizona Project authorization in 1968, and the Navajo code talkers contribution in 1942.
- **Personas:** Ramon (Bisbee mine geologist) and Yazhi (Navajo weaver and guide), written in English.
- **Positions:** desert resilience (conservation/native wisdom), southwest frontier (mining/self-reliance).
- **Places:** Grand Canyon, Bisbee Mine.
- **Processes:** copper smelting (mineral extraction), navajo weaving (traditional textile craft).
- **Pieces:** copper ore, a navajo rug.
- **Plan:** the groundwater management plan.
