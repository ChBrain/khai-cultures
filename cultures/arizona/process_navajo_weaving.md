# Process: navajo weaving

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

Navajo traditional weavers and family apprentices, conducted year-round in reservation homes and community centers.

## Direction

At the vertical loom. Cleaning and carding raw sheep wool, hand-spinning the fibers with spindle whorls, dyeing yarn with native plants, and hand-interlacing the warp and weft to build geometric designs.

## Lever

The ancestral craft process that preserves Navajo cosmological stories and cultural identity. It supports local weavers and family micro-trade, though it is limited by manual production times and reduced sheep grazing lands.

## Echo

The weaving patterns record historic narratives. The process defines the daily work of [Yazhi](persona_yazhi.md) in Monument Valley, while geologist [Ramon](persona_ramon.md) respects the method as an early model of structured complexity.
