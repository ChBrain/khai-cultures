# Djibouti: a khai culture

Djibouti staged as a full khai play, ISO DJ, languages fr and ar (with Somali and
Afar as the tongues of the home), as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its
people as the cast, the roles its languages and faith encode, its places, its
practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_djibouti.md](play_djibouti.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_fr.md](pitch_fr.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Djibouti's profile (a collective, clan- and lineage-minded society, respect for
  age and authority, a patient relationship to time, and the endurance and
  hospitality of a nomadic, pastoral world) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the open sea and Islam, the two peoples and the sultanates,
  the port and the railway, and the country of the two peoples - each casting the
  elements it needs, and each staging the meeting of the peoples with equal
  dignity.
- **Personas:** Abdourahman Waberi (the writer who gives the country its
  conscience), William J. F. Syad (the pioneer poet of the desert and the nomad),
  Aidarous (the voice of the cosmopolitan city), and the Sultan of Tadjoura (the
  master of the old white city of the gulf) as the named cast, plus one archetype,
  the salt caravanner of Lake Assal. Each is wired to the language engine by the
  language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** the meeting of the two peoples (the Issa, of the Somali branch,
  and the Afar, held in equal dignity, with the Arab heritage that came by sea),
  the crossroads (a small country whose greatness rests on its threshold at the
  mouth of the Red Sea), pastoral nomadism (the patience, hospitality, and poetry
  of the herding life).
- **Places:** Djibouti City (the port and the cosmopolitan capital of the two
  peoples), Lake Assal (the great salt pan at the lowest point in Africa).
- **Processes:** the khat sitting (the shared afternoon ritual on the mat), the
  tea (the rite of hospitality and the open door).
- **Pieces:** the dhow (the lateen-sailed boat that made this coast a crossroads
  of the two shores), the khat (the green branch that sets the social clock of the
  Djiboutian afternoon).

## Provenance
