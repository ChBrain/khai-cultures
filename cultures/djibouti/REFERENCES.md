# Djibouti: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical and cultural
sources behind the plots, personas, places, pieces, and processes.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_fr.md](pitch_fr.md) is written from Djibouti's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
Djibouti is not individually published in the standard Hofstede dataset; the
profile used here is read from the regional pattern of the Horn of Africa and the
Arab world (collective, high power distance, restrained), to which Djibouti
belongs. The values are recorded here as an approximate, regionally read profile so
the data is on the record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Djibouti profile (approx., regionally read) |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | high                                        |
| Individualism                 | low (strongly collective)                   |
| Motivation toward Achievement | moderate                                    |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | moderate to high                            |
| Long Term Orientation         | low to moderate                             |
| Indulgence                    | low (restrained)                            |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the six-dimensions model of national
culture and the Country Comparison tool). As Djibouti is not separately published,
the profile is read from the Horn of Africa and Arab regional pattern and is
approximate; no per-country score is claimed for Djibouti.

dignity, taking no political side and dwelling on no conflict.

The one archetypal persona, [le caravanier du sel](persona_caravanier.md), is not a
historical individual but a composite: the salt caravanner who has descended to
Lake Assal for centuries, carrying the endurance, the poetry, and the wells of the
nomadic, pastoral world.
