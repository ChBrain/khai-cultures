# Palestine: a khai culture

Palestine staged as a full khai play, ISO PS, language ar (Modern Standard
Arabic in a distinctly Palestinian voice). A people rooted in the land like the
olive, keeping its memory with dignity and patience, staged as a theatre: its
enduring culture as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its language
encodes, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a
single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_palestine.md](play_palestine.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_ar.md](pitch_ar.md), the Hofstede layer, written from the
  Palestinian cultural profile (warmth and hospitality, the family and the close
  group above the individual, a quiet pride and dignity, patience before
  hardship, and the rootedness of the land) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (the enduring culture):** the land and the holy cities, the world of
  the village, the tree of poetry, and the diaspora and the key - each casting
  the elements it needs, and each staged strictly through enduring culture.
- **Personas:** Mahmoud Darwish (the national poet), Fadwa Tuqan (the poet),
  Ghassan Kanafani (the novelist and storyteller), and Sliman Mansour (the
  painter of the land) as the historic cast, plus one archetype, the embroiderer
  who carries the stitch from mother to daughter. Each is wired to the language
  engine by the relationship to the mother tongue it holds.
- **Positions:** the land and the olive (the deep attachment to the land and the
  olive tree as root and inheritance), the embroidered memory (the tatreez and
  the thobe, each village its own pattern), and sumud (steadfast, patient
  rootedness).
- **Places:** al-Quds / Jerusalem (the holy city of shared sacredness, with
  Bethlehem and Hebron near it) and the village (the house, the courtyard, and
  the world built on the land).
- **Processes:** the olive harvest (the mawsim, the family gathered to the tree)
  and the dabke (the line dance and the wedding songs).
- **Pieces:** the embroidered thobe (the tatreez that records the village in
  thread) and the key (the iron key kept with the story of the home).

## Provenance

The cultural source data behind the pitch and the public-record cultural and
literary sources are recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never
enters the prose; the pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public record
are credited, never claimed. The staging takes no political side and keeps all
conflict, contemporary politics, borders, and displacement events entirely out
of scope; any sorrow is carried with restraint and dignity, through enduring
culture alone. The staging and the architecture are original work.
