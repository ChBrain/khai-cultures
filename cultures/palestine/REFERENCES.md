# Palestine: References

The provenance behind this culture: the cultural source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-record cultural and literary
sources behind the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Cultural source data

The pitch [pitch_ar.md](pitch_ar.md) is written from the Palestinian cultural
profile on Geert Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and
carries no number. Hofstede Insights does not publish a separate scored country
profile for Palestine; the pitch is therefore written from the broadly shared
Levantine Arab cultural profile (high collectivism, the family and close group
above the individual, a relationship of respect to authority and elders, and a
strong sense of hospitality, dignity, and honour), recorded here so the
orientation is on the record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Levantine Arab profile (qualitative) |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------------------ |
| Power Distance                | high                                 |
| Individualism                 | low (strongly collectivist)          |
| Motivation toward Achievement | moderate                             |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | high                                 |
| Long Term Orientation         | moderate                             |
| Indulgence                    | low to moderate (restraint)          |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group (the
six-dimensions model of national culture and the Country Comparison tool, which
covers the wider Arab-region grouping). The profile is qualitative and
approximate; no scored Palestine profile is published.

## Cultural and literary sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, the practices, and the
pieces are drawn from the public cultural and literary record. They are credited
here and never claimed; the staging is original work. The staging takes no
political side and keeps the conflict, contemporary politics, borders, wars, and
displacement events entirely out of scope; Palestine is staged strictly from
within its own enduring cultural self-understanding, and any sorrow is carried
with restraint and dignity.

daughter and so keeps the embroidered memory alive across the generations.
