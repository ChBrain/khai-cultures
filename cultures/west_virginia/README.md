# West Virginia: a khai culture

West Virginia staged as a full khai play, ISO US-WV, language en. The Mountain State: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its Statehood Civil War breakaway, Blair Mountain labor uprising, and New River Gorge tourism boom encodes, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house that holds this culture and answers for it.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_west_virginia.md](play_west_virginia.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_west_virginia.md](pitch_west_virginia.md), the West Virginia key.
- **Plots (history):** the Statehood Proclamation of 1863, the Battle of Blair Mountain of 1921, and the New River Gorge Tourism Boom of 1978.
- **Personas:** Billy (underground coal miner) and Lilly (river guide), written in English.
- **Positions:** mountain sovereignty (Appalachian independence/union labor loyalty), gorge stewardship (wilderness recreation/ecological preservation).
- **Places:** Blair Mountain, New River Gorge.
- **Processes:** raft guiding (river rapid navigation), coal shoveling (underground mining safety).
- **Pieces:** a union badge, a river raft.
- **Plan:** the reclamation plan.
