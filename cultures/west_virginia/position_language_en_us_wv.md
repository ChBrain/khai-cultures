# Position: West Virginian English

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The Appalachian English of the Mountain State, the coal-camp cadence of the southern coalfields and the warm river drawl of the New River Gorge. It holds a steep, plainspoken mountain independence that both the mine and the raft dock carry, a South Midland speech shaped by the ridges and the hollows.

## Orders

Where it passes as native ground: the mine portal, the union hall, the New River raft dock, the mountain holler. It orders which turns of phrase and vowels mark a speaker as West Virginian and sets the coalfield ear against the river-gorge one.

## Loses

Ground to General American, the flat national broadcast standard the screen and the commercial board carry, which stamps the Appalachian mountain speech as backward and trims the drawl off the young. The corporate extraction register also crowds the slower river-guiding speech of the gorge toward the margin, treating the season-bound talk of the water as secondary to the vocabulary of high-volume coal.

## Drives

It carries [Billy](persona_billy.md), whose coal-camp cadence is the working English of the southern coalfields, and [Lilly](persona_lilly.md), whose warm mountain drawl holds the river English of the New River Gorge. So the one West Virginian speech shows who turns toward the extraction ground and who keeps the river one.
