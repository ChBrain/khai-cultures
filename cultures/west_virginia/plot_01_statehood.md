## Cue

In June 1863, President Abraham Lincoln proclaims West Virginia statehood, separating it from Virginia during the Civil War.

## Action

The act establishes the state as a free-state enclave, developing [mountain sovereignty](position_mountain_sovereignty.md). The [union badge](piece_union_badge.md) represents early labor solidarity. Billy coordinates historical statehood archives in Wheeling, while Lilly audits safety reports for regional mountain trails.

## Stage

- [mountain sovereignty](position_mountain_sovereignty.md) : the Appalachian independence, union labor loyalty, and mountain pride.
- [union badge](piece_union_badge.md) : the historic metal pin representing labor solidarity.
- [place_blair_mountain](place_blair_mountain.md) : the historic mountain ridge that later hosted major labor conflicts.

## Tension

The breakaway is rejected by Confederate Virginia, triggering years of violent border warfare and deep family polarization.
