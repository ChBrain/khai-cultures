# Persona: Lilly

## Taxonomy

[gorge stewardship](position_gorge_stewardship.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A fifty-two-year-old river guide in Fayetteville. English, spoken with a warm drawl, is her mother tongue, the language in which she [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and writes her daily river logs. It is the [West Virginian English](position_language_en_us_wv.md) of the New River Gorge, the warm mountain drawl of the river and the raft dock. She speaks no foreign languages. What reaches her thin and secondhand is the opposite trade: the quick, technology-oriented talk of coal miners like Billy and the digital demands of energy boards, which [she hears only like a distant echo](process_hearing_followed.md), and she feels the patient, season-dependent rhythm of river water reclamation counts for less than corporate mining and speedway entertainment. She belongs to [the Mountain State](position_culture_mountain_state.md) through its gorge-stewardship half, the newer trade that reads the same mountain by its rivers rather than by its coal seams.

## Action

She preserves [river raft](piece_river_raft.md) records, coordinates field conservation under [the reclamation plan](plan_kanawha_river_water_reclamation.md), and studies [mountain sovereignty](position_mountain_sovereignty.md).

## Shadow

Her pride in traditional mountain independence and local property rights can make her suspicious of state-wide zoning and environmental housing mandates, fearing they will restrict family property use.

## Tell

She looks out over the river canyon and says thoughtfully: "The rapids have their own cycles; you can't protect watershed ecology if you're only focused on digital speed and coal production metrics."
