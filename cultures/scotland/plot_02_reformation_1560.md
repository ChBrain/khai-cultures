# Plot: the Reformation 1560

## Taxonomy

[play_scotland](play_scotland.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

The old church is rich, distant, and distrusted. In [Edinburgh](place_edinburgh.md) the reforming preaching of John Knox gathers force, and the Parliament is pressed to break with Rome and remake the faith of the nation.

## Action

In 1560 the Scots Parliament abolishes the authority of the Pope and adopts a reformed confession, and a Presbyterian [Kirk](position_the_kirk.md) is built: governed not by bishops but by elders, suspicious of show, and bent on a school in every parish so that each soul can read Scripture for itself. It is a sterner, more levelling reform than the one to the south, and it stamps the nation with rigour, thrift, and the conviction that learning is everyone's right.

## Stage

- [Edinburgh](place_edinburgh.md): the capital where the reform is preached and enacted.
- [the Kirk](position_the_kirk.md): the levelling, learned church that the Reformation raises.

## Tension

The Kirk gives the nation a school in every parish and a deep equality before God, but its rigour can harden into a narrow, joyless severity. The same reform that levels rank and spreads learning also lays a heavy hand on pleasure and dissent.
