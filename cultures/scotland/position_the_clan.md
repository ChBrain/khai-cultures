# Position: the clan

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The power of kinship made into a society. In the Highlands the clan - the children of one name and forebear - binds people to a chief by blood, loyalty, and shared land, ordering the whole of life by who is kin to whom.

## Orders

That one stand by one's own, follow the chief, hold the name in honour, and repay loyalty with loyalty. The bond of kindred comes before the distant crown or law, and a slight to the name is a slight to all who bear it.

## Loses

Ground where loyalty narrows into feud and the chief's word becomes tyranny, and where, after Culloden, the whole system is broken and the glens cleared of the very people kinship was meant to bind. The clan loses when the tie that protected its folk is severed and they are turned out for sheep.

## Drives

It is carried by the keeper who holds its memory and its Gaelic alive, and it stood at the back of the king in the spear-rings of [Bannockburn 1314](plot_01_bannockburn_1314.md).
