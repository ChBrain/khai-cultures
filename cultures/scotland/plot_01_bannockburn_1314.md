# Plot: Bannockburn 1314

## Taxonomy

[play_scotland](play_scotland.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

The Wars of Independence have ground on for years. A great English army marches north to relieve Stirling Castle, and [Robert the Bruce](persona_bruce.md) must stand and fight for the crown and the freedom of the kingdom.

## Action

By the Bannock burn the Scots foot, drawn up in the bristling spear-rings of the schiltron, hold and then break the heavy English chivalry on the soft ground. Bruce wins back the independence of the realm, and a decade later the Declaration of Arbroath sets it in words: that the Scots fight not for glory or riches but for freedom alone, which no honest man gives up but with his life.

## Stage

- [Robert the Bruce](persona_bruce.md): the king who stands and wins back the kingdom.
- [the clan](position_the_clan.md): the kindred and spearmen who hold the line at his back.

## Tension

The freedom is won on the field, but it is the freedom of a small and contested kingdom that must be defended again and again. The independence taken here becomes the deep grain of the nation, a thing fought for rather than given, and never quite taken for granted.
