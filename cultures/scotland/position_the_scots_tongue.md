# Position: the Scots tongue

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The braid Scots of the Lowlands, a tongue in its own right and not mere slang: the sister of English, not its gutter, with its own words, its own grammar, and a literature that [Burns](persona_burns.md) raised to song. It holds the warmth of the hearth, the byre, and the street, the leid a Scot speaks among their own.

## Orders

That one speaks Scots with kin and on the street and turns to standard English for the school, the office, and the page: the quiet two-handedness of the Lowland Scot, in which the one tongue carries the heart and the other the form.

## Loses

Ground to standard English, which rules the classroom, the screen, and the printed page, and which long taught Scots to call its own speech "bad English." It thins toward a register and an accent, kept whole only by those who still claim it as a leid.

## Drives

It lives in [Burns](persona_burns.md), who made the ploughman's tongue a literature, and in [Eilidh](persona_eilidh.md), who slips into it on the street without thinking; and it drives Scotland to argue, still, whether what it speaks is a language or a way of speaking English, and to take a levelling pride in the plain word over the genteel one.
