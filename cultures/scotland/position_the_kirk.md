# Position: the Kirk

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The power of a church without bishops, governed by elders chosen from the congregation. The Presbyterian Kirk sets each soul equal before God, demands a school in every parish so all can read Scripture, and stamps the nation with rigour, thrift, and self-examination.

## Orders

That one live plainly, work hard, distrust show, and answer to one's own conscience and the kirk session rather than to rank. Display is suspect, idleness a fault, and learning a duty owed by every household, high or low.

## Loses

Ground where the rigour hardens into a narrow, joyless severity that polices pleasure and dissent. The Kirk loses when its discipline smothers the very freedom of conscience and equality it was built to defend.

## Drives

It is carried by [Robert Burns](persona_burns.md), who lampoons the unco guid while loving the levelling, lettered faith that schooled him, and it springs from the [Reformation 1560](plot_02_reformation_1560.md).
