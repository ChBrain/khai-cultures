# Piece: the Saltire

## Taxonomy

Parent group: pieces

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Place

Flown above castles, schools, and football terraces across the country, the white diagonal cross of Saint Andrew on a field of blue, said to be among the oldest national flags in the world.

## Load Bearing

It stands for the independence of the nation and its equal worth among peoples. Legend holds that the white cross appeared as clouds against a blue sky before a battle, a sign that the smaller host would prevail; ever since it has been the plain emblem of a people who fought to be their own. Without this shared sign the nation's independence of spirit would lack its image.

## Apparent

A simple white saltire, the X-shaped cross on which Saint Andrew was martyred, set on a sky-blue ground. Nothing about it is ornate; it is a clear, bold mark, as much at home on a fan's face as on an official mast.

## Yearbook

- Said to derive from the cross of Saint Andrew, the nation's patron.
- Born, by legend, of white clouds against a blue sky before a battle.
- Flown today as the everyday emblem of an independent, equal people.
