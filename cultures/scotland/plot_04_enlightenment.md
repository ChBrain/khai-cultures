# Plot: the Scottish Enlightenment

## Taxonomy

[play_scotland](play_scotland.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

A poor, devout nation has, through its parish schools and its four old universities, raised a people uncommonly given to reading and argument. In the closes and taverns of [Edinburgh](place_edinburgh.md) around 1750, that schooled temper catches fire.

## Action

In a few decades a small country gives the world an extraordinary harvest of mind: David Hume in philosophy, Adam Smith in political economy, and a crowd of others in geology, medicine, and engineering. Edinburgh is hailed the Athens of the North. It rests on the [democratic intellect](position_egalitarianism.md) - the belief that learning is the common right of the lad o' pairts, not the privilege of birth.

## Stage

- [Edinburgh](place_edinburgh.md): the Old Town crucible, the Athens of the North.
- [egalitarianism](position_egalitarianism.md): the democratic intellect that makes the flowering possible.

## Tension

The flowering proves that a small, levelling nation can lead the world in thought rather than in arms. But its very brilliance draws its talent outward, and the question becomes whether the democratic intellect can be kept at home as well as exported to the world.
