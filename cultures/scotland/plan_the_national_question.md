# Plan: the national question

## Taxonomy

A mandate: the deliberate modern drive for Scotland to govern itself, from the reconvened Parliament to the open question of independence, carried by [Eilidh](persona_eilidh.md)'s generation, proud of its country and impatient with it at once.

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Direction

To settle how far Scotland rules itself, within the Union or without it: a Parliament of its own restored after three centuries, and a question of independence put to the people rather than the sword, so that the old claim that sovereignty rests with the Scottish people is made real by consent.

## Orders

- To the parties and the Parliament: use and widen the devolved power, and govern well enough to earn the larger claim.
- To the people: decide the question at the ballot box, by argument and not by arms.
- To Westminster: recognise the Scottish claim of right and the people's say over their own future.

## Implementation

The reconvened Scottish Parliament of 1999; the 2014 independence referendum; the devolution settlement and the rise of the SNP; and the claim of right that sovereignty lies with the people of Scotland.

## Targets

- [x] A Scottish Parliament reconvened in 1999 after almost three centuries.
- [x] Scotland decided the independence question at the ballot box in 2014, by argument not arms.
- [W] The 2014 No settled the matter for a while only; the question stays open and divides the country.
- [ ] Settle Scotland's place, self-government or independence, by lasting consent.
