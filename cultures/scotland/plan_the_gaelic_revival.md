# Plan: the Gaelic revival

## Taxonomy

A mandate: the deliberate, ongoing effort to bring Gaelic back from the edge of death, the tongue [Mairi](persona_mairi.md) holds and that the schools and the state once beat out of the glens.

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Direction

To make Gaelic a living, everyday tongue again rather than a relic of the cleared Highlands: to win back for it the school, the screen, and the official world, so that what now survives only with the old reaches the young again before the last native glens fall silent.

## Orders

- To Bòrd na Gàidhlig and the schools: grow Gaelic-medium education so children leave able to live in the tongue, not only name it.
- To the state: hold Gaelic's official status, its signage and its broadcasting, as a right and not a favour.
- To the Highlands, the islands, and the diaspora: speak it daily, where [Mairi](persona_mairi.md)'s generation still keeps it alive.

## Implementation

The Gaelic Language (Scotland) Act 2005 and Bòrd na Gàidhlig; Gaelic-medium schools in the cities and the isles; BBC Alba; and the heartland Gaelic of the Western Isles.

## Targets

- [x] Gaelic won official recognition in the Gaelic Language (Scotland) Act 2005.
- [x] Gaelic-medium education and BBC Alba were established and are growing.
- [F] The census still records the number of Gaelic speakers falling, not rising.
- [ ] New speakers raised so Gaelic lives beyond [Mairi](persona_mairi.md)'s generation in the glens.
