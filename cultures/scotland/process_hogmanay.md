# Process: Hogmanay

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

Anyone who sees out the old year and welcomes the new with neighbours and strangers alike. Hogmanay needs only the turn of midnight, an open door, and the will to first-foot from house to house.

## Direction

Toward a great levelling welcome: the bells at midnight, "Auld Lang Syne" with crossed hands, the first-foot crossing the threshold with coal and a dram, and doors thrown open to all comers. It is the year's largest act of fellowship, where rank and stranger-hood dissolve in the welcome.

## Lever

The need for warmth and fellowship in the dark of winter drives it, and a tradition that has long made the New Year, more than Christmas, the great Scottish feast. Against it stands only the cold and the year's grudges, and Hogmanay sweeps both out with the old year.

## Echo

Hogmanay makes the warmth beneath the dour reserve plain for one night, and carries [Robert Burns](persona_burns.md)'s "Auld Lang Syne" round the whole world at the turn of every year.
