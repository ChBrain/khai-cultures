# Process: the ceilidh

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

Anyone who gathers folk for music, song, and dance, in a village hall or a kitchen. The ceilidh needs only a fiddle or a box, a floor to dance on, and people willing to take their turn.

## Direction

Toward shared joy that levels the room: reels and jigs danced by all together, songs and stories passed round, where the old and the young, the able and the awkward, take the floor as equals. It is not a performance for an audience but a making of music by the whole company.

## Lever

The pull of belonging and the love of the old songs drive it, and a tradition that holds music as common property, not the gift of a few. Against it stands only shyness and side, and the ceilidh dissolves both in the first reel.

## Echo

The ceilidh makes the levelling temper audible and danceable, where, as [Màiri](persona_mairi.md) knows, the old songs are kept alive by being sung together rather than stored away.
