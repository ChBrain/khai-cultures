# Play: Scotland

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the house that holds this production and answers for it.

## Name

Scotland, the northern country of glen and firth, Highland and Lowland, between the North Sea and the Atlantic. ISO GB-SCT, language en, with Scots and Scottish Gaelic in its cast.

## Arc

A small, hard-won nation that fought free on the field, reformed its faith into a stern and levelling kirk, lost its Highland clans to the sword and the Clearance, and then, out of a country that had every reason to look inward, lit one of the great flowerings of the human mind. Beneath it all runs a single nerve: that nae man is better born than another, that the parish school and the keen argument matter more than rank, and that pride and grievance, dour reserve and sudden warmth, the glen and the city, are held together in one stubborn, questioning people.

## Triggers

**[Plot 1: Bannockburn 1314](plot_01_bannockburn_1314.md)**

[Robert the Bruce](persona_bruce.md) breaks the English host at Bannockburn and wins back the independence of the kingdom, with [the clan](position_the_clan.md) and the spearmen of the schiltron at his back.

**[Plot 2: The Reformation 1560](plot_02_reformation_1560.md)**

In [Edinburgh](place_edinburgh.md) the old church is overthrown and a Presbyterian [Kirk](position_the_kirk.md) is raised, levelling and learned, distinct from the church to the south.

**[Plot 3: Culloden 1746](plot_03_culloden_1746.md)**

On the moor the Jacobite rising is broken; the clan system of [the Highlands](place_the_highlands.md) is shattered, and people like [Mairi](persona_mairi.md) watch a whole way of life torn out by the root.

**[Plot 4: The Scottish Enlightenment](plot_04_enlightenment.md)**

Out of a poor, devout, schooled nation [Edinburgh](place_edinburgh.md) becomes the Athens of the North, and the [democratic intellect](position_egalitarianism.md) gives the world Hume and Adam Smith.

## Stakes

Whether a small nation that won its freedom, levelled its church, lost its clans, and then taught the world to think can keep its egalitarian temper and its independent mind, holding glen and city, Gaelic and Scots and English, pride and grievance, together as one people.
