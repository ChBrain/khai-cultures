# Scotland: a khai culture

Scotland staged as a full khai play, ISO GB-SCT. A proud, egalitarian nation -
a constituent nation of the United Kingdom - held together across English, Scots,
and Scottish Gaelic. Its history is the plot line, its people the cast, the roles
its society encodes, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all
tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## Three tongues

The play and most of the cast are in English, but two personas are written in
their own tongue, each with its own declared `language:`:

- **English** - Robert the Bruce, Eilidh
- **Scots** (`sco`) - Robert Burns
- **Scottish Gaelic** (`gd`) - Mairi

The play and the shared instances are in English (the nation's common tongue);
Burns is authored in Scots and Mairi in Scottish Gaelic, each routed to its own
language detector.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_scotland.md](play_scotland.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_sct.md](pitch_sct.md), the Hofstede layer, written from the
  Great Britain profile (Scotland has no separate Hofstede profile and inherits
  the national base) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** Bannockburn (1314), the Reformation (1560), Culloden
  (1746), and the Scottish Enlightenment - each casting the elements it needs.
- **Personas:** Robert the Bruce and Robert Burns as the historic cast, plus two
  defining archetypes, Mairi (older, Gaelic, the clans and crofting life) and
  Eilidh (younger, urban, modern egalitarian Scotland). Each is wired to the
  language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** the Kirk (Presbyterian rigour and equality), egalitarianism (the
  democratic intellect, we're a' Jock Tamson's bairns), the clan (Highland kinship
  and loyalty).
- **Places:** Edinburgh (the capital, the Athens of the North), the Highlands
  (the glens and isles, the clans, Culloden, the Clearances).
- **Processes:** the ceilidh, Hogmanay.
- **Pieces:** the Saltire (the flag of Saint Andrew), whisky (the water of life).
