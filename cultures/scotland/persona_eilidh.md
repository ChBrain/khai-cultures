# Persona: Eilidh

## Taxonomy

[egalitarianism](position_egalitarianism.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A young woman in her twenties, a city Scot of Glasgow or Edinburgh, raised on the conviction that we're a' Jock Tamson's bairns and nobody is better than anyone else. Quick-witted, warm beneath a dry edge, scornful of side and swank, proud of her country and impatient with its grievances at once. She [speaks, hears, and thinks in](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) her mother tongue plainly and fast, with the levelling humour of the street, slipping between the [braid Scots](position_language_sco_gb_sct.md) of her pals and the [standard English](position_language_en_gb_sct.md) of work without thinking. The Gaelic of the Highlands, though, she has [barely caught a word of](process_hearing_caught.md): there is the gap, the one tongue lives in her in two registers, warm and kent on the street, buttoned for the form and the interview, while the auldest leid of her own country stays a stranger to her. She belongs to [Scottishness](position_culture_scottishness.md) and lives its levelling temper without ever having to argue for it.

## Action

She lives [egalitarianism](position_egalitarianism.md) without thinking about it, takes her turn at [the ceilidh](process_ceilidh.md) and sees in [Hogmanay](process_hogmanay.md) the whole town made equal at the bells. She argues politics in the pub, takes nobody's rank on trust, and reminds her elders that the open, modern Scotland still has to be made.

## Shadow

Her levelling edge can tip into a chippiness that mistakes every success for showing off and resents anyone who rises. The pride she carries can curdle into grievance, blaming the old neighbour to the south for what the country could mend itself.

## Tell

She cuts pretension down with a quick line and treats the famous and the unknown exactly alike.
