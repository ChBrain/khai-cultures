# Myanmar: a khai culture

Myanmar staged as a full khai play, ISO MM, language my (Burmese, written in the
Burmese script). A golden land saturated by Theravada Buddhism and bound by the
Irrawaddy, set as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast,
the roles its faith and language encode, its places, its practices, and its
defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_myanmar.md](play_myanmar.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_my.md](pitch_my.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Myanmar's profile (a strong collective and family orientation, a marked respect
  for hierarchy and elders, a restrained and gentle emotional register, and a
  merit-minded, present-accepting outlook) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** Bagan and the Dhamma (King Anawrahta's establishment of
  Theravada and the temple plain), the Toungoo union (the gathering of many
  peoples under one realm), the Konbaung flowering (the high age of Burmese
  literature and the ensemble), and the enduring land (the river, the festivals,
  and the faith carried on through changing times). Each casts the elements it
  needs; any historical upheaval is treated soberly, neutrally, and without
  detail, taking no side.
- **Personas:** King Anawrahta, King Bayinnaung, the poet U Ponnya, and the
  scholar-poet Zawgyi as the historic cast, plus one archetype, the novice
  (the shinbyu boy who carries the Dhamma to the next generation). Each is wired
  to the language engine by the relationship it holds to its mother tongue.
- **Positions:** the Dhamma (Theravada Buddhism saturating daily life), the union
  (the land of many peoples and languages held as one), and merit (the
  merit-making life of giving and alms).
- **Places:** Bagan (the temple plain where the Dhamma was raised in brick) and
  Yangon (the city beneath the Shwedagon, the river-delta hub of faith and the
  tea shop).
- **Processes:** the shinbyu (the novitiation that hands the Dhamma on) and the
  alms round (the daily giving that makes merit a part of ordinary life).
- **Pieces:** thanaka (the cooling yellow paste of the cheeks, the land's
  everyday face) and the saing waing (the classical ensemble that gives every
  festival its voice).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the staging holds to enduring ground (Buddhism, Bagan, the river,
festivals, literature, the land, and the peoples), treats any historical change
soberly and from within Myanmar's own self-understanding, takes no political side,
and dwells on no conflict; the staging and the architecture are original work.
