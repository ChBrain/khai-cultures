# Gabon: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain sources behind the plots,
personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_fr.md](pitch_fr.md) is written from Gabon's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
Gabon is not always reported individually in the published tables; where a national
figure is absent, the West African / Central African regional cluster is used as
the closest published proxy. The approximate scores and their source are recorded
here so the data is on the record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Gabon score (approx., regional proxy) |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 77                                    |
| Individualism                 | 20                                    |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 46                                    |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 54                                    |
| Long Term Orientation         | 16                                    |
| Indulgence                    | 40                                    |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate and, for Gabon,
drawn from the West/Central African regional cluster published by Hofstede
Insights / The Culture Factor Group as the closest available proxy; high power
distance and strong collectivism are the salient features carried into the pitch.

## Historical and cultural sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, the processes, and the
pieces are drawn from the public record. They are credited here and never claimed;
the staging is original work. The slave trade, the colonial period, and the cost
of resource extraction are staged soberly on the public record, taking no side;
the Bwiti is treated with dignity as a living spirituality.
