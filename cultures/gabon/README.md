# Gabon: a khai culture

Gabon staged as a full khai play, ISO GA, language fr. A small people under a
vast equatorial forest at the edge of the ocean, as a theatre: its history as the
plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its language and faith encode, its
places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch. The
prose is written in Gabonese French, not a metropolitan one.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_gabon.md](play_gabon.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_fr.md](pitch_fr.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Gabon's
  profile (high collectivism and respect for elders and the chief, a measured and
  unhurried pace, the family and the house weighing more than the lone individual)
  and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the first peoples under the forest, the meeting at the
  ocean, the founding of Libreville for freed captives, and oil and the forest in
  the twentieth century, each casting the elements it needs and each staged
  soberly, taking no side.
- **Personas:** Pierre Akendengue (the musician-poet), Andre Raponda Walker (the
  first Gabonese priest and folklorist), King Denis (the historic Myene coastal
  chief), and a Bwiti nganga handled with dignity as a living spirituality, plus
  one archetype, the mask carver. Each is wired to the language engine by the
  language-crossing it holds, between a mother tongue and French.
- **Positions:** the forest (the vast green that covers nearly nine-tenths of the
  country, a mother and not a coffer), the ancestors (the dead kept close, the
  reliquaries and the masks), the river (the Ogooue, the road before the road).
- **Places:** Libreville (the city of freedom, founded for freed captives), Lope
  (the national park where savanna bites the forest, elephants and gorillas at
  home).
- **Processes:** the Bwiti (the spiritual path of the iboga and the ngombi harp,
  the passage to the ancestors), going down the river (the Ogooue as the country's
  artery toward the coast and the world).
- **Pieces:** the white mask (the Punu/Lumbu spirit mask, world-famous as art and,
  at home, the face of a returning ancestor), the ngombi (the sacred eight-string
  harp of the Bwiti).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain sources are recorded in
[REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the pitch is its
invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never claimed; the
slave trade, the colonial period, and the cost of resource extraction are treated
soberly on the public record, taking no political side; the Bwiti is staged with
dignity as a living spirituality; the staging and the architecture are original
work.
