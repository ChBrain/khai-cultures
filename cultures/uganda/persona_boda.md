# Persona: the boda boda rider

## Taxonomy

[the many held as one](position_obuntu.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The young man on the motorcycle who threads the city's jams and carries Kampala on his back wheel, the boda boda rider who knows every hill and shortcut and pothole, who will take you, your sack of matoke, your goat, or your whole family where no car can go. Quick, fearless, and full of talk, he hustles from dawn for the day's bread, knows the price of everything and the gossip of everywhere, and greets the regulars by name on his stage at the corner. Luganda he has from home and the street: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in his mother tongue all day, bargaining and joking and calling out, the fast city Luganda of the stage. Swahili and the mixed street slang of the trade he [borrows and carries](process_speaking_borrowed.md) for the up-country passenger and the cross-town deal, [catching by ear](process_hearing_caught.md) the bits of a dozen tongues that pass through his back seat, for every people in the country rides behind him. He carries the Muganda and the Munyankole and the Acholi and the up-country trader through the same streets without minding which is which, the nation made one on a back wheel, and the only gap at the limit of the tongue he meets is the one he leaps with a grin and a price, finding a shared word for every fare.

## Action

In [the Pearl renewed](plot_04_pearl_renewed.md) he is the engine of the everyday city, the one who keeps the people and the goods and the news moving when everything else is stuck, the hustle and the warmth of the country in one young man on a machine. He carries [the many held as one](position_obuntu.md) by ferrying every people's child through the same streets for the same fare, the obuntu of the road, no passenger a stranger once they are on the seat.

## Shadow

The hustle that keeps the city moving can run on too little rest and too much risk, the day's bread bought at the edge of safety, and the warmth of the stage can sit over the hard truth of a young man with no other road open to him; the figure who carries everyone can be the one the country leaves to carry himself.

## Tell

He will weave a goat, a mattress, and a whole family through a standstill jam, greet three peoples in three tongues before the next corner, and call you "ssebo" or "nyabo" before he has even asked where you are going.
