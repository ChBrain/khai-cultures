# Plan: one Uganda of many peoples

## Taxonomy

The standing aim the country set itself the day the crested crane flag went up: the long, warm work of making the many one, of holding [the kingdoms](position_obwakabaka.md) and the nation in the same heart, of widening the circle of [the many held as one](position_obuntu.md) until every people sits whole at the one table, and of making the plenty of [the Pearl of Africa](position_pearl.md) reach the bread on every hill alike.

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Direction

Make the many one without flattening the many: hold every people, the Muganda and the Munyoro and the Acholi and the Munyankole and the Karimojong and all the rest, whole and proud inside one Uganda, so that to be fully a Musoga is also to be fully a Ugandan. Carry the kingdoms with dignity into the present, honouring the throne and the elder without letting one people's pride become a wall against the next. And make good on the gift of the Pearl, sharing the plenty the land keeps pouring into its lap so that the beauty on the postcard is matched by the bread on the table, slowly by slowly, mpola mpola, at the same wide table.

## Orders

- To the kingdom and the clan, in the line of [the Kabaka Mutesa I](persona_mutesa.md): keep your drums, your tombs, and your tongue whole, and carry your dignity into the nation rather than against it.
- To the maker and the hustler, in the spirit of [the boda boda rider](persona_boda.md): keep the everyday country moving and the pot open, ferrying every people's child through the same streets for the same fare.
- To the nation: widen the circle, not flatten it; honour the many and hold them as one; and let the plenty of the Pearl reach every hill, so no one is praised over the people who live hard upon the land.

## Implementation

A young nation, drawn in 1962 over old and proud kingdoms and more than thirty peoples and tongues, that has held together through hard seasons and good with its warmth intact, kept the kingdoms and the nation in one heart, and carried the obuntu of the village into the roar of the city. It has won a name in song and sport and made the world come to stand at the source of the Nile, and it leaves open still the long work of sharing the Pearl's plenty fairly and of making the many fully and warmly one.
