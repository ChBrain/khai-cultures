# Process: the rolex

## Taxonomy

[the Pearl of Africa](position_pearl.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

The young man at the roadside stand with his charcoal stove, his frying pan, and his stack of chapati: the rolex man on the corner, on the campus edge, outside the bar at midnight, anywhere the people pass hungry and a few shillings change hands. All it takes is a hot pan, eggs whisked with tomato and onion and a little cabbage, a fresh chapati ready to roll, and the call to the passer-by, ssebo, nyabo, the rolex is ready.

## Direction

Toward the people's food, made fast, cheap, and full of warmth: the rolled eggs in a chapati, "rolex" from the rolling of it, the snack a hungry country invented for itself out of what was to hand. It is the food of the hustle and the late night, the student's supper and the worker's lunch, born on the street and grown into a thing the whole country claims with pride, a small everyday genius of the Pearl. The point is to feed the many for little, hot off the pan, with a joke thrown in for free, so that even the one with the last coin in the pocket goes home filled.

## Lever

It is carried by the country's quick wit and its way of making plenty out of little, the chapati from the trade routes and the eggs from the home shamba turned by a young hustler into a thing that is purely Ugandan. It holds against the long day at the stove for thin margins, the day's bread earned coin by coin at the edge of the road; against it stands the strain on the young man with no other road open to him, the warmth of the corner sitting over a hard living. Yet the pan stays hot and the people keep coming, slowly by slowly.

## Echo

The rolex makes plain what drives [the Pearl of Africa](position_pearl.md): that a country given this much plenty makes the gift go round, turning what the land gives into something to share at a price the many can meet. On the same corner [the boda boda rider](persona_boda.md) pulls up for a rolex between fares, the hustle of the road and the hustle of the pan feeding each other, the everyday warmth of the Pearl rolled into a chapati.
