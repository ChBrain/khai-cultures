# Position: the many held as one

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The conviction, older than the nation and now its hardest task, that a person is held by their people and that many peoples can be held as one without any of them ceasing to be itself. It is obuntu, the plain truth that a human being is a human being through other human beings, that no one is meant to eat alone, that the pot is kept for the stranger and the visitor is fed before they are asked their business. From the clan that claims you at birth to the village that buries its dead together, the country runs on belonging; and the work of the nation, since the day the flag went up, has been to stretch that belonging across more than thirty peoples and tongues until the Muganda and the Acholi and the Munyankole and the Karimojong are all, without losing what they are, one Uganda.

## Orders

That a person is never only themselves but is held by clan, family, and neighbour, and holds them in turn; that you greet before you ask, share before you are sure you can spare it, and make room at the table for the one who has none; and that the many peoples of the land are to be made one nation by widening the circle of belonging, not by flattening the differences inside it, so that to be fully a Muganda or a Musoga is also, and not against it, to be fully a Ugandan.

## Loses

Ground when the warm circle is drawn too tight, when belonging to my people becomes a fence against yours, and the obuntu that should reach the stranger stops at the edge of the clan; and ground, too, when the patience that holds the nation together slides into a waiting that lets old wounds fester unspoken, the brave face kept so long over what divides that no one ever sits down to set it right, slowly by slowly, at the same table.

## Drives

Lives in [Okot p'Bitek](persona_okot.md), who sang the dignity of his own people's tongue and ways not to wall them off but to claim a seat for them at the nation's table, and in [the boda boda rider](persona_boda.md), who carries every people's child through the same streets and knows the whole city by its hills and its hands. It drives Uganda toward the long task of making the many one, the circle widening, the pot kept open.
