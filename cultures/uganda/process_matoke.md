# Process: the matoke kitchen

## Taxonomy

[the many held as one](position_culture_obuntu.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

The mother, the auntie, the daughter who rises early and goes to the back to peel: anyone in the home with a bunch of green matoke fresh from the garden or the market, a sharp knife, a little cooking oil to keep the hands from staining, and the banana leaves to wrap it in. From the village shamba to the city kitchen, wherever a Ugandan sits down to eat the real food, the day begins with the peeling of the matoke, and there is always one more knife handed to the visitor who offers to help.

## Direction

Toward the table that holds the home together. To cook matoke is not just to make a meal but to keep the people fed in the way that says they are at home: the green bananas peeled and wrapped in their own leaves, steamed slowly until they go soft and golden, then mashed into the smooth, warm food the country grew up on. It is the food of the welcome and the food of the everyday alike, the dish that says a guest has truly arrived, and the pot is always made bigger than the household so that whoever comes by, expected or not, will find a plate kept for them, for here no one eats alone.

## Lever

It is carried by the deep belief that to feed a person is to hold them, and by the banana itself, the plant that gives all year round in a land where the rains come twice and the soil gives back whatever it is given. It holds against the long hours of the slow steam and the work of the peeling, and against the lean season when the pot must stretch; against it stands the strain on the one who keeps giving until there is little left, the open hand spent past good sense. Yet mpola mpola the matoke steams, and the table is set wide.

## Echo

The matoke kitchen makes plain what drives [the many held as one](position_culture_obuntu.md): that a human being is held through other human beings, that the pot is kept open for the stranger and the visitor is fed before they are asked their business. Around this table [the boda boda rider](persona_boda.md) carries his sack of matoke home through the city jams, the obuntu of the road meeting the obuntu of the hearth, no one in the country meant to eat alone.
