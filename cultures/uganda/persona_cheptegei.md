# Persona: Joshua Cheptegei

## Taxonomy

[the Pearl of Africa](position_pearl.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The long-distance runner from the cool highlands of Sebei in the east, the man who carried Uganda's name to the front of the world's track and held world records over the long distances, an Olympic and world champion bred on the thin air and the steep hills near the Kenyan border. Quiet, disciplined, and unshowy, he trains where the air is high and the roads run up, and runs for a country that watches the long races leaning into the radio, ready to roar his name across every hill at once. Kupsabiny, the tongue of his Sebei people, he has from home: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in his mother tongue with his own, the small, strong tongue of the highlands that few outside the hills will ever learn. Swahili and English he [carries as his working tongues](process_speaking_carried.md), [reading and following them with ease](process_reading_followed.md) in the wide world of the meets and the press, the languages of the start line and the interview. But the cheer that lifts him home, the praise shouted from the roadside, comes in the home tongue and the home names, and no translation of the post-race interview ever quite carries the village joy in it, so the gap at the limit of the tongue is the quiet distance between the global record and the hillside that raised the man who set it.

## Action

In [the Pearl renewed](plot_04_pearl_renewed.md) he runs the nation's name onto the world's track and brings the gold home, giving a country sure of its hills a champion of its lungs and a fresh, clean pride. He carries [the Pearl of Africa](position_pearl.md) by turning the beauty of the highlands into the strength that wins, and by reminding the country that the plenty of the land includes the breath and the heart of its children.

## Shadow

The champion lifted up as the face of the Pearl can let the country mistake one man's medals for the whole nation's plenty, cheering the gold on the track while the bread on the table still fails to reach every hill; the pride in the runner can stand in for the harder work of sharing the country's gift around.

## Tell

He runs the last lap with a whole country leaning into the radio behind him, and crosses the line for hills that will shout his Sebei name long after the world has filed the world record under Uganda.
