# Place: Kampala

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Shown

The capital spread over its famous hills above the lake, the heartland of Buganda grown into the country's largest city. The Lubiri, the Kabaka's palace, sits on its hill, and not far off the Kasubi Tombs where the kings are laid; the great cathedrals and the mosque crown other hills, and between them the markets roar, the boda bodas weave, the taxis pack the stages, and the matoke and the rolex carts feed the crowd. Old kingdom and young nation, royal drum and dancehall beat, the village and the metropolis, all packed onto seven hills and many more, loud, warm, and never still.

## Offers

A place where the kingdom and the nation share the same hills, where a Ugandan can stand by the Kabaka's palace and the seat of the republic in one afternoon; the warm, crowded, hustling heart of the country, where every people of Uganda meets in the market and the matatu and the matoke pot, and where the obuntu of the village is carried, somehow, into the roar of the city.

## Withheld

The ease its energy advertises. Kampala carries the strain beneath the bustle, the jams and the hustle and the long day's grind for the day's bread, the young man on the boda earning his living at the edge of safety; and it holds, on its very hills, the old question whole, the kingdom's palace and the nation's seat side by side, the dignity of Buganda and the claim of all Uganda asked to share one city and one heart.
