# Plot: the Pearl renewed

## Taxonomy

[play_uganda](play_uganda.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

In the decades after independence the young nation passes through hard seasons and good, seasons the country holds soberly and with dignity, and comes through them with its warmth intact. The drum keeps its beat, the pot stays open, the rains keep coming, and slowly by slowly the Pearl finds its feet and its voice again, on the lake, on the world's stage, and on the world's track.

## Action

The country renews itself the way it has always done, through its people, its music, and its land. [Geoffrey Oryema](persona_oryema.md) carries the home song across the world and makes strangers hear the Pearl in a tongue they cannot translate; [Joshua Cheptegei](persona_cheptegei.md) runs the nation's name to the front of the world's track and brings the gold home to hills that roar it in their own tongue. In [Kampala](place_kampala.md) and on every stage between, [the boda boda rider](persona_boda.md) keeps the everyday city moving, carrying every people's child through the same streets for the same fare. The kadodi drums and the new dancehall sound side by side, the rolex sizzles on the corner, and the country, sure of its hills and its welcome, makes good slowly by slowly on the plenty the land keeps pouring into its lap.

## Stage

- [Geoffrey Oryema](persona_oryema.md): the singer who carries the home song and the Pearl across the world.
- [Joshua Cheptegei](persona_cheptegei.md): the runner who carries the nation's name onto the world's track.
- [the boda boda rider](persona_boda.md): the young hustler who keeps the everyday city, and its many peoples, moving.
- [the Pearl of Africa](position_pearl.md): the land and the pride in it, renewed through its people.
- [Jinja](place_jinja.md): the source of the Nile, the country's beauty and its draw to the world.

## Tension

The Pearl shines again, in song and sport and the daily hustle, and the warmth that carried the country through its hard seasons carries it forward still. Yet the standing question remains: whether the beauty on the postcard and the gold on the track can be matched by the bread on every table, and whether the many proud peoples, held together this far by patience and obuntu, can be made fully and fairly one Uganda, so that the plenty of the Pearl reaches every hill alike.
