# Uganda: a khai culture

Uganda staged as a full khai play, ISO UG, language en. The Pearl of Africa,
many peoples and old kingdoms held as one nation at the source of the Nile, as a
theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its
languages and ways encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects,
all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the house that holds
this production and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its Estate; a
culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_uganda.md](play_uganda.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Uganda's profile (warm and unhurried, the person before the matter, respect
  owed upward, the group holding the person, patience and the open hand) and
  naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the kingdoms, the protectorate, independence (1962), and
  the Pearl renewed, each casting the elements it needs, and each staging the
  country's hard seasons soberly and with dignity, taking no political side.
- **Personas:** the Kabaka Mutesa I, Okot p'Bitek, Geoffrey Oryema, and Joshua
  Cheptegei as the historic cast, plus one archetype, the boda boda rider (the
  young hustler who carries every people's child through the same streets). Each
  is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** the kingdoms (the old, proud nations over which the state was
  later drawn), the many held as one (obuntu, the long task of making the many one
  without flattening them), and the Pearl of Africa (the land and the country's
  pride in it).
- **Places:** Kampala (the capital on its hills where kingdom and nation share
  one city), Jinja (the source of the Nile and the early industry by the lake).
- **Processes:** the matoke kitchen (the table that holds the home together and
  keeps the pot open for the stranger), the rolex (the people's food made fast,
  cheap, and warm on the corner).
- **Pieces:** the crested crane (the one emblem of a nation made of many
  peoples), the royal drums (the engoma and the engalabi, the dignity of the
  kingdom and the joy of the people on the one skin).
- **Plan:** [plan_one_uganda.md](plan_one_uganda.md), one Uganda of many peoples,
  the standing aim of making the many one and the plenty of the Pearl reach every
  hill.

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain and standard sources
behind the plots, personas, places, processes, and pieces are recorded in
[REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the pitch is its
invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never claimed; the
country's hard seasons are treated soberly on the public record, taking no
political side and with all kingdoms and peoples held in equal dignity; the
staging and the architecture are original work.
