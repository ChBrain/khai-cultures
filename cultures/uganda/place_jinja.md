# Place: Jinja

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Shown

The town on the lake in Busoga country where the great river begins, the source of the Nile, where Nalubaale spills out and starts its long walk north to Egypt. Here the railway from the coast once ended its climb, the mills and the dam came, and the country's industry took root by the water. Today the source draws travellers from the world over to stand where the Nile is born, the rafts ride the white water below, and the fishing boats go out on the wide silver of the lake at dawn. The exact spot where a river that waters half a continent takes its first step is here, in the Pearl, quietly pouring.

## Holds

## Offers

A place where the country can see its own gift made plain: the source of the longest river on earth, the wide lake the world calls Victoria and the country calls Nalubaale, the plenty of water in a landlocked land. The town where the Pearl shows the visitor why it earned the name, and where Uganda meets the world that comes to stand at the river's birth.

## Withheld

The simplicity of the postcard. Jinja carries the gap between the beauty admired and the plenty shared, the source photographed by the world while the town that holds it works for its bread; and it holds the harder truth that the gift must be kept as well as shown, the lake and the river to be guarded for the children, lest the Pearl be praised for what it has and lose the keeping of it.
