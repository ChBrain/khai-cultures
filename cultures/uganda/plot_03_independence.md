# Plot: independence (1962)

## Taxonomy

[play_uganda](play_uganda.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

By the early 1960s the country is ready to take itself back. The peoples and their leaders, the kingdoms among them, negotiate the terms on which the many will go forward as one, and a date is set for the lowering of one flag and the raising of another over [Kampala](place_kampala.md).

## Action

On the ninth of October, 1962, the flag with the crested crane goes up and Uganda becomes a nation in her own hands, the many peoples and old kingdoms now one independent country. It is a season of pride and of music, of the drum and the dance and the new anthem, and of the hard, hopeful work of making a single state out of so many proud nations. In the same years [Okot p'Bitek](persona_okot.md) gives the country Song of Lawino, singing the dignity of the home tongue and the home ways back into pride at the very moment the young nation is finding its feet, claiming for his own people, and so for all, a seat at the new table that does not ask them to stop being themselves.

## Stage

- [Okot p'Bitek](persona_okot.md): the poet who sings the old tongue and ways back into pride as the nation begins.
- [the many held as one](position_culture_obuntu.md): the task the new nation sets itself, to make the many one.
- [Kampala](place_kampala.md): the capital over which the crested crane flag is raised.

## Tension

Independence hands the country to itself with all its plenty and all its pride intact, and with its hardest question now its own to answer: how to be one Uganda made of so many whole peoples, each a nation in its own right, without any losing what it is or feeling the new flag fly more for one hill than another. The promise of 1962 is real, and the long work of making the many one, fairly and warmly, is only just begun.
