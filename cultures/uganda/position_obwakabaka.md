# Position: the kingdoms

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The old, proud kingdoms over which the nation was later drawn: Buganda on the lake with its Kabaka and his Lubiri and the Kasubi Tombs where the kings are laid; Bunyoro-Kitara that once ruled wide across the west; Tooro and Ankole of the long-horned cattle and the cool highlands; Busoga by the water; and the many other peoples and chiefdoms besides, each a whole nation in itself with its own tongue, its drums, its clans, and its way of being a people. The kingdom is older than the state, and for many a Ugandan it is the first thing they are before they are anything else: a Muganda, a Munyoro, a Mutooro, a Munyankole, a Musoga, the country a roof raised later over houses that already stood.

## Orders

That a person belongs first to a people and a king older than the nation, owes respect upward to the elder and the throne, and carries the dignity of the clan into everything they do; that the kingdom is to be honoured and kept, its drums and tombs and tongue passed on whole; and that the nation, when it came, had to be built not over the kingdoms but among them, leaving each its dignity.

## Loses

Ground when the pride of the kingdom hardens into a wall against the others, when "my people first" forgets that the next hill is Uganda too, and the very thing that gives a person their dignity is turned into a reason to look down on the neighbour; and ground, too, when the throne is dragged into the quarrels of the day and made a thing to fight over rather than a thing to honour, so that what should bind a people is set to dividing the nation.

## Drives

Lives in [the Kabaka Mutesa I](persona_mutesa.md), who ruled Buganda from the Lubiri as a king in his own right and held his kingdom's power before any nation was raised over it. It drives Uganda to carry its kingdoms with pride into the present, to honour the elder and the throne, and to face the standing question of how so many proud peoples are to be held, each whole, as one.
