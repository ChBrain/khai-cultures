# Persona: the Kabaka Mutesa I

## Taxonomy

[the kingdoms](position_obwakabaka.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The Kabaka who ruled Buganda from his Lubiri on the hill in the second half of the nineteenth century, a king in his own right over one of the great lake kingdoms before any map was drawn over it. Shrewd, formidable, and curious about the wide world that was beginning to reach his court, he received the strangers who came up from the coast and from down the Nile, weighed them, and kept his own counsel, a ruler who knew exactly the worth of his throne. Luganda he has whole and commanding: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in his mother tongue the orders that move a kingdom, the deep, layered Luganda of the court with its proverbs and its respect-words, every clan and title in its proper place. The tongues of the visitors who came to his Lubiri he [held at arm's length and caught by ear](process_hearing_caught.md), and what was written in their books he [had deciphered](process_reading_deciphered.md) to him through others, taking their measure without ever giving them the inside of his own. He was a king fluent in the tongue of his own land and his own people, and the strangers' words came to him only at second hand, so the gap at the edge of the tongue was theirs to cross to him, never his to cross to them, a king who made the world translate itself into Luganda before it reached the throne.

## Action

In [the kingdoms](plot_01_kingdoms.md) he rules Buganda as a sovereign in his own right, holding [the kingdoms](position_obwakabaka.md) as proud nations on the lake long before the nation of Uganda was thought of. By keeping his throne, his court, and his counsel through a time of strangers and change, he hands the country its oldest dignity, the kingdom that was a whole people before it was ever a part of anything.

## Shadow

The pride and the power of the throne, kept so jealously whole, are the nation's own shadow: the temptation of the proud kingdom to hold itself first and apart, to make the dignity of one people a wall against the others, so that the very thing that gives a Ugandan their oldest self can be turned to keeping the many from ever becoming one.

## Tell

He receives the wide world at his Lubiri on his own terms and in his own tongue, and leaves the country a kingdom older than its borders and a king it still buries with drums.
