# Plot: the protectorate

## Taxonomy

[play_uganda](play_uganda.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

In the late nineteenth and early twentieth centuries the kingdoms and peoples of the lake region are gathered, not always by their own choosing, under a single protectorate and a single new name taken from Buganda. The railway climbs up from the coast, the steamer crosses the lake, and a border is drawn that holds many proud nations inside one line on a map.

## Action

A new order is laid over the old. The kingdoms keep their thrones and much of their dignity under the new arrangement, Buganda above all, while the country is run as one territory and knitted together by the railway and the lake trade that reach [Jinja](place_jinja.md) and the water. Cotton and coffee go down the line to the coast, mission schools spread reading and the English of the new offices, and peoples who had governed themselves apart find themselves counted, taxed, and schooled inside a single border. The Pearl is named and admired by the newcomers even as it is drawn into a wider world it did not ask to join.

## Stage

- [the kingdoms](position_obwakabaka.md): the old nations kept on under the new order, their dignity bent but not broken.
- [the many held as one](position_obuntu.md): the many peoples drawn, not of their own choosing, inside one border.
- [Jinja](place_jinja.md): the lake town the railway reaches, the source of the Nile and the country's industry.

## Tension

The border that gathers the peoples into one territory is the seed of both the nation and its hardest question. It throws many proud kingdoms and peoples into a single country without making them, in their own hearts, a single people, and it favours some and unsettles others as it goes. The line on the map says Uganda; the making of that line into one nation, fairly and without any people losing what it is, is left as the whole task of the years to come.
