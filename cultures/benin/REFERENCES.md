# Benin: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_fr.md](pitch_fr.md) is written from Benin's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record without
entering the prose. Where a country-specific score is not published, the regional
West African profile is used as the reference.

| Dimension                     | Benin score (approx., West African profile) |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 77                                          |
| Individualism                 | 20                                          |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 46                                          |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 54                                          |
| Long Term Orientation         | 16                                          |
| Indulgence                    | 78                                          |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group for the West African region used as
Benin's reference; Benin's low Individualism and high Power Distance mark a
collectivist, community-first society with strong respect for elders and chiefs.

## Historical sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn
from the public historical record. They are credited here and never claimed; the
staging is original work. The Kingdom of Dahomey, the women soldiers of its army,
the memory of the coast and the Door of No Return at Ouidah, and the colonial
period are staged soberly on the public record, with no graphic detail; Vodun is
staged with full respect as a living faith.

The one defining archetype, [le tisserand d'appliques](persona_le_tisserand.md),
is not a historical individual but a composite: the applique artisan of Abomey who
keeps alive the royal craft of the sewn emblem and the proverb that goes with each
figure.
