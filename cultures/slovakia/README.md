# Slovakia: a khai culture

Slovakia staged as a full khai play, ISO SK, language sk. A young nation, long
under others, as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast,
the roles its language and faith encode, its places, its practices, and its
defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_slovakia.md](play_slovakia.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_sk.md](pitch_sk.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Slovakia's
  profile (deference to those above, hard pragmatism and drive, thrift and
  restraint, the patience and faith of a small, long-ruled nation) and naming no
  dimension.
- **Plots (history):** Jánošík (1713), Štúr and the Slovak language (1843), the
  Prague Spring (1968), and Independence (1993) - each casting the elements it
  needs.
- **Personas:** Ľudovít Štúr, Juraj Jánošík, and Alexander Dubček as the historic
  cast, plus two defining archetypes, Anna (older, rural Catholic faith and the
  mother tongue) and Marek (younger, the EU-era emigrant whose Slovak thins
  between Czech and English). Each is wired to the language engine by the
  language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** slovenčina (the tongue codified late and defended against
  Magyarization), mladý národ (the young nation, sovereign only since 1993), viera
  (the rural Catholic faith - the mirror-opposite of the Czech twin's godlessness).
- **Places:** Bratislava (the Danube capital on three borders), Vysoké Tatry (the
  High Tatras of the anthem and the shepherd folk).
- **Processes:** folklór (the living folk festival of song, costume, and fujara),
  púť (the Marian pilgrimage to Šaštín).
- **Pieces:** fujara (the great shepherd's overtone flute), bryndza (the sheep
  cheese at the heart of the national dish).
