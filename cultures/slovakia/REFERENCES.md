# Slovakia: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_sk.md](pitch_sk.md) is written from Slovakia's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no
number. The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the
record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Slovakia score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------ |
| Power Distance                | 100                      |
| Individualism                 | 52                       |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 100                      |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 51                       |
| Long Term Orientation         | 77                       |
| Indulgence                    | 28                       |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published
by Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; Slovakia's Power Distance and
Motivation scores are notably extreme in the published data.

The two defining personas, [Anna](persona_anna.md) and [Marek](persona_marek.md),
are archetypes, not historical individuals: composites that carry the tension
between the older generation (rural Catholic faith, the parish, the pilgrimage,
folk song, the mother tongue) and the younger one (the post-2004 emigration and
the pull of Czech and global English on a small language).
