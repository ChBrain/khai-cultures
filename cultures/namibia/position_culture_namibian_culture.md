# Position: Namibian culture

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

Membership in a young nation, free only since 1990, set on the oldest desert on earth. It carries [the desert](position_the_desert.md), which taught patience and the making do with little, it carries [the many peoples](position_the_many_peoples.md), the Ovambo, Herero, Himba, Nama, Damara, San, the river folk of the Caprivi, the Baster of Rehoboth and the German and Afrikaans speaking communities held equal under one law, and it carries [conservation](position_conservation.md), the bargain written into the Constitution that gave the wild back to the people who share the ground with it. Its public tongue is [Namibian English](position_language_en_na.md), carried alongside the many home tongues.

## Orders

That a person keep the tongue, the dress and the way of their own people and still count as Namibian in the keeping, not in the giving up. That a stranger met on an empty road be greeted, for out here people are scarce and owed a hello. That the wild be guarded as a trust the community gains from and not a fence the state throws up against the local. And that the space, the silence and the patience the land breeds be carried into every dealing, at the braai as much as at the council table.

## Loses

Ground when the old lines between the peoples, though laid to rest under one law, are let harden again, one favoured and another forgotten. Ground when the patience the desert teaches curdles into mere endurance, the drought that will not break and the fence the elephant breaks along with it. And ground when the wild is romanced from far off as a postcard while the people who actually share the ground with it, and the cost the bargain lays on them, are forgotten.

## Drives

Lives in the elder who held the many peoples to one cause with a steady patient voice, the printmaker who cut their faces into an image the world could read, the poet who put the dry land's longing on the page, and the ranger who walks the veld at dawn keeping the community's wild in trust. It drives whoever is raised in it toward an unhurried self-reliance, a readiness to share what there is, and the conviction that a country of many peoples and little rain can still be held, patiently, as one.
