# Persona: the conservancy ranger

## Taxonomy

[conservation](position_conservation.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A man or woman of a communal conservancy out in the Kunene or the north-west, who walks the veld at first light counting spoor, logging the elephant and the rhino, and keeping watch over the game their own community holds in trust. Hardy, patient, and quietly proud, raised to the land and now paid by it, this is the new Namibian: the herder turned game guard, the one who learned that the wild is worth more kept than killed, and who carries the conservancy's bargain in a notebook and a pair of worn boots. The mother tongue, Khoekhoegowab or Otjiherero or Oshiwambo, is the language of the work itself: in it the ranger [reads the land and names every track](process_reading_deciphered.md), [speaks the count to the elders](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), and [hears the old knowledge of the animals](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) handed down. The shared [Namibian English](position_language_en_na.md) of the report and the tourist lodge is [carried as a working tongue](process_speaking_carried.md), the language the data is written in and the visitor met in. The deepest reading of the country, the spoor in the sand, the lion's mood, the coming of the rain, is done in the mother tongue and in no other, so that the gap at the limit of the tongue is the gap between what the land says to one born to it and what any report in English can ever hold. The ranger holds [Namibian culture](position_culture_namibian_culture.md) too, for the trust kept over the veld each dawn is the same conservation that culture carries as its bargain with the wild.

## Action

In [the conservation nation](plot_04_conservation_nation.md) the ranger is the living proof of the bargain Namibia struck, the community member who guards the wild because the wild now pays the community, the game brought back across the veld by the very people who once had no stake in it. By keeping the count and the watch, the ranger carries [conservation](position_conservation.md) from a clause in the Constitution to a thing done on the ground each dawn.

## Shadow

The bargain that pays the community to keep the wild can fray when the drought bites and the elephant breaks the fence and eats the maize, when the lion takes the goats, and the patience of folk who must live beside the animals wears thin. The cost of holding the wild in trust falls heaviest on the ones who share the land with it, and the ranger stands in the middle, between the neighbour's loss and the creature's worth.

## Tell

The ranger reads a whole night's traffic off a stretch of sand at dawn, names every animal that crossed it in the mother tongue, and writes the count up in English for a report read far away.
