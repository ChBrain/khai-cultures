# Piece: the welwitschia

## Taxonomy

Parent group: pieces

## Owner

## Place

Out on the gravel plains of the Namib, low and ragged on the bare ground, the welwitschia keeps its slow watch: a strange ancient plant found almost nowhere else on earth, two leaves only, splitting and fraying with the centuries into a tangled heap, some of them older than a thousand years. It is on the coat of arms and in the heart of every Namibian who has stood before one, the living emblem of a country that endures the dry by patience, and it gives its name to the nation's own honour.

## Load Bearing

It stands for the country's deepest truth: that you live in the desert not by fighting it but by patience, taking the little there is and holding on across the long dry years. A plant that draws the fog off the sea and outlasts a thousand summers on ground that would kill anything in a hurry is the very picture of the Namibian temper, hardy, unhurried, content with little, and made to last. In a land of so much space and so little water, the welwitschia is the proof that endurance is its own kind of strength, and the nation took it for its own. Without this old survivor the country would lack its plainest sign that to wait well is to live.

## Apparent

A low untidy heap of two long leaves gone ragged and grey-green, sprawled on bare gravel, easy for a stranger to pass without a glance, looking half dead. In itself it is an odd desert plant; its whole weight is in its age and its patience, in the thousand years the unremarkable thing has quietly held the ground while everything quicker came and went.
