# Persona: Mvula ya Nangolo

## Taxonomy

[the desert](position_the_desert.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The poet and journalist counted among the country's own makers of verse, the man who gave the young nation's feeling words and put the dry land and its longing onto the page. Thoughtful, rooted, and quietly proud, he wrote of the land and the people and the hope of a country coming into itself, and he worked the radio and the newsroom too, so that the word reached the far places where the paper never went. His mother tongue Oshiwambo runs under everything he made: he [speaks it](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [hears in it](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) the cadence and the proverb that shape his lines, the rhythm of home beneath the words. The shared [Namibian English](position_language_en_na.md) of the poems he [writes as his crafted tongue](process_writing_drafted.md), the public language in which the nation could read itself, and [reads its poets to follow them](process_reading_followed.md) and answer them in his own voice. He wrote his country's verse in a tongue learned and not given, while the proverbs that fed it lived in the one he was born to, so that the gap at the limit of the tongue is the very seam he works, carrying the home language into the public one line by line. He holds [Namibian culture](position_culture_namibian_culture.md) too, for the dry land and its longing that he set into verse are the desert that culture carries as its own teaching.

## Action

In [the gathering of the peoples](plot_02_the_peoples.md) he gives the land and its many peoples a voice in verse, naming the dry country and its longing and its coming hope, so that a people learn to read their own feeling on the page. By turning the land into language he hands the nation a literature of its own, the desert and the open veld set down in words.

## Shadow

The poet who must write in the public tongue to be read carries the ache of the home tongue thinning underneath, the proverb that does not quite cross over, the feeling that arrives in translation a half-step from itself. The cost of being heard widely is the small constant loss at the seam between the language of the heart and the language of the page.

## Tell

He carries the proverb of his mother tongue into the English of the poem, so that the line reads in one language and remembers in another.
