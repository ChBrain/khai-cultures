# Play: Namibia

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the house that holds this production and answers for it.

## Name

Namibia, a wide and dry country in the south-west of the continent, between the Atlantic on the one side and the Kalahari on the other, with Angola above the Kunene, Botswana across the sand, and the great Orange River marking off South Africa down below. ISO NA, language en, with Oshiwambo, Afrikaans, Khoekhoegowab, Otjiherero, the Caprivi tongues and German all spoken in the homes and on the streets. The driest country south of the Sahara, and one of the emptiest on earth: more space than people, and the light comes down on it like a blessing.

## Arc

A land older than memory, named for the desert that runs the whole length of its coast, the Namib, reckoned the oldest desert on earth, where the red dunes of Sossusvlei stand against a hard blue sky and the sand meets the cold Atlantic at the Skeleton Coast. Onto this great dry stage the peoples came and stayed, each holding fast to its own: the Ovambo of the north where the rivers run, the Herero in the long Victorian dress they made their own, the Himba red with otjize ochre, the Nama and the Damara, the San who read these lands first, the river peoples of the Caprivi, the Baster of Rehoboth, and the German- and Afrikaans-speaking communities, every one of them counted equal under the one law. It is a young nation, free since 1990, and a sober pride it is, the flag run up at last over Windhoek. And it did a thing no country had done before: it wrote the care of the land and the wild into its very Constitution, so that the people themselves, in their own conservancies, hold the elephant and the rhino and the lion as a trust, and the game has come back across the open veld. Etosha shimmers white at its heart, the welwitschia and the quiver tree keep their old patience in the rock, and under it all runs one nerve: a quiet, hardy, unhurried people, used to space and silence, who reckon the land is to be shared, the wild to be kept, and the many to be held as one.

## Triggers

**[Plot 1: the first readers of the land](plot_01_first_peoples.md)**

Long before any line was drawn, the San read [the desert](position_the_desert.md), tracking game across the sand and leaving their hand upon the rock at places like Twyfelfontein, the first to know how to live light on a hard and beautiful land.

**[Plot 2: the gathering of the peoples](plot_02_the_peoples.md)**

Over the long centuries the nations settled the land, the Nama and Damara, the Herero and Ovambo and Himba and the river folk, each keeping its tongue and its way, so that [the many peoples](position_the_many_peoples.md) came to share one dry and open country.

**[Plot 3: independence (1990)](plot_03_independence_1990.md)**

In 1990 the flag was run up over [Windhoek](place_windhoek.md) and Namibia took its place among the free nations, soberly and at last, one people of many peoples under one law.

**[Plot 4: the conservation nation](plot_04_conservation_nation.md)**

The young republic wrote [conservation](position_conservation.md) into its Constitution and gave the wild back to the people in their own conservancies, so the game returned to the open veld and the country became known the world over for keeping its wild.

## Stakes

Whether a young and thinly peopled nation, the driest in the region, can hold its many peoples as one in equal dignity without letting the old lines harden; whether it can keep the bargain of [conservation](position_conservation.md) it struck, so the land feeds the people and the people keep the wild, when drought bites and the world presses in; and whether it can stay true to the unhurried, sharing temper [the desert](position_the_desert.md) taught it, keeping the space and the silence and the light that make it what it is, or whether the press of want and the pull of the wider world wear that quiet down.
