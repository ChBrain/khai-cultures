# Position: the many peoples

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The truth that Namibia is many peoples held as one: the Ovambo of the well-watered north, the Herero in the long Victorian dress they made wholly their own, the Himba red with otjize ochre keeping the old herding life, the Nama and the Damara, the San who read the land first, the river peoples of the Caprivi, the Baster of Rehoboth, and the German- and Afrikaans-speaking communities, every one counted equal under one law. It is a country of many tongues and many ways, where Oshiwambo and Afrikaans and Khoekhoegowab and Otjiherero and German are all at home, and where the braided speech of the braai and the township is the everyday sound of the place. None is asked to stop being what it is to be Namibian; the nation is the many, held together.

## Orders

That every people is owed equal dignity under the one law, the old lines laid to rest and none set above another; that a person keeps the tongue and the dress and the way of their own people and is Namibian in the keeping, not in the giving up; and that the courtesy of the country runs to all of them alike, so you ask after a person's people before their business and take a stranger as a neighbour you have not met yet.

## Loses

Ground when the old lines, though laid to rest under one law, are let harden again, when one people is favoured or another forgotten, and the equal dignity sworn to on paper is not kept up by hand; and ground when the many is mistaken for division rather than richness, the differences read as a crack to be papered over instead of a wealth to be held, so that the work of holding the many as one is let slide.

## Drives

Lives in the elder statesman who held the many peoples to one cause with a steady patient voice; in the printmaker who cut their faces and their stories into prints the world came to know; and in the musician who braided their tongues into one beat the whole country owns. It drives Namibia to hold its many peoples as one in equal dignity, the one nation made of many and the stronger for it.
