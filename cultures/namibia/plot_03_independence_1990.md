## Cue

After a long road the day came at last, the answer to all the patient years of asking that elders like [Hosea Kutako](persona_kutako.md) had given their lives to and not lived to see. In the capital the country made ready to take its own place among the free nations of the world, soberly and with a quiet pride, one people of many peoples.

## Action

At midnight on the twenty-first of March, 1990, in [Windhoek](place_windhoek.md), the new flag of Namibia was run up the pole and the country took its place among the free nations. The land gave itself a Constitution that held every people equal under one law, that bound the many tongues and ways into one nation without asking any to be given up, and that wrote, alone in the world, the care of the land and its wild into the founding charter. It was a day met not with noise but with the deep steady gladness of a patient people: the many peoples one nation at last, the equal dignity of each set down in law, and the long waiting answered.

## Tension

A nation born soberly and at last must carry its hard-won freedom as a trust and not a trophy, holding its many peoples as one in the equal dignity the Constitution swears, and keeping the patience and the sharing that the dry land taught. The flag is raised; the work of being one nation of many peoples, kept up by hand under one law, is only just begun.
