# Position: conservation

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The bargain Namibia struck with its own wild: the country wrote the care of the land and its creatures into its Constitution, the first in the world to do so, and then gave the wild back to the people, letting rural communities form their own conservancies and hold the game on their land as a trust they manage and gain from. Etosha shimmers white at the heart of it, and out on the communal lands the elephant, the rhino, the lion and the great herds have come back across the open veld, guarded now by the very people who share the ground with them. It is the conviction that the wild is worth more kept than killed, and that the surest keeper of an animal is the community that profits from its living.

## Orders

That the wild is a trust and not a spoil, to be kept for the ones who come after; that the people who live beside the game should be the ones who guard it and the ones who gain from it, so that conservation is not a fence the state throws up against the local but a bargain the local is party to; and that the land and its creatures are part of the nation's wealth and its name in the world, to be tended with the same patience the desert taught.

## Loses

Ground when the bargain frays, when the drought bites and the elephant breaks the fence and the lion takes the stock, and the cost of living beside the wild falls heavier on the community than the gain; and ground when the wild is romanced from far off as a postcard while the people who actually share the ground with it are forgotten, or when poaching and the press of want outrun the watch the conservancies can keep.

## Drives

Lives in [the conservancy ranger](persona_omukwetu.md), who walks the veld at dawn counting spoor and keeps the community's wild in trust, the herder turned game guard who carries the bargain in a notebook and a pair of worn boots. It drives Namibia to keep its wild by keeping faith with its people, the land feeding the people and the people keeping the land, a thing held slowly and so held well.
