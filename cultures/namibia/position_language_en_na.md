# Position: Namibian English

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The authority of the shared public tongue of Namibia, the English made the one official language at independence and kept as the neutral ground on which the many peoples meet. It holds no single people's mother tongue and belongs to none of them by birth, and that is its whole point: it is the borrowed common speech in which Oshiwambo and Afrikaans and Otjiherero and Khoekhoegowab speakers can stand on level ground, the language of the courts, the schools, the reports and the charts, chosen precisely because it was nobody's first and so could be everybody's second.

## Orders

What counts as the public language of the land, the tongue of the classroom and the statute, the newsroom and the lodge, the report written up for a reader far away. It orders which words go on the record and cross to the wider world, and it sets the register in which a nation of many mother tongues does its shared and official business, so that a country braided of many peoples speaks with one public voice.

## Loses

Ground to the mother tongues that carry the heart, for the proverb and the prayer and the count of the spoor live in Oshiwambo or Otjiherero or Khoekhoegowab and not in the borrowed public speech, and the feeling arrives in the English a half-step from itself. It loses too to the braided street talk of the location, where the tongues switch mid-sentence and no clean English holds, and to Afrikaans, still the everyday lingua franca of many a town and bakkie long after English took the official seat.

## Drives

It drives Namibia to meet as one across its many tongues, giving each people a shared ground that asks none to give up its own. It is carried as borrowed coin by [Hosea Kutako](persona_kutako.md), who sent the nation's case across the sea in it; lettered by hand into the prints of [John Muafangejo](persona_muafangejo.md); worked as the crafted public tongue of [Mvula ya Nangolo](persona_nangolo.md), who wrote the country's verse in it; borrowed for a line by [Gazza](persona_gazza.md), who folds it into the home mix on the beat; and carried as the working tongue of the report by [the conservancy ranger](persona_omukwetu.md), who reads the land in the mother tongue and writes the count in this one. So the borrowed common speech shows who leans on it to be heard and who keeps the heart in a tongue of their own.
