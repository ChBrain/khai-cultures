# Place: the Namib

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Shown

The desert that gives the country its name, reckoned the oldest on earth, running the whole length of the coast: the great red dunes of Sossusvlei rising over the white cracked clay of Deadvlei with its black dead camel-thorn trees, the highest sand in the world catching fire at dawn, and away to the north the Skeleton Coast where the cold Atlantic fog rolls in over the sand and the bones of old wrecks. The welwitschia keeps its thousand-year patience on the gravel plains, the quiver tree holds the ridge, and over all of it comes the hard clean light and a silence you can hear.

## Offers

The space and the silence and the light that are the country's deepest wealth, a place that teaches patience and a light tread to whoever would live on it; the oldest desert on earth as a thing to stand small before, where the dunes and the fog-coast and the ancient plants keep a time longer than any nation, and where the emptiness is not a lack but a gift.

## Withheld

The easy life and the ready water. The Namib gives nothing for free and forgives no carelessness; it is beautiful and merciless at once, killing the unprepared and rewarding only the patient, the driest face of the driest country, a stage so grand it can be romanced from far off while the hardness of living on it falls all on the ones who must.
