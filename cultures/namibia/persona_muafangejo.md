# Persona: John Muafangejo

## Taxonomy

[the many peoples](position_the_many_peoples.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The printmaker from the north who cut the life of his land into linoleum, the artist who gave Namibia a face the world could read, and whose bold black-and-white prints carry the people, the cattle, the missions and the Bible stories of his country into galleries far away. Warm, devout, and watchful, he set down what he saw with a plain truth and a child's clear eye, and he wrote his own words right into the block, captions in his own hand running round the figures. He grew up speaking Oshikwanyama, the Oshiwambo of his home, and in it he [thought and felt](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) and [heard the stories](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) that filled his prints. The shared [Namibian English](position_language_en_na.md) he learned at the mission he [carried as a working tongue](process_speaking_carried.md) and [wrote into the very surface of his art](process_writing_drafted.md), lettering it by hand around the images, plain and unschooled and all his own. He told his land in pictures because the pictures crossed every tongue at once, and lettered them in an English not born to him, so that the gap at the limit of the tongue he closed not in speech but in the cut line, where the image says what no single language could carry alone.

## Action

In [the gathering of the peoples](plot_02_the_peoples.md) he gives the many peoples of the land a likeness and a record, cutting their faces and their stories into prints that travel the world, so that a country little known abroad is seen and known through his hand. By making the art of his land its ambassador, he carries the many into the one image the world remembers.

## Shadow

The gift that let the world see his country burned hard and went early: he worked at a great heat and died young, his vision larger than the years he was given to fill it. The cost of giving a land its face so fast is the strain it lays on the one hand doing the cutting, spent before its time.

## Tell

He cut his people, his cattle and his Scripture into the block and lettered the meaning by hand around them, so that whoever cannot read his tongue can still read his land.
