# Process: the conservancy

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

A rural community that comes together to take its own land's wild into its own hands: the people of a stretch of communal ground who agree to manage the game between them, register their conservancy, set its bounds, choose their committee, and put their own out on the veld to keep the count and the watch. It begins not in a far capital but in a meeting under a tree, the community deciding to hold the wild as a trust and gain from its keeping.

## Direction

Toward the keeping of the wild by the people who live with it: the conservancy turns the elephant and the rhino and the great herds from a pest and a danger into a wealth the community owns, paying for them to live by drawing the world to come and see them. It is the bargain made daily on the ground, the game guard walking the dawn veld, the count written up, the benefit shared out, the slow patient work of holding the land and its creatures as a trust.

## Lever

It is driven by the Namibian conviction that the surest keeper of an animal is the community that profits from its living, and by the founding charter that gave rural people the right to hold their own wild. It holds against poaching and the press of want; against it stand the drought and the broken fence and the lion at the kraal, the hard truth that the cost of the bargain falls heaviest on the ones nearest the game.

## Echo

The conservancy makes plain what drives [conservation](position_conservation.md): that the wild is kept by keeping faith with the people, the land feeding the people and the people keeping the land. Out on this veld [the conservancy ranger](persona_omukwetu.md) walks the dawn count and carries the bargain from a clause in the Constitution to a thing done by hand each morning.
