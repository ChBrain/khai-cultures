# Persona: Gazza

## Taxonomy

[the many peoples](position_the_many_peoples.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The kwaito star out of Windhoek who made the country dance and gave the young their own sound, one of Namibia's biggest names in music, a hitmaker and a businessman who built a label and an empire of his own from the township beat. Loud, warm, sharp and proud of home, he sings the streets and the parties and the everyday of a young free nation, and he does it on purpose in the people's own mix of tongues, so that the song belongs to nobody but here. He grew up speaking Oshiwambo and the street Afrikaans of the location, and it is in that mother-tongue mix that he [thinks the hook](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) and [carries the rhythm of the talk around him](process_speaking_carried.md). The English of the wider charts he [borrows for a line where it lands](process_speaking_borrowed.md), folding it into the verse, but the heart of the song stays in the home tongues that the dancefloor answers to. He sings in the people's own braided speech, switching tongue mid-bar the way the township talks, so that the gap at the limit of the tongue he does not bridge but plays, making the very mixing of the languages the sound of the place.

## Action

In [the conservation nation](plot_04_conservation_nation.md) he gives the young free country its own popular voice, a homegrown sound that holds its own against the music of bigger places, so that Namibians dance to themselves and not only to imports. By making the local mix a hit, he carries the many peoples into one beat the whole country owns.

## Shadow

The star who sings the streets so loud can be mistaken for noise, the homegrown sound dismissed as less than the polished imports it stands beside, and the businessman in him second-guessed as if pride and profit could not also be art. The cost of building it all yourself, on your own terms and in your own tongues, is the doubt that follows the self-made man who would not wait to be let in.

## Tell

He switches from Oshiwambo to Afrikaans to English inside a single bar, the way the location actually talks, and the whole country sings it back word for word.
