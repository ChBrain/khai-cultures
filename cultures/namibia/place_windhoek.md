# Place: Windhoek

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Shown

The capital set high on the central plateau, a small clean city ringed by dry hills, where the air is thin and the light is hard and the country's many peoples meet and mix. Low buildings and wide quiet streets, the jacarandas in flower, the old church on the rise and the new towers of a young state, the markets where Oshiwambo and Afrikaans and German and Khoekhoegowab braid together in a single breath. It is the seat of the republic and its meeting-ground, the one place where the whole nation gathers, unhurried even in its busyness.

## Offers

A meeting-place where the many peoples of the country come together under one law, where the nation governs itself and shows itself to itself; a small capital with the manners of a big village, where you still greet a stranger and ask after his people, and where the braided speech of the whole land can be heard in a single street.

## Withheld

The crowding and the hurry of a great metropolis. Windhoek stays small and dry and far from the sea, a capital that must hold a whole nation's many peoples in one place and keep the equal dignity sworn at its founding, a thin-aired city that knows the drought and the long roads out, and never lets the nation forget how much space and how few people lie beyond its hills.
