# Persona: Hosea Kutako

## Taxonomy

[the many peoples](position_the_many_peoples.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The old Herero chief who carried the cause of his people, and through them of the whole land, to the wide world with nothing but patience and a steady voice, the elder whom Namibians remember as a father of the nation. A teacher and a leader, dignified, unhurried, and immovable, he held that a people robbed of their say must still be heard, and he sent the country's case abroad, petition after patient petition, for decades he did not live to see answered. He spoke, thought and prayed in Otjiherero, his mother tongue, the language in which he [held his counsel](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [framed his case to his own people](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md). The English and the Afrikaans of those who ruled he learned to use as a tool, [carrying them as borrowed coin](process_speaking_borrowed.md) so that the wider world might [follow what he wrote](process_reading_followed.md) and the petitions cross the sea in a tongue the powerful would read. He pleaded the cause of his people in languages not his own, so that the world might hear, and prayed for them in the only one that was, so that the gap at the limit of the tongue ran between the words that carried his case and the words that carried his heart.

## Action

In [the gathering of the peoples](plot_02_the_peoples.md) he stands for the dignity of a people who would not be erased, and through them for all the peoples of the land, keeping alive across long decades the claim that they were one country owed its own voice. By his patient witness he hands the nation the example of a leader who sought to be heard rather than to strike, and who held the many to the hope of one.

## Shadow

The patience that was his strength was bought with a long waiting that outran his own life: he sent his case to the world and did not live to see the answer come. The cost of choosing the steady word over the quick blow is the years it takes, and the elders who carry the cause and lie down before the morning they worked for breaks.

## Tell

He kept faith for decades that a steady voice would be heard at last, and held the many peoples to one cause without ever raising it, so that the nation counts him a father though he never saw it born.
