# Plot: the conservation nation

## Taxonomy

[play_namibia](play_namibia.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

The young free republic, with its founding charter already the first in the world to write the care of the land into a Constitution, set out to make the words true on the ground. The question was how a thin and dry country could keep its wild when the people who shared the land with the game had no stake in its living, and every reason to count it a pest.

## Action

Namibia answered with a bargain no country had tried at this scale: it let rural communities form their own conservancies and gave them the right to hold and gain from the game on their own land. The wild became the community's trust and the community's wealth, guarded now by the very people who shared the ground with it, watched over by game guards like [the conservancy ranger](persona_omukwetu.md) walking the veld at dawn. And the game came back: the elephant and the desert-adapted rhino and the lion and the great herds returned across the open veld, the country becoming known the world over for keeping its wild, while the young nation found its own voice in everything from the verse on the page to the kwaito beat that [Gazza](persona_gazza.md) made the streets dance to. The land fed the people and the people kept the land.

## Stage

- [the conservancy ranger](persona_omukwetu.md): the community game guard who keeps the wild in trust and carries the bargain on the ground.
- [Gazza](persona_gazza.md): the musician whose homegrown sound gave the young free nation its own popular voice.
- [conservation](position_conservation.md): the bargain that gave the wild back to the people to guard and gain from.
- [Windhoek](place_windhoek.md): the capital from which the young republic governs the trust it set itself.

## Tension

A bargain that pays the community to keep the wild must hold when the drought bites, when the elephant breaks the fence and the lion takes the stock, and the cost of living beside the game falls heaviest on the ones who share its ground. Whether the country can keep faith with both the people and the wild, the land feeding the one and the one keeping the land, is the standing test of the conservation nation, a thing held slowly and so far held well.
