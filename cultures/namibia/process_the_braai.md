# Process: the braai

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

Anyone with a fire, some meat, and people to share it: a family at the weekend, neighbours of an evening, friends marking anything or nothing at all. The wood is lit and left to coal down, the boerewors and the chops and the biltong come out, and folk gather around the flames as the light goes long and the cool comes in. No invitation is needed beyond the smell of the smoke, and a passer-by is waved over without a second thought.

## Direction

Toward the holding together of the many: the braai is the one fire where the whole country meets, where Oshiwambo and Afrikaans and English and German braid together over the coals and a person is asked after his people before his business. It is not just the cooking of meat but the slow unhurried gathering itself, the long sitting and the easy talk, the sharing of what there is, the desert's lesson of patience and plenty-from-little turned into an evening around a flame.

## Lever

It is driven by the deep Namibian conviction that food is to be shared and time is not to be rushed, and by the open land that taught a scattered people to make much of the rare gathering. It holds against the long distances and the busyness of the week; against it stands only the drought that can thin the table, and the hurry of the wider world that would have folk eat fast and alone.

## Echo

The braai makes audible what drives [the many peoples](position_the_many_peoples.md): that the nation is held together not by sameness but by the sharing of one fire, the many tongues over one set of coals. Around this flame [the conservancy ranger](persona_omukwetu.md) and the neighbour and the stranger sit down as one, the country's many peoples made one company for the length of an evening.
