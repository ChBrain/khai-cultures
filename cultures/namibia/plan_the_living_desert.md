# Plan: the living desert

## Taxonomy

The open mandate the young nation set itself: to keep [the desert](position_the_desert.md) and its space and silence as a living wealth and not a wasteland, to hold [the many peoples](position_the_many_peoples.md) as one in equal dignity under one law, and to keep faith with the bargain of [conservation](position_conservation.md) so the land feeds the people and the people keep the wild.

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Direction

Hold the many peoples as one, keeping the equal dignity of each tongue and way and never letting the old lines harden again; keep the conservation bargain whole, so the community that lives beside the wild both guards it and gains from it, even when the drought bites and the cost falls heavy; and keep the unhurried, sharing temper the dry land taught, guarding the space and the silence and the light that make the country what it is, against the press of want and the hurry of the wider world.

## Orders

- To the citizen like [the conservancy ranger](persona_omukwetu.md): keep the count and the watch, hold the wild in trust for the ones who come after, and carry the bargain on the ground each dawn.
- To the makers like [Gazza](persona_gazza.md) and [Mvula ya Nangolo](persona_nangolo.md): give the many peoples their own voice, in the verse and the beat that the whole young nation owns.
- To the nation: hold the many as one, keep faith with both the people and the wild, and keep the patience and the sharing the desert taught.

## Implementation

A young republic, free since 1990, that wrote conservation into the first such Constitution in the world and gave the wild back to its people in their own conservancies, so the game has returned across the open veld and the country is known the world over for keeping its wild. It holds its many peoples as one under one equal law and keeps the unhurried sharing temper the dry land bred, while the standing tests remain: the drought, the cost of living beside the game, and the work, never finished, of holding the many in equal dignity.
