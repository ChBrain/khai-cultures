# Plot: the first readers of the land

## Owner

## Cue

Long before any line was drawn on any map, the dry land lay open under its hard clean light, the oldest desert on earth running the length of the coast. Into it came the San, the first people, who learned to live light on a country that gives nothing for free, and who read the sand and the sky as others read a page.

## Action

The San tracked the game across the veld and knew where the water hid, living by what the land would give and taking no more, the first to master a place that would defeat the careless. And they left their mark upon the rock: at Twyfelfontein and a thousand other faces of stone they engraved and painted the eland and the giraffe and the dancing figures, one of the great galleries of rock art on earth, the oldest record of the country, the first reading of the land set down in the land itself. The patience and the sharing and the light tread that the desert teaches were learned here first, and they run under the whole nation still.

## Tension

A land beautiful and merciless at once, that destroys the careless and rewards the patient, teaches the first lesson the whole nation will keep: that you live light on the dry country, take no more than it gives, and read it well or not at all. The rock art endures where the people who cut it have thinned, the oldest voice of the land speaking still from the stone, a record older than every later claim upon the country.
