# Austria: a khai culture

Austria staged as a full khai play, ISO AT, language de. The same German tongue
as its northern neighbour, in a different key: a small Alpine country that was
once an empire and now draws its pride from form, music, and charm rather than
power. Its history is the plot line, its people the cast, the roles its language
encodes, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a
single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## Not Germany

Austria shares German with Germany but is emphatically its own culture: where the
German tone is direct and plain, the Austrian one is indirect and charming (the
Schmäh); where Germany prizes thoroughness, Austria prizes Gemütlichkeit; titles
are a courtesy rather than a hierarchy, and under the baroque surface runs the
wistfulness of a lost empire. The shared language separates them as much as it
joins them.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_austria.md](play_austria.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_at.md](pitch_at.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Austria's profile (indirect charm, low hierarchy yet high formality,
  Gemütlichkeit, the wistful empire) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** Maria Theresa's reforms, the Congress of Vienna 1815, fin-
  de-siècle Vienna around 1900, and the State Treaty of 1955 - each casting the
  elements it needs (the treaty casts a place and a piece, no persona).
- **Personas:** Maria Theresa, Metternich, and Freud as the historic cast, plus
  two defining archetypes, Grete (older, Kaffeehaus and titles) and Lukas
  (younger, global and ironic). Each is wired to the language engine by the
  language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** der Titel (the title), der Schmäh (the charming indirection),
  der Grant (the loving grumble).
- **Places:** Wien (the capital), Salzburg (baroque and Alpine).
- **Processes:** das Kaffeehaus, der Heurige.
- **Pieces:** die Sachertorte, der Walzer.
