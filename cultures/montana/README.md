# Montana: a khai culture

Montana staged as a full khai play, ISO US-MT, language en. The Treasure State: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its Little Bighorn battle, Butte copper mining boom, and Glacier National Park establishment encodes, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house that holds this culture and answers for it.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_montana.md](play_montana.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_montana.md](pitch_montana.md), the Montana key.
- **Plots (history):** the Battle of the Little Bighorn of 1876, the Butte Copper Boom of the 1880s, and the Glacier National Park Establishment of 1910.
- **Personas:** Clark (mine historian) and Cheyenne (Crow heritage coordinator), written in English.
- **Positions:** copper mining (mining engineering/copper industry pride/union solidarity), montana wilderness (alpine recreation/wildlife conservation/park protection).
- **Places:** Butte Mine, Glacier National Park.
- **Processes:** copper smelting (smelting copper ore), trail conservation (building mountain trails).
- **Pieces:** a copper ore, a pack saddle.
- **Plan:** the Clark Fork River restoration plan.
