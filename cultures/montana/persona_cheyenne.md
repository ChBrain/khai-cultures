# Persona: Cheyenne

## Taxonomy

[montana wilderness](position_montana_wilderness.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

She's fifty-two, a tribal coordinator working out of Crow Agency, and she's outlasted more mine bosses' promises than she cares to count. Prairie-drawl English is her mother tongue, the one she [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and writes her preservation reports in, day after day. It's [Montanan English](position_language_en_us_mt.md) the way Crow Agency speaks it - unhurried, drawn out at the edges, built for a long conversation, not a short one. She carries no other language. The fast, screen-lit talk of mine historians like Clark, and the boardroom push to move more copper faster, reaches her [like something happening a long way off](process_hearing_followed.md), and she's not convinced the river's own slow, treaty-bound recovery ought to wait its turn behind an export schedule. She belongs to [the Treasure State](position_culture_treasure_state.md) as the one holding the claim that was on this ground before any shaft went down - and she intends to keep holding it.

## Action

She keeps [pack saddle](piece_pack_saddle.md) records in order, runs field conservation work under [the Clark Fork plan](plan_clark_fork_river_restoration.md), and keeps a close eye on [copper mining](position_copper_mining.md) besides.

## Shadow

Her pride in tribal sovereignty and the right to run her own land her own way makes her quick to distrust any state zoning rule or environmental mandate handed down from Helena, however well meant, for what it might end up costing a family on its own ground.

## Tell

She runs a hand over an old pack saddle and says it without raising her voice: "The river's got its own treaties, same as we do. You don't restore a watershed by watching a production spreadsheet."
