# Mauritania: References

The provenance behind this culture: the tone that tunes the pitch (kept here,
never in the prose) and the public-domain historical and cultural sources behind
the plots, personas, places, processes, and pieces.

## The pitch

The pitch [pitch_ar.md](pitch_ar.md) is the tone the whole play is performed in,
not a layer of data. It is written from Mauritania's own bearing - an unhurried
patience and gravity, a quiet desert dignity, a wealth measured in hospitality and
learning rather than money, and a steady care for memory against the encroaching
sand - and carries no number and names nothing it does not change. It is recorded
here so that the bearing behind the prose is on the record without entering it.

## Historical and cultural sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, the processes, and the
pieces are drawn from the public historical and cultural record. They are credited
here and never claimed; the staging is original work. The plurality of the land -
the Arab-Berber (Bidhan), the Haratin, and the Afro-Mauritanian peoples (the
Halpulaar, the Soninke, and the Wolof) - is staged soberly and with dignity,
taking no political side.

The Tea-Pourer ([persona_tea_pourer.md](persona_tea_pourer.md)) is an archetype,
not a historical individual: a composite of the tent who carries the patience,
hospitality, and Hassani inheritance of the badia, and whose three poured glasses

- bitter as life, sweet as love, gentle as death - hold the welcome that seats the
  stranger before it asks his name.
