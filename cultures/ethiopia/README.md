# Ethiopia: a khai culture

Ethiopia staged as a full khai play, ISO ET, language am (Amharic, written in the
Ge'ez / Fidel script). An ancient, never-fully-colonised civilisation, as a
theatre: its history as the plot line, its peoples as the cast, the roles its
faiths and language encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects,
all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_ethiopia.md](play_ethiopia.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_am.md](pitch_am.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Ethiopia's profile (deep respect for age and hierarchy, a collective and family
  centred life, patient endurance, and the warmth of hospitality) and naming no
  dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Kingdom of Aksum (the cradle of humankind, the
  obelisks, Ge'ez, and early Christianity), Lalibela and the early refuge (the
  rock-hewn churches, and the early home of Islam at Negash alongside the Beta
  Israel), the Victory of Adwa (1896, the victory that kept the nation free), and
  the modern nation (Addis Ababa as the seat of African diplomacy, the runner,
  and Ethio-jazz) - each casting the elements it needs.
- **Personas:** Saint Yared (the ancient composer of Ge'ez chant), Emperor
  Menelik II (handled soberly, as national pride), and Abebe Bikila and Mulatu
  Astatke as the modern cast, plus one archetype, the coffee host (the keeper of
  the coffee ceremony and of hospitality). Each is wired to the language engine by
  the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** faith (the Orthodox Tewahedo Church and Ge'ez, Islam, and the
  Beta Israel, all held with dignity), freedom (the never-conquered land and the
  pride of Adwa), plurality (the many peoples - Amhara, Oromo, Tigray, Sidama,
  Somali, and dozens more - held in equal dignity, many as one).
- **Places:** Aksum (the ancient kingdom, the obelisks, and Ge'ez), Addis Ababa
  (the capital and the seat of African diplomacy).
- **Processes:** the coffee ceremony (buna, the rite of hospitality and the home
  of coffee), Timkat and Meskel (the great festivals of the unique 13-month
  calendar).
- **Pieces:** injera (the shared bread and the common mesob), the Fidel (the
  Ge'ez-born syllabary, the nation's own script).
