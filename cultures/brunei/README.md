# Brunei: a khai culture

Brunei Darussalam staged as a full khai play, ISO BN, language ms (Malay). A
small, enduring sultanate on the rivers of Borneo, the abode of peace, as a
theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its
language and faith encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects,
all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_brunei.md](play_brunei.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_ms.md](pitch_ms.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Brunei's
  profile (a calm respect for hierarchy and the throne, a strong sense of
  belonging to family and community, an emphasis on courtesy and restraint, and a
  quiet contentment) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the founding of the Sultanate, the golden age under Sultan
  Bolkiah, modern Brunei under Sultan Omar Ali Saifuddien III, and independence in
  1984, each casting the elements it needs and staged soberly on the public
  record.
- **Personas:** Sultan Bolkiah and Sultan Omar Ali Saifuddien III as the historic
  royal cast, the poet and statesman Yura Halim who gave the nation its anthem,
  the novelist Muslim Burmat who set the life of the river and the people into
  Malay prose, and one archetype, the Kampong Ayer boatman who keeps the oldest
  way of life on the water. Each is wired to the language engine by the
  language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** Melayu Islam Beraja (Malay Islamic Monarchy, the national
  philosophy of three woven strands), Darussalam (the abode of peace, the nation's
  name and self-understanding), and Kampong Ayer (the water village, the oldest
  root of the people).
- **Places:** Bandar Seri Begawan (the capital, the mosque mirrored in the water,
  and the seat of faith and state), Kampong Ayer (the historic water village on
  the Brunei River).
- **Processes:** mengaji Quran (learning to recite the Quran, faith passed to each
  child), menenun songket (weaving songket, heritage and craft passed down).
- **Pieces:** songket (the gold-threaded ceremonial cloth of Malay refinement),
  the mosque (the landmark of national identity and the sign of Islam at the
  center of national life).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Brunei is staged from within its own
self-understanding, on the public record, taking no political side; the monarchy
is treated with respect as the nation's central enduring institution and as
heritage. The staging keeps to enduring culture, faith, the river, the
rainforest, and the people. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the staging and the architecture are original work.
