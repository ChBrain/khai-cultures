# Brunei: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_ms.md](pitch_ms.md) is written from Brunei's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
Brunei is not always listed individually in the published comparison; the profile
is read from Hofstede's findings for the region (notably Malaysia and the
neighbouring Malay-world societies, with which Brunei shares language, religion,
and social form) and from the country's own documented values. The figures below
are approximate and regional, recorded here so the basis is on the record without
entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Brunei / Malay-world profile (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | high                                   |
| Individualism                 | low (collectivist)                     |
| Motivation toward Achievement | moderate                               |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | moderate                               |
| Long Term Orientation         | moderate                               |
| Indulgence                    | restrained                             |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the six-dimensions model of national
culture and the Country Comparison tool). Values are approximate and read from the
Malay-world regional profile, as published by Hofstede Insights / The Culture
Factor Group; high power distance and strong collectivism are the most marked
features of the profile.

## Historical sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn
from the public historical record. They are credited here and never claimed; the
staging is original work. Brunei is staged from within its own self-understanding,
taking no political side; the monarchy is treated with respect as the nation's
central enduring institution and as heritage.

The one archetype persona, the [Kampong Ayer boatman](persona_tukang_sampan.md),
is not a historical individual but a composite that carries the oldest way of life
on the water: the river, the stilt house, the sampan, and the courtesy and faith
of the water village.
