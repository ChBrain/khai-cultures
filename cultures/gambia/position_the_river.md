# Position: the river

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The River Gambia, the one thing this country cannot be without, for we are the only nation in the world named for our river and shaped to its course. It runs from far inland out to the Atlantic, and the whole thin land follows it on both banks, so that wherever a Gambian stands, the river is near. It is road and field and table all at once: the highway before there were roads, the water for the rice, the home of the fish and the mangrove and the great gathering of birds that gives the Smiling Coast its wings. The river is not a feature of the country; the river is the country, and the people are the people of its banks.

## Orders

That this is a land read along its length and not its breadth, the upriver and the downriver more than the north and the south; that the river binds the many peoples of its banks into one nation, the Mandinka and Wolof and Fula and Jola and Serahule all the people of the same water; and that to know The Gambia you follow the river, for the kingdoms rose on it, the trade came up it, the sorrow went down it, and the life of the land still turns on it.

## Loses

Ground when the river is taken for granted as mere scenery, its mangroves cleared and its waters fished thin and its banks let go, the giver forgotten by those it feeds; and ground when the thin land along it is treated as small and of no account, as if a country measured by its width and not by the long living artery that is its true measure.

## Drives

Lives in the voice that stood up for the small land along the river and gave it a Gambian voice, and shapes the home of [Kunta Kinteh Island](place_kunta_kinteh_island.md), the island in the river's mouth that holds its hardest memory. It drives The Gambia to know itself as the nation of one river, gathered on its banks and carried by its water from the first kingdoms to the coast today.
