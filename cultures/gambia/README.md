# The Gambia: a khai culture

The Gambia staged as a full khai play, ISO GM, language en. The smallest nation
on the African mainland, shaped to its one river and held by its word and its
welcome, staged as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the
cast, the roles its language and faith encode, its places, its practices, and its
defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_gambia.md](play_gambia.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md), the Hofstede layer, written from The
  Gambia's profile (a warm, group-minded, unhurried and welcoming register, the
  person before the business and the elder held high) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the river kingdoms, the river of trade and sorrow, a
  Gambian voice, and the Smiling Coast - each casting the elements it needs, and
  each staging the river's memory honestly and soberly.
- **Personas:** Alhaji Bai Konte and Foday Musa Suso (kora masters of the griot
  lines), Kebba Kanji Fofana (the keeper whose carried Kinte oral history reached
  across the ocean), and Edward Francis Small (the political pioneer) as the
  historic cast, plus one archetype, the jali (the griot at every gathering,
  keeper of memory and welcome). Each is wired to the language engine by the
  language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** the river (the artery the nation is named for and gathered
  along), jaliya (the griot's craft, the history sung not written), teranga (the
  warmth and welcome of the Smiling Coast).
- **Places:** Banjul (the capital at the river's mouth), Kunta Kinteh Island (the
  island in the river that holds its hardest memory, with Juffureh on the bank).
- **Processes:** attaya (the slow three-round tea that keeps people sitting
  together), the naming ceremony (the new child taken in by the whole community).
- **Pieces:** the kora (the twenty-one-string harp-lute, the voice of jaliya),
  benachin (the one-pot rice shared from the common bowl).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the Atlantic slave trade and the river's memory are treated soberly on
the public record, with dignity and without atrocity detail; the staging and the
architecture are original work.
