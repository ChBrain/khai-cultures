# Persona: Kebba Kanji Fofana

## Taxonomy

[jaliya](position_jaliya.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The keeper of the word at Juffureh, the elder who held in his memory the long oral history of the Kinte family of that village, and who, when a searcher came across the ocean looking for the people his own family had been taken from, recited the line back through the generations and named the son who long ago was lost to the river. Grave, careful and steeped in jaliya, he carried what the page never recorded, the genealogy of a family kept alive in the mouth for two centuries, and gave it back to those it belonged to. Mandinka he holds whole and keeping, the only tongue the deep memory was ever held in: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md), and [hears](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) the lineage in his mother tongue, the one language the carried history lives in. What came to him from the searcher in English he met across an interpreter, the word [carried over](process_speaking_carried.md) from one tongue to another and the answer [followed back](process_hearing_followed.md) the same long way. The history he carried had to cross from Mandinka into [the English of the record and the wider world](position_language_en_gm.md) to reach the ones it was for, the memory passing hand to hand over the gap that waits at the limit of the tongue, and arriving home even so. He belongs to [the Smiling Coast](position_culture_smiling_coast.md) as the keeper whose carried word turned the river's hardest memory into a road back home.

## Action

In [the river of trade and sorrow](plot_02_the_river_trade.md) he is the keeper whose carried word reaches across the ocean, the griot whose memory of a Juffureh family answers a searcher's question and turns the river's hardest memory into a road home. He carries jaliya forward by proving the spoken word a true keeping, the oral history holding what no ledger ever wrote down.

## Shadow

The memory kept in one elder's mouth is precious and frail at once: held perfectly for generations, it can still be lost in a breath when the keeper goes, and what the griot carries the page cannot check, so the trust placed in the spoken word is the very thing that makes its loss complete.

## Tell

He sits before the searcher from across the water and recites the line of the Kinte going back and back, until he names the son who went to the river and never came home, and a family parted for two hundred years is joined again in a single sentence.
