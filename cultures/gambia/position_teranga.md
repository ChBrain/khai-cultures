# Position: teranga

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The warmth and welcome that is the Gambian way with a stranger, the open hand and the open door that earn us the name of the Smiling Coast. Teranga is not a manner put on for guests; it is how we are with one another and with whoever the road brings. It is the long greeting that comes before any business, salaam aleikum and the patient asking after the home and the body and the people of the house; it is the place kept at the bowl for one more, the bed found for the traveller, the readiness to share what little there is rather than send a guest away wanting. In a small and not a wealthy land, the welcome is the wealth, and the measure of a person is how they hold a stranger.

## Orders

That the person comes before the business and the greeting before the deal, so you do not rush past a man or get to the matter before you have asked after his people; that a guest is a blessing and a trust, to be fed and sheltered and never sent off empty, the bowl always stretching to one more; and that the worth of the small nation is shown not in its size but in its welcome, the smile of the coast a true thing the people live by.

## Loses

Ground when the welcome is given so freely on so little that it wears the giver thin and is taken for granted or not returned; and ground when teranga is turned into a slogan, sold to the visitor as a brand on a poster while the warmth behind it is hollowed out, the smile kept for the camera and the welcome made a thing for hire rather than a thing the people are.

## Drives

Lives in the griot, welcomed and honoured at every gathering and himself the keeper of the welcome in word and song, and breathes in [attaya](process_attaya.md), the slow three-round tea that keeps people sitting and talking together. It drives The Gambia to hold itself together by its welcome, the small land that meets the world with an open hand and reckons the stranger greeted well a stranger no longer.
