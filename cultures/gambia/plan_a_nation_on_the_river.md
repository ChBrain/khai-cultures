# Plan: a nation on the river

## Taxonomy

The open work the small land sets itself: to hold the many peoples of [the river](position_the_river.md) together as one nation by its word and its welcome and not its size; to carry [jaliya](position_jaliya.md) and the kora into a written and recorded age without thinning the old memory; to keep the river's hardest history honoured soberly and turned, where it can be, into a road home; and to keep [teranga](position_teranga.md) and the Smiling Coast a true thing the people live by, not only a name the world is sold.

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Direction

Hold the small nation together along its one river, the many peoples of its banks bound into one by the shared water, the word and the welcome; keep the sung history alive, honouring the jali and the keeping families so the deep memory is carried whole and not let thin; honour the river's sorrow with dignity, marking it soberly and keeping the road home open to those whose people it carried away; and keep the welcome real, teranga lived by the people and not hollowed into a slogan, as the small land meets a wider and harder world.

## Orders

- To the keeper like [the jali](persona_the_jali.md): hold the deep memory whole, train the young to the word and the kora, and keep the welcome in your song.
- To the pioneer's heirs after [Edward Francis Small](persona_edward_small.md): keep the Gambian voice heard and the small land's say in itself real, won in peace.
- To the nation: hold the many of the river together by the word and the welcome, honour the river's memory soberly, and keep teranga a thing the people are.

## Implementation

A small nation gathered along one river, the smallest on the mainland, that came to its own flag in peace and holds its many peoples together by a warmth the world underrates. It has kept its kora masters and its griot lines, marked the river's hardest history with dignity at Kunta Kinteh Island and Juffureh, and earned its name as the Smiling Coast, while the old sung memory thins where the young turn from the long apprenticeship, the welcome stretches far on little, and many of its sons must seek a way abroad the home cannot yet give.
