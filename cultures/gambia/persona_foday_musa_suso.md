# Persona: Foday Musa Suso

## Taxonomy

[jaliya](position_jaliya.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The kora master of the Suso griot line who took the Gambian kora the furthest of all, carrying the old strings into the wider world of music and sitting them down beside instruments they had never met, so that the song of the river kingdoms found new ears across the ocean. Rooted in jaliya and restless to share it, he held the tradition deeply and opened it boldly, sure that the kora was big enough to keep its own soul and still speak to the whole earth. Mandinka he holds whole and shaping, his birthright and the soul the strings are tuned to: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in his mother tongue, and counts in it the lineages the kora was made to carry. English he [carried as his working tongue](process_speaking_carried.md) in the wider world and [read and followed](process_reading_followed.md) to make his way in it, the tongue of the rooms where he played far from home. He found that the kora's run could cross where the Mandinka word could not, the music understood where the song's own language stayed behind, and so the keeper who carried his tradition the furthest met most often the gap that waits at the limit of the tongue.

## Action

In [the Smiling Coast](plot_04_the_smiling_coast.md) he is the kora taken to the world, the Gambian griot who carries the heartland of the strings out across the ocean and brings the river's song to listeners who had never heard it. He carries jaliya forward by opening it without thinning it, proving the small land's music big enough for the whole earth.

## Shadow

The master who carries the tradition out into the world risks loosening it from the ground that gave it meaning: the kora admired abroad as fine music while the deep names and lineages it was made to keep are left behind, the soul of jaliya thinning into pure sound when the word that anchored it does not travel with the strings.

## Tell

He sits the kora down beside instruments from the far side of the earth, and makes them all keep his time, the river's strings leading and the world following.
