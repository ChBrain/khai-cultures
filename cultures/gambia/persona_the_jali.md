# Persona: the jali

## Taxonomy

[teranga](position_teranga.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The griot of any river town, born into one of the keeping families, the Kouyate or the Suso or the Konte, trained from a child to the kora and the names, and called to every gathering the community holds. He is the one who comes to sing at the naming and the wedding, who praises the family and recites its line, who keeps the peace between those at odds and the warm welcome of teranga in word and song. Honoured and fed wherever he goes, he is himself a vessel of the welcome, the keeper of the people's memory who is kept in turn by the people. Mandinka, or Wolof, he holds whole and shaping, the tongue the kora is tuned to and the names are sung in: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md), and [hears](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) in his mother tongue, the language the whole keeping is held in. The [Gambian English](position_language_en_gm.md) of the school and the visitor he [carries as a borrowed tongue](process_speaking_borrowed.md), enough to greet a stranger and bring him into the welcome. He keeps a whole people's past in a tongue the wider world does not read, and knows that the song may travel where the words cannot, the keeper forever meeting the gap that waits at the limit of the tongue.

## Action

In [the Smiling Coast](plot_04_the_smiling_coast.md) he is the living keeper of jaliya and of teranga both, the griot at every gathering who sings the family's name, welcomes the stranger, and keeps the old memory and the warm welcome alive at once. He carries the small nation forward by holding its history in his song and its welcome in his hands, the keeper of who the people are.

## Shadow

The keeper honoured at every door can be hollowed into a hired voice, the praise sung for pay and the deep memory let thin, his standing spent and his calling mistaken for mere entertainment; and the welcome he embodies can curdle into a smile kept for the visitor while the trust behind it wears away.

## Tell

He arrives at the gathering, is fed before he is asked to sing, and then takes up the kora and gives the family back its name, so the welcome and the memory come from the same pair of hands.
