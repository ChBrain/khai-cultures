# Persona: Edward Francis Small

## Taxonomy

[the river](position_the_river.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The Bathurst man who would not be quiet, the journalist, trade unionist and political pioneer who stood up under the long British years and said plainly that a Gambian voice must be heard in the running of the Gambian land. He founded a newspaper and an early political body, organised the working people, and pressed for the small colony to have a say in its own affairs, the first of his kind to put the case so squarely. Sharp, principled and tireless, he gave the nation along the river its early political voice and would not be talked down. [Gambian English](position_language_en_gm.md) he holds whole and shaping, the tongue he turned back on the colonial power as his own weapon: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) it as a working tongue and [writes](process_writing_drafted.md) in it the editorials and the demands that pressed the Gambian case, taking the master's language and making the argument for the Gambian in it. The Wolof and Mandinka of the people he stood for he [carried in his own mouth](process_speaking_carried.md), the home tongues of Bathurst and the river he spoke for. He pressed the Gambian case in the coloniser's English because that was the tongue that would be heard in the halls of power, and found, like every man who must argue his people's cause in another's language, the gap that waits at the limit of the tongue. He belongs to [the Smiling Coast](position_culture_smiling_coast.md) as the one who first made a small river nation's voice heard on its own terms.

## Action

In [a Gambian voice](plot_03_a_gambian_voice.md) he is the pioneer who stands up and says the people of the river must be heard, organising and writing and pressing until a Gambian voice enters the running of the land. He carries the river nation forward by giving it its early political voice, setting the small colony on the long road that ends in its own flag, raised in peace.

## Shadow

The pioneer who argues his people's cause in the coloniser's tongue and through the coloniser's forms risks being heard only on terms not his own, the early voice for the many constrained by the very channels that allow it to speak, and the patient pressing within the system slow where the impatient would have it faster.

## Tell

He sets the Gambian case down in print in the empire's own English, and makes the language that ruled the colony argue for the colony's own voice.
