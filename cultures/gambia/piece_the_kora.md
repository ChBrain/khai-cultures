# Piece: the kora

## Taxonomy

Parent group: pieces

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Place

In the hands of the jali at every naming, wedding and gathering, at the bantaba under the tree, and on stages far across the ocean: a tall harp-lute of twenty-one strings, built on a great half-calabash covered with cowhide, with a long hardwood neck, and played with the thumbs and forefingers of both hands. The Gambia is a heartland of the kora and of the masters who play it, the Konte and the Suso and the other keeping families, and the instrument is the very voice of jaliya.

## Load Bearing

It is the instrument the history rides on, the strings to which the names and lineages are sung, the sound of the keeper's word. In a land that sang its past rather than writing it, the kora is how the past is carried, the run of its strings under the genealogy of a great house, the music that makes the memory bearable to hold and beautiful to give. It is the small nation's gift to the world's music, the sound by which The Gambia is known abroad, and the truest emblem of jaliya, for the keeper and his kora cannot be parted. Without it the sung history would have no voice.

## Apparent

A calabash, a cowhide, a length of hardwood and twenty-one strings, an object a craftsman can build by hand, light enough to carry and beautiful enough to draw every eye. In itself it is gourd and string; its whole weight is in the trained hands that play it and the deep memory those hands give back, the keeper's word made sound.

## Yearbook

- Carried out to the wider world by Gambian masters, sat down beside the instruments of the whole earth, and made a voice known far beyond the river.
- Played still at every naming, wedding and gathering, the strings under the sung names that keep each family's memory alive.
