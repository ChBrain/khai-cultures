# Place: Kunta Kinteh Island

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Shown

A small island in the lower reaches of the River Gambia, long known as James Island and renamed Kunta Kinteh Island for the son of nearby Juffureh whose story reached the world. On it stand the worn ruins of an old river fort, a place of trade and of holding in the long years when ships came up the river. The island and the village of Juffureh on the bank are honoured together as a place of memory, recognised by the world as a site to be kept, where the nation marks soberly and with dignity the river's hardest history.

## Offers

A place of remembrance and of return: a quiet island reached by boat, where the visitor and the descendant come to stand before the worn fort and the wide river, to remember what the river carried away, and at Juffureh to meet the keeping of the word that, generations on, brought a parted family's name back across the water. A site the world keeps, where memory is held with dignity.

## Withheld

Any easy comfort or any dwelling on the detail of the sorrow. The island offers no spectacle of suffering, only the worn stone, the wide river and the sober weight of what is remembered, the hardest memory of the land named for its river, held plainly and with respect, and turned, for some, into a road home.
