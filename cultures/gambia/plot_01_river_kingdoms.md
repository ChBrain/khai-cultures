# Plot: the river kingdoms

## Cue

Long before any border was drawn on a map, the River Gambia is already the road, the field and the gathering place of the peoples along its banks. Mandinka, Wolof, Fula, Jola and Serahule live and trade by its water, and out of the old empire of Mali its reach extends west, until small kingdoms take shape on the river.

## Action

Along the river the kingdoms of Niumi, Niani and the wider realm of Kaabu rise and hold, ruled by their chiefs and bound by trade and kinship up and down the water. The peoples of the banks do not write their history down; they keep it in song, and the jali, born to the keeping families, carries in his head and his kora the names, the deeds and the lineages of the great houses going back many generations. The kora is tuned and the genealogies are sung, and so the past of a whole land is held in the trained mouth of its keepers, the river binding its many peoples into the shared life of one water.

## Stage

- [Alhaji Bai Konte](persona_bai_konte.md): the master jali who holds the names and deeds of the river's old families in his head and his kora.
- [the river](position_the_river.md): the artery along which the kingdoms rise and the peoples are bound into one.
- [jaliya](position_jaliya.md): the craft of the keeper, the history sung and not written.

## Tension

A land that keeps its whole past in song rather than on the page holds a memory at once deep and frail: held perfectly across generations in the trained mouths of the griot families, it lives only as long as the keepers and the keeping do. The river binds the many peoples into one shared life, but the binding is carried, not written down, and the strength of jaliya, that the past is a living thing in a living mouth, is also its risk.
