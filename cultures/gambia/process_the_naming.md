# Process: the naming ceremony

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

A family with a new child, on the seventh or eighth day after the birth, gathering the whole compound and the neighbourhood and sending word to the jali. The elders, the imam or the marabout, the women of the household and the griot all come, the kitchen sends up the rice, and the small life is brought out to be given its name before the community.

## Direction

Toward binding the new child into the family, the faith and the line. The name is given and announced, prayers are said, the head may be shaved, and a sheep or a fowl prepared and shared, the food going round the gathered guests in the way of teranga. The jali sings the family's name and recites its line, placing the new child in the long story of the house, so that the naming is not a private matter but the whole community taking the child as its own.

## Lever

It is driven by the deep sense that a person is born into a people and not alone, and by the standing of the jali, whose sung genealogy gives the child a place in the line. It holds against the thinning of the old ways and the pull of a faster, smaller life; against it stand the cost of the feast on a thin purse and the long welcome it asks of a tired household.

## Echo

The naming ceremony makes audible the whole working of the small land: the child taken in by the whole community, the welcome and the food shared round, the jali singing the line so the new life knows whose it is. In the giving of a name before the gathered people, the nation does what it does best, holding each one by the word, the welcome and the song.
