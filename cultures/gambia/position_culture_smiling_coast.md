# Position: the Smiling Coast

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

Belonging to the one nation in the world named for its river, a thin strip of land where [the river](position_the_river.md) is never far from wherever you stand, and where the kingdoms of Niumi, Niani, and Kaabu rose long before any border was drawn. It carries [jaliya](position_jaliya.md), the griot's trained memory that keeps a whole people's history in the mouth and the kora rather than on a page, and it carries [teranga](position_teranga.md), the warmth that puts the greeting before the business and the guest before the stranger's unfamiliarity. It speaks [Gambian English](position_language_en_gm.md) in the office and the school, alongside Mandinka, Wolof, Fula, Jola, and Serahule at home. It hands down, without anyone choosing it, the conviction that a small land held together by its word and its welcome is worth more than a large one held together by nothing at all.

## Orders

That the person come before the business and the greeting before the deal, so no one is passed on the road without being asked after; that the elder and the griot be honoured and heard, since the past lives in a trained mouth and not in a locked book; that a guest be fed and sheltered as a blessing rather than a burden, the bowl always stretching for one more; and that a small nation of many peoples, gathered along one river, measure itself by its welcome rather than by its size.

## Loses

Ground when the welcome given so freely on so little wears the giver thin and goes unreturned, and when teranga is sold to the visitor as a brand on a poster while the warmth behind it hollows out; when the deep genealogies the jali keeps thin to a song or two because the young of the griot families turn from the long apprenticeship, or the recording machine stands in for the trained memory; when the young must leave by the back-way road for a living the home cannot give them; and when the river's old sorrow, the sons and daughters it once carried away, is remembered only as grief and never turned into a road home.

## Drives

Drives its people to hold themselves together not by size but by word and welcome, sitting a matter out together rather than forcing it. It lives in the griot who carries a whole people's lineage in his kora and reaches across an ocean to answer a searcher's question, in the political voice that first stood up under the colonial years and said a Gambian must be heard, and in the master who carries the strings furthest into the world without letting the names they were tuned to carry go silent. It drives every Gambian to know that the smile of the coast is not a thing sold to the visitor but the truest thing the people own.
