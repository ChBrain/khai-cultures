# Position: jaliya

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The craft and calling of the jali, the griot, the keeper of the word. Here we did not write our history down; we sang it, and the jali carried it. Born into the griot families, the Kouyate and the Suso and the Konte and the others, the jali learns from a child the names and deeds and lineages of the great families going back many generations, and holds the past of a whole people in his head. He is genealogist and historian, praise-singer and peacemaker, the one called to speak at the naming and the wedding and the burial, and the one whose kora and whose voice keep the memory of Kaabu and the old kings alive. The word is his by birth and by training, and the trust the people place in that word is the whole of his standing.

## Orders

That the past is a living thing kept in the mouth of a trained keeper, not a dead thing locked in a book, so the jali must be honoured and heard for the country to know itself; that the spoken word and the song carry truth and bind a people, the praise and the genealogy reminding each family who it is and what it owes; and that the jali stands between the great and the common, between families at odds, the one whose word can settle and reconcile what force cannot.

## Loses

Ground when the old memory is let thin, the young of the griot families turning from the long apprenticeship and the recording machine standing in for the trained mouth, so the deep lineages shorten to a song or two; and ground when jaliya is taken for mere entertainment or reduced to praise-for-pay, the keeper of the people's history mistaken for a hired voice and the trust that is his true standing spent.

## Drives

Lives in [Alhaji Bai Konte](persona_bai_konte.md) and [Foday Musa Suso](persona_foday_musa_suso.md), masters who carried the kora to the wider world; and in [Kebba Kanji Fofana](persona_kebba_fofana.md), the keeper whose carried memory reached across the ocean. It drives The Gambia to keep its history in song and its word as a trust, the heartland of the kora telling its own story in its own voice.
