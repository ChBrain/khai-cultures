# Play: The Gambia

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the house that holds this production and answers for it.

## Name

The Gambia, the smallest country on the African mainland, a thin strip of land on the far west coast that follows the River Gambia from the Atlantic deep inland, surrounded on every side but the sea by Senegal. ISO GM, language en, the working tongue of office and school, while at home and in the market the people carry Mandinka, Wolof, Fula, Jola, Serahule and more. We call ourselves the Smiling Coast, and the smile is not for the brochure; it is teranga, the way we hold a stranger.

## Arc

This is the one country in the world named for its river, and rightly so, for the River Gambia is the whole nation, threading the land end to end so that wherever you stand you are never far from its water. Long before the borders, the river was the road: the kingdoms of Niumi, Niani and Kaabu rose along its banks, and the jali, the griot, kept the memory of the great families in his head and his kora, since here we did not write our past, we sang it. Up this same river the European ships came for trade, and the river that gave life carried away its sons and daughters in the time of the slave trade, a sorrow this country holds soberly and with dignity, marked at Kunta Kinteh Island and the village of Juffureh, where a Gambian griot's word reached across the ocean and brought the world's children home to find their roots. The land knew the British for long years and gave the world Edward Francis Small, who stood up and said that a Gambian voice must be heard. We came to our own flag without a shot fired. Today the coast hums with its beaches and its birds, the wrestling ground roars for the borreh champion, the kitchen sends up benachin and domoda, and around the fire the attaya goes three rounds while the talk runs long. Under it all runs one nerve: that a small land of many peoples, gathered along one river, is held together not by its size but by its word, its welcome, and its song.

## Stakes
