# Place: Banjul

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Shown

The capital at the very mouth of the River Gambia, a small city on an island where the river meets the Atlantic, founded in colonial days as Bathurst and renamed Banjul at independence. Arch 22 stands at its gate, the old quarter holds the offices and the ferry terminal, and the wide brown river runs out past it to the sea. Across the water spreads Serekunda, the larger, busier town, and between them the country's life flows, but Banjul keeps the seat of the nation at the spot where the river ends and the ocean begins.

## Offers

A place where the river meets the sea and the nation meets the world, the seat of government and the old trading town, the ferry crossing and the market, the point from which the country is run and the door through which the visitor arrives, the small capital of the land named for its river standing at the river's very mouth.

## Withheld

The bustle of a great metropolis. Banjul is a small and low-lying capital, easily passed over for the larger life of Serekunda across the water, and it carries quietly the river's long memory, the mouth up which the trading ships once came and down which the river's sorrow once went, a small city holding a large past.
