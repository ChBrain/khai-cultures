# The Gambia: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical and cultural
sources behind the plots, personas, places, processes and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md) is written from The Gambia's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
The Gambia is not separately scored in the principal published Hofstede dataset;
the profile is read from West African regional patterns in Hofstede's work
(notably the collectivist, higher-power-distance, group-oriented register of the
region), so the figures below are indicative rather than country-specific, and are
recorded here only so the basis is on the record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Indicative West African profile |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | high                            |
| Individualism                 | low (collectivist)              |
| Motivation toward Achievement | moderate                        |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | moderate                        |
| Long Term Orientation         | low to moderate                 |
| Indulgence                    | moderate                        |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the six-dimensions model of national
culture and the West Africa regional figures). As The Gambia has no separate
country score in the principal published data, the pitch is written from the
regional profile; the values are indicative, as published by Hofstede Insights /
The Culture Factor Group.
