# Plot: the Smiling Coast

## Taxonomy

[play_gambia](play_gambia.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

The small nation along the river settles into itself, and the wider world begins to find it. The Atlantic beaches at the river's mouth draw visitors from cold places, the mangroves and wetlands fill with a great gathering of birds that brings the watchers, and the country earns its name as the Smiling Coast of Africa.

## Action

The coast at Serekunda and the resort strip hums with the visitor and the trade he brings, but the smile is older and deeper than the brochure, for it is teranga, the welcome the people have always lived by. The wrestling ground roars for the borreh champion thrown in the sand, the kitchen sends up benachin and domoda by the great shared bowl, and around the fire the attaya goes its slow three rounds while the talk runs long into the evening. The kora masters carry the river's song out to the world and back, the jali sings at every naming and wedding, and the small land meets the stranger with an open hand, sure that its warmth is the truest thing it owns.

## Stage

- [Foday Musa Suso](persona_foday_musa_suso.md): the kora master who carries the river's song out to the world and brings it home.
- [the jali](persona_the_jali.md): the griot at every gathering, keeping memory and welcome alive at once.
- [teranga](position_teranga.md): the welcome the Smiling Coast is named for and the people live by.
- [benachin](piece_benachin.md): the great shared bowl that gathers the family and the guest around one meal.

## Tension

The welcome that earns the Smiling Coast its name is a true thing the people live by, older and deeper than any poster, yet the same welcome is now sold to the visitor as a brand, and the small land must keep teranga a thing the people are and not only a name the world is shown. The warmth given so freely on so little is the nation's glory and its strain at once, the open hand stretched far across a thin and not a wealthy land.
