# Persona: Alhaji Bai Konte

## Taxonomy

[jaliya](position_jaliya.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The master kora jali of Brikama, born into a griot line and trained from a boy to the strings and the names, who among the first carried the Gambian kora out to the wider world and played it before listeners who had never heard the twenty-one strings before. A man of quiet dignity and a deep, patient hand, he held the old repertoire whole and gave it freely, an elder of jaliya who knew the worth of what he kept. Mandinka he holds whole and shaping, the tongue the kora itself is tuned to: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in his mother tongue, and in it he names the lineages and the deeds the strings are made to carry. The [Gambian English](position_language_en_gm.md) of office and of the visitors he [carried as a borrowed tongue](process_speaking_borrowed.md), enough to greet and to guide, and what reached him in it from the wider world he [followed by ear](process_hearing_followed.md) as best he could. He told the deep history of his people in a tongue the world did not read, and so he learned that the strings could cross the water sooner than the words could, the song travelling where the language stayed behind, the gap waiting at the limit of the tongue.

## Action

In [the river kingdoms](plot_01_river_kingdoms.md) he stands as the living keeper of jaliya, the master who holds in his head and his kora the names and deeds of the old families of the river, the Mandinka heartland singing its own past. By keeping the deep repertoire whole and handing it on, he carries the memory of the river's kingdoms unbroken into the present.

## Shadow

The master who holds so much in his head alone carries the fear of every griot line: that what is kept in the mouth and not on the page can thin or be lost when the keeper goes, the deep genealogies shortening if the young turn from the long apprenticeship, the recording standing in for the trained tongue.

## Tell

He tunes the twenty-one strings and, before a single name is sung, the room goes quiet, for everyone knows the man with the kora is about to give back to a family the memory of who they are.
