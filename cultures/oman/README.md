# Oman: a khai culture

Oman staged as a full khai play, ISO OM, language ar. A seafaring nation of the
Indian Ocean and the land of frankincense, staged as a theatre: its history as
the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its faith and language encode,
its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_oman.md](play_oman.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_ar.md](pitch_ar.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Oman's
  profile (a calm, collective, hierarchy-respecting people, restrained and
  patient, who prize moderation, dignity, and the keeping of the old covenant
  between people, land, and water) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Frankincense Road, the Navigators of the Indian Ocean,
  the Imamate of the Interior, and the Renaissance - each casting the elements it
  needs, and each staged soberly from within Oman's own self-understanding.
- **Personas:** Ahmad ibn Majid (the master navigator), Jabir ibn Zayd (the
  founding scholar of Ibadi jurisprudence), Abu Muslim al-Bahlani (the poet and
  scholar), and Qaboos bin Said (the modern Renaissance, on the public record),
  plus one archetype, the Falaj Keeper (the guardian of the irrigation channel's
  covenant of water). Each is wired to the language engine by the
  language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** the Sea (the seafaring nation that looked to the ocean as its
  road), frankincense (the scent of the land and the trade that linked Oman to the
  ancient world), Ibadi Islam (the distinctive, tolerant school of moderation and
  consultation).
- **Places:** Muscat (the capital and ocean gateway, old forts and new
  Renaissance), Nizwa (the heart of the interior, its great fort, market, and
  schools of jurisprudence).
- **Processes:** dhow building (the craft of the sewn ship of the navigators), the
  frankincense harvest (the covenant between the gatherer and the tree).
- **Pieces:** the khanjar (the curved dagger, emblem of dignity and the national
  symbol), frankincense (the scent of home, of welcome, and of Oman's oldest
  glory).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the modern Renaissance is noted soberly on the public record as
heritage, and contemporary politics is out of scope; the staging and the
architecture are original work.
