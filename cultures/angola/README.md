# Angola: a khai culture

Angola staged as a full khai play, ISO AO, language pt. A land of old kingdoms,
many peoples, and great music on the Atlantic, staged as a theatre: its history
as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its languages encode, its
places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_angola.md](play_angola.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_pt.md](pitch_pt.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Angola's profile (close family and community, respect for elders, a relationship
  to time that puts the person above the clock, the warmth of the open yard, and
  the resilient good humour of getting by) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the kingdom of Kongo, the Ndongo and Matamba of Queen
  Njinga, the Atlantic crossing and the creole life of the Luanda musseques, and
  the twentieth-century flowering of letters and music. The war of independence
  and the civil war are deliberately out of scope; the staging takes no political
  side and gives no atrocity detail.
- **Personas:** Njinga Mbande, Agostinho Neto, Bonga, and Pepetela as the historic
  cast, plus one archetype, the zungueira, the Luanda street-seller who carries the
  city on her head. Each is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it
  holds.
- **Positions:** the kingdoms (the ancient root of a land that began from within),
  the music (semba, kizomba and kuduro, the heart that never stops), the peoples
  (the many tongues and peoples that make one Angola).
- **Places:** Luanda (the bustling Atlantic capital and its musseques), the Kwanza
  (the great river, the south of desert, the Tundavala, the Welwitschia and the
  Himba).
- **Processes:** the kizomba (the slow embrace born of semba), the zunga (the
  street-selling that turns the whole city into a market).
- **Pieces:** the Mwana Pwo mask of the Chokwe (the land's ancient art), the
  Welwitschia (the desert plant that endures for centuries, the image of a
  resilient people).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the kingdoms, the peoples, and the Atlantic crossing are treated soberly
on the public record, and the staging and the architecture are original work.
