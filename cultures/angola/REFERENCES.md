# Angola: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical and cultural
sources behind the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_pt.md](pitch_pt.md) is written from Angola's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record without
entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Angola score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ---------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 83                     |
| Individualism                 | 18                     |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 20                     |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 60                     |
| Long Term Orientation         | 15                     |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; where a country's published figures
are partial, the profile is read in line with the regional pattern.

## Historical and cultural sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn
from the public historical and cultural record. They are credited here and never
claimed; the staging is original work. The war of independence and the civil war
are deliberately out of scope; the kingdoms, the peoples, and the Atlantic
crossing are staged soberly, taking no political side and giving no atrocity
detail.

The defining persona [a zungueira](persona_a_zungueira.md) is an archetype, not a
historical individual: a composite that carries the everyday face of the Luanda
musseques, the street-seller who holds the household together with her trade and
her good humour.
