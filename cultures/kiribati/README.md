# Kiribati: a khai culture

Kiribati staged as a full khai play, ISO KI, language gil (te taetae ni
Kiribati). A Micronesian people of thirty-three atolls scattered across the
equator and the date line, the only nation in all four hemispheres, staged as a
theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its
language and faith encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects,
all tuned by a single pitch. The prose is written in Gilbertese; only the
structural headings are in English.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_kiribati.md](play_kiribati.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_gil.md](pitch_gil.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Kiribati's profile (communal, consensus-bound in the maneaba, respectful of the
  unimwane, resilient, faith-filled, and ocean-wise) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** people of the maneaba (Micronesian settlement, the atolls,
  te katei), the colony and the war (the Banaba phosphate and displacement, the
  British, and the WWII battle of Tarawa, handled soberly), independence (1979 and
  Ieremia Tabai), and the vanishing land (the climate threat, Anote Tong's
  migration with dignity, and the diaspora) - each casting the elements it needs.
- **Personas:** Ieremia Tabai (the first president) and Anote Tong (the
  climate-leader president of "migration with dignity") as the historic cast, plus
  three defining archetypes, te unimwane (the elder of the maneaba), te tia akawa
  (the master fisherman and voyager), and te tia mwaie (the dancer of te mwaie).
  Each is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds:
  Gilbertese the mother tongue, English carried.
- **Positions:** te maneaba (the meeting house, the unimwane, consensus and te
  katei), te marawa (the vast ocean, the atolls across four hemispheres, fishing
  and voyaging), te aba ae e a kerikaki (the vanishing land, the climate threat,
  and migration with dignity).
- **Places:** Tarawa (the capital atoll and the WWII battleground), Banaba (the
  raised phosphate island whose people were displaced).
- **Processes:** te botaki n te maneaba (the community assembly and feast in the
  meeting house), te mwaie (the standing dance of Kiribati).
- **Pieces:** te baurua (the swift ocean canoe), te karewe (the sweet coconut
  toddy, the daily sustenance).

## Provenance

The Hofstede orientation behind the pitch and the public-record sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources on the public record are credited, never
claimed; the Banaba phosphate displacement and the WWII battle of Tarawa are
treated soberly, from within Kiribati's own self-understanding, with no atrocity
detail and taking no side; the staging and the architecture are original work.
