# Kiribati: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede orientation that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-record sources behind the plots,
personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_gil.md](pitch_gil.md) is written from Kiribati's cultural
orientation on Geert Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension
and carries no number. Kiribati is not separately scored in the published
Hofstede country data; the pitch is tuned from the qualitative orientation it
shares with Pacific island and other collectivist societies, recorded here so the
basis is on the record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Kiribati orientation (qualitative) |
| ----------------------------- | ---------------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | high (respect for the unimwane)    |
| Individualism                 | low (strongly communal)            |
| Motivation toward Achievement | moderate                           |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | moderate                           |
| Long Term Orientation         | low to moderate                    |
| Indulgence                    | moderate                           |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the six-dimensions model of national
culture). Kiribati has no separate published score; the orientation above is a
qualitative reading, not a measured value, and is offered as the pitch's basis
only.

## Languages

The play and the whole cast are written in Gilbertese (`gil`), te taetae ni
Kiribati, with only the structural headings in English. Gilbertese is not in
franc's model, so it is carried on the house's `khai.languages` exempt list and
verified by the NLP layer rather than by franc. The Gilbertese prose is the
authors' own composition, offered in good faith and best reviewed by a native
speaker.

## Historical sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn
from the public record. They are credited here and never claimed; the staging is
original work. The Banaba phosphate displacement and the WWII battle of Tarawa are
staged soberly, from within Kiribati's own self-understanding, with no atrocity
detail and taking no side.

The three defining personas, [te unimwane](persona_te_unimwane.md), [te tia
akawa](persona_te_tia_akawa.md), and [te tia mwaie](persona_te_tia_mwaie.md), are
archetypes, not historical individuals: composites that carry the culture's living
roles - the elder who keeps consensus and te katei in the maneaba, the master
fisherman and voyager of te marawa, and the dancer of te mwaie who carries the
love of the land and the sea.
