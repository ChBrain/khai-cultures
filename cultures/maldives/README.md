# the Maldives: a khai culture

The Maldives staged as a full khai play, ISO MV, language div (Dhivehi, written
right to left in the Thaana script). A nation of coral atolls held by one faith,
one tongue, and the sea, as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as
the cast, the roles its language and faith encode, its places, its practices, and
its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_maldives.md](play_maldives.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_div.md](pitch_div.md), the Hofstede layer, written from the
  Maldives' profile (a small, close community shaped by faith, family, and the
  patience of a life lived on the sea) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the coming of Islam (1153), Thakurufaanu freeing the nation
  (1573), the age of the sultans, and the modern nation and the rising sea - each
  casting the elements it needs, and each staging the questions of the nation
  soberly and with dignity.
- **Personas:** Muhammad Thakurufaanu (the national hero who freed the nation),
  Bodufenvalhuge Sidi (the poet of the Dhivehi tongue), Hussain Salahuddin
  (scholar and man of letters), and Mohamed Jameel Didi (poet of the national
  anthem) as the historic cast, plus one archetype, the fisherman (masveriya), the
  spirit of a nation that lives from the sea. Each is wired to the language engine
  by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** Islam (the faith of the whole nation since 1153), the sea (the
  source of all life, that gives and takes), the atoll nation (one tongue, one
  faith, one country bound across scattered reefs).
- **Places:** Male (the capital, the heart of the nation), the reef (the coral
  wall that shelters every island and feeds it).
- **Processes:** the tuna voyage (the dawn sailing for the skipjack that feeds the
  nation), bodu beru (the big drum, song and dance that gathers the island).
- **Pieces:** the dhoni (the traditional boat of palm wood), the woven mat (thundu
  kunaa, the hand-woven mat of an island craft).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are

soberly on the public record, taking no side; the staging and the architecture are
