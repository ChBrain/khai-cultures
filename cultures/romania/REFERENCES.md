# Romania: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_ro.md](pitch_ro.md) is written from Romania's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no
number. The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the
record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Romania score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ----------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 90                      |
| Individualism                 | 30                      |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 42                      |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 90                      |
| Long Term Orientation         | 52                      |
| Indulgence                    | 20                      |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published
by Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; Romania's high Power Distance and
Uncertainty Avoidance, with its low Individualism and Indulgence, are the marked
features of the published data.

The two defining personas, [Maria](persona_maria.md) and [Andrei](persona_andrei.md),
are archetypes, not historical individuals: composites that carry the tension
between the older generation (rural Orthodox faith, the doina, the Miorița
fatalism, the mother tongue) and the younger one (the post-2007 emigration among
the Latin cousins and the pull of Italian and global English on a small language).
