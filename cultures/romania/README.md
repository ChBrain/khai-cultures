# Romania: a khai culture

Romania staged as a full khai play, ISO RO, language ro. A Latin island in a
Slavic-Hungarian sea, as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as
the cast, the roles its language and faith encode, its places, its practices, and
its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_romania.md](play_romania.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_ro.md](pitch_ro.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Romania's
  profile (hierarchy and fatalism, anxiety for certainty, the family and clan
  bond, endurance, melancholy, and a resourceful getting-by) and naming no
  dimension.
- **Plots (history):** Dacia (106 AD), Eminescu and the 19th-century awakening,
  the Great Union (1918), and the 1989 Revolution - each casting the elements it
  needs.
- **Personas:** Mihai Eminescu, Constantin Brâncuși, and Mihai Viteazul as the
  historic cast, plus two defining archetypes, Maria (older, rural Orthodox faith,
  the doina, and the mother tongue) and Andrei (younger, the EU-era emigrant whose
  Romanian thins between Italian and English). Each is wired to the language engine
  by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** limba română (the Latin tongue that switched from Cyrillic to
  Latin letters to claim its descent from Rome), latinitatea (Latinity, the
  descent from Trajan's Romans and the Westward gaze toward the Latin kin), dor
  (the untranslatable longing, the melancholy of the doina and the Miorița
  fatalism).
- **Places:** Transilvania (the contested Latin heartland shared with Hungarians
  and Saxons, the castle land of the Dracula myth, united with the country in
  1918), București (the francophone "little Paris," scarred by Ceaușescu and by the
  1989 revolution).
- **Processes:** doina (the free-rhythm folk song of longing, the solitary voice
  of the soul), hora (the round dance of the village, the communal counterpoint to
  the doina).
- **Pieces:** lupoaica (the Capitoline she-wolf, the badge of Roman descent in
  Romanian squares), ia (the embroidered peasant blouse, "la blouse roumaine,"
  folk identity become a global icon).
