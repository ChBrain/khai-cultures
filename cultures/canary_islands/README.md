# Canary Islands: a khai culture

Canary Islands staged as a full khai play, ISO ES-CN, language es. Islas Canarias: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its Conquest of Tenerife, Columbus stopover, and Teide Observatory founding encodes, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house that holds this culture and answers for it.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_canary_islands.md](play_canary_islands.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_canary_islands.md](pitch_canary_islands.md), the Canary Islands key.
- **Plots (history):** the Conquest of Tenerife of 1496, the Columbus Stopover of 1492, and the Teide Observatory founding of 1964.
- **Personas:** Yaiza (banana plantation manager) and Ayose (silbo whistling ranger), written in Spanish.
- **Positions:** island isolation (ocean reliance/island identity/cooperative farming), volcanic agriculture (soil fertility/terraced farming/volcanic water management).
- **Places:** Teide Peak, Banana Plantation.
- **Processes:** banana cultivation (traditional terrace farming), silbo whistling (traditional whistled language).
- **Pieces:** silbo whistle, volcanic stone mortar.
- **Plan:** the Teide national park conservation plan.
