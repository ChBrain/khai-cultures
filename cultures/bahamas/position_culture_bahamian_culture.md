# Position: Bahamian culture

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The belonging of a people who are sea-people before they are anything else, spread across [the sea and the isles](position_the_sea_and_the_isles.md), an archipelago of some seven hundred islands and two thousand cays where the water joins more than it divides. It carries the road [from colony to nation](position_colony_to_nation.md), the patient climb from a slaveholding crown colony run off Bay Street to a self-governing country with an Afro-Bahamian majority at its own helm. And it carries [Junkanoo and rake-and-scrape](position_junkanoo_and_rake_and_scrape.md), the African-rooted music and masquerade the islands made out of what they were given least of, spoken in the [Bahamian dialect](position_language_en_bs.md) of the settlement and the sloop.

## Orders

Orders that the Bahamian read the weather and the water as a mainlander reads the road, and that the islands be bound by the sea between them rather than split by it, one scattered nation of harbours and cays. It orders that the descendants of the enslaved are the nation and its rightful government, the franchise won by the ballot and the union hall rather than the gun, and that the young and the settlements keep the costume sewn and the drum tuned each year lest the voice of Junkanoo thin into a souvenir. And it orders hospitality and neighbourliness across the Out Islands, the toy and the wake shared, so that no cay is left to fend for itself.

## Loses

Ground when the sea is treated as scenery rather than livelihood, the reef sold to the brochure and the conch fished faster than it breeds, and when the young trade the water for a wage in town. It loses ground when the freedom won by the franchise is narrowed again, power and wealth gathering back at the capital while the Out Islands go unheard, and when Junkanoo and rake-and-scrape are sold as a poolside performance instead of lived in the settlement that made them. And it frays wherever the climb from colony to nation is claimed as finished, the cost of the vote and of independence forgotten by those who did not have to pay it.

## Drives

Drives the Bahamian to know the reef and the shoal the way a sailor knows a road, taking what the sea gives and respecting what it can take back. It drives the nation to hold what it climbed to win, governing itself for every island and not only for the few who once held Bay Street, and to keep its own voice sounding in the costume, the cowbell, and the scraped saw against the pull of the borrowed tune. And it drives every generation to ask whether the wealth of the sand and the sea reaches the Family Islands too, or whether the distance between the capital and the cay is allowed to grow.
