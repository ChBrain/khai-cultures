# the Bahamas: a khai culture

The Bahamas staged as a full khai play, ISO BS, language en. An archipelago of
seven hundred islands where the New World was first sighted, staged as a theatre:
its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its language and
faith encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a
single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_bahamas.md](play_bahamas.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md), the Hofstede layer, written from the
  Bahamas' profile (a warm, sociable, easygoing collectivism, an unhurried
  present-focus and island time, a relaxed distance from authority, and the long
  rhythm of the sea) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the First Landfall, Pirates and Loyalists, the Road to
  Majority Rule, and the Archipelago Nation - each casting the elements it needs,
  and each staging the gap between the promise and the reality honestly.
- **Personas:** Lynden Pindling, Milo Butler, Doris Johnson, and Joseph Spence as
  the historic cast, plus one defining archetype, the wrecker (the sea-salvager
  and sponger of the reefs who reads the water like a page). Each is wired to the
  language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** the sea and the isles (the archipelago and its sea-people),
  colony to nation (the road from a slaveholding colony through majority rule to
  independence), Junkanoo and rake-and-scrape (the African-rooted music and
  masquerade that is the nation's own voice).
- **Places:** Nassau (the old pirate capital, Bay Street, the seat of wealth and
  power), the Out Islands (the Family Islands, the sea life, and the old ways).
- **Processes:** Junkanoo (the Boxing Day and New Year masquerade rush), the
  Family Island Regatta (the wooden-sloop sailing races of the settlements).
- **Pieces:** conch (the national staple of the sea-people, who take its name for
  their own), the Bahamian sloop (the wooden working sailboat that made the
  archipelago one).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the Lucayans' disappearance, slavery, and the Loyalist plantations are
treated soberly on the public record; the staging and the architecture are
original work.
