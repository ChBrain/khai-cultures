# Place: the Out Islands

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Shown

The Family Islands, the scattered hundreds of cays and islands beyond New Providence: Eleuthera where the Adventurers first settled, long Andros with its blue holes and pine forests, the Abacos and the Exumas, Cat Island and the far Inaguas. Small settlements of conch shells and clapboard along the shore, the wooden sloops drawn up on the sand, the reef and the flats teeming with fish and conch, and the quiet of a place the world mostly passes by. The sea life and the old island ways, the deepest Bahamas, kept where the tide and the settlement set the pace.

## Holds

- [the First Landfall](plot_01_the_first_landfall.md): the islands where the Lucayans lived and Columbus first came ashore, and where the Eleutheran Adventurers settled seeking the freedom to worship.
- [the Archipelago Nation](plot_04_the_archipelago_nation.md): the Family Islands of the modern nation, the home of the sea life and the old music and the question of whether Nassau's wealth ever reaches them.

## Offers

A place where the oldest and truest Bahamas survives: the settlements where rake-and-scrape is still played and the Regatta still sailed, the reefs and flats the sea-people still work, and the home cays the Junkanoo costume and the conch and the sloop all come from. The Out Islands offer the nation its roots and its sea, the source of the culture the capital performs.

## Withheld

A fair share of the nation's wealth and a hold on its own young. The Out Islands carry the gap the brochure hides: behind the postcard of empty turquoise water lie settlements thinning as the young sail off to Nassau or the mainland for work, the school and the clinic far, and the prosperity that fills the capital slow to reach the cay - the paradise the visitor flies to and the home the islander must often leave.
