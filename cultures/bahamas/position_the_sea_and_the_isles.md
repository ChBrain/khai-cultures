# Position: the sea and the isles

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The proposition that the Bahamas is not a land but a sea with islands in it: an archipelago of some seven hundred islands and two thousand cays scattered across the shallow banks, where the water joins more than it divides and the people are sea-people first. The reef and the shoal that wreck the careless ship feed the one who knows them; the sloop is the road, the conch and the sponge and the fish are the harvest, and the settlement looks out, not in. It is a nation whose wealth, history, and danger all come from the same turquoise water - the New World first sighted from it, the wreckers and spongers fed by it, and the tourist drawn across it.

## Orders

That the Bahamian belongs to the sea before the soil, reading the weather and the water as a mainlander reads the road; that the islands are bound by the water between them and not split by it, one scattered nation of harbours and cays; and that the sea is at once the larder, the highway, and the hazard, to be worked with a sailor's skill and a sailor's patience, taking what it gives and respecting what it can take back.

## Loses

Ground when the sea is treated as scenery rather than livelihood - when the reef is sold to the brochure and emptied by the net, when the conch is fished faster than it breeds, and when the young leave the water for the wage in town; and ground, too, when the distance the sea sets between the islands hardens into neglect, and the far cay is left to thin and empty while the capital fills.

## Drives

Lives in [the wrecker](persona_the_wrecker.md), the sea-salvager and sponger who reads the reef and lives off the water and the ships it tears open, the islands' deepest sea-craft made flesh. It drives the Bahamas to know itself as a people of the water and to keep the sea that feeds it whole.
