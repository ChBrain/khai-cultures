# Persona: Milo Butler

## Taxonomy

[colony to nation](position_colony_to_nation.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

Sir Milo Butler, the market trader turned labour and rights pioneer who fought for the working Bahamian's franchise and dignity across decades and lived to become the first Bahamian Governor-General, the crown's own office filled at last by a son of the islands. Fiery, plain-spoken, fearless, and beloved, he was the people's tribune, the man who would not be quiet and would not be bought. English he has whole and ringing: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [hears](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) in his mother tongue, but it is the broad Bahamian dialect of the market and the settlement he speaks it in, the dropped h and the rolling lilt of the working islands, the speech of the people he stood for. The high English of the colonial assembly and the dispatch box he [followed and answered](process_hearing_followed.md), [reading the official record](process_reading_followed.md) closely to throw the rulers' own rules back at them on the floor. He shouted the working Bahamian's case in the dialect of the market into the polished English chamber of the merchant class, while beneath that dialect lay the African tongues the enslaved forebears of the market traders had carried over and lost - so the gap at the limit of the tongue is the distance between the home speech that named the grievance and the chamber's English that pretended not to hear it.

## Action

In [the Road to Majority Rule](plot_03_the_road_to_majority_rule.md) he forces open the road from [colony to nation](position_colony_to_nation.md), fighting for the working man's vote and dignity until the franchise widens. By making himself the loud and unbought voice of the labouring majority, he turns the grievance of the settlements into a political force the merchant class can no longer ignore.

## Shadow

The tribune who will not be quiet carries the cause's shadow: the risk that the loud voice is heard and then honoured into harmlessness, the rebel made a knight and a Governor-General, the fierce demand softened into a ceremony - the danger that the establishment answers the people's champion by giving him the office and keeping the power.

## Tell

He spends a lifetime shouting down the merchant class on behalf of the market and the settlement, and ends it standing in the crown's own highest island office - the people's loud man become the people's dignity, the long fight made flesh.
