# Play: the Bahamas

## Name

The Commonwealth of the Bahamas, an archipelago nation strung across the warm shallow sea off the southeastern coast of Florida, some seven hundred islands and two thousand cays scattered from the Bimini reefs to the Inaguas, of which only a few dozen are settled. ISO BS, language en, an Afro-Bahamian majority on the soil where the New World was first sighted. A nation not of one island but of many, held together less by land than by the sea between them.

## Arc

The story begins on the water: the Lucayan Taino paddling their canoes among the cays, and the morning in 1492 when Columbus made his first New World landfall on one of these islands and the old world and the new met on a Bahamian beach. The Lucayans were carried off and gone within a generation, and the islands lay near empty until English Puritans, the Eleutheran Adventurers, sailed down from Bermuda seeking the freedom to worship as they chose. The reefs and the sheltered harbours bred a sea-people: spongers and wreckers who lived off the ships the shoals tore open, and the pirates who made Nassau their republic until the crown drove them out. After the American Revolution the Loyalists came, bringing enslaved Africans and the dream of cotton plantations that the thin island soil soon defeated - and so the gap between promise and reality is written into the land itself, the freedom the islands offered some bought with the bondage of others. Slavery ended, and an Afro-Bahamian majority remained, working the sea by sloop and the reef by diving for sponge and conch, blockade-running to the Confederacy and rum-running to dry America when the chance came. Then came the long road to majority rule and the franchise, won at last in 1967, and independence in 1973. Under it all runs one nerve: a scattered, easygoing, sea-rhythmed people, dancing Junkanoo in the streets at dawn, who turned a poor archipelago into a nation of tourism and banking and asked whether the wealth of the sand could reach the Family Islands too.

## Stakes

Whether a scattered archipelago nation can share the wealth of its sea and sand across all its islands and not only at the capital, can hold its Family Islands together against the long pull toward Nassau and toward the mainland beyond, can keep [the sea and the isles](position_the_sea_and_the_isles.md) that feed it and the [Junkanoo and rake-and-scrape](position_junkanoo_and_rake_and_scrape.md) that voice it, and can steer its own course as a small nation in a great sea - or whether the distance between the brochure and the Out Island, between Bay Street and the cay, finally pulls the seven hundred islands apart.
