# Plot: Pirates and Loyalists

## Owner

## Cue

The sheltered harbour at Nassau and the maze of reefs and cays around it make the islands a perfect haunt for those who live off the sea-lanes. By the early eighteenth century the crown's hold is thin, the harbour is full of freebooters, and Nassau has become a republic of pirates.

## Action

For a brief age Nassau is the pirate republic, a lawless harbour where the buccaneers divide their plunder, until the crown sends a governor who drives them out under the motto that piracy is expelled and commerce restored. Decades later, after the American Revolution, the Loyalists arrive: subjects of the crown fleeing the new United States, who bring with them enslaved Africans and the dream of cotton plantations on the islands. The thin island soil soon defeats the cotton, and what remains is an Afro-Bahamian people descended from the enslaved, working the sea by sloop while the wreckers and spongers of the reefs go on living off the water and the ships it tears open.

## Stage

- [the wrecker](persona_the_wrecker.md): the sea-salvager and sponger who lives off the reef and the wreck through the whole age of pirates and Loyalists.
- [Nassau](place_nassau.md): the pirate harbour and then the port where the Loyalists land with their enslaved and their failing cotton.
- [the sea and the isles](position_the_sea_and_the_isles.md): the archipelago and its sea-people, the reefs that wreck the ship and feed the wrecker.

## Tension

The islands that offer some a refuge and a fresh start are made by the bondage of others: the Loyalists come seeking to keep their world and bring enslaved Africans to build it, and when the cotton fails the islands are left with the deeper truth of slavery on their soil. The same sea that frees the pirate and feeds the wrecker holds the ships of the slave trade, and the gap between the freedom the islands promised and the bondage they practised is written into the land and the people the plantations left behind.
