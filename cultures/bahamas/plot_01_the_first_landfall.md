# Plot: the First Landfall

## Taxonomy

[play_bahamas](play_bahamas.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

For centuries the Lucayan Taino people live among these islands, paddling their canoes through the cays, fishing the flats, and trading across the banks, a sea-people on a sea of islands. Out of the eastern horizon, on a October morning in 1492, three strange ships come on the wind toward one of their islands.

## Action

On the twelfth of October, 1492, Christopher Columbus makes his first New World landfall on a Bahamian island the Lucayans call Guanahani, and the old world and the new world meet for the first time on a Bahamian beach. Within a generation the Lucayans are gone, carried off to labour elsewhere, and the islands lie all but empty. A century and a half later a band of English Puritans, the Eleutheran Adventurers, sail down from Bermuda to settle Eleuthera, seeking the freedom to worship as they choose - the islands offering refuge and a fresh start even as their first peoples have already vanished from them.

## Stage

- [the Out Islands](place_the_out_islands.md): the cays where the Lucayans lived, where Columbus came ashore, and where the Adventurers first settled.
- [the sea and the isles](position_the_sea_and_the_isles.md): the archipelago and its sea-people, the stage on which the meeting and the settling both happen.

## Tension

The islands where the New World was first sighted are also where its first cost was first paid: the Lucayans who met the ships are gone within a generation, and the freedom the Adventurers come seeking is settled on emptied ground. The Bahamas begins, like the whole New World, with both a discovery and a disappearance - the promise of refuge and the fresh start written over the loss of the people who were here first, the gap between welcome and reality present from the very first landfall.
