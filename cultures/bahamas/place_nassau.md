# Place: Nassau

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Shown

The capital on the island of New Providence, the old pirate harbour grown into the heart of the nation: Bay Street running along the waterfront, the pink colonial government buildings, the forts above the town, and the cruise ships crowding the port. The sheltered harbour the buccaneers once made their republic, the merchant street where a small white class long held the colony's money and power, and the stage where Junkanoo rushes down Bay Street before dawn. The nation's wealth, its government, and most of its people, gathered on one small island.

## Holds

- [the Road to Majority Rule](plot_03_the_road_to_majority_rule.md): Bay Street and the assembly where Butler, Johnson, and Pindling fought the merchant class for the franchise and won the islands their own government.

## Offers

A place where the Bahamas governs itself, trades, and shows itself to the world: the seat of the parliament and the courts, the marketplace where the tourist money and the offshore banking gather, and the great civic stage of Junkanoo. Nassau is where the nation's history turned, from pirate haven to slaveholding colony to self-governing capital, and where its present prosperity is mostly made.

## Withheld

The whole of the nation. Nassau carries the gap the archipelago lives with: the capital that holds most of the people, the money, and the power while the Out Islands thin and wait, so that "Nassau" can come to mean the centre that forgets its own edges, the Bay Street that prospers while the far cay is left to the tide.
