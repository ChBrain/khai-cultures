# Persona: Joseph Spence

## Taxonomy

[Junkanoo and rake-and-scrape](position_junkanoo_and_rake_and_scrape.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The legendary Bahamian folk guitarist, a stonemason and sponger from Andros who played a guitar like no one before or since, his ragged hymns and rake-and-scrape tunes growling and humming under his breath until the world's musicians came to the islands to learn from him. Earthy, joyful, unschooled, and wholly original, he made the island music speak and never once left the islands to do it. English he has whole and homemade: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [hears](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) in the broad [Bahamian dialect](position_language_en_bs.md) of Andros, the rich Out Island speech of the settlement and the sloop, the dropped consonants and rolling vowels that ran straight into the half-sung mumble that rode over his playing. The old hymns and church songs he [carried by ear and made his own](process_speaking_carried.md), never reading a note, [catching the melodies he heard](process_hearing_followed.md) in the chapel and the yard and reshaping them whole on the strings. He sang the island's faith and joy in a dialect so thick and a voice so growled that the words half dissolved into pure sound, and beneath that dialect lay the African tongues his enslaved forebears had carried over and lost into the music itself - so the gap at the limit of the tongue is the place where his words give out and the guitar and the wordless humming say what the dialect cannot, the African root sounding through the strings where the African words no longer can.

## Action

In [the Archipelago Nation](plot_04_the_archipelago_nation.md) he gives [Junkanoo and rake-and-scrape](position_junkanoo_and_rake_and_scrape.md) its purest voice, the Out Island music carried to the world by a man who stayed home. By making the settlement's guitar and hymn into art the whole world came to hear, he proves the islands have a voice that owes nothing to the brochure and everything to the people.

## Shadow

The home genius the world discovers carries the islands' shadow: the danger that the music is treasured abroad and let go at home, the original turned into a recording and a curio while the saw falls silent in the settlement, and the unschooled master's gift admired as a wonder rather than carried on by the young who could keep it living.

## Tell

He plays a guitar so strange and so sure that trained musicians cross the sea to sit at his feet, and he does it as a working sponger from Andros who never learned a note and never wanted to leave the islands that made him.
