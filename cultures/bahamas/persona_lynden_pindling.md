# Persona: Lynden Pindling

## Taxonomy

[colony to nation](position_colony_to_nation.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The London-trained lawyer who became the father of the nation, the first black premier of the Bahamas, who led the islands out of the long rule of a small white merchant class to majority rule and on to independence in 1973. Eloquent, commanding, patient, and shrewd, he made himself the voice of the Afro-Bahamian majority and bent the whole arc of the colony toward self-rule. English he has whole and wielding: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in his mother tongue and [drafted](process_writing_drafted.md) the case for a people's government in it, the very English of the empire he was prising the islands loose from. The lawyer's English of Westminster and the courts he [carries as a borrowed robe](process_speaking_borrowed.md), [reading the law deeply](process_reading_followed.md) at the Inns of Court and turning the colonizer's own statutes and procedures into the levers of liberation. He argued the nation into being in the tongue and the law of the crown that ruled it, while behind that polished English lay the [Bahamian dialect](position_language_en_bs.md) of the settlements he spoke for and, behind that, the African tongues the enslaved who built the islands had carried over and lost - so the gap at the limit of the tongue is the distance between the courtroom English that won the freedom and the home speech and the older speech beneath it that the freedom was won for.

## Action

In [the Road to Majority Rule](plot_03_the_road_to_majority_rule.md) he leads the islands to [colony to nation](position_colony_to_nation.md), carrying the majority to power in 1967 and to independence in 1973. By winning self-rule with the ballot and the law rather than the gun, he gives the Bahamas its own government and the harder task that follows: to run a scattered nation for all its islands.

## Shadow

The liberator who gathers a nation's hope into himself carries its shadow: the long hold on power that can outstay the cause, the wealth and the patronage that gather around the man who freed the islands, and the temptation to mistake the leader's grip for the people's rule, so that the very capital he won for the majority can forget the far settlements that voted him in.

## Tell

He takes a colony run from a few feet of Bay Street and makes it a nation governed by its own majority, and does it by the patient weapons of the franchise and the law - winning the country, not seizing it.
