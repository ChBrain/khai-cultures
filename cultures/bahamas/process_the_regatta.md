# Process: the Family Island Regatta

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

The settlements and the boatbuilders of the Out Islands, the working sloops hauled out and made ready, the crews gathered from the cays, the whole of George Town in Exuma filling for the spring races. It takes the wooden sloops the islands have always built and sailed and the men who crew them, brought together each year to race under sail alone, the old working craft turned out for honour and the crowd ashore cheering its settlement's boat.

## Direction

Toward pride, skill, and the keeping of the sea-craft: the regatta of native wooden sloops that honours the boats and the men who built and sailed them, the working vessel of the islands raced for glory. It is the sea-people celebrating the sea-people, the sloop that was the road and the livelihood made a thing of beauty and contest, the settlements measuring themselves against each other under canvas as their grandfathers did.

## Lever

It is driven by the islands' love of the sloop and the sailor and by the wish to keep the wooden boats and the skill of sailing them from passing away in the age of the outboard and the ferry. It holds against the slow loss of the working sail and the drift of the young from the sea; against it stand the cost of building and keeping a wooden sloop and the danger that the races, like so much else, become a show for the visitor rather than a rite of the islands.

## Echo

The Regatta makes visible what [the sea and the isles](position_the_sea_and_the_isles.md) hold: that the Bahamian belongs to the water and the wooden sloop is the islands' true vessel. To these races [the wrecker](persona_the_wrecker.md) and the sea-people bring the same craft they read the reef with, the sea-knowledge of the islands turned out under sail.
