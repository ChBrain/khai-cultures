# Plan: Forward, Upward, Onward, Together

## Taxonomy

The open mandate the nation set itself in its motto: the standing work of sharing the wealth that [the sea and the isles](position_the_sea_and_the_isles.md) and the tourist trade bring across all the islands and not only at the capital, of holding the scattered Family Islands together as one country, of guarding the sea and the reef that feed the nation, of keeping the voice of [Junkanoo and rake-and-scrape](position_junkanoo_and_rake_and_scrape.md) sounding, and of steering the small nation's own course in a great sea now that [colony to nation](position_colony_to_nation.md) has been won.

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Direction

Spread the wealth of tourism and banking beyond Nassau so the Out Islands share in what the sea and sand earn; hold the archipelago together, keeping the far settlements peopled and served against the long pull toward the capital and the mainland; guard the sea, the reef, the conch, and the sponge that are the nation's larder, fishing and selling them no faster than the sea can bear; keep the islands' own voice, the Junkanoo and the Regatta and the rake-and-scrape, lived and not merely performed; and steer the nation's own course as a small country in a great sea, dependent on the world's visitors yet master of its own house.

## Orders

- To the sea-people like [the wrecker](persona_the_wrecker.md): read and work the sea as ever, but guard the reef and the beds you live off, taking no more than the water can give back.
- To the voice-keepers like [Joseph Spence](persona_joseph_spence.md): carry on the home music and the masquerade, teaching the young the saw, the drum, and the costume so the islands' own tongue does not thin into a souvenir.
- To the nation: hold the islands together and share the wealth across them all, keeping the freedom that [Lynden Pindling](persona_lynden_pindling.md) won a freedom for every cay and not only for Bay Street.

## Implementation

A young nation of seven hundred islands, independent since 1973, that has built a real prosperity on tourism and banking and stands among the better-off of its region, yet carries the gap still open: wealth and people gathered at Nassau while the Out Islands thin, a living dependent on a sea of strangers, a sea and a reef pressed by overfishing and a warming, rising ocean, and the standing question of whether the culture of the settlements can outlast the show staged for the visitor.
