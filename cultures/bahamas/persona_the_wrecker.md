# Persona: the wrecker

## Taxonomy

[the sea and the isles](position_the_sea_and_the_isles.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The sea-salvager and sponger of the reefs and Out Islands, who knows every shoal and channel of the shallow banks and lives off what the sea gives and takes: the cargo of the ship the reef tears open, the sponge raked from the bottom, the conch and the fish of the flats. Weathered, canny, devout, and hard, he is the islands' deepest sea-craft made flesh, a man who reads the water like a page. The [Bahamian dialect](position_language_en_bs.md) is his mother tongue, and he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [hears](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) it whole - "she gone on de reef, b'oy, le' we go" - the broad Out Island speech of the settlement and the sloop, the dropped h and the rolling lilt that is the true voice of the working islands. The few words of the law and the wreck-court, the salvage and the bill of lading, he [carries as a borrowed scrap](process_speaking_borrowed.md), [catching what he can](process_hearing_followed.md) of the [official English](../united_kingdom/position_language_en_gb.md) that decides whose the wreck is. He talks the sea and the reef in a dialect so rooted it has no need of the mainland's English, and beneath that dialect lie the African tongues his enslaved forebears carried over and lost on these same cays - so the gap at the limit of the tongue is the place where the dialect names every shoal and current the sea has, and the lost African words that first knew another shore are gone under the same water.

## Action

In [Pirates and Loyalists](plot_02_pirates_and_loyalists.md) he is [the sea and the isles](position_the_sea_and_the_isles.md) at their hardest and surest, the sea-people who live off the reef and the wreck while the pirate republic rules Nassau and the Loyalists fail at cotton. He carries the islands' truth that the sea is larder, highway, and hazard all at once, taking his living from the same water that wrecks the careless.

## Shadow

The man who lives off the wreck carries the sea's shadow: the temptation to want the ship to founder, to light the false fire or pray the reef takes her, so that another's loss is his harvest - and the harder shadow of the sea fished out, the sponge taken till the beds die and the conch raked till the flats go quiet, the larder emptied by the very skill that read it.

## Tell

He stands on the reef-edge reading the water for the glint of a stranded hull, and gives thanks to God for the wreck that feeds his family - the islands' whole hard bargain with the sea in a single morning's work.
