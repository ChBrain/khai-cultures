# Position: colony to nation

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The proposition that the Bahamas made itself a nation by degrees, climbing from a slaveholding crown colony run from Bay Street to a self-governing country with an Afro-Bahamian majority at its own helm. Where the New World story began with conquest and the islands were peopled by the enslaved the Loyalists brought, the long arc bends the other way: emancipation, then the slow opening of the franchise that a small white merchant class had kept narrow, then the vote for women, then majority rule won in 1967, and independence in 1973. It is the road from being owned to owning oneself, run not in a single revolution but in a patient, lawful climb toward the simple thing of a people governing itself.

## Orders

That the descendants of the enslaved are the nation and its rightful government, the majority become the masters of their own house; that the franchise is the lever and the law the road, the climb made by the ballot and the union hall rather than the gun; and that independence is not the end but the beginning of the harder work of running a scattered country for all its islands and not only for the few who once held Bay Street.

## Loses

Ground when the freedom won is narrowed again - when power gathers back at the capital and the Out Islands go unheard, when the wealth of the nation pools where the old merchant money pooled, and when the form of self-rule is kept while its promise to the far settlement is let slide; and ground to the cynicism that, having won the vote, forgets what it cost and how lately it came.

## Drives

Lives in [Lynden Pindling](persona_lynden_pindling.md), the father of the nation who led the islands to majority rule and independence; in [Milo Butler](persona_milo_butler.md), the labour and rights pioneer who became the first Bahamian Governor-General; and in [Doris Johnson](persona_doris_johnson.md), who led the women's suffrage movement and won the vote for half the nation. It drives the Bahamas to hold what it climbed to win and to govern itself for all its islands.
