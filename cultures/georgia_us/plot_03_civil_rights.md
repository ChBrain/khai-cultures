# Plot: The Civil Rights Movement 1960

## Taxonomy

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

In the early 1960s, civil rights leaders establish the Southern Christian Leadership Conference headquarters in Atlanta, directing non-violent protest campaigns.

## Action

The activism centered at [Ebenezer Baptist Church](place_ebenezer_baptist.md) makes the city a national stage for civil rights, led by the moral authority of the [pulpit](piece_pulpit.md). Evelyn curates oral histories and manuscripts from SCLC campaigns, while Carter respects the legacy of MLK Jr. in the state.

## Stage

- [Ebenezer Baptist Church](place_ebenezer_baptist.md) : the historic church that served as a moral center for the movement.
- [pulpit](piece_pulpit.md) : the wooden pulpit representing the spiritual leadership of civil rights ministers.
- [civil rights organizing](process_civil_rights_organizing.md) : the non-violent protest planning and community coordination.

## Tension

The movement faces violent resistance from segregationists and state officials, exposing deep-seated racial hostility in both rural districts and urban centers.
