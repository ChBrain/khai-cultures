# Plot: Savannah Founding 1733

## Taxonomy

[play_georgia_us](play_georgia_us.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

In February 1733, James Oglethorpe lands at Yamacraw Bluff on the Savannah River and establishes the colony of Georgia.

## Action

The founding establishes [Southern hospitality](position_southern_hospitality.md) principles through a unique grid system of public squares, designed to foster community equality. Evelyn curates early colonial plans in the Atlanta archives, documenting Oglethorpe's anti-slavery stance.

## Stage

- [Southern hospitality](position_southern_hospitality.md) : the welcoming, community-focused regional courtesy and respect.
- [Savannah Squares](place_savannah_squares.md) : the geometric public squares designed as common areas for residents.
- [Evelyn](persona_evelyn.md) : the Atlanta archivist who preserves the city's early layout maps.

## Tension

Despite Oglethorpe's ban on slavery and lawyers, wealthy colonists successfully lobby the trustees to permit slavery by 1751, dismantling the utopian experiment.
