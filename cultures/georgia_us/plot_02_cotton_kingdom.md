# Plot: The Cotton Kingdom 1793

## Taxonomy

[play_georgia_us](play_georgia_us.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

In 1793, Eli Whitney invents the cotton gin near Savannah, sparking a massive expansion of short-staple cotton cultivation.

## Action

The agricultural boom transforms the state into a slaveholding empire, establishing a deep division between the plantation aristocracy and the enslaved population. The [peach basket](piece_peach_basket.md) becomes a symbol of the state's commercial agriculture. Carter's peach orchard land in central Georgia was originally cleared for cotton cultivation during this antebellum boom.

## Stage

- [Southern hospitality](position_southern_hospitality.md) : the traditional codes of honor and courtesy developed in the plantation era.
- [peach basket](piece_peach_basket.md) : the wooden basket representing the state's transition to diversified agriculture.
- [peach harvesting](process_peach_harvesting.md) : the intensive manual harvesting that replaced the cotton fields.

## Tension

The rapid cotton expansion relies entirely on the brutal exploitation of enslaved African Americans and causes the systematic forced removal of the Cherokee Nation from northern Georgia.
