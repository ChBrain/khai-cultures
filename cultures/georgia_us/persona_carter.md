# Persona: Carter

## Taxonomy

[Southern hospitality](position_southern_hospitality.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A fifty-eight-year-old peach orchard manager in central Georgia. English, spoken with a slow, hospitable drawl, is his mother tongue, the language in which he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and writes his agricultural logs. Spanish [he wears as an overgarment](process_speaking_worn.md) in his daily orchard work, coordinating with seasonal agricultural crews. Yet the loss lies in the seam: The fast, policy-oriented talk of metropolitan developers like Evelyn and the digital demands of the Atlanta transit boards [he hears only like a distant echo](process_hearing_followed.md), feeling that the patient, season-dependent rhythm of farming is being forgotten in the state's modern corporate economy.

## Action

He manages [peach harvesting](process_peach_harvesting.md), utilizes resources under [the Atlanta transit plan](plan_atlanta_transit_expansion.md), and studies [New South](position_new_south.md).

## Shadow

His pride in traditional hospitality and rural independence can make him suspicious of state-wide zoning and transit regulations, fearing they will increase farming costs and lead to urban sprawl.

## Tell

He looks at a wooden crate of peaches and says hospitably: "A peach orchard requires patience and sun; you can't rush the harvest just because some developer in Atlanta wants a faster return on investment."
