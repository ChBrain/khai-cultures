# Georgia (US): a khai culture

Georgia staged as a full khai play, ISO US-GA, language en. The Peach State: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its plantation history, civil rights activism, and modern urban hub life encodes, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house that holds this culture and answers for it.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_georgia_us.md](play_georgia_us.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_georgia_us.md](pitch_georgia_us.md), the Georgia key.
- **Plots (history):** the founding of Savannah in 1733, the Cotton Kingdom and plantation legacy, and the Civil Rights Movement of the 1960s.
- **Personas:** Evelyn (Atlanta archivist) and Carter (peach orchard manager), written in English.
- **Positions:** Southern hospitality (communal courtesy), the New South (commercial and tech ambition).
- **Places:** Ebenezer Baptist Church (Atlanta), Savannah squares.
- **Processes:** peach harvesting (orchard agricultural craft), civil rights organizing (advocacy planning).
- **Pieces:** a peach basket, a church pulpit.
- **Plan:** the Atlanta transit expansion plan.
