# Persona: Evelyn

## Taxonomy

[New South](position_new_south.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A fifty-two-year-old archivist in Atlanta. English, spoken with a warm and rhythmic southern cadence, is her mother tongue, the language in which she [speaks, hears, and thinks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), the defining code of her professional life. She speaks no foreign languages. Yet the loss lies in the seam: The slower, tradition-bound agricultural talk of orchard managers like Carter and the quiet history of [Southern hospitality](position_southern_hospitality.md) [she reads only as historical records](process_reading_followed.md) on her terminal, feeling that the patient spiritual legacy of the community is being replaced by corporate-driven urban development.

## Action

She preserves [pulpit](piece_pulpit.md) archives, coordinates resources under [the Atlanta transit plan](plan_atlanta_transit_expansion.md), and studies [New South](position_new_south.md).

## Shadow

Her focus on academic research and urban development can make her elitist, overlooking the immediate material struggles of rural working-class families outside the metropolitan center.

## Tell

She looks at a historic letter and says thoughtfully: "Progress isn't just about office towers; it's a commitment to preserve the moral voices that demanded justice for the entire community."
