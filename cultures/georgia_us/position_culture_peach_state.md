# Position: the Peach State

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

Belonging to a state that carries a complex Southern legacy inside a soaring commercial ambition, from James Oglethorpe's utopian grid of public squares at Savannah, through the slaveholding Cotton Kingdom the gin created, to the pulpits of Atlanta that organized a nation's civil rights movement. It carries [Southern hospitality](position_southern_hospitality.md), the traditional courtesy and family focus born of that regional history, and it carries [New South](position_new_south.md), the postwar drive toward commercial growth, transit modernization, and metropolitan expansion. It speaks [Georgian English](position_language_en_us_ga.md), the drawling, hospitable cadence of the countryside beside the faster talk of the office tower. It hands down, without anyone choosing it, a taste for courtesy that runs deep and an ambition that runs just as deep beside it, so that welcoming a stranger and closing a deal can come from the very same instinct.

## Orders

That one act with respect and courtesy, welcome the newcomer, and keep the family and the neighbor close; that one honor the moral voice raised from the church pulpit as seriously as the boardroom's; that one support the growth of the metropolis and its transit while still keeping faith with the rural counties left slower by it; and that a matter of real weight, whether a harvest or a movement, be given the patience its season requires rather than rushed for someone else's convenience.

## Loses

Its moral ground when traditional courtesy becomes a polite cover that lets segregation and inequality go unaddressed rather than confronted; its balance when corporate interest overrides community preservation and gentrifies the very neighborhoods the civil rights movement organized in; and its footing when the divide between the fast-growing, diverse metropolitan center and the traditional rural interior hardens into open conflict over voting representation and the sharing of resources.

## Drives

Drives the orchard manager who reads the harvest by patience and season to distrust the developer who reads it by quarterly return, and drives the archivist who preserves the pulpit's old moral voice to see the office tower as only half the state's real progress. It lives in the grid Oglethorpe laid for a colony that first refused slavery and in the pulpit from which a nonviolent movement was organized to redeem the promise that grid never kept. It drives the whole state to hold its courtesy and its ambition together rather than let either one answer for the other, since a peach must still ripen on its own season even in a state building toward the next skyline.
