# Niger: a khai culture

Niger staged as a full khai play, ISO NE, working language fr (with Hausa,
Zarma-Songhai, Tamasheq, Fulfulde, Kanuri and Toubou as the tongues the country
truly speaks), as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the
cast, the roles its languages and faith encode, its places, its practices, and
its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_niger.md](play_niger.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_fr.md](pitch_fr.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Niger's
  profile (a collective and hierarchical society of deep respect for age and
  rank, restraint and modesty, patience and endurance before a hard climate, and
  the given word held as a bond) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the kingdoms and caravan cities, Queen Sarraounia,
  independence, and the land of sand and millet - each casting the elements it
  needs, and each staged soberly and without taking any side.
- **Personas:** Boubou Hama (writer and historian who set the country's oral
  memory down in writing), Abdoulaye Mamani (poet and novelist who restored Queen
  Sarraounia to memory), Sarraounia Mangou (the Azna queen-priestess of Lougou),
  and Mali Yaro (the great voice of Hausa song) as the cast, plus one archetype,
  the caravanner of the salt routes. Each is wired to the language engine by the
  language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** the desert and the nomadic life (the Sahara, the Air, the salt
  caravans, the freedom and endurance of the nomad), Sahelian hospitality (the
  sacred guest, the shared dish, the open mat), restraint and respect (the deference
  to elders, the held word, the dignity carried quietly).
- **Places:** Niamey (the capital on the great river) and Agadez (the caravan
  city of the Air with its earthen-minaret mosque).
- **Processes:** the azalai (the salt caravan across the Tenere to Bilma) and the
  Gerewol (the Wodaabe Fulani gathering of beauty and courtship).
- **Pieces:** the cross of Agadez (the chiseled silver pendant of the Tuareg) and
  millet (the grain that feeds the Sahel and the dish that opens the home).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain sources are recorded
in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the pitch is
its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never claimed; the
country is staged from within its own self-understanding, taking no political side
and dwelling on no hardship; the staging and the architecture are original work.
