# Russia: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces. The modern state and its wars are
treated soberly and critically on the public record; no regime is endorsed.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_ru.md](pitch_ru.md) is written from Russia's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no
number. The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the
record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Russia score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ---------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 93                     |
| Individualism                 | 39                     |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 36                     |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 95                     |
| Long Term Orientation         | 81                     |
| Indulgence                    | 20                     |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published
by Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; Russia's very high Power
Distance and Uncertainty Avoidance and its very low Indulgence are notable in the
published data.

from the public historical record. They are credited here and never claimed; the
staging is original work. The Soviet repressions and the modern state's imperial
turn and wars are noted soberly and critically, with no atrocity detail and no
endorsement of any regime.

The two defining personas, [Nina](persona_nina.md) and
[Dmitri](persona_dmitri.md), are archetypes, not historical individuals:
composites that carry the tension between the older generation (the dacha, the
Orthodox faith, the memory of the war, the patience given to every regime) and
the younger one (the post-2022 emigration of the dissenting and the thinning of
Russian into the global English of exile).
