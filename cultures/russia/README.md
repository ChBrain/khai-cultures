# Russia: a khai culture

Russia staged as a full khai play, ISO RU, language ru. A vast land and a strong
state, as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the
roles its language and faith encode, its places, its practices, and its defining
objects, all tuned by a single pitch. A portrait of a civilization of great depth
and its recurring tragedy, not a brief for any regime.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_russia.md](play_russia.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_ru.md](pitch_ru.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Russia's
  profile (vast and hierarchical, deferent to a strong state yet inwardly distant
  from it, enduring and patient, maximalist and melancholic, spiritually deep and
  restrained) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Third Rome and the gathering of the lands, Pushkin and
  the golden age, the Great Patriotic War (1941-1945), and the Soviet collapse and
  the new state - the last handled critically and soberly, the recurring derzhava
  pattern and its cost to the people and the neighbours, endorsing nothing.
- **Personas:** Alexander Pushkin, Leo Tolstoy, and Peter the Great as the historic
  cast, plus two defining archetypes, Nina (older, the dacha and the Orthodox
  faith, who endured every regime) and Dmitri (younger, among those who left after
  2022, carrying the great tongue into exile). Each is wired to the language engine
  by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** russkiy yazyk (the Russian language, the great literary tongue
  Pushkin forged and the imperial lingua franca), derzhava (the all-powerful
  central state and the people's distant, enduring, submissive-yet-resentful
  relationship to it), russkaya dusha (the Russian soul, the depth, the suffering,
  and the spiritual maximalism, with Orthodoxy and the messianic sense woven in).
- **Places:** Moscow (the Kremlin, the gathering of the lands, the seat of state
  and Church), Saint Petersburg (Peter's window to Europe, the city of the great
  literature, the Europe-facing counterpoint to Moscow's power).
- **Processes:** zastolye (the long feast of toasts, where the soul is poured out),
  dacha (the summer cottage and its potato plot, the private refuge from the state).
- **Pieces:** ikona (the Orthodox icon, the window to heaven), matryoshka (the
  nesting doll, Russia within Russia, the layered self).
