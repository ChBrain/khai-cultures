# Tunisia: a khai culture

Tunisia staged as a full khai play, ISO TN, language ar (Modern Standard
Arabic with a distinctly Tunisian voice and a Derja flavor where it reads
natural). A small Mediterranean land of ancient layers, faith, learning, the
olive, and the sea, as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as
the cast, the roles its language and faith encode, its places, its practices,
and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_tunisia.md](play_tunisia.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_ar.md](pitch_ar.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Tunisia's profile (a moderate, educated, Mediterranean self-image, warm and
  understated, group-minded and wary of excess) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** Carthage and Hannibal, Kairouan and the dawn of Islam in
  the Maghreb, Al-Andalus and malouf, and Ibn Khaldun and the modern nation -
  each casting the elements it needs, each layer laid soberly over the one
  before.
- **Personas:** Hannibal (Carthage and the Punic depth), Ibn Khaldun (the
  Tunis-born father of the science of society), Aboul-Qacem Echebbi (the national
  poet of the will to live), and Saliha (the keeper of malouf) as the historic
  cast, plus one archetype, the olive farmer (the land, the olive, and the
  craft). Each is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** the bridge (the Mediterranean crossroads, open and moderate),
  learning and faith (Kairouan, the Zaytuna, and the love of the book), the olive
  and the sea (the land and the livelihood, patience like the tree).
- **Places:** Carthage and Tunis (the layers from Punic ruin to the old medina
  and the blue of Sidi Bou Said), Kairouan (the first great city of Islam in the
  Maghreb and its Great Mosque).
- **Processes:** malouf (the Andalusian song kept by voice), couscous (the Friday
  table that binds the generations).
- **Pieces:** harissa (the flavor that the Tunisian table is known by), the blue
  and white (the Andalusian and Mediterranean color become the face of the
  country).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the deep layers, the coming of Islam, the Andalusian migration, and the
modern nation are treated soberly on the public record, taking no political side
and dwelling on no upheaval; the staging and the architecture are original work.
