# Libya: a khai culture

Libya staged as a full khai play, ISO LY, language ar. A great desert nation
between the Mediterranean and the deep Sahara, ancient and hospitable, as a
theatre: its long history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its
language and customs encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects,
all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_libya.md](play_libya.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_ar.md](pitch_ar.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Libya's
  profile (a collectivism centred on family and tribe, a deep respect for the
  guest and the elder, patience bred by the desert, and a strong attachment to
  the near and the known) and naming no dimension. The Arabic is written in a
  distinctly Libyan voice, not a generic register.
- **Plots (history):** the Garamantes and the desert civilization, the ancient
  cities of the coast (Leptis Magna, Sabratha, and Cyrene), the Arab and Islamic
  age and the oasis caravan routes, and the bedouin heritage and the desert
  teacher - each casting the elements it needs, and each kept strictly on
  enduring cultural and historical ground.
- **Personas:** Omar al-Mukhtar (the revered Quran teacher of the Cyrenaican
  badia, remembered for his patience and dignity), Septimius Severus (the son of
  Leptis Magna who became emperor of Rome), Khalifa al-Tillisi (the Libyan writer
  and scholar who recorded the country's heritage and dialect), and Ahmad Rafiq
  al-Mahdawi (the poet of the homeland), plus one archetype, the Ghadames caravan
  trader. Each is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** hospitality (the bedouin law of the honoured guest, the tea, the
  kept word), the desert (the Sahara as a land of settlement, not emptiness, with
  its oases, foggaras, and caravan routes), and the ancient heritage (the layered
  stone and sand of Phoenician, Greek, Roman, and Arab Libya).
- **Places:** Leptis Magna (among the finest Roman ruins anywhere, on the
  Tripolitanian coast) and Ghadames (the pearl of the desert, with its covered
  lanes).
- **Processes:** the tea (the rite of hospitality, served over three sips) and
  the caravan (the desert crossing that made the Sahara a road, not a barrier).
- **Pieces:** the date palm (the pillar of the oasis and the sign of life in the
  sand) and bazin (the shared dish, with couscous, that makes the table one).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the staging keeps strictly to enduring cultural and historical ground -
the ancient cities, the desert and oases, the Amazigh and bedouin heritage,
hospitality, and the people - and any historical change is noted soberly and
without detail. Contemporary politics and conflict are out of scope. The staging
and the architecture are original work.
