# the United Arab Emirates: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain sources behind the plots,
personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_ar.md](pitch_ar.md) is written from the United Arab Emirates'
profile on Geert Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and
carries no number. The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on
the record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | UAE score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 90                  |
| Individualism                 | 25                  |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 50                  |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 80                  |
| Long Term Orientation         | 30                  |
| Indulgence                    | 40                  |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; the UAE is commonly read with the
Arab-world cluster, marked by high power distance and strong group orientation.

## Historical and cultural sources

The plots, the personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn from the
public record and the cultural heritage of the Emirates. They are credited here
and never claimed; the staging is original work. The founding and the modern rise
are staged soberly from within Emirati self-understanding, taking no political
side; contemporary politics is out of scope.

The poet [Al Majidi ibn Daher](persona_ibn_daher.md) and the nokhada
[Mubarak bin London](persona_bin_london.md) are figures of the public record and

a composite of the men who dived for the pearl that sustained the coast before
