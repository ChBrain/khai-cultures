# Plan: One People, One Nation, One Destiny

## Taxonomy

The open mandate the nation set itself in the motto stamped on its arms: the endless work of closing the gap [the plural society](position_the_plural_society.md) holds open between the many it is and the one it has sworn to be, of reckoning honestly with [the plantation legacy](position_the_plantation_legacy.md) that made the people and the divide alike, of keeping [the Land of Many Waters](position_the_land_of_many_waters.md) and its first peoples whole, and of turning the sudden oil windfall into the lifting of the many rather than the few.

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Direction

Heal the racial-political divide between the Indo- and Afro-Guyanese, making "one people" true in fact and not only on the arms; steward the oil windfall justly, so the sudden wealth lifts the whole nation and is not captured by a part or squandered against the divide; protect the forest and the rivers, keeping the Land of Many Waters whole and the first peoples honoured as the world comes for the country's wealth; and hold all the peoples, Indo- and Afro-Guyanese, Amerindian, and the rest, as one nation with one destiny.

## Orders

- To the canecutter like [the canecutter](persona_the_canecutter.md): be honoured as the maker of the nation, not romanced and forgotten; let the dignity of the bound and the freed who built the country be the republic's first debt paid.
- To the bridge-builder like [Walter Rodney](persona_walter_rodney.md): call the peoples across the line into a common cause, and refuse the politics that holds the many apart to rule them.
- To the nation: keep working to make the motto true, never mistaking the words on the arms for the keeping of them, and turn the windfall and the forest into the lifting and the holding of one people.

## Implementation

A young republic, independent since 1966 and still working its founding question, that has held its plural peoples on one coast through deep division and made a shared life of festival and faith and food across the line, and that now stands before a sudden oil fortune and a contested, mostly untouched interior. The divide endures in the politics and the count; the windfall is just beginning and its justice not yet decided; the forest is still whole and its keeping not yet secured; and the standing question is whether a small plural nation can make "one people, one nation, one destiny" real before the riches that came to keep it test it apart.
