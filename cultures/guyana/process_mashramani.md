# Process: Mashramani

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

The whole nation on Republic Day, the twenty-third of February: the costume bands and the float parade, the steel pan and the soca trucks, the masqueraders in feather and sequin pouring through Georgetown, and the crowds lining the route. It takes only the day and the streets, and a country named the Co-operative Republic turns out together for "Mash," the festival of its own republic.

## Direction

Toward joy and the nation made one in the street: Mashramani, the name carried from an Amerindian word for a celebration after hard co-operative work, marks the day Guyana became a republic and turns it into the country's great shared carnival. It is the plural nation dancing as one body for a day, every community in the same parade, the costume and the soca and the pan making a single festival of the many.

## Lever

It is driven by the need of a plural people for a rite that belongs to no one community and so to all of them - a republic's carnival in place of any one faith's festival, a day the whole country can be Guyanese together. It holds against the racial-political divide it dances over; against it stands the hard truth that one bright day in the street does not by itself close the divide the rest of the year holds open.

## Echo

Mashramani makes audible what [the plural society](position_the_plural_society.md) needs: a festival of the republic itself, belonging to all the peoples and to none alone, where the many can be one in the street for a day. Into this parade [Cheddi Jagan](persona_cheddi_jagan.md)'s long fought-for republic comes dancing, the nation he helped make celebrating the day it was declared.
