# Position: the Land of Many Waters

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The vast interior behind the thin coast: the rainforest that covers most of the country almost untouched, the great rivers - Essequibo, Demerara, Berbice - that gave the land its Amerindian name, the savannahs of the Rupununi, and Kaieteur Falls thundering down in a single immense drop. It is the indigenous heartland, the home of the first peoples before any colony, and it holds the Essequibo, the western region long claimed by a neighbour and held by Guyana. It is the green deep of the nation, the part of the country most of the country has never seen, and the ground on which its future, in forest and in oil, now turns.

## Orders

That Guyana is not only its coast but its immense interior, and that the forest, the rivers, and the first peoples who live among them are at the heart of what the nation is; that the land and its waters are a trust to be kept whole, not spent; and that the indigenous peoples are the oldest claim on the country and the first to be honoured when the nation reckons what it owes its own ground.

## Loses

Ground when the interior is treated as empty - a frontier to be cut, mined, drilled, and emptied for the coast's gain, the forest and the first peoples counted as nothing against the wealth beneath them; and ground, too, when the coast forgets the interior entirely, governing a country whose green heart it neither knows nor visits, so that the Land of Many Waters becomes a name on the map and not a place the nation holds.

## Drives

Lives in the first Amerindian member of parliament, who carried the voice of the first peoples and their land into the nation's councils. It drives Guyana to remember that it is the Land of Many Waters, to keep its forest and its rivers whole, and to honour the peoples who held this ground first.
