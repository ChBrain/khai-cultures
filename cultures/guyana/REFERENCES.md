# Guyana: References

The provenance behind this culture: the cultural framing that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-record sources behind the plots,
personas, places, and pieces.

## Cultural framing

The pitch [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md) is written from Guyana's place in the
Caribbean cultural profile and names no dimension and carries no number. Guyana is
not separately scored in the most-cited national-culture datasets; the pitch is
drawn from the broadly documented character of the English-speaking Caribbean and
of Guyana's plural society - warm and communal hospitality, a layered religiosity
across Hindu, Muslim, and Christian faith, strong family and village ties,
resilience and pride, and the deep racial-political divide between Indo- and
Afro-Guyanese. This framing is kept here so the basis is on the record without
entering the prose.

## Historical sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn
from the public historical record and staged from within Guyana's own
self-understanding, taking no side. They are credited here and never claimed; the
staging is original work. The hard history at the heart of the story - slavery,
the abolition that freed the enslaved, Indian indenture, and the Indo-/Afro-Guyanese
racial-political divide - is staged soberly on the public record and without blame,
with no atrocity detail, as the gap between the promise of "one people, one nation,
one destiny" and the reality lived under it.
