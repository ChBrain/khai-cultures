# Place: the Interior

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Shown

The vast green deep behind the thin coast: rainforest covering most of the country almost untouched, the great rivers Essequibo, Demerara, and Berbice winding down through it, the savannahs of the Rupununi opening to the south, and Kaieteur Falls plunging in one immense unbroken drop into the gorge below. The indigenous heartland, where the river villages of the first peoples stand among trees no road reaches, the gold and diamond grounds, and the contested western region of the Essequibo. The part of the country most of the country has never seen, the Land of Many Waters made ground.

## Offers

A place where the nation is something other than its coast - the forest kept whole, the rivers and the falls, the first peoples' home, and the immense reserve of a country's natural wealth; the green heart that makes Guyana South American as well as Caribbean, and the trust on which its future, in forest and in oil, is now being decided.

## Withheld

The simplicity of an empty wilderness. The interior carries the gap between its untouched look and the claims pressing on it - the first peoples whose home it is and whose land is contested, the gold and timber and oil that draw the coast and the world to cut and drill it, and the western Essequibo long claimed by a neighbour. The Land of Many Waters is no blank space but a place held, lived in, and reckoned for, the country's future and its oldest claim at once.
