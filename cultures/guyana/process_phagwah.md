# Process: Phagwah

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

Anyone with a fistful of abeer and powder on a spring morning: the Indo-Guyanese community first, gathering at the mandir and the open ground for the festival of colour carried from India, and then the neighbours and the whole village joining in, smeared in red and pink and laughing. It takes only the powder, the coloured water, and the willingness to be drenched, and the street becomes a riot of colour.

## Direction

Toward renewal and the sharing of joy across the line: Phagwah, the Guyanese name for Holi, is the Hindu spring festival of colour the indentured carried from India and planted on this coast, the dousing in abeer that washes the year clean. Brought by one community, it has become a day the others join, the colour landing on every face alike, a festival of one heritage opened into a festival of the plural nation.

## Lever

It is driven by the deep Guyanese habit of the shared festival, the calendar of one small nation holding Diwali and Eid and Christmas and Mashramani and this, each community's day open to the rest. It holds against the racial-political divide by crossing it in play, the colour erasing for a morning the line between the peoples; against it stands the truth that the divide returns when the colour washes off, and that a shared festival is a hope of unity, not yet its keeping.

## Echo

Phagwah makes visible what [the plural society](position_the_plural_society.md) reaches for: a festival of one community opened to all, the colour landing on every face without asking which, the many made one in play across the very line that divides them. To this colour [Walter Rodney](persona_walter_rodney.md)'s hope speaks, that the peoples who play together in the spring might stand together the rest of the year.
