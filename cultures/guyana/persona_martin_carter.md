# Persona: Martin Carter

## Taxonomy

[the plantation legacy](position_the_plantation_legacy.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The poet of the struggle, author of "Poems of Resistance," who wrote "I come from the nigger yard of yesterday" and made of the colony's hardship and hope a national voice, jailed for his politics and never silenced. Fierce, lyrical, and rooted in the canefield and the yard, he gave the bound and the freed their words and the nation its conscience in verse. [English](position_language_en_gy.md) he has whole and forges into poetry: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in his mother tongue, the Creolese-shaded English of Georgetown and the estate, and he [writes it drafted](process_writing_drafted.md) into lines that a whole people came to know by heart. The literature of the English language and of resistance the world over he [read and followed deeply](process_reading_followed.md), and [carried its music into his own as if it were his](process_speaking_carried.md). He took the English the plantation had imposed and turned it back on the plantation as poetry, claiming the nigger yard in the very tongue that had named it, making the colonial language sing the colonised's resistance - so that the gap at the limit of the tongue became the seam where an imposed language was seized and made to speak for those it was meant to keep down.

## Action

In [the New Horizon](plot_04_the_new_horizon.md) he gives [the plantation legacy](position_the_plantation_legacy.md) its voice, turning the canefield's bondage and the yard's hope into the poetry by which the nation remembers what it came from. He carries the bound and the freed into the country's own words, and makes of the estate's hard history a literature the whole plural nation can hold as its own.

## Shadow

The voice that names the wound can be honoured into harmlessness - the poem quoted at the ceremony, carved and recited, while the bondage it indicts is treated as safely past; the poet made a national treasure and the hard truth he carried softened into pride, the comfort of the famous line mistaken for the keeping of its demand.

## Tell

He claims the nigger yard of yesterday in his own first lines and refuses to let the country forget it - turning the language of the estate against the estate, and making the bound and the freed speak in poetry that outlasted the cane.
