# Persona: Cheddi Jagan

## Taxonomy

[the plural society](position_the_plural_society.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The dentist born on a sugar estate to indentured parents, who left the canefield for a profession and then gave his life to the working people who stayed, leading the colony's labour movement and its long march toward independence as the champion of the cane labourer and the Indo-Guyanese. Earnest, tireless, principled, and bound to the estate he rose from, he fought for the many and watched the nation he loved split along the very line he had hoped to dissolve. English he has whole and uses as the working tongue of the cause: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md), and [writes](process_writing_drafted.md) in his mother tongue, the Creolese-inflected English of the estate and the platform, and turns it into the speech of the labour meeting and the page of the political pamphlet. The Bhojpuri of his grandparents, the tongue they carried bound from India, he [catches only by ear](process_reading_caught.md) in a remembered phrase at home, and never had whole. He led an Indian-descended people in the English of the empire that had bound them to the cane, the only tongue that could reach the whole plural nation at once, and the ancestral Bhojpuri thinned in his mouth to a few words of home, so that the gap at the limit of the tongue ran between the leader and the very heritage in whose name he rose.

## Action

In [the Road to Independence](plot_03_the_road_to_independence.md) he leads the labour movement and the colony toward self-rule, building [the plural society](position_the_plural_society.md) into a political force even as the racial-political divide hardens around him. He carries the cane labourer's cause to the brink of nationhood, and gives the plural nation both its first great champion of the working people and, in the divide he could not close, its hardest unfinished question.

## Shadow

The champion of one people can become, against his will, the standard-bearer of the divide: the leader who fought for the many seen by the other half as the leader of a side, his very loyalty to the people he rose from read as the sharpening of the line. The cause that meant to unite the workers across race can be turned, by the politics of the count, into the deepening of the split it set out to heal.

## Tell

He leaves the dentist's chair for the labour platform and never returns to it, and speaks for the cane labourer in the English of the estate to the very end - meaning the whole nation by it, and reaching, in the end, the half that was already his.
