# Plot: the Road to Independence

## Taxonomy

[play_guyana](play_guyana.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

By the middle of the twentieth century British Guiana stirs for self-rule. The cane labourer and the worker want a voice, the empire is loosening its hold across the world, and on this coast a labour movement rises to lead the colony out from under the crown and toward a nation of its own.

## Action

[Cheddi Jagan](persona_cheddi_jagan.md) and the labour movement build a mass party of the working people and win the colony's first votes under universal suffrage, carrying it to the threshold of self-rule; but the movement splits along the racial line the plantation drew, into a party drawing chiefly on the Indo-Guyanese and another on the Afro-Guyanese, and the politics of the count sets the two peoples against each other even as the nation forms. Independence comes in 1966, and the country becomes a republic, the only English-speaking nation in South America - born free at last, and born already divided, the racial-political split that will shadow it set hard in the very moment of its birth.

## Stage

- [Cheddi Jagan](persona_cheddi_jagan.md): the dentist and labour leader who champions the working people and carries the colony toward self-rule.
- [the plural society](position_the_plural_society.md): the Indo- and Afro-Guyanese nation and the divide that hardens into politics on the road to independence.
- [Georgetown](place_georgetown.md): the seat of the colony's politics, where the labour movement marched and the republic was declared.

## Tension

The nation wins its freedom and its divide in the same passage - the labour movement that meant to lift all the working people split by the politics of race into two peoples voting against each other, so that independence arrives carrying the very gap "one people, one nation" was meant to close. The plot stages the birth of the republic and the hardening of its divide as one moment, the promise and the wound declared together.
