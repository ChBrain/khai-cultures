# Piece: Demerara rum

## Taxonomy

Parent group: pieces

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Place

In the glass at the rumshop and on the shelves of the world: the dark, deep rum distilled on the Demerara from the molasses of the sugar estates, aged in old wooden stills no other distillery keeps, and shipped under names known wherever rum is drunk. From the village rumshop where the coast limes at evening to the connoisseur's bottle abroad, the spirit of the canefield poured.

## Load Bearing

It stands for the sugar estate turned from bondage into renown. Demerara rum is the canefield's other harvest, distilled from the molasses of the same plantations that the bound and the freed worked, carrying [the plantation legacy](position_the_plantation_legacy.md) in every dark glass - the hardship of the estate become, in the bottle, the country's most celebrated export. It is the rumshop's lime and the world's prize at once, the spirit of the cane that made the people, drunk by the people the cane made. Without this rum the canefield's long history would have no glass the world raises in the country's name.

## Apparent

A measure of dark, heavy rum, near-black and rich, the smell of molasses and old wood over it. Plain spirit in a plain glass at the counter; its weight is in the canefield it came from, the wooden stills that make it like no other, and the estates of the bound and the freed whose long labour is, in the end, what fills the glass.
