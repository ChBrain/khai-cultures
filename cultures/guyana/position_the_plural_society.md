# Position: the plural society

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The fact of a nation made of many peoples and no majority: Indo-Guyanese and Afro-Guyanese the two largest, with Amerindian, Portuguese, Chinese, and Mixed among them, brought or descended from three continents and the first peoples of this one, set side by side on a single thin coast. It is the promise of "one people, one nation, one destiny," a country that must be held together not by one blood but by the will of many to be one. And it is a promise founded on a divide: the same plantation that gathered the peoples set them against one another, and the racial-political split between Indo- and Afro-Guyanese runs through the vote, the village, and the nation as the gap between what the motto declares and what the country has lived.

## Orders

That the nation belongs to all its peoples equally, none of them more native than another to a country they were all made into; that the plural society is a standard the nation can be measured and found wanting against, and held to; and that the work of the republic is the endless task of closing the distance between the many it is and the one it has sworn to become.

## Loses

Ground when the divide is worked rather than healed - when the line between the peoples is counted at the ballot and stoked for power, so that the many are held apart to be ruled; and ground, too, to the despair that, seeing how old and deep the split runs, gives up on the one nation and settles for two peoples sharing a coast, forgetting that the same history that divided them also made them, together, a single people the world calls Guyanese.

## Drives

Lives in the leader who championed the working people and the Indo-Guyanese and led the colony toward self-rule even as the divide hardened, and in the scholar who sought to bridge the line between the peoples and call them to a common cause. It drives Guyana to keep measuring itself against its own motto and to keep working to make the many one.
