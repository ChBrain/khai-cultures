# Persona: Walter Rodney

## Taxonomy

[the plural society](position_the_plural_society.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The historian and scholar, author of "How Europe Underdeveloped Africa," who came home to Guyana and sought to call its peoples across the racial line into a common cause, organising African and Indian working people together, and whose life was cut short in 1980. Brilliant, principled, and unafraid, he turned his learning into a summons to unity and paid for it. English he has whole and as a scholar's instrument: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in his mother tongue, and he [drafts](process_writing_drafted.md) it on the page into history and argument that crossed the world. The languages of his African subject and his global study he [followed deeply in his reading](process_reading_followed.md) and [carried into his work as nearly his own](process_speaking_carried.md), drawing the wider world into the Guyanese cause. He wrote of how the African past had been underdeveloped, and called the African and Indian working people of Guyana to one struggle, in the English the empire had left as the only tongue all of them shared - the common ground and the colonial inheritance at once - so that the gap at the limit of the tongue was the one English both bridged and marked, the divide it could carry across and the conquest it could not undo.

## Action

In [the New Horizon](plot_04_the_new_horizon.md) he works to bridge the racial divide that [the plural society](position_the_plural_society.md) holds open, summoning the working people of both communities to a common cause beyond the line the plantation drew. He gives the plural nation its sharpest vision of a unity earned across race, and, in the cost he bore, the measure of how hard that unity is to win.

## Shadow

The call to cross the line can be heard as a threat by those whose power rests on the line's remaining - the unifier read as the danger, the very breadth of his summons making him the one the divided order most feared. The vision of one people, pressed too far ahead of the nation ready to hold it, can leave the bridge-builder standing alone above the gap he meant to close.

## Tell

He turns a historian's learning into a summons across the divide and asks the African and Indian worker to see one cause, not two peoples - the scholar who came home to call his country to itself, and was not allowed to finish.
