# Piece: pepperpot

## Taxonomy

Parent group: pieces

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Place

On the Christmas table and the holiday morning across the country: a dark, rich stew of meat slow-cooked in cassareep, the boiled-down juice of the bitter cassava, spiced with cinnamon, clove, and hot pepper, eaten with plait bread and kept on the stove for days. The national dish of Guyana, given to the whole plural nation by the first peoples whose root and recipe it is.

## Load Bearing

It stands for the indigenous gift at the root of the nation's table. Pepperpot is Amerindian in origin, made with cassareep drawn from the cassava the first peoples cultivated, and it became the dish of the whole country - Indo-, Afro-, and every Guyanese keeping it on the holiday stove, the one national dish given not by the coast's many arrivals but by the land's first people. It ties the nation back to [the Land of Many Waters](position_the_land_of_many_waters.md) and the peoples who held the ground first, the indigenous heart at the centre of the plural table. Without this pot the country would have no dish that is the gift of its own first people.

## Apparent

A heavy pot of dark stew, meat gone soft and glossy in near-black cassareep, the smell of clove and pepper and cinnamon over it. Plain and homely on the stove; its weight is in the cassava root the first peoples tamed, the cassareep that lets it keep for days, and the holiday table on which the whole plural nation eats the indigenous gift as its own.
