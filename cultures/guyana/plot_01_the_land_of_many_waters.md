# Plot: the Land of Many Waters

## Taxonomy

[play_guyana](play_guyana.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

Long before any colony, the indigenous peoples live along the rivers and in the forest of the land they know by its many waters, the Essequibo, the Demerara, the Berbice winding down from the deep interior to the Atlantic shore. Then European ships find the coast, and the Dutch come first to trade and to plant.

## Action

The Dutch settle the river mouths, drain the coastal swamp below the sea, and begin the diked plantation coast; the British take the colonies in their turn and unite them as British Guiana, ruling the thin coast and naming the vast interior theirs while the first peoples keep the rivers and the forest. The land of many waters becomes a colony, its name an Amerindian inheritance and its government a European import, the indigenous heartland held behind a coast now turned to cane. Generations on, [Stephen Campbell](persona_stephen_campbell.md) will carry the first peoples' claim from those rivers into the parliament of the nation the colony becomes.

## Stage

- [Stephen Campbell](persona_stephen_campbell.md): the first Amerindian member of parliament, carrying the first peoples and their interior into the nation's councils.
- [the Land of Many Waters](position_the_land_of_many_waters.md): the rainforest interior, the rivers, and the indigenous heartland behind the coast.
- [the Interior](place_the_interior.md): the forest, the rivers, and Kaieteur Falls, the home of the first peoples and the green deep of the land.

## Tension

The country is named in the tongue of the first peoples who held its waters, and then governed by a colony that knew only its coast - the oldest claim on the land made the least heard, the interior counted as the colony's possession while its first inhabitants kept living the rivers as home. The plot sets the gap that runs through the whole story, between the Land of Many Waters as the first peoples' home and as the colony's, and later the nation's, frontier.
