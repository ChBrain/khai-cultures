# Play: Guyana

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the house that holds this staging and answers for it.

## Name

The Co-operative Republic of Guyana, on the northern shoulder of South America, between Venezuela, Brazil, and Suriname, its narrow coast facing the Atlantic and its vast interior climbing into rainforest and savannah. ISO GY, language en, the only English-speaking nation on the continent, Caribbean in culture and a member of CARICOM. A small country of many peoples and many waters, named in the Amerindian tongue the Land of Many Waters.

## Arc

A land that was first the home of indigenous peoples along its rivers, then a Dutch and later a British colony, British Guiana, drained and diked into a sugar coast below the sea. That coast was a plantation society built on the enslaved labour of Africans, and then, after abolition, on indentured labourers brought from India, and from Portugal and China besides, until a uniquely plural people had been made: Indo-Guyanese and Afro-Guyanese the two largest, with Amerindian, Portuguese, Chinese, and Mixed among them, no group a majority. The same plantation that made the people set them against one another, and the long racial-political divide between Indo- and Afro-Guyanese runs as the gap between the promise of "one people, one nation" and the reality lived under it. The country won independence in 1966 and became a republic, the only English-speaking nation in South America, Caribbean in its rum and its cricket and its Creolese, and South American in its forest. Behind the thin coast lies an immense interior almost untouched, the rivers, Kaieteur Falls thundering down in a single drop, the contested Essequibo, and the indigenous heartland. And in this generation a vast offshore oil discovery has begun to remake the nation's whole future at once. Under it all runs one nerve: a warm, hospitable, plural people, layered in Hindu and Muslim and Christian faith, proud and resilient, asking whether the many can at last be held as one.

## Triggers

**[Plot 1: the Land of Many Waters](plot_01_the_land_of_many_waters.md)**

Before the coast was a colony the interior was already home, and [the Land of Many Waters](position_the_land_of_many_waters.md) held its indigenous peoples along the rivers; then Dutch and after them British settlers came to the shore, and [Stephen Campbell](persona_stephen_campbell.md) would one day carry the first peoples' voice into the nation's parliament.

**[Plot 2: the Bound and the Freed](plot_02_the_bound_and_the_freed.md)**

On the sugar coast [the plantation legacy](position_the_plantation_legacy.md) made the people: Africans enslaved on the estates, and after abolition indentured labourers brought from India, and [the canecutter](persona_the_canecutter.md) standing for all of them, the bound and the freed who cut the cane that built the country.

**[Plot 3: the Road to Independence](plot_03_the_road_to_independence.md)**

In the twentieth century [Cheddi Jagan](persona_cheddi_jagan.md) and the labour movement led the colony toward [the plural society](position_the_plural_society.md) and self-rule, the racial-political split hardening even as independence came in 1966 and the republic was declared.

**[Plot 4: the New Horizon](plot_04_the_new_horizon.md)**

In the decades since, [Walter Rodney](persona_walter_rodney.md) sought to bridge the divide and [Martin Carter](persona_martin_carter.md) gave the struggle its poetry, while the nation found its Caribbean and CARICOM self and stood before [the Interior](place_the_interior.md), the forest, and a sudden oil future.

## Stakes

Whether a small plural nation can heal the racial-political divide between its peoples and at last make "one people, one nation, one destiny" true rather than merely declared; whether it can steward a sudden and enormous oil windfall justly, so the wealth lifts the many and not the few; whether it can keep its vast forest and its rivers, the Land of Many Waters, intact while the world remakes it; and whether it can hold all its peoples, Indo- and Afro-Guyanese, Amerindian, and the rest, as one - or whether the old divide the plantation left behind pulls the promise apart.
