# Plot: the New Horizon

## Taxonomy

[play_guyana](play_guyana.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

The young republic carries its divide into its decades, the politics still running along the line the plantation drew. Some seek to bridge it and some to sing it, the nation takes its place among its Caribbean neighbours, and then, far offshore beneath the sea, a discovery is made that will remake the whole country's future at once.

## Action

[Walter Rodney](persona_walter_rodney.md) comes home and works to call the African and Indian working people across the divide into a common cause, and [Martin Carter](persona_martin_carter.md) gives the long struggle its poetry, the nation finding its conscience and its voice. Guyana takes its Caribbean and CARICOM identity, English-speaking and rum-and-cricket among its island neighbours though South American in its forest. And then an enormous offshore oil discovery begins to transform the country, a sudden windfall set against the standing questions of the divide still unhealed and the vast interior, [the Interior](place_the_interior.md), the forest and the rivers, still to be kept whole. The new horizon opens on both a fortune and a reckoning at once.

## Stage

- [Walter Rodney](persona_walter_rodney.md): the historian who seeks to bridge the racial divide and call the working people to a common cause.
- [Martin Carter](persona_martin_carter.md): the poet who gives the struggle and the plantation's memory their national voice.
- [the Interior](place_the_interior.md): the forest, the rivers, and the offshore wealth on which the nation's coming future turns.

## Tension

The nation arrives at sudden wealth with its oldest question still open - a fortune from beneath the sea set against a divide not yet healed and a forest not yet safe, the windfall able to lift the many or deepen the split, to fund the country or to spend its green heart. The plot stages the new horizon as both promise and test, the chance to make "one people, one nation, one destiny" true at last, or to lose it to the very riches that came to keep it.
