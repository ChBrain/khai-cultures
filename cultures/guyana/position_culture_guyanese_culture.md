# Position: Guyanese culture

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The belonging to a small plural nation made of many peoples and no majority, [the plural society](position_the_plural_society.md) of Indo-Guyanese and Afro-Guyanese, Amerindian, Portuguese, Chinese, and Mixed, held together on a single thin coast by the promise of one people, one nation, one destiny rather than by one blood. It carries [the plantation legacy](position_the_plantation_legacy.md), the sugar estate that made the country, its village and its logie, its rum and its food and its speech, and the hierarchy of bound and freed whose memory still shapes the land it built. It carries [the Land of Many Waters](position_the_land_of_many_waters.md), the vast rainforest and the great rivers behind the thin coast, the indigenous heartland that was home before any colony, and the Essequibo the nation holds and a neighbour claims. It speaks [Guyanese English](position_language_en_gy.md) and the Creolese grown up over three continents' silenced mother tongues, and it carries, without ever choosing it, a warmth layered in Hindu and Muslim and Christian faith, and now the sudden weight of an oil-rich future arriving all at once.

## Orders

That the nation belongs to all its peoples equally, none of them more native than another to a country the plantation made them into together; that the estate's old hierarchy of owner and owned is a thing to be reckoned with and never repeated, the line between bound and freed never drawn again; that the interior, its forest and its rivers and its first peoples, is held as a trust and not a frontier to be emptied for the coast's gain; and that the many keep working, village by village and generation by generation, to close the distance between the motto they have sworn and the divide they have lived.

## Loses

Ground when the racial-political divide between Indo- and Afro-Guyanese is worked rather than healed, counted at the ballot and stoked for power so the many are held apart to be ruled; ground when the plantation's hardship is hidden behind the romance of the village and the divide it set between African and Indian labour is left to harden into the nation's own; and ground when the coast forgets its own interior, governing a country whose green heart it neither knows nor visits, so the sudden oil wealth lifts the few at the capital and leaves the forest, the rivers, and the first peoples who were there before any colony unheard.

## Drives

Drives whoever is raised in it to measure the nation against its own motto and to keep working, in the face of an old and deep divide, to make the many one; drives it to honour the bound and the freed who cut the cane that built the country, and to carry their memory rather than let it be smoothed into nostalgia. Drives the Guyanese to remember the land is not only its coast but its immense interior, to keep the forest and the rivers whole and to honour the peoples who held that ground first, and to steward whatever wealth the country finds, whether in cane or in oil, so it lifts all its peoples together rather than the few.
