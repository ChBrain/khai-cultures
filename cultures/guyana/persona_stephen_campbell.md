# Persona: Stephen Campbell

## Taxonomy

[the Land of Many Waters](position_the_land_of_many_waters.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The first Amerindian member of parliament, who carried the voice of the indigenous peoples and their land from the rivers and the bush into the nation's councils, championing the first peoples in a country whose politics had been made on the coast. Steadfast, dignified, and rooted in the interior, he stood for those whose claim on the land was the oldest and least heard. His indigenous mother tongue he has whole and from the first: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), [hears](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md), and [writes](process_writing_mother_tongue.md) in the tongue of his people, the language of the river village and the heartland. The [English](position_language_en_gy.md) of the colony and the parliament he [carried as his second tongue](process_speaking_borrowed.md), having [followed it well enough on the page](process_reading_followed.md) to take his people's case into the chamber where the nation was governed. He spoke for the first peoples of the Land of Many Waters, but had to speak in the coast's English to be heard at all in its parliament, carrying the interior's claim in a borrowed tongue while his own mother tongue stayed in the village - so that the gap at the limit of the tongue was the distance between the river and the chamber, the first language of the land and the imposed language of the state that governed it.

## Action

In [the Land of Many Waters](plot_01_the_land_of_many_waters.md) he carries the indigenous peoples and their interior into the nation's reckoning, the first of the first peoples to sit where the country's laws are made. He gives the Land of Many Waters a voice in the councils of the coast, and puts the oldest claim on the country, long unheard, on the record of the nation at last.

## Shadow

The single voice for the first peoples can be made to stand in for a hearing the nation never truly gives - the one Amerindian in the chamber pointed to as proof the interior is heard, while the river villages go on unseen and the land goes on contested; the breakthrough honoured and the deeper silence left unbroken behind it.

## Tell

He brings the river and the bush into a chamber built by and for the coast, and speaks for the first peoples in the coast's own tongue - the oldest claim on the land carried, of necessity, in the language that came last.
