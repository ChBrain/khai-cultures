# Place: Georgetown

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Shown

The wooden colonial capital on the Demerara where it meets the Atlantic, much of it below sea level and kept dry behind a long seawall and a grid of canals the Dutch first dug and the British widened. The white-painted timber buildings - St. George's Cathedral among the tallest wooden churches in the world, the great Stabroek Market under its iron clock, the Parliament and the law courts - and the kokers and sluices that hold the sea back from the streets. The Garden City of the Caribbean coast, where the plural nation's commerce, government, and history all gather at the water's edge.

## Holds

- [the Bound and the Freed](plot_02_the_bound_and_the_freed.md): the port and market town of the sugar coast, where the estates shipped their cane and the bound and the freed came and went.
- [the Road to Independence](plot_03_the_road_to_independence.md): the seat of the colony's politics, where the labour movement marched and the republic was declared.

## Offers

A place where the plural nation governs and sells and gathers itself - the market where every community's food and goods meet, the cathedral and the mandir and the masjid within reach of one another, the Parliament where the country's quarrels and its motto are both made law, and the seawall promenade where the city walks at evening above the held-back sea.

## Withheld

The safety of solid ground. Georgetown carries the gap between the warmth above and the history beneath - a city built by drained plantation labour, kept alive only by the seawall and the pumps against a sea that would take it back, and long the stage of the racial-political divide whose marches and counts ran through these same streets. The Garden City holds the nation's beauty and its precariousness, the welcome and the old wound, at once.
