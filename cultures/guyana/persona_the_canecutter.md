# Persona: the canecutter

## Taxonomy

[the plantation legacy](position_the_plantation_legacy.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The sugar-estate labourer who is the soul of the plantation, African in the time of slavery and Indian in the time of indenture, bent in the canefield from before light, cutting the cane that made the country and the people both. Enduring, faithful, and bound to the estate by chain and then by contract, the canecutter is the figure beneath the whole society, the one who paid for the coast with labour and was made, in the paying, into a Guyanese. The tongues carried from across the oceans were the first tongues: the African brought the speech of the homelands he was taken from, [speaking](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [hearing](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) the mother tongue of a continent torn away, and the Indian who came after [carried Bhojpuri bound across the sea](process_speaking_carried.md), [hearing it](process_hearing_followed.md) in the logie at evening and [holding onto its words](process_reading_deciphered.md) in a borrowed script. On the estate all those tongues were pressed under the [English](position_language_en_gy.md) of the overseer, and the canecutter [took up that English as a borrowed working tongue](process_speaking_borrowed.md) to be commanded in and to answer, until the African languages and the Bhojpuri thinned to a word of prayer, a song, a name for a dish - so that the gap at the limit of the tongue is the whole crossing itself, the mother tongues of three continents lost in the canefield and an imposed English left in their place, the Creolese of Guyana grown up over the silence where the first languages had been. The canecutter belongs to [Guyanese culture](position_culture_guyanese_culture.md), the plantation legacy's own soul and the labour that made its plural people one.

## Action

In [the Bound and the Freed](plot_02_the_bound_and_the_freed.md) the canecutter cuts the cane that builds the colony and, in the cutting, becomes the plural people of Guyana, African then Indian made into one country's labour. The bound and the freed who worked the estate carry [the plantation legacy](position_the_plantation_legacy.md) as living memory, the soul beneath the village and the source of the people the canefield made.

## Shadow

The figure who built the country can be romanced into a picture and forgotten as a person - the canecutter on the postcard, the village folklore, the heritage festival, while the bondage and the contract that bent the back are smoothed into nostalgia; the soul of the plantation honoured as a symbol and the cost it bore left unweighed.

## Tell

The canecutter rises before light and bends in the field for the cane that feeds the estate and never the cutter, and carries, in a word of prayer and the name of a dish, all that is left of the tongues that crossed the ocean bound.
