# Guyana: a khai culture

Guyana staged as a full khai play, ISO GY, language en. The only English-speaking
nation of South America, Caribbean in culture and a plural people made of many
origins, as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the
roles its language and faith encode, its places, its practices, and its defining
objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_guyana.md](play_guyana.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Guyana's
  Caribbean profile (warm and plural hospitality, a layered religiosity, communal
  closeness and family, resilience and pride, and the deep undertow of the
  racial-political divide) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Land of Many Waters, the Bound and the Freed, the Road
  to Independence, and the New Horizon - each casting the elements it needs, and
  each staging the gap between the promise and the reality honestly.
- **Personas:** Cheddi Jagan, Walter Rodney, Martin Carter, and Stephen Campbell
  as the historic cast, plus one defining archetype, the canecutter (African then
  Indian, the soul of the plantation and the maker of the plural people). Each is
  wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** the plural society (the Indo- and Afro-Guyanese nation and the
  divide to overcome, "one people, one nation"), the plantation legacy (sugar,
  slavery, and indenture, the canefield that made the people), the Land of Many
  Waters (the rainforest interior, the rivers, the indigenous heartland and the
  Essequibo).
- **Places:** Georgetown (the wooden colonial capital below sea level behind its
  seawall, on the Demerara), the Interior (the rainforest, Kaieteur Falls, the
  rivers and the Essequibo).
- **Processes:** Mashramani (the republic-day carnival of costume and soca),
  Phagwah (the Indo-Guyanese spring festival of colour, shared across the
  communities).
- **Pieces:** pepperpot (the national dish of Amerindian origin, made with
  cassareep), Demerara rum (the dark rum of the sugar estates, world-renowned).

## Provenance

The Hofstede framing behind the pitch and the public-record historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; slavery, Indian indenture, and the Indo-/Afro-Guyanese racial-political
divide are treated soberly on the public record and from within Guyana's own
self-understanding; the staging and the architecture are original work.
