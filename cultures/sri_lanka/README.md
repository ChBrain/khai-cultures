# Sri Lanka: a khai culture

Sri Lanka staged as a full khai play, ISO LK, language si. The resplendent island,
the pearl of the Indian Ocean, as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its
people as the cast, the roles its faith and language encode, its places, its
practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_sri_lanka.md](play_sri_lanka.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_si.md](pitch_si.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Sri
  Lanka's profile (respect for elders and authority, a strong group and family
  orientation, a long view shaped by karma and rebirth, and a patient, indulgent
  ease) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Anuradhapura hydraulic civilization, Polonnaruwa and
  Parakramabahu the Great, the colonial centuries and the Kandyan kingdom, and
  independence and the modern nation. The period of sorrow the nation passed
  through is acknowledged only soberly and briefly, with dignity, taking no side.
- **Personas:** Parakramabahu the Great (the tank-builder who unified the island),
  Anagarika Dharmapala (the Buddhist revivalist), Martin Wickramasinghe (the
  novelist of the island's soul), and Ananda Samarakoon (the composer of the
  national anthem), plus one archetype, the paddy farmer who has fed the island
  for a thousand years. Each is wired to the language engine by the
  language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** Theravada Buddhism (the faith brought in the third century BCE
  and guarded ever since, alongside the island's Hindu, Muslim, and Christian
  communities), the tank (the human-made reservoirs and the hydraulic
  civilization), the island home (the one home shared by all the island's
  peoples).
- **Places:** Anuradhapura (the ancient kingdom of tank and dagoba) and Kandy (the
  last kingdom and the home of the sacred Temple of the Tooth).
- **Processes:** the Esala Perahera (the procession of the tooth relic) and the
  rice harvest (the rhythm of tank and paddy).
- **Pieces:** Ceylon tea (the highland tea that carried the island's name to the
  world) and the lion flag (the national flag whose colours hold all the island's
  communities).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed. The two great peoples of the island, the Sinhala and the Tamil, and the
Muslim and all other communities, are treated with equal dignity; the period of
conflict the nation passed through is acknowledged soberly and with restraint,
taking no political side and dwelling on no detail. The staging and the
architecture are original work.
