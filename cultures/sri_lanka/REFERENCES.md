# Sri Lanka: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_si.md](pitch_si.md) is written from Sri Lanka's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record without
entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Sri Lanka score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 80                        |
| Individualism                 | 35                        |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 10                        |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 45                        |
| Long Term Orientation         | 45                        |
| Indulgence                    | 46                        |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; some dimensions for Sri Lanka are
estimates within the published model. The pitch reflects a high respect for
authority and elders, a strong group and family orientation, and a long view
shaped by Buddhist ideas of karma and rebirth.

## Historical sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn
from the public historical record. They are credited here and never claimed; the
staging is original work. The period of conflict the nation passed through is
staged soberly and with restraint, taking no political side and dwelling on no
detail, treating the Sinhala, Tamil, Muslim, and all communities with equal
dignity.

The defining persona [the paddy farmer](persona_goviya.md) is an archetype, not a
historical individual: a composite of the villager who, beside the tank and the
paddy field, has fed the island for a thousand years. The composer [Ananda
Samarakoon](persona_samarakoon.md) wrote the national anthem, later sung in both
Sinhala and Tamil, a sign of the one home shared by all the island's communities.
