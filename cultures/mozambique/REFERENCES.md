# Mozambique: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain cultural and historical
sources behind the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_pt.md](pitch_pt.md) is written from Mozambique's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
Published Hofstede data for Mozambique is limited; the scores below are
approximate and, where Mozambique is not separately published, are read against
the regional Southern and Lusophone African pattern. They are recorded here so the
data is on the record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Mozambique score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | -------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 85                         |
| Individualism                 | 15                         |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 38                         |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 44                         |
| Long Term Orientation         | 11                         |
| Indulgence                    | 80                         |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; where Mozambique is not separately
published they are estimated from the regional African pattern. The profile read
into the pitch is a high-power-distance, strongly collective, present-minded, and
indulgent one: respect for elders, the extended family, the long table, and a
patient, sharing warmth.

The one defining archetype, [the capulana mother](persona_a_mae_da_capulana.md),
is not a historical individual but a composite: the market woman of the coast and
the city who carries the child on her back and the basket on her head, sustains
the household, and keeps the home language and the shared table alive.
