# Mozambique: a khai culture

Mozambique staged as a full khai play, ISO MZ, language pt. A long Indian Ocean
coast and a nation of many peoples and many poets, as a theatre: its history as
the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its languages and faith encode,
its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.
The prose is written in Mozambican Portuguese, not the Lisbon variety, warmed by
the Bantu languages of the land (Changana and Ronga in the south, Makhuwa in the
north, Sena in the centre) and the idiom of Maputo.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_mozambique.md](play_mozambique.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_pt.md](pitch_pt.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Mozambique's profile (a warm, collective, present-minded patience: respect for
  elders, the long table, the open door, and a stubborn joy) and naming no
  dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Indian Ocean coast and the Island of Mozambique, the
  wood that sings (Makonde sculpture and Chopi timbila), the nation of the word
  (the country's poets), and Maputo and marrabenta - each casting the elements it
  needs. The war of independence and the civil war are out of scope.
- **Personas:** José Craveirinha, Mia Couto, Paulina Chiziane, and the marrabenta
  great Fany Pfumo as the real cast, plus one archetype, the capulana mother (the
  market woman who carries the household and the nation on her back). Each is
  wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** the Indian Ocean coast (the seafaring, mixing, open-door land),
  the nation of poets (the country that said itself first in the word), the many
  peoples (Makhuwa, Tsonga, Sena, Makonde, Ndau, Chopi and more, in equal
  dignity).
- **Places:** Maputo (the acacia capital and modern heart) and the Island of
  Mozambique (the old stone-and-reed trading town, a World Heritage site).
- **Processes:** matapa (the coconut-and-cashew coastal cooking and the shared
  table) and mapiko (the Makonde masked dance of the northern plateau).
- **Pieces:** the capulana (the printed cloth that is dress, sling, and gift) and
  the Makonde sculpture (the blackwood ujamaa and shetani carvings).
