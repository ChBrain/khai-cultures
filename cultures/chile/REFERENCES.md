# Chile: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_es.md](pitch_es.md) is written from Chile's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record without
entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Chile score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | --------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 63                    |
| Individualism                 | 23                    |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 28                    |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 86                    |
| Long Term Orientation         | 31                    |
| Indulgence                    | 68                    |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; Chile's high Uncertainty Avoidance
and low Motivation toward Achievement underlie the sober, ordered, modest register
of the pitch.

## Historical sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn
from the public historical record. They are credited here and never claimed; the
staging is original work. The hardest chapters of the story - the conquest and the
long frontier war with the Mapuche, and the 1973 coup, the military government,
and the human-rights reckoning - are staged soberly on the public record, naming
the facts and the nation's memory without graphic or atrocity detail and taking no
political side.

The one defining archetype, [el minero](persona_el_minero.md), is not a historical
individual but a composite: the miner of the north who is the backbone of the
copper-and-nitrate nation and the figure of its resilience in the dark, recalling
the 2010 rescue of the thirty-three miners as the country's own image of endurance.
