# Fiji: a khai culture

Fiji staged as a full khai play, ISO FJ, language fij. A nation of two peoples,
the indigenous iTaukei and the Indo-Fijian descendants of the girmitiya, as a
theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its
languages and faith encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects,
all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_fiji.md](play_fiji.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_fij.md](pitch_fij.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Fiji's profile (warm and respectful, communal and chiefly, faith-filled and
  easygoing, the sevusevu and the talanoa) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the chiefdoms and the cession of 1874, the ships from
  India and indenture, independence in 1970 and the coups carried soberly, and
  modern Fiji of the sea, the kava, and the scrum - each casting the elements it
  needs, and each staging the relationship of the two peoples with dignity.
- **Personas:** Ratu Seru Cakobau, Ratu Sir Lala Sukuna, Ratu Sir Kamisese Mara,
  and A.D. Patel as the historic cast, plus the girmitiya as the defining
  archetype of the indentured cane labourer from India. Each is wired to the
  language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** the vanua and the chiefs (the iTaukei bond of land and people
  under the chiefs), the two peoples (the iTaukei and Indo-Fijian sharing one
  nation), the way of the sea and the kava (island life and ceremony).
- **Places:** Viti Levu (the main island, Suva the capital, the cane belt), Bau
  (the tiny island seat of the paramount chiefs).
- **Processes:** the kava ceremony (the sevusevu and the tanoa circle), rugby
  (the national passion, the sevens gold).
- **Pieces:** the tanoa (the kava bowl, the body of the ceremony), masi (the tapa
  bark cloth of ceremony and lineage).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-record historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources on the public record are credited, never
claimed; indenture and the coups are treated soberly, from within Fiji's own
self-understanding, taking no side; the staging and the architecture are original
work.
