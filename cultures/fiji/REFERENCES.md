# Fiji: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-record historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_fij.md](pitch_fij.md) is written from Fiji's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record without
entering the prose.

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; Fiji's profile reads as strongly
communal, hierarchical, and easygoing.

The defining persona [na girmitiya](persona_na_girmitiya.md) is an archetype, not
a historical individual: a composite of the indentured cane labourer brought from
India under the girmit, whose descendants became the Indo-Fijian community, and
who carried the ancestral Hindi and Hindustani that thinned into Fiji Hindi while
Fijian and English became the shared tongues.
