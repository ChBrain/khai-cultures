# Persona: Ray

## Taxonomy

[motor speed](position_motor_speed.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A thirty-eight-year-old race crew chief in Indianapolis. English, spoken with a practical midwestern cadence, is his mother tongue, the language in which he [speaks, hears, and thinks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), the defining code of his daily mechanical operations. It is the [Hoosier English](position_language_en_us_in.md) of the Indianapolis race garages, the clipped, practical cadence of the working floor. He speaks no foreign languages. Still, there is a seam he feels every day: the slower, nature-bound farm talk of farmers like Helen and the quiet history of [hoosier hospitality](position_hoosier_hospitality.md) [he reads only as regional logistics updates](process_reading_followed.md) on his track workstation, while the patient, cooperative rhythm of early farming communities gets crowded out by the push for ever-higher speed. He holds [Hoosier culture](position_culture_hoosier_culture.md) from its newer end, the motor speed that turned a frontier territory into a state measured by lap times.

## Action

He preserves [checkered flag](piece_checkered_flag.md) relics, coordinates engine safety programs under [the soil plan](plan_agricultural_soil_conservation.md), and studies [motor speed](position_motor_speed.md).

## Shadow

His focus on speedway performance metrics and venture-capital racing investments can make him indifferent to municipal environmental problems in rural agricultural counties.

## Tell

He adjusts a telemetry sensor on a racecar and says directly: "We are calibrating high-performance combustion cycles; you can't build a competitive engine if you're only focused on historic local preservation."
