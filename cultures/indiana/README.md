# Indiana: a khai culture

Indiana staged as a full khai play, ISO US-IN, language en. The Hoosier State: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its Tippecanoe frontier control, Indianapolis 500 motor racing, and limestone extraction industry encodes, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house that holds this culture and answers for it.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_indiana.md](play_indiana.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_indiana.md](pitch_indiana.md), the Indiana key.
- **Plots (history):** the Battle of Tippecanoe of 1811, the Indianapolis 500 Inauguration of 1911, and the Limestone Quarrying Boom of the 1880s.
- **Personas:** Ray (automotive race crew chief) and Helen (corn and soy farmer), written in English.
- **Positions:** hoosier hospitality (cooperative community/farming pride), motor speed (racing speedway/automotive engineering).
- **Places:** Indianapolis Speedway, Bedford Quarry.
- **Processes:** engine tuning (motor calibration), limestone cutting (quarry masonry).
- **Pieces:** a checkered flag, a limestone block.
- **Plan:** the soil plan.
