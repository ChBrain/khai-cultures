# Position: Hoosier culture

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

Membership in a flat, agricultural state that also built the fastest race in the country, where the limestone quarried at Bedford went into national monuments and the speedway at Indianapolis became a yearly rite. It carries [hoosier hospitality](position_hoosier_hospitality.md), the warm, unhurried welcome of the farm counties and the co-op; it carries [motor speed](position_motor_speed.md), the mechanical audacity of the racing garages that turned a frontier territory into a place measured by lap times; and beneath both it carries the frontier claim first won at Tippecanoe, a territory secured before it was a state. Its tongue is [Hoosier English](position_language_en_us_in.md), the warm midwestern drawl of main street and the clipped, practical cadence of the garage floor alike. This is what a Hoosier carries without ever having chosen it: a state that must be as patient with its soil as it is fast with its engines.

## Orders

That a stranger be welcomed at the door and fed before being asked their business, the hospitality that gives the state its name; that the row crop be tended through its full season with the same discipline the pit crew gives an engine; that the limestone block be cut true, since it will outlast the quarry and the quarrier both; and that the speedway's history, from the first five-hundred-mile race to the present, be kept as a shared civic achievement rather than a private hobby. It orders that the farm counties and the racing counties treat each other as one Hoosier family even when their daily work looks nothing alike.

## Loses

Ground where the quick, technology-driven talk of racing engineers and venture-capital speedway investment drowns out the slower, season-dependent rhythm of the family farm, so that soil conservation gets treated as secondary to speedway entertainment and corporate tourism. It loses ground, too, when state-wide environmental mandates on erosion and fertilizer runoff meet the suspicion of counties that prize their independence, and in the memory of Tippecanoe, a frontier victory that opened the territory to settlement at the cost of the land's first people.

## Drives

It drives the Hoosier to answer the door before answering the question, to treat hospitality as a discipline rather than a decoration, and to bring the same exacting care to a cut limestone block, a tuned engine, or a planted row. It drives the state to hold cooperative community farming and high-speed mechanical innovation as one inheritance, not two, and it drives whoever is raised here to measure a season by both the harvest brought in and the race brought home.
