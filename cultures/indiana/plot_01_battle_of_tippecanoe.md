## Cue

In November 1811, Governor William Henry Harrison leads U.S. forces against Tenskwatawa's native confederacy near the Tippecanoe River.

## Action

The battle secures territorial control for expansion, developing early settlement infrastructure. Helen documents local family histories in Lafayette archives, while Ray studies the frontier military engineering history.

## Stage

## Tension

The conflict destroys the native settlement of Prophetstown, permanently displacing Shawnee and Miami families from their ancestral lands.
