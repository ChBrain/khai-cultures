# Alabama: a khai culture

Alabama staged as a full khai play, ISO US-AL, language en. The Yellowhammer State: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its Montgomery bus boycott civil rights activism, Huntsville space center engineering, and Birmingham steel boom encodes, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house that holds this culture and answers for it.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_alabama.md](play_alabama.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_alabama.md](pitch_alabama.md), the Alabama key.
- **Plots (history):** the Montgomery Bus Boycott of 1955, the Huntsville Space Flight Center Founding of 1960, and the Birmingham Steel Industry Boom of the 1880s.
- **Personas:** Albert (civil rights archivist) and Sarah (aerospace engineer), written in English.
- **Positions:** civil rights (justice struggle/community organizing), space pioneer (scientific ambition/aerospace design).
- **Places:** Dexter Avenue Church, Huntsville Test Stand.
- **Processes:** archival preservation (oral recording), rocket propulsion (engine calibration).
- **Pieces:** a boycott flyer, a rocket nozzle.
- **Plan:** the estuary plan.
