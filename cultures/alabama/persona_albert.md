# Persona: Albert

## Taxonomy

[civil rights](position_civil_rights.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A fifty-two-year-old museum curator in Montgomery. [Alabama English](position_language_en_us_al.md), carried in an expressive Southern cadence, is his mother tongue, the language he [speaks, hears, and thinks in](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), the code his community presentations are built on. He speaks no foreign languages. Here is where it thins out for him: the clipped, tech-forward talk of aerospace engineers like Sarah and the digital planning coming out of Huntsville's space boards [reach him only as memos to file](process_reading_followed.md), and he worries the patient, door-to-door rhythm that built the early civil rights movement is getting written out of the story by every new push toward modernization. He belongs to the [Heart of Dixie](position_culture_heart_of_dixie.md), the state's double inheritance, and he holds fast to the half carried in Montgomery's churches and on its picket lines.

## Action

He preserves [boycott flyer](piece_boycott_flyer.md) prints, coordinates community feedback under [the estuary plan](plan_mobile_bay_estuary_protection.md), and studies [civil rights](position_civil_rights.md).

## Shadow

His focus on preserving civil rights records and community history can make him suspicious of state-wide zoning and commercial real-estate development, fearing it will overwrite historical neighborhoods.

## Tell

He looks at a historic protest flyer and says directly: "A simple pamphlet organized an entire city; you can't build a sustainable community if you're only focused on aerospace profits and digital streaming metrics."
