# Persona: Albert

## Taxonomy

[civil rights](position_civil_rights.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A fifty-two-year-old museum curator in Montgomery. [Alabama English](position_language_en_us_al.md), spoken with an expressive Southern cadence, is his mother tongue, the language in which he [speaks, hears, and thinks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), the defining code of his community presentations. He speaks no foreign languages. Yet the loss lies in the seam: The fast, tech-oriented talk of aerospace engineers like Sarah and the digital planning of Huntsville space boards [he reads only as administrative updates](process_reading_followed.md) on his workstation, feeling that the patient, cooperative rhythm of early civil rights organizing is being forgotten in the race for technological modernization.

## Action

He preserves [boycott flyer](piece_boycott_flyer.md) prints, coordinates community feedback under [the estuary plan](plan_mobile_bay_estuary_protection.md), and studies [civil rights](position_civil_rights.md).

## Shadow

His focus on preserving civil rights records and community history can make him suspicious of state-wide zoning and commercial real-estate development, fearing it will overwrite historical neighborhoods.

## Tell

He looks at a historic protest flyer and says directly: "A simple pamphlet organized an entire city; you can't build a sustainable community if you're only focused on aerospace profits and digital streaming metrics."
