# the Dominican Republic: a khai culture

The Dominican Republic staged as a full khai play, ISO DO, language es. The cradle
of the New World, a people of rhythm and resilience, as a theatre: its history as
the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its language encodes, its places,
its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch. The prose is
written in Spanish; only the structural headings are in English.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house that
holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its Estate; a
culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_dominican_republic.md](play_dominican_republic.md), the culture
  itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_es.md](pitch_es.md), the Hofstede layer, written from the
  Dominican Republic's profile (warm, loud, joyful, fast Caribbean Spanish, deeply
  familial, musical, and resilient, with the joy carried over old wounds and
  inequality) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** la Primada de America (the Taino and Santo Domingo, the
  first city of 1496), la Independencia (1844) (independence from Haiti, Duarte and
  the Trinitaria, the young republic and its letters), la Era y las Mariposas (the
  Trujillo dictatorship soberly, the courage of the Mirabal sisters), and la Patria
  del Merengue y la Pelota (modern DR, merengue and bachata to the world, baseball,
  and the diaspora) - each casting the elements it needs.
- **Personas:** Juan Pablo Duarte, Salome Urena, the Mirabal sisters, and Juan Luis
  Guerra as the historic cast, plus one defining archetype, el pelotero (the
  ballplayer from the sugar towns who dreams of the big leagues). Each is wired to
  the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** la cuna de America (the first colony and city and the lettered
  tradition of Santo Domingo), la patria y la libertad (independence and
  resistance, from Duarte to the Mirabal), el ritmo y el juego (merengue, bachata,
  and baseball, the joyful national arts).
- **Places:** Santo Domingo (the first city of the Americas, the Zona Colonial and
  the malecon), San Pedro de Macoris (the sugar town become the cradle of baseball
  talent).
- **Processes:** el merengue (the national dance of guira, tambora, and accordion),
  el beisbol (the national game and the winter-league passion).
- **Pieces:** el mangu (the mashed-plantain breakfast, los tres golpes), la tambora
  (the two-headed drum at the heart of merengue).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-record sources are recorded in
[REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the pitch is its
invisible hand. Sources on the public record are credited, never claimed; the
Trujillo dictatorship and the resistance of the Mirabal sisters are treated soberly
and reverently, with no atrocity detail, and the relationship with Haiti is handled
with dignity and no blame; the staging and the architecture are original work.
