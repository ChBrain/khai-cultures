# the Dominican Republic: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-record sources behind the plots,
personas, places, processes, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_es.md](pitch_es.md) is written from the Dominican Republic's
profile on Geert Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and
carries no number. The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on
the record without entering the prose.

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the

Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; the Dominican Republic reads as
collectivist, warm, and short-term oriented, with a strong indulgent streak.

The defining archetype, [el pelotero](persona_el_pelotero.md), is not a historical
individual but a composite that carries the tension of the sugar-town ballplayer
who dreams of the big leagues - the talent that may be cradled or exploited, and the
mother tongue that leaves him mute in the country where he finally succeeds. The
cultural framing - the colmado, the malecon, the mulato identity, the diaspora, and
the joy carried over old wounds - is original staging, not a claim on any source.
