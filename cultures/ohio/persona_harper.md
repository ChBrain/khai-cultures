# Persona: Harper

## Taxonomy

[industrial heartland](position_industrial_heartland.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

Harper's forty-five and works out of Cleveland, where a flat Great Lakes accent is just how the words come out of her - the one language she's ever [spoken, heard, and thought in](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), and the only one her job runs on. It's [Ohio English](position_language_en_us_oh.md) the way the Great Lakes cities clip it, Inland North flat, the accent of the research floor. No second language, never needed one. Where she comes up short is the other Ohio: the slower talk of farmers like Samuel, the whole patient history behind [middle ground](position_middle_ground.md) consensus, reaches her [mostly as a column of numbers on a spreadsheet](process_reading_followed.md), and she'll admit, if pressed, that the old manufacturing blocks are getting traded for zoning maps faster than she likes. She's part of [Ohio culture](position_culture_ohio_culture.md) same as Samuel, just steering its industrial heartland toward whatever future reads clearest on a terminal - clearer than it ever reads out in the valley.

## Action

She logs [rubber tire](piece_rubber_tire.md) stock, audits under [the brownfield redevelopment plan](plan_brownfield_redevelopment.md), and keeps close tabs on [industrial heartland](position_industrial_heartland.md).

## Shadow

Her focus on redevelopment deals and clean-tech fixes can leave her blind to where the families who used to work those plants actually end up living now.

## Tell

She looks over a scrubbed-clean factory lot and says it straight: "We're not just pulling bad soil out of the ground. We're building the next thing this valley makes for a living."
