# Equatorial Guinea: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical and cultural
sources behind the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_es.md](pitch_es.md) is written from a regional reading of
Equatorial Guinea's profile on Geert Hofstede's model of national culture and
names no dimension and carries no number. Equatorial Guinea is not individually
published in the core dataset, so the pitch is tuned from the West African
regional profile Hofstede published (grouped with Ghana, Nigeria, Sierra Leone),
which the country's group-and-elders ethos broadly fits. The figures are recorded
here so the data is on the record without entering the prose.

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture), West Africa regional grouping. Scores
are approximate and regional, as published by Hofstede Insights / The Culture
Factor Group; they stand in for a country not separately scored in the core data.

The one defining archetype, [el cacaotero](persona_cacaotero.md), is not a
historical individual but a composite: the finca worker who tends the cacao under
the forest, speaks his village mother tongue, and closes his deal at the port in
the inherited Spanish.
