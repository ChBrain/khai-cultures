# Equatorial Guinea: a khai culture

Equatorial Guinea staged as a full khai play, ISO GQ, language es. Africa's only
Spanish-speaking nation, an island and a mainland bound into one across the
Atlantic, staged as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its peoples as the
cast, the roles its inherited tongue and many mother tongues encode, its places,
its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_equatorial_guinea.md](play_equatorial_guinea.md), the culture
  itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_es.md](pitch_es.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Equatorial Guinea's regional profile (a community held by group and elders, a
  patient present-focus, and a warm, unhurried register) and naming no dimension.
  The prose is in the country's own Equatoguinean Spanish, the African-cadenced
  variety distinct from any peninsular or Latin American one.
- **Plots (history):** the first peoples, the coming of the Europeans, cacao and
  the forest, and the nation - each casting the elements it needs, and each
  staging the country's making soberly and without taking a side.
- **Personas:** Donato Ndongo-Bidyogo and Maria Nsue Angue (the writers who built
  the country's first literature in Spanish), Desmali (the musician who carried
  the malamba and the balele to record), and King Moka (the Bubi sovereign of
  Bioko) as the historic cast, plus one archetype, the cacao grower (el
  cacaotero). Each is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it
  holds, between the inherited Spanish and the mother tongues of the land.
- **Positions:** the inherited tongue (Spanish as Africa's only such heritage,
  the common language above all the mother tongues), the peoples (the Bubi, Fang,
  Ndowe, Annobonese, Bisio, none of them the whole country), the Atlantic (the
  sea and the equatorial forest that ring and feed the land).
- **Places:** Malabo (the island capital on Bioko, the old town and the
  cathedral beneath the volcanic peak), Rio Muni (the mainland of forest and
  cacao where most of the nation lives).
- **Processes:** the balele (the village dance that gathers the peoples without
  words), the cacao harvest (the forest opened to the world bean by bean).
- **Pieces:** the malamba (the rhythm and sound in which the island and the
  mainland recognize one another), Malabo Cathedral (the Spanish heritage made
  stone, the face of the capital).

## Provenance

The Hofstede regional source data behind the pitch and the public-domain
historical and cultural sources are recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md).
The data never enters the prose; the pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the
public domain are credited, never claimed; the colonial legacy and the country's
making are treated soberly on the public record, taking no political side; the
staging and the architecture are original work.
