# Position: Virginian English

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The Tidewater and Piedmont English of the Old Dominion, the soft, formal southern cadence of the Williamsburg interpreter and the articulate mid-Atlantic register of the Northern Virginia policy corridor. It holds a courteous, historied southern plainness that both the colonial hall and the Arlington office carry.

## Orders

Where it passes as native ground: the Colonial Williamsburg hall, the Shenandoah Valley farm, the Arlington defense office, the Richmond history forum. It orders which turns of phrase and vowels mark a speaker as Virginian and sets the traditional southern ear against the modern federal one.

## Loses

Ground to General American, the flat national broadcast standard the screen and the federal contract carry, which trims the soft drawl off the young and stamps the colonial-district speech as antiquated. The digital defense register also crowds the older Tidewater speech toward the margin.

## Drives

It carries [Thomas](persona_thomas.md), whose soft, formal southern cadence is the working English of the Williamsburg interpretive hall, and [Sasha](persona_sasha.md), whose articulate mid-Atlantic cadence holds the same native tongue in the Arlington defense corridor. So the one Virginian speech shows who keeps the colonial ground and who turns toward the federal trade.
