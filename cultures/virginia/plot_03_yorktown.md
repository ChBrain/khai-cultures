# Plot: The Siege of Yorktown 1781

## Taxonomy

[play_virginia](play_virginia.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

In October 1781, American and French forces trap the British army at Yorktown, forcing Cornwallis to surrender and securing independence.

## Action

The victory establishes Virginia as a crucial theater of military planning and logistics, laying the foundation for a permanent naval presence. The [shipbuilding](process_shipbuilding.md) industry grows to support national defense. Thomas explains battlefield strategies to visitors, while Sasha analyzes modern naval logistics.

## Stage

- [the Shenandoah Valley](place_shenandoah_valley.md) : the agricultural region that supplied the continental armies.
- [shipbuilding](process_shipbuilding.md) : the naval construction that grew along the Hampton Roads harbors.
- [Cradle of Presidents](position_cradle_of_presidents.md) : the military and civic leadership forged during the revolutionary campaign.

## Tension

The victory secures political independence for the new republic but does not alter the social status of enslaved laborers who built the defense fortifications.
