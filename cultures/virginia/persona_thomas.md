# Persona: Thomas

## Taxonomy

[Cavalier heritage](position_cavalier_heritage.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A sixty-two-year-old historic interpreter in Williamsburg. English, spoken with a soft, formal southern cadence, is his mother tongue, the language in which he [speaks, hears, and thinks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), the defining code of his daily presentation. It is the [Virginian English](position_language_en_us_va.md) of Colonial Williamsburg, the soft, formal southern cadence of the interpretive hall. He speaks no foreign languages. What passes him by comes garbled: the quick, technology-oriented defense talk of contractors like Sasha and the digital planning of Northern Virginia, which [he reads only as administrative metrics](process_reading_followed.md) on his tablet, and he suspects the patient, constitutional values of the early republic are being crowded out by defense-lobbying transactions. In [the Old Dominion](position_culture_old_dominion.md) he is read as the colonial half of the pairing, reading the founding record the way Sasha reads the policy brief.

## Action

He preserves [tobacco pipe](piece_tobacco_pipe.md) artifacts, coordinates community history forums under [the Chesapeake Bay plan](plan_chesapeake_bay_cleanup.md), and studies [Cradle of Presidents](position_cradle_of_presidents.md).

## Shadow

His focus on historical accuracy and colonial preservation can make him elitist, overlooking the immediate material struggles of modern working-class families outside the historic area.

## Tell

He looks at a colonial brick assembly building and says politely: "We aren't just wearing costumes; we are preserving the constitutional ideas that laid the foundations of the entire republic."
