# Japan: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_ja.md](pitch_ja.md) is written from Japan's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no
number. The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the
record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Japan score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | --------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 54                    |
| Individualism                 | 46                    |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 95                    |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 92                    |
| Long Term Orientation         | 88                    |
| Indulgence                    | 42                    |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published
by Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; Japan's Uncertainty Avoidance
and Long Term Orientation scores are among the higher in the published data.

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn
from the public historical record. They are credited here and never claimed; the
staging is original work. The wars and deep losses of the modern period are
staged soberly on the public record, without graphic detail and without taking a
side.

The one defining persona, [the artisan](persona_shokunin.md), is an archetype,
not a historical individual: a composite that carries the culture's living spirit
of craft - the shokunin who masters one path for a lifetime, the carrier of
kaizen, omotenashi, and the wa of the workplace, joining the old craft to modern
technology, manga, and design.
