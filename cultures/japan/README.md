# Japan: a khai culture

Japan staged as a full khai play, ISO JP, language ja. An island country of four
seasons and long continuity, forever joining the old way and the new, as a
theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its
language and faith encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects,
all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_japan.md](play_japan.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_ja.md](pitch_ja.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Japan's
  profile (restraint and modesty, a strong avoidance of uncertainty, a long-term
  orientation, a place for the group above the self, and the indirection of
  reading the air) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the ancient court, the age of the warriors and the Edo
  peace, the Meiji Restoration and the opening to the world, and the postwar
  recovery and reinvention - each casting the elements it needs, and each staged
  soberly and without taking a side.
- **Personas:** Murasaki Shikibu, Matsuo Basho, Fukuzawa Yukichi, and Akira
  Kurosawa as the historic cast, plus one defining archetype, the artisan (the
  shokunin who masters one craft for life, the carrier of kaizen and
  omotenashi). Each is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it
  holds.
- **Positions:** wa (harmony, the group before the self, reading the air),
  bushido (the way of the samurai, honour and the mastery of a path), and the
  intertwining of Shinto and Buddhism (the eight million gods, impermanence, and
  mono no aware).
- **Places:** Kyoto (the thousand-year capital, the four seasons and the temples,
  the old preserved) and Tokyo (the warrior town turned modern capital, the old
  and the new side by side).
- **Processes:** hanami (cherry-blossom viewing, the love of the passing) and
  omotenashi (wholehearted, anticipatory hospitality).
- **Pieces:** the katana (the soul of the samurai, use and beauty in one blade)
  and the kimono (the four seasons worn, the grace of the gesture).
- **Plan:** [plan_kaizen.md](plan_kaizen.md), kaizen, the endless work of small
  improvement, of joining the old heart to the new craft.

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the wars and the deep losses of the modern period are treated soberly on
the public record, without graphic detail and without taking a side; the staging
and the architecture are original work.
