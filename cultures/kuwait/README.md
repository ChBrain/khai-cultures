# Kuwait: a khai culture

Kuwait staged as a full khai play, ISO KW, language ar. A seafaring people of the
dhow, the pearl, the desert, and the diwaniya, staged as a theatre: its history
as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its sea and language encode,
its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_kuwait.md](play_kuwait.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_ar.md](pitch_ar.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Kuwait's profile (a warm, group-first society of strong family and diwaniya
  ties, deference to elders and consensus, hospitality, and the patience the sea
  taught) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the founding of the town on the shore, the pearling era
  and the great dive (al-ghaus al-kabir), the great trade voyages to India and
  East Africa, and the flowering of letters, the majlis, and memory - each
  casting the elements it needs.
- **Personas:** Mubarak Al-Sabah (the founding figure of the modern town), the
  poet Fahd Al-Askar, the dhow-captain and pearl merchant Hilal Al-Mutairi, and
  the folk-theatre pioneer Mohammed Al-Nashmi as the historic cast, plus one
  defining archetype, the pearl diver who built the old town with his breath.
  Each is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** the sea (the saltwater farm, road, and fate of a shore with no
  field), the diwaniya (the gathering of speech, counsel, and coffee), and
  hospitality (the karam inherited from desert and sea alike).
- **Places:** Kuwait Town and the old harbour (the mud-brick town and the
  shipbuilding wharf and pearl market), and Failaka island (the ancient site
  beneath the sand, the deep time under the country's feet).
- **Processes:** dhow building (the qallaf's inherited craft, plank by plank),
  and the diwaniya gathering (the open majlis where the coffee is poured and the
  word is kept).
- **Pieces:** the boom (the great dhow that carried the divers and the trade
  across the ocean), and the sadu (the Bedouin weaving, the desert face of a
  country known for its sea).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the harshness of the pearling life and the divers who built the wealth
they barely shared are treated soberly on the public record; the staging and the
architecture are original work.
