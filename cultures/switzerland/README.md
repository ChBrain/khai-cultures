# Switzerland: a khai culture

Switzerland staged as a full khai play, ISO CH. A **Willensnation** - a nation by
will, not by tongue or blood - held together across **all four national
languages**: German (`de`), French (`fr`), Italian (`it`), and Romansh (`rm`).
Its history is the plot line, its people the cast, the roles its society encodes,
its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## Four languages

The point of Switzerland is that it coheres without a shared tongue, so the cast
speaks all four national languages, each persona in its own:

- **German** - Wilhelm Tell, Vreni
- **French** - Guillaume-Henri Dufour, Henry Dunant
- **Italian** - Marco (Ticino)
- **Romansh** - Gian (Graubünden)

The play and the shared instances are in German (the plurality, the play's
declared language); each region's personas declare their own `language:`. `de`
and `fr` are validated by the local detector; `it` and `rm` are registered in
`khai.languages` and routed to assisted verification.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_switzerland.md](play_switzerland.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_ch.md](pitch_ch.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Switzerland's profile (consensus, patience, distrust of central power,
  precision, the will to be one) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Rütli oath (1291), the federal state (1848), the Red
  Cross (1863), and women's suffrage (1971) - each casting the elements it needs.
- **Personas:** Tell, Dufour, Dunant as the historic cast, plus defining
  archetypes across the language regions - Vreni (German), Marco (Italian), Gian
  (Romansh). Each is wired to the language engine by the crossing it holds (Dufour
  carries the other national tongues; Marco wears German; Dunant forged the
  written word).
- **Positions:** die Konkordanz (consensus power-sharing), der Milizgedanke (the
  citizen who serves), die Neutralität.
- **Places:** Bern (the federal city), Genf (Geneva, the francophone, humanitarian
  west).
- **Processes:** die Volksabstimmung (direct democracy), das Jassen.
- **Pieces:** das Taschenmesser (the army knife), das Fondue (one pot, many
  cheeses - the confederation on a plate).
