# Sweden: a khai culture

Sweden staged as a full khai play, ISO SE, language sv. A large, reserved,
consensus-seeking country as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people
as the cast, the roles its culture encodes, its places, its practices, and its
defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_sweden.md](play_sweden.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_se.md](pitch_se.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Sweden's profile (consensus, lagom, the folkhem, modern reserve, trust) and
  naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** Gustav Vasa and independence (1523), the people's home
  (1928), Raoul Wallenberg in Budapest (1944), and women's suffrage (1921) - each
  casting the elements it needs (women's suffrage casts a place and a position, no
  persona).
- **Personas:** Gustav Vasa and Raoul Wallenberg as the historic cast, plus two
  defining archetypes, Astrid (older, lagom and the folkhem) and Linnea (younger,
  global, design and tech, the open world). Each is wired to the language engine
  by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** lagom (the right measure), allemansrätten (the right of public
  access), folkhemmet (the people's home).
- **Places:** Stockholm (the capital), Gamla Uppsala (the nation's old seat).
- **Processes:** fikan, midsommar.
- **Pieces:** Dalahästen (the Dala horse), knäckebrödet (crispbread).
