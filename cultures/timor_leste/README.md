# Timor-Leste: a khai culture

Timor-Leste staged as a full khai play, ISO TL, language tet (Tetum). The land of
the rising sun at the eastern end of the archipelago, held by lulik (the sacred),
by faith, and by the long road to freedom carried with dignity. Its history is the
plot line, its people the cast, the roles its language and faith encode, its
places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## Language

The play and the whole cast are written in Tetum (`tet`), the national lingua
franca that binds together the many local languages of Timor-Leste and stands
alongside Portuguese as an official tongue. The H2 chapter headings, this README,
and REFERENCES are in English; all prose bodies are in Tetum.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_timor_leste.md](play_timor_leste.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_tet.md](pitch_tet.md), the Hofstede layer, written from a
  community-first, sacred, present-rooted profile and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the grandfather crocodile (the origin legend), the coming
  of the Cross (Catholicism woven with ancestral belief), the long road (the
  nation's long road to freedom, handled soberly), and the restoration of
  independence (2002), each casting the elements it needs.
- **Personas:** Xanana Gusmao, Jose Ramos-Horta, Dom Carlos Filipe Ximenes Belo,
  and the poet Francisco Borja da Costa as the public-record cast, plus one
  defining archetype, Beimalae the tais weaver (the highland keeper of lisan and
  the cloth of identity). Each is wired to the language engine by the
  language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** lulik (the sacred, the uma lulik, and the bonds of clan and
  ancestor), lia-tetun (the unifying tongue among many local languages), the long
  road to nationhood (the memory carried with honour).
- **Places:** Dili (the seaside capital where the land meets the world), Mount
  Ramelau / Tatamailau (the highest mountain, the highlands, and the coffee).
- **Processes:** tais weaving (the cloth passed hand to hand), the lisan ceremony
  (the customary rite that binds clan and ancestor).
- **Pieces:** the tais (the woven cloth of identity and ceremony), the uma lulik
  (the sacred house at the centre of the clan).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain sources are recorded in
[REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the pitch is its
invisible hand. The nation's long road to freedom is treated with the utmost
sobriety on the public record, with no atrocity detail and taking no political
side; sources in the public domain are credited, never claimed; the staging and
the architecture are original work.
