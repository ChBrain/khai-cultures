# Timor-Leste: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain and public-record sources
behind the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_tet.md](pitch_tet.md) is written from a cultural profile in the
spirit of Geert Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and
carries no number. Timor-Leste is not among the countries with published scores in
the standard Hofstede dataset; the profile below is an approximate, qualitative
reading offered so the basis of the pitch is on the record without entering the
prose, and should be read as indicative only.

| Dimension                     | Timor-Leste profile (qualitative)  |
| ----------------------------- | ---------------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | high (respect for elders and rank) |
| Individualism                 | low (clan and community first)     |
| Motivation toward Achievement | moderate                           |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | moderate to high                   |
| Long Term Orientation         | rooted in ancestor and lisan       |
| Indulgence                    | restrained                         |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the six-dimensions model of national
culture), used here as a framing model only; no official Timor-Leste scores are
published, so the reading above is the authors' qualitative estimate.

## Languages

The play and the whole cast are written in Tetum (`tet`), the national lingua
franca of Timor-Leste, which stands alongside Portuguese as an official language
and binds together the country's many local tongues. The Tetum is the authors' own
composition, offered in good faith and best reviewed by a native speaker.

never claimed; the staging is original work. The nation's long road to freedom is
