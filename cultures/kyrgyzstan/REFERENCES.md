# Kyrgyzstan: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical and cultural
sources behind the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_ky.md](pitch_ky.md) is written from Kyrgyzstan's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
Kyrgyzstan is not always listed individually in the published data; the profile is
read from the model's findings for Central Asian and post-Soviet societies of the
region (collectivist, with strong respect for elders and the community word, modest
individualism, and a kin-centered hospitality). The orientation is recorded here so
the basis is on the record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Kyrgyzstan orientation (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | -------------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | high                             |
| Individualism                 | low (collectivist)               |
| Motivation toward Achievement | moderate                         |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | high                             |
| Long Term Orientation         | moderate to high                 |
| Indulgence                    | low to moderate                  |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the six-dimensions model of national
culture and the Country Comparison tool), read for the Central Asian region. Values
are approximate orientations, not exact published scores for Kyrgyzstan.

from the public historical and cultural record. They are credited here and never
claimed; the staging is original work. The Soviet era and modern political
turbulence are acknowledged soberly and kept out of scope, with the focus held on
the enduring culture, the mountains, the epic, and the language.
