# Kyrgyzstan: a khai culture

Kyrgyzstan staged as a full khai play, ISO KG, language ky. A land of high
mountains, the nomad, and the world's longest epic, staged as a theatre: its
history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its language and
traditions encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned
by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_kyrgyzstan.md](play_kyrgyzstan.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_ky.md](pitch_ky.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Kyrgyzstan's profile (a collectivist, kin- and elder-centered society of strong
  hospitality, modest individualism, and respect for the word of the community)
  and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Epic of Manas (the oral past and the longest epic),
  Alai and Kurmanjan Datka (the nineteenth-century stateswoman), the century the
  word opened (the flowering of letters and music), and Independence (1991) - each
  casting the elements it needs, the Soviet and modern political turbulence kept
  soberly out of scope.
- **Personas:** Chingiz Aitmatov (the writer who carried the nation's voice to the
  world), Toktogul Satylganov (the great akyn and komuz master), Sayakbai Karalaev
  (the great manaschy of Manas), and Kurmanjan Datka (the "Tsaritsa of Alai") as
  the historic cast, plus one archetype, the Herder (the nomad of the jailoo). Each
  is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** Manas and the word (the oral memory of a people, anchored by the
  epic), the mountains and the nomad life (the Tien Shan, the jailoo, the horse and
  the yurt), hospitality and the elders' word (the guest in the seat of honor and
  the council of elders).
- **Places:** Lake Issyk-Kul (the sacred lake of the Tien Shan, Aitmatov's
  homeland), the jailoo (the high summer pasture, the heart of nomad life).
- **Processes:** reciting Manas (the manaschy keeping the epic alive in living
  voice), kok-boru (the great horseback game of the nomad).
- **Pieces:** the komuz (the three-stringed national instrument, the heart of
  Kyrgyz music), the kalpak (the white felt hat, the emblem of Kyrgyz dignity).
