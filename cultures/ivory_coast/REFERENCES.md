# Cote d'Ivoire: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain and standard sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_fr.md](pitch_fr.md) is written from Cote d'Ivoire's profile on
Geert Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no
number. Where a national score is not separately published, West African
regional values are used. The scores and their source are recorded here so the
data is on the record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Cote d'Ivoire / West Africa score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 77                                          |
| Individualism                 | 20                                          |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 46                                          |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 54                                          |
| Long Term Orientation         | 16                                          |
| Indulgence                    | 78                                          |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published
by Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; where a country-specific score
is unavailable, the West Africa regional figure is used. The profile is collective
and welcoming, with a marked respect for elders and authority.

## Historical and cultural sources

The plots, the personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn from the
public historical record, oral tradition, and standard reference works. They are
credited here and never claimed; the staging is original work. Any period of
national trial is staged soberly on the public record, taking no political side
and dwelling on no atrocity; north and south, all faiths and all peoples are
treated with equal dignity.

The one defining archetype, [the tantie of the maquis](persona_la_tantie.md), is
not a historical individual: a composite that carries the everyday spirit of the
hustle and the open table - the woman who feeds the neighbourhood out of next to
nothing and keeps the spare chair for whoever is hungry.
