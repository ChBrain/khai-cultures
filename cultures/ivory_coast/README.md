# Cote d'Ivoire: a khai culture

Cote d'Ivoire staged as a full khai play, ISO CI, language fr. A West African
land born of mixing, the world's leading cocoa producer, that holds together
north and south, all faiths and more than sixty peoples, as a theatre: its
history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its languages and
faiths encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by
a single pitch. The prose is written in Ivorian French, in the warm, Abidjanais
register of the country itself, not a metropolitan one.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_ivory_coast.md](play_ivory_coast.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_fr.md](pitch_fr.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Cote
  d'Ivoire's profile (warm and welcoming, family- and group-minded, present- and
  people-focused, resourceful, with respect for elders and the chief) and naming
  no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** Pokou's sacrifice and the founding of the Baoule, the many
  peoples and their masks, independence and cocoa (1960), and Abidjan, music and
  football - each casting the elements it needs, and each staged soberly and with
  dignity.
- **Personas:** Queen Abla Pokou, Felix Houphouet-Boigny, Bernard Dadie, and
  Didier Drogba as the historic and legendary cast, plus one archetype, the tantie
  of the maquis who feeds the neighbourhood. Each is wired to the language engine
  by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** Pokou's sacrifice (giving what is dearest so the others may
  pass), the mixing (a nation that knows itself by how it blends everyone), the
  hustle (getting by on next to nothing, and with a smile on top).
- **Places:** Abidjan (the Manhattan of West Africa on the lagoon) and
  Yamoussoukro (the political capital and the Old Man's home village).
- **Processes:** the maquis (the country's open table) and coupe-decale (the
  music and dance that turns hardship into a feast).
- **Pieces:** the mask (the carved face of the invisible, the living archive of
  the peoples) and attieke (fermented cassava semolina, the shared plate the
  nation knows itself by).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain and standard sources
behind the plots, personas, places, and pieces are recorded in
[REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the pitch is its
invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never claimed; north
and south, all faiths and all peoples are staged with equal dignity, and any
period of trial is acknowledged soberly, taking no side, with no atrocity detail;
the staging and the architecture are original work.
