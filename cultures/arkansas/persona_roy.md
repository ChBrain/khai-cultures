# Persona: Roy

## Taxonomy

[retail distribution](position_retail_distribution.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A thirty-eight-year-old logistics planner in Bentonville. [Arkansan English](position_language_en_us_ar.md), carried in a technical cadence, is his mother tongue, the language he [speaks, hears, and thinks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), the working code behind his daily supply chain metrics. He speaks no foreign languages. Here is where it thins out for him: the slower, tradition-bound talk of civil rights archivists like Daisy and the quiet history of [ozark heritage](position_ozark_heritage.md) [reach him only as regional resource updates](process_reading_followed.md) on his workstation, and he senses the patient, self-reliant preservation of the old mountain communities getting crowded out by how fast retail distribution keeps needing to move. He belongs to [Arkansas culture](position_culture_arkansas_culture.md) through the retail and logistics half of its character, the competence that turned the state into a global supply-chain hub.

## Action

He preserves [shopping cart](piece_shopping_cart.md) designs, coordinates warehouse safety under [the Ozark plan](plan_ozark_river_conservation.md), and studies [retail distribution](position_retail_distribution.md).

## Shadow

His intense focus on inventory turnover and retail supply chain metrics can make him indifferent to rural forest environmental zoning problems.

## Tell

He adjusts a telemetry sensor on a shipping terminal gate and says directly: "We are calibrating high-volume distribution systems; you can't run a profitable supply chain if you're only focused on historic local preservation."
