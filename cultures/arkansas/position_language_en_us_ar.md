# Position: Arkansan English

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The authority of Arkansas's own variety of American English, the Southern-drawled speech of the Natural State spoken from the Ozark ridges to the Delta and the corporate campuses of the northwest, one regional voice of the wider American tongue. It carries the warm cadence of the courthouse and the civil rights archive alongside the fast technical talk of the retail logistics headquarters. It holds the plain, direct register in which Arkansans conduct their working and civic life, from the preservation report to the supply-chain metric.

## Orders

What the state conducts its shared life in: the archive and the school exhibition, the warehouse briefing and the logistics report, the ruling and the broadcast. It orders the common ground on which the civil rights archivist of Little Rock and the logistics planner of Bentonville address one another and the nation, a single regional speech spanning the mountain heritage and the distribution center.

## Loses

Ground when its warm, tradition-bound drawl is heard only as a distant echo beside the fast, technical register of the retail boards, so that the patient rhythm of the integration history and the mountain communities sounds secondary against the talk of high-volume distribution; and ground when the regional voice is flattened toward a generic corporate English that carries none of the state's memory.

## Drives

It is the mother tongue of [Daisy](persona_daisy.md), the Little Rock archivist who speaks and writes her preservation reports in a warm Southern drawl, and of [Roy](persona_roy.md), the Bentonville logistics planner whose technical cadence is the same regional tongue turned to the supply-chain metric. It drives Arkansas to hold its heritage and its corporate-logistics voices as one speech, so that the Ozark community and the distribution center answer each other in the same Arkansan English.
