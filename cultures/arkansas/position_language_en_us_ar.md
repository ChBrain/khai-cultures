# Position: Arkansan English

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

Arkansan English carries the same analytic grammar as the rest of American English, along with a Southern innovation English elsewhere lacks: y'all, a second person plural built to repair the gap left when English collapsed thou and you into a single word, and fixin' to, a marker that grammaticalizes the sense of being on the verge of an action before it starts.

## Orders

It forces the same obligations as English generally, fixed word order, obligatory articles, do-support for questions and negatives, and it forces, in this region's speech, the long vowel of words like 'ride' or 'time' to flatten toward a single held tone rather than the two-part glide standard English keeps, and it forces a merger of the vowels in 'pin' and 'pen' before a nasal so that only context tells them apart.

## Loses

It lost, as English generally did, any grammatical way to mark a noun's gender or case, and any T/V split for address; and where the drawled vowel flattens two sounds into one, the ear that relies on that vowel to keep 'pin' and 'pen' apart loses the distinction outright and must lean on context instead.

## Drives

Y'all trains the ear to expect a plural marker where plain English leaves none, restoring a distinction the language once had and gave up; and the held, lengthened vowel of the drawl trains an unhurried pace onto speech itself, so that the shape of the vowel becomes the shape of the pace of thought. Fixin' to trains the mind to announce intention a beat before the act, marking imminence as a habit of speech rather than leaving it unsaid.
