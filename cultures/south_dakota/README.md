# South Dakota: a khai culture

South Dakota staged as a full khai play, ISO US-SD, language en. The Mount Rushmore State: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its Homestake gold rush, Mount Rushmore construction, and Wounded Knee occupation encodes, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house that holds this culture and answers for it.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_south_dakota.md](play_south_dakota.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_south_dakota.md](pitch_south_dakota.md), the South Dakota key.
- **Plots (history):** the Homestake Gold Rush of 1876, the Mount Rushmore Construction of 1927, and the Wounded Knee Occupation of 1973.
- **Personas:** Alma (historic site coordinator) and Dakota (Lakota cultural coordinator), written in English.
- **Positions:** black hills heritage (mountain forestry/mineral history/tribal land sovereignty), monument preservation (granite engineering/park safety/memorial preservation).
- **Places:** Mount Rushmore, Wounded Knee.
- **Processes:** gold sifting (placer gold sifting), timber harvesting (Black Hills pine logging).
- **Pieces:** a gold nugget, a sculptor chisel.
- **Plan:** the Black Hills aquifer plan.
