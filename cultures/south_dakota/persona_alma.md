# Persona: Alma

## Taxonomy

[monument preservation](position_monument_preservation.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A thirty-eight-year-old historic site coordinator at Mount Rushmore. English, spoken with a mountain cadence, is her mother tongue, the language in which she [speaks, hears, and thinks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), the defining code of her daily monument safety checks. It is the [South Dakota English](position_language_en_us_sd.md) of the Black Hills, the mountain cadence of the Mount Rushmore crews. She speaks no foreign languages. Yet the loss lies in the seam: The slower, tradition-bound talk of tribal coordinators like Dakota and the quiet history of [black hills heritage](position_black_hills_heritage.md) [she reads only as regional reservation updates](process_reading_followed.md) on her workstation, feeling that the patient, self-reliant land preservation of early forest communities is being forgotten in the race for high-volume tourism development. She belongs to [the Mount Rushmore State](position_culture_mount_rushmore_state.md), and her half of its inheritance is the granite monument and the visitor safety record the state's tourism economy depends on.

## Action

She preserves [sculptor chisel](piece_sculptor_chisel.md) tools, coordinates site safety under [the Black Hills plan](plan_black_hills_watershed_protection.md), and studies [monument preservation](position_monument_preservation.md).

## Shadow

Her intense focus on safety metrics and tourist numbers can make her indifferent to municipal environmental zoning problems in rural counties.

## Tell

She adjusts a telemetry sensor on a granite rock face and says directly: "We are calibrating high-pressure pneumatic tools; you can't run a safe monument if you're only focused on historic land preservation."
