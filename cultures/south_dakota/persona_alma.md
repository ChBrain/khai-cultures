# Persona: Alma

## Taxonomy

[monument preservation](position_monument_preservation.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

Thirty-eight years old, she runs historic-site safety at Mount Rushmore, and the mountain-cadence English she [speaks, hears, and thinks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) in is the only tongue she's ever needed - the [South Dakota English](position_language_en_us_sd.md) of the crews who work the granite. No foreign language, and no want of one. What she doesn't have a native ear for is Dakota's side of the hills: the slower, treaty-paced talk of tribal coordinators, the whole weight of [black hills heritage](position_black_hills_heritage.md), which crosses her workstation [as a line or two on a reservation status report](process_reading_followed.md) and nothing more. She half-suspects the patient, unhurried work of holding land is losing out to whatever draws the next busload of tourists, and she isn't proud of thinking it. She belongs to [the Mount Rushmore State](position_culture_mount_rushmore_state.md) same as Dakota does, but her half of the inheritance is the granite face and the safety record the tourist season runs on.

## Action

She keeps [sculptor chisel](piece_sculptor_chisel.md) tools in working order, runs site safety under [the Black Hills plan](plan_black_hills_watershed_protection.md), and keeps up her study of [monument preservation](position_monument_preservation.md).

## Shadow

Watching gate counts and incident logs all day can leave her tone-deaf to the zoning fights playing out in the rural counties around her, the ones that never show up on any safety report.

## Tell

She adjusts a telemetry sensor bolted to the rock face and doesn't look up: "I've got pneumatic drills running at four hundred PSI on a mountain full of tourists. You can worry about the history once nobody's standing under it when it lets go."
