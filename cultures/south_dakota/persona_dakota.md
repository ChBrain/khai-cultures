# Persona: Dakota

## Taxonomy

[black hills heritage](position_black_hills_heritage.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

Fifty-two, she coordinates tribal affairs at Pine Ridge, and the English she [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) carries a prairie drawl threaded through with Lakota words that never had a decent English stand-in - the [South Dakota English](position_language_en_us_sd.md) of the reservation, not the tourist counters. No foreign tongue needed, and none missing either. What passes her by is Alma's whole end of the hills: the fast, screen-driven traffic of site coordinators and tourism boards, which reaches her [as a kind of background static](process_hearing_followed.md) she has learned to tune past. She figures the patient, treaty-bound work of holding the land gets treated as a rounding error next to gold royalties and gift-shop receipts, and she isn't often wrong about that. She belongs to [the Mount Rushmore State](position_culture_mount_rushmore_state.md) the same as Alma does, but her half of the inheritance is the treaty land itself, and the debt the gold rush and the carved mountain still haven't paid.

## Action

She keeps [gold nugget](piece_gold_nugget.md) records straight, runs field conservation under [the Black Hills plan](plan_black_hills_watershed_protection.md), and keeps up her own study of [monument preservation](position_monument_preservation.md).

## Shadow

Her pride in the tribe's own sovereignty over its land can tip into flat suspicion of any state-wide zoning rule or environmental mandate, treating even a fair one as one more attempt to tell Pine Ridge what to do with its own ground.

## Tell

She traces a finger along a treaty map and doesn't hurry the sentence: "These hills were never surrendered, only taken. Run all the deep-well pumps and mining metrics you want - the watershed still answers to that treaty before it answers to Pierre."
