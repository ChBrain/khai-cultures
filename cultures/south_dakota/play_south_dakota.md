## Name

South Dakota, the Mount Rushmore State, split down the middle by the Missouri River into an East River of black-dirt farm ground and a West River of cattle range running out to the Black Hills. ISO US-SD, language en.

## Arc

Two halves of one state, worked by the same patient hands: East River's corn and soybean townships, and West River's cattle country climbing to the pine-and-granite lift of the Black Hills. Gold pulled thousands into those hills in 1876, in flat violation of the Fort Laramie treaty that had promised the ground to the Lakota forever; sixty years on, drill and dynamite gave four presidents' faces to that same granite, a monument the Lakota never agreed to. Wounded Knee carries both endings of that history, the 1890 massacre and the 1973 occupation that finally made the country answer for it - and the state still drills its wells deep into the same hills it keeps as monument, over ground it has never settled up on.

## Triggers

**[Plot 1: The Homestake Gold Rush 1876](plot_01_gold_rush.md)**

Gold turned up in French Creek in 1874, and within two years thousands were pouring into hills the United States had sworn, by treaty, belonged to the Lakota forever - breaking Fort Laramie before the ink had even cooled.

**[Plot 2: The Mount Rushmore Construction 1927](plot_02_mount_rushmore.md)**

Sculptor Gutzon Borglum turned his drills on a Black Hills peak the Lakota call the Six Grandfathers and gave it four presidential faces instead, raising the monument the whole country now recognizes on sight.

**[Plot 3: The Wounded Knee Occupation 1973](plot_03_wounded_knee.md)**

Oglala Lakota and AIM activists held Wounded Knee for seventy-one days, naming both a corrupt tribal government and a century of broken treaty promises the country still hadn't squared up on.

## Stakes

Whether a state that has drilled the Black Hills aquifers hard enough to worry its own ranchers can rein in that thirst before the watershed gives out - on ground half the state calls monument and the other half still calls unceded.
