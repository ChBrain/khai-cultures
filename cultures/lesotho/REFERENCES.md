# Lesotho: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind the
plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_st.md](pitch_st.md) is written from Lesotho's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
Lesotho is not always listed individually in the published comparison; where a
direct national score is unavailable, the regional Southern / sub-Saharan African
profile is used as a proxy. The values below are approximate and are recorded here
so the data is on the record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Lesotho / regional profile (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------------------ |
| Power Distance                | high                                 |
| Individualism                 | low (strongly collectivist)          |
| Motivation toward Achievement | moderate                             |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | moderate                             |
| Long Term Orientation         | lower-moderate                       |
| Indulgence                    | moderate                             |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the six-dimensions model of national
culture and the Country Comparison tool). Scores are approximate and, for Lesotho,
drawn from the regional Southern African profile as published by Hofstede Insights
/ The Culture Factor Group; the strong collectivism is the load-bearing trait
behind the pitch.

from the public historical record. They are credited here and never claimed; the
staging is original work. The lifaqane upheaval, the keeping of the land, and the
era of outside protection are staged soberly on the public record, taking no
political side and dwelling on no atrocity.

The one composite persona, [Molisana](persona_molisana.md), is an archetype, not a
historical individual: the blanket-wrapped pony herd-boy of the highlands who
carries the life of the mountains, the blanket, the lesiba, and the daily keeping
of the land.
