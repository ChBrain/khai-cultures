# Lesotho: a khai culture

Lesotho staged as a full khai play, ISO LS, languages Sesotho and English. The
Mountain Kingdom, the only country entirely above 1000m, staged as a theatre: its
history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its language and
kingship encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by
a single pitch. The prose is written in Sesotho, the tongue of nearly all Basotho
and a rare national unifier; this README and REFERENCES are in English.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_lesotho.md](play_lesotho.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_st.md](pitch_st.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Lesotho's profile (deep collectivism and community, humility and restraint, a
  strong sense of dignity and shelter) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** Thaba Bosiu and the founding, keeping the land, the writing
  of Sesotho at Morija, and independence and the Mountain Kingdom - each casting
  the elements it needs, the founding and the keeping of the land staged soberly.
- **Personas:** Moshoeshoe I (the founder of the nation), Thomas Mofolo (the first
  Sesotho novelist, author of Chaka), Moshoeshoe II (the monarch of the
  independence era), and Puseletso Seema (the famo singer who carried the mountain
  sound abroad), plus one archetype, Molisana (the blanket-wrapped pony herd-boy of
  the highlands). Each is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it
  holds, every Projection closing on the gap at the limit of the tongue.
- **Positions:** borena (the kingship that unites the nation and the pitso where it
  is answered), lefatshe la lithaba (the mountain land, the only country wholly
  above 1000m), botho (the conviction that a person is a person through others).
- **Places:** Maseru (the capital on the Caledon, the seat of kingship and state),
  Thaba Bosiu (the mountain-fortress where the nation was founded and sheltered).
- **Processes:** the pitso (the public assembly where the king listens and the
  people speak), wearing the blanket (carrying the nation on one's shoulder).
- **Pieces:** the Basotho blanket (the seanamarena worn with pride and meaning),
  the mokorotlo hat (the conical grass hat that crowns the national flag).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the lifaqane upheaval, the keeping of the land, and the era of outside
protection are treated soberly on the public record, taking no political side; the
staging and the architecture are original work.
