# South Korea: a khai culture

South Korea staged as a full khai play, ISO KR, language ko, written in Hangul.
A people of deep feeling who gave themselves a written alphabet, a Confucian
family order, and a wave the world now follows, set out as a theatre: its history
as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its language and heritage
encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a
single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_south_korea.md](play_south_korea.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_ko.md](pitch_ko.md), the Hofstede layer, written from South
  Korea's profile (a strong collectivism and family orientation, a long-term and
  achievement-driven outlook, a deep respect for hierarchy and learning, and a
  restraint that holds deep feeling underneath) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the creation of Hangul (1443), the age of the Confucian
  scholars, the division of the peninsula, and the Miracle on the Han River -
  each casting the elements it needs. The division is staged soberly and with
  dignity, taking no side and giving no graphic detail.
- **Personas:** King Sejong (who made the alphabet), Yi Hwang (the Confucian
  scholar), and Sin Saimdang (the artist and mother) as the historic cast, the
  film director Bong Joon-ho as the figure of the modern, democratic, cultural
  era, and one archetype, the Mother, who carries han and jeong. Each is wired to
  the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** Hangul (the alphabet a nation made for itself), the Confucian
  heritage and the family (filial duty, learning, and rite), and han and jeong
  (the deep grief and the deep warmth that live together in one heart).
- **Places:** Seoul (the alphabet made, the division borne, the miracle built on
  the Han) and Andong (the heartland of the scholars, the mountains and the
  land).
- **Processes:** kimjang (the communal making of kimchi for the winter) and the
  ancestral rites (jesa, the family joined in one line across the living and the
  dead).
- **Pieces:** kimchi (the dish at the center of the table) and the hanbok (the
  dress worn on the great days).
