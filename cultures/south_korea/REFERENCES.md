# South Korea: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_ko.md](pitch_ko.md) is written from South Korea's profile on
Geert Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no
number. The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record
without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | South Korea score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | --------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 60                          |
| Individualism                 | 18                          |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 39                          |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 85                          |
| Long Term Orientation         | 100                         |
| Indulgence                    | 29                          |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; South Korea's Long Term Orientation
score is among the highest in the published data.

giving no graphic detail.

The one defining persona, [the Mother](persona_eomeoni.md), is an archetype, not a
historical individual: a composite who carries the tension between han (the deep,
settled grief of division, hardship, and parting) and jeong (the deep warmth that
holds family and neighbor together), endured and turned outward into care.
