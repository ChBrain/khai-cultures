# Senegal: a khai culture

Senegal staged as a full khai play, ISO SN, language fr. The land of teranga,
where the word is given and the door stays open, rendered as a theatre: its
history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its language and faith
encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single
pitch. The prose is written in Senegalese French, not a metropolitan register.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_senegal.md](play_senegal.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_fr.md](pitch_fr.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Senegal's profile (a strongly collective and relational society, marked respect
  for age and rank, a warm and present-focused indulgence, and a preference for the
  long greeting and the shared bowl over haste) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the old kingdoms and the coming of Islam through the Sufi
  brotherhoods, Saint-Louis and Goree and the colonial time (the memory of the
  slave trade treated soberly and with dignity), independence and Senghor in 1960,
  and the Senegal that sings - each casting the elements it needs.
- **Personas:** Leopold Sedar Senghor (the poet president of negritude), Cheikh
  Amadou Bamba (founder of the Mouride way), Youssou N'Dour (the voice of mbalax),
  and Mariama Ba (the great woman of letters) as the historic cast, plus one
  archetype, the griot (the keeper of the word and the memory of lineages). Each is
  wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** teranga (hospitality as the national ethic), the brotherhoods
  (the Sufi orders, Mouride and Tijani, as the spiritual frame), and negritude
  (black pride turned toward dialogue rather than the closed fist).
- **Places:** Dakar (the bubbling capital and showcase, with Goree offshore held
  in sober memory) and Saint-Louis (the old river city and first colonial capital).
- **Processes:** the laamb (traditional wrestling and the theatre of honour) and
  the ataya (the three-round tea ceremony, the school of patience and the cement of
  the neighbourhood).
- **Pieces:** the kora (the griot's harp-lute that carries the memory of lineages)
  and the boubou (the long robe of Senegalese dignity).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the slave trade and the colonial period are treated soberly on the public
record, with dignity and without atrocity detail, and no political side is taken;
the staging and the architecture are original work.
