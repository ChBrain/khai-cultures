# Senegal: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind the
plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_fr.md](pitch_fr.md) is written from Senegal's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
Senegal is most often read through Hofstede's West Africa cluster; the scores and
their source are recorded here so the data is on the record without entering the
prose.

| Dimension                     | Senegal / West Africa score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 77                                    |
| Individualism                 | 20                                    |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 46                                    |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 54                                    |
| Long Term Orientation         | 25                                    |
| Indulgence                    | 78                                    |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate and, for Senegal,
are commonly drawn from Hofstede's West Africa regional grouping as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; the low Individualism reflects the
strongly collective and relational character the pitch renders as teranga.

from the public historical record. They are credited here and never claimed; the
staging is original work. The memory of the slave trade and the colonial period is
staged soberly on the public record, with dignity and without atrocity detail, and
no political side is taken.
