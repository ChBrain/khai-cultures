# Position: the South Sudanese culture

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The belonging to a young country far older than its own nation, carried in [the cattle](position_the_cattle.md) that give a man his wealth and his praise-name, in [the river](position_the_river.md) whose rise and fall set the whole year's rhythm, and in the equal dignity of [the many peoples](position_the_many_peoples.md), Dinka and Nuer, Bari and Shilluk, Azande and more, none the owner of the land and none its guest. It carries the long waiting the elders sang would surely end, the morning that came at last on the ninth of July, 2011, the newest flag raised on earth after a road too hard to walk again in the telling. It carries the wrestling-ground and the dance where a person stands for the whole camp, and the pride of the named ox, sung into the night by whoever was given it. It carries, too, a common tongue that trade and school and government hold in [English](position_language_en_ss.md), laid over the many mother tongues that still carry the deepest praises no other tongue can hold.

## Orders

That every people the land holds is honoured in equal measure, the small beside the great, and that a person is first a child of a clan and a tongue and proud of it before anything the state can name. That the cattle be kept, watered, and sung to as wealth and kinship both, and that a man know his finest ox as he knows his own family. That the wrestling-ground and the dance carry a person's honour before the whole camp, and that dignity be worn quietly, in song and bearing, rather than claimed loudly. And that the long waiting, once it ends, be carried forward in patience rather than spent whole in a single season of celebration.

## Loses

Ground where the pride of one people is raised over another, where the count of cattle becomes a claim to rule rather than a bond shared, and where the many peoples the land has always held in equal dignity are pulled toward one people's telling of the country. Ground in the long, hard road to nationhood that this staging does not walk in detail, whose weight nonetheless presses on every family it touched. Ground, too, where the young of the growing towns trade the cattle camp and the mother tongue of the clan for the common talk of the market alone, so that a grandchild can trade in the market but can no longer answer a grandmother in her own tongue. And ground where the newness of the nation and the size of the task outrun the hope that first raised the flag.

## Drives

Drives whoever is raised in it to carry dignity in bearing and in song rather than in loud claim, and to know the cattle, the river, and the wrestling-ground as the oldest school of a person's worth. Drives the many peoples to hold each other in equal honour under one flag without asking any one of them to stop being itself, carrying the mother tongue of the clan beneath the common talk of the market rather than trading one for the other. Drives the nation, still newest on earth, to turn a long-awaited morning into a home worthy of the waiting, patient with the size of the task before it. And it drives the South Sudanese, wherever the road since independence has taken them, to answer first to the honour of their people and their word, the way a young man answers to the colour of his ox before his own name.
