# Persona: Taban lo Liyong

## Taxonomy

[the many peoples](position_the_many_peoples.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The poet, story-teller, and critic of these rivers, a restless and provoking mind who carried the tales of the cattle plains into books and argued the worth of African letters to anyone who would listen and many who would not. Sharp-tongued, playful, and never one to sit quiet, he gathered the songs and the proverbs of the many peoples and set them down so they would not thin away. His mother tongue of the home ground he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [hears](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) as the first music in his ear, and the common [English](position_language_en_ss.md) of his books he [writes](process_writing_drafted.md) and [reads and follows](process_reading_followed.md) as the tool that carried his people's tales to the wide world. He set the oral songs of a dozen peoples into the one written tongue that the world reads, and in the setting down he knew what every gatherer of tales knows: that the praise-song breathes in the mother tongue and on the tongue of the singer, and that the page, for all it saves, can only point at the gap at the limit of the tongue, never close it. He belongs to [the South Sudanese culture](position_culture_south_sudanese_culture.md) as the gatherer who set its many peoples' songs into the one written tongue the world reads.

## Action

In [the long road to nationhood](plot_03_the_long_road.md) he carries the songs and tales of [the many peoples](position_the_many_peoples.md) into the written word, so the country comes to its morning with its voice already gathered into books. By writing the gathering down, he holds the many tongues in his pages and gives the young nation a literature drawn from its own ground.

## Shadow

The page that saves the song can also still it: a tale gathered into a book may be honoured on a shelf and no longer sung at the fire, the living voice of the camp quietly traded for the printed line, so that what is kept is also, in part, what is lost the moment it is set down.

## Tell

He turned the praises of the cattle plains into books the world could read, and argued to the last that the songs of his peoples belonged in the front rank of letters - and that the truest of them were still the ones sung, not the ones printed.
