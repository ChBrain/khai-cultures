# Place: the Sudd

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Shown

The great wetland at the country's heart, one of the largest swamps on the face of the earth, where the White Nile spreads itself wide into a vast country of reeds and floating papyrus and slow brown channels that braid and lose themselves. The fish are many, the birds rise in clouds, and beyond the reeds the savanna runs out toward Boma, where the wildlife moves on its long seasonal migrations. In the dry months the cattle camps come down to its edge for grazing and water; when the floods rise the whole life of the land falls back before it.

## Offers

A place that feeds those who know its channels, the fisherman in his slim canoe and the herd at its edge; the giver of fish and grazing and the country's oldest road of water; and one of the great wonders of the earth, a swamp so vast it sets the rhythm of a whole nation's year, breathing in and out with the flood.

## Withheld

The ease of passage. The Sudd turns back those who do not know its braided channels, the reeds closing over the way, and it long held the south apart from the wider world by the sheer weight of its water. It withholds the simple road, asking instead the patience and the knowledge of those raised to read it, and reminding the young nation that its heart is a wilderness as much as a wealth.
