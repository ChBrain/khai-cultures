# Pitch: the South Sudanese tone

## Taxonomy

The key in which the staging of [South Sudan](play_south_sudan.md) is played. It changes nothing of what happens - not the rivers, not the cattle, not the long waiting, not the dawn of 2011. It sets only how it sounds, the register in which the whole show is given.

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Tenor

Played warm, patient, and held together by the people around you. Here a person is not first a self standing alone but a son of a clan, a child of a cattle camp, one of a long line who answers to the elders and the kin before he answers to himself. The group comes first, the family is the strong house, and the man with many relatives is a rich man whatever his pocket holds. Time is read off the river and the herd, not off a clock; the dry season and the flood set the pace, and a thing is done when it is ready and not before. Talk runs through the proverb and the praise-song, respect is shown to age and to the chief, and what one owns one shares, for a guest is fed and a stranger given water as a matter of course. Under the patience runs pride, the straight back of a cattle-keeping people who bow to no one and carry themselves tall.

## Undertow

Under the warmth runs the weight of the long waiting, the patience of a people who learned to hold on through years that asked much of them and gave back slowly. There is grief carried quietly and not spoken of, the cost of a hard road kept private the way a proverb keeps its meaning folded. And under the togetherness runs the pull of the many tongues, the question of whether so many peoples, each proud and each its own, can be carried in one house without one crowding out another, and whether the young who go to the town keep the camp and the praise-song or let them thin away.

## Nerve

It strikes the chord of the dawn long promised and at last arrived: the conviction that the morning was always coming, that the waiting was not in vain, that a people can hold their dignity through anything if they hold it together. It plays on the pride of the named ox and the praise-song, the joy of the wrestling-ground where a whole village comes to sing, the strong bond of the clan that is a man's true wealth, and the deep faith that this country, the newest on earth, is the home the elders foretold. It plays, too, on the ache underneath - the long patience and the quiet grief that the morning cost.

## Echo

What is left is the image of a people standing tall by the river: patient, proud, bound to their kin and their cattle, many in tongue and one in their waiting. A nation that runs on the faith that the dawn it waited for has truly come, and that a country held by shared dignity and shared blood-of-the-cattle, many peoples in one young house, is a thing worth the long keeping.
