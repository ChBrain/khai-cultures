# Plan: the new nation

## Taxonomy

The open task the country set itself the morning it was born: to make the long-awaited dawn into a home worthy of the waiting. It is the work of carrying [the many peoples](position_the_many_peoples.md) in one house in equal dignity, of keeping the camps and praises of [the cattle](position_the_cattle.md) alive as the towns grow, and of living well by [the river](position_the_river.md) that gives the country its water, its fish, and its oldest road.

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Direction

Build the young house on the ground the speeches drew in the air: carry every people of the land in equal honour, the small beside the great; keep the mother tongues alive beneath the common talk so the grandchild can still answer the grandmother; hold the cattle camps and the praise-songs and the wrestling-grounds as the towns and the road press in; and live well by the river and the Sudd, reading the year off the water as the country always has, so the morning becomes a home and not only a flag.

## Orders

- To the young like [the cattle-camp youth](persona_the_cattle_camp_youth.md): carry the camp and the praise-song into the new country, and keep the ox-name even when the road calls.
- To the makers like [Yaba Angelosi](persona_yaba.md) and the writers like [Taban lo Liyong](persona_taban.md): carry the songs and tales of the many peoples forward, so the young nation keeps the voice it came to its morning with.
- To the nation: hold the many in one house in equal dignity, and make the long-awaited morning into a home worthy of the long waiting.

## Implementation

A country only newly raised, the newest nation on earth, with the whole work of the house still before it: a young seat at Juba on the river, the many peoples to be carried in equal honour, the cattle camps and the praise-songs and the wrestling to be kept alive as the towns grow, and the great river and the Sudd to be lived by well. The morning has come; the home is still being built.
