# Position: the river

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The White Nile and the great wetland it makes. Coming up from the lakes of the south, the river spreads itself into the Sudd, one of the largest swamps on the face of the earth, a vast country of reeds and floating papyrus and slow brown water where the channels braid and lose themselves and the fish are many. The river sets the year: in the dry months the people and the cattle come down to its edge for water and grazing, and when the floods rise they fall back to the high ground, the whole life of the land breathing in and out with the water. The river feeds the fisherman in his slim canoe, waters the herd, and carries the canoe and the catch and, in time, the nation's first roads of trade.

## Orders

That the year is read off the water, the dry season and the flood telling the people when to move the cattle and when to fish; that the Sudd is both a wealth and a wall, feeding those who know its channels and turning back those who do not; and that a people of the river measure their wealth in water and grazing as much as in cattle, and hold the fishing-grounds and the watering-places as a common trust to be shared and not fenced.

## Loses

Ground when the waters are made to serve places far away rather than the camps along the bank, when the channels are cut or the floods come wrong and the grazing fails; and ground, too, when the slow rhythm of the river is forgotten in the rush of the town, and the young no longer read the water or know the way through the reeds that their grandfathers knew by heart.

## Drives

Lives in the cattle-camp youth, who moves with the water between the high ground and the bank, and shows itself whole in [the Sudd](place_the_sudd.md), the great wetland at the country's heart. It drives South Sudan to live by the rhythm of the flood, to hold the river and the swamp as the giver of fish and grazing and the country's oldest road, and to read its year off the rising and the falling of the water.
