# Piece: the beads

## Taxonomy

Parent group: pieces

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Place

On the neck and the waist and the wrist of the young across the cattle country: the beadwork worn by the peoples of the rivers, corsets and collars and bands strung in bright patterns, each colour and each design carrying a meaning the camp can read. Among many of the peoples a young man's beaded corset speaks of his age and his standing, and a young woman's beads of her people and her readiness; worn with the marks of the body, they are the dress of celebration and the daily pride of the camp.

## Load Bearing

It carries the dignity and the belonging of a people in a string of colour. The beadwork says, without a word, who a person is, which people they belong to, what age and what standing they hold, and it does so in patterns handed down and read at a glance across the camp. Worn alongside the traditional marks of the body, noted here with the dignity they are given by those who carry them, the beads are identity made visible, the pride of the young and the artistry of the makers. Without this work of colour the standing and the belonging that the camp reads in an instant would have no body to be worn upon.

## Apparent

To an outside eye strings of bright glass and bone, threaded into collars and corsets and bands, colourful and finely made. Their whole weight is in the patterns the camp can read: the colours that name a people, the designs that mark an age, the work of the hands that strung them. In itself it is ornament; in the camp it is a person's name and place worn openly on the body.
