# Persona: Yaba Angelosi

## Taxonomy

[the cattle](position_the_cattle.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A singer of the young nation, a maker of dance music who took the praise-song and the camp rhythm of the cattle country and set them to a beat the towns and the diaspora could move to, carried here soberly as an artist of the new South Sudan. Bright, hard-working, and at home on a stage, he is one of the voices that gave the country its own modern sound, the camp's old music dressed for the dance floor without losing its blood. His mother tongue of the home ground he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in, the praises of the ox the first songs he knew, and the common [English](position_language_en_ss.md) and the talk of the town he [carries](process_speaking_carried.md) and [reads and follows](process_reading_followed.md) to reach the young across all the tongues at once. He blends the praise-song with the wide world's beat so a Bari and a Nuer dance to one tune, yet the oldest praises, the colours and the names of the cattle, sit so deep in the mother tongues that no beat can carry them whole across, and so the dance music too meets the gap at the limit of the tongue, and keeps its truest words at home. He belongs to [the South Sudanese culture](position_culture_south_sudanese_culture.md) as the young sound that dresses its oldest praise-songs for a new nation's dance floor without losing their blood.

## Action

In [the dawn of independence (2011)](plot_04_the_dawn.md) he gives the young nation a sound of its own, carrying the rhythm of [the cattle](position_the_cattle.md) and the camp into a modern music the towns and the diaspora claim as theirs. By dressing the old praise for the dance floor, he keeps the camp alive in the ear of the young.

## Shadow

The beat that fills the dance floor can thin the praise it came from: the camp song dressed for the town can be loved as a tune and forgotten as a praise, the named ox and its colours fading into a chorus, so the music that keeps the camp alive can also wear its meaning smooth with playing.

## Tell

He set the praise-song of the cattle to a beat the whole young country could dance to, and kept the colours and the names of the oxen folded inside the chorus, where only those raised in the camp still hear them.
