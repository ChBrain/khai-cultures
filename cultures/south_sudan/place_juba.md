# Place: Juba

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Shown

The capital on the bank of the White Nile, a town of the Bari ground grown into the seat of the newest nation on earth. The river runs broad and brown past its edge, the markets hum in Juba Arabic where every people of the country meets to trade, the boda-bodas weave the dusty streets, and the mango trees throw their shade over the talk of the day. Here the cattle country and the wider world meet, the camp and the town in one place, and here the new flag was first raised.

## Offers

A place where the many peoples meet as one, the common talk of the market letting a Bari trade with a Nuer and a Zande greet a Shilluk; the seat where the young nation governs itself and shows itself to the world; and the river town where the cattle country and the wider world sit down at one table by the Nile.

## Withheld

The ease of an old and settled capital. Juba carries the whole weight of a nation only newly born, a town asked to be the house of a country still building itself, where the long task of making the morning into a home is plainest and heaviest, and where the hope of the dawn meets the hard work that must follow it.
