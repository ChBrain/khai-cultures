# Piece: the named ox

## Taxonomy

Parent group: pieces

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Place

In the heart of the cattle camp and the heart of its keeper: the favourite ox, chosen young, its horns sometimes trained into a great curving shape, given a name drawn from its colour and its markings, and sung to through the night. A young man takes the ox's name and colour as his own, walks at its side, and would no more sell it lightly than sell his own kin. It is the pride of the camp and the subject of its finest poetry.

## Load Bearing

It carries the whole cattle culture in one beast. In a country where wealth and name and song all live in the herd, the named ox is the herd made personal, the single animal in which a man pours his pride, his poetry, and his standing. The praises composed for it are among the truest poems the rivers have made, sung into the dark and known to the whole camp. Through the named ox a young man becomes who he is, takes a name to be called by, and joins the long line of keepers before him. Without this one beast the praise-song and the ox-name and the deep love of cattle would have no single face to wear.

## Apparent

To an outside eye a bull among bulls, large-horned and well-kept, one animal in a herd. Its whole weight is in what the camp has poured into it: the name, the colour-words that hold a hundred shades, the praises sung to it, and the keeper who answers to its name before his own. In itself it is a fine ox; in the camp it is a man's pride and his poem walking on four legs.
