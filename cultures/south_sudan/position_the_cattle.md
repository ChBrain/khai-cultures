# Position: the cattle

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The cow at the centre of the world. Among the cattle-keeping peoples of these rivers the herd is not only food and milk but wealth, name, and song: the count of a man's cattle is the measure of his standing, the bride is brought home with cattle, the dispute is settled in cattle, and a quarrel healed when the cattle change hands. A young man takes the name and the colour of his favourite ox and wears it as his own, composes praises to it through the night, and walks among the herd as among his own kin. The cow is the thread that ties the clan to the clan and the living to the ancestors, and to lose the herd is to lose more than wealth.

## Orders

That a person's worth is read first in cattle and in kin, not in coin; that the great passages of a life - the marriage, the peace-making, the naming - are paid and sealed in cattle; that the ox of a man's heart is his pride and his poem, sung to and shown off and never sold lightly; and that the herd binds the generations, the calf today standing for the calf the grandfather kept, so that to keep cattle is to keep faith with those who came before.

## Loses

Ground when the herd is cut off from the camp and the water, when drought or the press of the town thins the grazing and the young leave the cattle for the road; and ground, too, when the deep love of cattle hardens into a counting that forgets the kin the cattle were always meant to bind, so the herd is hoarded rather than shared and the cow becomes a wall between clans instead of the bridge it was made to be.

## Drives

Lives in the cattle-camp youth, who takes his ox-name and sings its praises into the dark, and stands behind the musicians whose songs carry the camp's rhythm into the town. It drives South Sudan to measure dignity in the herd and the kin, to seal its peace and its marriages in cattle, and to keep the praise-song to the ox alive as the truest poetry the rivers have made.
