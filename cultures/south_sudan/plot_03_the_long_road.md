# Plot: the long road to nationhood

## Taxonomy

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

Held far from any seat of power and folded into borders not their own, the peoples of the south set out on the long road toward a country of their own, a road this staging honours soberly and does not walk in detail.

## Action

Across hard and patient years the people of the south keep faith that their morning will come. The particulars of that road, its trials and its cost, lie outside this staging, which takes no side and dwells on no wound; what it holds is the waiting itself and the dignity carried through it. In these years a founding figure such as John Garang gives the long waiting words, holding up before the country the vision of a land its many peoples could each call their own, and the gathering of peoples comes to understand itself as one country waiting to be born. At the road's end stands the promise of a vote, the chance for the south to speak at last for itself.

## Stage

- [John Garang](persona_garang.md): the founding figure remembered for putting the long waiting into words and holding up the vision of a shared country.
- [the many peoples](position_the_many_peoples.md): the gathering of peoples coming to understand itself as one country waiting to be born.
- [Juba](place_juba.md): the river town that would become the seat of the morning the road was walked toward.

## Tension

This is the road of the long waiting, told soberly and without detail of its hardship, taking no side. Its dignity is the patience of a people who kept faith through years that asked much of them; its quiet weight is that the vision held up along the way was larger than any one figure could carry, and that the morning, when it came, would hand the long task of the house to those who reached it.
