# Persona: the cattle-camp youth

## Taxonomy

[the cattle](position_the_cattle.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A young man of the cattle camp, up before the light to drive the herd to water and grazing, who took the name and the colour of his finest ox the day it was given him and wears it as proudly as his own. Tall, proud, and tireless, he sleeps by the herd in the dry months by the river and falls back to the high ground when the floods come, and on the wrestling-ground and at the dance he stands for his whole camp. The praises he composes for his ox he sings into the dark, a poetry the whole camp knows. His mother tongue of the camp he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in as the only tongue the cattle and the praises need, and the common talk of the market he [carries](process_speaking_carried.md) just far enough to trade a beast and greet a stranger, and the official [English](position_language_en_ss.md) of school and state he only [wears](process_speaking_worn.md) faintly, a few worn words of the new nation come to the camp from far off. He sings the praises of his ox in the tongue of the camp, where the colours and the names of cattle hold a hundred shades no other tongue can carry, and when he goes to the town and tries to tell what the ox is to him, the words thin in the common talk, and he meets the gap at the limit of the tongue, where the deepest praise stays home in the mother tongue.

## Action

In [the rivers and the cattle peoples](plot_01_the_rivers.md) he is the keeper of [the cattle](position_the_cattle.md) and the maker of its praises, the young man who moves with the water and the herd and sings the ox into the night. He carries the oldest life of the rivers into the present, the camp and the praise-song alive in him as they were in his grandfather.

## Shadow

The camp can lose him to the town: the road and the market and the wage can pull the young man off the cattle, and the praises he alone knew go unsung, the named ox and its hundred colours fading from a generation that traded the camp for the street and can no longer make the poem the herd once gave.

## Tell

He answers to the colour of his ox before he answers to his own name, and sings its praises into the dark by the river - and finds, the first time he tries to say in the town's tongue what the ox is to him, that the words will not come.
