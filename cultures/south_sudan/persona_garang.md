# Persona: John Garang

## Taxonomy

[the many peoples](position_the_many_peoples.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A son of the south, a cattle-keeper's child who went far for his learning and came home carrying the vision of a country its many peoples could each call their own, remembered soberly here only as a founding national figure who did not live to see the morning he had spoken of. Steady, learned, and a great speaker, he could hold a crowd by the river the way an elder holds a cattle camp, and he is held in the people's memory as the man who put the long waiting into words. His mother tongue of the cattle plains he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in as in his own breath, and the common English of his learning he [carries](process_speaking_carried.md) and [reads and follows](process_reading_followed.md) with ease, the two tongues meeting in a man who had to speak to the whole gathering of peoples at once. He spoke for a country of many tongues in the one common tongue that all could follow, yet the deepest things, the praise of the ox and the grief of the long road, sit in the mother tongues of the clans and cannot all be carried over into one, and so even the great speaker met the gap at the limit of the tongue.

## Action

In [the long road to nationhood](plot_03_the_long_road.md) he is remembered as the founding figure who held up the vision of a country all its peoples could share, and put the long waiting of [the many peoples](position_the_many_peoples.md) into words the whole gathering could follow. By giving the waiting a voice, he is kept in the people's memory as a father of the morning, though he did not live to walk in it.

## Shadow

The vision held up before a country is larger than any one man can carry, and the morning long spoken of arrives heavier than the words that promised it: the temptation to let the speech stand for the doing, to honour the dream and leave the long work of the house to those who come after, who must make the country real on the ground the speeches drew in the air.

## Tell

He spoke of one country for all its peoples and is remembered for the speaking, leaving the young nation a vision larger than himself and the long task of making it true on the ground.
