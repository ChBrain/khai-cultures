# South Sudan: a khai culture

South Sudan staged as a full khai play, ISO SS, language en. The world's newest
nation, a land far older than the country that sits on it, staged as a theatre:
its history as the plot line, its peoples as the cast, the roles its rivers and
cattle and tongues encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects,
all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_south_sudan.md](play_south_sudan.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md), the Hofstede layer, written from a
  collectivist, kin-first, present-and-tradition-bound profile (the group before
  the self, the family the strong house, time read off the river and the herd,
  respect to age and chief) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the rivers and the cattle peoples, the meeting of the
  wider world, the long road to nationhood, and the dawn of independence (2011) -
  each casting the elements it needs, and each staged soberly, taking no side and
  dwelling on no wound, with the wars entirely out of scope.
- **Personas:** John Garang (a founding national figure, remembered soberly for
  the vision of a shared country), Taban lo Liyong (the poet and critic who
  gathered the songs into books), Emmanuel Jal and Yaba Angelosi (artists who
  carried the country's music to the world), plus one archetype, the cattle-camp
  youth who takes his ox-name and sings its praises. Each is wired to the language
  engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** the cattle (the herd as wealth, name, and song), the river (the
  White Nile and the great Sudd wetland and the life set by the flood), the many
  peoples (the dozens of peoples held in one house in equal dignity).
- **Places:** Juba (the capital on the river, where the new flag was raised), the
  Sudd (the great wetland at the country's heart).
- **Processes:** the cattle camp (the great seasonal move with the herd), the
  wrestling (traditional wrestling as celebration that binds village to village).
- **Pieces:** the named ox (the favourite ox of the praise-songs, the cattle
  culture in one beast), the beads (the beadwork that carries a people's dignity
  and belonging in colour).

## Provenance

The Hofstede orientation behind the pitch and the public-domain cultural and

detail entirely out of scope; the staging and the architecture are original work.
