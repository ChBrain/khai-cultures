# Process: the wrestling

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

The young men of the camps and villages, who meet to wrestle when the harvest is in and the season turns to celebration. A village challenges a village, a camp a camp, and the whole community gathers, the women singing, the drums going, the elders watching, the wrestlers stepping into the ring with their people behind them. It begins when the work eases and the people come together to play.

## Direction

Toward celebration and the honour of the camp: the wrestling is not a quarrel but a feast, a way for the young to show their strength and for the village to come together in song and pride. The wrestler carries the name of his whole community into the ring, and a good bout is praised the way a fine ox is praised, the winner sung and the loser honoured for standing. It binds village to village in play rather than in grievance, and turns strength into festival.

## Lever

It is driven by the love of celebration and the pride of the camp, and by the gathering of the whole community to sing and drum and watch. It holds against the long labour of the cattle and the field, the feast that crowns the work; against it stand the press of the town and the road, which scatter the young and quiet the wrestling-grounds.

## Echo

The wrestling makes audible what binds [the many peoples](position_the_many_peoples.md): that strength is shown in celebration and honour shared in song, village meeting village in play. On the wrestling-ground [the cattle-camp youth](persona_the_cattle_camp_youth.md) stands for his whole camp, and a community comes together to sing the strong and honour the brave.
