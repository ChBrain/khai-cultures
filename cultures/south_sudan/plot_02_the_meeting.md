# Plot: the meeting of the wider world

## Taxonomy

[play_south_sudan](play_south_sudan.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

Across the long years the wider world reaches the rivers from the north and from beyond the seas, traders and travellers and then the makers of empires, who look at the country of cattle and water and begin to draw their lines across it.

## Action

The peoples of the rivers, who had known the wider world only through the long trade routes, are folded into borders drawn far away and not of their making. The south, with its swamp and its distance, is held far from the seats of power, the road north long and the road to any voice of its own longer still. Through these years the markets grow and a common talk takes hold, Juba Arabic spreading as the tongue that lets a Bari trade with a Nuer and a Zande greet a Shilluk across all their mother tongues. The many peoples keep their cattle, their praises, and their dignity through it all, and begin the long road toward speaking for themselves, a road this staging does not walk in detail and on which it takes no side.

## Stage

- [the many peoples](position_the_many_peoples.md): the gathering of peoples folded into borders not their own, holding their dignity through the long years.
- [the river](position_the_river.md): the White Nile and the Sudd, the swamp and the distance that long held the south apart.
- [the Sudd](place_the_sudd.md): the great wetland whose channels kept the south far from any distant seat of power.

## Tension

Here the old country of the rivers meets the wider world and its borders, and the south finds itself held far from the table where its future is decided. Soberly told and taking no side, the weight of it is the long distance between the cattle camps and any voice of their own; its quiet promise is that through all of it the many peoples kept their cattle and their praises and their dignity whole, and kept faith that their morning would one day come.
