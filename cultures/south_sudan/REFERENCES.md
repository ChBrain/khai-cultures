# South Sudan: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede orientation that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain cultural and historical
sources behind the plots, personas, places, pieces, and processes.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md) is written from a national-culture
orientation in the spirit of Geert Hofstede's six-dimensions model and names no
dimension and carries no number. South Sudan is not separately scored in the
published Hofstede data; the orientation used here is the broad collectivist,
kin-first, present-and-tradition-bound profile common to the cattle-keeping
societies of the region, recorded here so the orientation is on the record without
entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | South Sudanese orientation (qualitative) |
| ----------------------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | high (respect to age, elder, and chief)  |
| Individualism                 | low (the clan and the group before self) |
| Motivation toward Achievement | balanced, toward care of kin and herd    |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | read off the rhythm of the flood         |
| Long Term Orientation         | tradition-bound, patient                 |
| Indulgence                    | restrained, with celebration in season   |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the six-dimensions model of national
culture). South Sudan is not separately published in the Country Comparison data;
the orientation here is a qualitative reading in the spirit of the model and is
not a published score.

credited here and never claimed; the staging is original work. Independence and
the long road to it are staged soberly and take no political side; the wars, both
the long war that preceded independence and any later conflict, are entirely out
of scope, with no atrocity detail.
