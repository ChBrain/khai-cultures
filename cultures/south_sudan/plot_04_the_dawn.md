# Plot: the dawn of independence (2011)

## Taxonomy

[play_south_sudan](play_south_sudan.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

At the end of the long road the people of the south come to the vote they had waited for, and choose, in their great number, to raise a country of their own.

## Action

On the ninth of July, 2011, in Juba on the river, the newest flag on earth goes up, and South Sudan is born, the long-awaited dawn the elders had always sung would surely come. The many peoples, each its own and each held in equal dignity, gather as one young nation with its seat by the White Nile. There is dancing and there is song, the praise of the cattle and the joy of the morning carried on the new music of the young, and voices such as Emmanuel Jal and Yaba Angelosi sing the country's hope out to the world. The morning, so long in coming, has come, and with it the whole long task of making the young house a home worthy of the waiting.

## Stage

## Tension

This is the morning the whole country waited for, the dawn raised in joy and song by the river. Its glory is the long-awaited arrival, a people speaking at last for themselves; its sober weight, taking no side and dwelling on no wound, is that the morning is only the beginning, that the young house must still be built, and that the hope that raised the flag now faces the long, real work of making the country worthy of the waiting.
