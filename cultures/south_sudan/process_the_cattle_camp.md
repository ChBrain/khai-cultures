# Process: the cattle camp

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

The young men and women of the cattle-keeping peoples, who take the herds out on the great seasonal move. When the dry months come they drive the cattle down toward the river and the Sudd for water and grazing; when the floods rise they fall back to the high ground. All it takes is the turning of the season and the reading of the water, and the camp is on the move with the herd.

## Direction

Toward the life set by the water and the herd: the cattle camp is the year itself made into a journey, the people moving with the flood between the river and the high ground as their grandfathers did. It is not only herding but a whole way of being, the young living among the cattle, milking and watering and guarding them, taking the ox-name and singing the praises into the night, the camp a school of the cattle culture and a home on the move.

## Lever

It is driven by the deep faith that wealth and name and song all live in the herd, and by the rhythm of the flood that no one can argue with. It holds against drought and the thinning of the grazing; against it stand the pull of the town and the road, which draw the young off the cattle and leave the praises unsung.

## Echo

The cattle camp makes audible what drives [the cattle](position_the_cattle.md): that the herd is the centre of the world and the year is read off the water. In the camp [the cattle-camp youth](persona_the_cattle_camp_youth.md) takes his ox-name and sings its praises into the dark, carrying the oldest life of the rivers forward one season at a time.
