# Jordan: a khai culture

Jordan staged as a full khai play, ISO JO, language ar. A land of carved rock and
open desert, the crossroads of ancient caravans, staged as a theatre: its history
as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its hospitality and heritage
encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single
pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_jordan.md](play_jordan.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_ar.md](pitch_ar.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Jordan's
  profile (a quiet dignity and honor, strong ties of clan and family, deep
  hospitality, respect for elders, and a present, relational warmth) and naming no
  dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Nabataeans and Petra, the crossroads of civilizations,
  the desert and the Bedouin, and the founding of the modern state - each casting
  the elements it needs, and each treating the founding of the modern Hashemite
  state soberly as heritage.
- **Personas:** Aretas IV (the great Nabataean builder-king of Petra), Mustafa
  Wahbi al-Tal "Arar" (Jordan's foremost poet of the people and the desert), King
  Abdullah I (the sober founding figure of the modern state), and Roks bin Zaid
  al-Uzayzi (the scholar who documented Bedouin heritage), plus one archetype, the
  Host (the Bedouin code of hospitality alive in one man). Each is wired to the
  language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** hospitality (the unwritten code of the guest, the coffee, the
  shared platter), the carved rock and the desert (Petra and the great sandstone of
  Wadi Rum), the crossroads of caravans (the land as the meeting place of routes
  and civilizations).
- **Places:** Petra (the rose-red Nabataean city carved into the rock) and Wadi
  Rum (the towering sandstone desert of the Bedouin).
- **Processes:** the coffee ritual (the bitter coffee poured as a covenant of
  welcome), making mansaf (the national dish shared from one platter).
- **Pieces:** the keffiyeh (the red-and-white headdress of Jordanian dignity), the
  dallah (the brass coffee pot, the emblem of hospitality).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the founding of the modern state is treated soberly as heritage, with
regional conflicts and contemporary politics kept out of scope; the staging and
the architecture are original work.
