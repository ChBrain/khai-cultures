# Portugal: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_pt.md](pitch_pt.md) is written from Portugal's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no
number. The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the
record without entering the prose.

The two defining personas, [Amália](persona_amalia.md) and [Tiago](persona_tiago.md),
are archetypes, not historical individuals: composites that carry the tension
between the older generation (fado, saudade, the neighbourhood, the saints'
festivals, quiet endurance) and the younger one (the emigrant tradition and the
pull between staying and leaving, the modern Atlantic-facing Portugal). "Amália" is
used here as a generic given name for the fado everywoman, not as the historical
singer Amália Rodrigues.
