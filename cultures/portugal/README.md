# Portugal: a khai culture

Portugal staged as a full khai play, ISO PT, language pt. A small, Atlantic-facing
country as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the
roles its language and feeling encode, its places, its practices, and its defining
objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_portugal.md](play_portugal.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_pt.md](pitch_pt.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Portugal's profile (saudade, gentle ways, a deep need for certainty, strong
  attachment to family and community) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Age of Discovery (15th-16th c.), Camoes and Os Lusiadas
  (1572), the Lisbon earthquake of 1755, and the Carnation Revolution of 1974 -
  each casting the elements it needs.
- **Personas:** Luis de Camoes and Vasco da Gama as the historic cast, plus two
  defining archetypes, Amalia (older, fado and saudade, the neighbourhood) and
  Tiago (younger, the emigrant pull, the Atlantic-facing future). Each is wired to
  the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** saudade (the bittersweet longing), os brandos costumes (the gentle,
  mild manner), a vocacao atlantica (the seafaring vocation and the diaspora).
- **Places:** Lisboa (the capital on the Tagus), Sagres (Henry the Navigator's
  promontory, the end of the land and the beginning of the sea).
- **Processes:** o fado, a mesa (the shared table).
- **Pieces:** o azulejo (the blue tiles), o pastel de nata (the custard tart of Belem).
