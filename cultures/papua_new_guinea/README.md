# Papua New Guinea: a khai culture

Papua New Guinea staged as a full khai play, ISO PG, prose in Tok Pisin (language
tpi). The most linguistically diverse nation on earth, some 800 tok ples held
together by Tok Pisin and the wantok kinship, set out as a theatre: its history as
the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its languages encode, its places,
its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch. The body of
every file is written in Tok Pisin, the lingua franca of the country; only the
English chapter headings are kept in English.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_papua_new_guinea.md](play_papua_new_guinea.md), the culture
  itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_tpi.md](pitch_tpi.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Papua
  New Guinea's profile (a strongly communal, kastom-rooted, wantok-bound people,
  proud of place, given to oratory and to consensus) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the first gardeners (Kuk and the ancient Melanesian and
  Papuan settlement, and the Sepik art), contact and colony (the late contact, the
  1930s Highlands, German, British, and Australian administration, and WWII at
  Kokoda), independence and the Melanesian Way (1975, Somare, Narokobi, and Guise),
  and a thousand tongues today (the Tok Pisin nation, the Highlands, Bougainville,
  and resources) - each casting the elements it needs.
- **Personas:** Sir Michael Somare (the Grand Chief and founding Prime Minister),
  Bernard Narokobi (the philosopher of the Melanesian Way and architect of the
  constitution), and Sir John Guise (the first Governor-General) as the historic
  cast, plus two defining archetypes, the big-man (the Highlands leader by feast
  and oratory) and the Sepik carver (the master carver of the haus tambaran). Each
  is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** a thousand tongues, one country (the nation of near a thousand tok
  ples made one by Tok Pisin), the wantok and kastom (the kinship of language and
  the custom of the ancestors), the Melanesian Way (Narokobi's path of self-rule
  rooted in Melanesian custom).
- **Places:** the Highlands (the densely peopled valleys and the sing-sing), the
  Sepik (the great river of art and the haus tambaran).
- **Processes:** the sing-sing (the great gathering of tribes in paint and
  feathers) and the mumu (the earth-oven feast of giving and gathering).
- **Pieces:** the bilum (the looped string bag of the women's hand) and the kundu
  drum (the hourglass hand drum that keeps the time of the sing-sing).

## Provenance

The Hofstede framing behind the pitch and the public-record historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources on the public record are credited, never
claimed; contact, the colonial era, WWII, and Bougainville are staged soberly from
within Papua New Guinea's own self-understanding and with no atrocity detail; the
staging and the architecture are original work.
