# Taiwan: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind the
plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_zh.md](pitch_zh.md) is written from Taiwan's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record without
entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Taiwan score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ---------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 58                     |
| Individualism                 | 17                     |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 45                     |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 69                     |
| Long Term Orientation         | 93                     |
| Indulgence                    | 49                     |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; Taiwan's Long Term Orientation is
among the highest in the published data.

## Historical sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn
from the public historical record. They are credited here and never claimed; the
staging is original work. The culture is staged strictly from within Taiwan's own
self-understanding and takes no side on questions of cross-strait political status
or sovereignty; the Japanese era and the postwar decades are staged soberly, with
dignity and no atrocity detail.

everyday faith and human feeling, the temple incense lit before the stall and the
extra ladleful pressed on a customer.
