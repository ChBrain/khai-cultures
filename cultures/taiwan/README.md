# Taiwan: a khai culture

Taiwan staged as a full khai play, ISO TW, language zh. A layered island of many
peoples, faiths, and tongues, made new by its own hands, rendered as a theatre:
its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its languages and
faith encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a
single pitch. The prose is composed natively in Traditional Chinese; the headings,
this README, and the references are in English.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house that
holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its Estate; a
culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_taiwan.md](play_taiwan.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_zh.md](pitch_zh.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Taiwan's
  profile (a warm, diligent, relationship-minded society that prizes harmony and
  human feeling, works hard and practically, holds long-standing respect for elders
  and order alongside an open civic life) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the first peoples and the settlers, the Japanese era
  (1895-1945), the postwar decades, and the peaceful democratization - each casting
  the elements it needs, and each staged soberly and with dignity.
- **Personas:** Uyongu Yatauyungana (the Tsou educator and musician, a culture-
  bearer of the indigenous first peoples), Lai Ho (the doctor and writer called the
  father of modern Taiwanese literature), Yin Haiguang (the scholar who kept reason
  and freedom alive through hard decades), and Morris Chang (who put the island's
  craft at the heart of the world's chips), plus one archetype, the night-market
  vendor. Each is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** the layered island (the many peoples layered one upon another),
  folk faith (Mazu, the temple, the pilgrimage, the daily faith of the household),
  and civil society (the noisy, resilient web of an open society).
- **Places:** Tainan (the old city, the oldest temples, the island's earliest
  roots) and Taipei (the modern capital, the window on the world).
- **Processes:** the night market (the island's most everyday joy) and the Mazu
  pilgrimage (folk faith at its most communal).
- **Pieces:** the chip (the island's craft at the heart of the world's machines)
  and bubble tea (the island's tea culture remade into a sweet that conquered the
  world).

## Sensitivity

Taiwan is staged strictly from within its own self-understanding and on the public
record. This culture takes no side whatsoever on questions of cross-strait
political status or sovereignty, and asserts or denies no political claim; it
focuses on the lived island culture and society. The Japanese era and the postwar
decades are acknowledged soberly and with dignity, with no atrocity detail.
