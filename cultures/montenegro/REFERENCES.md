# Montenegro: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_cnr.md](pitch_cnr.md) is written from Montenegro's profile on
Geert Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no
number. The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the
record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Montenegro score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | -------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 88                         |
| Individualism                 | 24                         |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 48                         |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 90                         |
| Long Term Orientation         | 55                         |
| Indulgence                    | 30                         |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). The figures are approximate and the
data for Montenegro is limited: robust country-level scores are lacking, and these
values are read from the regional / former-Yugoslav profiles as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group. They are indicative, not exact.

The two defining personas, [Milena](persona_milena.md) and [Vuk](persona_vuk.md),
are archetypes, not historical individuals: composites that carry the tension
between the older generation (the highland stone house, the gusle, the Orthodox
faith, the warrior sons) and the younger one (the 2006 generation of the coast,
tourism, emigration, and the question whether the tongue and the nation are
Montenegrin or Serb).
