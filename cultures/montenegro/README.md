# Montenegro: a khai culture

Montenegro staged as a full khai play, ISO ME, language cnr. A clan-warrior
nation of the Black Mountain, as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its
people as the cast, the roles its language and faith encode, its places, its
practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_montenegro.md](play_montenegro.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_cnr.md](pitch_cnr.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Montenegro's profile (proud and clan-bound, hierarchical, honour- and
  heroism-driven, anxious for certainty and defiant, with the leisurely coastal
  counterpoint to the stern highland) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** centuries of resistance ("never under the Turks"), Njegoš
  (1830-1851), the kingdom and 1918, and independence (2006) - each casting the
  elements it needs.
- **Personas:** Njegoš, Marko Miljanov, and King Nikola as the historic cast,
  plus two defining archetypes, Milena (older, the highland stone house, the
  gusle, the Orthodox faith) and Vuk (younger, the 2006 generation of the coast,
  emigration, and the identity question). Each is wired to the language engine by
  the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** junaštvo i čojstvo (heroism tempered by humanity, the
  mountain-warrior code), brđanski kamen (the Black Mountain and its highlands
  never fully conquered, Lovćen the sacred peak, mountain against the Adriatic
  coast), crnogorstvo (the modern identity question, Montenegrin against Serb, the
  language and the church).
- **Places:** Lovćen (the sacred black mountain with Njegoš's mausoleum at the
  summit), Cetinje (the old royal capital in the rock, the prince-bishops' seat).
- **Processes:** oro (the Montenegrin eagle dance, men leaping in a ring), guslanje
  (epic singing to the one-string gusle that carried the heroic ethos).
- **Pieces:** kapa crnogorska (the round cap whose embroidery reads as the whole
  national story), Gorski vijenac (Njegoš's "Mountain Wreath," the supreme national
  text).
