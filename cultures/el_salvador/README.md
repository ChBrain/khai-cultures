# El Salvador: a khai culture

El Salvador staged as a full khai play, ISO SV, language es. The smallest and
most densely peopled country of the isthmus, volcanic, wounded by its war and
bound to its distant children, as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its
people as the cast, the roles its languages and faith encode, its places, its
practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_el_salvador.md](play_el_salvador.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_es.md](pitch_es.md), the Hofstede layer, written from El
  Salvador's profile (warm, hardworking and resilient, plain-spoken and
  guanaco, deeply familial and devout) and naming no dimension; its undertow the
  resilience over an unhealed wound, the deep inequality, and the empty chair at
  every table.
- **Plots (history):** Cuzcatlan and indigo, coffee and the fourteen families,
  the war and the prophet, and Tom Thumb and his children - each casting the
  elements it needs, and each staging the wound of the country soberly and on the
  public record.
- **Personas:** Anastasio Aquino, Salarrue, Monsignor Oscar Romero, and Roque
  Dalton as the historic cast, plus one defining archetype, el hermano lejano (the
  migrant abroad on whom the nation depends). Each is wired to the language engine
  by the language-crossing it holds; for the Pipil-rooted figures the mother
  tongue is Nahuat with Spanish carried.
- **Positions:** lo pipil (the Pipil-Cuzcatlan root and the volcanic
  pulgarcito), la tierra y la herida (the coffee oligarchy, the inequality, the
  war and Romero's witness), el hermano lejano (the migrant nation, a quarter of
  its people abroad).
- **Places:** San Salvador (the capital under the volcano, the cathedral where
  Romero rests), la cordillera (the volcanic coffee highlands and the Ruta de las
  Flores).
- **Processes:** la pupusa (the shared griddle of the stuffed corn cake), las
  Fiestas Agostinas (the patron feast of the Divine Savior of the World).
- **Pieces:** el añil (the colonial blue dye, the old blue gold), la flor de
  izote (the national flower, also eaten).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the 1932 events, the civil war, and Monsignor Romero's martyrdom are
named soberly on the public record, with no atrocity detail and no political side,
and Romero is treated reverently as the canonized saint and voice of the
voiceless; the staging and the architecture are original work.
