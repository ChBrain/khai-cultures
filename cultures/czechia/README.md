# Czechia: a khai culture

Czechia staged as a full khai play, ISO CZ, language cs. A small, skeptical,
godless nation as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast,
the roles its language and irony encode, its places, its practices, and its
defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_czechia.md](play_czechia.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_cs.md](pitch_cs.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Czechia's
  profile (irony and skepticism, distrust of grand words and authority, a strong
  need for certainty, withdrawal into private life) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** Jan Hus (1415), White Mountain (1620), Munich (1938), and the
  Velvet Revolution (1989) - each casting the elements it needs.
- **Personas:** Jan Hus, Jaroslav Hašek, and Václav Havel as the historic cast, plus
  two defining archetypes, Jiřina (older, the quiet ironist who outlasted two
  totalitarian regimes) and Tomáš (younger, the secular EU programmer whose Czech
  thins toward English). Each is wired to the language engine by the
  language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** český jazyk (the tongue revived from near-death after White
  Mountain), švejkování (the Švejk way of ironic, passive resistance), bezvěrectví
  (the godlessness and distrust of all authority that make Czechia one of the
  most atheist nations).
- **Places:** Praha (the hundred-spired capital), pohraničí (the borderlands, the
  Sudetenland where the German question turned).
- **Processes:** hospoda (the pub, the real public sphere), chataření (the weekend
  cottage, withdrawal into private life).
- **Pieces:** loutka (the marionette that kept the Czech tongue alive on the puppet
  stage), knedlík (the dumpling, the homely Sunday staple).
