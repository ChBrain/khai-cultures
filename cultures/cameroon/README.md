# Cameroon: a khai culture

Cameroon staged as a full khai play, ISO CM, language fr. A nation that holds the
whole continent in one country, officially bilingual in French and English, as a
theatre: its history as the plot line, its peoples as the cast, the roles its
languages and chiefdoms encode, its places, its practices, and its defining
objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_cameroon.md](play_cameroon.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_fr.md](pitch_fr.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Cameroon's profile (a warm and collective people, strong family and village
  ties, respect for rank and elders, a deep talent for getting by, and pride in a
  diversity held as one) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** Foumban and the sultanate, the encounter with outsiders,
  independence and reunification, and the Indomitable Lions of 1990 - each casting
  the elements it needs, and each staged soberly and from within.
- **Personas:** Manu Dibango, Sultan Ibrahim Njoya, Roger Milla, and Mongo Beti as
  the historic cast, plus one defining archetype, the buyam-sellam (the market
  woman who feeds the nation from below). Each is wired to the language engine by
  the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** Africa in miniature (the whole continent and all its peoples held
  as one), bilingualism (French and English as two official tongues at equal
  dignity, with over two hundred languages beneath), the chiefdom (the old
  authority of the palaces, the fon, the sultan, the lamido).
- **Places:** Foumban (the Bamum royal capital, the palace and the birthplace of
  the Bamum script), Mount Cameroon (the living volcano on the coast, the whole
  country in one mountain).
- **Processes:** ndole (the national dish, the shared pot and the honoured guest),
  makossa (the music of Douala carried to the world).
- **Pieces:** the Indomitable Lions jersey (the most unifying emblem of the
  nation), the Bamum script (the proof of a homegrown genius).

The francophone and the anglophone heritages are honoured throughout at equal
dignity, as Cameroon's own self-understanding requires.

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the difficult passages of the country's history are treated soberly on
the public record, taking no political side and dwelling on no detail; the staging
and the architecture are original work.
