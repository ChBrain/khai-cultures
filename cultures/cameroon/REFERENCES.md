# Cameroon: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_fr.md](pitch_fr.md) is written from Cameroon's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record without
entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Cameroon score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------ |
| Power Distance                | 70                       |
| Individualism                 | 20                       |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 50                       |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 55                       |
| Long Term Orientation         | 24                       |
| Indulgence                    | 40                       |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the six-dimensions model of national
culture). Cameroon is not always published as a single country score; the values
above are approximate, drawn from West and Central African regional estimates in
the Hofstede tradition, and are used only to tune the register of the pitch, never
quoted in the prose.

## Historical sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn
from the public historical and cultural record. They are credited here and never
claimed; the staging is original work. The difficult passages of the country's
past - the era of outside rule and the drawing of borders - are staged soberly on
the public record, taking no political side and dwelling on no detail. Recent
politics is entirely out of scope.

The defining persona [the buyam-sellam](persona_la_buyam_sellam.md) is an
archetype, not a historical individual: a composite that carries the market woman
who buys at the field and sells in town, feeding the nation from below and binding
its many peoples and languages across her stall.
