# Bhutan: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_dzo.md](pitch_dzo.md) is written from Bhutan's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
Bhutan is not among the countries with a full published score set; the values
below are read from the regional South Asian and Himalayan Buddhist pattern and
are recorded here, approximate and indicative, so the basis is on the record
without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Bhutan score (approx., indicative) |
| ----------------------------- | ---------------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 80                                 |
| Individualism                 | 25                                 |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 40                                 |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 40                                 |
| Long Term Orientation         | 55                                 |
| Indulgence                    | 40                                 |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the six-dimensions model of national
culture). As Bhutan lacks a full published country score, the figures are
indicative readings from the South Asian regional pattern, published by Hofstede
Insights / The Culture Factor Group; they are approximate and used only to set the
tone, never quoted in the prose.

## Historical sources

from the public historical record. They are credited here and never claimed; the
staging is original work. The monarchy and the Buddhist faith are treated
respectfully as the enduring heart of the nation, and the move to constitutional
democracy is staged soberly and with dignity, taking no political side.
