# Bulgaria: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_bg.md](pitch_bg.md) is written from Bulgaria's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no
number. The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the
record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Bulgaria score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------ |
| Power Distance                | 70                       |
| Individualism                 | 30                       |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 40                       |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 85                       |
| Long Term Orientation         | 69                       |
| Indulgence                    | 16                       |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published
by Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; Bulgaria's high Uncertainty
Avoidance and very low Indulgence are notable in the published data.

The two defining personas, [Nevena](persona_nevena.md) and
[Georgi](persona_georgi.md), are archetypes, not historical individuals:
composites that carry the tension between the older generation (Orthodox faith,
the village, the rose harvest, gratitude to "Grandfather Ivan," the mother tongue)
and the younger one (the post-2007 emigration and the pull of global English on a
small language amid the world's fastest depopulation).
