# Pitch: the Canadian tone

## Taxonomy

The key in which the staging of [Canada](play_canada.md) is played. It changes nothing of what happens, not Confederation, not the two solitudes, not peacekeeping, not the reckoning with the residential schools. It sets only how it sounds, the register in which all of it is given.

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Tenor

Played individualist but communitarian, with a quiet, consensual modesty: a free people who nonetheless build the common things together, who hold medicare and the public order in common, who are tolerant of difference and at ease with the cold and the vastness. It is the tone of a country that apologizes, that prefers the reasonable accommodation to the grand gesture, that would rather be peaceable and decent than great, and that knows itself most clearly by being the quieter, gentler version of the neighbour to the south.

## Undertow

Under the politeness runs the long shadow of the elephant next door: a self defined too much by what it is not, an anxiety about whether the country has a story of its own or only the not-American one. Under the tolerance runs the unfinished business: two peoples who share a state but not a tongue and a unity that could still come apart, a mosaic that can drift into solitudes that never touch, and a founding wrong, the residential schools, that the politeness long preferred not to name.

## Nerve

It strikes the chord of belonging-across-difference and of decency held in common: the warmth of the rink and the double-double shared by everyone regardless of tongue, the pride of the peacekeeper and the welcomer of the world, the quiet conviction that a country can be built by joining rather than conquering. It plays on the feeling that one belongs by being reasonable, tolerant, and kind, that the common good is worth the common cost, and that one need not be loud or grand to be good.

## Echo

What remains is the image of a country that chose to join rather than break, to keep rather than melt, and to be decent rather than great: bilingual and many-origined, peaceable and modest, polite to a fault, at ease with diversity and the cold, forever holding two peoples and many peoples together beside a louder neighbour, and learning, late but in earnest, to face the harm at its own foundation and make it right.
