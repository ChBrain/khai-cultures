# Process: hockey

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

A frozen pond, a backyard rink, or an arena on a winter night, and anyone who laces up skates, pulls down the toque, and drops a puck. A pair of nets, a stick, and a sheet of ice are enough, and from the smallest town rink, where the parents holler give 'er from the boards, to Hockey Night in Canada on the television, the country gathers around the game it made its own.

## Direction

Toward a shared passion that crosses every line in the country: hockey is the one thing English and French, newcomer and old stock, East and West all hold in common, the game everyone follows whatever their tongue. It is more than sport; it is the winter ritual in which a vast and divided country feels, for a few periods, like one people.

## Lever

It is driven by the long northern winter that made ice the country's natural element and by the pride of a nation that gave the world its game. It is held by the rinks, the leagues, and Hockey Night; against it run the cost of the game and the pull of warmer, newer pastimes, but nothing has displaced it as the common passion. Sorry, the country says when it checks you into the boards, and means that too.

## Echo

Hockey makes audible what holds [Canada](position_pogg.md) together across its solitudes: that a country of two languages and many origins still shares one winter game, and feels itself one people on the ice as nowhere else. It is the warmth at the centre of the cold that [Therese](persona_therese.md) and her grandchildren, French and English alike, can cheer in the same breath.
