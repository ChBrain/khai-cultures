# Persona: John A. Macdonald

## Taxonomy

[peace, order, and good government](position_pogg.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The first prime minister of Canada, the chief architect of Confederation in 1867 and of the transcontinental railway that bound the dominion from sea to sea, a shrewd, charming, hard-drinking Scots-born lawyer who stitched a country together by deal and compromise under the creed of "peace, order, and good government." A Scottish immigrant to British North America, he carried English as his whole tongue: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md), and [writes](process_writing_mother_tongue.md) in the English he was raised in, and made it the working language of the dominion he built. The French of the Canadiens whose votes he needed and whose pact he had to keep he [carried as a tool of statecraft](process_speaking_carried.md) more than spoke, and what crossed his desk in it he [followed on the page](process_reading_followed.md) only as far as the deal required. He built a bilingual dominion of two peoples and governed it almost entirely in his own tongue, and there opens the gap at the limit of the tongue.

## Action

In [Confederation (1867)](plot_01_confederation_1867.md) he federates the colonies into a dominion of [peace, order, and good government](position_pogg.md) and drives the railway that makes it one country. By joining rather than conquering, he gives the new nation its founding form and its quiet, communitarian creed.

## Shadow

His government founded the residential school system, the network of church-run schools that took Indigenous children from their families and set out to erase their languages and cultures, and his Indian Act made wards of the first peoples. The dominion he built rests on this dark foundation: the same hand that joined a country to keep it together broke apart the families of those who were there first.

## Tell

He builds by the deal and the compromise rather than the grand stroke, holds two peoples in one frame by giving each enough to stay, and governs a bilingual dominion he himself can speak only half of.
