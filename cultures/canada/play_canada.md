# Play: Canada

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the house that holds this staging and answers for it.

## Name

Canada, the second-largest country on earth by area, stretched across the top of North America from the Atlantic to the Pacific to the Arctic, a confederation of provinces and territories with two official languages, English and French, beside the United States, the elephant next door. ISO CA, languages en and fr.

## Arc

A country built by joining rather than breaking: the British North American colonies federated in 1867 into a dominion of "peace, order, and good government," knit together by a transcontinental railway across a land the first peoples had lived on for millennia. From the start it carried two founding European peoples, English and French, the two solitudes who share a country yet mostly cannot speak each other's tongue. It chose to be a mosaic and not a melting pot, letting those who came keep the colour and the labour and the faith they brought, and it found a vocation in the world as a middle power, the inventor of peacekeeping, the quieter and more communitarian neighbour defined against the United States. Under all of it runs a wound that the rest was built upon: the residential schools and the Indian Act that set out to erase the languages and cultures of First Nations, Metis, and Inuit children, and the long, unfinished reckoning that followed. The arc is a vast, cold, polite federation forever holding itself together across two languages and many origins, from the rink and the Timmies lineup to the cottage on the lake and the immensity of the North, and learning at last, with a quiet sorry it long withheld, to face the harm at its own foundation.

## Triggers

**[Plot 1: Confederation (1867)](plot_01_confederation_1867.md)**

In 1867 the British North America Act federates the colonies into a dominion of [peace, order, and good government](position_pogg.md), with [John A. Macdonald](persona_macdonald.md) as its first prime minister and a railway to bind it - a founding that comes at a hard cost to the [first peoples](plot_04_reconciliation.md) and the West.

**[Plot 2: les deux solitudes](plot_02_les_deux_solitudes.md)**

The country carries [two peoples](position_les_deux_peuples.md) who mostly cannot speak each other's tongue; the Quiet Revolution wakes Quebec, [Rene Levesque](persona_levesque.md) founds a sovereignty movement, and two referendums leave the unity question forever open.

**[Plot 3: the middle power](plot_03_the_middle_power.md)**

[Lester Pearson](persona_pearson.md) invents United Nations peacekeeping and wins the Nobel Peace Prize, gives Canada the maple-leaf flag and medicare and the groundwork of official multiculturalism: the not-American [mosaic](position_the_mosaic.md) finds its place in the world as the gentler neighbour, defence of the common good held in honour above the grand gesture.

**[Plot 4: reconciliation](plot_04_reconciliation.md)**

The residential schools, the Indian Act, and the harm done to First Nations, Metis, and Inuit are named on the public record; the Truth and Reconciliation Commission begins the reckoning that the [dominion](position_pogg.md) owes the first peoples it was built upon.

## Stakes

Whether a vast, cold federation of [two founding peoples](position_les_deux_peuples.md) who mostly cannot speak each other's tongue, that chose the [mosaic](position_the_mosaic.md) over the melting pot and made itself the quieter, communitarian, [not-American](position_pogg.md) North, can hold itself together across its languages and origins, keep its distinct temper beside the elephant next door, and complete the [reconciliation](plot_04_reconciliation.md) it owes the first peoples for the wrong at its foundation.
