# Plot: Confederation (1867)

## Taxonomy

[play_canada](play_canada.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

In the 1860s the scattered British colonies of North America face a deadlocked legislature at home and a powerful, expanding republic to the south. Their leaders, [John A. Macdonald](persona_macdonald.md) chief among them, meet at Charlottetown and Quebec and resolve to join the colonies into one country rather than be picked apart or absorbed.

## Action

On 1 July 1867 the British North America Act federates the colonies into the Dominion of Canada under the creed of "peace, order, and good government," with Macdonald as its first prime minister. A transcontinental railway is driven thousands of kilometres across the continent to bind the new country from sea to sea, and the West is opened to settlement. The dominion is built by joining rather than conquering, but the binding comes at a hard cost: the railway and the settlement push across lands the first peoples had lived on for millennia, and the new state begins to make wards of them.

## Stage

- [peace, order, and good government](position_pogg.md): the founding creed of the joined dominion.
- [John A. Macdonald](persona_macdonald.md): the architect who stitches the colonies into one country.
- [Ottawa](place_ottawa.md): the compromise capital chosen on the English-French border, the seat of the new dominion.

## Tension

A country is made by joining rather than breaking, peaceably and by compromise, and given the quiet, communitarian creed it keeps to this day. Yet the same binding is laid across the lands and lives of the first peoples, and the dominion of peace and order is founded on a wrong it will take a century and a half to begin to face.
