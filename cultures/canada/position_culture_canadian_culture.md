# Position: Canadian culture

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

Membership in a country built by joining rather than by breaking, a confederation that carries three inheritances at once rather than one. It carries [les deux peuples](position_les_deux_peuples.md), the English and the French, the two founding solitudes who share a state without sharing a tongue; it carries [the mosaic](position_the_mosaic.md), the policy and the daily fact of letting every later arrival keep what it brought rather than melting it down; and it carries [peace, order, and good government](position_pogg.md), the quiet, communitarian creed set against the louder individualism of the neighbour to the south. It speaks in [Canadian English](position_language_en_ca.md) and [le français québécois](position_language_fr_ca.md) at once, and it holds, without ever having chosen it, the unfinished reckoning with the residential schools and the Indian Act, the wound the whole federation was built upon.

## Orders

That the country be held together by reasonable accommodation and negotiation rather than by revolution or decree, that a newcomer be let keep the mother tongue and the home custom rather than shed them, that the French fact endure on an English continent and be met as a partner rather than a relic, and that the common good and the shared institution, the public broadcaster, medicare, the peacekeeping mission, be built together before the loud assertion of the self.

## Loses

Ground when the two solitudes drift far enough that they no longer touch at all, each carrying its own grievance in its own tongue; ground when the mosaic's separate pieces sit side by side without meeting, tolerance thinning into mere indifference; and ground, most gravely, wherever politeness is used to leave the founding wrong against First Nations, Metis, and Inuit unspoken rather than reckoned with, a sorry withheld standing in for the harder work of repair.

## Drives

Drives the joining of the colonies at [Confederation in 1867](plot_01_confederation_1867.md) into a dominion of peace, order, and good government, and drives, a century later, the vocation of [the middle power](plot_03_the_middle_power.md) that gives the country its flag, its medicare, and its official multiculturalism. It shapes the one raised in it toward a communitarian modesty, a readiness to hold two languages and a hundred origins in one frame without melting them into a single shape, and it drives Canada, at last, toward [reconciliation](plot_04_reconciliation.md): to name plainly the harm at its own foundation rather than let good manners stand in for justice.
