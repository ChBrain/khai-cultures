# Place: Ottawa

## Owner

## Shown

The capital, set on the river that marks the line between English Ontario and French Quebec, chosen as a compromise precisely because it sits on the border of the two solitudes. The Gothic towers of Parliament Hill rise above the Ottawa River; it is a modest, snowbound, governmental city of toques and frozen canals, the seat of "peace, order, and good government" rather than a grand metropolis.

## Offers

The place where the country governs itself and shows itself: a capital chosen not for its size or splendour but for sitting on the seam between the two founding peoples, the working seat of a federation built by compromise.

## Withheld

The grandeur of an imperial capital. Ottawa was a lumber town raised to capital by compromise, and it keeps the country's modesty: it withholds the spectacle of a great seat of power, and stands instead for the quiet, reasonable temper of the dominion it serves.
