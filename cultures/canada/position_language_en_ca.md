# Position: Canadian English

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The authority of English as it is written and spoken across Canada, the working tongue of the federation and the mother tongue of most of its people, the language of Parliament, the school, the newspaper, and the marketplace from the Atlantic to the Pacific. It holds the standard of Canadian English, a tongue of its own set between the British it was schooled in and the American it lives beside, with its own spellings (colour, centre, cheque) and its own words (toque, chesterfield, riding), the settled written norm against which correct Canadian usage is measured.

## Orders

What counts as good English on the page in Canada: the spelling that keeps the British "-our" and "-re" while taking the American "-ize", the register of the civil service, the national broadcaster, and the press. It orders what goes into the official record in English, sets the public tone of the anglophone country, and marks the line between the loose speech of the rink and the kitchen and the measured written form the country expects of itself.

## Loses

Ground to the pull of American English next door, whose films, screens, and spellings press on it daily, so that its distinctness is always a thing defended rather than assumed. And it is never the whole country's tongue: beside it stands French, the other official language, equal in law, so that English rules the record over most of Canada but not over Quebec, and it is never imposed on the French fact it was bound to share the dominion with.

## Drives

It drives most of Canada to live and govern in English while holding the door open to the other founding tongue. It lives in [John A. Macdonald](persona_macdonald.md), who built a bilingual dominion and ran it almost wholly in his own English; in [Lester Pearson](persona_pearson.md), who made Canada's case to the world in it; in [Jun](persona_jun.md), the child of the mosaic for whom English is the common tongue he keeps beside the heritage one; and it is the tongue [Therese](persona_therese.md) meets only at the edge, the English of the continent she catches by ear from her whole French life. So the common tongue of the anglophone country is at once the language of the record and the bridge the two solitudes are meant to cross.
