# Persona: Lester Pearson

## Taxonomy

[peace, order, and good government](position_pogg.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

Prime minister and diplomat, the man who invented United Nations peacekeeping to defuse the Suez crisis of 1956 and won the Nobel Peace Prize for it, who gave Canada the maple-leaf flag in 1965, medicare, and the groundwork of official multiculturalism, the architect of the country's vocation as a middle power. Mild, bow-tied, a former history professor and ambassador, he was an Anglo Canadian who built the bridge to the other solitude, and won the country its highest honour abroad. [English](position_language_en_ca.md) was his whole tongue: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md), and [writes](process_writing_mother_tongue.md) in the English he was raised in, and made the case for Canada to the world in it. The French of the country's other founding people he [carried as a tool](process_speaking_carried.md) of diplomacy and [followed on the page](process_reading_followed.md) as a statesman who knew the unity question turned on it, crossing to the other solitude as a diplomat more than as a neighbour. He gave Canada its flag and its peacekeeping vocation and built the bridge between two peoples while crossing it himself only as a diplomat, and there opens the gap at the limit of the tongue. He belongs to [Canadian culture](position_culture_canadian_culture.md), for the middle power's modest, communitarian vocation he gave the country is that culture's creed carried onto the world stage.

## Action

In [the middle power](plot_03_the_middle_power.md) he invents UN peacekeeping, wins the Nobel Peace Prize, and gives Canada a flag, medicare, and a vocation in the world. He makes the not-American [Canada](position_pogg.md) a force for peace and the welcoming [mosaic](position_the_mosaic.md) its way of being many in one.

## Shadow

The middle power's vocation can become a comfortable self-regard, a country sure it is nicer than the neighbour and the world; and the bridge-builder who reaches the other solitude as a diplomat can mistake official bilingualism for the lived sharing of a tongue that the two peoples still mostly do not have.

## Tell

He reaches for the reasonable accommodation and the quiet diplomatic stroke rather than the grand gesture, and he gives the country the symbols, the flag and the blue beret, by which it learns to be peaceably itself, the gentler neighbour holding the common good in honour.
