# Plot: reconciliation

## Taxonomy

[play_canada](play_canada.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

Beneath the dominion lies the wrong it was built upon. For more than a century, beginning in the years of Confederation, the state and the churches ran the residential school system: Indigenous children were taken from their families and forbidden their languages, and the Indian Act made wards of First Nations, Metis, and Inuit and set out to erase their ways. The harm ran deep, across generations, and for most of that century the country preferred not to name it.

## Action

The reckoning comes late. Survivors and their communities bring the truth onto the public record, and from 2008 to 2015 the Truth and Reconciliation Commission hears them, documents the residential schools and their lasting harm, and issues its Calls to Action. The country says its formal sorry, the apology the politeness long withheld, and begins the long, unfinished work of reconciliation: the revival of the Indigenous languages the schools set out to kill, and the slow attempt to make right what was done to the first peoples of the land.

## Stage

- [peace, order, and good government](position_pogg.md): the dominion and the state that owe amends for the wrong at the foundation.
- [Ottawa](place_ottawa.md): the seat of the government that made the apology and must complete the reckoning.

## Tension

A country built on the joining of peoples faces at last the breaking of families at its own foundation, and tries, soberly and on the public record, to make it right. Yet the harm done over a century cannot be undone by an apology, the first languages are being revived only after they were nearly lost, and the reconciliation the dominion owes remains the country's unfinished and most necessary work.
