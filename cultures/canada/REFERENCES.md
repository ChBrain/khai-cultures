# Canada: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md) is written from Canada's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record without
entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Canada score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ---------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 39                     |
| Individualism                 | 80                     |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 52                     |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 48                     |
| Long Term Orientation         | 36                     |
| Indulgence                    | 68                     |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; Canada reads as strongly
individualist yet low in power distance, moderate on the rest, and notably
indulgent and short-term in orientation.

The reckoning with the residential schools, the Indian Act, and the harm done to
First Nations, Metis, and Inuit is staged soberly and respectfully on the public
record. The Indigenous gift of the canoe and the suppression and revival of the
Indigenous languages are credited, never claimed.

The two defining personas, [Therese](persona_therese.md) and [Jun](persona_jun.md),
are archetypes, not historical individuals: composites that carry the tension
between the French solitude held whole from within (an older Quebecoise of the
Quiet Revolution generation) and the mosaic's keeping of the heritage tongue (a
younger first- or second-generation immigrant) that the melting pot would dissolve.
