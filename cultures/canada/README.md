# Canada: a khai culture

Canada staged as a full khai play, ISO CA, languages en and fr. A bilingual
federation of two founding peoples and a welcoming mosaic, the not-American North,
as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its
two languages and its origins encode, its places, its practices, and its defining
objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_canada.md](play_canada.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Canada's
  profile (individualist but communitarian and consensual, tolerant and modest,
  polite and peaceable, less driven and less grand than the neighbour, at ease
  with diversity and the cold) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** Confederation (1867), the two solitudes and the Quiet
  Revolution, the middle power and peacekeeping, and reconciliation with the first
  peoples - each casting the elements it needs. The reckoning with the residential
  schools, the Indian Act, and the Truth and Reconciliation Commission is staged
  soberly and on the public record.
- **Personas:** John A. Macdonald, Rene Levesque, and Lester Pearson as the
  historic cast, plus two defining archetypes, Therese (an older Quebecoise of the
  Quiet Revolution generation) and Jun (a younger immigrant of the mosaic). Each
  is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** les deux peuples (the two founding peoples and the two solitudes),
  the mosaic (the cultural mosaic, not the melting pot), peace, order, and good
  government (the communitarian, not-American temper and the middle power).
- **Places:** Ottawa (the compromise capital on the English-French border), le
  Quebec (the French fact, the walled city and the province).
- **Processes:** hockey (the winter game shared across both languages and the whole
  country), the Tim Hortons run (the everyman daily coffee ritual).
- **Pieces:** the maple leaf (the flag of 1965 and the syrup), the canoe (the first
  peoples' birchbark craft that opened the continent).

This is the house's first officially bilingual culture: English and French as two
co-official mother tongues of one nation. Three of its files are written in French
to embody the French fact - [persona_levesque.md](persona_levesque.md),
[position_les_deux_peuples.md](position_les_deux_peuples.md), and
[place_quebec.md](place_quebec.md) - while the rest are in English.

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the reckoning with the first peoples is treated soberly and respectfully
on the public record, and the staging and the architecture are original work.
