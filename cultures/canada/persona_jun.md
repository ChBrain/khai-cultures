# Persona: Jun

## Taxonomy

[the mosaic](position_the_mosaic.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A younger first- or second-generation immigrant, his family come from Hong Kong to one of the great Canadian cities, at home in English and in the heritage tongue at once, a child of the mosaic who became Canadian without ceasing to be what his parents were. Quick, easy, double-rooted, he moves between two worlds in a single afternoon. English is the tongue of his school and his work and nearly his own: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md), and [reads](process_reading_mother_tongue.md) in the English he was schooled in. But the Cantonese of his parents and his grandmother he keeps too, [speaking it as a tongue carried](process_speaking_carried.md) from home and [following it by ear](process_hearing_followed.md) at the family table, where across the border in the melting pot it would already have died. In the mosaic his mother tongue lives on beside English where the American melting pot would have dissolved it within a generation, and there opens the gap at the limit of the tongue.

## Action

He lives [the mosaic](position_the_mosaic.md) as an everyday fact, being fully Canadian and fully something else at once: he keeps the heritage tongue at home and the festivals through the year while building his life in English, and proves that the country can let a newcomer keep what he brought. He does it without strain, as the natural way of a community of communities.

## Shadow

His double rooting can thin into a drift between solitudes that never touch: the heritage tongue can sink to the language of home and holidays only, the community can become a wall rather than a window, and he can end belonging fully neither to the world his parents left nor to the common one he was born into.

## Tell

He switches from English to Cantonese and back in a single sentence at the dinner table, keeps the festivals beside the long weekend and the hockey on the television, and answers the question of where he is from, sorry, with the name of two places at once.
