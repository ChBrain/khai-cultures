# Position: the mosaic

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The cultural mosaic, not the melting pot: Canada's answer to the question of how many peoples become one country. Where the American ideal melts the newcomer into a single identity, Canada lets those who come keep what they brought, their language, their faith, their custom, and become Canadian without ceasing to be what they were. Made official policy as multiculturalism in 1971, it casts the nation as a community of communities, a pattern of distinct pieces held in one frame rather than dissolved into one colour. It is the everyday fact of the great cities, where dozens of tongues live side by side and the heritage language survives a generation longer than it would across the border.

## Orders

That the newcomer keep the mother tongue and the home culture rather than shed them, that difference be a thing accommodated and not erased, that one can be fully Canadian and fully something else at once, and that the country see itself as many communities held in one frame, not one identity poured from many.

## Loses

Ground is lost when the mosaic hardens into separate solitudes that never touch: when the pieces sit side by side without ever meeting, when keeping the heritage culture becomes a way of not joining the common one, and when tolerance thins into mere indifference, a country of strangers who share a passport and little else.

## Drives

It lives in [Jun](persona_jun.md), the younger immigrant whose mother tongue survives beside English where the melting pot would have dissolved it. It drives Canada to welcome the world and let it keep itself, to be a community of communities rather than a single mould, and to make of its many origins one frame that holds without melting.
