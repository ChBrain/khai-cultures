# u Burundi: a khai culture

Burundi staged as a full khai play, ISO BI, language rn (Kirundi). A land of hills
that shares a single tongue and a sacred drum, staged as a theatre: its history as
the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its language and its drum encode,
its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house that
holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its Estate; a
culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_burundi.md](play_burundi.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_rn.md](pitch_rn.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Burundi's
  profile (high respect for elders and authority, a strong collective and community
  bond, restraint and dignity over haste, and the weight of the spoken word) and
  naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the founding of the drum under Ntare Rushatsi, the reign of
  Mwezi Gisabo, the drum as the heartbeat of the nation, and Kirundi and the music
  carried into the world - each casting the elements it needs, the old kingdom and
  its heritage handled soberly.
- **Personas:** Ntare Rushatsi (the founding mwami of the oral record), Mwezi
  Gisabo (the great nineteenth-century mwami), Khadja Nin and Jean-Christophe
  Matata (the musicians who carried the tongue and the music abroad), plus one
  archetype, the umutimbo (the Ingoma drummer of the hills, keeper of the drum and
  the cattle). Each is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it
  holds.
- **Positions:** ingoma (the sacred drum as the heartbeat of the nation, once tied
  to kingship), ururimi (Kirundi, the one shared tongue, and the prized art of oral
  eloquence, the proverb and the praise-poem), inka (the deep cattle culture and the
  long-horned cattle of honour).
- **Places:** Gitega (the capital, the heart of the kingdom and the drum
  sanctuaries), Lake Tanganyika (one of the world's deepest and oldest lakes, the
  night fishing and the fishermen's lights).
- **Processes:** ukuvuza ingoma (the drumming, the abatimbo on the hills), uburobyi
  bwo mw'ijoro (the night fishing on Tanganyika).
- **Pieces:** Karyenda (the sacred royal drum, the heart of the nation in one
  object), inka y'amahembe maremare (the long-horned cattle of honour, wealth and
  bond).
