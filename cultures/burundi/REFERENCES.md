# u Burundi: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain heritage sources behind the
plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_rn.md](pitch_rn.md) is written from Burundi's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
Burundi is not separately published in the main Hofstede dataset; the figures below
are an approximate East African regional profile used only to set the register, and
are recorded here so the data is on the record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Burundi profile (approx., regional) |
| ----------------------------- | ----------------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | high                                |
| Individualism                 | low (collective)                    |
| Motivation toward Achievement | moderate                            |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | moderate                            |
| Long Term Orientation         | moderate                            |
| Indulgence                    | low (restrained)                    |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the six-dimensions model of national
culture). Where Burundi is not separately published, an East African regional
profile is used as an approximation, qualitative only; the pitch carries no number.

## Heritage sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn
from the public record and the oral heritage of Burundi. They are credited here and
never claimed; the staging is original work. The old kingdom, the mwami, and the
ganwa princes are staged soberly as heritage, taking no political side.

The Royal Drummers of Burundi and the ritual dance of the royal drum (the ingoma
drumming) are inscribed on UNESCO's Representative List of the Intangible Cultural
Heritage of Humanity; the heritage is credited here, never claimed.

The one defining archetype, the [umutimbo](persona_umutimbo.md), is not a historical
individual but a composite: the Ingoma drummer of the hills who keeps the drum, the
tongue, and the cattle of his people.
