# Persona: Clara

## Taxonomy

[bluegrass pride](position_bluegrass_pride.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A thirty-six-year-old horse trainer in Lexington. English, spoken with a bluegrass cadence, is her mother tongue, the language in which she [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and writes her daily training logs. It is the [Kentuckian English](position_language_en_us_ky.md) of the Lexington horse country, the soft bluegrass cadence of the stable and the track. She speaks no foreign languages. Yet the loss lies in the seam: The slower, tradition-bound talk of Appalachian miners like Silas and the quiet labor histories of the [coal solidarity](position_coal_solidarity.md) [she hears only like a distant echo](process_hearing_followed.md), feeling that the patient, resource-conserving rhythm of the land is treated as secondary to commercial racing entertainment and corporate tourism. She belongs to [Kentucky culture](position_culture_kentucky_culture.md) through the bluegrass-pride half of its character, the pedigree that reads a bloodline for what it is worth.

## Action

She preserves [derby cup](piece_derby_cup.md) records, coordinates track safety under [the forest reclamation plan](plan_appalachian_forest_reclamation.md), and studies [bluegrass pride](position_bluegrass_pride.md).

## Shadow

Her focus on breeding metrics and speedway performance can make her indifferent to municipal environmental problems in rural mining counties.

## Tell

She looks at a horse pedigree chart and says directly: "We are breeding championship bloodlines; you can't build a competitive stable if you're only focused on historic local preservation."
