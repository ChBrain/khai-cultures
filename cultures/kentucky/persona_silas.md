# Persona: Silas

## Taxonomy

[coal solidarity](position_coal_solidarity.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A fifty-two-year-old coal miner in Harlan County. English, spoken with a mountain cadence, is his mother tongue, the language in which he [speaks, hears, and thinks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), the defining code of his family records. He speaks no foreign languages. Yet the loss lies in the seam: The fast, commercial talk of horse breeders like Clara and the digital demands of Louisville race boards [he reads only as administrative updates](process_reading_followed.md) on his tablet, feeling that the patient, labor-protecting history of the mining valleys is being forgotten in the race for tourist development.

## Action

He preserves [coal pick](piece_coal_pick.md) relics, coordinates valley reclamation under [the forest reclamation plan](plan_appalachian_forest_reclamation.md), and studies [coal solidarity](position_coal_solidarity.md).

## Shadow

His focus on union memory and resource rights can make him suspicious of state-wide zoning and commercial real-estate development, fearing it will overwrite local property rights.

## Tell

He looks at a historic union card and says directly: "A simple pick and shovel built this state's energy; you can't protect our valleys if you're only focused on speedway profits and digital streaming metrics."
