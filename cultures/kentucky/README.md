# Kentucky: a khai culture

Kentucky staged as a full khai play, ISO US-KY, language en. The Bluegrass State: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its Kentucky Derby, Bourbon Whiskey Act, and Appalachian coal boom encodes, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house that holds this culture and answers for it.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_kentucky.md](play_kentucky.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_kentucky.md](pitch_kentucky.md), the Kentucky key.
- **Plots (history):** the Kentucky Derby Inauguration of 1875, the Bourbon Whiskey Act of 1964, and the Coal Mining Boom of the 1910s.
- **Personas:** Silas (Appalachian coal miner) and Clara (bluegrass horse trainer), written in English.
- **Positions:** bluegrass pride (horse breeding/rural musical traditions), coal solidarity (labor union resilience/Appalachian heritage).
- **Places:** Churchill Downs, Kentucky Hills.
- **Processes:** horse breeding (pedigree training), coal mining (underground extraction).
- **Pieces:** a derby cup, a coal pick.
- **Plan:** the forest reclamation plan.
