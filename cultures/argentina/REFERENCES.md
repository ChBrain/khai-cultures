# la Argentina: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_es.md](pitch_es.md) is written from Argentina's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record without
entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Argentina score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 49                        |
| Individualism                 | 46                        |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 56                        |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 86                        |
| Long Term Orientation         | 20                        |
| Indulgence                    | 62                        |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; Argentina's high Uncertainty
Avoidance and indulgence sit behind the pitch's reading of intensity, expressive
passion, and unease before an uncertain future.

## Historical sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn
from the public historical record. They are credited here and never claimed; the
staging is original work. The sober chapters of the story - the last military
dictatorship, the desaparecidos, and the nation's memory of Nunca Más - are staged
on the public record and in Argentina's own remembering, named without graphic or
atrocity detail.

The defining persona [el gaucho](persona_el_gaucho.md) is an archetype, not a
historical individual: a composite that carries the criollo soul of the plain, the
free horseman of the pampas whose freedom the fences and the boats pushed to the
edges of the nation he stood for. The figures of Maradona and, after him, Messi
are named as the public face of the fútbol that consoles the country.
