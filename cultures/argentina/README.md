# la Argentina: a khai culture

Argentina staged as a full khai play, ISO AR, language es. A nation of immigrants
and passion, once among the richest on earth and haunted by the greatness it let
slip, as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the
roles its language and faith encode, its places, its practices, and its defining
objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_argentina.md](play_argentina.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_es.md](pitch_es.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Argentina's profile (warm collectivism and strong relationships, expressive
  passion and indulgence, a marked unease before uncertainty, and a felt distance
  from power) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** la Independencia (1810-1816), la Pampa y los Barcos, el
  Peronismo (1945), and los Ciclos: crisis, memoria y pasión - each casting the
  elements it needs, and each staging the wound of frustrated greatness and the
  memory of the darkest years soberly and on the public record.
- **Personas:** José de San Martín, Eva Perón, Jorge Luis Borges, and Diego
  Maradona as the historic cast, plus one defining archetype, el gaucho (the free
  horseman of the pampas, the criollo soul of the country). Each is wired to the
  language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** el peronismo (the movement and the dividing line that defines a
  century), el crisol de razas (the melting pot of the European immigration and
  its cosmopolitan, self-questioning mind), la pampa y el gaucho (the land and the
  criollo independence at the root).
- **Places:** Buenos Aires (the Paris of the South, the port and the Plaza de
  Mayo), la Pampa (the endless grassland, the estancia and the granary of the
  world).
- **Processes:** el asado (the communal grill, the Sunday rite of family and
  friends), el tango (the embrace of the port, melancholy made movement).
- **Pieces:** el mate (the shared gourd and bombilla, the ritual of intimacy and
  equality), el bandoneón (the breathing accordion that gives the tango its
  voice).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the last military dictatorship and the desaparecidos are treated soberly
on the public record and in the nation's own memory of Nunca Más, named without
graphic or atrocity detail; the staging and the architecture are original work.
