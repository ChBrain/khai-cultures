# Plot: The Boston Tea Party 1773

## Taxonomy

[play_massachusetts](play_massachusetts.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

On December 16, 1773, the Sons of Liberty disguise themselves and dump 342 chests of British East India Company tea into Boston Harbor.

## Action

The protest against the British tea tax establishes [the revolutionary spirit](position_revolution.md) of defiance and intellectual dissent. The act unites the colonies and leads directly to the American Revolution. The [tea crate](piece_tea_crate.md) becomes a symbol of resistance. Abigail displays tea leaves recovered from the harbor in the local museum archive.

## Stage

- [the revolutionary spirit](position_revolution.md) : the legacy of intellectual dissent and resistance to arbitrary power.
- [tea crate](piece_tea_crate.md) : the wooden crates holding the taxed tea that was dumped into the harbor.
- [town meetings](process_town_meeting.md) : the local direct-democracy assemblies that coordinated the political resistance.

## Tension

The British government responds with the Intolerable Acts, closing Boston Harbor and suspending local self-governance, causing severe economic hardship for the city's residents.
