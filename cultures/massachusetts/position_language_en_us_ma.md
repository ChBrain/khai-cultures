# Position: Massachusetts English

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The voice of Massachusetts English, the Boston accent and broad New England vowel of the Bay State. It possesses the dropped r and flat a heard from Plymouth to Cambridge, the idiom of the town meeting, the wharf, and the university yard.

## Orders

What counts as native speech in Massachusetts, the talk of the common, the lecture hall, and the neighborhood parish. It orders how the old towns and the research corridor address one another, holding the sharp, argumentative turn that marks a native.

## Loses

Ground to the flattened broadcast English of national media, which softens the Boston accent in each younger generation. It also loses ground to the Latin and technical registers of the research corridor, which overlay the plain civic speech with a specialist tongue.

## Drives

It drives Massachusetts to keep its own accent even as its institutions speak a global English. It carries the mother tongue of archivist [Abigail](persona_abigail.md), the language in which she speaks, hears, and thinks her studies; and it carries the native English of biotech researcher [Liam](persona_liam.md), the tongue in which he speaks and publishes his papers before he wears Latin over it. So the one accent shows who holds it.
