## Arc

A theater of moral conviction, intellectual rigor, and revolutionary dissent, shaped by its role as the cradle of American independence and education. From the early self-governance of the Mayflower Compact and the defiant protest of the Boston Tea Party to the pioneering research of Harvard Yard and the modern biotechnology corridor of Cambridge. A culture that values civic responsibility, public education, and intellectual debate, constantly testing its moral foundations.
