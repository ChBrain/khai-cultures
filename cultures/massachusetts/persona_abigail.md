# Persona: Abigail

## Taxonomy

[the Puritan legacy](position_puritan_legacy.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A sixty-two-year-old archivist in Plymouth. English, spoken with a precise New England accent, is her mother tongue, the language in which she [speaks, hears, and thinks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), the voice of her studies. She speaks no foreign languages. Yet the loss lies in the seam: The fast biotech jargon of researchers like Liam and the digital demands of modern laboratories [she reads only with a quiet sigh](process_reading_followed.md), feeling that the patient study of archives and the moral duties of the past are being forgotten in the pursuit of efficiency.

## Action

She preserves the [Mayflower Compact](piece_mayflower_compact.md) records, coordinates local [town meetings](process_town_meeting.md), and studies [the revolutionary spirit](position_revolution.md).

## Shadow

Her focus on preservation can make her resistant to modern infrastructure upgrades and zoning changes needed to address rising sea levels in coastal towns.

## Tell

She looks at a weathered colonial diary and says quietly: "A community isn't just a collection of individuals chasing progress; it's a covenant built on shared moral duty."
