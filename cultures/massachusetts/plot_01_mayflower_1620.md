## Cue

In November 1620, Pilgrim settlers aboard the Mayflower anchor at Cape Cod and sign a compact to establish a self-governing colony.

## Action

The signing of the [Mayflower Compact](piece_mayflower_compact.md) establishes [the Puritan legacy](position_puritan_legacy.md) of social covenant and moral governance. The colony establishes a model of shared civic responsibility. Abigail curates the historic documents at Plymouth, explaining how the early compact shaped New England's town meeting system.

## Stage

- [the Puritan legacy](position_puritan_legacy.md) : the focus on covenant, civic duty, and moral stewardship.
- [Plymouth Rock](place_plymouth_rock.md) : the landing site and symbolic monument of the Pilgrim fathers.
- [Mayflower Compact](piece_mayflower_compact.md) : the signed agreement establishing the early political body.

## Tension

The arrival of the settlers leads to severe epidemics and land disputes that devastate the local Wampanoag population, who had lived on the land for generations.
