# Colombia: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind the
plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_es.md](pitch_es.md) is written from Colombia's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record without
entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Colombia score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------ |
| Power Distance                | 67                       |
| Individualism                 | 13                       |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 64                       |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 80                       |
| Long Term Orientation         | 13                       |
| Indulgence                    | 83                       |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; Colombia's strongly collectivist,
high-power-distance, uncertainty-avoiding, and highly indulgent profile underlies
the warm, courteous, group-centered, and joyful tone of the pitch.

## Historical sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn
from the public historical record. They are credited here and never claimed; the
staging is original work. The wound at the heart of the story - the conquest and
the slave port, and the long internal armed conflict and the narco years the nation
has carried and is now healing in the peace - is staged soberly on the public
record, naming the fact and the resilience without graphic or atrocity detail and
taking no political side.

The defining archetype, [el cafetero](persona_el_cafetero.md), is not a historical
individual but a composite: the patient coffee grower of the Andean slopes who
carries the campesino backbone of the nation and the lived, marvel-minded way of
seeing the world. The historic personas - Bolívar, La Pola, García Márquez, and
Shakira - are public figures drawn from the record and credited, never claimed.
