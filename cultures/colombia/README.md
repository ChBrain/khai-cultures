# Colombia: a khai culture

Colombia staged as a full khai play, ISO CO, language es. A nation of many regions
made one, of magical realism and resilient joy, on the long road from its wound to
peace, as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the
roles its language and faith encode, its places, its practices, and its defining
objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house that
holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its Estate; a
culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_colombia.md](play_colombia.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_es.md](pitch_es.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Colombia's profile (warm and courteous hospitality, a person-before-matter
  closeness, expressive and indulgent joy, a careful Andean formality against an
  exuberant Caribbean openness, and an optimism forged in hardship) and naming no
  dimension.
- **Plots (history):** El Dorado and the Conquest, Independence (1819), the Divided
  Nation, and Macondo and the Modern Nation - each casting the elements it needs,
  and each staging the wound and the resilience honestly.
- **Personas:** Simón Bolívar, Policarpa Salavarrieta "La Pola," Gabriel García
  Márquez, and Shakira as the historic cast, plus one defining archetype, el
  cafetero (the patient coffee grower of the Andean slopes, the marvel-minded
  backbone of the nation). Each is wired to the language engine by the
  language-crossing it holds - Spanish the mother tongue throughout, the indigenous
  inheritance carried in the campesino's speech and the Arabic and English carried
  by Shakira to the world.
- **Positions:** magical realism (the Colombian way of seeing, where the marvelous
  is ordinary), resilient joy (the gift of happiness and music carried through
  hardship), the road to peace (the unfinished nation-building and the healing of a
  divided land).
- **Places:** Cartagena de Indias (the walled Caribbean colonial port, the old
  slave port, the city of stone and sea), the Coffee Region (the green Andean
  slopes, the cultural coffee landscape, the heart of the campesino nation).
- **Processes:** the Barranquilla Carnival (the great Caribbean carnival of cumbia
  and color, the nation dancing as one), the tinto (the shared small black coffee,
  the daily ritual of warmth and welcome).
- **Pieces:** the sombrero vueltiao (the woven Zenú indigenous hat become a
  national emblem), coffee (the world's byword for quality, the campesino's labor
  and the nation's name abroad).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the conquest and the slave port, and the long internal conflict and narco
years the nation has carried and is healing, are treated soberly on the public
record, naming the wound and the resilience without graphic detail and taking no
political side; the staging and the architecture are original work.
