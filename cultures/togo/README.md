# Togo: a khai culture

Togo staged as a full khai play, ISO TG, language fr. A slender ribbon of a
country that holds its dry north and its humid south as one, as a theatre: its
history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its languages and
faith encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a
single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_togo.md](play_togo.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_fr.md](pitch_fr.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Togo's
  profile (a group-first belonging over the lone self, deference to age and rank,
  patience and restraint, and a quiet pride that does not bend) and naming no
  dimension. It is written in a Togolese French voice, not a Parisian one.
- **Plots (history):** the peoples and the migrations, the coast and the trading
  posts, the Togolese nation, and the voice of Togo - each casting the elements
  it needs, and each honoring north and south equally and the coast's harder
  passages soberly.
- **Personas:** Felix Couchoro (the pioneer novelist), Bella Bellow (the first
  great singing voice), King Mensah (the musician who carries the old drum
  forward), and Chief Mlapa of Togoville as the historic cast, plus one
  archetype, the Nana Benz (the matriarch wax-cloth trader of the grand market).
  Each is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** the two Togos (the dry north and the humid south held as one),
  the drum and the song (the beat that binds the country, from agbadza to the
  hills), and the land and the spirit (Vodun and ancestral belief, and the
  knowledge of plants, held with dignity).
- **Places:** Lome (the seaside capital, the grand market, and Akodessewa), and
  Koutammakou (the land of the Batammariba and their takienta tower-houses, a
  UNESCO World Heritage landscape).
- **Processes:** the grand market (the wax-cloth trade where north meets south),
  and the agbadza (the Ewe drum-and-dance that says joy and grief alike).
- **Pieces:** the wax cloth (the named, proverb-bearing pagne that is both finery
  and currency), and the takienta (the earthen tower-house of the north).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain cultural and
historical sources are recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never
enters the prose; the pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain
are credited, never claimed; the coast's harder passages and the country's
formation are treated soberly on the public record, honoring north and south
equally and taking no political side; the staging and the architecture are
original work.
