# Persona: Martha

## Taxonomy

[pioneer industry](position_pioneer_industry.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A fifty-two-year-old fruit farmer in Box Elder County. English, spoken with a warm drawl, is her mother tongue, the language in which she [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and writes her daily crop logs. It is the [Utahn English](position_language_en_us_ut.md) of the agricultural valleys, the warm drawl of the Box Elder orchards. She speaks no foreign languages. What reaches her from outside her own trade comes thin: the quick, technology-oriented talk of software engineers like Joseph and the digital planning of the Salt Lake boards, which [she hears only like a distant echo](process_hearing_followed.md), and she feels the patient, season-dependent rhythm of agricultural water preservation counts for less than corporate technology and speedway entertainment. In [the Beehive State](position_culture_beehive_state.md) she is read as the farmer half of the pairing, tending the soil the way Joseph tends the server rack.

## Action

She preserves [canning jar](piece_canning_jar.md) records, coordinates field conservation under [the Great Salt Lake plan](plan_great_salt_lake_conservation.md), and studies [outdoor adventure](position_outdoor_adventure.md).

## Shadow

Her pride in traditional family farming and local county independence can make her suspicious of state-wide ecological zoning and environmental agricultural mandates, fearing they will increase farming costs.

## Tell

She looks out over the ripening peach fields and says thoughtfully: "The soil has its own seasons; you can't protect farmland productivity if you're only focused on digital speed and technology entertainment metrics."
