# Solomon Islands: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_pis.md](pitch_pis.md) is written from the Solomon Islands'
profile on Geert Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and
carries no number. The Solomon Islands are not always listed individually in the
published data; the profile is read from the Melanesian and broader Pacific
pattern (a strongly collectivist, low-individualism culture with relatively high
power distance and a long, present-rooted communal orientation). The estimates and
their source are recorded here so the data is on the record without entering the
prose.

| Dimension                     | Solomon Islands profile (approx., regional) |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | high                                        |
| Individualism                 | very low (strongly communal)                |
| Motivation toward Achievement | low to moderate                             |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | moderate                                    |
| Long Term Orientation         | low to moderate                             |
| Indulgence                    | moderate                                    |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the six-dimensions model of national
culture), read across the Melanesian and Pacific regional pattern where a
country-specific score is not published. Values are approximate and qualitative;
the defining feature is the very strong collectivism (the wantok) against a
high-individualism baseline.

## Historical sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn
from the public historical record. They are credited here and never claimed; the
staging is original work. The hard chapters of the story - the labour trade
(blackbirding), the Second World War on Guadalcanal, Maasina Rule, and the
Tensions of 1998 to 2003 - are staged soberly on the public record, with no
atrocity detail and no side taken, and with reconciliation in view.

The two defining personas, [the shell-money maker](persona_man_blong_seleni.md)
and [the lagoon fisherman](persona_man_blong_fising.md), are archetypes, not
historical individuals: composites that carry the saltwater world of the lagoon,
the inherited craft of the shell money, and the everyday life that the Tensions
disrupted and the reconciliation seeks to restore.
