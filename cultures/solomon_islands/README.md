# Solomon Islands: a khai culture

The Solomon Islands staged as a full khai play, ISO SB, language pis (Solomon
Islands Pijin). A Melanesian archipelago of many tongues bound by Pijin and the
wantok, as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the
roles its language and kastom encode, its places, its practices, and its defining
objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_solomon_islands.md](play_solomon_islands.md), the culture
  itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_pis.md](pitch_pis.md), the Hofstede layer, written from the
  Solomon Islands' profile (a strongly communal, wantok-bound people, easygoing
  and present-minded, rooted in kastom and minded toward reconciliation, who hold
  authority lightly beneath the elders and kastom) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the islands of Solomon (Melanesian settlement, the naming,
  the labour trade, and the saltwater people and their shell money), Guadalcanal
  (the Second World War and the turning of the Pacific war), independence and
  Maasina Rule (the anti-colonial movement and independence in 1978), and the
  time of reconciliation (the Tensions of 1998 to 2003 and the long reconciliation
  after) - each casting the elements it needs, and each staged soberly on the
  public record, with reconciliation in view.
- **Personas:** Sir Peter Kenilorea (the founding Prime Minister), Sir Jacob Vouza
  (the Guadalcanal scout and hero), and Sir Baddeley Devesi (the first
  Governor-General) as the historic cast, plus two defining archetypes, the
  shell-money maker of Langa Langa and the lagoon fisherman of the saltwater
  people. Each is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds,
  the local tok ples or Pijin as the mother tongue and English carried.
- **Positions:** the wantok and kastom (the kinship and custom that hold the many
  islands as one, with Pijin as the shared tongue), the saltwater and the bush
  (the coastal and inland worlds joined by shell money), the great war and the
  reconciliation (Guadalcanal and the Tensions, and the kastom way of healing
  after a wrong).
- **Places:** Guadalcanal (Honiara the capital, the WWII battleground, and the
  ground of the Tensions), Langa Langa Lagoon (the saltwater people, the
  shell-money makers, and the artificial islands).
- **Processes:** the panpipes (the bamboo ensembles where no one plays alone),
  kastom reconciliation (the custom feast that settles a wrong and rejoins
  communities).
- **Pieces:** shell money (the strung shell-money of Langa Langa, true money of
  the saltwater people), the war canoe (the carved plank tomoko of the western
  islands).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the labour trade, the Second World War on Guadalcanal, Maasina Rule, and
the Tensions and reconciliation are treated soberly on the public record, with no
atrocity detail and no side taken, and with reconciliation in view. The staging
and the architecture are original work.
