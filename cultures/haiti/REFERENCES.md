# Haiti: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-record historical and cultural
sources behind the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_ht.md](pitch_ht.md) is written from Haiti's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record without
entering the prose.

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; where Haiti is not separately listed,
the figures are read against the broader Caribbean / regional profile and the
country's strong community, hierarchy, and faith bonds.

## Historical and cultural sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn
from the public record. They are credited here and never claimed; the staging is
original work. The hard chapters - slavery, the revolution, and the crushing
19th-century indemnity demanded by France - are staged soberly on the public
record, with no atrocity detail and with the pride of a nation that ended its own
slavery at the center.

The one defining persona, [abitan an](persona_abitan.md), is an archetype, not a
historical individual: a composite that carries the soul of the nation, the
Kreyol-speaking majority who work the land, serve the lwa, and bear the country on
their backs - and the gap between the language they live in (Kreyol) and the
language power is written in (French).
