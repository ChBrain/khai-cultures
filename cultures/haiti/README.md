# Haiti: a khai culture

Haiti staged as a full khai play, ISO HT, language ht (Haitian Creole, with French
as the co-official tongue of the educated elite), as a theatre: its history as the
plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its languages and faith encode, its
places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch. The
prose is written in Haitian Creole; only the chapter headings stay in English.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_haiti.md](play_haiti.md), the culture itself - Ayiti, the
  first free Black republic, born of the only victorious large-scale slave
  revolution.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_ht.md](pitch_ht.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Haiti's
  profile (strong community and family bonds, deep faith both Catholic and Vodou,
  proverb-rich speech, dignity over hardship, and resilience) and naming no
  dimension.
- **Plots (history):** Revolisyon an (the revolution, 1791-1804), Endepandans 1804
  (the declaration of independence and the first Black republic), Peyi Peyizan an
  (the 19th-20th century peasant country under the shadow of the indemnity), and
  Ayiti Jodi a (Haiti today, the earthquakes, the diaspora, and the art) - each
  casting the elements it needs, and each staging the gap between the glory of
  self-liberation and the hardship that followed soberly and with pride at the
  center.
- **Personas:** Toussaint Louverture, Jean-Jacques Dessalines, Catherine Flon, and
  Jacques Roumain as the historic cast, plus one defining archetype, abitan an (the
  peasant farmer, the Kreyol-speaking majority and the soul of the nation). Each is
  wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds: Kreyol the mother
  tongue of all, French carried or borrowed for the educated leaders.
- **Positions:** premye repiblik nwa a (the first Black republic, the pride of
  self-liberation), nanm Vodou ak kreyol la (the Vodou-and-Kreyol soul, the culture
  of the majority), and rezistans ak fyete (resilience and dignity through
  hardship).
- **Places:** Potoprens (Port-au-Prince, the capital, resilient through earthquake
  and hardship), and Sitadel Laferye (the Citadelle, the great mountain fortress
  built to defend a free Haiti).
- **Processes:** Soup Joumou (the Independence Day pumpkin soup, once forbidden to
  the enslaved, now the taste of freedom), and Rara (the Lenten street bands and
  processions, Vodou-rooted).
- **Pieces:** the Tanbou (the drum that carries Vodou, the ancestors, and the
  rhythm of the nation), and Haitian Painting (the vivid naive painting that
  carried Haiti's vision to the world).
- **Plan:** diyite ak devlopman (dignity and development), the open mandate to turn
  founding glory and resilience into dignity and development, win recognition, and
  hold homeland and diaspora as one.

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-record historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; slavery, the revolution, and the crushing 19th-century indemnity to France
are staged soberly on the public record, with no atrocity detail and pride at the
center; the staging and the architecture are original work.
