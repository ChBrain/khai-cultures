# Piece: Vegemite

## Taxonomy

Parent group: pieces

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Place

In the cupboard of nearly every Australian kitchen, on the morning toast and the school lunch and the cracker handed to a baby: the dark brown jar of thick, salty yeast spread that the nation grew up on, made from the leftover yeast of brewing and spread thin - thin is the rule - over buttered bread. An everyday taste, not a delicacy, the flavour of an ordinary Australian breakfast and the one the whole country shares.

## Load Bearing

It carries belonging in the most ordinary form: the in-joke of being Australian, the taste that locals love and visitors recoil from, the shibboleth on the breakfast table. To eat it without flinching, and to know it goes on thin, is a small daily proof of being one of the mob - not a grand symbol but a homely one, the fair go and the no-airs temper served at breakfast. It binds the multicultural nation the easy way, around a jar that asks nothing of your origin and only that you spread it right, the shared taste of an unpretentious people. Without this dark spread the nation would lose its plainest, funniest token of who is in on it.

## Apparent

A squat jar of near-black paste, salty and strong, made of brewer's yeast extract: in itself a humble by-product turned into a spread, nothing fine about it. Its weight is entirely in the belonging it carries and the shared joke of it, the everyday taste a whole country grew up on and outsiders cannot fathom, the homely badge of being in on the national in-joke.

## Yearbook
