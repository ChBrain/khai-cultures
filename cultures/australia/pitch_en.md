# Pitch: the Australian tone

## Taxonomy

The key in which the staging of [Australia](play_australia.md) is played. It changes nothing of what happens - not the First Fleet, not the gold, not Federation, not Anzac and the reckoning. It sets only how it sounds, the register in which all of it is given.

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Tenor

Played laconic, dry, and level: a people allergic to pretension and to skites, who deflate the big-noter with a quiet joke and back the underdog on principle. Egalitarian to the bone - first names for everyone, no airs, no my-lording, the boss called by his Christian name and the stranger called mate. Warm and direct, irreverent and self-deprecating, quick to take the mickey out of a friend and quicker to take it out of themselves, with a cheerful, understated stoicism in the face of a harsh and lonely land. Authority is met not with deference but with a raised eyebrow; the tall poppy is cut down to the height of the rest; and the highest praise is to be called a good bloke or a hard worker who never put on side.

## Undertow

Under the easygoing surface runs a levelling that can punish the very excellence it claims to admire, cutting the tall poppy not out of fairness but out of envy, so that to stand out is to invite the chop. There is a loneliness under the cheer - the small population on the vast, dry continent, the isolation at the far edge of the world, the mateship that can shade into a closed circle and a wary suspicion of the outsider. And under the larrikin shrug runs the unfinished reckoning: the relaxed, no-worries surface laid over the first peoples dispossessed and the question of belonging never quite settled, an ease bought partly by not looking too hard.

## Nerve

It strikes the chord of the fair go and the level field: the conviction that no one is anyone's better, that a person is taken as they come and not by their birth or their bank, that effort and a willingness to have a go earn respect where rank and pretension earn only a ribbing. It plays on the loyalty of mateship, the love of the underdog, the pleasure of cutting the pompous down to size, and the dry, deadpan delight of a people who would rather make a joke than make a fuss. And it plays, too, on the ache underneath - the long way from anywhere, and the business not yet finished.

## Echo

What is left is the image of a people determined to keep things level: dry, irreverent, warm, and unimpressed, who back the battler, salute the underdog, and trust the mate over the master and the deed over the title. A nation that runs on the promise of the fair go, and that, for all the gap between the easy surface and the unfinished reckoning beneath it, holds to the faith that everyone deserves a fair crack and that no one gets to put on side.
