# Place: Uluru

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Shown

The great red monolith rising alone from the flat heart of the continent, the vast sandstone dome that glows from ochre to deep crimson as the desert sun crosses it. Sacred to the Anangu, its traditional owners, woven into the law and story of the oldest living cultures on earth, named and known long before any map. The spiritual centre of the land, returned to its traditional owners in 1985, and the place from which, in 2017, the first peoples spoke the Uluru Statement from the Heart to the nation. The red centre made visible: ancient, immense, and alive with meaning.

## Holds

- [Anzac and the Reckoning](plot_04_anzac_and_the_reckoning.md): the heart of the continent from which the Uluru Statement from the Heart calls the nation to Voice, Treaty, and Truth, the place where the reckoning speaks.

## Offers

A place where the nation can meet the depth and the age of the land it stands on: the sacred heart of the first peoples, the country older than any human memory elsewhere, the centre where the oldest cultures speak and ask the youngest nation to answer. To stand before it is to stand before the time and the belonging the founding tried to declare away.

## Withheld

The innocence of a mere landmark. Uluru holds against the fiction of [terra nullius](position_terra_nullius.md) the plainest refutation - a place sacred and owned and known for tens of thousands of years, never empty, never no one's. It withholds the comfort of the easy surface, and from its red heart the first peoples ask the nation the question it has not yet finished answering.
