# Plot: the First Fleet (1788)

## Taxonomy

[play_australia](play_australia.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

For tens of thousands of years - perhaps sixty-five thousand - hundreds of Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander nations have lived across the continent, the longest continuous cultures on earth, the land known, named, and sung in their own laws and tongues. Then, an empire across the world losing its American colonies needs a place to send its convicts, and the British Crown turns its eye to a far southern coast its navigators have charted, and declares it, in law, a land belonging to no one.

## Action

In January 1788 eleven ships of the First Fleet, carrying convicts and the marines who guard them, make landfall and drop anchor at Sydney Cove under [Captain Arthur Phillip](persona_phillip.md), the first governor. The flag is raised, the penal colony is founded, and the continent is claimed for the Crown on the doctrine of terra nullius - the land declared empty though it had been home to hundreds of nations for tens of thousands of years. A nation begins as a prison at the edge of the world, planted on a fiction of emptiness over a peopled shore, and the dispossession of the first peoples begins with it, written into the very ground of the founding.

## Stage

- [Captain Arthur Phillip](persona_phillip.md): the first governor who plants the colony and founds the nation on the doctrine of empty land.
- [terra nullius](position_terra_nullius.md): the legal fiction, declared at the founding, that the land belonged to no one and could be freely claimed.
- [Sydney](place_sydney.md): the cove where the fleet landed and the colony was raised, the birthplace of the nation.

## Tension

The nation is founded twice over in contradiction: a prison made into a country, and a peopled land declared empty. The same act that begins Australia begins the dispossession of the oldest cultures on earth, the fiction of terra nullius laid over a continent loud with people and law and speech. The country starts with both its beginning and its founding wrong, and the whole story after is the slow, unfinished struggle to face the gap planted at Sydney Cove.
