# Position: mateship

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The bond of loyalty between equals that runs as the deepest social code of the nation: that you stand by your mate, share what you have, and never leave a man behind. Born of the convict gang, the goldfield claim, the shearing shed, and the trench, it is solidarity among people who have only each other against a hard land and a distant authority - no master and no servant, only mates who are level and owe each other a hand. It is loyalty without rank, the highest of the bush and digger virtues, and it carries the egalitarian faith down into the unwritten rules of how people actually treat one another. And, like the fair go it shares a root with, it is a bond drawn with an edge: a circle that warms those inside it can shut out those it does not count as mates, and the loyalty that levels can also exclude.

## Orders

That you back your mates and the underdog, share the load and the credit, and put the bonds of equals above the claims of rank; that loyalty is owed to the person beside you, not the office above you, so the mate is held above the master; and that the test of a person is whether they stand by their word and their friends when it costs them something.

## Loses

Ground when the circle of mates closes into a club - when mateship becomes a wall against the outsider, the migrant, or the one who is different, and the loyalty that should level instead divides into us and them; and ground when it shades into a code of silence, the standing-by-your-mate that protects the wrong done rather than the person, loyalty curdled into a refusal to call a thing what it is.

## Drives

Lives in [the larrikin](persona_the_larrikin.md), the cheeky, anti-authority figure who holds the mate above the master and meets every officer and every airs-and-graces with an irreverent grin, the loyalty of equals turned into a refusal to bow. It drives Australia to value the bond between ordinary people above every rank and title, and to be measured by whether that loyalty reaches everyone or stops at the edge of the circle.
