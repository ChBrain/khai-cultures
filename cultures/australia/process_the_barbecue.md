# Process: the barbie

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

Anyone with a backyard, a grill, and a few people to feed: a family on a warm Saturday, a group of mates on a public holiday, a street getting together, a household welcoming the new neighbours. All it takes is to fire up the barbie, throw on the snags and the steaks, tell everyone to bring a plate, and crack open something cold while the smoke goes up.

## Direction

Toward the egalitarian gathering, the level field made into a meal: no head of the table, no formal sitting, everyone milling about with a plate in hand and a drink, the cook and the guests on the same footing. It is not a banquet but a muck-in - bring your own, lend a hand, no fuss, no airs - the fair go served from the grill, the company easy and the rank left at the gate.

## Lever

It is driven by the deep Australian faith that the best gathering is the most level one, and by a climate and a backyard that make the open-air meal the natural way to be together. It holds against pretension and formality - the barbie cannot be stood on ceremony - and against the isolation of a spread-out, suburban people; against it stand the same closed circles any easy gathering can draw, the welcome that warms the insider and overlooks the one not asked.

## Echo

The barbie makes audible what drives [mateship](position_mateship.md): that the country is held together not by rank or ceremony but by ordinary people on a level footing, sharing what they have and taking each other as they come. Around this grill the [larrikin](persona_the_larrikin.md) holds court, taking the mickey, deflating the pompous, and keeping the company as level as the fire is hot.
