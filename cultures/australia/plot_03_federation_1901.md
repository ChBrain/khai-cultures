# Plot: Federation (1901)

## Taxonomy

[play_australia](play_australia.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

By the close of the nineteenth century the six self-governing colonies on the continent - separate, with their own parliaments, tariffs, and even railway gauges - feel the pull toward union. A movement for Federation gathers, conventions are held, a constitution is drafted and put to the people, and the colonies vote to bind themselves into one nation under the Crown. Leading the movement is the barrister [Sir Edmund Barton](persona_barton.md).

## Action

On the first day of 1901 the six colonies federate into the Commonwealth of Australia, a single democratic dominion with Barton as its first Prime Minister. The new nation is early and proud in its democracy - the secret ballot, the franchise wide, a society framed to protect the ordinary person and give the fair go the force of law - one of the world's pioneering social democracies. And among the very first acts of the new Commonwealth, on the public record, is the legislation of a White Australia policy that draws the line of who may belong to the nation by race, closing the door against the non-European even as it opens the democracy wide for the white settler. The nation is founded democratic and bounded in the same breath.

## Stage

- [Sir Edmund Barton](persona_barton.md): the first Prime Minister who unites the colonies and writes the fair go into the new nation's law.
- [the fair go](position_the_fair_go.md): the egalitarian creed given the force of national law in one of the world's earliest social democracies.
- [Sydney](place_sydney.md): the city where the Commonwealth was inaugurated and the new nation took its first form.

## Tension

The nation comes into being as one of the freest and most egalitarian democracies on earth, the fair go made national law - and in the same founding it draws the line of belonging by race, the White Australia policy written in alongside the franchise and the ballot. The Commonwealth begins with both its democratic creed and the gap inside it, the fairness for all founded as fairness for some, and the long work of the century to come will be to widen the door it framed so narrow.
