# Australia: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-record historical sources behind the
plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md) is written from Australia's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record without
entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Australia score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 38                        |
| Individualism                 | 90                        |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 61                        |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 51                        |
| Long Term Orientation         | 21                        |
| Indulgence                    | 71                        |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; Australia's Individualism score is
among the highest in the published data, paired with a low Power Distance that
underwrites its egalitarian, anti-pretension temper.

## Historical sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn
from the public historical record. They are credited here and never claimed; the
staging is original work. The contradiction at the heart of the story - the
dispossession of the Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples, the doctrine of
terra nullius, the White Australia policy, and the long, unfinished reckoning - is
staged soberly on the public record.

The two defining personas, [the battler](persona_the_battler.md) and
[the larrikin](persona_the_larrikin.md), are archetypes, not historical
individuals: composites that carry the tension between the ordinary worker who has a
go and backs the underdog (the goldfields, the shed, the suburb) and the irreverent,
anti-authority mate who holds the mate above the master (the digger of the trench,
the cheek that deflates all pomp).
