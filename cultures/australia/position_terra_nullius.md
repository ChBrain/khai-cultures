# Position: terra nullius

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The legal fiction on which the colony was founded: that the land belonged to no one - terra nullius, land that is empty - and so could be claimed by the Crown without treaty, purchase, or consent. It was declared over a continent that had been home for tens of thousands of years to hundreds of Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander nations, the oldest continuous living cultures on earth, whose belonging to the land was so deep it was sung, named, and held in law of their own. The doctrine is the founding gap of the nation made plain: the fiction of emptiness laid over a peopled land, the dispossession it licensed, and the long silence that followed. It stood for two centuries as the unspoken ground of the whole settlement, until in 1992 the High Court, in the landmark case brought before it, held that it had never been true, and that the first peoples' prior presence and title to the land were real in law.

## Orders

That the land was empty and so freely the Crown's - the fiction that ordered the colony, dispossessed the first peoples, and denied them standing for two centuries; and, since 1992, the opposite order it now compels: that the land was never empty, that native title survived, and that the nation must reckon with the people it was founded by erasing.

## Loses

Ground at last and decisively in 1992, when the landmark High Court ruling overturned it and the law admitted what had always been true; and ground further with every step of the reckoning since - the recognition of native title, the National Apology, the Uluru Statement - though it loses ground slowly, for the dispossession it licensed left a gap in health, in justice, and in belonging that the overturning of the fiction has not yet closed.

## Drives

Lives in the founding act that raised the colony on the doctrine in 1788 and planted the fiction of emptiness on a peopled shore, and in the decades of legal pursuit that carried a Torres Strait Islander people's truth of belonging through the colony's own courts until the fiction fell. It drives Australia to confront the gap at its founding, and to measure itself by how far it has come in closing it.
