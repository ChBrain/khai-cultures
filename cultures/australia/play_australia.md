# Play: Australia

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the house that holds this staging and answers for it.

## Name

The Commonwealth of Australia, an island continent in the southern seas, the whole of a landmass and the smallest of them, a federation of six states and two territories ringed by ocean and turned in on a vast, dry, ancient interior. ISO AU, language en, with no official tongue named in law. A young nation of some twenty-six million people holding the oldest continuously inhabited land on earth, and carrying within it the oldest living cultures the world has known.

## Arc

The oldest human story meeting the youngest of nations. For tens of thousands of years - perhaps sixty-five thousand - hundreds of Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander nations lived across this continent, the longest continuous cultures on the planet, the land sung and known and never empty. Then in 1788 a British fleet of convicts and their keepers made landfall at Sydney Cove and founded a penal colony on a doctrine that the land belonged to no one - terra nullius - the gap between that fiction and the people already there present from the first day, and the engine of the whole story since. The colony grew on gold and free settlement into a string of self-governing democracies, early to the secret ballot and the eight-hour day, and in 1901 the six colonies federated into one Commonwealth - a democratic dominion founded, on the public record, with a White Australia policy that drew the line of who belonged by race. The nation found its legend not in a victory but in a defeat, at Gallipoli in 1915, where the Anzac diggers forged a self-image of mateship, irreverence, and grit under fire. After the Second World War it threw its doors open to the world, and waves of migration from Europe, then Asia, then everywhere remade a British outpost into one of the most successful multicultural societies on earth. And across the same century it began, slowly and unfinished, to reckon with the first peoples it had dispossessed: Mabo overturning terra nullius in 1992, the National Apology in 2008, the Uluru Statement from the Heart in 2017. Under it all runs one nerve: the fair go, the egalitarian faith of a small people on a harsh and immense land that no one is anyone's better, the underdog backed, the tall poppy cut, and the mate held above the master.

## Triggers

**[Plot 1: the First Fleet (1788)](plot_01_first_fleet_1788.md)**

In 1788 a British fleet of convicts and marines lands at Sydney Cove under [Captain Arthur Phillip](persona_phillip.md) and founds a penal colony on the doctrine of [terra nullius](position_terra_nullius.md), the land declared empty though hundreds of nations had lived on it for tens of thousands of years.

**[Plot 2: the Gold Rush](plot_02_the_gold_rush.md)**

In the 1850s gold draws free immigrants from across the world, and at the Eureka Stockade in 1854 the diggers raise a flag against the colonial authorities, igniting [the fair go](position_the_fair_go.md) and the democratic, egalitarian temper that [the battler](persona_the_battler.md) will carry from the goldfields to the suburbs.

**[Plot 3: Federation (1901)](plot_03_federation_1901.md)**

On the first day of 1901 the six colonies federate into the Commonwealth of Australia under its first Prime Minister [Sir Edmund Barton](persona_barton.md), a democratic dominion that gives the nation [the fair go](position_the_fair_go.md) in law - and, on the public record, draws the line of belonging with a White Australia policy.

**[Plot 4: Anzac and the Reckoning](plot_04_anzac_and_the_reckoning.md)**

From Gallipoli in 1915 the [larrikin](persona_the_larrikin.md) digger forges a national legend, postwar migration ends White Australia and remakes the nation as multicultural, and the long reckoning with the first peoples runs from [Eddie Mabo](persona_mabo.md) overturning [terra nullius](position_terra_nullius.md) in 1992 to the National Apology and the Uluru Statement.

## Stakes

Whether the fair go can be made real for everyone, including the first Australians dispossessed at the founding, so that the gap between the egalitarian promise and the lived reality is at last closed; whether a nation founded as a British colony can complete its reckoning with the oldest cultures on earth and perhaps complete its own independence as a republic; and whether the multicultural nation drawn from a hundred origins can stay one people, holding the fair go open for each new arrival, or whether the easygoing surface finally cracks over the unfinished business beneath it.
