# Australia: a khai culture

Australia staged as a full khai play, ISO AU, language en. A young nation on the
oldest inhabited land on earth, staged as a theatre: its history as the plot line,
its people as the cast, the roles its language and faith encode, its places, its
practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house that
holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its Estate; a
culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_australia.md](play_australia.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Australia's profile (intense individualism with a strong egalitarian, anti-
  pretension temper, optimism and indulgence, an easygoing tolerance of
  uncertainty, and a deep suspicion of authority) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the First Fleet (1788), the Gold Rush, Federation (1901),
  and Anzac and the Reckoning - each casting the elements it needs, and each
  staging the gap between the fair go and the first peoples honestly.
- **Personas:** Captain Arthur Phillip, Eddie Mabo, and Sir Edmund Barton as the
  historic cast, plus two defining archetypes, the battler (the ordinary worker who
  has a go and backs the underdog) and the larrikin (the irreverent, anti-authority
  mate). Each is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** the fair go (egalitarianism and the level field, and the gap
  between fairness for all and fairness for some), mateship (loyalty between equals,
  no master and no servant), terra nullius (the founding fiction of empty land, the
  dispossession it licensed, and its overturning).
- **Places:** Sydney (the landing place of the First Fleet and the nation's
  brightest face), Uluru (the sacred red heart of the continent and the place of
  the Statement from the Heart).
- **Processes:** the barbie (the egalitarian gathering, the level field made into a
  meal), the dawn service (the Anzac Day rite of remembrance).
- **Pieces:** the boomerang (emblem of the deep time of the first peoples),
  Vegemite (the everyday taste and the in-joke of belonging).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-record historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources on the public record are credited, never
claimed; the dispossession of the Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples,
the White Australia policy, and the long reckoning are treated soberly on the
public record; the staging and the architecture are original work.
