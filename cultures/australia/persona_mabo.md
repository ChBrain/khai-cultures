# Persona: Eddie Mabo

## Taxonomy

[terra nullius](position_terra_nullius.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A Torres Strait Islander man of Mer, the Murray Island in the eastern strait, who carried his people's belonging to their land through a decade in the colony's own courts until, in 1992, the High Court held that terra nullius had never been true and that native title was real in law - the case that bears his name and the moment the founding fiction fell. Determined, eloquent, and unyielding, a man who knew without doubt that his island had always been his people's, he turned that certainty into the most consequential judgment in the nation's history, though he died months before it came down. His mother tongue is Meriam Mir, the language of Mer, in which the land and its boundaries and its owners were named and held long before the colony came: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [hears](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) the island tongue in which his people's title to the land was always spoken. English he [carries as the tongue of the colony](process_speaking_carried.md), the working language he [reads and follows](process_reading_followed.md) and in which he made his case, and there lies the hard truth at the limit of the tongue: the law that had erased his people was argued and overturned in English, the language of the court that had once declared the land empty, so that to prove his ancient belonging he had to win it in the conqueror's tongue, the gap between the mother tongue that held the truth and the borrowed tongue that had to be made to admit it.

## Action

In [Anzac and the Reckoning](plot_04_anzac_and_the_reckoning.md) he overturns [terra nullius](position_terra_nullius.md) and forces the nation's law to admit that the land was never empty and the first peoples were always there. By carrying his certainty through the colony's courts to victory, he opens the long reckoning and gives Australia the legal ground on which to close the gap at its founding.

## Shadow

The man who must win his own people's truth in the tongue and the court of those who denied it is the nation's own shadow: the temptation to treat the law's late admission as the whole of justice, to mistake the overturning of the fiction for the closing of the gap, and to let the famous judgment stand in for the harder work it only began.

## Tell

He proves in an English courtroom what his people had always known in Meriam Mir - that the land was theirs - and dies before the verdict, leaving the country the truth it had refused for two centuries and the name by which the reckoning is now told.
