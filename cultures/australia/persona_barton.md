# Persona: Sir Edmund Barton

## Taxonomy

[the fair go](position_the_fair_go.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

Barrister, leader of the Federation movement, and the first Prime Minister of the Commonwealth of Australia, who in 1901 brought six separate colonies together into one democratic nation - and who carried, as part of that founding and on the public record, the White Australia policy that drew the line of belonging by race. Steady, persuasive, and a builder of the union, he gave the new nation a federal democracy with the fair go early in its law: the secret ballot, the franchise extended, a society made to protect the ordinary person. And in the same founding he embodied its fault line, for the egalitarian Commonwealth was framed for the white settler and closed against the non-European, the gap between the fair go for all and the fair go for some written into the nation at its birth. English he has whole and lawyerly: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md), and [writes](process_writing_mother_tongue.md) in his mother tongue, [Australian English](position_language_en_au.md), the tongue of the Crown and the constitution he helped to draft, in which the new nation's law and its lines of belonging were both composed. The hundreds of first tongues of the land, and the many languages of the migrants his policy turned away, found no place in the English founding he wrote, and so the very fluency that built the democratic nation also drew its borders at the limit of the tongue, deciding in one language who would and would not be heard.

## Action

In [Federation (1901)](plot_03_federation_1901.md) he unites the colonies into the Commonwealth and writes [the fair go](position_the_fair_go.md) into the new nation's law. By founding a democratic dominion early to the secret ballot and the protection of the ordinary person - while drawing the line of belonging by race - he gives Australia both its egalitarian creed and the gap that creed would have to grow to close.

## Shadow

The founder who can build the fairest of democracies and bar the door by race is the nation's own shadow: the temptation to celebrate the fair go as if it had always meant everyone, to recite the egalitarian founding while forgetting whom it shut out, and to let the genuine achievement of the union excuse the line drawn through it.

## Tell

He frames a Commonwealth of the fair go and frames into it a White Australia, building a democracy for the ordinary man while deciding by race which men counted - leaving the country a creed of fairness larger than the founding that proclaimed it.
