# Place: Sydney

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Shown

The great harbour city on the eastern coast, raised around the deep blue water of Port Jackson where the First Fleet dropped anchor at Sydney Cove in 1788 - the landing place of the colony and the birthplace of the nation. The land of the Gadigal of the Eora nation, peopled for tens of thousands of years before the ships came. The white sails of the Opera House on the point, the great steel arch of the Harbour Bridge above the water, the ferries crossing the busy blue, the beaches and the bush at the city's edge: the founding place become the nation's brightest face to the world.

## Holds

## Offers

A place where the nation can see its own beginning and its own brightness in one view: the cove of the founding and the harbour of the modern multicultural city, the convict shore become a global capital, the meeting of the oldest land and the youngest nation laid out around the water.

## Withheld

The innocence of a clean beginning. Sydney carries the gap between the bright harbour city and the founding it stands on - the cove where the flag was raised on a peopled land declared empty, the Gadigal ground beneath the marble and the sails, the reminder that the nation's most beautiful face is built over its first dispossession.
