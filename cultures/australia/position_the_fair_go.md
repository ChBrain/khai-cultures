# Position: the fair go

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The egalitarian creed of the nation: that everyone deserves a fair go, a fair crack at a decent life, and that no one - not by birth, not by rank, not by wealth - is anyone's better. A people of convicts and free settlers on a hard land turned this into a faith in the level field, and built one of the world's earliest social democracies on it: the secret ballot, the eight-hour day, the basic wage, the vote extended early. It is the conviction that a society is judged by how it treats the ordinary person and the one who is down on their luck, and that the proper response to the high and mighty is to bring them back to the height of everyone else. And it is a creed founded with a fault line through it: the fair go was written for the white settler and withheld at the founding from the first peoples and the non-European, the gap between the promise of fairness for all and the fairness reserved for some present from the start.

## Orders

That the society be levelled and the ordinary person protected, the underdog backed and the battler given a leg up; that no one be allowed to lord it over another, so the tall poppy who puts on side is cut back down; and that the deepest work of the nation is to make the fair go true for everyone it once left out - the first Australians above all, and each new arrival the world sends to its shores.

## Loses

Ground when the levelling turns from fairness into envy - when the tall poppy is cut not because they put on airs but because they dared to excel, and excellence is punished as a betrayal of the level field; and ground, too, when the fair go is claimed as already finished, recited as a flattering self-image while the gap stays open for those it never reached, the easy faith in fairness used to avoid the harder work of making it real.

## Drives

Lives in [Sir Edmund Barton](persona_barton.md), who federated the colonies into a democratic Commonwealth and wrote the fair go into the new nation's law even as its founding drew the line of who that fairness was for, and in [the battler](persona_the_battler.md), the ordinary worker who has a go, backs the underdog, and carries the egalitarian temper from the goldfields to the suburbs. It drives Australia to keep widening the fair go until it reaches everyone, and to keep itself honest about how far it has still to go.
