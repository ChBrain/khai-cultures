# Plan: reconciliation

## Taxonomy

The open mandate the nation has set itself: to close the gap [terra nullius](position_terra_nullius.md) holds open between the founding and the first peoples, to make [the fair go](position_the_fair_go.md) real for everyone it once left out - the first Australians and each new arrival alike - and to decide, in its own time, the question of its full independence as a republic. It is the unfinished work of the nation's own conscience, carried by [mateship](position_mateship.md) and the fair go beyond the circle they first drew.

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Direction

Close the gap with the first peoples - in health, in justice, in opportunity, and in standing - and answer the call of the Uluru Statement from the Heart for Voice, Treaty, and Truth, so that the oldest cultures on earth are recognised in the nation built over them; keep the fair go open and true for every newcomer, holding the multicultural nation one across a hundred origins; and decide, when the nation is ready, whether to complete its independence and become a republic, carrying its British founding to its own conclusion.

## Orders

- To the battler like [the battler](persona_the_battler.md): widen the fair go past the edge of your own circle, back the underdog you were taught not to see, and let the level field reach the first Australians and the newcomer alike.
- To the larrikin like [the larrikin](persona_the_larrikin.md): turn the irreverence that deflates every false authority toward the real reckoning, and stop, for once, to hear the older voices of the land your wit has not yet listened to.
- To the nation: answer the Uluru Statement, close the gap, and keep the multicultural union whole, never mistaking the easy surface for the work that is finished.

## Implementation

A young nation on the oldest land that has come a long way and has far to go: terra nullius overturned and native title recognised, the National Apology made, the White Australia policy long dismantled and the country remade as a genuinely multicultural society - and yet the gap in Indigenous health, justice, and opportunity stays open, the Uluru Statement awaits a full answer, the question of the republic stands undecided, and the easygoing nation must keep choosing the unfinished work over the comfortable surface.
