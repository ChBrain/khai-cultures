# Persona: the larrikin

## Taxonomy

[mateship](position_mateship.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The larrikin: the cheeky, irreverent, anti-authority figure at the heart of the national self-image - quick with a joke, allergic to pomp, loyal to his mates and rude to his betters, the digger who salutes no officer he does not respect and takes the mickey out of every airs and graces. Disrespectful of rank and devoted to equals, he holds the mate above the master and meets pretension with a grin, the underdog's champion who would rather cut a tall poppy than bow to it. He is the irreverent spirit of mateship made flesh, the embodiment of the easygoing, level, no-bull temper the nation likes best in itself. English he has whole and quick, the dry, deadpan, slang-rich [Australian English](position_language_en_au.md) of the trench and the pub: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in his mother tongue, the irreverent vernacular that turns every grand thing down to size. The first tongues of the land he stands on, the oldest speech on earth, he mostly [cannot hear or follow](process_hearing_followed.md), and so the larrikin who deflates every authority has not yet reckoned with the authority of the people who named this country first, the gap between his levelling wit and the older voices it has not heard marking the limit of his own tongue. The larrikin belongs to [Australian culture](position_culture_australian_culture.md) as its irreverent self-image, the mate held above the master in the very spirit the nation likes best in itself.

## Action

In [Anzac and the Reckoning](plot_04_anzac_and_the_reckoning.md) the larrikin is the irreverent digger of Gallipoli, who grumbles at the officers, jokes under fire, and stands by his mates - forging from a defeat the legend of mateship, grit, and cheerful insubordination that becomes the nation's image of itself. By holding [mateship](position_mateship.md) above rank even in the trench, the larrikin gives Australia a self-image rooted not in victory or empire but in loyalty between equals.

## Shadow

The larrikin who deflates every authority can also refuse the authority that should be heard: the irreverence that punctures pomp can shade into a refusal to take anything seriously, the levelling wit used to dodge the hard reckoning, and the loyalty to mates turned into a code that backs the wrong done rather than face it. The grin that will not bow can also be the grin that will not look.

## Tell

He jokes under fire and salutes no rank he has not earned for itself, holding his mates above every master - and turns that same wit on everything but the older voices of the land, whose authority he has not yet stopped to hear.
