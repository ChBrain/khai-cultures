# Guatemala: a khai culture

Guatemala staged as a full khai play, ISO GT, language es. The living heartland of
the Maya world and a country of many peoples, as a theatre: its history as the plot
line, its people as the cast, the roles its languages and faith encode, its places,
its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch. The prose body
is written in Spanish; only the chapter headings stay English.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house that
holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its Estate; a
culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_guatemala.md](play_guatemala.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_es.md](pitch_es.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Guatemala's profile (courteous and soft-spoken, formal and devout, indirect, warm
  yet reserved, deeply marked by the highland-indigenous and ladino worlds) and
  naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Maya World, the Conquest and the Colony, the Republic and
  the Fruit Company, and the War and the Peace - each casting the elements it needs,
  and each staging the gap between the promise and the reality soberly, with no
  atrocity detail and no blame.
- **Personas:** Tecún Umán, Brother Pedro (San Pedro de Betancur), Miguel Ángel
  Asturias, and Rigoberta Menchú as the historic cast, plus one defining archetype,
  the Maya weaver (the keeper of the huipil and the living root). Each is wired to
  the language engine by the language-crossing it holds; the Maya-rooted figures hold
  a Maya mother tongue (K'iche') with Spanish carried.
- **Positions:** the Maya world (the living indigenous root, the Popol Vuh, the men
  of maize), the country of peoples (the many indigenous peoples and ladinos, and the
  divide to overcome), the wound and the word (the legacy of the conflict, the faith,
  and the literature that named the nation).
- **Places:** Tikal (the great Maya city of temples in the Petén jungle), Antigua
  (the baroque colonial capital beneath the volcanoes).
- **Processes:** Holy Week (the great processions over sawdust carpets in Antigua),
  the Giant Kites (the Maya-Catholic kites of Sumpango on the Day of the Dead).
- **Pieces:** the huipil (the woven blouse, each village its own pattern), the
  marimba (the national instrument of wood, the common voice of all the peoples).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-record sources are recorded in
[REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the pitch is its
invisible hand. Sources on the public record are credited, never claimed; the
conquest, the indigenous/ladino divide, and the internal armed conflict are treated
soberly, with no atrocity detail and no blame; the staging and the architecture are
original work.
