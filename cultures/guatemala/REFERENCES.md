# Guatemala: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-record sources behind the plots,
personas, places, and pieces. Sources on the public record are credited here and
never claimed; the staging is original work.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_es.md](pitch_es.md) is written from Guatemala's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record without
entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Guatemala score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 95                        |
| Individualism                 | 6                         |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 37                        |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 99                        |
| Long Term Orientation         | 26                        |
| Indulgence                    | n/a                       |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; Guatemala's Individualism score is
among the lowest, and its Uncertainty Avoidance among the highest, in the published
data.

original work. The contradiction at the heart of the story - the conquest, the
indigenous/ladino divide, and the internal armed conflict - is staged soberly, with
no atrocity detail and no blame, as the gap between the promise and the reality.
