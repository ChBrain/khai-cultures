# India: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical and cultural
sources behind the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_hi.md](pitch_hi.md) is written from India's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record without
entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | India score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | --------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 77                    |
| Individualism                 | 48                    |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 56                    |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 40                    |
| Long Term Orientation         | 51                    |
| Indulgence                    | 26                    |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group.

## Historical and cultural sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn
from the public historical and cultural record. They are credited here and never
claimed; the staging is original work. The partition of the subcontinent and all
communal matters are staged soberly on the public record, with no detail and no
side, and every faith and community is treated with equal respect.

The one defining archetype, [the farmer](persona_kisan.md), is not a historical
individual but a composite: the village India where much of the nation still
lives, the kisan whose silent labour feeds the country and whose own voice is
often the least heard.
