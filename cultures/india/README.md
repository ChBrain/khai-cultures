# India: a khai culture

India staged as a full khai play, ISO IN, language hi. An ancient and living
civilization, many languages and faiths held as one, staged as a theatre: its
history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its language and
faith encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by
a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_india.md](play_india.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_hi.md](pitch_hi.md), the Hofstede layer, written from India's
  profile (a collective, family- and community-first orientation, a long view and
  deep patience, a high tolerance of plurality and contradiction, and respect for
  hierarchy and the elder) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Vedic heritage and the epics, Ashoka and the path of
  dharma, the freedom movement, and the Republic - each casting the elements it
  needs. The partition of the subcontinent is acknowledged soberly and with
  dignity, with no detail and no side.
- **Personas:** Mahatma Gandhi (nonviolence and satyagraha), Rabindranath Tagore
  (the poet and Nobel laureate who composed the anthem), Emperor Ashoka (the
  dharma-king of the classical age), and A. P. J. Abdul Kalam (the scientist and
  beloved president), plus one archetype, the farmer (the village India that
  feeds the nation). Each is wired to the language engine by the
  language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** dharma (duty, righteousness, and the cosmic order from which the
  faiths born here draw their meaning), unity in diversity (the plural civilization
  held as one), and nonviolence (ahimsa, the principle that freed a nation).
- **Places:** Varanasi (the Ganga and the living continuity of the civilization)
  and Delhi (the seat of power and the heart of the Republic).
- **Processes:** yoga (the inward discipline of body, breath, and mind) and the
  festival (Diwali, Holi, and the shared joy that binds a plural society).
- **Pieces:** the great epics (the Ramayana and the Mahabharata, the shared moral
  memory) and the sitar (the voice of Indian classical music).

## Provenance
