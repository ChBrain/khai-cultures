# Suriname: a khai culture

Suriname staged as a full khai play, ISO SR, language nl. The most plural nation
of the Americas staged as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its peoples as
the cast, the roles its languages and faiths encode, its places, its practices,
and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch. The prose body is written
in Dutch, Suriname's official language, with Sranan Tongo and the country's many
tongues woven through it; only the chapter headings stay English.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_suriname.md](play_suriname.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_nl.md](pitch_nl.md), written from Suriname's profile (warm,
  plural, polyglot, easygoing, religiously layered and tolerant, Dutch in form and
  Sranan in feeling) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** Traded for Manhattan (the Dutch colony kept while New
  Amsterdam went to the English in 1667, and the slave plantation), the Free Forest
  (Boni and the Maroons, and abolition in 1863), the Ships from India and Java
  (indenture and the building of the plural society), and the Many-Leaved Tree
  (independence in 1975, the diaspora, and Dobru's unity) - each casting the
  elements it needs, and each staging the gap between promise and reality soberly.
- **Personas:** Anton de Kom (the anti-colonial conscience), Boni (the Maroon war
  leader), Cynthia McLeod (the historical novelist), and Dobru (the poet of unity)
  as the historic cast, plus one defining archetype, the indentured labourer from
  India and Java. Each is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it
  holds, with Dutch as the written mother tongue, Sranan Tongo carried, and the
  Maroon, Sarnami, and Javanese tongues in their place.
- **Positions:** wan bon (one tree, many leaves, the radical plurality of the
  nation), the Maroon freedom (the free forest societies and African resistance),
  and the colonial wound (slavery, the plantation, and the Dutch tie).
- **Places:** Paramaribo (the Dutch wooden UNESCO old town and river capital where
  mosque stands beside synagogue) and the Interior (the rainforest of the Maroon
  and indigenous peoples).
- **Processes:** Keti Koti (the July 1 emancipation festival, the broken chains)
  and Phagwa (the Hindustani spring festival of colour shared across communities).
- **Pieces:** pom (the Creole baked tayer-and-chicken dish of the festive table)
  and the apinti (the Maroon talking drum that carries messages and ancestors).
- **Plan:** [plan_wan_bon.md](plan_wan_bon.md), the open mandate to keep the
  coexistence of the many peoples, develop without losing the forest, and hold
  homeland and diaspora together.

## Provenance

The cultural framing behind the pitch and the public-record historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The framing never enters the prose as
data; the pitch is its invisible hand. Sources on the public record are credited,
never claimed; slavery, the Maroon wars, and Asian indenture are treated soberly as
the gap between promise and reality, with no side taken and no atrocity detail; the
staging and the architecture are original work.
