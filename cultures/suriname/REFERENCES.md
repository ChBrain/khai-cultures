# Suriname: References

The provenance behind this culture: the cultural framing that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose as data) and the public-record historical sources
behind the plots, personas, places, pieces, and processes.

## Cultural framing

The pitch [pitch_nl.md](pitch_nl.md) is written from Suriname's cultural profile as
a warm, plural, polyglot, easygoing, religiously layered and tolerant society,
Dutch in form and Sranan in feeling. No dimension is named and no number is carried;
the framing is recorded here so it is on the record without entering the prose.
Suriname's self-understanding as wan bon, one tree with many leaves, and as the
most ethnically diverse nation of the Americas, is drawn from its own public record
and national imagery.

## Historical sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, the pieces, and the
processes are drawn from the public historical record. They are credited here and
never claimed; the staging is original work. The gap between promise and reality -
the Dutch colony and the slave plantation, the Maroon wars, and Asian indenture -
is staged soberly on the public record, with no side taken and no atrocity detail.

The historical novelist [Cynthia McLeod](persona_cynthia_mcleod.md) is credited for
her work "De vrije negerin Elisabeth" and her other historical novels, which bring
the colonial past to a wide readership. The one defining archetype,
[the indentured labourer](persona_de_contractarbeider.md), is not a historical
individual but a composite that carries the experience of the contract labourers
from India and Java who became a new leaf on the tree of the nation.
