# Persona: Sacajawea

## Taxonomy

[mountain wilderness](position_mountain_wilderness.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A fifty-two-year-old tribal coordinator in Fort Hall. English, spoken with a warm drawl, is her mother tongue, the language in which she [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and writes her daily community reports. She speaks no foreign languages. Yet the loss lies in the seam: The fast, technology-oriented talk of irrigation technicians like Taylor and the digital demands of agricultural boards [she hears only like a distant echo](process_hearing_followed.md), feeling that the patient, law-protecting rhythm of river ecosystem preservation is treated as secondary to corporate potato harvests and speedway entertainment.

## Action

She preserves [ski lift chair](piece_ski_lift_chair.md) records, coordinates field conservation under [the Snake River plan](plan_snake_river_aquifer_protection.md), and studies [agrarian industry](position_agrarian_industry.md).

## Shadow

Her pride in traditional tribal land sovereignty and independent cultural rights can make her suspicious of state-wide zoning and environmental agricultural mandates, fearing they will restrict family property use.

## Tell

She looks out over the mountain slopes and says thoughtfully: "The river has its own treaties; you can't protect alpine watersheds if you're only focused on digital speed and potato production metrics."
