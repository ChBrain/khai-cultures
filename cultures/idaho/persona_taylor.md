# Persona: Taylor

## Taxonomy

[agrarian industry](position_agrarian_industry.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A thirty-eight-year-old quality technician in Idaho Falls. English, spoken with a technical cadence, is his mother tongue, the language in which he [speaks, hears, and thinks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), the defining code of his daily mechanical checks. It is the [Idahoan English](position_language_en_us_id.md) of the canal yards, the clipped technical cadence of the working plant floor. He speaks no foreign languages. Still, there is a seam he feels every day: the slower, tradition-bound talk of tribal coordinators like Sacajawea and the quiet history of [mountain wilderness](position_mountain_wilderness.md) [he reads only as regional resource updates](process_reading_followed.md) on his workstation, while the patient, self-reliant resource preservation of early forest communities gets crowded out by the push for ever-higher potato yields. He holds [Idahoan culture](position_culture_idahoan_culture.md) from its newer end, the canal-built agrarian industry that turned the dry plain into farmland.

## Action

He preserves [potato sack](piece_potato_sack.md) relics, coordinates canal safety under [the Snake River plan](plan_snake_river_aquifer_protection.md), and studies [agrarian industry](position_agrarian_industry.md).

## Shadow

His intense focus on harvest metrics and commercial crop exports can make him indifferent to municipal forest zoning problems in northern wilderness counties.

## Tell

He adjusts a telemetry sensor on an irrigation canal gate and says directly: "We are calibrating high-pressure agricultural water flows; you can't run a profitable canal system if you're only focused on historic land preservation."
