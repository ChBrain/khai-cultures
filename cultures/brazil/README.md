# Brazil: a khai culture

Brazil staged as a full khai play, ISO BR, language pt. A continental nation made
of the meeting of three peoples, as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its
people as the cast, the roles its language and faith encode, its places, its
practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_brazil.md](play_brazil.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_pt.md](pitch_pt.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Brazil's
  profile (warm collectivism and the relationship over the rule, high power
  distance softened by cordiality, indulgence and the festa, and a present-leaning
  improvisational ease) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Meeting and the Colony, Independence and the Empire,
  Abolition and the Republic, and the Modern Giant - each casting the elements it
  needs, and each staging the gap between the warm story Brazil tells of itself and
  the unequal life it has lived honestly and soberly.
- **Personas:** Zumbi dos Palmares, Dom Pedro I, Getulio Vargas, and Pele as the
  historic cast, plus one defining archetype, o malandro (the charming rogue of the
  post-abolition morro, the jeitinho made flesh). Each is wired to the language
  engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** a miscigenacao (the meeting and mixing of the three peoples and
  the contested myth of racial democracy), the jeitinho (the warm improvised
  workaround and the cordial relational way), o pais do futuro (the continental
  promise forever arriving, grandeur and inequality together).
- **Places:** Rio de Janeiro (the Marvelous City, the former capital, Carnaval and
  the morros), the Amazon (the green giant, home of indigenous peoples, the
  planet's lungs and Brazil's frontier and responsibility).
- **Processes:** Carnival (the nation turned inside out in joy), football (the
  jogo bonito as national religion).
- **Pieces:** feijoada (the black-bean stew of slave origin become the national
  dish), the berimbau (the African bow of capoeira, resistance carried into play).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; slavery, the myth of racial democracy and its critique, the military
years, and inequality are treated soberly on the public record as the gap between
the promise and the reality; the staging and the architecture are original work.
