# Brazil: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_pt.md](pitch_pt.md) is written from Brazil's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record without
entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Brazil score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ---------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 69                     |
| Individualism                 | 38                     |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 49                     |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 76                     |
| Long Term Orientation         | 44                     |
| Indulgence                    | 59                     |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; Brazil's relatively high Power
Distance and Uncertainty Avoidance and its collectivist, indulgent leaning shape
the warm, relational, festive tone of the pitch.

## Historical sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn
from the public historical record. They are credited here and never claimed; the
staging is original work. The contradiction at the heart of the story - the
colony and the largest enslaved society of the Americas, the late abolition, the
myth of racial democracy and its critique, the military years, and the world's
deepest inequality - is staged soberly on the public record as the gap between the
promise and the reality.

The one defining persona, [o malandro](persona_o_malandro.md), is an archetype, not
a historical individual: a composite that carries the figure of the charming Rio
rogue of samba and survival, born of the post-abolition morros, the jeitinho made
flesh and the cost of an abolition without land.
