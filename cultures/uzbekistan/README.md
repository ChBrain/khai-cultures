# Uzbekistan: a khai culture

Uzbekistan staged as a full khai play, ISO UZ, language uz (modern Uzbek Latin
script). The heart of the Silk Road staged as a theatre: its history as the plot
line, its people as the cast, the roles its language, learning, and faith encode,
its cities, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_uzbekistan.md](play_uzbekistan.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_uz.md](pitch_uz.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Uzbekistan's profile (a community- and family-centred collectivism, deference
  to age and authority, restraint and patience, and a quiet pride in heritage)
  and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Silk Road and the jewelled cities, the golden age of
  learning, Amir Temur and the Timurid renaissance, and Navoiy and the flowering
  of the mother tongue, each casting the elements it needs.
- **Personas:** Amir Temur (the conqueror and patron), Mirzo Ulug'bek (the
  astronomer-king of Samarkand), Alisher Navoiy (the father of Uzbek literature),
  and Ibn Sino (Avicenna, the great scholar of medicine) as the historic cast,
  plus one defining archetype, Usta (the bazaar craftsman and mahalla elder who
  passes the handcraft down). Each is wired to the language engine by the
  language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** ilm (the deep reverence for learning that gave the world algebra
  and Avicenna), iymon (the inner faith of Islam and the Sufi tradition), mahalla
  (the neighborhood community and its hospitality).
- **Places:** Samarkand (the jewel of the Silk Road, the blue domes, the
  observatory) and Bukhara (the holy city, the centre of learning and Sufism).
- **Processes:** osh (the plov gathering that seats the community at one table)
  and Navruz (the spring new-year festival of renewal and forgiveness).
- **Pieces:** the suzani (the embroidered cloth that carries a family's hopes) and
  atlas (the ikat silk of the Silk Road, the rainbow festival cloth).
