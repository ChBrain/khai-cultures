# Botswana: a khai culture

Botswana staged as a full khai play, ISO BW, language tn (Setswana). A quiet
land held by botho and the kgotla, by cattle and the desert that holds water, as
a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its
language and values encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects,
all tuned by a single pitch. The prose bodies are written in Setswana, the
national language that unifies the nation; this README and REFERENCES are in
English, as the gold templates are.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_botswana.md](play_botswana.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_tn.md](pitch_tn.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Botswana's profile (a calm, consensus-seeking register of low assertiveness,
  respect for elders and the collective, and an unhurried preference for talking
  things through) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the San and the Kalahari, the tribes and the chiefs,
  independence and Seretse (1966), and diamonds and development - each casting
  the elements it needs, and each staged soberly and with dignity.
- **Personas:** Seretse Khama (the founding president), Khama III (a chief of
  note who led by the kgotla), Bessie Head (the writer who made her home in
  Serowe), and Ratsie Setlhako (the celebrated Setswana musician), plus one
  archetype, Modisa (the cattle-post herdsman). Each is wired to the language
  engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** botho (the Setswana value that a person is a person through
  other people), the kgotla (the assembly under the tree where the community
  decides by consensus, the root of Botswana's democratic tradition), and
  dikgomo (cattle as wealth and life).
- **Places:** Gaborone (the capital, the seat of self-government and
  development), the Okavango (the river that never reaches the sea, the inland
  water-world and its wildlife).
- **Processes:** going to the cattle-post (the moraka, where the herdsman learns
  duty and self-reliance), borankana (the Setswana song and dance that gathers
  the community).
- **Pieces:** seswaa (the pounded meat of the feast, shared by all), the mokoro
  (the dugout canoe poled through the Okavango).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the San first peoples, the founding, and the development story are
treated soberly and with dignity, taking no political side.
