# Position: the callaloo nation

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The proposition that the nation is not one people but a mix of all of them, simmered together like the dish that gives it its name: Afro-Trinidadian out of slavery and Indo-Trinidadian out of indenture beside each other, and Chinese, Syrian, Portuguese, European, and the first peoples folded in - one of the most plural societies of the Americas. Many races, many creeds, Hosay and Divali and Christmas all kept in one calendar, every tongue in one rumshop. And it is a plurality founded in the gap between promise and reality: the indentured were promised a free wage and given the barracks, the slaved were promised nothing and owed everything, and the callaloo simmered together over divisions of race the fete has never quite cooked away.

## Orders

That the nation belongs to no single race and to all of them, so Afro- and Indo-Trinidadian are equally Trini and the newcomer is folded into the pot; that the difference is the dish, the strength and the flavour of the place rather than a flaw to be smoothed out; and that the work of the republic is to keep the many simmering as one without letting the divisions under the picong boil over.

## Loses

Ground when the mix curdles into the divide - when race is played at the polls and the picong turns to grievance, when one community fears the other holds the state, and the callaloo separates back into its parts. And ground, too, to the dependence on the oil that paid for the plenty all the peoples share, for the pot holds best when there is enough in it for everyone.

## Drives

Lives in the historian of slavery who built the plural nation and named no race its master, and in the novelist who wrote the Indo-Trinidadian world of the callaloo into world letters and saw both its richness and its wounds. It drives Trinidad and Tobago to keep the many peoples one and the pot full enough to hold them.
