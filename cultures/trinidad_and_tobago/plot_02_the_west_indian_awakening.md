# Plot: the West Indian Awakening

## Taxonomy

[play_trinidad_and_tobago](play_trinidad_and_tobago.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

In the early 20th century a generation of West Indians schooled in the colony's classrooms begins to read its way out of the empire. In Port of Spain and in London, in the squares and the little magazines, minds like [C.L.R. James](persona_clr_james.md) take the coloniser's learning and turn it into the argument for the colony's own freedom.

## Action

James writes "The Black Jacobins," the history of the only successful slave revolt, and "Beyond a Boundary," reading a whole society through its cricket, and gives the region a mind that thinks the world. The intellectual ferment of the West Indian awakening runs through the squares and the unions and the labour unrest of the 1930s, and home from Oxford comes Eric Williams to teach the people their history in the open air. The argument carries the islands forward, and in 1962 Trinidad and Tobago takes its independence, the colony at last become a country that thinks for itself.

## Stage

- [C.L.R. James](persona_clr_james.md): the mind that reads the colony out of the empire and the Caribbean into history.
- [the West Indian mind](position_the_west_indian_mind.md): the intellectual and decolonizing tradition the awakening gives the islands.
- [Port of Spain](place_port_of_spain.md): the city of the squares and the schools where the ferment gathered and the road to independence ran.

## Tension

The minds that free the colony are made by the very learning meant to keep it down - the West Indian awakening claims the master's canon and turns it into the case for liberation. Yet the small place that produces them so often cannot hold them: the awakening that thinks the world for the islands is also the first turn of the brain drain that will carry the islands' best minds away.
