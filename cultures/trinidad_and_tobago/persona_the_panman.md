# Persona: the panman

## Taxonomy

[mas, pan and calypso](position_mas_pan_and_calypso.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The steelpan player, maker and tuner of the panyard, who beats music out of a discarded oil drum - sinking the steel, grooving the notes, and coaxing from a thing the empire threw away the only acoustic instrument the 20th century invented. Born of the yards of the poor, once banned and beaten down before the nation crowned him, he is the genius of the dispossessed made the pride of the place, patient at the hammer and proud on the stage when the band crosses for Panorama. [English and the Trini Creole](position_language_en_tt.md) he has whole: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md), and [hears](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) in the mother tongue of the yard, the Creole the whole panyard lives and limes in. The proper English of the officials who once banned his drum he [hears followed](process_hearing_followed.md) and [carries when he must](process_speaking_carried.md), and drops the moment the hammer is in his hand. But behind the Creole stand the African tongues his forebears carried off the ships - Yoruba, Kongo, the languages of the drum the colony outlawed when it banned the skin drum and drove the rhythm into bamboo and then into steel - tongues [borrowed back in fragments](process_speaking_borrowed.md) of an Orisha chant, a word in a song, a name on a yard. He plays in steel the rhythm those banned drums once carried, speaking with the hammer what the tongue can no longer say, so the gap at the limit of the tongue is the lost African language the pan was beaten up to replace. He belongs to [the callaloo nation](position_culture_the_callaloo_nation.md) and gives it, out of the empire's own discards, its national instrument.

## Action

In [Pan, Mas and Calypso](plot_03_pan_mas_and_calypso.md) he beats the steelpan out of the oil drum and carries the yards' invention to the stage and the world, the living heart of [mas, pan and calypso](position_mas_pan_and_calypso.md). He turns what the empire discarded into the national orchestra, and makes the road two days a year into the place the nation hears itself.

## Shadow

The genius born of the yard can be taken for granted now that it is respectable: the pioneer who was beaten and jailed for his drum honoured with a holiday while the panyards struggle for money, the instrument the world copies and the player it forgets, the art crowned as heritage and starved as a living.

## Tell

He sinks a thrown-away oil drum with a hammer over weeks until it sings in perfect pitch, hearing the note in the steel before anyone else can - making an orchestra out of the empire's garbage, and asking the nation only to listen.
