# Position: mas, pan and calypso

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The proposition that the soul of the nation is its Carnival and the arts it threw up: mas, the masquerade that takes the streets; pan, the steelpan beaten from oil drums in the yards, the only acoustic instrument the 20th century invented; calypso and its faster child soca, the song that carries the news, the scandal and the picong; and the picong itself, the sharp warm wit that spares no man. A people with nothing made an art out of everything, turning castoff oil drums into a national orchestra and the road into a stage two days a year. And it is an art born of the gap: pan came up out of the yards of the poor, banned and beaten down before it was crowned, the genius of the dispossessed made the pride of the nation.

## Orders

That the worth of the place is in what it makes and plays, not in what it owns; that the road belongs to the people two days a year and the band crossing the stage is the nation showing itself to itself; and that no man is too grand for the picong, the wit that levels everyone and lets the whole society be argued out in a sweet kaiso.

## Loses

Ground when the art is sold off its roots - the mas turned to bikini-and-beads for the tourist, the calypso's bite filed down for the radio, the pan's genius taken for granted now that it is respectable. And ground to the easy take that mistakes the fete for the whole of the place, feting through the hard truths the picong is meant to name.

## Drives

Lives in the [Mighty Sparrow](persona_mighty_sparrow.md), the king of calypso who put the nation's whole life into kaiso, and in [the panman](persona_the_panman.md), who beats and tunes the steelpan and carries the yards' invention to the world. It drives Trinidad and Tobago to keep turning trouble into tune and the road into the greatest fete in the hemisphere.
