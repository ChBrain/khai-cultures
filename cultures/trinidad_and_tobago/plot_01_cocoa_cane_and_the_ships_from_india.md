# Plot: Cocoa, Cane and the Ships from India

## Taxonomy

[play_trinidad_and_tobago](play_trinidad_and_tobago.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

The Taino and Kalinago hold the two islands when Columbus sights them in 1498. Spain claims Trinidad and barely settles it, planting a little cocoa; Tobago changes hands among the empires more often than any island in the Caribbean. In 1797 Britain takes Trinidad and turns both islands to sugar and slavery.

## Action

The British work the cane on the backs of the enslaved, and the cocoa rises beside it. When slavery is abolished in 1834 and the freed walk off the estates, the planters send the ships from India: between 1845 and 1917 a hundred and forty thousand indentured labourers cross the ocean to the cane, promised a free wage and a passage home and given instead the barracks, the long contract, and the gap between the promise and the reality. Out of slavery and indenture, and the Chinese, Syrian, Portuguese and European who come after, one of the most plural societies of the Americas begins to form - Afro- and Indo-Trinidadian beside each other, the callaloo set to simmer.

## Stage

- [the callaloo nation](position_the_callaloo_nation.md): the plural society first set simmering here out of slavery, indenture, and all the world sent.
- [Tobago](place_tobago.md): the old colonial island of the forts and the plantations, fought over by the empires and worked on cane.

## Tension

The islands are peopled by force and by false promise - the enslaved owned and the indentured tricked, the cane built on the gap between what was promised and what was given. Yet from that gap grows the callaloo: peoples who would never have met poured onto two islands and made, out of suffering and survival, the most plural society of the Americas, so the place begins in the wound and the wealth at once.
