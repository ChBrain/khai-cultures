# Piece: doubles

## Taxonomy

Parent group: pieces

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Place

On every corner and outside every fete, from the early-morning breakfast cart to the post-lime small hours: two soft fried flatbreads of bara folded around curried chickpea channa, dressed with cucumber, shadon beni and a pepper that is slight, medium or slap-yuh-down hot, wrapped in waxed paper and eaten on the foot. The national snack, sold by the doubles man wherever Trinis gather and hungry.

## Load Bearing

It stands for the Indo-Trinidadian table folded into the nation's everyday mouth - the food of the ships from India become the breakfast of every race and creed. Brought by the descendants of the indentured out of the channa and the curry of the ancestral kitchen, doubles crossed every line in the callaloo until the Afro-Trinidadian and the Indo-Trinidadian, the rich man and the worker, all queue at the same cart and order the same way. In a plural nation it is the snack that everyone shares without thinking, the indenture's contribution become the common table. Without this two-bara handful the callaloo would have no everyday food the whole nation eats as one.

## Apparent

Two thin discs of turmeric-yellow fried dough wrapped around a spoon of soft curried chickpeas, a slick of pepper and chutney, handed over hot in a twist of paper. Plain and cheap on the cart; its weight is in the spice the maker carries from the indentured kitchen and in the line of every kind of Trini waiting, dollar in hand, for the doubles man.

## Yearbook

- Spread from the Indo-Trinidadian table to the whole nation as the everyday snack of every race and creed.
- Crowned the national snack of Trinidad and Tobago, the dollar food eaten standing on every corner.
