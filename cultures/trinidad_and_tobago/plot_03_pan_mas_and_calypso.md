# Plot: Pan, Mas and Calypso

## Taxonomy

[play_trinidad_and_tobago](play_trinidad_and_tobago.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

In the yards of Laventille and Port of Spain in the 1930s and 1940s, after the colony has banned the skin drum, a poor and dispossessed people drive the rhythm into bamboo and then into steel. From discarded oil drums [the panman](persona_the_panman.md) beats out a new instrument, while in the calypso tents the singers sharpen the kaiso into the people's own newspaper.

## Action

The steelpan is born, the only acoustic instrument the 20th century invents, beaten up out of the very drums the island's wealth came in - banned and beaten down before the nation crowns it. Carnival fills the streets with mas and J'ouvert and pan, and the [Mighty Sparrow](persona_mighty_sparrow.md) crowns calypso, singing the whole life of the place - the politics, the scandal, the picong - into one sweet road march. And over it [Eric Williams](persona_eric_williams.md) builds the new nation, giving the callaloo a state and asking its genius to be the pride of one people. The yards' invention becomes the sound the country knows itself by.

## Stage

- [Eric Williams](persona_eric_williams.md): the historian who builds the new nation around its own genius and names no race its master.
- [the Mighty Sparrow](persona_mighty_sparrow.md): the king of calypso who makes the kaiso the voice and conscience of the young nation.
- [the panman](persona_the_panman.md): the maker and tuner who beats the steelpan out of the oil drum and carries it to the stage.

## Tension

The nation's clearest gift to the world comes up out of its poorest yards and is banned and beaten down before it is crowned - the genius of the dispossessed made the pride of the place. Yet once the pan is respectable the yards that made it can be left behind, and the calypso's bite filed down, so the country must keep faith with where its genius came from or lose it.
