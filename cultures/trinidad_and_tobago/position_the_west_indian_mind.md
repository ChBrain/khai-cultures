# Position: the West Indian mind

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The proposition that a small colonial place can think for itself and for the world: the intellectual and decolonizing tradition that ran out of two islands far beyond their size, the historians and writers and thinkers who took the tools of the empire and turned them on the empire. C.L.R. James writing the revolution of the enslaved into history, Eric Williams arguing that slavery built the wealth of Britain, a whole West Indian generation reading itself into being and reading the colony out of existence. And it is a mind formed in the gap: it had to claim a learning made to keep it down and prove the colonized could master the masters' own canon, then use it to demand the freedom the masters withheld.

## Orders

That the place is no backwater but a maker of ideas, owing the world no apology for its size; that the colonized may take the coloniser's learning and turn it into the argument for liberation; and that the work of the mind is to read the society honestly, history and all, and refuse the story the empire told about it.

## Loses

Ground when the gifted leave and do not return - the brain drain that sends the best minds to London and Toronto and keeps them, the small place forever exporting its genius; and ground to the anti-intellectual ease that prizes the fete over the thought and lets the hard reading go undone while the music plays.

## Drives

Lives in [C.L.R. James](persona_clr_james.md), who wrote "The Black Jacobins" and "Beyond a Boundary" and made the cricket pitch and the slave revolt both into history. It drives Trinidad and Tobago to keep thinking for itself and to keep producing minds the world must read.
