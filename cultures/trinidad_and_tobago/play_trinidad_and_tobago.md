# Play: Trinidad and Tobago

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the house that holds this staging and answers for it.

## Name

Trinidad and Tobago, a twin-island republic at the southern end of the Caribbean, off the coast of Venezuela where the Atlantic meets the Gulf of Paria. ISO TT, language en, English the official tongue with a Trinidadian Creole spoken in every yard and rumshop. The larger Trinidad and the smaller, quieter Tobago bound into one nation: small in land, vast in noise, the richest of the Caribbean and the loudest, a callaloo of all the peoples the empire and the world poured onto two islands.

## Arc

Two islands the Taino and Kalinago held first, that Columbus sighted in 1498 and Spain claimed and barely settled, planting cocoa and a little cane. The British took Trinidad in 1797 and worked it on sugar and slavery, and when slavery ended they sent the ships from India, indentured labourers by the tens of thousands, so that the gap between the promise of a free wage and the reality of the barracks runs through the whole story as the gap between the words and the deed. From slavery and indenture and the rest the world sent - Chinese, Syrian, Portuguese, European - grew one of the most plural societies of the Americas, Afro- and Indo-Trinidadian beside each other, the callaloo mix. Oil came up under it, and then gas, and the twin islands grew rich beyond any of their neighbours, while in the yards of Port of Spain a people with nothing beat the only acoustic instrument invented in the 20th century out of discarded oil drums - the steelpan - and made calypso and soca and the picong, the sharp warm wit that spares no one. Carnival took over the streets, mas and J'ouvert and pan, the greatest fete in the hemisphere, and a small place sent up a great intellectual tradition - C.L.R. James, Eric Williams, V.S. Naipaul - and kept Hosay and Divali and a hundred tongues all going at once. Under it all runs one nerve: a fast, witty, lyrical people who fete over every division and ask whether the genius can outlast the oil.

## Stakes

Whether a plural, oil-rich twin-island nation can hold its many peoples as one - Afro- and Indo-Trinidadian and all the rest the world sent - across the real divisions of race the fete papers over, can diversify beyond the oil and gas that made it rich before they run dry, and can keep turning its genius into Carnival, pan and letters; or whether the dependence on the oil and the divisions under the picong finally pull the callaloo apart.
