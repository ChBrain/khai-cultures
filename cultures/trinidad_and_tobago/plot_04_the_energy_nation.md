# Plot: the Energy Nation

## Taxonomy

[play_trinidad_and_tobago](play_trinidad_and_tobago.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

Oil comes up under the southern island and then natural gas in great quantity, and the twin republic grows rich beyond any of its Caribbean neighbours. The refineries and the rigs hum, the petrodollars flow, and a small place finds itself the energy nation, its plenty and its politics both running on what comes out of the ground.

## Action

The oil-and-gas wealth remakes the islands - the roads, the schools, the boom years and the busts that follow the price of crude. [V.S. Naipaul](persona_vs_naipaul.md), grandson of the indentured, writes the Indo-Trinidadian world into world letters and wins the Nobel, holding the cosmopolitan present up to the cold light. Divali lights its thousands of deyas and Hosay drums through the streets, the festivals of the ships from India kept as the whole nation's own, and the callaloo carries on - plural, oil-rich, and cosmopolitan - while the standing question grows louder: what becomes of the energy nation when the energy runs dry.

## Stage

- [V.S. Naipaul](persona_vs_naipaul.md): the laureate who writes the Indo-Trinidadian world into world letters and holds the present to the light.
- [Tobago](place_tobago.md): the slower, greener counterweight to the oil-rich main island, the reef beside the rigs.
- [the callaloo nation](position_the_callaloo_nation.md): the plural, cosmopolitan society the energy wealth feeds and the festivals keep.

## Tension

The oil and gas that made the twin islands the richest of the Caribbean is the very thing that cannot last - the plenty all the peoples share runs on a ground that will run dry, and the divisions the picong covers are played hardest when the question is who holds the wealth. The energy nation must turn its genius into something that outlasts the crude, or watch the callaloo strain when the boom is gone.
