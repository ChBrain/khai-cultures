# Trinidad and Tobago: a khai culture

Trinidad and Tobago staged as a full khai play, ISO TT, language en. A plural,
oil-rich twin-island nation peopled by slavery, indenture and all the world sent,
as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its
language and faith encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects,
all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_trinidad_and_tobago.md](play_trinidad_and_tobago.md), the
  culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Trinidad and Tobago's profile (a warm collectivism, a relaxed ease with
  authority and time, an indulgent, festive present, and a verbal, irreverent
  picong wit) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** Cocoa, Cane and the Ships from India; the West Indian
  Awakening; Pan, Mas and Calypso; and the Energy Nation - each casting the
  elements it needs, and each staging the gap between the promise and the reality
  honestly.
- **Personas:** Eric Williams, C.L.R. James, the Mighty Sparrow and V.S. Naipaul
  as the historic cast, plus one defining archetype, the panman (the maker and
  tuner of the steelpan, the genius of the yards). Each is wired to the language
  engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** the callaloo nation (the Afro-Indo plural, cosmopolitan
  society), mas, pan and calypso (Carnival and its arts, the steelpan, the kaiso
  and the picong), the West Indian mind (the intellectual and decolonizing
  tradition).
- **Places:** Port of Spain (the capital, the Savannah, the beating heart of
  Carnival and the panyards), Tobago (the quieter sister island, the reef and the
  old colonial forts).
- **Processes:** Carnival (the great pre-Lenten fete of mas, J'ouvert, pan and
  soca), Divali (the Hindu festival of lights kept as the whole plural nation's
  own).
- **Pieces:** the steelpan (the national instrument, beaten from oil drums in the
  yards), doubles (the curried-channa street food of the Indo-Trinidadian table,
  become the national snack).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-record sources are recorded in
[REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the pitch is its
invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never claimed; slavery,
Indian indenture, and the divisions of race are treated soberly on the public
record as the gap between promise and reality; the staging and the architecture
are original work.
