# Process: Divali

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

The Hindu households of the nation in the dark of the autumn moon, and the whole country with them: the families lighting the deyas, the little clay lamps set in their thousands along walls and roads and yards, the sweet-hands making barfi and kurma, the temples and the great Divali Nagar at Chaguanas glowing. It takes only a deya and the dark, and a nation that came from the ships from India lights up the night.

## Direction

Toward light and belonging: the Hindu festival of lights, among the largest celebrations outside India, kept by the Indo-Trinidadian descendants of the indentured and marked as a national holiday by the whole plural society. The lamps that mark the triumph of light over dark become the sign that the people of the ships from India are no guests but founders, their festival the nation's own, the callaloo lit up by every flame.

## Lever

It is driven by the need of the callaloo to honour all its peoples and creeds, the festival of the indentured raised to a day the whole nation keeps. It holds against the long memory of the barracks and the gap between the wage promised and given; against it stand the divisions of race that politics still plays, and the strain of keeping every community's festival truly the whole nation's own.

## Echo

Divali makes visible what [the callaloo nation](position_culture_the_callaloo_nation.md) needs: a way for one of its founding peoples to shine and the rest to share the light. By these deyas the world of [V.S. Naipaul](persona_vs_naipaul.md) keeps its faith and its India, the temple and the lamps the ancestral home he carried in his name.
