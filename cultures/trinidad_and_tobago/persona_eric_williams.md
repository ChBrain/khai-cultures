# Persona: Eric Williams

## Taxonomy

[the callaloo nation](position_culture_the_callaloo_nation.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The Oxford-trained historian of "Capitalism and Slavery," who argued that the wealth of the empire was built on the backs of the enslaved, then came home to make himself the father of the nation - the first Prime Minister, who led the twin islands out of the empire to independence in 1962 and held the people's school in the Woodford Square he called the University of Woodford Square. Brilliant, austere, commanding, and proud, he gave the callaloo a state and asked it to be one people. [English](position_language_en_tt.md) he has whole and weaponised: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in the mother tongue of the empire that schooled him, and he [drafts](process_writing_drafted.md) it - the lectures, the history, the case for independence - into argument the colonial office could not answer. The [Latin](../holy_see/position_language_la.md) and the high learning of Oxford he [carries almost as his own](process_speaking_carried.md) and [reads its archives deeply](process_reading_followed.md), and turns the masters' own scholarship into the indictment of the masters. He proved the colony could out-read the coloniser and wrote the empire's crime in the empire's own English, then stood in a square and taught a whole people their history in it - yet the African tongues his enslaved forebears carried off the ships were broken in the barracks and never written down, so the gap at the limit of the tongue is the silence behind his own perfect English.

## Action

In [Pan, Mas and Calypso](plot_03_pan_mas_and_calypso.md) he builds the new nation around its own genius, giving the callaloo a state and naming no race its master, the historian of slavery become the maker of [the callaloo nation](position_culture_the_callaloo_nation.md). By taking the islands to independence he turns the colony into a country and asks the many peoples to hold together as one.

## Shadow

The father who frees the nation can father it too closely: the one mind that taught the people their history can come to think it the only mind, the brilliance that founded the state hardening into the rule that brooks no rival, and the plural nation he made can be played race against race by the very politics he built to hold it.

## Tell

He stands in an open square and lectures the whole island on the history of slavery as if a country were a classroom - and means it, handing a people their own past as the first thing a free nation must own.
