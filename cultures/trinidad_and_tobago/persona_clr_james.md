# Persona: C.L.R. James

## Taxonomy

[the West Indian mind](position_the_west_indian_mind.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The towering West Indian intellectual who wrote "The Black Jacobins," the history of the only successful slave revolt, and "Beyond a Boundary," the book that made cricket the lens on a whole society and asked what they know of cricket who only cricket know. Historian, Marxist, cricketer, and Pan-Africanist, he read the Caribbean into world history and gave the decolonizing generation its mind. Restless, omnivorous, and exact, he carried the colony's learning to London and turned it back on the empire. [English](position_language_en_tt.md) he has whole and remade: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in the mother tongue the colonial school drilled into him, and he [reads its whole canon deeply](process_reading_followed.md), from Thackeray to Hegel, having [read his way through the masters](process_reading_deciphered.md) line by line in a Tunapuna boyhood. He took the literature meant to make him a good colonial and [carried it as his own](process_speaking_carried.md), then wrote the revolution of the enslaved into the very language and form of the empire that enslaved them - making the masters' English carry the slaves' uprising and the colony's claim - so that the gap at the limit of the tongue is the one he crossed by mastering the master's words and turning them around. He belongs to [the callaloo nation](position_culture_the_callaloo_nation.md) and gives it the mind to read itself into world history.

## Action

In [the West Indian Awakening](plot_02_the_west_indian_awakening.md) he gives the islands [the West Indian mind](position_the_west_indian_mind.md), reading the colony out of the empire and the Caribbean into history, and lighting the intellectual ferment that carries the region toward independence. He makes the small place a maker of ideas and proves the colonized can think the world.

## Shadow

The mind that can read the whole world can be read by his own island least of all: the prophet honoured abroad and half a stranger at home, the towering thinker the place produced and could not hold, his books taught in distant universities while the brain drain he embodied carries the next generation of minds away after him.

## Tell

He measures a society not by its rulers but by the way it plays and watches cricket, and asks what they know of the game who only the game know - turning a colonial pastime into the sharpest reading of the colony itself.
