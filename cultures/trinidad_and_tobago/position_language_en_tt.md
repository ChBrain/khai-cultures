# Position: the Trinidadian tongue

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The tongue the nation has whole and made its own: the English the empire schooled it in, and the Trinidadian Creole spoken in every yard and rumshop, the two bent together into one instrument. It is the official standard of the law, the school and the letters, and it is the Creole of the road that carries the picong, the calypso and the lime. Carried in on the ships and remade in the mouth of the callaloo, it is the empire's English turned to carry the slaves' uprising and the colony's claim, the standard and the dialect sliding from one to the other in a single line - the one tongue in which Afro- and Indo-Trinidadian, and all the rest the world sent, can lime and argue and sing as one people.

## Orders

That the nation own the tongue it was given rather than beg it, speaking the standard where the world must read it and the Creole where the yard lives; that the letters and the calypso both count as the voice of the place; and that the dialect no school will teach and no dictionary will hold be honoured as the language the nation actually sings in, not scorned as broken English.

## Loses

Ground when the standard is made to despise the Creole, when the dialect of the road is called bad English and driven out of the school and the page; and ground, too, in the silence behind the perfect English - the African and Bhojpuri tongues the forebears carried off the ships, broken in the barracks and thinned in a single generation, that the mastered English can never quite carry back.

## Drives

Lives in [C.L.R. James](persona_clr_james.md) and [Eric Williams](persona_eric_williams.md), who mastered the master's English and turned it around to write the enslaved into world history and the colony out of the empire; in [V.S. Naipaul](persona_vs_naipaul.md), who honed it to a blade and won the callaloo a place in world letters; and in [the Mighty Sparrow](persona_mighty_sparrow.md) and [the panman](persona_the_panman.md), who play the standard and the Trini Creole as one and make the dialect of the yard a national instrument. It drives Trinidad and Tobago to hold its many peoples in one tongue and to sing itself in the language it actually speaks.
