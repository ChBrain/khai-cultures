# Place: Port of Spain

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Shown

The capital on the Gulf of Paria, the beating heart of the nation and of Carnival: the Queen's Park Savannah, the great open green where the bands cross the stage and the city limes; the panyards in the hills of Laventille where the steelpan was born; the Woodford Square that Eric Williams made the people's university; the Red House and the cathedral and the markets, and the whole town turned over to mas, pan and J'ouvert when Carnival comes. The callaloo at its loudest, every race and creed in one street.

## Holds

- [the West Indian Awakening](plot_02_the_west_indian_awakening.md): the city of the squares and the schools where the intellectual ferment gathered and the road to independence ran.
- [Pan, Mas and Calypso](plot_03_pan_mas_and_calypso.md): the home of the panyards, the calypso tents, and the Savannah stage, where the steelpan was beaten up and Carnival takes the streets.

## Offers

A place where the nation gathers and shows itself to itself: the Savannah where the bands parade and the people lime, the panyards where the music is made, the squares where a free people argued itself into being and where, two days a year, the road belongs to the masqueraders and the whole callaloo crosses the stage as one.

## Withheld

The ease of a city only of fete. Port of Spain carries the gap behind the carnival - the Laventille hills that gave the world the pan and got little back, the crime and the hard yards behind the bright Savannah, the divisions the picong covers and the politics that played them - and reminds the nation that the genius came up out of the poor and is owed more than a holiday.
