# Place: Tobago

## Taxonomy

## Owner

## Shown

The quieter sister island across the channel from Trinidad, the green and slower half of the nation: the reef and the calm water of Buccoo, the rainforest of the Main Ridge, the old colonial forts on the heights where the flags of half a dozen empires once flew. Fishing villages, the goat races, the fete a little gentler than the city's. The island that changed hands more often than any in the Caribbean and settled at last beside Trinidad into one republic.

## Holds

- [the Energy Nation](plot_04_the_energy_nation.md): the slower, greener counterweight to the oil-rich main island, the tourism and the reef beside the rigs and the refineries.

## Offers

A place where the nation slows and remembers it is two islands, not one: the reef and the forest and the old forts, the gentler pace and the sea, the counterweight to Port of Spain's noise. Tobago offers the twin republic its quiet half, the green island where the callaloo catches its breath.

## Withheld

The equal footing the union promised. Tobago carries the gap of the smaller sister - the island that often feels ruled from Trinidad and overlooked in the sharing of the oil wealth, the forts that recall how many masters it bore, and the long argument over how much it should govern itself - and reminds the nation that holding two islands as one is a promise still being kept.
