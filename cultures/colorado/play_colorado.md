## Name

## Arc

A land of rugged elevation and conservation values, defined by the spine of the Continental Divide. From the frantic prospectors of the Pike's Peak Gold Rush and the preservation of alpine wilderness, to the post-war development of the mountain ski industry by military veterans. A culture that balances the outdoor lifestyle of its high-altitude peaks with the technological innovation of the Front Range cities, even as it manages complex water allocation agreements along the Colorado River.

**[Plot 1: Pike's Peak Gold Rush 1859](plot_01_gold_rush.md)**

Prospectors discover gold in the Rocky Mountains, drawing over a hundred thousand settlers and establishing mining camps that would grow into Front Range cities.

**[Plot 2: Rocky Mountain National Park Act 1915](plot_02_national_park.md)**

President Woodrow Wilson signs the bill establishing Rocky Mountain National Park, protecting alpine peaks and glaciers from commercial logging and development.

**[Plot 3: The Ski Industry Boom 1946](plot_03_ski_industry.md)**

Veterans of the 10th Mountain Division return from WWII to develop Aspen and Vail, transforming mountain wilderness into a premier global ski destination.
