# Connecticut: a khai culture

Connecticut staged as a full khai play, ISO US-CT, language en. The Constitution State: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its Fundamental Orders, brass and clock manufacturing, and submarine base expansion encodes, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house that holds this culture and answers for it.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_connecticut.md](play_connecticut.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_connecticut.md](pitch_connecticut.md), the Connecticut key.
- **Plots (history):** the Fundamental Orders of 1639, the Clock and Brass Manufacturing Boom of the 1830s, and the Groton Submarine Base Expansion of 1916.
- **Personas:** Charles (shipyard inspector) and Abigail (financial analyst), written in English.
- **Positions:** constitutional order (rule of law/institutional heritage), industrial precision (machining excellence/manufacturing pride).
- **Places:** Yale Quadrangle, Groton Shipyard.
- **Processes:** clock assembly (timekeeper calibration), marine inspection (submarine hull auditing).
- **Pieces:** a brass clock, a Yale charter.
- **Plan:** the Long Island Sound plan.
