# Persona: Ebenezer Joshua

## Taxonomy

[colony to nation](position_colony_to_nation.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The labour leader and the people's champion, the union man and orator who gave the worker and the small farmer a voice in the colony and pressed the crown for the vote and a say in their own affairs: the firebrand who carried the people's cause up the long road to nationhood. Fiery, plainspoken, and tireless for the working man, he stood on the platform and at the picket and made the labourer's grievance into a politics. English is his whole mother tongue, the English of the Caribbean turned to the people's purpose: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in it, and on the stump he drops without thought into the warm Vincy dialect of the cane field and the market, the tongue the worker answers to. The crown's English of statute and dispatch he [read and followed](process_reading_followed.md) closely enough to turn the colony's own rules against it, and the borrowed grammar of the wider labour movement he [carried](process_speaking_carried.md) into island speech. He spoke for a people in the very tongue of the empire that ruled them, bending the master's English into the worker's weapon and the dialect into the people's own, so that the gap at the limit of the tongue was the one he closed each time the platform crowd answered him back in the speech of home.

## Action

In [the Road to Independence](plot_03_the_road_to_independence.md) he gives the worker and the small farmer their voice and drives the colony up the road of labour toward self-rule, making [colony to nation](position_colony_to_nation.md) a thing the people themselves demand. By turning grievance into politics he opens the way the nation will walk to its flag.

## Shadow

The champion of the people can be remembered as a name on a road and forgotten as a cause: the labour won taken for granted by those who inherit it, the firebrand reduced to a figure in the textbook while the worker he fought for is left out of the story of the nation he helped to make.

## Tell

He stands before a crowd of cane workers and market women and gives their grievance back to them as a politics, in the dialect they speak at home and the English the crown must answer, and the crowd roars because at last someone is speaking for them.
