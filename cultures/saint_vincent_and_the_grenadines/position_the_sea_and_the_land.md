# Position: the sea and the land

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The two halves of one same home: the green volcanic mainland and the scatter-out islands of the Grenadines strung out across the sea. On the mainland the land is the living, the rich black soil under La Soufriere where the farmer grow arrowroot and bananas on the volcano slope, blessed and dangerous the same time. Down south run the little islands, Bequia and the rest, where it have little land and the sea is the living instead, the road and the trade and the harvest, worked by sailormen and boatbuilders and the old whalers in their open boats. It hold the truth that this is one nation make of two ways of living, the grower under the volcano and the sea-man among the islands, split by the water that does tie them too.

## Orders

That the nation is one home make of mainland and islands, the land and the sea each one a way of living the country need whole; that the farmer under the volcano and the sailor among the Grenadines is every bit as Vincentian one as the other, the scatter-out hold together as one; and that the sea is not just the divide between the islands but the road that tie them, to be sail and not fear.

## Loses

Ground when the water divide more than it tie, when the little islands feel the mainland forget them or the mainland look right past the islands, and the one nation start to fray along the sea between them; and ground, too, when the old ways of the land and the sea, the arrowroot, the boatbuilding, the sail, get let go with nothing to take their place, and a way of living lost.

## Drives

Lives in the Bequia whaler, the sailor of the Grenadines for who the sea is the living and the road, and in the arrowroot farmer, the mainland grower who work the rich slope under the volcano. It drive Saint Vincent and the Grenadines to hold its land and its sea, its mainland and its islands, together as one home.
