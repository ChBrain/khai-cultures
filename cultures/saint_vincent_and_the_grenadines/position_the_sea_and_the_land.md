# Position: the sea and the land

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The two halves of a single home: the green volcanic mainland and the scattered isles of the Grenadines strung out across the sea. On the mainland the land is the living, the rich black soil under La Soufriere where the farmer grows arrowroot and bananas on the volcano's slopes, blessed and dangerous at once. South run the small isles, Bequia and the rest, where there is little land and the sea is the living instead, the road and the trade and the harvest, worked by sailors and boatbuilders and the old whalers in their open boats. It holds the truth that this is one nation made of two ways of living, the grower under the volcano and the seafarer among the isles, divided by the water that also binds them.

## Orders

That the nation is one home made of mainland and isles, the land and the sea each a way of living that the country needs whole; that the farmer under the volcano and the sailor among the Grenadines are equally Vincentian, the scattered held together as one; and that the sea is not only the divide between the isles but the road that ties them, to be sailed and not feared.

## Loses

Ground when the water divides more than it binds, when the small isles feel forgotten by the mainland or the mainland looks past the isles, and the one nation frays along the sea between them; and ground, too, when the old ways of the land and the sea, the arrowroot, the boatbuilding, the sail, are let go without anything to take their place, and a way of living is lost.

## Drives

Lives in [the Bequia whaler](persona_the_bequia_whaler.md), the sailor of the Grenadines for whom the sea is the living and the road, and in [the arrowroot farmer](persona_the_arrowroot_farmer.md), the mainland grower who works the rich slopes under the volcano. It drives Saint Vincent and the Grenadines to hold its land and its sea, its mainland and its isles, together as one home.
