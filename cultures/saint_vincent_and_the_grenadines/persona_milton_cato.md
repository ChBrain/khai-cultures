# Persona: Milton Cato

## Taxonomy

[colony to nation](position_colony_to_nation.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

Robert Milton Cato, the father of independence and the nation's first Prime Minister, the lawyer and statesman who carried Saint Vincent and the Grenadines across from colony to sovereign nation in 1979: the steady hand who took the people's long climb and made it a flag. Measured, lawyerly, and constitutional where the firebrand was fiery, he served in war, came home to the law and to politics, and led the country through self-rule to the day of independence. English is his whole mother tongue, the formal English of the court and the constitution: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in it, and he [writes](process_writing_mother_tongue.md) it with the precision of a man who built a nation out of clauses and statutes. The legal Latin and the inherited law of the Westminster mould he [read and followed](process_reading_followed.md) deeply, and turned the master's own constitutional language into the instrument of the colony's release. He drew up a free nation in the very tongue and the very legal forms of the empire it was leaving, making the coloniser's English carry the country's sovereignty, so that the gap at the limit of the tongue closed into a paradox: the nation declared itself free in the only tongue it had, the one the empire had left behind.

## Action

In [the Road to Independence](plot_03_the_road_to_independence.md) he carries the country across the threshold to nationhood, taking the long labour and the people's climb and setting them into a constitution and a flag in 1979, the keystone of [colony to nation](position_colony_to_nation.md). By turning the struggle into a state he gives the nation the steady forms it needs to stand on its own.

## Shadow

The father of the nation can be honoured and the nation's charge forgotten: independence treated as a finished thing, the flag raised and the hard work of standing alone left half done, the founder remembered while the country he founded leans where it should stand.

## Tell

He takes the firebrand's cause and the people's long climb and sets them down, clause by careful clause, into the document by which a small scattered colony becomes a nation in its own right.
