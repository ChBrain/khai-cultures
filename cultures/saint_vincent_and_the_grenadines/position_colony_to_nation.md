# Position: colony to nation

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The long road the island walked from a worked colony to a nation of its own. After the Garifuna were broken, the land was held for the empire and worked, soberly, by the labour of the enslaved and then of the freed, for sugar, for arrowroot, for the bananas of the small farmer. Out of that labour rose the people's champions, the union men and the politicians who spoke for the worker and the grower and pressed the crown for a voice, a vote, and at last a flag. It holds the slow climb through self-rule to the independence won in 1979, and the standing question of every small new nation: how to stand on its own, hold its people, and make a living in the world.

## Orders

That the nation was won, not given, climbed to up the long road of labour and the ballot by the champions of the worker and the small farmer; that the freed people who came up that hard road are owed their place in the country's story alongside its first free people; and that independence is a charge to be answered, the work of making a small scattered country stand on its own and hold its own.

## Loses

Ground when the road is taken for granted, the struggle of the labour men and the long climb to the flag forgotten by those who inherit the nation they won; and ground, too, when the small nation cannot make the living independence demands, when the young must leave to find work and the country leans too hard on others to stand.

## Drives

Lives in [Ebenezer Joshua](persona_ebenezer_joshua.md), the labour leader and people's champion who gave the worker a voice, and in [Milton Cato](persona_milton_cato.md), the father of independence and the nation's first Prime Minister, who carried the country across to nationhood in 1979. It drives Saint Vincent and the Grenadines to honour the road it walked and to answer the charge of standing on its own.
