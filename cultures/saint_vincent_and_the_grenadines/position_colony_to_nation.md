## Has

The long road the island walk from a worked colony to a nation of its own. After the Garifuna get broke up, the land was held for the empire and worked, soberly, by the labour of the enslaved and then of the freed, for sugar, for arrowroot, for the bananas of the small farmer. Out of that same labour the people's champions rise up, the union men and the politicians who talk for the worker and the grower and press the crown for a voice, a vote, and at last a flag. It hold the slow climb through self-rule to the independence win in 1979, and the standing question of every small new nation: how to stand on its own, hold its people, and make a living in the world.

## Orders

That the nation was won, not give, climb up to along the long road of labour and the ballot by the champions of the worker and the small farmer; that the freed people who come up that hard road is owe their place in the country story right alongside its first free people; and that independence is a charge to be answer, the work of making a small scatter-out country stand on its own and hold its own.

## Loses

Ground when the road get take for granted, the struggle of the labour men and the long climb to the flag forget by them that inherit the nation the labour men win; and ground, too, when the small nation cannot make the living independence demand, when the young ones have to leave to find work and the country lean too hard on others to stand.
