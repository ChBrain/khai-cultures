# Process: Nine Mornings

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

The whole nation in the nine days before Christmas, rising in the dark to gather before dawn: the people of the towns and villages out in the cool pre-dawn streets, the bands and the carollers, the sea baths and the early services, the bicycle rides and the dancing, all in the hours before the sun. It takes only the village and the dark of an early morning, and a festivity found nowhere else on earth wakes the whole country before light, nine mornings running.

## Direction

Toward joy and belonging in the deep of the Christmas season: a uniquely Vincentian rite, the pre-dawn carols and revels of the nine days before Christmas, when the nation gathers in the dark to sing, bathe, ride, and worship as one. It binds the village and the country in a shared waking, the warmth and the faith of a small people made visible in the streets before sunrise, a custom the nation claims as its own and shares with no one.

## Lever

It is driven by the devotion and the neighbourliness of a small-island people and by the long habit of gathering as a village, the church and the street made one in the dark before Christmas. It holds against the scattering of families and the pull of the wider world's plainer Christmas; against it stand the drift of custom and the risk that a thing found nowhere else is let fade into the ordinary holiday everyone else keeps.

## Echo

Nine Mornings makes audible what [colony to nation](position_colony_to_nation.md) holds dear: a custom that is the nation's own and no one else's, a self-made tradition a small people claim with pride as proof of who they are. Through these dark streets [the arrowroot farmer](persona_the_arrowroot_farmer.md) walks before dawn with her village, the whole country waking as one in the cool before light.
