# Saint Vincent and the Grenadines: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md) is written from Saint Vincent and the
Grenadines' cultural profile and names no dimension and carries no number. Hofstede
Insights does not publish a full six-dimension score for Saint Vincent and the
Grenadines on its own; the pitch is written from the broadly shared profile of the
small-island anglophone Caribbean (a warm, collectivist, family- and
community-centred culture of strong faith, easy expressiveness, and resilient good
humour), so that the data informs the tone without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Caribbean small-island profile (qualitative) |
| ----------------------------- | -------------------------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | moderate to high                             |
| Individualism                 | low (collectivist, family and community)     |
| Motivation toward Achievement | moderate                                     |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | moderate                                     |
| Long Term Orientation         | low to moderate                              |
| Indulgence                    | high (warm, expressive, festive)             |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group (the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Where no national score is published for
Saint Vincent and the Grenadines, the profile above is qualitative and drawn from
the model as applied to the small-island anglophone Caribbean; it is approximate and
indicative, recorded here so the data is on the record without entering the prose.

## Historical sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn
from the public historical record. They are credited here and never claimed; the
staging is original work. The deep wound of the story - the Garifuna (Black Carib)
resistance, the Carib Wars, and the 1797 deportation that seeded the Garifuna
diaspora of Central America, together with slavery on the island - is staged
soberly on the public record, from within the nation's own self-understanding and
taking no side, with no atrocity detail.

The two defining personas, [the Bequia whaler](persona_the_bequia_whaler.md) and
[the arrowroot farmer](persona_the_arrowroot_farmer.md), are archetypes, not
historical individuals: composites that carry the tension between the seafaring life
of the Grenadines (the boat, the sail, the old whaling tradition, the sea as the
road) and the grower's life on the mainland (the volcanic soil, the arrowroot and
the banana, the land that blesses and burns).
