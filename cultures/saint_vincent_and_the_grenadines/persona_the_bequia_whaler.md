# Persona: the Bequia whaler

## Taxonomy

[the sea and the land](position_the_sea_and_the_land.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A sailor of Bequia, the small Grenadine isle of seafarers, a man of the open boat who carries the island's old whaling and sailing heritage in his hands and his eye, born to a place where there is little land and the sea is the living. Weathered, patient, and skilled past telling, he reads the water and the weather, builds and sails his boat, and keeps a tradition handed down through generations of Grenadine seamen. English is his whole mother tongue, the salt English of the Grenadines: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in it, and on the water it thickens into the close Bequia dialect of the boat crews, quick with the names of winds and gear no landsman knows. The sailor's words of distant crews and far ports he [borrowed](process_speaking_borrowed.md) over a lifetime of voyages, and the charts and the old logs of the sea he [deciphered](process_reading_deciphered.md) by long practice more than by school. He learned his deepest knowing in no tongue at all, in the feel of the swell and the set of the sail that no word holds, so that the gap at the limit of the tongue is where his real skill lives, in the wordless reading of the sea that language can name but never teach.

## Action

In [the Volcano and the Isles](plot_04_the_volcano_and_the_isles.md) he carries the sea-life of the Grenadines, the sailor and boatbuilder for whom the water is the road and the harvest, and he is the living face of [the sea and the land](position_the_sea_and_the_land.md) on the isles. He holds the scattered country together by sailing between its isles, making the sea the thread that binds the one nation.

## Shadow

The old skill can pass with the old man: the boatbuilding and the sail and the hard sea-knowledge let go as the young leave for easier work and the engine replaces the rig, until the heritage of the isle survives only as a model on a shelf and the sea is no longer a way of living but a view.

## Tell

He can read a squall an hour off and raise his sail to meet it, and could no more put into words how he knows than he could teach the sea to a man who has never left the land.
