# Persona: the Bequia whaler

## Taxonomy

[the sea and the land](position_the_sea_and_the_land.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A sailorman of Bequia, the little Grenadine island of sea people, a man of the open boat who carry the island old whaling and sailing heritage in his two hands and his eye, born to a place where it have little land and the sea is the living. Weather-beaten, patient, and skilled past any telling, he read the water and the weather, build and sail his own boat, and keep a tradition hand down through generation after generation of Grenadine seamen. English is his whole mother tongue, the salt English of the Grenadines: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in it, and out on the water it thicken up into the close Bequia talk of the boat crews, quick with the names of winds and gear no landsman know, [the Vincentian tongue](position_language_en_vc.md) his whole mother tongue in its saltest Grenadine register. The sailor words of far-off crews and far ports he [borrowed](process_speaking_borrowed.md) over a whole life of voyaging, and the charts and the old logs of the sea he [deciphered](process_reading_deciphered.md) more by long practice than by any school. He learn his deepest knowing in no tongue at all, in the feel of the swell and the set of the sail that no word can hold, so that the gap at the limit of the tongue is the very place his real skill living, in the wordless reading of the sea that language can name but never teach. He belong to [Vincy](position_culture_vincy.md) as the half of the culture that hold the sea, tying the scatter-out islands to the mainland by sail and by patience.

## Action

In [the Volcano and the Isles](plot_04_the_volcano_and_the_isles.md) he carry the sea-life of the Grenadines, the sailor and boatbuilder for who the water is the road and the harvest, and he is the living face of [the sea and the land](position_the_sea_and_the_land.md) on the islands. He hold the scatter-out country together by sailing between its islands, making the sea the thread that tie the one nation.

## Shadow

The old skill can dead out with the old man: the boatbuilding and the sail and the hard sea-knowledge let go as the young ones leave for easier work and the engine take the place of the rig, till the heritage of the island survive only as a model on a shelf and the sea is no way of living any more but just a pretty view.

## Tell

He can read a squall coming a whole hour off and set his sail to meet it, and he could no more put in words how he know it than he could teach the sea to a man who never once leave the land.
