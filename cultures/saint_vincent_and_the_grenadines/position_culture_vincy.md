# Position: Vincy

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

Belonging to a small scatter-out nation that carry [Hairouna and the Garifuna](position_hairouna_and_the_garifuna.md) as its deepest root and its warmest word for itself, the land of the blessed that a free people, broke up and carried away, still claim as home. It have a volcano standing right up in the middle of the mainland and the sea running between the little islands, so a Vincy grow up knowing [the sea and the land](position_the_sea_and_the_land.md) as the two halves of the one same home, the grower under La Soufriere and the sailor of the Grenadines counted every bit as Vincentian, one as the other. It carry the warm Vincy talk of [the Vincentian tongue](position_language_en_vc.md), the English of the Caribbean turn easy and quick, full of ole talk and play. And it carry the memory of the long road up from colony to nation, the labour man and the small farmer that win the flag in 1979 by patience and by faith more than by force.

## Orders

That you hail up the stranger by name and hold your neighbour close before you hold the office; that faith, family, church, and village come before the man standing alone; that hard time get meet with a easy calm and a good humour, not bare complaint; that the volcano and the hurricane get answer with the same steady building back every time, no matter how much it cost; and that a Vincy owe respect to the elder and to the land both, the same rich black soil the Garifuna did hold before there was ever a colony to leave.

## Loses

Ground when the easy top side calm bury the deep wound clean out of mind, the deportation and the labour men struggle forgot by them that only inherit the flag; ground when the scatter-out islands pull apart along the sea between them, the Grenadines feeling forgot by the mainland or the mainland looking right past the little islands; and ground when the young ones leave for foreign to find work, the village emptying out house by house till the small nation cannot hold its own people no more.

## Drives

Lives in the chief who dead refusing another man rule, in the labour leader who turn grievance into a flag, and in the farmer and the sailor who each hold one half of the one same home together. It drive a Vincy to meet the volcano and the hurricane with a calm that does not break, to keep faith and family and village before self, and to remember, under all the warm and the easy talk, that this is a free people country, root deep in Hairouna, still walking the long road to stand fully on its own.
