## Name

Saint Vincent and the Grenadines, a multi-island nation in the eastern Caribbean, the volcanic mainland of Saint Vincent strung southward to a chain of small isles, the Grenadines, that reach toward Grenada. ISO VC, language en, the English of the Caribbean carried in the warm Vincentian dialect the people speak among themselves. A country the Kalinago called Hairouna, the land of the blessed, a single nation made of one green volcanic isle and many scattered ones.

## Arc

This is the story of an archipelago that carries a wound at its root and a fire at its centre. Before the Europeans it was Hairouna, the land of the blessed, home to the Kalinago, the island Caribs. Onto that island came Africans, by shipwreck and by escape, and out of the meeting of African and Carib grew a free people, the Garifuna, the Black Caribs, who held the land as their own. When Britain came for it they resisted fiercely, under the paramount chief Joseph Chatoyer, their national hero, until their defeat and the deportation of 1797 carried thousands away across the sea, seeding the Garifuna communities that live to this day along the coasts of Central America: a deep wound, and the root of a diaspora. The island that remained was worked for sugar, then for arrowroot, of which it became the world's leading grower, then for bananas, the green gold of the small farmer under the volcano. Over the mainland stands La Soufriere, a living volcano whose eruptions in 1902, 1979, and 2021 mark the nation's life like a heartbeat, the land at once blessed and burning. South run the Grenadines, the isles of sailors and boatbuilders, of Bequia and its old whaling and sailing heritage, where the sea is the road and the trade. The country walked the long road from colony through the rise of labour to independence in 1979, and gives itself over each year to its festivals, the summer Carnival of Vincy Mas and the unique pre-dawn carols of the Nine Mornings before Christmas. Under it all runs one nerve: a warm, devout, neighbourly people, calm on the surface over a buried history of resistance and loss, who endure the volcano and hold the scattered isles together as one.
