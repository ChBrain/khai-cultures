## Cue

Long before any European sail come in sight, the island stand green and volcanic in the eastern Caribbean, and the Kalinago, the island Caribs, call it Hairouna, the land of the blessed. Onto this Carib island, over time, the Africans reach, some by shipwreck, some by run-away, and out of African meeting Carib a new free people start to come up, holding the land as their own under the high cone of [La Soufriere](place_la_soufriere.md).

## Action

The Kalinago and, in time, the Garifuna, the Black Caribs, live the blessed and dangerous land, farming the rich black dirt the volcano make, fishing the sea, and holding the island as a free people that answer to no foreign crown. The volcano give the soil its richness and now and then its fire, and the people take the blessing along with the danger as the price of the land. This is the deep root of the nation, older than its colonial name: a free people on a volcanic island they call the land of the blessed, before the long contest for it start.

## Tension

The island is the land of the blessed and a free people hold it as their own, the deepest and proudest root of the nation. But the blessing carry the danger of the volcano that make it, and the freedom carry the contest coming, for the same island the Kalinago and the Garifuna holding free a empire going come and want, and the root of the nation pride is the same ground of the wound to come.
