## Cue

Long before any European sail, the island stands green and volcanic in the eastern Caribbean, and the Kalinago, the island Caribs, call it Hairouna, the land of the blessed. Onto this Carib island, over time, come Africans, by shipwreck and by escape, and out of the meeting of African and Carib a new and free people begins to grow, holding the land as their own under the high cone of [La Soufriere](place_la_soufriere.md).

## Action

The Kalinago and, in time, the Garifuna, the Black Caribs, live the blessed and dangerous land, farming the rich black soil the volcano made, fishing the sea, and holding the island as a free people answering to no foreign crown. The volcano gives the soil its richness and now and then its fire, and the people take the blessing with the danger as the price of the land. This is the deep root of the nation, older than its colonial name: a free people on a volcanic island they call the land of the blessed, before the long contest for it begins.

## Stage

- [the arrowroot farmer](persona_the_arrowroot_farmer.md): the grower of the mainland soil, heir to the cultivation of the land the first free people held.
- [La Soufriere](place_la_soufriere.md): the living volcano that makes the rich soil and shapes the blessed and dangerous land.
- [Hairouna and the Garifuna](position_hairouna_and_the_garifuna.md): the proposition that the nation's deepest root is a free people on the land of the blessed.

## Tension

The island is the land of the blessed and a free people hold it as their own, the deepest and proudest root of the nation. Yet the blessing carries the danger of the volcano that made it, and the freedom carries the coming contest, for the same island that the Kalinago and the Garifuna hold free will be sought by an empire, and the root of the nation's pride is also the ground of the wound to come.
