# Persona: the arrowroot farmer

## Taxonomy

[the sea and the land](position_the_sea_and_the_land.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A grower of the mainland, working the rich black slopes under La Soufriere for arrowroot and bananas, the small farmer who lives by the volcano's blessing and at its mercy, heir to the labour that came up the long hard road on this land. Steady, devout, and rooted to the soil, she rises early to the field, plants and reaps by the turn of the season, and trusts the land and the Lord to see her through. English is her whole mother tongue, the warm Vincy dialect of the country districts: she [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in it, and she [hears](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) the talk of the market and the village in the same soft island speech. The English of the buyer and the contract, the grades and the prices of the produce trade, she [reads only haltingly](process_reading_caught.md), catching what she can of the paper that sets the worth of her crop. She works a land older than its English name, ground the Garifuna held before her, and the deepest names of plant and place are half lost, the older tongue gone with the deportation, so that the gap at the limit of the tongue runs back through the soil itself, where the land remembers a speech the farmer no longer has.

## Action

In [Hairouna](plot_01_hairouna.md) she works the blessed and dangerous land under the volcano, the grower on the rich slopes that the Garifuna and the Kalinago held before her, and she is the living face of [the sea and the land](position_the_sea_and_the_land.md) on the mainland. She carries the land forward by keeping it in cultivation, holding the inheritance of the soil through the volcano's turns.

## Shadow

Her rootedness can become a hold on a way of living the world is leaving behind: the arrowroot the country once led the world in fading from the fields, the small farm undercut and the young drawn off the land, until the soil that fed the nation grows quiet and a heritage of the ground is let go.

## Tell

She plants her ground in the shadow of a volcano she knows will burn it again one day, and gives thanks for the blessing of the same black soil that the fire made and will take and make once more.
