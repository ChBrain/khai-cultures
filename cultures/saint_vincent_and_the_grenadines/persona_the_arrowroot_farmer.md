# Persona: the arrowroot farmer

## Taxonomy

[the sea and the land](position_the_sea_and_the_land.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A grower of the mainland, working the rich black slope under La Soufriere for arrowroot and bananas, the small farmer who live by the volcano blessing and at its mercy too, heir to the labour that come up the long hard road on this land. Steady, God-fearing, and root deep in the soil, she get up before day to the field, plant and reap by the turn of the season, and trust the land and the good Lord to carry her through. English is her whole mother tongue, the warm Vincy talk of the country districts: she [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in it, and she [hears](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) the ole talk of the market and the village in the same soft island speech, [the Vincentian tongue](position_language_en_vc.md) her whole mother tongue in its warm Vincy register. The English of the buyer and the contract, the grades and the prices of the produce trade, she [reads only haltingly](process_reading_caught.md), catching what she can off the paper that does set the worth of her crop. She work a land older than its English name, ground the Garifuna did hold before her, and the deepest names of plant and place half lost now, the older tongue gone with the deportation, so that the gap at the limit of the tongue run right back through the soil itself, where the land remember a speech the farmer ain't have no more.

## Action

In [Hairouna](plot_01_hairouna.md) she work the blessed and dangerous land under the volcano, the grower on the rich slope that the Garifuna and the Kalinago hold before her, and she is the living face of [the sea and the land](position_the_sea_and_the_land.md) on the mainland. She carry the land forward by keeping it in cultivation, holding the inheritance of the soil through the volcano turns.

## Shadow

Her rootedness can turn into a holding-on to a way of living the world leaving behind: the arrowroot the country once lead the whole world in fading out the fields, the small farm undercut and the young ones draw off the land, till the soil that did feed the nation grow quiet and a heritage of the ground get let go.

## Tell

She plant her ground in the shadow of a volcano she know going burn it again one day, and she give God thanks for the blessing of the same black soil that the fire did make and going take and make over again.
