# Persona: Joseph Chatoyer

## Taxonomy

[Hairouna and the Garifuna](position_hairouna_and_the_garifuna.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The paramount chief of the Garifuna, the Black Caribs, the national hero who led his free people in their long resistance to Britain for the land that was theirs, and who fell in that fight: the heart of the nation's pride and the face of its deepest wound. Proud, commanding, and unbending, he held Hairouna as the inheritance of a free people and refused to yield it. The Garifuna tongue is his whole and only mother tongue, the Igneri speech of the island Caribs carried down and married to the African inheritance of his people: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in it, [hears](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) his people and his council in it, and rallies them in it to the defence of the land. The English of the empire that came for that land was never his, the colonisers' tongue he met across the gulf of war: what their terms he [deciphered](process_reading_deciphered.md) only through others, and what passed between chief and crown he [followed](process_hearing_followed.md) at one remove, through go-betweens, never in his own speech. He led a free people in a tongue the empire would not learn, against an empire whose tongue he would not take, and when his people were broken and carried away their language went with them into exile, so the gap at the limit of the tongue became the very gulf the war was fought across, and the wound it opened.

## Action

In [Chatoyer and the Carib Wars](plot_02_chatoyer_and_the_carib_wars.md) he leads the Garifuna resistance against Britain to the end, the free people defending Hairouna as their own, and he gives [Hairouna and the Garifuna](position_hairouna_and_the_garifuna.md) its hero and its heart. By falling for the land he sets the nation's deepest pride and its deepest grief into one man, and roots the country in a free people who would not be ruled.

## Shadow

The hero held high can become a hero held only on a plinth: the pride saluted on his day while the heritage he died for is let slip, the wound recited and not felt, the free people he led remembered as a defeat instead of a living root, the grief allowed to swallow the blessing he fought to keep.

## Tell

He stands for a land the empire would name for a saint and calls it by its own name, Hairouna, the land of the blessed, refusing to the last to be ruled in another man's tongue or hold his ground on another man's terms.
