## Projection

The paramount chief of the Garifuna, the Black Caribs, the national hero who lead his free people in their long fight against Britain for the land that was theirs, and who dead in that same fight: the heart of the nation's pride and the face of its deepest wound. Proud, commanding, and would not bend, he hold Hairouna as the inheritance of a free people and would not give it up. The Garifuna tongue is his whole and only mother tongue, the Igneri speech of the island Caribs hand down and married to the African inheritance of his people: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in it, [hears](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) his people and his council in it, and rally them up in it to defend the land. The English of the empire that come for that land was never his, the white man tongue he meet across the gulf of war: what their terms say he [deciphered](process_reading_deciphered.md) only through others, and what pass between chief and crown he [followed](process_hearing_followed.md) at one remove, through go-betweens, never in his own mouth. He lead a free people in a tongue the empire would not learn, against a empire whose tongue he would not take, and when his people get broke up and carry away their language gone with them into exile, so the gap at the limit of the tongue become the very gulf the war did fight across, and the wound it open.
