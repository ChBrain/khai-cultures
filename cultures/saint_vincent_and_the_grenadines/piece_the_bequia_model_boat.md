# Piece: the Bequia model boat

## Taxonomy

Parent group: pieces

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Place

On the workbench of the model-maker in Bequia, in the windows of the isle's small shops, and on the shelves and mantels of homes across the nation and far beyond it: a handmade miniature of a Bequia sailing craft, a whaleboat or a schooner, carved and rigged plank by plank and sail by sail by the seafaring isle that knows the real boats best. The work of the same hands and the same eye that built and sailed the full-size vessels, the island's craft made small.

## Load Bearing

It carries the story of [the sea and the land](position_the_sea_and_the_land.md): the heritage of the Grenadine isle where the sea is the living, the boatbuilding and the sailing and the old whaling tradition made into an object a hand can hold. The model is how the isle keeps and shares its sea-knowledge, the proof that on a place of little land the craft of the boat is the real inheritance. It carries the skill of the seafarer into the home and the wider world, the small wooden hull standing for a whole way of living by the water. Without it the heritage of the sea-isle would have no shape on the shelf to remember it by.

## Apparent

A small wooden boat, hull and mast and rigging and tiny sails, carved and painted by hand: in itself only a model, an ornament, a thing made to be looked at. Its weight is entirely in the hands that made it, the same hands that built the real boats, and in the sea-heritage of the isle that the little hull holds in miniature.
