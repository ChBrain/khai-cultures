# Plan: Peace and Justice

## Taxonomy

The charge the nation set itself in its motto: to honour the deep root of [Hairouna and the Garifuna](position_hairouna_and_the_garifuna.md), the free people and the chief who died for the land, holding the heritage and the wound soberly; to endure the fire of the living volcano with the calm resilience [the sea and the land](position_the_sea_and_the_land.md) demands; and to carry the long work of [colony to nation](position_colony_to_nation.md) forward, making a small scattered country stand on its own and hold its mainland and its isles as one home.

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Direction

Keep the Garifuna heritage alive, honouring Chatoyer and the free people, remembering the deportation soberly, and holding the wound and the blessing together rather than letting the calm bury the root; meet the volcano with resilience, sheltering and rebuilding through every eruption and farming the blessed and dangerous soil with faith and care; develop the scattered isles as one, binding the mainland and the Grenadines across the sea and keeping a living for the grower and the sailor alike; and answer the charge of independence, making the small nation stand on its own and keeping its young at home.

## Orders

- To the grower like [the arrowroot farmer](persona_the_arrowroot_farmer.md): keep the blessed soil in cultivation, hold the heritage of the land through the volcano's turns, and farm the slopes with faith and resilience.
- To the founder's heirs like [Milton Cato](persona_milton_cato.md): answer the charge of the flag they raised, making the nation truly stand on its own and finishing the work of standing alone that independence began.
- To the nation: honour the Garifuna root and the chief who died for it, endure the volcano as one, and hold the mainland and the isles together as a single home.

## Implementation

A young nation, independent since 1979, that has held itself together as one across the volcano and the sea, kept its festivals and its faith, and carried the calm resilience of a small island people through eruption after eruption, the latest in 2021. It has begun the honouring of its Garifuna root, naming Chatoyer its first national hero, and has left open still the deeper work of keeping the heritage living, of making the scattered isles prosper as one, of keeping the young at home, and of standing fully on its own in the world.
