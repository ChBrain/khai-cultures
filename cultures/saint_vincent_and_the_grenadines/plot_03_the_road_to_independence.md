# Plot: the Road to Independence

## Taxonomy

[play_saint_vincent_and_the_grenadines](play_saint_vincent_and_the_grenadines.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

Coming into the twentieth century the island still a colony, its people working the land for sugar, arrowroot, and the bananas of the small farmer, with little voice in their own affairs. Out of the labour of the cane field and the wharf the worker grievance rise up, and the people's champions step forward to give it a politics, [Ebenezer Joshua](persona_ebenezer_joshua.md) on the platform and the picket and [Milton Cato](persona_milton_cato.md) in the law and the chamber.

## Action

The labour movement give the worker and the small farmer their voice, and the union men press the crown for the vote, for a say, and at last for a flag. Joshua, the firebrand, turn the labourer grievance into a politics and win the people a place in their own affairs; Cato, the steady lawyer, carry the country through self-rule and at last across the threshold to nationhood. On 27 October 1979 Saint Vincent and the Grenadines come to be an independent nation, mainland and Grenadines, Bequia among them, join under one flag. The long road from worked colony to sovereign country walk right to its end, and a small scatter-out people stand, for the first time ever, on their own.

## Stage

- [Ebenezer Joshua](persona_ebenezer_joshua.md): the labour leader who give the worker a voice and drive the colony toward self-rule.
- [Milton Cato](persona_milton_cato.md): the father of independence and first Prime Minister, who carry the country to nationhood in 1979.
- [Bequia](place_bequia.md): the Grenadine island whose sea people join the mainland in one nation under one flag.

## Tension

The nation is won, not give, climb up to along the long road of labour and the ballot by the worker and the champions who talk for them. But independence is a charge as much as it is a triumph: the flag raise up over a small, scatter-out country that now have to make its own living and hold its own people, and the hard work of standing alone, of keeping the young ones home and joining the islands, open up just as the colony road reach its end.
