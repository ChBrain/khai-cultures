# Plot: the Road to Independence

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

Into the twentieth century the island is still a colony, its people working the land for sugar, arrowroot, and the bananas of the small farmer, with little voice in their own affairs. Out of the labour of the cane field and the wharf rises the worker's grievance, and the people's champions step forward to give it a politics, [Ebenezer Joshua](persona_ebenezer_joshua.md) on the platform and the picket and [Milton Cato](persona_milton_cato.md) in the law and the chamber.

## Action

The labour movement gives the worker and the small farmer their voice, and the union men press the crown for the vote, for a say, and at last for a flag. Joshua, the firebrand, makes the labourer's grievance into a politics and wins the people a place in their own affairs; Cato, the steady lawyer, carries the country through self-rule and at last across the threshold to nationhood. On 27 October 1979 Saint Vincent and the Grenadines becomes an independent nation, mainland and Grenadines, Bequia among them, joined under one flag. The long road from worked colony to sovereign country is walked to its end, and a small scattered people stand, for the first time, on their own.

## Stage

- [Ebenezer Joshua](persona_ebenezer_joshua.md): the labour leader who gives the worker a voice and drives the colony toward self-rule.
- [Milton Cato](persona_milton_cato.md): the father of independence and first Prime Minister, who carries the country to nationhood in 1979.
- [Bequia](place_bequia.md): the Grenadine isle whose seafarers join the mainland in one nation under one flag.

## Tension

The nation is won, not given, climbed to up the long road of labour and the ballot by the worker and the champions who spoke for them. Yet independence is a charge as much as a triumph: the flag is raised over a small, scattered country that must now make its own living and hold its own people, and the hard work of standing alone, of keeping the young and joining the isles, opens just as the colony's road comes to its end.
