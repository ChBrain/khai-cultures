## Place

In the fields on the volcano slope, in the factory that wash and grind and settle the root into a fine white starch, and one time in kitchens and sickrooms right round the world: the root of a plant the island grow better than anybody, dig, wash, and turn into the pure, easy-to-digest starch that Saint Vincent lead the whole world in producing for generation after generation. The crop of the small mainland farmer, the green leaf in the field and the white powder in the sack, the island name one time carry abroad on the label of its starch.

## Load Bearing

It carry the story of [colony to nation](position_colony_to_nation.md): the crop that make a small colony matter in the world markets, the produce of the freed people labour on the volcanic land, and the green gold that, along with the banana, carry the island economy through the long road from sugar toward a nation of its own. Arrowroot is the proof that this small island one time lead the whole world in something, the pride of the mainland farmer and the work of the soil that did feed the country. Without it the island place in the world trade, and the small grower living, would be a much thinner story.

## Apparent

A fine, pure white powder, hardly any taste to it, ground and settle from a wash root: in itself just a starch, a thing to thicken a sauce, a food for the baby and the sick. Its whole weight is in what it one time mean to the island that grow it best, the world first producer, and in the slope and the labour and the factories the plain white powder stand for.

## Yearbook

- Grow better than anywhere on the volcanic soil of the mainland and turn into a starch prize for its purity and how easy it digest.
- Make the island the world leading producer of arrowroot, carrying the small colony name into the world markets.
- Fade out as the world turn to other starches, leaving the crop a heritage of the land and a memory of when the island did lead the world.
