# Piece: arrowroot

## Taxonomy

Parent group: pieces

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Place

In the fields on the volcano's slopes, in the factory that washes and grinds and settles the root into a fine white starch, and once in kitchens and sickrooms around the world: the rhizome of a plant the island grew to perfection, dug, washed, and processed into the pure, easily digested starch for which Saint Vincent was for generations the world's leading producer. The crop of the small mainland farmer, the green leaves in the field and the white powder in the sack, the island's name once carried abroad on the label of its starch.

## Load Bearing

It carries the story of [colony to nation](position_colony_to_nation.md): the crop that made a small colony matter in the world's markets, the produce of the freed people's labour on the volcanic land, and the green gold that, with the banana, carried the island economy through the long road from sugar toward a nation of its own. Arrowroot is the proof that this small island once led the whole world in something, the pride of the mainland farmer and the work of the soil that fed the country. Without it the island's place in the world's trade, and the small grower's living, would be a thinner story.

## Apparent

A fine, pure white powder, all but tasteless, ground and settled from a washed root: in itself only a starch, a thickener for a sauce, a food for the infant and the invalid. Its weight is entirely in what it once meant to the island that grew it best, the world's first producer, and in the slopes and the labour and the factories that the plain white powder stands for.

## Yearbook

- Grown to perfection on the volcanic soil of the mainland and processed into a starch prized for its purity and easy digestion.
- Made the island the world's leading producer of arrowroot, carrying the small colony's name into the world's markets.
- Faded as the world turned to other starches, leaving the crop a heritage of the land and a memory of when the island led the world.
