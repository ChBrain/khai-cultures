# Saint Vincent and the Grenadines: a khai culture

Saint Vincent and the Grenadines staged as a full khai play, ISO VC, language en.
A volcanic archipelago of deep Garifuna roots, as a theatre: its history as the
plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its language and faith encode, its
places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_saint_vincent_and_the_grenadines.md](play_saint_vincent_and_the_grenadines.md),
  the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Saint
  Vincent and the Grenadines' profile (a warm, neighbourly, family-centred people
  of strong faith and easy good humour, resilient and unhurried, the calm over a
  deep history) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** Hairouna, Chatoyer and the Carib Wars, the Road to
  Independence, and the Volcano and the Isles - each casting the elements it needs,
  and each staging the deep wound of the Garifuna resistance and the 1797
  deportation, and slavery, soberly and on the public record.
- **Personas:** Joseph Chatoyer (the paramount chief of the Garifuna and national
  hero), Ebenezer Joshua (the labour leader and people's champion), and Robert
  Milton Cato (the father of independence and first Prime Minister) as the historic
  cast, plus two defining archetypes, the Bequia whaler (the sailor and traditional
  whaler of the Grenadines) and the arrowroot farmer (the mainland grower under the
  volcano). Each is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** Hairouna and the Garifuna (the land of the blessed and the free
  people, their resistance, and the deportation), colony to nation (the road from
  worked colony through labour to independence), the sea and the land (the
  Grenadines and the sea, and the mainland under the volcano, as one home).
- **Places:** La Soufriere (the living volcano whose eruptions mark the nation's
  life), Bequia (the Grenadine isle of sailors and whalers).
- **Processes:** the Nine Mornings (the unique pre-dawn festivities of the nine
  days before Christmas), Vincy Mas (the summer Carnival that binds the nation).
- **Pieces:** arrowroot (the crop of which the nation was the world's leading
  producer), the Bequia model boat (the handmade model sailboats of the whaling
  isle).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the Garifuna resistance and the 1797 deportation, and slavery, are treated
soberly on the public record, from within the nation's own self-understanding and
taking no side; the staging and the architecture are original work.
