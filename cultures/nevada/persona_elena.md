# Persona: Elena

## Taxonomy

[basin resilience](position_basin_resilience.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A solar engineer in Nye County, thirty-six and inclined to trust a number over a hunch. English, in an articulate technical cadence, is her mother tongue, the language she [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and writes her daily engineering logs in. It is the [Nevadan English](position_language_en_us_nv.md) of the Nye County solar field, the cadence of the desert basin. She speaks no foreign languages. Where her patience thins is with the casino floor: the fast, commercial talk of dealers like Jack and the digital demands of Las Vegas boards, she [hears only as distant chatter](process_hearing_followed.md), sensing that the patient, season-dependent rhythm of desert water preservation counts for less next to corporate tourism and entertainment. She carries [the Silver State](position_culture_silver_state.md) in her own key - betting the basin's future on the same nerve that once bet a fortune on silver ore.

## Action

She preserves [silver ore](piece_silver_ore.md) records, coordinates field conservation under [the Lake Mead plan](plan_lake_mead_water_management.md), and studies [desert chance](position_desert_chance.md).

## Shadow

Her focus on solar efficiency metrics and power testing can make her indifferent to municipal wastewater infrastructure disinvestment in the rural interior.

## Tell

She looks at a photovoltaic computer and says directly: "We are calibrating heavy solar array outputs; you can't optimize advanced power designs if you're only focused on historic local preservation."
