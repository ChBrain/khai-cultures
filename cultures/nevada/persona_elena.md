# Persona: Elena

## Taxonomy

[basin resilience](position_basin_resilience.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A thirty-six-year-old solar engineer in Nye County. English, spoken with an articulate technical cadence, is her mother tongue, the language in which she [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and writes her daily engineering logs. It is the [Nevadan English](position_language_en_us_nv.md) of the Nye County solar field, the articulate technical cadence of the desert basin. She speaks no foreign languages. Yet the loss lies in the seam: The fast, commercial talk of casino dealers like Jack and the digital demands of Las Vegas boards [she hears only like a distant echo](process_hearing_followed.md), feeling that the patient, season-dependent rhythm of desert water preservation is treated as secondary to corporate tourism and entertainment. She belongs to [the Silver State](position_culture_silver_state.md) as the engineer who bets the basin's future on the same nerve that once bet a fortune on silver ore.

## Action

She preserves [silver ore](piece_silver_ore.md) records, coordinates field conservation under [the Lake Mead plan](plan_lake_mead_water_management.md), and studies [desert chance](position_desert_chance.md).

## Shadow

Her focus on solar efficiency metrics and power testing can make her indifferent to municipal wastewater infrastructure disinvestment in the rural interior.

## Tell

She looks at a photovoltaic computer and says directly: "We are calibrating heavy solar array outputs; you can't optimize advanced power designs if you're only focused on historic local preservation."
