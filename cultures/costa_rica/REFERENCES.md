# Costa Rica: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_es.md](pitch_es.md) is written from Costa Rica's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record without
entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Costa Rica score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | -------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 35                         |
| Individualism                 | 15                         |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 21                         |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 86                         |
| Long Term Orientation         | 35                         |
| Indulgence                    | 75                         |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; Costa Rica's low Motivation toward
Achievement and high Indulgence are among the markers of its gentle, contented
tone.

The one defining persona, [el labriego sencillo](persona_el_labriego_sencillo.md),
is an archetype, not a historical individual: the humble smallholder of the
national anthem, the figure in which the nation reads its egalitarian, rural
temper. The cultural framing - the "pura vida" ethic, the parejo self-image and
its gaps, the Afro-Caribbean coast of Limon and its Creole English, and the
Nicoya blue zone - is original staging on the public record.
