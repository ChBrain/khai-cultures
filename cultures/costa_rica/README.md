# Costa Rica: a khai culture

Costa Rica staged as a full khai play, ISO CR, language es. A small,
demilitarized democracy that traded its army for schools and measures its
greatness in peace and green abundance, as a theatre: its history as the plot
line, its people as the cast, the roles its language and faith encode, its
places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_costa_rica.md](play_costa_rica.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_es.md](pitch_es.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Costa
  Rica's profile (gentle, polite, conflict-averse, optimistic and present-fond,
  proud of peace and nature) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the poor backwater of the colony and the smallholder seed,
  Juan Santamaria and the 1856 defense of sovereignty, coffee and the
  institutional republic, and the fatherland without an army (1948, the abolition
  of the army, the welfare state, and Arias's Nobel peace) - each casting the
  elements it needs, and each staged soberly on the public record.
- **Personas:** Juan Santamaria, Jose Figueres, Oscar Arias, and Carmen Lyra as
  the historic cast, plus one defining archetype, el labriego sencillo (the humble
  smallholder of the national anthem). Each is wired to the language engine by the
  language-crossing it holds; the Afro-Caribbean coast of Limon carries its Creole
  English at the edge of the parejo national story.
- **Positions:** la patria sin ejercito (the abolished army and the culture of
  peace), la democracia del labriego (the egalitarian small-farmer republic), la
  soberania (the heroic defense of sovereignty).
- **Places:** el Valle Central (the coffee highlands, San Jose and the Teatro
  Nacional), Monteverde (the cloud forest, emblem of the biodiverse green nation).
- **Processes:** la Romeria (the August pilgrimage to the Virgin of los Angeles),
  el boyeo (the painted oxcart and the ox-driving heritage).
- **Pieces:** el cafe (the grano de oro that built the republic), la carreta
  tipica (the brightly painted oxcart, a national emblem).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the 1948 civil war and the abolition of the army are named soberly on the
public record, and the gaps the nation still carries are staged honestly; the
staging and the architecture are original work.
