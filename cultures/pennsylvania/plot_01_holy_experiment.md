# Plot: Penn's Holy Experiment 1681

## Taxonomy

[play_pennsylvania](play_pennsylvania.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

In 1681, William Penn receives a charter from King Charles II and establishes Pennsylvania as a haven for Quakers and persecuted religious minorities.

## Action

The founding of the colony establishes [religious tolerance](position_tolerance.md) as a constitutional principle. Penn drafts a frame of government that guarantees civil liberties. Clara curates early legal documents in Philadelphia, presenting Penn's treaties with the Lenape people.

## Stage

- [religious tolerance](position_tolerance.md) : the belief in religious pluralism, peace, and civil liberties.
- [Quaker meetings](process_quaker_meeting.md) : the silent worship assemblies that shaped the colony's social conscience.
- [Clara](persona_clara.md) : the Philadelphia archivist who presents the historical treaties and founding documents.

## Tension

Despite Penn's peaceful intentions, subsequent waves of settlers expand rapidly westward, violating treaties and causing conflicts with native populations.
