# Persona: John

## Taxonomy

[industrial grit](position_industrial_grit.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A sixty-eight-year-old retired steelworker in Pittsburgh. English is his mother tongue, the language in which he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and writes his logs. It is the [Pennsylvania English](position_language_en_us_pa.md) of Pittsburgh, the broad Western Pennsylvania speech of the steel valleys. Polish [he wears as an overgarment](process_speaking_worn.md) during neighborhood church gatherings, keeping alive the language of his immigrant grandparents. Yet the loss lies in the seam: The quiet, consensus-driven constitutional talk of Clara and the historical archives in the east [he hears only like a distant echo](process_hearing_followed.md), feeling that the physical labor of the mills and the hard reality of working-class survival are being forgotten in the state's modern service economy.

## Action

He conducts tours at [Pittsburgh Point](place_pittsburgh_point.md), helps draft [the industrial transition plan](plan_industrial_transition.md), and studies [religious tolerance](position_tolerance.md).

## Shadow

His pride in legacy heavy industry makes him suspicious of environmental regulations, fearing that green policies will destroy the remaining working-class jobs.

## Tell

He looks at a rusted section of a blast furnace and says directly: "The nation was built on the steel we smelted right here in these valleys; you can't build bridges out of financial services."
