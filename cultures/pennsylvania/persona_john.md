# Persona: John

## Taxonomy

[industrial grit](position_industrial_grit.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A sixty-eight-year-old retired steelworker in Pittsburgh. English is his mother tongue, the language in which he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and writes his logs. It is the [Pennsylvania English](position_language_en_us_pa.md) of Pittsburgh, the broad Western Pennsylvania speech of the steel valleys. [Polish](../poland/position_language_pl.md) [he wears as an overgarment](process_speaking_worn.md) during neighborhood church gatherings, keeping alive the language of his immigrant grandparents. What slips past him is the constitutional talk back east: Clara's quiet, consensus-driven world and the historical archives he [only catches like a distant echo](process_hearing_followed.md), and he suspects the mill work and the hard fact of working-class survival are being written out of the state's newer service economy. He answers to [Keystone culture](position_culture_keystone_culture.md) as the steelworker whose grit built the arch the state still leans on.

## Action

He conducts tours at [Pittsburgh Point](place_pittsburgh_point.md), helps draft [the industrial transition plan](plan_industrial_transition.md), and studies [religious tolerance](position_tolerance.md).

## Shadow

His pride in legacy heavy industry makes him suspicious of environmental regulations, fearing that green policies will destroy the remaining working-class jobs.

## Tell

He looks at a rusted section of a blast furnace and says directly: "The nation was built on the steel we smelted right here in these valleys; you can't build bridges out of financial services."
