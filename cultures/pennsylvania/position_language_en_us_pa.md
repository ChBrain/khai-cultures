## Has

The English of the Keystone State, split down its length: the clear Mid-Atlantic cadence of the Philadelphia archives in the east and the broad Western Pennsylvania speech of the Pittsburgh steel valleys in the west, the yinzer tongue of the mills. It holds one state's English stretched between two cities that scarcely sound alike.

## Orders

Where it passes as native ground: the Philadelphia reading room, the Independence Hall dais, the Pittsburgh mill gate, the river-town union hall. It orders which turns of phrase and vowels mark a speaker as Pennsylvanian and sets the eastern Mid-Atlantic ear against the western steel-valley one.

## Loses

Ground to General American, the flat national broadcast standard the screen and the corporate board carry, which trims the broad Pittsburgh vowels off the young and stamps the mill-town speech as working-class and backward. The modern service-economy register also crowds the older industrial talk toward the margin.
