# Pennsylvania: a khai culture

Pennsylvania staged as a full khai play, ISO US-PA, language en. The Keystone State: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its pluralistic, constitutional, and industrial life encodes, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house that holds this culture and answers for it.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_pennsylvania.md](play_pennsylvania.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_pennsylvania.md](pitch_pennsylvania.md), the Pennsylvania key.
- **Plots (history):** Penn's Holy Experiment of 1681, the Declaration of Independence of 1776, and the rise of the steel/coal industry in Pittsburgh.
- **Personas:** Clara (Philadelphia archivist) and John (retired Pittsburgh steelworker), written in English.
- **Positions:** religious tolerance (pluralism), industrial grit (manufacturing legacy).
- **Places:** Independence Hall (Philadelphia), Pittsburgh Point.
- **Processes:** Quaker silent meetings, steel smelting.
- **Pieces:** the Liberty Bell, a steel beam.
- **Plan:** the industrial transition plan.
