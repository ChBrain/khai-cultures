# Tuvalu: a khai culture

Tuvalu staged as a full khai play, ISO TV, language tvl, te gana Tuvalu. A
Polynesian people of nine low atolls, anchored in the falekaupule and made the
world's conscience on the rising sea, staged as a theatre: its history as the
plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its language and faith encode, its
places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_tuvalu.md](play_tuvalu.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_tvl.md](pitch_tvl.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Tuvalu's profile (communal and consensus-bound in the falekaupule, faith-filled,
  modest, resilient, and ocean-bound) and naming no dimension. The prose body is
  written in Tuvaluan; only the H2 headings stay English.
- **Plots (history):** the Polynesian settlement of the nine atolls, the split
  from the Gilberts and independence in 1978, Sopoaga's climate diplomacy and the
  world's conscience, and Simon Kofe's vision of the first digital nation - each
  casting the elements it needs.
- **Personas:** Toaripi Lauti (the first Prime Minister), Enele Sopoaga (the
  climate-diplomacy Prime Minister), and Simon Kofe (the foreign minister of the
  underwater speech and the digital-nation vision) as the historic cast, plus two
  defining archetypes, the elder of the falekaupule and the fatele singer. Each is
  wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds: Tuvaluan the
  mother tongue, English the tongue carried.
- **Positions:** the falekaupule (the island community, the council and meeting
  house, and faith), the sinking nation and the digital nation (the climate threat,
  the world's conscience, and the .tv lifeline), the atoll and the sea (Polynesian
  atoll life and fishing).
- **Places:** Funafuti (the capital atoll, the runway-life on the airstrip, the
  lagoon), the outer islands (the outer atolls of falekaupule life).
- **Processes:** the fatele (the dance-and-song, the box-drum and the rising
  chorus), the falekaupule assembly (the island council's gathering and feast).
- **Pieces:** the .tv domain (the internet domain that is a lifeline of national
  revenue), the pandanus mat (the woven mat of home and ceremony).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-record sources are recorded in
[REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the pitch is its
invisible hand. Sources on the public record are credited, never claimed; the
existential climate threat to a low-lying nation is treated soberly from within
Tuvalu's own self-understanding, taking no side; the staging and the architecture
are original work.
