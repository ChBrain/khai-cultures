# Saudi Arabia: a khai culture

Saudi Arabia staged as a full khai play, ISO SA, language ar. The heartland of
the Arabian Peninsula and the cradle of Islam, as a theatre: its history as the
plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its faith and language encode, its
places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_saudi_arabia.md](play_saudi_arabia.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_ar.md](pitch_ar.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Saudi
  Arabia's profile (faith first and a strong respect for authority and age, a
  deeply collective loyalty to family and tribe, a guarded caution toward the new,
  and the restraint and patience of the desert) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Message (the birth of Islam in Mecca and Medina),
  the First State (the founding in Diriyah, 1744), the Unification (King Abdulaziz
  and the founding of the Kingdom, 1902 to 1932), and the Awakening (the modern
  nation carrying its heritage forward) - each casting the elements it needs, and
  each treating faith and the holy sites with reverence.
- **Personas:** King Abdulaziz Al Saud (the unifier and founder), Muhammad
  al-Awni (the great Nabati poet), Hamad al-Jasir (the scholar of the Peninsula),
  and Talal Maddah (the voice of the land) as the historic cast, plus one
  archetype, the Bedouin (the desert host, falconer, and coffee-pourer). Each is
  wired to the language engine by the relationship to the mother tongue it holds.
- **Positions:** the Two Holy Mosques (faith first and the trust of serving the
  holy sites), hospitality (the open hand, honor, and the kept word), and the
  desert and Bedouin heritage (patience, the camel, the falcon, and Nabati
  poetry).
- **Places:** Mecca and the Hijaz (the cradle of the Message and the destination
  of the Hajj), Najd and Riyadh (the heartland of the desert, the oases, and the
  founding of the state).
- **Processes:** the Hajj (the pilgrimage to the center of meaning, handled
  reverently), the majlis and the coffee (the open welcome of Arabic coffee and
  dates).
- **Pieces:** the date (the food of the desert, of hospitality, and of the fast),
  the ardah and the sword (the dance of honor that joins the sword, the Nabati
  poem, and the drum).
