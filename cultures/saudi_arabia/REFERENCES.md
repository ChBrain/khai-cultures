# Saudi Arabia: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_ar.md](pitch_ar.md) is written from Saudi Arabia's profile on
Geert Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no
number. The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record
without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Saudi Arabia score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ---------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 95                           |
| Individualism                 | 25                           |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 60                           |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 80                           |
| Long Term Orientation         | 36                           |
| Indulgence                    | 52                           |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; the high Power Distance and low
Individualism reflect the strong respect for authority and age and the collective
loyalty to family and tribe.

from the public historical record. They are credited here and never claimed; the
staging is original work. Islam and the Two Holy Mosques are treated with
reverence as the heart of the culture, and contemporary politics and economics are
kept largely out of scope.

The defining persona [the Bedouin](persona_albadawi.md) is an archetype, not a
historical individual: a composite that carries the desert heritage - the open
tent, the camel and the falcon, the Arabic coffee poured for every passerby, and
the patience and honor the hard land taught its people.
