# Persona: Mae

## Taxonomy

[appalachian valley](position_appalachian_valley.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A fifty-two-year-old park ranger in the Great Smoky Mountains. English, spoken with an expressive mountain drawl, is her mother tongue, the language in which she [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and writes her daily logs. It is the [Tennessean English](position_language_en_us_tn.md) of the Great Smoky Mountains, the expressive mountain drawl of the ridges and the ranger station. She speaks no foreign languages. What she can't quite keep up with is the business-driven talk of Nashville songwriters like Jackson and the digital demands of Middle Tennessee utility boards, which she [only catches like a distant echo](process_hearing_followed.md), while she worries the patient, season-dependent rhythm of mountain forest preservation gets treated as secondary to corporate tourism metrics. She carries [the Volunteer State](position_culture_volunteer_state.md)'s readiness to show up and do the work into the daily stewardship of the ridges.

## Action

She preserves [whiskey barrel](piece_whiskey_barrel.md) relics, coordinates trail safety under [the Smoky Mountains air plan](plan_smoky_mountains_air_quality.md), and studies [music city](position_music_city.md).

## Shadow

Her pride in mountain heritage and rural independence can make her suspicious of state-wide zoning and environmental forestry mandates, fearing they will increase park operating costs and disrupt local tourism.

## Tell

She looks out at the misty ridges of the Smokies and says thoughtfully: "The forests have their own rhythms; you can't protect mountain ecology if you're only focused on digital speed and country music commercialization."
