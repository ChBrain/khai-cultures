# Persona: Jackson

## Taxonomy

[music city](position_music_city.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A thirty-eight-year-old country songwriter in Nashville. English, spoken with a melodic Southern cadence, is his mother tongue, the language in which he [speaks, hears, and thinks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), the defining code of his artistic and professional life. It is the [Tennessean English](position_language_en_us_tn.md) of the Nashville songwriting rooms, the melodic Southern cadence of the studio and the stage. He speaks no foreign languages. What slips past him is the slower, nature-dependent mountain talk of park rangers like Mae and the quiet history of [appalachian valley](position_appalachian_valley.md), which he [only meets as historical source material](process_reading_followed.md) on his workstation, while he watches the patient, acoustic roots of the region get commercialized for digital music streaming metrics. He carries [the Volunteer State](position_culture_volunteer_state.md)'s craft pride into the business of songwriting.

## Action

He preserves [acoustic guitar](piece_acoustic_guitar.md) designs, coordinates recording projects under [the Smoky Mountains air plan](plan_smoky_mountains_air_quality.md), and studies [music city](position_music_city.md).

## Shadow

His focus on publishing royalties and metropolitan music business metrics can make him indifferent to the municipal infrastructure disinvestment in rural counties outside the entertainment centers.

## Tell

He tunes an acoustic guitar and says directly: "We are writing stories that connect with working people; you can't compose a great country lyric if you're only focused on digital music streaming algorithms."
