# Position: the Volunteer State

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

Membership in a state that earned its nickname by answering the call before being asked, a spirit that runs from the militia volunteers of the early republic through the songwriters of [music city](position_music_city.md) and the rangers and farmers of the [appalachian valley](position_appalachian_valley.md). It carries the [Tennessean English](position_language_en_us_tn.md) cadence of both the Nashville studio and the mountain ridge, and a state built along the same spine of the Appalachian Mountains that divides its people into hill and valley, city and country, and binds them under one flag all the same.

## Orders

That one shows up and does the work without waiting to be asked, whether that means tracking a session, running a trail, or tending a dam gate. It orders a pride taken as much in craft, a song, a barrel of whiskey, a well-tended forest, as in the finished product sold to the world, and a stubborn attachment to the particular corner of the state one calls home, be it river valley, ridge, or music row.

## Loses

Ground where the commercial hustle of the music business looks past the slower, season-bound life of the mountain valleys, and where federal power projects and modern regulation collide with local land rights and rural independence. It loses its balance when the state's industrial power and urban highway corridors are allowed to outpace the air and water that the Great Smoky Mountains forests depend on.

## Drives

It drives the state to answer first and ask questions later, from the volunteer companies that gave Tennessee its name to the songwriters, engineers, and rangers who keep building and rebuilding it today. It pushes the [music city](position_music_city.md) craftsman and the [appalachian valley](position_appalachian_valley.md) ranger toward the same end even when their working days pull apart, and it drives the state to prove that industrial power and mountain wilderness can still be kept in the same hand.
