# Plot: The Tennessee Valley Authority Act 1933

## Owner

## Cue

In May 1933, President Franklin D. Roosevelt signs the bill creating the Tennessee Valley Authority, launching river engineering works.

## Action

The act establishes massive river control systems, generating electricity through [hydroelectric generation](process_hydroelectric_generation.md). Mae monitors forest canopy health around reservoirs, while Jackson writes songs celebrating the electrification of rural mountain farms.

## Stage

- [appalachian valley](position_appalachian_valley.md) : the mountain communities and river valleys modernized by the federal program.
- [hydroelectric generation](process_hydroelectric_generation.md) : the management of dam turbines and river water flows.
- [the Smoky Mountains air plan](plan_smoky_mountains_air_quality.md) : the conservation plan developed to mitigate regional power plant pollution.

## Tension

The construction of dams floods thousands of acres of fertile farmland, displacing over fifteen thousand rural families and historic Cherokee settlements.
