# Tennessee: a khai culture

Tennessee staged as a full khai play, ISO US-TN, language en. The Volunteer State: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its Grand Ole Opry stages, Tennessee Valley Authority dams, and Memphis blues and rock revolutions encodes, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house that holds this culture and answers for it.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_tennessee.md](play_tennessee.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_tennessee.md](pitch_tennessee.md), the Tennessee key.
- **Plots (history):** the Grand Ole Opry founding in 1925, the Tennessee Valley Authority Act of 1933, and the Memphis blues and rock revolution in the 1950s.
- **Personas:** Jackson (country songwriter) and Mae (Great Smoky Mountains park ranger), written in English.
- **Positions:** music city (songcraft/showmanship), appalachian valley (mountain independence/TVA stewardship).
- **Places:** Ryman Auditorium, Beale Street.
- **Processes:** whiskey distillation (charcoal fermentation), hydroelectric generation (TVA dam control).
- **Pieces:** an acoustic guitar, a whiskey barrel.
- **Plan:** the Great Smoky Mountains air quality plan.
