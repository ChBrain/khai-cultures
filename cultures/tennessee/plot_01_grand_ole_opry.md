# Plot: The Grand Ole Opry Founding 1925

## Taxonomy

[play_tennessee](play_tennessee.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

In November 1925, WSM radio begins broadcasting the weekly WSM Barn Dance, later renamed the Grand Ole Opry, in Nashville.

## Action

The radio show establishes country music commercial markets, developing [music city](position_music_city.md) standards. The [acoustic guitar](piece_acoustic_guitar.md) represents this performance era. Jackson composes country songs for Opry stages, while Mae records oral histories of early mountain acoustic performers.

## Stage

- [music city](position_music_city.md) : the commercial country music business and songwriting focus.
- [acoustic guitar](piece_acoustic_guitar.md) : the wood acoustic guitar representing Nashville songcraft.
- [Ryman Auditorium](place_ryman_auditorium.md) : the historic tabernacle that served as the Opry's home.

## Tension

The growth of commercial country music commercializes traditional mountain folk music, causing debates over artistic purity and artist compensation.
