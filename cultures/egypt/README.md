# Egypt: a khai culture

Egypt staged as a full khai play, ISO EG, language ar. The gift of the Nile and
the oldest civilization on earth, staged as a theatre: its history as the plot
line, its people as the cast, the roles its language and faith encode, its
places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_egypt.md](play_egypt.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_ar.md](pitch_ar.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Egypt's
  profile (warmth and the primacy of family and the group, patience and the long
  view of an ancient people, a hierarchy held lightly under the river's calm, and
  a humor that meets hardship with wit) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Pharaonic civilization, the Coptic heritage and the
  early faiths, the Fatimid and Azhar golden age, and the modern nahda of letters,
  music, and cinema, each casting the elements it needs.
- **Personas:** Naguib Mahfouz, Umm Kulthum, and Taha Hussein as the modern cast,
  Imhotep as the Pharaonic heritage figure, plus one archetype, the fellah (the
  Nile farmer who keeps the unchanging rhythm of the fields). Each is wired to the
  language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** the Nile and the land (the river that made the nation and the
  patience of the fields), faith and learning (the Coptic and Islamic heritages
  carried with one dignity, and Al-Azhar the beacon), the word and the song
  (Egypt as the source of Arabic letters, music, and cinema).
- **Places:** Cairo (the victorious, the city of a thousand minarets) and Luxor
  (ancient Thebes, the open-air museum of the Pharaohs on the Nile).
- **Processes:** the Nile flood and harvest (the unchanging rhythm of the river
  and the fields) and the ahwa gathering (the coffeehouse sit that holds warmth,
  wit, and patience).
- **Pieces:** the obelisk and the papyrus (the oldest written heritage on earth)
  and the oud (the heart of Arabic music that carried the Egyptian song).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the deep Pharaonic past is carried as cultural heritage, the Coptic and
Muslim heritages are treated with equal dignity, and modern political upheavals
are kept out of scope; the staging and the architecture are original work.
