# Vanuatu: a khai culture

Vanuatu staged as a full khai play, ISO VU, language bis. A Melanesian
archipelago of fiercely held kastom and a dual British-French heritage, as a
theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its
languages and kastom encode, its places, its practices, and its defining
objects, all tuned by a single pitch. The prose is written in Bislama, the
English-lexified creole that is the nation's unifying tongue.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_vanuatu.md](play_vanuatu.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_bis.md](pitch_bis.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Vanuatu's profile (deep communalism rooted in kastom, an easygoing and content
  temperament, quiet consensus in the nakamal, and respect for chiefly rank) and
  naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the chiefs and Roi Mata, the Condominium, independence and
  the Coconut War, and the happy isles today - each casting the elements it
  needs, and each staging blackbirding, the Condominium, and the Santo secession
  soberly on the public record.
- **Personas:** Walter Lini, Grace Mera Molisa, and Roi Mata as the historic and
  legendary cast, plus two defining archetypes, Man Naghol (the Pentecost
  land-diver) and Jif Kastom (the kastom chief of the nakamal, keeper of pig-tusk
  rank). Each is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds,
  between the local lanwis or Bislama and the dual colonial tongues, English and
  French.
- **Positions:** kastom (custom fiercely held, the nakamal, pig-tusk wealth),
  the Condominium and independence (the dual British-French rule and the road to
  nationhood), the happy isles (kava, community, and contentment).
- **Places:** Tanna (Mount Yasur, the John Frum movement, and kastom villages),
  Efate (Port Vila the capital and Roi Mata's domain).
- **Processes:** the naghol (the Pentecost land-dive, the origin of bungee),
  kava at the nakamal (the evening kava rite in the men's meeting ground).
- **Pieces:** the pig tusk (the curved boar's tusk, wealth and rank), the
  slit-drum (the carved tamtam, the voice of the community).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical and
cultural sources are recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never
enters the prose; the pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain
are credited, never claimed; blackbirding, the Condominium, and the Santo
secession are treated soberly on the public record and from within Vanuatu's own
self-understanding, taking no side; the staging and the architecture are original
work.
