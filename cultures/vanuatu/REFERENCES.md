# Vanuatu: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical and cultural
sources behind the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_bis.md](pitch_bis.md) is written from Vanuatu's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
Vanuatu is not separately published in the main Hofstede dataset; the profile is
read from the wider Melanesian and Pacific pattern (low individualism and strong
communalism, high power distance and respect for chiefly rank, and an easygoing,
indulgent temperament). The indicative reading is recorded here so the data is on
the record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Vanuatu reading (indicative) |
| ----------------------------- | ---------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | high                         |
| Individualism                 | low (strongly communal)      |
| Motivation toward Achievement | low                          |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | moderate                     |
| Long Term Orientation         | low (present-focused)        |
| Indulgence                    | high                         |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the six-dimensions model of national
culture). Vanuatu is not separately scored in the published Country Comparison
tool; the reading above is indicative, drawn from the Melanesian and Pacific
pattern, and is used only to set the pitch's register, never as a published score.

story - blackbirding, the Anglo-French Condominium, the missions, and the Santo
secession of the Coconut War - are staged soberly on the public record, from
within Vanuatu's own self-understanding, and taking no side.

The two defining personas, [Man Naghol](persona_man_naghol.md) and
[Jif Kastom](persona_jif_kastom.md), are archetypes, not historical individuals:
composites that carry the kastom-rooted life of Vanuatu - the Pentecost
land-diver who keeps the naghol, and the chief of the nakamal who keeps the
pig-tusk rank and the loa of the bubu.
