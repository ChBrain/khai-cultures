# Persona: Elijah

## Taxonomy

[plantation resistance](position_plantation_resistance.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A fifty-two-year-old Gullah basket weaver in Mt. Pleasant. English, spoken with a melodic Gullah Lowcountry cadence, is his mother tongue, the language in which he [speaks, hears, and thinks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), the defining code of his family life. It is the [South Carolina English](position_language_en_us_sc.md) of the Lowcountry, the melodic Gullah cadence of the Mt. Pleasant sea-island communities. He speaks no foreign languages. What he can't quite square is the commercial talk of preservation architects like Charlotte and the digital demands of Charleston's tourism boards, which he [only catches like a distant echo](process_hearing_followed.md), while he watches the ancestral, resource-conserving wisdom of the coastal marshes treated as secondary to corporate resort development. He holds his share of [the Palmetto State](position_culture_palmetto_state.md)'s inheritance in the ancestral coastal land and the Gullah custom that the same tourist economy keeps threatening to displace.

## Action

He preserves [sweetgrass basket](piece_sweetgrass_basket.md) designs, coordinates community feedback under [the salt marsh plan](plan_lowcountry_salt_marsh_conservation.md), and studies [historic stewardship](position_historic_stewardship.md).

## Shadow

His pride in traditional Gullah land ownership and independent coastal rights can make him suspicious of state-wide zoning and environmental marsh regulations, fearing they will restrict family property use.

## Tell

He looks at a coiled sweetgrass basket and says thoughtfully: "A basket pattern tells the history of our relationship with this coast; you can't preserve a community if you're only focused on historic resort gentrification."
