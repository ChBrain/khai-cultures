# Process: sweetgrass weaving

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

Gullah Geechee traditional weavers and family apprentices, conducted year-round in coastal markets and community centers.

## Direction

In the weaving circle. Gathering sweetgrass, palmetto leaves, and pine needles; drying the fibers; coiling the sweetgrass; and binding the coils with split palmetto ribbons using a bone or metal tool.

## Lever

The ancestral craft process that preserves West African coiled basketry methods and cultural identity. It supports family micro-trade and local heritage tourism, though it is limited by manual production times and reduced access to coastal sweetgrass fields.

## Echo

The weaving patterns record historic narratives. The process defines the daily work of [Elijah](persona_elijah.md) in Mt. Pleasant, while preservation architect [Charlotte](persona_charlotte.md) respects the method as an early model of organic structure.
