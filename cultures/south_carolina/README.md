# South Carolina: a khai culture

South Carolina staged as a full khai play, ISO US-SC, language en. The Palmetto State: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its Fort Sumter Civil War battle, indigo and rice plantation trade, and Gullah Geechee heritage corridor encodes, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house that holds this culture and answers for it.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_south_carolina.md](play_south_carolina.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_south_carolina.md](pitch_south_carolina.md), the South Carolina key.
- **Plots (history):** the Battle of Fort Sumter of 1861, the Indigo and Rice Trade Boom of the 1740s, and the Gullah Geechee Cultural Heritage Act of 2006.
- **Personas:** Elijah (Gullah sweetgrass basket weaver) and Charlotte (historic preservation architect), written in English.
- **Positions:** plantation resistance (colonial trade trauma/cultural preservation), historic stewardship (architectural preservation/Southern hospitality).
- **Places:** Fort Sumter, Charleston Historic District.
- **Processes:** sweetgrass weaving (traditional basket weaving), historic masonry (lime-mortar restoration).
- **Pieces:** a sweetgrass basket, lime mortar.
- **Plan:** the salt marsh plan.
