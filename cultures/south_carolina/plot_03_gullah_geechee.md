# Plot: Gullah Geechee Heritage Corridor 2006

## Owner

## Cue

In October 2006, Congress passes the Gullah Geechee Cultural Heritage Act, establishing the federal preservation corridor.

## Action

The act establishes federal protections for Gullah cultural traditions, developing [the salt marsh plan](plan_lowcountry_salt_marsh_conservation.md). Elijah teaches sweetgrass basket weaving at heritage centers, while Charlotte drafts preservation plans for coastal Gullah schoolhouses.

## Stage

- [plantation resistance](position_plantation_resistance.md) : the Gullah cultural identity and West African heritage.
- [the salt marsh plan](plan_lowcountry_salt_marsh_conservation.md) : the marsh conservation plan designed to regulate coastal development.
- [historic masonry](process_historic_masonry.md) : the traditional masonry process that preserves historic coastal structures.

## Tension

Gentrification and rising property taxes continue to displace Gullah families from their ancestral coastal lands, threatening the preservation of the corridor.
