## Cue

In the 1740s, Eliza Lucas Pinckney standardizes high-quality indigo cultivation, launching a major colonial export.

## Action

The trade boom establishes the plantation economy, developing [sweetgrass weaving](process_sweetgrass_weaving.md) as a local resource skill. Charlotte designs restoration strategies for historic plantation mills, while Elijah documents West African agricultural methods.

## Stage

- [position_plantation_resistance](position_plantation_resistance.md) : the legacy of colonial labor exploitation and Gullah cultural preservation.
- [sweetgrass weaving](process_sweetgrass_weaving.md) : the traditional coiled basket weaving.
- [sweetgrass basket](piece_sweetgrass_basket.md) : the coiled basket used historically in rice processing.

## Tension

The agricultural boom dramatically increases the demand for enslaved labor, institutionalizing a brutal plantation system across the Lowcountry.
