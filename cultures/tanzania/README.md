# Tanzania: a khai culture

Tanzania staged as a full khai play, ISO TZ, language sw. A nation built not on
blood or descent but on a single chosen language, Kiswahili, that binds more than
a hundred and twenty peoples into one, as a theatre: its history as the plot line,
its people as the cast, the roles its language and unity encode, its places, its
practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_tanzania.md](play_tanzania.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_sw.md](pitch_sw.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Tanzania's profile (strong collectivism and brotherhood, respect for age and
  authority held with gentleness, patience and a long view, restraint over
  indulgence, and an ethic of consensus and peace) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** Olduvai Gorge and the cradle of humankind, the Swahili
  coast and the Zanzibar trade, independence and nation-building under Nyerere,
  and Kiswahili and Tanzania today - each casting the elements it needs, and each
  staging its tensions soberly.
- **Personas:** Julius Nyerere (Mwalimu, the teacher and nation-builder who chose
  Kiswahili and brotherhood), Shaaban Robert (the father of Swahili letters), Bi
  Kidude (the queen of taarab and keeper of the coast's voice), and Filbert Bayi
  (the runner who carried the young nation's pride), plus one archetype, the dala
  dala conductor who binds every people of the city into one tongue each day. Each
  is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** Kiswahili (the chosen national language no single people owns,
  binding all as equals), unity and brotherhood (umoja na undugu, the deliberately
  planted kinship and the ujamaa ethic, treated soberly as a cultural ideal taking
  no political side), and the Swahili coast culture (the seaborne world that bore
  the language).
- **Places:** Zanzibar (the spice islands, Stone Town, taarab, and the old trade),
  the Serengeti and Ngorongoro (the great migration, the Ngorongoro crater,
  Olduvai Gorge, and Kilimanjaro on the horizon).
- **Processes:** taarab (the coast's art of speaking the heart in proverb and
  riddle), the dala dala journey (the daily ride that turns many peoples into one
  set of fellow passengers speaking one tongue).
- **Pieces:** ugali (the shared staple eaten by hand from one dish), the khanga
  (the printed cloth that carries a Swahili proverb as a walking message).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the slave-trade history of the old Zanzibar entrepots is acknowledged
soberly and with dignity, with no atrocity detail, and no political side is taken;
the staging and the architecture are original work.
