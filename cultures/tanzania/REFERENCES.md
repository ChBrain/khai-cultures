# Tanzania: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_sw.md](pitch_sw.md) is written from Tanzania's profile (read with
the East African regional data) on Geert Hofstede's model of national culture and
names no dimension and carries no number. The scores and their source are recorded
here so the data is on the record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Tanzania score (approx., East Africa) |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 70                                    |
| Individualism                 | 27                                    |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 41                                    |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 52                                    |
| Long Term Orientation         | 32                                    |
| Indulgence                    | 40                                    |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, drawn from the
published East Africa regional cluster used where a single-country score is not
separately published; the low Individualism reflects the strong collectivism and
brotherhood the pitch carries.

staging is original work. The slave-trade history of the old Zanzibar entrepots is

The defining persona [Konda wa dala dala](persona_konda.md) (the dala dala
conductor) is an archetype, not a historical individual: a composite that carries
the daily binding of more than a hundred and twenty peoples into one tongue, the
city's many turned into one set of fellow passengers speaking Kiswahili.
