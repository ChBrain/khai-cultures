# Serbia: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_sr.md](pitch_sr.md) is written from Serbia's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no
number. The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the
record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Serbia score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ---------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 86                     |
| Individualism                 | 25                     |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 43                     |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 92                     |
| Long Term Orientation         | 52                     |
| Indulgence                    | 28                     |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published
by Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; Serbia's high Power Distance,
strong Uncertainty Avoidance, and low Individualism and Indulgence stand out in
the published data.

The two defining personas, [Ljubica](persona_ljubica.md) and
[Stefan](persona_stefan.md), are archetypes, not historical individuals:
composites that carry the tension between the older generation (Orthodox faith,
the slava, the inat held through communism, the 1990s sanctions, and the 1999
bombing) and the younger one (the diaspora and the pull of English and the
unadmitted Croatian twin on a small language).
