# Serbia: a khai culture

Serbia staged as a full khai play, ISO RS, language sr. An Orthodox nation marked
by the Kosovo covenant and loss, as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its
people as the cast, the roles its language and faith encode, its places, its
practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_serbia.md](play_serbia.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_sr.md](pitch_sr.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Serbia's
  profile (hierarchical and clan-bound, proud and defiant, anxious and
  suffering-honoring, Orthodox and heroic-tragic) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Battle of Kosovo (1389), the Ottoman centuries, Vuk's
  revival and the national awakening (19th c.), and the breakup of Yugoslavia and
  the loss of Kosovo (1990s-2008) - each casting the elements it needs.
- **Personas:** Saint Sava, Prince Lazar, and Vuk Karadžić as the historic cast,
  plus two defining archetypes, Ljubica (older, Orthodox faith, the slava, the
  inat of the 1990s) and Stefan (younger, the diaspora emigrant whose Serbian
  thins between English and the unadmitted Croatian twin). Each is wired to the
  language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** српски језик (Serbian in Cyrillic, the twin of Croatian held
  apart by script and altar, codified phonetically by Vuk), косовски завет (the
  Kosovo covenant - glory in defeat, the heavenly kingdom over the earthly, the
  lost sacred cradle), инат (stubborn, self-costing defiance that will not bend to
  pressure).
- **Places:** Косово (the Field of Blackbirds, the sacred cradle and its
  monasteries, lost), Београд (Belgrade at the confluence of Sava and Danube, the
  much-besieged gate between East and West).
- **Processes:** слава (the family patron-saint feast, uniquely Serbian
  Orthodox), гуслање (epic singing to the one-string gusle that carried the
  Kosovo myth through the Ottoman centuries).
- **Pieces:** оцило (the Serbian cross with four firesteels, "Само слога Србина
  спасава"), шљивовица (plum brandy, the welcome and the wake).
