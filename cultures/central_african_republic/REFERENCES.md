# the Central African Republic: References

The provenance behind this culture: the cultural-orientation source that tunes the
pitch (kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain sources behind the
plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_fr.md](pitch_fr.md) is written from the Central African Republic's
broad orientation on Geert Hofstede's model of national culture and names no
dimension and carries no number. The Central African Republic is not separately
scored in the published Hofstede data; the pitch is written from the orientation
common to the region and to the country's own self-understanding (a collective,
relationship-first society with respect for elders and hierarchy, patience, and a
present, person-to-person warmth), recorded here so the basis is on the record
without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Central African orientation (qualitative) |
| ----------------------------- | ----------------------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | High (respect for elders and chiefs)      |
| Individualism                 | Low (kin and community first)             |
| Motivation toward Achievement | Moderate                                  |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | Moderate                                  |
| Long Term Orientation         | Lower (present-focused)                   |
| Indulgence                    | Warm, sociable                            |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the six-dimensions model of national
culture and the Country Comparison tool). The Central African Republic is not
individually published; the orientation above is qualitative and regional, used
only to set the register of the pitch.
