# Turkmenistan: a khai culture

Turkmenistan staged as a full khai play, ISO TM, language tk. A desert nation of
nomadic tribes forged into one people, as a theatre: its history as the plot
line, its people as the cast, the roles its language and faith encode, its
places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_turkmenistan.md](play_turkmenistan.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_tk.md](pitch_tk.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Turkmenistan's profile (a collective, hierarchy-respecting people, restrained
  and ceremonious, with deep loyalty to tribe, family, and the elders) and naming
  no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** ancient Nisa and Parthia, the tribes and the epic of
  Gorogly, the age of Magtymguly, and the modern nation - each casting the
  elements it needs, and each kept on the public record and out of contemporary
  political dispute.
- **Personas:** Magtymguly Pyragy, Mollanepes, Kemine, and Berdy Kerbabaev as the
  historic literary cast, plus one defining archetype, the carpet weaver
  (halycy). Each is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it
  holds.
- **Positions:** the desert and the horse (the Karakum and the Akhal-Teke, the
  two pillars of life), the carpet (the haly and its tribal gol medallions), the
  poet's word (the literary language and the heritage of the bards).
- **Places:** Nisa (the ancient Parthian capital in the Akhal oasis), the Karakum
  (the great desert that holds the nomadic life).
- **Processes:** carpet weaving (the wordless record passed from mother to
  daughter), welcoming a guest (the law of hospitality in the desert).
- **Pieces:** the carpet (the haly, the wordless record of the nation whose gol
  medallions appear on the flag), the telpek (the sheepskin hat of the Turkmen
  man).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the long and difficult passages of the modern era are treated soberly
and without taking any political side; the staging and the architecture are
original work.
