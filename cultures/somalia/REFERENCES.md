# Somalia: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_so.md](pitch_so.md) is written from Somalia's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
Country-level data for Somalia is sparse in the published model; the figures below
are indicative of the East African / Arab-world cluster Somalia sits within
(strong collectivism, higher power distance, a long pastoral patience) and are
recorded here so the orientation is on the record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Somalia orientation (indicative) |
| ----------------------------- | -------------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | high                             |
| Individualism                 | low (strongly collectivist)      |
| Motivation toward Achievement | moderate                         |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | moderate to high                 |
| Long Term Orientation         | low to moderate                  |
| Indulgence                    | low to moderate                  |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the six-dimensions model of national
culture and the regional clusters). Where no published country score exists for
Somalia, the orientation is read from the surrounding East African and Arab-world
cluster; the figures are indicative, not exact, and never enter the prose.

The one defining archetype, the [camel-herder poet](persona_geeljire.md), is not a
historical individual: a composite that carries the pastoral heritage, the customary
law of xeer, the lineage system, and the prestige of the unwritten word kept in
memory and recited at the gathering.
