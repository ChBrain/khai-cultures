# Somalia: a khai culture

Somalia staged as a full khai play, ISO SO, language so. The Nation of Poets on
the longest coast in mainland Africa, as a theatre: its history as the plot line,
its people as the cast, the roles its language and faith encode, its places, its
practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_somalia.md](play_somalia.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_so.md](pitch_so.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Somalia's profile (the weight of kin and clan above the individual, the
  authority of elders and lineage, the prestige of the spoken word, the reach of
  hospitality, and the patience of the pastoral life) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the trading coast and the Land of Punt, the coming of
  Islam, the era of the Sayid (the poet-historic figure Mohamed Abdullah Hassan
  handled soberly as a poet), and the script of 1972 and the Nation of Poets -
  each casting the elements it needs.
- **Personas:** Sayid Mohamed Abdullah Hassan, Hadraawi, Maryam Mursal, and
  Nuruddin Farah as the historic cast, plus one archetype, the camel-herder poet
  (the pastoral keeper of xeer, lineage, and the unwritten word). Each is wired to
  the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** the Somali tongue and poetry (the supreme prestige of the word
  and the gabay, the Nation of Poets), Islam (the bedrock of life, law, and
  ethics), xeer and kinship (the customary law and the lineage system that are the
  social fabric, treated neutrally and with dignity).
- **Places:** Mogadishu / Xamar (the seaward capital, the trading hub, the seat of
  the land), Zeila / Saylac (the ancient northern trading city and one of the
  early gateways of Islam into Africa).
- **Processes:** the wedding (aroos, with henna and song, binding two lineages),
  the poetry gathering (the recitation and contest of gabay that carries the word
  from one generation to the next).
- **Pieces:** the camel (geel, the most prized wealth of the pastoral life and the
  most common figure in verse), canjeero (the everyday flatbread of the Somali
  table and the emblem of hospitality).
