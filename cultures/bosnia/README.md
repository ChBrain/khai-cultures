# Bosnia and Herzegovina: a khai culture

Bosnia and Herzegovina staged as a full khai play, ISO BA, language bs. A land on
the border of worlds, the bridge between East and West, as a theatre: its history
as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its faiths and its one tongue
encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single
pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_bosnia.md](play_bosnia.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_bs.md](pitch_bs.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Bosnia's
  profile (warm hospitality and deference to those above, a family- and
  neighbour-bound order, anxiety for certainty after much suffering, a melancholy
  that is also a love of life) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** medieval Bosnia and the stećci, Ottoman Bosnia, Sarajevo
  1914, and the siege and Dayton (1992-1995) - each casting the elements it needs.
- **Personas:** Ivo Andrić, Mehmed-paša Sokolović, and Mak Dizdar as the historic
  cast, plus two defining archetypes, Mejra (older, the Sarajevo woman who kept
  komšiluk through the siege) and Tarik (younger, the post-war diaspora whose
  mother tongue thins into English and German). Each is wired to the language
  engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** komšiluk (the centuries-old intermixed living of the faiths),
  sevdah (the melancholic longing and soul of the love-song), ćuprija (Bosnia as
  the bridge between East and West, faiths, and peoples).
- **Places:** Sarajevo (the "Jerusalem of Europe," the four faiths within blocks,
  1914 and the siege), Mostar (the Stari Most destroyed and rebuilt).
- **Processes:** kahva (the slow Bosnian coffee ritual at the heart of komšiluk),
  sevdalinka (the communal singing of the melancholic love-song at a sijelo).
- **Pieces:** stećak (the medieval carved tombstone, Bosnia's deep-identity
  monument), ćevapi (the grilled dish at the shared table of the bazaar).
