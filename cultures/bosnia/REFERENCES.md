# Bosnia and Herzegovina: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_bs.md](pitch_bs.md) is written from Bosnia and Herzegovina's
profile on Geert Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and
carries no number. The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on
the record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Bosnia and Herzegovina score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 90                                     |
| Individualism                 | 22                                     |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 48                                     |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 87                                     |
| Long Term Orientation         | 70                                     |
| Indulgence                    | 44                                     |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Robust, independently published data
for Bosnia and Herzegovina is limited; the scores above are approximate and
indicative, in places derived from regional and former-Yugoslav estimates, and
should be read as a profile rather than as precise measurements.

The two defining personas, [Mejra](persona_mejra.md) and [Tarik](persona_tarik.md),
are archetypes, not historical individuals: composites that carry the tension
between the older generation (the intermixed komšiluk of the faiths, the coffee,
the meeting-place soul, the mother tongue) and the younger one (the post-war
diaspora and the pull of English and German on a small language).
