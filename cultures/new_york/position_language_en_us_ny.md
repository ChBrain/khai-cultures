# Position: New York English

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The English of the Empire State, split between the dense, fast, famously marked New York City accent of the five boroughs and the flatter Inland North speech of the upstate cities and the Great Lakes shore. It holds a quick, blunt, layered metropolitan plainness that both the Wall Street floor and the Broadway wing carry.

## Orders

Where it passes as native ground: the Wall Street trading floor, the Brooklyn theater, the deli counter, the upstate canal town. It orders which vowels and turns of phrase mark a speaker as a New Yorker and sets the downstate city ear against the upstate one.

## Loses

Ground to General American, the flat national broadcast standard the screen and the corporate board carry, which trims the city accent off the young and stamps it as a caricature. The corporate financial register also crowds the older immigrant-inflected borough speech toward the margin.

## Drives

It carries [Tony](persona_tony.md), whose rapid cadence is the working English of the Wall Street floor, and [Sarah](persona_sarah.md), whose Brooklyn cadence holds the immigrant-layered English of the Broadway wing. So the one New York speech shows who turns toward the financial engine and who keeps the artistic city ground.
