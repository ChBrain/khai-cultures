The opening of the canal links the Hudson River to the Great Lakes, funneling the trade of the Midwest through New York City and establishing its commercial hegemony.

**[Plot 2: Ellis Island 1892](plot_02_ellis_island_1892.md)**

The federal immigration station opens, becoming the entry portal for twelve million immigrants whose labor and cultures reshape the state.
