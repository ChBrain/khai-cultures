# Plot: Ellis Island 1892

## Taxonomy

[play_new_york](play_new_york.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

On January 1, 1892, the federal government opens the Ellis Island immigration station in New York Harbor.

## Action

The station processes millions of newcomers, establishing [the melting pot](position_melting_pot.md) as a core cultural identity. The influx of diverse peoples transforms the state's labor, art, and food. The delicatessen becomes a staple of urban life. Sarah's grandparents entered through Ellis Island, settling in Brooklyn and opening a dry goods store.

## Stage

- [the melting pot](position_melting_pot.md) : the belief in assimilation and the strength of diverse cultural heritages.
- [delicatessen dining](process_delicatessen.md) : the food culture that emerged from Eastern European and Italian immigrant neighborhoods.
- [Broadway billboard](piece_broadway_billboard.md) : the theater district where immigrant composers and actors shaped American musical theater.

## Tension

Immigrants face grueling health inspections, language barriers, and the threat of deportation, alongside nativist hostility in their new neighborhoods.
