# Persona: Sarah

## Taxonomy

[the melting pot](position_melting_pot.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A thirty-eight-year-old Broadway stage manager in Brooklyn. English is her mother tongue, the language in which she [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and directs the backstage crew. It is the [New York English](position_language_en_us_ny.md) of the five boroughs, the immigrant-layered Brooklyn cadence of the theater district. Yiddish [she wears as an overgarment](process_speaking_worn.md) in her creative writing and conversations, keeping Brooklyn's immigrant heritage close. Where it gets complicated is the money talk: the transaction-oriented financial vocabulary of Tony and corporate real estate values reach her [only like a distant echo](process_hearing_followed.md), and she suspects the city's collective, artistic soul is being priced out from under it. Her stake in [New York culture](position_culture_new_york_culture.md) is the stage manager's stake, keeping the melting pot's welcome alive against the pressure of its own success.

## Action

She directs [Broadway shows](process_broadway_show.md), coordinates transit schedules under [the New York subway plan](plan_subway_modernization.md), and studies [the Empire State](position_empire_state.md).

## Shadow

Her nostalgia for the gritty, historic New York art scene can make her resistant to urban improvements and safety regulations in historic theaters.

## Tell

She stands backstage in the dim light of the theater and whispers: "A Broadway production isn't just about the ticket sales; it's about the magic created when people from every background come together in the dark."
