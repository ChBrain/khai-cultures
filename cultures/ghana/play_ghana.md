# Play: Ghana

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the house that holds this staging and answers for it.

## Name

Ghana, the land on the Gulf of Guinea where the great river meets the sea, ISO GH, language en, the tongue of office laid over Akan, Ga, Ewe, Dagbani and the many mother tongues of the house. A republic that took its name not from its own soil but from a far older empire of the western Sudan, reaching back across the centuries to borrow a greatness and carry it forward. We for use English to do the nation's business, but the heart still beats in the tongue of the grandmother and the proverb she keeps.

## Arc

Ghana is a young state wearing an old name, and that, chale, tells you the whole matter from the start. The ancient Ghana empire lay far to the north and west of these forests, but when the Gold Coast came to itself and claimed its freedom, it reached back across a thousand years to take that name as its own, the way a wise child takes the strong name of an ancestor so that the strength may walk again. On this very ground rose the Asante, who gathered the clans under one Golden Stool said to have come down from the sky to hold the soul of a whole people, ruled by an Asantehene and counselled by the okyeame whose mouth carries the chief's word wrapped in proverb. The forest and the savanna held many houses besides, the Ga by the sea, the Ewe to the east, the Dagomba and the northern peoples under their own old kingdoms, each with its drum and its tongue. The European came for gold and stayed, and the castles at the coast, Cape Coast and Elmina, hold a sorrow this house carries with dignity and does not parade. Out of the colony called the Gold Coast, Kwame Nkrumah led the people to be the first below the Sahara to lift the flag of their own freedom in 1957, and raised the Black Star over the whole continent's hope. Through it all runs one nerve: that a person is a person through other persons, that akwaaba, you are welcome, is said and meant, that the dead and the living and the not-yet-born sit at one table, and that the deepest truth is never spoken straight but folded, gently, inside a proverb.

## Stakes

Whether a young nation wearing the name of an ancient one can keep faith with both the old and the new: hold the Golden Stool and the chieftaincy that came before the state alongside the republic that came after, keep saying akwaaba and meaning it when the world grows hard and hurried, honour the sorrow at the coast without letting it harden the heart, carry the Black Star's old promise of freedom and unity past the disappointments that followed it, and keep the grandmother's proverb alive in the mouths of children who now do their thinking in English, or whether the warmth and the wisdom that hold the house together thin out into something colder and more borrowed.
