# Plot: the war of the Golden Stool

## Taxonomy

[play_ghana](play_ghana.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

By the turn of the twentieth century the foreigner has pressed into the Asante heartland, and the Asantehene himself has been taken away into exile. Then comes a demand that no Asante can stomach: that the Golden Stool, the sacred seat that holds the soul of the people, be handed over for a stranger to sit upon. In the council the men fall silent, weighing the cost.

## Action

Into that silence rises Yaa Asantewaa, the queen mother of Ejisu, who shames the hesitating men and takes up the defence of the stool herself, declaring that if the men will not go forward then the women will. The Asante rally to defend the soul of their nation, and though the contest goes hard against them, the one thing the foreigner came for he never gets: the Golden Stool is hidden away and kept, its soul never handed over and never sat upon. The kingdom is overcome, but its soul is saved, and the memory of the elder woman who would not yield becomes a thing the whole nation carries.

## Stage

- [Yaa Asantewaa](persona_yaa_asantewaa.md): the queen mother who rises to defend the sacred seat when the men hesitate.
- [the Golden Stool](position_the_golden_stool.md): the soul of the nation, demanded by the foreigner and never surrendered.

## Tension

A people may lose the contest and still keep the thing that matters most. The foreigner reaches for the soul of a nation and is met by an elder woman who knows that some things are not for surrender; the kingdom is overcome, the cost is borne with dignity, and yet the stool is saved, so that what looks like a defeat carries within it the proof that the soul of a people cannot be taken by force.
