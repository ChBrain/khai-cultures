# Ghana: a khai culture

Ghana staged as a full khai play, ISO GH, language en. A young republic wearing
the borrowed name of an ancient empire, the first nation below the Sahara to take
its freedom, as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast,
the roles its language and faith encode, its places, its practices, and its
defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the house that holds
this production and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its Estate; a
culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_ghana.md](play_ghana.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Ghana's
  profile (a warm, deeply communal people, hospitable and unhurried, honouring
  the elder and the chief, faithful out loud, and given to saying the hard thing
  softly through a proverb) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the golden land and the rise of the Asante under the
  Golden Stool, the war of the Golden Stool, the castles at the coast, and
  independence in 1957 - each casting the elements it needs, and each carrying the
  sorrow at the coast soberly and with dignity.
- **Personas:** Kwame Nkrumah, Yaa Asantewaa, Kofi Annan, and Ama Ata Aidoo as
  the historic cast, plus one archetype, the Okyeame (the chief's linguist and
  keeper of the proverb). Each is wired to the language engine by the
  language-crossing it holds, between the Akan, Ga, Ewe, and northern mother
  tongues and the English laid over them.
- **Positions:** akwaaba (the law of welcome and the truth that a person is a
  person through other persons), the Golden Stool (chieftaincy held as a sacred
  trust handed down from the ancestors), the proverb (wisdom inherited and said
  softly, with Anansi the spider and the adinkra signs).
- **Places:** Accra (the capital by the sea where the Gold Coast became Ghana and
  the Black Star was raised), Cape Coast (the old castle by the sea kept standing
  as a place of sober remembrance and return).
- **Processes:** the durbar (the gathering where the chief is carried high and a
  whole community shows itself to itself), the kitchen (the feeding that is the
  very act of welcome, the jollof and the fufu and the open pot).
- **Pieces:** kente (the woven royal cloth of the Asante and the Ewe, each
  pattern a proverb), adinkra (the stamped Akan symbols, each a proverb made
  visible, sankofa first among them).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the memory of the slave trade carried through the coastal castles is
treated soberly and with dignity, never paraded; the staging and the architecture
are original work.
