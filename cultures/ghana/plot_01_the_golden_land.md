# Plot: the golden land

## Taxonomy

[play_ghana](play_ghana.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

Long before any flag, the forests and the trade routes of this land gave up their gold, and the name of the far older Ghana empire of the western Sudan was carried south on the tongues of the traders as a byword for a place of wealth and of kings. In the central forest the Asante clans, scattered and quarrelling, await the one thing that will bind them into a single people.

## Action

The priest Okomfo Anokye, the story is told, calls down from the sky the Golden Stool, the Sika Dwa Kofi, said to hold the very soul of the Asante, and seats Osei Tutu upon the kingship it anchors, gathering the clans under one Asantehene. Around the stool rises a whole order: the chiefs and the queen mothers, the elders in council, and the okyeame whose mouth carries the chief's word wrapped in proverb. The Ga by the sea, the Ewe to the east, the Dagomba and the northern kingdoms keep their own old houses and drums beside it. A land of many peoples orders itself by the stool, the council, and the spoken word, and the gold gives it the name a young nation will one day reach back to claim.

## Stage

- [the Okyeame](persona_okyeame.md): the linguist who carries the chief's word to the gathered people, wrapped in proverb.
- [the Golden Stool](position_the_golden_stool.md): the sacred seat that binds the Asante clans into one people.

## Tension

A land rich enough to lend its gold its very name orders itself not by wealth alone but by a sacred trust: the stool that is no throne but a soul, the chief who is custodian and not owner, the word that passes only through the proverb. The greatness here is held in trust from the ancestors for the children, and the whole story after will turn on whether that trust is kept.
