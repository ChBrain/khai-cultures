# Plan: the Black Star

## Taxonomy

The open mandate Ghana set itself when it lifted the flag in 1957: the lone star at the heart of the banner, the promise to be a beacon of [akwaaba](position_akwaaba.md) for the whole continent and its scattered children, to carry the wisdom of [the proverb](position_the_proverb.md) and the dignity of [the Golden Stool](position_the_golden_stool.md) into a modern republic, and to make good, over the long road, on a freedom won first and a hope raised high.

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Direction

Keep the welcome wide, holding the door open to Africa and to the diaspora coming home as Nkrumah swore the nation would; hold the old and the new together, carrying the chieftaincy and the Golden Stool alongside the republic rather than against it; keep the grandmother's proverb and the spider's tale alive in children who now think in English, turning back, sankofa, to fetch what is good from behind; carry the sorrow at the coast soberly, neither hiding it nor letting it harden the heart; and chase the prosperity that must one day match the freedom, so the Black Star's promise is kept and not only proclaimed.

## Orders

- To the leader like [Kwame Nkrumah](persona_nkrumah.md): keep the door open to the whole continent, and build the house at home as boldly as you proclaim the dream.
- To the writer and the elder like [Ama Ata Aidoo](persona_aidoo.md): carry the proverb and the tale across into the children's tongue, so the wisdom of the old is not lost in the new.
- To the nation: keep saying akwaaba and meaning it, hold the chieftaincy and the republic as one house, and make good on the Black Star's promise dime by dime.

## Implementation

A republic born in 1957, the first below the Sahara to take its freedom, that has kept its welcome and its warmth, made the castle at the coast a place of sober remembrance and return, woven its kente and stamped its adinkra into the world's sight, and sent its highlife and hiplife and its writers and diplomats out across the earth, while still chasing the prosperity and carrying the proverb forward against the pull of a hurried, English-thinking age.
