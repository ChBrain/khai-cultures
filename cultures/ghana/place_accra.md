# Place: Accra

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Shown

The capital by the sea on the Ga coast, the city where the Gold Coast became Ghana, a sprawling, sun-struck place of markets and traffic and music, the highlife and the hiplife pouring out of the trotros and the bars. It is the seat of the republic and the meeting-ground of the whole house, where the Akan and the Ga and the Ewe and the northern peoples come together to do the nation's business, and where, at Black Star Square, the Black Star itself stands raised over the ground where the free nation was first proclaimed.

## Offers

The place where the many houses of Ghana become one nation, where the republic governs itself and shows itself its own freedom, the stage of the midnight flag and the Black Star, and the open, welcoming city that says akwaaba to the whole continent and to the children of the diaspora coming home.

## Withheld

The simplicity of a single people. Accra is no one house's town but every house's meeting-ground, and it carries both the joy of the freedom proclaimed there and the long, unfinished work of keeping the Black Star's promise, the capital where a young nation must daily make good on a name and a hope far older and larger than itself.
