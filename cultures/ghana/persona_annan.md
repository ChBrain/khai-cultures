# Persona: Kofi Annan

## Taxonomy

[akwaaba](position_akwaaba.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The son of Kumasi, born of Fante and Asante stock into a family of chiefs, who rose to be the first from below the Sahara to lead the United Nations as its Secretary-General, and who carried the quiet Ghanaian courtesy onto the largest stage the world keeps. Soft-spoken, patient, and unfailingly dignified, he was the diplomat as elder, the man who took the matter to the council, wrapped the hard word gently, and held the door open between peoples who would not otherwise sit at one table. His mother tongue is Akan, the Twi and Fante he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [hears](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) at home; but his life's work was conducted in [English](position_language_en_gh.md) and in French, the tongues of the world's councils, which he [carried as his own](process_speaking_carried.md), [reading and following](process_reading_followed.md) the papers of a hundred nations and [drafting](process_writing_drafted.md) in them the careful words on which peace might turn. He spent his life translating between peoples who could not hear one another, the elder of the world's council whose whole craft was to carry one side's meaning into the other's tongue without losing it, and he knew better than most that every quarrel between nations is, at bottom, a gap at the limit of the tongue, where what one means is not what the other hears.

## Action

In [independence (1957)](plot_04_independence_1957.md) he is the inheritor of the freedom that nation won, the Ghanaian carrying its courtesy of the careful welcome out to the world, embodying [akwaaba](position_akwaaba.md) at the scale of the whole family of nations. He makes of the small free nation's manners a way of holding the world's table open, greeting before he questions and feeding before he asks.

## Shadow

The patience that holds the door open can be mistaken for weakness, and the elder who will not shame a party to his face can be blamed for the violence he laboured to prevent; the careful welcome, offered to all sides at once, can leave the keeper of it answerable for the failures of them all. To stand between warring peoples is to be reached for by both, and Annan bore that weight.

## Tell

He answers the world's loudest quarrels in the quietest voice in the room, taking the matter to the council and wrapping the hardest truth in the gentlest words, the Ghanaian elder's courtesy carried to the very edge of the world.
