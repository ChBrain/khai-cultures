# Position: akwaaba

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The whole law of welcome held in one Akan word, akwaaba, you are welcome, and the way of being it commands: that the stranger who comes in peace is received before he is questioned, given a chair and a little water and a little food, and asked after his journey and his household before any business is so much as named. It rests on the deeper truth that a person is a person through other persons, that no one is made alone and no one is held alone, and that the household, the hometown, and even the dead and the not-yet-born sit at one table. It is hospitality not as a nicety but as the very shape of the self, and it is offered, the elders insist, whether the house has plenty or little.

## Orders

That the guest is sacred and the greeting comes before the business, so you ask after a person before you ask anything of them; that the one who has done well must carry the many, opening his hand to family, hometown, and stranger alike; and that the nation, having itself been first to freedom, must keep an open door to the whole continent and to its scattered children coming home.

## Loses

Ground when the welcome thins into mere performance, the smile offered and the heart withheld; ground, too, when the world grows so hard and hurried that the chair is no longer pulled and the guest goes unasked after, and when the burden of carrying the many curdles into resentment rather than honour, so that the open hand begins, quietly, to close.

## Drives

Lives in [Kwame Nkrumah](persona_nkrumah.md), who flung the door open to the whole continent and called its scattered children home, and in [Kofi Annan](persona_annan.md), who carried the Ghanaian courtesy of the careful welcome onto the world's largest stage. It drives Ghana to greet before it questions, to feed before it asks, and to keep saying akwaaba and meaning it.
