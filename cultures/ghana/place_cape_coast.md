# Place: Cape Coast

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Shown

The old town on the central coast where the great white castle stands above the fishing boats and the surf, one of the line of forts and castles raised along this shore for the trade that gave the Gold Coast its names. Today it is a quiet, dignified place of remembrance, the castle kept standing and its rooms kept open, where Ghanaians and the returning children of the diaspora walk in silence and where the nation holds its deepest sorrow soberly and with grace.

## Holds

- [the castles at the coast](plot_03_the_castles.md): the castle by the sea, kept standing as the place where the nation remembers, with dignity, those who were carried away.

## Offers

A place of remembrance and of return: the ground where the nation keeps faith with a grief that cannot be undone, neither hiding it nor parading it, and where the descendants of those who were carried away come back across the sea to stand, and are met with akwaaba, welcome home, over a sorrow no word can fully hold.

## Withheld

Any easy comfort or any forgetting. Cape Coast withholds the lie that the past can be tidied away; it keeps the castle standing precisely so the memory is not lost, asking the nation and the world to look soberly at what happened here and to carry it without letting it harden the heart.
