# Process: the kitchen

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

Anyone with a pot, a fire, and people to feed: the mother and the aunties at the coalpot before the family wakes, the woman at the chop bar ladling for all who come, the household cooking for a naming or a funeral. The fufu is pounded, the jollof is left to catch its smoky bottom, the waakye is wrapped in its leaf, and the food is made always for more than just oneself.

## Direction

Toward the feeding that is the very act of welcome: the jollof that every Ghanaian swears is the best on the whole continent, the waakye of rice and beans, the fufu and light soup, the kenkey and banku by the sea, the kelewele frying at the roadside. No guest is let go hungry, the pot is always made a little bigger than the household, and to be fed in a Ghanaian house is to be told, without a word, akwaaba, you are one of us now.

## Lever

It is driven by the law that the guest is sacred and that you feed before you ask, and by the truth that the table is where a household and a hometown know themselves. It holds against scarcity itself, for the pot is shared whether the house has much or little; against it stand the hurried days and the city's small kitchens, which would shrink the table the kitchen labours to keep wide.

## Echo

The kitchen makes flavourful what drives [akwaaba](position_akwaaba.md): that the welcome is not a word only but a plate set down, the guest fed before he is questioned. From this pot [Kwame Nkrumah](persona_nkrumah.md) drew the homely truth he raised to a nation's law, that the door and the table are held open to all who come in peace.
