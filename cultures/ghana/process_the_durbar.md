# Process: the durbar

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

A chief, a town, or a festival that calls the people together: the Asantehene at Akwasidae, a town at its yam festival or its homowo, a community marking a great occasion. The drums are sent out, the word goes round, and the people gather in the open ground to honour the chief and to be a people together.

## Direction

Toward the gathering where a whole community shows itself to itself: the chiefs carried high in their palanquins under the great umbrellas, draped in kente, the linguists with their gold-topped staffs, the drums and the horns sounding the praise-names, the dancing and the pouring of libation to the ancestors. It is not mere pageant but the order of the society made visible, the chief honoured, the elders seated, the people one.

## Lever

It is driven by the deep Ghanaian honouring of the chief and the elder and by the truth that a person is a person through other persons, made plain when the whole people stands as one. It holds against the pull of the hurried, scattered modern day; against it stand the cost and the time it asks, and the question of whether the young, living now in the city, will keep gathering.

## Echo

The durbar makes visible what drives [the Golden Stool](position_the_golden_stool.md): that authority is a sacred trust shown forth before the people, the chief honoured and the order kept. At its heart stands [the Okyeame](persona_okyeame.md), whose staff and whose proverb carry the chief's word to the gathered house.
