# Piece: kente

## Taxonomy

Parent group: pieces

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Place

On the shoulders of the Asantehene and the chiefs at the durbar, on the graduate at the ceremony and the bride at the wedding, on the dignitary at the great occasion, and now on the necks of the diaspora the world over: the woven cloth of the Asante and the Ewe, narrow strips sewn side by side into one bright royal cloth, the most honoured garment Ghana has given the world.

## Load Bearing

It carries the dignity and the wisdom of a people in a thing you can wear. Once the cloth of kings, woven at Bonwire and worn only by royalty, each pattern bears a name and a meaning, a proverb woven into the very threads, so that to wear a particular kente is to speak without words. It clothes the chief in his authority and the graduate in her achievement, and when the children of the diaspora drape it on, it says they belong. Without this cloth, the Ghanaian honouring of the chief, the occasion, and the proverb would have no body to wear; in the kente it becomes something woven, worn, and seen.

## Apparent

Strips of brightly coloured cloth, woven on a narrow loom and sewn together, gold and green and red and blue in bold geometric blocks. In itself it is thread and dye and patient handwork; its whole weight is in the names the patterns carry, the rank it marks, and the meaning each Ghanaian reads in it at a glance.
