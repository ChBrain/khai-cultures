# Plot: independence (1957)

[play_ghana](play_ghana.md)

## Owner

## Cue

By the middle of the twentieth century the colony called the Gold Coast is stirring, its people no longer willing to be ruled from far away. Kwame Nkrumah, returned from his studies abroad, gathers the movement under the cry of self-government now, and the whole of a continent under colonial rule watches to see whether one of its own can take back its freedom.

## Action

In Accra, at the stroke of midnight on the sixth of March, 1957, the old flag comes down and the new one goes up, red, gold, and green with a single Black Star at its heart, and the Gold Coast becomes Ghana, the first nation below the Sahara to take its freedom, wearing the borrowed name of an ancient empire to carry an old greatness forward. Nkrumah stands before the people and declares that the independence of Ghana is meaningless unless it is linked to the total liberation of the continent, and the Black Star is raised as a beacon for all of Africa and for its children scattered across the sea. A young state is born wearing an old name, and it throws its door open to a whole continent's hope.

## Stage

- [Kwame Nkrumah](persona_nkrumah.md): the leader who takes back the nation's freedom and calls the continent home.
- [akwaaba](position_akwaaba.md): the welcome flung open to all of Africa and its scattered children.
- [Accra](place_accra.md): the capital where the new flag is raised and the free nation is proclaimed.

## Tension

A young nation lifts a name far older and a promise far larger than itself. The freedom is real and the joy is real, the first below the Sahara to take it; yet the Black Star is raised so high, the welcome held so wide, and the dream of a free and united continent proclaimed so boldly, that the keeping of the promise will prove harder than the making of it, and the whole story after turns on whether the welcome and the hope can be carried past the disappointments that follow.
