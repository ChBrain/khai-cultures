# Persona: Kwame Nkrumah

## Taxonomy

[akwaaba](position_akwaaba.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The teacher's son from Nkroful in the west who went away to study and came back to lead the Gold Coast to be the first below the Sahara to lift the flag of its own freedom, the man who stood in Accra in 1957 and declared that the independence of Ghana was meaningless unless it was linked up with the total liberation of the continent. Restless, learned, and large of vision, he flung the door wide and called the whole of Africa and its scattered children home, raising the Black Star over more than one nation's hope. His mother tongue is Nzema, and the Akan of the south he [carries as his own](process_speaking_carried.md) and [hears](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) in the markets and the meetings; but the nation he was building had to be addressed in [English](position_language_en_gh.md), the tongue of the colony he was breaking from, so he [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) his Pan-African vision and [drafts](process_writing_drafted.md) his books and his speeches in it, the borrowed tongue that could reach Lagos and Nairobi and Harlem all at once where no single African tongue could. He summoned a continent of a thousand tongues to one freedom in the one tongue the colonizer had left behind, so that the very unity he preached had to pass, of necessity, through the gap at the limit of the tongue, where the dream of the many speaking as one runs up against the tongues they were each born to.

## Action

In [independence (1957)](plot_04_independence_1957.md) he leads the people to take their own freedom, raises the Black Star, and embodies [akwaaba](position_akwaaba.md) at the scale of a whole continent, throwing the door open to Africa and to its children scattered across the sea. He turns a colony into the first free nation below the Sahara and makes its freedom a welcome held out to all.

## Shadow

The vision so large can outrun the ground it stands on, and the door flung open to a continent can leave the house at home still to be built; the dream of unity, mistaking the speech for the deed, can ask of one young nation more than one young nation can carry. The Black Star raised so high casts, beneath it, the long shadow of a promise easier to proclaim than to keep.

## Tell

He stands in the Accra night and tells a free people that their own freedom is not enough until the last of the continent is free too, and means it, leaving Ghana a name larger than itself and a debt of welcome owed to all of Africa.
