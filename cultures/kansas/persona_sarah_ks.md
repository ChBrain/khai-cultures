# Persona: Sarah

## Taxonomy

[homestead resolve](position_homestead_resolve.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A fifty-two-year-old wheat farm manager in Salina. English, spoken with a prairie cadence, is her mother tongue, the language in which she [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and writes her daily crop logs. It is the [Kansan English](position_language_en_us_ks.md) of the wheat counties, the flat, understated prairie cadence of the Salina co-op. She speaks no foreign languages. Still, there is a seam she feels every day: the quick, technology-oriented talk of aircraft inspectors like Henry and the digital demands of aviation boards [she hears only like a distant echo](process_hearing_followed.md), while the patient, law-protecting rhythm of agricultural soil preservation gets treated as secondary to corporate factories and speedway entertainment. She belongs to [Kansas culture](position_culture_kansas_culture.md) through the homestead-resolve half of its character, the patience that reads the wheat field season by season.

## Action

She preserves [wheat sheaf](piece_wheat_sheaf.md) records, coordinates field conservation under [the aquifer plan](plan_kansas_ogallala_aquifer_protection.md), and studies [aviation pioneer](position_aviation_pioneer.md).

## Shadow

Her pride in traditional homestead resolve and local property rights can make her suspicious of state-wide zoning and environmental agricultural mandates, fearing they will restrict family property use.

## Tell

She looks out over the winter wheat fields and says thoughtfully: "The land has its own seasons; you can't protect prairie productivity if you're only focused on digital speed and factory assembly metrics."
