# Persona: Ruth

## Taxonomy

[literary craft](position_literary_craft.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

Fifty-two years old, coordinating the Writers' Workshop in Iowa City. English, carried in a slow prairie drawl, is her mother tongue, the language she [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) in and writes her workshop logs in every day. It's the [Iowan English](position_language_en_us_ia.md) of Iowa City - measured, unhurried, chosen word by word, same as it's spoken at the workshop table. She speaks no foreign languages. What she misses sits just past her hearing: the quick, spreadsheet-paced talk of corn breeders like George and the agriculture boards' digital paperwork, which reaches her, if at all, [like something happening a county over](process_hearing_followed.md), so that she sometimes worries the patient rhythm of literary critique gets filed under nice-to-have next to corporate farming and a Saturday night at the fairground track. She belongs to [Iowan culture](position_culture_iowan_culture.md) the way literary craft does: holding a manuscript to the same patient standard a field holds a crop.

## Action

She keeps the [typewriter](piece_typewriter.md) records in order, does her part for field conservation under [the Iowa river plan](plan_iowa_river_agricultural_runoff_control.md), and keeps half an eye on [heartland agriculture](position_heartland_agriculture.md), the way you keep half an eye on a neighbor's crop.

## Shadow

Her pride in the old writers' circles and in local county say-so can tip over into flat suspicion of any state-wide zoning rule or environmental mandate, on the guess that it's really just Des Moines telling a family what to do with its own ground.

## Tell

She sets down a dog-eared workshop manuscript and says it slow: "The written word's got its own clock. You can't call it excellence if all you're timing it against is corn yields and how fast the wifi runs."
