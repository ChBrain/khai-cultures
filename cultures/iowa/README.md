# Iowa: a khai culture

Iowa staged as a full khai play, ISO US-IA, language en. The Hawkeye State: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its Black Hawk Purchase, Iowa State Fair, and Writers' Workshop encodes, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house that holds this culture and answers for it.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_iowa.md](play_iowa.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_iowa.md](pitch_iowa.md), the Iowa key.
- **Plots (history):** the Black Hawk Purchase of 1832, the Iowa State Fair Inauguration of 1854, and the Writers' Workshop Launch of 1936.
- **Personas:** George (corn breeder) and Ruth (Writers' Workshop coordinator), written in English.
- **Positions:** heartland agriculture (corn farming pride/soil fertility/fair competitions), literary craft (narrative voice/peer review precision/writing discipline).
- **Places:** Iowa State Fair, Dey House.
- **Processes:** corn hybridization (hand-pollinating seed corn), manuscript critique (writers' peer feedback).
- **Pieces:** a corn ear, a typewriter.
- **Plan:** the Iowa river plan.
