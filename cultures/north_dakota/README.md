# North Dakota: a khai culture

North Dakota staged as a full khai play, ISO US-ND, language en. The Flickertail State: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its Red River Valley wheat boom, Garrison Dam construction, and Bakken shale oil boom encodes, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house that holds this culture and answers for it.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_north_dakota.md](play_north_dakota.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_north_dakota.md](pitch_north_dakota.md), the North Dakota key.
- **Plots (history):** the Red River Valley Wheat Boom of the 1870s, the Garrison Dam Construction of 1947, and the Bakken Shale Oil Boom of the 2000s.
- **Personas:** Harvey (wheat farmer) and Lincoln (oil rig technician), written in English.
- **Positions:** prairie agriculture (wheat farming pride/flatland soil/farming co-ops), shale energy (oil drilling engineering/energy independence/industrial logistics).
- **Places:** Red River Farm, Bakken Well.
- **Processes:** wheat threshing (mechanical grain separation), oil drilling (oil well drilling and fracturing).
- **Pieces:** a wheat sheaf, a drill bit.
- **Plan:** the Missouri River management plan.
