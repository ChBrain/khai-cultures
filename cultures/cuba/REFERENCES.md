# Cuba: References

The provenance behind this culture: the cultural framing that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-record historical and cultural
sources behind the plots, personas, places, pieces, and processes.

## Cultural framing

The pitch [pitch_es.md](pitch_es.md) is written from Cuba's broad cultural
profile - a warm, fast, present-facing collectivism centred on family, barrio and
the close group; the choteo humour carried over real hardship; a strong pride and
a stubborn resourcefulness (the everyday art of "resolver"); and, beneath it, the
ache of the divided family and the two shores. It names no dimension and carries
no number. Cuba is not among the countries with published national scores on Geert
Hofstede's six-dimension model, so this culture works from the qualitative profile
rather than from a numeric table, and the framing is kept here, on the record,
without entering the prose.

## Historical and cultural sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, the pieces, and the
processes are drawn from the public historical and cultural record. They are
credited here and never claimed; the staging is original work. The hard passages -
the Afro-Cuban slave society on which the colony was built, the wars of
independence, the 1959 revolution and the long standoff and embargo, and the
diaspora and the divided families - are staged soberly, on the public record, and
take no side.

historical individual: a composite that carries the rural campesino of the tobacco
and cane country, the decima and the tres, and the campesino root of the son
montuno.
