# Bahrain: a khai culture

Bahrain staged as a full khai play, ISO BH, language ar. An ancient island
people of the two seas, as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as
the cast, the positions its land and faith encode, its places, its practices, and
its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_bahrain.md](play_bahrain.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_ar.md](pitch_ar.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Bahrain's profile (a warm, group-first, hospitable people, patient as the
  diver, modest in number and deep of root, opening the majlis to the stranger)
  and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** ancient Dilmun, the coming of Islam and the sea trade, the
  age of pearls, and the modern awakening - each casting the elements it needs,
  and each staged soberly within Bahrain's own self-understanding.
- **Personas:** Geoffrey Bibby (the archaeologist who recovered Dilmun's name),
  Ibrahim al-Arrayedh (the great Bahraini poet), Mohammed bin Faris (the master
  of sawt music), and Ahmad ibn Majid (the great navigator of the Gulf) as the
  historical cast, plus one archetype, the pearl diver (al-ghawwas), who carries
  the breath and patience the whole age of pearls rested on. Each is wired to the
  language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** Dilmun (the deep antiquity, the land of immortality), the two
  seas (the sweet springs welling beneath the salt sea, in the very name
  al-Bahrayn), the sea (the patient, dangerous father of an island people).
- **Places:** Qal'at al-Bahrain (the tell that holds Dilmun's capital and five
  thousand years of port, a UNESCO site), Muharraq (the pearling city and the
  Pearling Path, a UNESCO heritage).
- **Processes:** pearl diving (the great summer season of breath and patience),
  the sawt gathering (the open majlis bound by music, coffee, and dates).
- **Pieces:** the pearl (the drop of light drawn from the deep, long the finest in
  the world), the dhow (the wooden sanbuk that was the sea people's floating
  home).
