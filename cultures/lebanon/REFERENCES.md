# Lebanon: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical and cultural
sources behind the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_ar.md](pitch_ar.md) is written from Lebanon's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record without
entering the prose.

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; the pitch reads Lebanon as a warm,
group-centered, present-focused culture, and names no dimension.

## Historical and cultural sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn
from the public historical and cultural record. They are credited here and never
claimed; the staging is original work. All of Lebanon's communities and faiths are
treated with equal dignity as a shared homeland, and any hardships the country has
passed through are acknowledged soberly, with no detail and taking no side.

The one defining archetype, [the Mountain Host](persona_the_villager.md), is not a
historical individual but a composite: the villager of the Lebanese mountain whose
open table, harvest, and hospitality carry the homeland's love of life and its
welcome of the stranger.
