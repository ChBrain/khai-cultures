# Lebanon: a khai culture

Lebanon staged as a full khai play, ISO LB, language ar. A small homeland of the
cedars and the sea, ancient as the alphabet, staged as a theatre: its history as
the plot line, its people as the cast, the positions its land and communities
encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a
single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_lebanon.md](play_lebanon.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_ar.md](pitch_ar.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Lebanon's profile (warm and people-first, family- and group-centered, openly
  expressive and life-loving, with a present focus and a deep attachment to the
  near) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** Phoenicia and the alphabet, the refuge of the mountain,
  the Awakening (the Nahda), and the diaspora and the enduring voice. Hardship
  that the country has passed through is acknowledged soberly and with dignity,
  with no detail and taking no side.
- **Personas:** Khalil Gibran (the poet-painter of "The Prophet"), Fairuz (the
  beloved voice of Lebanon), Cadmus (the legendary Phoenician bringer of the
  alphabet), and Saint Charbel (the mountain hermit honored across communities),
  plus one archetype, the Mountain Host (the villager whose open table is a
  homeland). Each is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it
  holds.
- **Positions:** the Phoenician heritage (the seafaring people who gave the world
  the alphabet), the cedars and the mountain (the geography of mountain meeting
  sea that shaped the spirit), and the mosaic of communities (the many faiths
  sharing one homeland, treated with equal dignity).
- **Places:** Byblos (one of the oldest cities, the cradle of the letter) and
  Beirut (the capital of culture and openness, ever rising anew).
- **Processes:** the mezze table (the long table of hospitality and joy) and the
  harvest season (the family gathering on the mountain to gather the year).
- **Pieces:** the cedar (the emblem on the flag, the sign of endurance) and the
  oud (the instrument that carries the Lebanese song and its longing).
- **Plan:** the Lebanese diaspora (keeping the worldwide Lebanese one homeland, a
  bridge of return rather than a parting).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical and
cultural sources are recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never
enters the prose; the pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain
are credited, never claimed; all of Lebanon's communities and faiths are treated
with equal dignity as a shared homeland, and the hardships the country has passed
through are acknowledged soberly, with no detail and taking no side. The staging
and the architecture are original work.
