# Nepal: a khai culture

Nepal staged as a full khai play, ISO NP, language ne (Nepali, Devanagari). The
land in the lap of the Himalaya, where many peoples and two faiths live as one,
rendered as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the
roles its languages and faith encode, its places, its practices, and its
defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_nepal.md](play_nepal.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_ne.md](pitch_ne.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Nepal's
  profile (a warm, group-first society of high power distance and strong family
  and community bonds, patient and restrained, finding contentment within the
  hardship of the mountains) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** Lumbini and the birth of the Buddha, the golden age of the
  Kathmandu Valley, unification and the birth of the Nepali language, and modern
  Nepal - each casting the elements it needs, and each staging the nation from
  within its own self-understanding.
- **Personas:** Prithvi Narayan Shah (the unifier), Bhanubhakta Acharya (the
  Adikavi, first poet of the Nepali language), Araniko (the medieval Newar master
  who carried the pagoda across Asia), and Laxmi Prasad Devkota (the Mahakavi),
  plus one archetype, the Sherpa (the mountain porter who carries the world to the
  summit). Each is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** dharma (the intertwined Hindu and Buddhist faith), the Himalaya
  (the mountains that name and shape the nation), and peoples and languages (the
  many castes and tongues held as one garden).
- **Places:** the Kathmandu Valley (the heart of art, faith, and power, with its
  pagodas, Pashupatinath, and Boudhanath), and Sagarmatha and the Himalaya (the
  highest summit and the face of the mountain nation).
- **Processes:** Dashain (the great festival that reunites the family), and the
  pilgrimage and the trail (faith and mountain met in a single step).
- **Pieces:** the khukuri (the curved blade of work and Gurkha valour), and the
  prayer flag (the five-coloured cloth that carries prayer on the wind).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the unification of the country and the transition from monarchy to
republic are treated soberly on the public record, taking no political side; the
staging and the architecture are original work.
