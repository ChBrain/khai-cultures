# Belarus: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces, with recent events treated factually on
the public record.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_be.md](pitch_be.md) is written from Belarus's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no
number. The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the
record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Belarus score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ----------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 95                      |
| Individualism                 | 25                      |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 20                      |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 95                      |
| Long Term Orientation         | 81                      |
| Indulgence                    | 15                      |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, and published
data for Belarus is limited and less firmly established than for many countries;
the figures are used here only as a direction, not as exact measurement.

The two defining personas, [Valiantsina](persona_valiantsina.md) and
[Ales](persona_ales.md), are archetypes, not historical individuals: composites
that carry the tension between the older generation (quiet endurance, daily life
in Russian, only fragments of the village mother tongue) and the younger one (the
post-2020 reclaiming of Belarusian by will and the pull of English in exile).
