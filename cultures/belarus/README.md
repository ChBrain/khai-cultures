# Belarus: a khai culture

Belarus staged as a full khai play, ISO BY, language be. A long-quiet nation,
long under others, as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the
cast, the roles its language and temperament encode, its places, its practices,
and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_belarus.md](play_belarus.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_be.md](pitch_be.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Belarus's
  profile (deference to those above, a collective and rural cast, anxiety for
  certainty, gentle endurance and a long melancholy) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Grand Duchy and Skaryna's printing, the Nasha Niva
  revival, the partisan land of the Second World War, and 2020 - each casting the
  elements it needs.
- **Personas:** Francysk Skaryna, Yanka Kupala, and Vasil Bykau as the historic
  cast, plus two defining archetypes, Valiantsina (older, quiet endurance, living
  in Russian and keeping only fragments of the Belarusian of her village
  grandmother) and Ales (younger, reclaiming Belarusian by will after 2020 and
  carrying it into exile in English). Each is wired to the language engine by the
  language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** беларуская мова (the Belarusian language, once the Grand Duchy's
  chancery tongue, now severely Russified and UNESCO-vulnerable), памяркоўнасць
  (the temperament of quiet, tolerant moderation and its 2020 rupture),
  партызанскі край (the partisan land and the catastrophic war that defines the
  official self-image).
- **Places:** Мінск (the capital razed in the war and rebuilt as a Soviet showcase,
  the squares of the 2020 protests), Полацак (the oldest city, Skaryna's homeland,
  the cradle of Belarusian letters).
- **Processes:** Купалле (the midsummer pagan-folk festival of fires, wreaths, and
  the fern flower), талака (communal mutual-aid labour).
- **Pieces:** Пагоня (the mounted-knight coat of arms, the Grand Duchy heritage),
  бусел (the white stork, the gentle rural soul of the land).
