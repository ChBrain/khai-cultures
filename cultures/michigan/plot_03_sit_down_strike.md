# Plot: Flint Sit-Down Strike 1936

## Taxonomy

[play_michigan](play_michigan.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

In December 1936, automotive workers at General Motors plants in Flint begin a sit-down strike, refusing to leave the manufacturing buildings.

## Action

The protest establishes the collective bargaining power of the UAW, transforming labor laws and union membership across the nation. The [automotive assembly](process_automotive_assembly.md) line is halted during the strike. Marcus researches the historical union contracts, while Clara records family stories of the early Flint strike coordinators.

## Stage

- [Up North](position_up_north.md) : the quiet, rural retreats where workers historically sought refuge during seasonal layoffs.
- [automotive assembly](process_automotive_assembly.md) : the physical manufacturing plants that served as the strike's battleground.
- [lake shipping](process_lake_shipping.md) : the raw material supply transport that was disrupted by the industrial shutdown.

## Tension

The strike leads to violent clashes between workers and local police, exposing deep class divisions and corporate resistance to collective bargaining.
