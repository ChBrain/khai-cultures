# Position: Michigan English

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The voice of Michigan English, the flat northern vowel of the Great Lakes State and its Inland North shift. It possesses the plain midwestern cadence heard from the Detroit shop floor to the Straits of Mackinac, the idiom of the assembly line and the ferry deck.

## Orders

What counts as native speech in Michigan, the talk of the plant, the union hall, and the lakeshore dock. It orders how the Motor City and the Up North towns address one another, holding the level, understated turn that marks a Michigander.

## Loses

Ground to the flattened broadcast English of national media, which pulls the Inland North vowels back toward a uniform standard. It also loses the lakeshore idiom to the engineering and corporate vocabulary of the auto industry, which overlays the old shipping talk.

## Drives

It drives Michigan to keep its own cadence from the shop floor to the straits. It carries the mother tongue of ferry captain [Clara](persona_clara.md), the language in which she speaks and writes her daily logs; and it carries the direct midwestern cadence of automotive engineer [Marcus](persona_marcus.md), the language in which he speaks, hears, and thinks his professional life. So the one cadence shows who holds it.
