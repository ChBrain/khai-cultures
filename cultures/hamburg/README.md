# Hamburg (Freie und Hansestadt Hamburg): a khai culture

Hamburg staged as a full khai play, ISO DE-HH, language de. The second largest city in Germany: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its maritime pride and merchant elite encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house that holds this culture and answers for it.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_hamburg.md](play_hamburg.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_hamburg.md](pitch_hamburg.md), the Hamburg key.
- **Plots (history):** the Barbarossa charter of 1189, the construction of the Speicherstadt in 1888, and the development of the St. Pauli subculture.
- **Personas:** Helmut (traditional patrician merchant "Pfeffersack" archetype) and Jan (modern automated terminal crane operator), written in German.
- **Positions:** der Kaufmannsstolz (merchant pride / Pfeffersack), das Tor zur Welt (gateway to the world), der Kiez (St. Pauli culture).
- **Places:** die Speicherstadt (historic warehouse district), die Reeperbahn (St. Pauli amusement street).
- **Processes:** der Fischmarkt (Sunday morning fish market).
- **Pieces:** das Barbarossa-Privileg (the charter of 1189), die Elbphilharmonie (modern concert hall monument).
- **Plan:** die Elbvertiefung (river deepening and port modernization plan).
