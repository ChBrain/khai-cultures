# Alaska: a khai culture

Alaska staged as a full khai play, ISO US-AK, language en. The Last Frontier State: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its Klondike gold rush, Prudhoe Bay oil strike, and Native Claims Settlement Act encodes, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house that holds this culture and answers for it.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_alaska.md](play_alaska.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_alaska.md](pitch_alaska.md), the Alaska key.
- **Plots (history):** the Klondike Gold Rush of 1896, the Prudhoe Bay Oil Strike of 1968, and the Alaska Native Claims Settlement Act of 1971.
- **Personas:** Karl (pipeline technician) and Siku (Inupiat hunter), written in English.
- **Positions:** arctic survival (extreme cold endurance/subsistence hunting), frontier wealth (resource extraction/pipeline logistics).
- **Places:** Prudhoe Bay, Kenai Fjords.
- **Processes:** salmon fishing (gillnet harvesting), pipeline monitoring (thermal inspections).
- **Pieces:** a harpoon head, a pipeline valve.
- **Plan:** the caribou plan.
