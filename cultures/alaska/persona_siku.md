# Persona: Siku

## Taxonomy

[arctic survival](position_arctic_survival.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A fifty-two-year-old Inupiat hunter in Barrow. [Alaskan English](position_language_en_us_ak.md), carried in a warm, unhurried drawl, is his mother tongue, the language he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and writes into his daily community logs. He speaks no foreign languages. Here is where it thins out for him: the quick, screen-lit talk of oil technicians like Karl and the digital demands of the pipeline boards [reach him only as a far-off signal](process_hearing_followed.md), and he feels the patient, season-by-season work of protecting the caribou keeps getting ranked below corporate oil and transport. He belongs to the [Last Frontier](position_culture_last_frontier.md), the state's double inheritance, and he holds fast to the half carried by the coastal hunting communities.

## Action

He preserves [harpoon head](piece_harpoon_head.md) records, coordinates field conservation under [the caribou plan](plan_arctic_caribou_protection.md), and studies [frontier wealth](position_frontier_wealth.md).

## Shadow

His pride in traditional tribal land sovereignty and independent hunting rights can make him suspicious of state-wide zoning and environmental agricultural mandates, fearing they will restrict family property use.

## Tell

He looks out over the arctic ice and says thoughtfully: "The ocean has its own laws; you can't protect whale migration if you're only focused on digital speed and pipeline transport metrics."
