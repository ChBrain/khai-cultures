# Norway: a khai culture

Norway staged as a full khai play, ISO NO, language no. A long, steep, nature-
bound and newly rich country as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its
people as the cast, the roles its language encodes, its places, its practices,
and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_norway.md](play_norway.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_no.md](pitch_no.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Norway's profile (nature-bound, egalitarian, sober, rich but restrained) and
  naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Battle of Stiklestad (1030), the Constitution at
  Eidsvoll (1814), the dissolution of the union with Sweden (1905), and the North
  Sea oil discovery (1969) - each casting the elements it needs (the oil plot
  casts a position and a place, no persona).
- **Personas:** St. Olav and Fridtjof Nansen as the historic cast, plus two
  defining archetypes, Kari (older, friluftsliv and dugnad) and Sondre (younger,
  global, the open world). Each is wired to the language engine by the language-
  crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** allemannsretten (the right to roam), Janteloven (the law of
  modesty), oljefondet (the sovereign-wealth restraint).
- **Places:** Oslo (the capital), Eidsvoll (where the constitution was written).
- **Processes:** dugnaden, friluftslivet.
- **Pieces:** bunaden (the national costume), brunosten (brown cheese).
