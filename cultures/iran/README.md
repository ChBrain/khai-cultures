# Iran: a khai culture

Iran staged as a full khai play, ISO IR, language fa. An ancient civilization that
kept its tongue, its poets, and its spring across two and a half millennia, set as
a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the dispositions
its language and faith encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects,
all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house that
holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its Estate; a
culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_iran.md](play_iran.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_fa.md](pitch_fa.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Iran's
  profile (warmth and hospitality, indirection and taarof, a deep collective and
  family bond, a long historical memory, and a poetry-steeped feeling for beauty
  and the passing moment) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Achaemenid Empire, the old faith and the coming of
  Islam, the Shahnameh and the survival of Persian, and the golden age of poetry
  and the garden, each casting the elements it needs, and each treating historical
  change soberly and from within Iran's own self-understanding.
- **Personas:** Ferdowsi, Hafez, Omar Khayyam, and Cyrus the Great as the historic
  cast, plus one defining archetype, the carpet weaver who passes the hand-art of
  the garden from one generation to the next. Each is wired to the language engine
  by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** poetry (verse as the soul of life, not its ornament), Iranshahr
  (the enduring sense of one ancient land that stays itself through every change),
  and the arts (beauty made by hand, from the carpet to the garden, the paradise on
  earth).
- **Places:** Persepolis and Shiraz (the Achaemenid stone and the cradle of the
  ghazal), Isfahan (half the world, the height of Persian art, square, mosque, and
  bazaar).
- **Processes:** Nowruz (the ancient new year and the haft-sin spread for the
  return of spring), the poetry night (the divan opened for a fal, the Shahnameh
  chanted in the teahouse).
- **Pieces:** the Persian carpet (the garden that never withers, woven knot by
  knot), the Shahnameh (the epic that kept the Persian tongue alive).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; faith, the coming of Islam, and historical change are treated soberly and
from within Iran's own enduring cultural self-understanding, taking no political
side; the staging and the architecture are original work.
