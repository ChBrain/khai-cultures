# Iran: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical and literary
sources behind the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_fa.md](pitch_fa.md) is written from Iran's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record without
entering the prose.

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group.

## Historical and literary sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn
from the public historical and literary record. They are credited here and never
claimed; the staging is original work. Faith, the coming of Islam, and historical
change are treated soberly and from within Iran's own enduring cultural
self-understanding, taking no political side and dwelling on no conflict.
