# Pitch: the Wyoming key

## Taxonomy

The pitch of [Wyoming](play_wyoming.md). It tunes the plainspoken, wind-worn, and fence-line-neighborly expression of life in the Equality State.

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Tenor

Wind first, then the weather, then whatever you came to say - that's the order business gets done in, because in a state this empty everyone is two handshakes from everyone else, and the handshake outweighs the paperwork. The talk stays plain and unhurried, one working vocabulary whether read off a geyser gauge or called across a branding pen, turning formal only for what's worth keeping - an aquifer plan, a historic courthouse.

## Undertow

Under the neighborliness runs a fault line between mountain towns fattening on tourist season and ranch land still measured by the calf crop. Every well punched for a new subdivision reopens the fight over what the watershed can spare, meeting a rancher's flat refusal to let Cheyenne, or Washington, say what he pumps from his own ground.

## Nerve

It strikes the chord of a debt owed and a handshake kept: family labor worth more than a resume, and a grazing board's word carrying a signed contract's weight.

## Echo

The hiss of a geyser letting off steam on schedule, the creak of saddle leather in a cold morning - loud, damp sounds that never argue their case.
