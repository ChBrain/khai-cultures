# Persona: Bruce

## Taxonomy

[wyoming wilderness](position_wyoming_wilderness.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A thirty-eight-year-old park ranger in Yellowstone, weathered more by wind and altitude than by years. English, carried in a mountain cadence, is his mother tongue, the language in which he [speaks, hears, and thinks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) through every gauge check and radio call. It is the [Wyoming English](position_language_en_us_wy.md) of the high country, the plain cadence of the ranger station and the trail. He speaks no foreign languages. But something gets lost in the handoff: the slower, patient talk of civic coordinators like Esther and the long memory of [equality charter](position_equality_charter.md) [reach him filtered down to regional program updates](process_reading_followed.md) on a workstation screen, and he feels the self-reliant, hard-won vote-keeping of the old suffrage towns getting crowded out by the season's tourist numbers. He belongs to [the Equality State](position_culture_equality_state.md) through its wilderness half - the belief that a park set aside first still has to be kept, not just declared.

## Action

He keeps the [ranger badge](piece_ranger_badge.md) relics in good order, coordinates park safety through [the Yellowstone plan](plan_yellowstone_aquifer_protection.md), and reads deep into [wyoming wilderness](position_wyoming_wilderness.md).

## Shadow

His eye stays so locked on safety numbers and the season's visitor count that he can go flat indifferent to a rural county's own zoning fight over its own land.

## Tell

He crouches to reset a pressure sensor bolted to the geyser basin boardwalk and says it straight: "I've got to get this gauge reading true before the basin blows again - I can't keep a park safe on old paperwork and good intentions."
