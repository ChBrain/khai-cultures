# Persona: Bruce

## Taxonomy

[wyoming wilderness](position_wyoming_wilderness.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A thirty-eight-year-old park ranger in Yellowstone. English, spoken with a mountain cadence, is his mother tongue, the language in which he [speaks, hears, and thinks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), the defining code of his daily mechanical checks. It is the [Wyoming English](position_language_en_us_wy.md) of the Yellowstone high country, the plain mountain cadence of the ranger station and the trail. He speaks no foreign languages. Yet the loss lies in the seam: The slower, tradition-bound talk of civic coordinators like Esther and the quiet history of [equality charter](position_equality_charter.md) [he reads only as regional program updates](process_reading_followed.md) on his workstation, feeling that the patient, self-reliant vote preservation of early suffrage communities is being forgotten in the race for high-volume tourism development.

## Action

He preserves [ranger badge](piece_ranger_badge.md) relics, coordinates park safety under [the Yellowstone plan](plan_yellowstone_aquifer_protection.md), and studies [wyoming wilderness](position_wyoming_wilderness.md).

## Shadow

His intense focus on safety metrics and tourist numbers can make him indifferent to municipal environmental zoning problems in rural counties.

## Tell

He adjusts a telemetry sensor on a geyser basin boardwalk and says directly: "We are calibrating geothermal pressure sensors; you can't run a safe national park if you're only focused on historic local preservation."
