# Position: the Equality State

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

Belonging to a territory that granted women the vote before it was even a state, and that set aside its geothermal basins and its igneous monoliths as the world's first national park and first national monument before most of the country had a word for such a thing. It carries [the equality charter](position_equality_charter.md), the civic conviction that a franchise once extended is a right to be defended rather than a favor to be revisited, and it carries [wyoming wilderness](position_wyoming_wilderness.md), the wilderness-protection instinct that treats a geyser basin or a rock tower as a public trust rather than a resource to be spent. It speaks [Wyoming English](position_language_en_us_wy.md), the plain mountain cadence of the ranger station and the town council alike. What it hands to whoever grows up in it is the sense that being first to grant a right or set aside a wonder is not a boast to be retired but a standard the state has to keep meeting.

## Orders

That a ballot cast and a boardwalk built over a geyser basin both be treated as things a person is answerable for, kept in good order and handed on intact; that a claim to being first, in suffrage or in conservation, be honored by continuing the practice rather than resting on the historical fact of having started it; that the county road and the county courthouse be trusted before a distant mandate, even as the same trust is extended to the rangers and the historians who keep the state's civic and wild inheritance in working order; and that whoever draws a living from the land, whether from the herd or the tourist season, be judged by the same plain standard of care for what the next season will need.

## Loses

Ground when the pride in having been first curdles into complacency, the charter recited as history rather than kept as practice, and the wilderness protection treated as a settled inheritance rather than a duty renewed against every new demand on the aquifers beneath the geothermal basins; ground when the fast, tourism-driven talk of the park economy and the slower, law-protecting talk of the civic reformers stop hearing each other, each reading the other's season, the ranger's high traffic count or the historian's ballot ledger, as somebody else's problem; and ground in a wariness of state-wide or federal mandates that can, at its hardest edge, resist even the water and land protections the state's own wonders now require.

## Drives

Drives Wyomingites to hold the ballot box and the ranger badge as one inheritance, the conviction that a right extended and a wonder protected are both promises that have to be kept choosing rather than facts the state was simply given. It carries the memory of a territory that led the nation in women's suffrage into every later argument over who gets heard and who gets protected, and it carries the memory of the world's first national park into every later argument over how much of the wild high country a mineral-extraction economy is allowed to spend, pressing the state to prove that being first at something is worth nothing unless it is still being done.
