## Name

Wyoming, the Equality State, high and wind-scoured in the Mountain West between the Rockies and the plains. ISO US-WY, language en. Fewer people call it home than any state but one, and the ones who do figure that's no accident - it leaves room enough for a herd to move, a geyser to breathe, and a person's word to still carry.

## Arc

Wyoming came up wind-first and people-last, Rocky Mountain high country where a handshake still closes more than a signature does. In 1869, when it was nobody's business to allow it, the territory gave its women the vote before any government on earth had thought to - not a slogan but a fact, and the state has answered to the name Equality State on the strength of it ever since. Three years later Congress fenced off the geyser basins and canyons of Yellowstone as the world's first national park, and in 1906 Devil's Tower went down as the nation's first national monument, a bare stone plug rising out of the plains as if left there on purpose. What holds the arc together is a plain, stubborn instinct to keep what was set aside - the ballot once granted, the wild ground once protected - even while the cattle still need feeding, the coal still comes out of the ground, and the aquifer under the geothermal basins has to be watched like a fence line.

## Triggers

**[Plot 1: The Women's Suffrage Act 1869](plot_01_suffrage.md)**

In 1869, with nobody outside the territory paying it much mind, Wyoming hands its women the ballot - the first government on the continent to do it, decided as a plain matter of who counts rather than a favor granted.

**[Plot 2: The Yellowstone National Park Establishment 1872](plot_02_yellowstone.md)**

In 1872 Congress sets the geyser basins and alpine valleys of Yellowstone apart from the taking, calling into being the world's first national park - proof a wonder can outlast whoever might want to cash it in.

**[Plot 3: The Devil's Tower Memorial 1906](plot_03_devils_tower.md)**

In 1906 Theodore Roosevelt looks at the bare stone tower rising off the plains and names it the nation's first National Monument, Devil's Tower standing witness that some ground gets set aside before anyone thinks to ask twice.

## Stakes

Whether a state built on mineral extraction and a swelling tourist season can hold the aquifer plan it has staked under the geothermal basins, or whether the wells run dry on the very wonder that put Wyoming on the map first.
