# Persona: Chloe

## Taxonomy

[the California Dream](position_california_dream.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A forty-two-year-old organic winegrower in Napa Valley. English, the [Californian English](position_language_en_us_ca.md) of the valley, is her mother tongue, the language she [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and writes into her marketing stories. [Spanish](../mexico/position_language_es_mx.md) [she puts on like a coat](process_speaking_worn.md) for her daily work in the fields, coordinating with the laborers who work it beside her. Here is where it thins out for her: the quick, disruptive tech narratives of Sam and the digital dreams of the Silicon Valley boom [reach her only like a distant echo](process_hearing_followed.md), and she feels the patient, season-dependent rhythm of agricultural work keeps getting outrun by everybody's rush toward digital efficiency. She belongs to [California culture](position_culture_california_culture.md), and hers is the older of its two rushes, the one that reinvents itself on soil rather than on a term sheet.

## Action

She practices [wine making](process_wine_making.md) on her family land, coordinates resources under [the California water plan](plan_water_conservation.md), and studies the values of [tech optimism](position_innovation.md).

## Shadow

Her focus on organic winemaking can make her elitist, targeting luxury markets while ignoring the economic pressures facing ordinary agricultural workers in the valley.

## Tell

She looks at a cluster of ripening grapes and says thoughtfully: "You can't speed up the seasons with an app; the wine takes the time it needs, and the soil doesn't care about your startup funding."
