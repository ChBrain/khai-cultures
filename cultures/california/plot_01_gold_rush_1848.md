# Plot: The Gold Rush 1848

## Taxonomy

[play_california](play_california.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

In January 1848, James W. Marshall finds gold flakes at Sutter's Mill in Coloma, triggering an international influx of fortune-seekers.

## Action

The massive migration transforms California from a sparsely populated territory into a booming state and establishes [the California Dream](position_california_dream.md) as a culture of sudden wealth and reinvention. The event draws people from around the globe. Chloe reflects on how her family's historic vineyard land was originally bought by a gold miner who chose agriculture over mining.

## Stage

- [the California Dream](position_california_dream.md) : the belief in the possibility of sudden success and beginning anew.
- [Yosemite Valley](place_yosemite.md) : the protected wilderness that stands as a contrast to the environmental destruction of hydraulic mining.
- [wine making](process_wine_making.md) : the agricultural industry that flourished as the gold fields faded.

## Tension

The rapid expansion leads to the systematic displacement and devastation of the Native American tribes in the region and the ecological destruction of riverbeds.
