# Sudan: a khai culture

Sudan staged as a full khai play, ISO SD, language ar (a Sudanese Arabic voice on
a Modern Standard Arabic matrix). The land of Kush and the two Niles, of Sufism
and a hospitality that knows no stranger, as a theatre: its history as the plot
line, its people as the cast, the roles its language and faith encode, its places,
its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the house that holds
this production and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its Estate; a
culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_sudan.md](play_sudan.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_ar.md](pitch_ar.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Sudan's
  profile (a warm, unhurried collectivism; the guest as a trust; dignity as a
  custom that is not broken; patience under hardship that returns no caller) and
  naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** Kerma and Kush, Meroe and the Black Pharaohs, Islam and
  Sufism, and the modern state, each casting the elements it needs and each
  staged from within Sudan's own self-understanding.
- **Personas:** Taharqa the Black Pharaoh, Sheikh Hamad al-Nil of the Sufi
  circle, the singer Mohammed Wardi, and the poet al-Majdhub as the historic
  cast, plus one archetype, Sit al-Shay, the woman who pours coffee from the
  jebena on the kerb. Each is wired to the language engine by the
  language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** Nubia and Kush (the ancient inheritance of the upper Nile valley,
  a civilisation standing in its own right), Sufism (the faith lived as a state
  and a chant, the soul of Sudan), and dignity (the guest as a trust and the
  custom of hospitality).
- **Places:** Meroe (the pyramids of Kush in the desert, the nation's memory in
  stone) and al-Mogran (the meeting of the two Niles in the capital, the living
  image of many peoples joined on one land).
- **Processes:** the jebena (the daily coffee ritual that opens the house to the
  caller) and the hadra (the Sufi circle of remembrance that dissolves the one
  into the many).
- **Pieces:** the kisra (the sorghum bread shared at the table) and the jebena pot
  (the long-necked clay vessel that carries the whole rite of welcome).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the Nubian and African and the Arab heritages are given equal dignity,
and the historical record is treated soberly, taking no political side. Recent and
ongoing conflict is out of scope. The staging and the architecture are original
work.
