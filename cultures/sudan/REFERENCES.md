# Sudan: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_ar.md](pitch_ar.md) is written from Sudan's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
Sudan is not scored on its own in the classic dataset; Hofstede's published data
places it within the Arab World cluster, whose scores are recorded here so the data
is on the record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Arab World cluster score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ---------------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 80                                 |
| Individualism                 | 38                                 |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 53                                 |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 68                                 |
| Long Term Orientation         | 23                                 |
| Indulgence                    | 34                                 |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the six-dimensions model of national
culture and the Country Comparison tool). Scores are approximate and are those
published for the Arab World grouping, within which Hofstede's data situates Sudan;
they are used here as the nearest published profile and read against Sudan's own
collectivist, hierarchical, and hospitable cast.

## Historical sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn
from the public historical record. They are credited here and never claimed; the
staging is original work. The history is staged soberly from within Sudan's own
self-understanding, giving the Nubian and African and the Arab heritages equal
dignity and taking no political side. Recent and ongoing conflict is out of scope.

The defining persona [Sit al-Shay](persona_sit_al_shay.md) is an archetype, not a
historical individual: a composite that carries the everyday face of Sudanese
hospitality, the woman who pours coffee from the jebena to every caller on the
kerb before he is asked his name.
