# the Comoros: a khai culture

The Comoros staged as a full khai play, ISO KM, house language fr (with the heart
in Shikomori and prayer in Arabic). The Perfumed Islands as a theatre: their
history as the plot line, their people as the cast, the roles their faith and
language encode, their places, their practices, and their defining objects, all
tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_comoros.md](play_comoros.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_fr.md](pitch_fr.md), the Hofstede layer, written from the
  Comorian profile (a collective, family- and community-first people, deference to
  age, faith, and rank, restraint and the keeping of face, generosity over
  accumulation, and the patient acceptance of what is willed) and naming no
  dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the sultanates and the Islam of the islands, the Perfumed
  Islands, the independence of 1975, and the Union of the three islands - each
  casting the elements it needs, and each staged soberly and from within.
- **Personas:** Said Mohamed Cheikh (a sober founding figure of island political
  life), Mbaye Trambwe (the classical Shikomori poet), Abou Chihabi (the
  twarab musician who carried the islands' voice abroad), and Mohamed Toihiri
  (the novelist who gave the islands their own fiction), plus one archetype, the
  ylang-ylang distiller who toils on the volcanic slopes and the sea for his grand
  marriage. Each is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** Islam (the bedrock and rhythm of life), the anda (the grand
  marriage that confers a man's place and honour), the sea (which separates and
  yet binds the three islands and ties them to the Indian Ocean world).
- **Places:** Moroni (the capital, its coral-stone medina and the Friday mosque by
  the water), Mount Karthala (the great living volcano whose lava made the islands'
  fertile slopes).
- **Processes:** the grand marriage (the anda made visible), the twarab (the
  Indian-Ocean music of the feasts and the great weddings).
- **Pieces:** ylang-ylang (the flower whose essence names the Perfumed Islands),
  the dhow (the boutre that binds the islands to the sea and the world).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain sources are recorded in
[REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the pitch is its
invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never claimed; the
colonial period and the work of building a young nation across separated islands
are treated soberly on the public record, taking no political side. The staging
and the architecture are original work.
