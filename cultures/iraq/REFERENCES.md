# Iraq: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical and heritage
sources behind the plots, personas, places, processes, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_ar.md](pitch_ar.md) is written from Iraq's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record without
entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Iraq score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | -------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 95                   |
| Individualism                 | 30                   |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 70                   |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 85                   |
| Long Term Orientation         | 25                   |
| Indulgence                    | 17                   |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; figures for Iraq are reported within
the Arab-region grouping and read as a high-collective, high-hierarchy profile.

The one archetype persona, [Ibn al-Ahwar](persona_ibn_al_ahwar.md) (the Marsh
Dweller), is not a historical individual but a composite: the living heir of the
Marsh Arabs of the southern marshes, who builds the reed mudhif as the Sumerians
did and carries the oldest continuous way of life on the land.
