# Cyprus: a khai culture

Cyprus staged as a full khai play, ISO CY, language el (with one Turkish-language
file). A small island at a crossroads, bi-communal and divided, as a theatre: its
history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its language and faith
encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single
pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_cyprus.md](play_cyprus.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_el.md](pitch_el.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Cyprus's
  profile (warm and family-bound, hospitable, anxious for certainty after the
  wound of division, proud, attached to faith and to the Greek world, resilient)
  and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the crossroads of empires (antiquity to British rule),
  Independence (the EOKA-era struggle, the enosis dream, and the 1960 bi-communal
  republic), 1974 (the coup and the invasion that divided the island), and EU
  accession (the 2003 openings, the 2004 Annan Plan referendum, and membership of
  a still-divided island) - each casting the elements it needs.
- **Personas:** Makarios III (the Archbishop-President and ethnarch) and Zeno of
  Citium (the Stoic philosopher born at Kition) as the historic cast, plus three
  defining archetypes: Androula (an older Greek-Cypriot refugee of 1974), Mehmet
  (an older Turkish-Cypriot, also displaced, written in Turkish), and Andreas (the
  younger EU generation who crosses the line as a visitor). Each is wired to the
  language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** η διαίρεση (the division and the Green Line, the displaced and
  the missing of both communities), το σταυροδρόμι (the Mediterranean crossroads
  and its long succession of rulers), η ελληνικότητα (the Greek Orthodox identity,
  the tie to the Greek world, and the enosis dream).
- **Places:** Λευκωσία (Nicosia, the last divided capital in Europe), Πάφος
  (Paphos, the birthplace of Aphrodite and the deep antiquity of the crossroads).
- **Processes:** το καφενείο (the coffeehouse and its slow sociability, shared in
  their separate ways by both communities), το πανηγύρι (the saint's festival of
  church, food, and music).
- **Pieces:** χαλλούμι (halloumi, the island's cheese, made and loved across the
  divide), χαλκός (copper, the metal that gave the island its very name).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. The culture is bi-communal: most files are in Greek
(language el), with the Turkish-Cypriot persona Mehmet written in Turkish
(language tr). The 1974 division is staged soberly and even-handedly on the public
record, honouring the suffering of both communities and taking no side. Sources in
the public domain are credited, never claimed; the staging and the architecture
are original work.
