# Singapore: a khai culture

Singapore staged as a full khai play, ISO SG, working language en. A little red
dot that turned scarcity into a global city, as a theatre: its history as the
plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its languages and communities
encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a
single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_singapore.md](play_singapore.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Singapore's profile (a strong long-term orientation and disciplined restraint,
  a more collective and order-respecting cast, and a careful, pragmatic
  pursuit of what works) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the trading port and the kampong, the occupation and the
  lesson of vulnerability, separation and independence (1965), and from port to
  global city - each casting the elements it needs, the colonial period, the
  wartime occupation, and the separation of 1965 acknowledged soberly and with
  dignity, taking no side and dwelling on no detail.
- **Personas:** Lee Kuan Yew, S. Rajaratnam, Goh Keng Swee, and Kuo Pao Kun as
  the founding-generation and cultural cast on the public record, plus one
  defining archetype, the hawker (the cook at the shared table). Each is wired to
  the language engine by the multilingual reality of the island, where mother
  tongues live alongside the common working English.
- **Positions:** multiracial harmony (one people of many races, languages, and
  faiths, the CMIO model and the kept peace), pragmatism and meritocracy (the
  survival creed of the small nation), the global port-city (the island made
  indispensable to the world by its position).
- **Places:** Chinatown and the hawker centre (the shophouse quarter and the
  nation's shared table), the harbour and Marina Bay (the working port and the
  city's face to the world).
- **Processes:** the hawker centre meal (the everyday shared table), the open
  house (each community's festival opened to all the others).
- **Pieces:** the Merlion (the lion-and-sea emblem of the city), the HDB block
  (the heartland home with multiracial harmony built into its floor plan).
