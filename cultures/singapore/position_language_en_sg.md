# Position: Singaporean English

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The authority of English as Singapore's working tongue, the neutral bridge chosen so that a nation of Chinese, Malay, Indian and other peoples could deal with one another and with the world on ground that belonged wholly to none of them. It is the language of the school, the office, the law and the trade that made a port with no hinterland useful to everyone, laid over the Mandarin and the dialects, the Malay of the region and the Tamil of the streets. Turned to the island's own mouth it becomes Singlish, quick and mixed and owned by all, the tongue in which the order is called and the joke lands, even as the state keeps the schooled English for the record and asks each community to hold its mother tongue as the root of who it is.

## Orders

What the nation sets down and conducts its public life and its business in: the statute, the lesson, the contract and the printed page. It orders that the affairs of a plural people pass through one shared working tongue, so that four communities can meet across one another without any one being made to speak inside another's language, and so that a small island can trade with everyone and lock out no one. It commands the common ground of the office and the school and the market, the language in which the pledge is sworn to be one people regardless of race, language or religion.

## Loses

Ground to the mother tongues it was meant only to join, the Mandarin and the dialects, the Malay and the Tamil in which the home still speaks, prays and remembers. The bridge tongue can thin the tongue it bridges: raised as the language of school and screen and advancement, it draws the young to do their thinking in it and lets the grandmother's speech fade at the far edge of the family. It loses, too, in the gap between the schooled English of the record and the Singlish of the street, the mixed and living tongue the state keeps trying to correct even as the island keeps speaking it, since something in the shared life will not pass into the proper form.

## Drives

It drives a plural nation to hold its public life, its business and its place in the world in one shared tongue that erases no one. It is held as their own by the English-educated founders: [Lee Kuan Yew](persona_lee_kuan_yew.md), who spoke and thought in it and made it the common bridge across the communities; [Goh Keng Swee](persona_goh_keng_swee.md), who reasoned the young economy in it; and [S. Rajaratnam](persona_rajaratnam.md), who wrote the National Pledge in it, the vow that a Singaporean is made by the choice to belong. It is held and guarded at once by [Kuo Pao Kun](persona_kuo_pao_kun.md), who worked most deeply in his Chinese mother tongue and built a multilingual stage so the common English could not flatten the rest. And it is borrowed daily, half-shared and wholly owned by none, at the stall of [the hawker](persona_the_hawker.md), who calls the order in the Singlish that lets any two strangers meet. So the working tongue binds the island's peoples on neutral ground without erasing the mother tongues they carry home.
