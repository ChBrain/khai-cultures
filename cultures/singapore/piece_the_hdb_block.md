# Piece: the HDB block

## Taxonomy

Parent group: pieces

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Place

Across the whole island, in every town and estate: the public housing block of the Housing and Development Board, tall and orderly, where the great majority of the nation lives. The flats stacked storey on storey, the bamboo poles of laundry poking out the windows, the playground and the wet market downstairs, and at the ground level the void deck - the open sheltered space left empty under the block for weddings, funerals, void-deck football, and the neighbours' daily passing, eh, going where, going to makan. The home of the heartland, the building the country lives in.

## Load Bearing

It stands for the nation's bargain with its own people - a roof for almost everyone, owned not rented, built fast and at scale to house a crowded island. In a single generation the HDB rehoused the population out of the old kampongs and shophouses into flats with running water and clean order, and made most Singaporeans owners of their homes. The blocks were planned so the communities would live mixed together rather than sorted by race, so that the Chinese, Malay, and Indian neighbours share the same stairwell and the same void deck, same lift, same corridor - multiracial harmony built into the floor plan. The void deck below is the heartland's common room, where the whole estate gathers, everybody also know everybody. Without this block the nation that promised order, housing, and a stake for all would have no walls to show for it.

## Apparent

A concrete apartment tower, numbered and gridded, balconies and corridors and a lift lobby, an open pillared deck left bare at its foot. Plain and utilitarian to the eye, one of thousands like it; its meaning is in the lives stacked inside it and the deliberate mixing of neighbours it was built to hold, the policy made into a place to live.

## Yearbook

- Built at speed from the early years of self-government to rehouse the island from kampong and shophouse into public flats.
- Sold to its residents so that most Singaporeans came to own their homes, a stake in the nation for nearly all.
- Planned with the communities living mixed in every estate, putting multiracial harmony into the very arrangement of the heartland.
