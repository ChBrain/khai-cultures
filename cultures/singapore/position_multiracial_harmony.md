# Position: multiracial harmony

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The founding proposition that a nation of many races, languages, and religions can live as one people by deliberate choice. Singapore's communities are recognised on the public record under the model of Chinese, Malay, Indian, and Others, each kept as itself - its mother tongue, its festivals, its faith - while all are bound by a common citizenship and a common working tongue. It is harmony not as sameness but as a kept peace across difference: the four official languages, the public holidays of every major faith, the four corners of the same table. And it is a harmony understood as fragile and won, never assumed - a thing the nation decided to be, and must keep deciding to be.

## Orders

That the nation belongs to no single race or religion, so each community keeps its own and none is asked to disappear into another; that the peace between communities is a public trust to be guarded actively, in housing, in schools, in law, and in daily courtesy; and that to be Singaporean is to choose this shared belonging across difference, regardless of race, language, or religion.

## Loses

Ground when the harmony is taken for a thing that takes care of itself rather than a discipline that must be renewed - when ease breeds the assumption that peace is permanent; and ground, too, when the carefulness that keeps the peace hardens into a silence that cannot name real friction, so that the smoothing-over is mistaken for the solving.

Lives in [S. Rajaratnam](persona_rajaratnam.md), who wrote the pledge to be one people regardless of race, language, or religion; in [Kuo Pao Kun](persona_kuo_pao_kun.md), who kept the many tongues in one conversation on the stage; and in [the hawker](persona_the_hawker.md), who sets the shared table where every community eats as one. It drives Singapore to guard the peace between its peoples as the first condition of its survival.
