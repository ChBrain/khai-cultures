# Persona: the hawker

## Taxonomy

[multiracial harmony](position_multiracial_harmony.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The cook at the stall in the hawker centre, who has made one dish for thirty years and made it better than anyone, until you taste it and can only say shiok: the char kway teow man, the nasi padang auntie, the roti prata maker, the Hokkien mee uncle. From the street hawkers of the old town to the licensed stalls under one roof, this is the figure who feeds the nation cheaply, daily, and well, and whose stalls of Chinese, Malay, Indian, and other food sit side by side at the country's shared table. Plainspoken, tireless, and proud of the craft, the hawker is multiracial harmony made edible. The tongue at the stall is the island's own mixture: the hawker [speaks the mother tongue](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) of the home community to family and regulars, [borrows the Singlish and the English](process_speaking_borrowed.md) that lets the order pass between any two strangers, and [hears the Malay and Mandarin and Tamil of the queue and follows it](process_hearing_followed.md) well enough to serve everyone who comes. Order what, the auntie wants to know, and one kopi-o, one chicken rice settles it. The whole nation eats here in every language at once, the order called in a [tongue half-shared by all and wholly owned by none](position_language_en_sg.md), where got anyone left out lah - and somewhere past the last word both can manage, where the dialect runs out and the gesture takes over, the gap at the edge of the tongue is bridged not by grammar but by the plate set down between them.

## Action

In [from port to global city](plot_04_global_city.md) the hawker keeps the common table set as the nation grows rich, the one place where every community and every income sits elbow to elbow over the same cheap, good food. By feeding the many under one roof, the hawker carries [multiracial harmony](position_multiracial_harmony.md) into daily life, makan the country came to honour as its own heritage.

## Shadow

The shared table can be taken for granted by a generation that has only ever found it set: the temptation to assume the hawker will always be there, the food always cheap and the trades always passed on. Got time, got money, but who still wants to stand at a wok for thirty years - and so the craft and the gathering-place quietly thin while everyone looks the other way.

## Tell

The hawker takes the order in three tongues and a shrug, the regular's usual already plated before he say one word, and sets it down where a banker and a bus driver chope the same table with a packet of tissue - the nation eating as one, nobody decide also.
