# Plot: the trading port and the kampong

## Cue

At the southern tip of the Malay Peninsula, where the sea lanes of the Strait of Malacca converge, a fishing settlement sits at a river mouth. In the early nineteenth century it is established as a free trading port, open to all comers and charging no duty, and the ships begin to gather in its roadstead.

## Action

The free port draws the world to the island. Chinese traders and labourers, Malays of the peninsula and the archipelago, Indians from the subcontinent, and others from across the seas come to the quays and stay, until the settlement is a meeting of peoples. They make their quarters side by side - the shophouse town, the Malay kampong, the Indian district - and their own communities within them, the Peranakan culture among them born of the long mingling of Chinese settler and Malay world. They speak a dozen tongues in the same market, somehow still can deal, and lay down, without anyone planning it, the multiracial island the nation will one day choose to become. The harbour makes the living and the crossroads makes the people.

## Tension

The crossroads gives the island both its gift and its task: a meeting of peoples that makes it rich and various, and a mixture of races, tongues, and faiths that will one day have to be held together on purpose. The harmony the nation later builds is not invented from nothing lah, but drawn from this beginning, when the world came to one small port and stayed.
