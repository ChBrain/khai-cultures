# Persona: S. Rajaratnam

## Taxonomy

[multiracial harmony](position_multiracial_harmony.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A founding-generation statesman and writer, on the public record the author of the National Pledge, the few lines in which the new nation swore to be one people regardless of race, language, or religion. A journalist and man of letters before he was a minister, he gave the country its words for itself: the promise that a Singaporean is made not by ancestry but by the choice to belong together. Of Tamil heritage and raised among the island's many tongues, he [wrote](process_writing_mother_tongue.md) and [spoke](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) his finest [English](position_language_en_sg.md) as a craftsman of it, the working tongue he turned into the common vow that all four communities could say as one. The languages of the peoples around him - the [Mandarin](../china/position_language_zh_cn.md), the [Malay](../malaysia/position_language_ms_my.md), the Tamil of the streets and the kampongs - he [heard and followed](process_hearing_followed.md) every day, and knew that the pledge he wrote in English had to hold true for each of them in its own. He set down in one shared tongue a promise meant to protect every other tongue on the island, and understood that the harder the words reached across the communities, the more carefully they had to be chosen - for it is exactly at the edge of one tongue, where it meets another it cannot fully enter, that a multiracial nation either keeps faith or loses it.

## Action

In [separation and independence (1965)](plot_03_independence_1965.md) he gives the young, anxious nation the words to be one: the pledge that binds many races, languages, and religions into a single people by choice rather than blood. By writing [multiracial harmony](position_multiracial_harmony.md) into a vow the whole country recites, he turns a fragile mixture of communities into a deliberate, daily act of belonging.

## Shadow

The carefully chosen word can become the word that smooths over what still needs saying: a harmony kept so courteously that the real frictions go unspoken, and a pledge recited so smoothly it is mistaken for a thing achieved rather than a thing that must be chosen again each day.

## Tell

He wrote in one tongue a vow for a people of four, weighing every word so that no community would hear itself left out, where got anyone said one race owns this place - the harmony of the island resting on the precision of a single sentence.
