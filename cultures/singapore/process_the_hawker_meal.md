# Process: the hawker centre meal

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

Anyone hungry at noon: an office worker on a lunch break, a family on a Sunday, a student, a retiree. You walk into the roofed hall, choose a table and reserve it with a packet of tissues, then join the queues at the stalls of your choosing - a plate of chicken rice here, laksa there, a sugar-cane juice to wash it down - and carry it back to eat among strangers at the common table.

## Direction

Toward the shared, everyday table of the nation: the hawker centre is multiracial harmony made into a meal, the one place where every community, every faith, and every income sits down together over food anyone can afford. It is not only eating but a daily rite of belonging, the country gathering without ceremony three times a day at the same kind of table, each picking from the dishes of every community.

## Lever

It is driven by the conviction that a nation of many peoples needs a common ground so ordinary it asks nothing of anyone - no dress, no creed, no money beyond a few dollars - and by the cheap, excellent food that makes the table worth coming to. It holds against the pull of wealth and of private rooms; against it stand the rising costs, the ageing hawkers, and the question of who will cook these dishes when the old hands are gone.

## Echo

The hawker centre meal makes audible what drives [multiracial harmony](position_multiracial_harmony.md): that a nation of many can be one most easily over a shared and humble table. To this table [the hawker](persona_the_hawker.md) brings the dish of a lifetime, and the whole country sits down to eat as one, in every tongue at once.
