# Process: the open house

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

A family marking its festival: a Chinese household at Lunar New Year, a Malay one at Hari Raya, an Indian one at Deepavali, a Christian one at Christmas. They open the home, lay out the food of the feast, and welcome not only their own community but their neighbours, colleagues, and friends of every race and faith - the door of the flat held open across the void deck for whoever comes.

## Direction

Toward the kept peace of multiracial harmony, lived as hospitality: the open house turns each community's holy day into an invitation to all the others, so that the whole nation tastes one another's festivals across the year. It is the calendar of every faith made public and shared, the deliberate habit of crossing into a neighbour's celebration and being welcomed at one's own, harmony practised as a guest list.

## Lever

It is driven by the understanding that peace across communities is kept by knowing one another, and by the generosity of a feast that asks the stranger in. It holds against suspicion and distance, the drift of communities into their own corners; against it stand the carefulness that can keep the visiting courteous but shallow, and the ease that lets a busy nation skip the crossing altogether.

## Echo

The open house makes visible what [multiracial harmony](position_multiracial_harmony.md) needs: a nation whose communities walk into one another's homes and feasts as a matter of course. Across the void deck and the open door, [the hawker](persona_the_hawker.md) and the banker, the Malay and the Tamil and the Chinese neighbour, sit at one another's tables and make the many one.
