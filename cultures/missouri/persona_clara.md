# Persona: Clara

## Taxonomy

[gateway west](position_gateway_west.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A riverboat pilot in St. Louis, forty-two years in the wheelhouse and still not one for talk that hasn't been earned. English, in a melodic river drawl, is her mother tongue, the language she [speaks, hears, and thinks in](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), the code her navigation runs on. It is the [Missourian English](position_language_en_us_mo.md) of the St. Louis waterfront, the drawl of the riverboat wheelhouse. She speaks no foreign languages. Where it wears thin is Kansas City's clubs: the fast, syncopated talk of jazz musicians like Marcus, and the digital scheduling of the club boards, she [reads only as calendar noise](process_reading_followed.md), sensing the river's patient rhythm counts for less each year against the pull of city speed. She carries [the Show-Me State](position_culture_show_me_state.md) in her own key - the gateway west, proving every freight run and every pioneer route against the current before she'll call it safe.

## Action

She preserves [river barge](piece_river_barge.md) logs, coordinates navigation safety under [the floodplain plan](plan_mississippi_floodplain_management.md), and studies [gateway west](position_gateway_west.md).

## Shadow

Her focus on cargo shipping quotas and lock navigation systems can make her indifferent to municipal wastewater regulations in expanding urban residential suburbs.

## Tell

She looks at a river depth chart and says directly: "The river has its own currents; you can't navigate safety if you're only focused on digital speed and jazz recording metrics."
