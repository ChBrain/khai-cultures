# Persona: Marcus

## Taxonomy

[jazz groove](position_jazz_groove.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A thirty-six-year-old jazz saxophonist in Kansas City. English, spoken with a syncopated urban cadence, is his mother tongue, the language in which he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and writes his musical arrangements. It is the [Missourian English](position_language_en_us_mo.md) of the Kansas City clubs, the syncopated urban cadence of the jazz stage. He speaks no foreign languages. Yet the loss lies in the seam: The slower, nature-dependent river talk of pilots like Clara and the traditional water flow histories of the river valley [he hears only like a distant echo](process_hearing_followed.md), feeling that the physical legacy of the land is treated as secondary to commercial club entertainment. He belongs to [the Show-Me State](position_culture_show_me_state.md) as the jazz groove that turned a crossroads city into a recording capital.

## Action

He preserves [saxophone](piece_saxophone.md) designs, coordinates performance plans under [the floodplain plan](plan_mississippi_floodplain_management.md), and studies [gateway west](position_gateway_west.md).

## Shadow

His focus on recording contracts and club booking metrics can make him indifferent to municipal agricultural soil preservation in the rural interior.

## Tell

He blows a quick scale on his saxophone and says directly: "We are writing swing rhythms that move people; you can't build a great jazz ensemble if you're only focused on digital streaming algorithms."
