## Cue

In the mid-1920s, Kansas City jazz orchestras begin recording unique big-band swing tracks for national distributors.

## Action

The music boom establishes Kansas City as a global jazz epicenter, developing [jazz groove](position_jazz_groove.md) performance standards. Marcus composes swing arrangements for local clubs, while Clara coordinates passenger travel along the musical river routes.

## Stage

- [jazz groove](position_jazz_groove.md) : the urban musical expression, big-band collaboration, and showmanship.
- [riverboat piloting](process_riverboat_piloting.md) : the steamboat transportation process that brought musicians to regional ports.
- [saxophone](piece_saxophone.md) : the brass instrument representing the swing orchestra style.

## Tension

Despite their massive musical influence, jazz performers face segregationist club rules and low wages in corporate-owned recording networks.
