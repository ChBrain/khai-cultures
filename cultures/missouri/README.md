# Missouri: a khai culture

Missouri staged as a full khai play, ISO US-MO, language en. The Show-Me State: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its Missouri Compromise, St. Louis Gateway Arch construction, and Kansas City jazz recording boom encodes, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house that holds this culture and answers for it.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_missouri.md](play_missouri.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_missouri.md](pitch_missouri.md), the Missouri key.
- **Plots (history):** the Missouri Compromise of 1820, the Gateway Arch Construction of 1965, and the Jazz and Blues Recording Boom of the 1920s.
- **Personas:** Clara (riverboat pilot) and Marcus (jazz saxophonist), written in English.
- **Positions:** gateway west (geographic crossroads/pioneer heritage), jazz groove (urban musical showmanship/club expression).
- **Places:** Gateway Arch, Mississippi Confluence.
- **Processes:** riverboat piloting (steamboat navigation), jazz improvisation (saxophone performance).
- **Pieces:** a river barge, a saxophone.
- **Plan:** the floodplain plan.
