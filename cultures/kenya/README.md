# Kenya: a khai culture

Kenya staged as a full khai play, ISO KE, language sw. A nation of many peoples
pulling together as one under Kiswahili and the spirit of harambee, as a theatre:
its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its language and
faith encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a
single pitch. All prose bodies are written in Kiswahili, Kenya's national and
unifying tongue; this README and REFERENCES.md are in English.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_kenya.md](play_kenya.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_sw.md](pitch_sw.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Kenya's
  profile (strong collectivism and the pull of the group, respect for elders and
  authority, warmth and humour, and a present-focused drive) and naming no
  dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the coastal Swahili civilisation, the struggle for
  independence (handled soberly as national pride, with no atrocity detail and no
  political side), the building of the nation through harambee, and Kenya before
  the world today.
- **Personas:** Jomo Kenyatta (the founding figure, handled soberly), Ngugi wa
  Thiong'o (the writer who returned to his mother tongue), Wangari Maathai (the
  Green Belt and the trees), and Eliud Kipchoge (the runners of the Rift) as the
  real cast, plus one archetype, Makanga, the matatu conductor of Nairobi's
  hustle. Each is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** harambee (pulling together, the many become one), Kiswahili (the
  unifying tongue born on the coast), the land and environment (the forests, the
  water, the Rift, and the wildlife).
- **Places:** Nairobi (the green city in the sun, the capital and the hustle),
  Mombasa (the coast, the dhows, taarab, and the cradle of Kiswahili).
- **Processes:** nyama choma and chai (the shared table that binds the many),
  the Rift Valley training run (the discipline that turns the land into runners).
- **Pieces:** ugali (the shared staple at every table), the kanga (the cloth that
  wears the language and its proverbs).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical and cultural
sources are recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the
prose; the pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited,
never claimed; the struggle for independence and the difficult passages of the
coastal trade are treated soberly on the public record, taking no political side.
The staging and the architecture are original work.
