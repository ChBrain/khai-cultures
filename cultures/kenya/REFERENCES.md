# Kenya: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical and cultural
sources behind the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_sw.md](pitch_sw.md) is written from Kenya's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record without
entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Kenya score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | --------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 70                    |
| Individualism                 | 25                    |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 60                    |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 50                    |
| Long Term Orientation         | 30                    |
| Indulgence                    | 40                    |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; Kenya's strong collectivism (a low
Individualism score) and high Power Distance shape the warm, group-centred,
elder-respecting tone.

the public historical and cultural record. They are credited here and never
claimed; the staging is original work. The struggle for independence and the

with no atrocity detail and taking no political side.

The defining archetype persona, [Makanga](persona_makanga.md), the matatu
conductor, is not a historical individual: a composite that carries the daily
harambee of Nairobi's hustle, the many peoples of the city carried together on one
vehicle, and the Sheng-flavoured speech of its young.
