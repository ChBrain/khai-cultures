# North Korea: a khai culture

North Korea staged as a full khai play, ISO KP, language ko. One Korean people on
the northern land, as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the
cast, the shared language and heritage its roles encode, its places, its
practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch. The staging is
strictly neutral: it presents the country and people from within their own
self-understanding, on the public record, takes no political side, makes no
political judgment, and gives no contested detail. It stays on enduring cultural
ground - the shared Korean language, the land, family, and shared heritage - with
dignity for ordinary people.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_north_korea.md](play_north_korea.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_ko.md](pitch_ko.md), the Hofstede layer, written from the
  shared Korean cultural profile (low individualism and a strong collective bond,
  respect for age and order, restraint, and a present held in common) and naming
  no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the ancient land and one people, Hangeul, the modern era
  and the 1945 division, and the founding of the republic (1948) - each casting
  the elements it needs, and each handled soberly, neutrally, and with no
  contested detail.
- **Personas:** Dangun (the founding ancestor of shared lore), King Sejong (the
  maker of the Korean script), Eulji Mundeok (the ancient defender of the land),
  and Kim Sowol (the poet of the people's tongue) as the cast, plus one archetype,
  the Mother (the everyperson who keeps the home and the language). Each is wired
  to the language engine by the language-relation it holds.
- **Positions:** one people (the shared blood, language, and heritage of a single
  Korean people), unity (the value placed on facing things together), and Juche
  (the state's own guiding idea of self-reliance, presented as self-description,
  never endorsed or judged).
- **Places:** Mount Paektu (the highest mountain, first in the people's lore) and
  Pyongyang (the city on the Taedong, the northern center).
- **Processes:** kimjang (the communal making of winter kimchi with the seasons)
  and Seollal (the Lunar New Year gathering of the family).
- **Pieces:** Korean dress (the shared festive garment of one people) and Arirang
  (the folk song of longing carried by the whole Korean people).
