# Plot: The Land Boom and Tourism 1920

## Taxonomy

[play_florida](play_florida.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

In the early 1920s, a period of rapid real estate speculation and railroad expansion transforms Florida's east and west coasts.

## Action

The speculative boom establishes [the Sunshine Dream](position_sunshine_dream.md) of leisure, resort life, and rapid agricultural development. Thousands migrate south to buy land. The [citrus crate](piece_citrus_crate.md) becomes a symbol of the state's commercial fertility. Mateo's family land in central Florida was developed during this era of orange grove expansion.

## Stage

- [the Sunshine Dream](position_sunshine_dream.md) : the belief in leisure, retirement, and the promise of endless growth under the sun.
- [citrus crate](piece_citrus_crate.md) : the wooden crates used to ship Florida oranges to northern markets.
- [the Everglades](place_everglades.md) : the vast wetlands that developers attempted to drain for agriculture and real estate.

## Tension

The speculative bubble bursts in 1926 due to devastating hurricanes and overvaluation, leaving thousands bankrupt and causing severe ecological damage to the southern wetlands.
