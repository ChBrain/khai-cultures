# Croatia: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_hr.md](pitch_hr.md) is written from Croatia's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no
number. The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the
record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Croatia score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ----------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 73                      |
| Individualism                 | 33                      |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 40                      |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 80                      |
| Long Term Orientation         | 58                      |
| Indulgence                    | 33                      |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published
by Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; Croatia's high Uncertainty
Avoidance and low Individualism and Indulgence are notable in the published data.

The two defining personas, [Kata](persona_kata.md) and [Ivan](persona_ivan.md),
are archetypes, not historical individuals: composites that carry the tension
between the older generation (Dalmatian Catholic faith, the parish, klapa, the
mother tongue, the memory of the Homeland War) and the younger one (the post-2013
emigration and the pull of English and the Serbian twin on a small language).
