# Croatia: a khai culture

Croatia staged as a full khai play, ISO HR, language hr. A narrow nation on the
border of Europe, half Central-European and half Mediterranean, as a theatre: its
history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its language and faith
encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single
pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_croatia.md](play_croatia.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_hr.md](pitch_hr.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Croatia's
  profile (proud and hierarchical, family- and clan-minded, Catholic, anxious for
  security, Mediterranean warmth over Central-European order, a defensive border
  pride) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Republic of Ragusa, the Bulwark against the Ottomans,
  the Illyrian Revival (1830s-1840s), and the Homeland War (1991-1995) - each
  casting the elements it needs.
- **Personas:** Ljudevit Gaj, Marko Marulić, and Ruđer Bošković as the historic
  cast, plus two defining archetypes, Kata (older, Dalmatian Catholic faith, klapa,
  and the mother tongue, who carried both through the war) and Ivan (younger, the
  EU-era emigrant whose Croatian thins between English and the Serbian twin). Each
  is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** hrvatski jezik (the tongue standardized late and held distinct
  from its Serbian twin by Latin script and Catholic culture), predziđe kršćanstva
  (the bulwark of Christendom, Catholic and Western-facing against the Ottoman and
  Orthodox East), Jadran (the Adriatic, Dalmatia, the islands, Dubrovnik's Libertas,
  the Mediterranean soul).
- **Places:** Dubrovnik (the walled Adriatic city-republic of Libertas, shelled in
  1991), Vukovar (the Danube martyr-city destroyed in the 1991 siege).
- **Processes:** klapa (the Dalmatian a cappella close-harmony singing, UNESCO
  heritage), fjaka (the Mediterranean art of blissful idleness on the coast).
- **Pieces:** šahovnica (the red-and-white checkerboard coat of arms and football
  jersey), kravata (the necktie the world wears, the small nation's gift).
