# Malaysia: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_ms.md](pitch_ms.md) is written from Malaysia's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record without
entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Malaysia score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------ |
| Power Distance                | 100                      |
| Individualism                 | 26                       |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 50                       |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 36                       |
| Long Term Orientation         | 41                       |
| Indulgence                    | 57                       |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; Malaysia's Power Distance score is
among the highest in the published data, and its Individualism score is low,
reflecting a strongly group- and family-oriented society.

The defining persona [Pak Tua](persona_pak_tua.md) is an archetype, not a
historical individual: a composite kampung elder who carries the everyday practice
of muhibbah, the prayer times and the adat kept alongside an open verandah that
welcomes neighbours of every community and faith.
