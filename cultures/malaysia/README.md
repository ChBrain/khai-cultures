# Malaysia: a khai culture

Malaysia staged as a full khai play, ISO MY, language ms. A multicultural nation
of Malay, Chinese, and Indian communities and the peoples of Borneo, bound into
one by muhibbah, as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the
cast, the roles its language and faiths encode, its places, its practices, and
its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_malaysia.md](play_malaysia.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_ms.md](pitch_ms.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Malaysia's profile (a respect for hierarchy and elders, a strong sense of the
  group and the family, a care for face and harmony, and an easy tolerance of
  difference) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Malacca Sultanate, the colonial era, Merdeka (1957),
  and the forming of the Federation (1963) - each casting the elements it needs,
  and each handling the colonial period, independence, and communal balance
  soberly, with dignity, and taking no side.
- **Personas:** Tunku Abdul Rahman, Munshi Abdullah, P. Ramlee, and Usman Awang
  as the historic cast, plus one defining archetype, Pak Tua (the kampung elder
  whose open verandah keeps muhibbah at its smallest and truest scale). Each is
  wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** muhibbah (the harmony between peoples that is the nation's
  keystone), bahasa Melayu (the national language as the shared meeting ground),
  Islam and plurality (Islam as the religion of the Federation alongside the
  freedom of other faiths).
- **Places:** Malacca (the great entrepot where the language, the faith, and the
  Malay world took root), Kuala Lumpur (the capital where Merdeka was proclaimed
  and where every community shares one city).
- **Processes:** balik kampung (the festive return to the home village and one's
  roots), melepak di mamak (the open table where every community meets as
  neighbours over teh tarik).
- **Pieces:** songket (the gold-threaded ceremonial cloth of Malay refinement),
  the wau (the great decorated moon kite that lifts village artistry into the
  sky).
