# South Africa: a khai culture

South Africa staged as a full khai play, ISO ZA, language en. A nation of many
peoples and eleven tongues that chose to forgive and build one home, as a
theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the truths its
languages and faith encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects,
all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_south_africa.md](play_south_africa.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md), the Hofstede layer, written from South
  Africa's profile (a strongly collective, group-held people, warm and indulgent,
  taking authority and time with an easy hand, and bound by hospitality and the
  shared fire) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Cradle of Humankind, the meeting of peoples, the era
  overcome, and the long walk to reconciliation - each casting the elements it
  needs, and each staging the country's wounds and its triumph soberly and with
  dignity.
- **Personas:** Nelson Mandela, Desmond Tutu, Miriam Makeba, and Hugh Masekela as
  the historic cast, plus one defining archetype, the Gogo (the grandmother who
  holds the family and the stranger's child alike, ubuntu in one pair of hands).
  Each is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** ubuntu (the old truth that a person is a person through other
  people), the Rainbow Nation (many peoples and tongues held in equal dignity),
  reconciliation (the choosing of forgiveness over revenge by which the hardest
  era was overcome).
- **Places:** Table Mountain (the Cape crossroads where the peoples and the two
  oceans meet), the Cradle of Humankind (the highveld caves where the human story
  begins).
- **Processes:** the braai (the shared fire where the country gathers and mends
  things face to face), the song (the harmony that makes many voices one chord
  while each keeps its line).
- **Pieces:** the six-colour flag (the sign of the one home chosen out of many),
  the Madiba shirt (the warm, unbowed dignity of the new nation worn as cloth).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the era of being held apart, the mining heritage, and the cost to the
first peoples are treated soberly on the public record, taking no political side;
the staging and the architecture are original work.
