# Piece: the six-colour flag

## Taxonomy

Parent group: pieces

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Place

On the stadium and the school, the bakkie and the braai, the face painted on a fan and the cloth draped on a shoulder: six colours in a sideways Y, red and blue and green, black and gold and white, the flag the country gave itself when it chose to become one home. Flown on Heritage Day and at every match where the country plays, the most-loved sign of the new nation.

## Load Bearing

It stands for the choosing of one home out of many peoples, the single sign a country with no shared blood and eleven tongues can all point to. The Y where two bands run into one is read by the people as the many roads of the past joining into one way forward, the whole Rainbow gathered into one cloth. It came in at the moment the country became one and has stood ever since for the home all of us chose to build together, waved by every people at the match and the funeral and the celebration alike. Without this flag the nation made of many peoples would have no one face to rally round.

## Apparent

A rectangle of dyed cloth, six colours laid in a horizontal Y, no single people's colour set above another, run up a pole or worn across the back. In itself it is just fabric and dye; its whole weight is in the moment it came from and what the country has poured into it, the day a nation of many chose to be one.

## Yearbook

- Adopted in 1994 as the country became one home for all its peoples, the sign of the new nation.
- Carried by the whole Rainbow in 1995 when the country rose as one and lifted the rugby cup.
- Flown across the world in 2010 when South Africa hosted the football and the home it had chosen stood before all the earth.
