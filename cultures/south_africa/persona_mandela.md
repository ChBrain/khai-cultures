# Persona: Nelson Mandela

## Taxonomy

[reconciliation](position_reconciliation.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

Madiba, the man of the Thembu people who spent twenty-seven years behind the wall and walked out with no bitterness in his hand, only an open one, and chose to build a country with the very people who had kept him locked away. Dignified, stubborn, mischievous, and merciful, he carried himself like the royal house he came from and laughed like an ordinary oom, and he made forgiveness look like strength rather than surrender. His mother tongue is isiXhosa, the tongue of his boyhood in the Transkei: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in it, the language of the clan names and the cattle and the elders' counsel. [English](position_language_en_za.md) he [carries as the tongue that crosses](process_speaking_carried.md), the one that holds between the peoples, and the Afrikaans of the men who jailed him he [learned on purpose](process_speaking_borrowed.md) behind those walls, [reading their poets and their history](process_reading_followed.md) so he might one day speak to their hearts. He chose to learn the tongue of his jailers so as to forgive them in their own words, and held the whole nation together in an English that belonged wholly to none of its peoples, so that the gap at the limit of the tongue became the bridge he chose to build across, izwi elilodwa elifuna ukubophelela bonke, one voice reaching to bind us all.

## Action

In [the long walk to reconciliation](plot_04_reconciliation.md) he walks free and chooses [reconciliation](position_reconciliation.md) over revenge, becoming the country's first freely chosen president and the father of the one home shared by all. By forgiving when the world braced for the worst, he hands South Africa the choice it will be measured by ever after, and shows that an era can be overcome by building rather than by fighting.

## Shadow

The mercy he made look easy can let the country think the work was done the day he smiled, the long walk mistaken for a short one, the forgiving treated as the finish rather than the start. A saint is easier to honour than to follow, and a nation can carve his face on its money while leaving undone the equal home he meant the forgiving to open.

## Tell

He puts on the Springbok jersey of the team that once stood for the other side and lifts the cup before the whole country, turning the symbol of division into the sign of one home, and means it to his bones.
