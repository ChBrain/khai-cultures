# Play: South Africa

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the house that holds this staging and answers for it.

## Name

South Africa, Mzansi to the people who live in it, the country at the foot of the continent where two oceans meet at the Cape. ISO ZA, three capitals between Pretoria, Cape Town, and Bloemfontein, and eleven official tongues on the books, with isiZulu and isiXhosa the most widely spoken and English the one that carries between them all. A nation that is not one people but many, Zulu and Xhosa, Afrikaner and Sotho, Tswana and Pedi, Venda and Tsonga, Ndebele and Swazi, the Cape Malay and the Indian community, and the San and Khoe who were here first of all, held together not by being the same but by the hard, lekker choice to share one home.

## Arc

This is the place where humankind itself got going, the Cradle on the highveld where the oldest of our grandmothers walked, so the story here runs deeper than any nation. Onto that ancient ground came peoples from every direction, the first San and Khoe, then the Bantu-speaking nations spreading south, then ships from Europe and labourers from the East, until the tip of the continent held more peoples and more tongues than almost anywhere on earth. The gold and the diamonds under the veld pulled the whole world in and put the peoples shoulder to shoulder in the mines and the cities, and for a long, hard era the country was kept apart, each people held separate from the others by law. That era was overcome, ja, not by another war but by the long walk to forgiveness, when a man came out of prison after twenty-seven years and chose to build with the very people who had locked him up, and the nation sat down together and chose to be one. Out of that choosing came the Rainbow Nation, eleven tongues and many peoples held in equal dignity under one flag, bound by ubuntu, the old truth that umuntu ngumuntu ngabantu, a person is a person through other people. Under it all runs one nerve: a people who carry deep wounds and deep song both, who would rather forgive and braai together than stay apart, and who reckon that a home shared by all of us, every one, is worth the hard work of building it.

## Triggers

**[Plot 1: the Cradle of Humankind](plot_01_cradle.md)**

Long before nations, the highveld holds [the Cradle of Humankind](place_cradle.md) where our oldest grandmothers walked, and the first San and Khoe peoples read the land and the stars, the beginning of the long human story that gives [ubuntu](position_ubuntu.md) its deepest root.

**[Plot 2: the meeting of peoples](plot_02_meeting_of_peoples.md)**

Over centuries peoples come from every direction to the tip of the continent, and the gold and diamonds under the veld pull the whole world in, putting many nations and many tongues shoulder to shoulder on one ground, the many that will one day have to become a [Rainbow Nation](position_rainbow_nation.md).

**[Plot 3: the era overcome](plot_03_era_overcome.md)**

For a long, hard era the peoples are kept apart by law, each held separate from the others, while [Miriam Makeba](persona_makeba.md) carries the country's song and conscience to the world, the wound the nation will have to heal before it can be one.

**[Plot 4: the long walk to reconciliation](plot_04_reconciliation.md)**

After twenty-seven years [Nelson Mandela](persona_mandela.md) walks free and chooses to forgive, the nation sits down together in truth led by [Desmond Tutu](persona_tutu.md), and in 1994 and 1995 and again in 2010 the whole country stands as one, choosing [reconciliation](position_reconciliation.md) over division.

## Stakes

Whether a nation of many peoples and eleven tongues, carrying the wounds of an era overcome, can keep choosing [ubuntu](position_ubuntu.md) over division and hold the [Rainbow Nation](position_rainbow_nation.md) together in real and equal dignity, can keep [reconciliation](position_reconciliation.md) a living thing and not just a fine word said once, can close the gaps still left between the peoples and make the one home truly a home for every one of us, or whether the many it is made of pull it back apart.
