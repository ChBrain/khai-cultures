# Pitch: the South African tone

## Taxonomy

The key in which the staging of [South Africa](play_south_africa.md) is played. It changes nothing of what happens, not the Cradle, not the meeting of peoples, not the era overcome, not the long walk. It sets only how it sounds, the register in which the whole show is given.

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Tenor

Played warm, mixed, and ready to laugh: a people who hold the group above the lone self, who reckon you are nothing without the others around you, and who would sooner sort a thing out face to face over a fire than stand on ceremony. Friendly to a fault and quick with a joke, they shrug off the small troubles with an ag, shame, man, swap languages mid-sentence without blinking, and call you bru or boet or sisi inside five minutes. Hospitality is sacred, a stranger is fed before he is asked his business, and the braai is where the real talk happens. Under the easy warmth runs a toughness too, a people who have been through plenty and learned to make a plan, to laugh when laughing is the only thing left, and to carry on. They take their time, now now is not now, just now is later, and the robot will change when it changes, ja, so relax.

## Undertow

Under the warmth runs the long ache of an era overcome, the knowledge of how close apart the peoples once stood and how much the healing still costs. There is a wariness under the welcome, the watching of who has and who has not, and the worry that the Rainbow is a fine word said over a country still pulling itself even. There is the eish of the daily struggle, the load shed and the plan made, the hard graft behind the easy smile. And under the togetherness runs the fear that the many could come apart again, that the choosing of one home is a thing the country has to keep choosing, not a thing it ever finished.

## Nerve

It strikes the chord of ubuntu and the shared fire: the conviction that a person is a person through other people, that no one is whole on their own, and that what is broken between people can be mended face to face. It plays on the deep pull of belonging, of being held by a family and a clan and a people, on the pride of a country that chose to forgive when the world braced for the worst, and on the joy that breaks out in song and dance at the smallest excuse. It plays, too, on the ache underneath, the long memory of being held apart, and the daily work of keeping the home one.

## Echo

What is left is the image of a people who carry deep wounds and deep song both, warm, tough, funny, and forgiving, who would rather share a fire than stand apart, and who reckon the home is only a home if every one of us has a place at it. A nation that runs on the truth that we are made by one another, and that for all the gaps still left between the peoples, the choosing of one home is worth it, and worth choosing again.
