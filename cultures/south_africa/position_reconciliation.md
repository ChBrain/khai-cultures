# Position: reconciliation

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The hard, lekker choice at the heart of how the country became one: that an era of being held apart would be overcome not by another war but by sitting down together, telling the truth, and choosing to build with one another rather than against. It is the wager that a wound is healed by facing it in the open and then forgiving, that the people who were once kept separate can share one home, and that the future is worth more than the score. When the whole world braced for the worst, the country chose to forgive, and it made of that choosing its founding act. Reconciliation is ubuntu put to the hardest test there is, the mending of what broke between whole peoples, done face to face.

## Orders

That the truth of what happened be told plainly and heard, for a thing cannot be healed while it is hidden; that forgiveness be chosen over revenge and the future over the score; that the peoples once held apart now build together as equals; and that the choosing be renewed, for reconciliation is not a deed done once but a thing the country must keep choosing every day.

## Loses

Ground when the forgiving is asked of some and the changing of none, so the word reconciliation becomes a way to close the book before the wound is truly healed; and ground, too, when the long walk is treated as finished, the truth told once and the gaps left standing, so the country mistakes the saying of sorry for the living of the new life it was meant to open.

## Drives

Lives in [Nelson Mandela](persona_mandela.md), who walked free after twenty-seven years and chose to build with the very people who had locked him up, and in [Desmond Tutu](persona_tutu.md), who led the country in telling its truth and named it the Rainbow Nation. It drives South Africa to overcome its hardest era by forgiving rather than fighting, and to keep the choosing of one home a living thing.
