# Process: the song

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

Anyone, anywhere, and almost any time: a church choir on a Sunday, a stadium of fans, a wedding, a funeral, a picket line, or just a group of people who feel a song coming on. One voice starts it, a second slides in underneath in harmony, a third lifts above, and inside a breath the whole room is singing in parts, no sheet music and no rehearsal. From the isicathamiya of Ladysmith Black Mambazo to the kwaito and amapiano of the townships, from the choir to the choir of the crowd, the country sings without being asked.

## Direction

Toward joy and unity and the carrying of what cannot be said: the harmony that makes many voices into one sound while each keeps its own line, the very picture of the Rainbow at song. It is the way the country celebrates, grieves, protests, and prays, the call-and-response that needs no leader and no translation, where a song in isiZulu is picked up by people who do not speak it and carried anyway. It is not just music but a way of being together, the many voices held in one chord.

## Lever

It is driven by the deep tradition of harmony singing carried in every people of the country, and by the truth that some things, joy and grief and longing, are best carried in a song that crosses where words give out. It holds against distance, division, and even sorrow; against it stand the limits of a sound that can unite a moment but must still do the daily work of building the home it sings of.

## Echo

The song makes audible what drives [the Rainbow Nation](position_rainbow_nation.md): that many voices can become one sound while each keeps its own line, held in equal dignity in a single chord. Into this song [Miriam Makeba](persona_makeba.md) poured the country's many tongues and carried it to the whole world, the home kept alive in the world's ear.
