# Persona: Miriam Makeba

## Taxonomy

[the Rainbow Nation](position_rainbow_nation.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

Mama Africa, the girl from Johannesburg who carried the country's song to every stage on earth, the voice that could swing from the click-songs of home to the great halls of the world and back without missing a beat. Warm, fierce, and homesick to her marrow, she sang her people when she could not live among them, and made the world hear a country it had half forgotten. Her mother tongue is isiXhosa, with isiZulu close beside: she [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [sings in her mother tongue](process_speaking_carried.md), and the famous Qongqothwane, the click-song, lives in sounds no other tongue on earth can make. [English](position_language_en_za.md) she [carries as the tongue of the wide world](process_speaking_carried.md), the one that introduced her to it, and the many tongues of the continent she [followed by ear](process_hearing_followed.md) on her long years away. She sang her country in a tongue most of her listeners could not follow and made them feel it anyway, so the meaning crossed where the words could not, and the gap at the limit of the tongue was the very space her voice leaped, ingoma engadingi ukuqondwa, a song that needs no translating to be understood.

## Action

In [the era overcome](plot_03_era_overcome.md) she carries the country's many-voiced song and its conscience to the world while her home is held apart, becoming the living proof of the [Rainbow Nation](position_rainbow_nation.md) before the country had yet become one. By singing the peoples of South Africa to the whole earth, she keeps the home alive in the world's ear through its hardest era, and readies the welcome it will get when it comes back in.

## Shadow

The voice the world loves can become a postcard, the country's music enjoyed abroad while the gaps at home go unseen, the Rainbow heard as a pretty sound rather than a thing still being fought for. A nation can play her records with pride and forget the long exile and the homesickness in them, the cost behind the joy.

## Tell

She stands on a stage on the far side of the world and sings a click-song in a tongue not one person in the hall can speak, and the whole place rises anyway, carried home with her by a sound alone.
