# Plot: the long walk to reconciliation

## Taxonomy

[play_south_africa](play_south_africa.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

After a long, hard era of being held apart, the country came to its turning. [Nelson Mandela](persona_mandela.md) walked free after twenty-seven years behind the wall, and the whole world braced for what twenty-seven years of injustice might unleash. Instead, the country chose another road, the road of forgiveness, and began the long walk to becoming one home.

## Action

In 1994 South Africa voted as one nation for the first time, the long queues winding for hours as people of every people stood together to choose their own government, and Mandela became the first freely chosen president of a country for all of us. In 1995 the whole nation rose as one when the Springboks lifted the rugby cup and Madiba pulled on the green jersey of the team that had stood for the other side, turning a symbol of division into the sign of one home. The country sat down together in truth, led by [Desmond Tutu](persona_tutu.md), to face what had been done and to choose [reconciliation](position_reconciliation.md) over revenge. And in 2010 the whole continent watched South Africa host the world at football, Bafana Bafana and the vuvuzelas and the whole Rainbow on its feet, the home it had chosen now standing before all the earth.

## Stage

- [Nelson Mandela](persona_mandela.md): the man who walked free and chose to build with those who had jailed him, father of the one home.
- [Desmond Tutu](persona_tutu.md): the one who led the country in telling its truth and named it the Rainbow Nation.
- [reconciliation](position_reconciliation.md): the choosing of forgiveness over revenge by which the era was overcome.

## Tension

The country did the rarest thing a wounded nation can do, overcoming its hardest era not by another war but by sitting down together and choosing to forgive, and the whole world marvelled at it. Yet the long walk that 1994 began was not the same as finished, and the gaps left between the peoples did not close the day the cup was lifted. The triumph was real, the forgiving was real, and the work of making the one home equal in life and not only in law is the walk the country must keep walking, choosing reconciliation again and again.
