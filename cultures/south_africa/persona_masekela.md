# Persona: Hugh Masekela

## Taxonomy

[the Rainbow Nation](position_rainbow_nation.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

Bra Hugh, the boy from Witbank who picked up a trumpet and never put it down, the horn that mixed the townships and the world into one sound, jazz and mbaqanga and the marabi of the shebeens all blown through a single brass bell. Joyful, restless, and rooted, he could make a trumpet shout, laugh, and grieve, and he carried the music of home through long years away and brought it back richer. His mother tongue is isiZulu, with Sesotho close beside: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks in his mother tongue](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md), the tongue of the mine dances and the township streets. [English](position_language_en_za.md) he [carries as the working tongue of the music and the wide world](process_speaking_carried.md), and the languages of the continent he [followed by ear](process_hearing_followed.md) across his years abroad and folded into his sound. He blew the songs of the mineworkers, who came from a dozen tongues and made one shared work-language underground, and turned what could not be said across all those tongues into something a horn could carry, so the gap at the limit of the tongue was the very thing his trumpet spoke past, umsindo othetha lapho amazwi ehluleka, a sound that speaks where words give out.

## Action

In [the era overcome](plot_03_era_overcome.md) he carries the township sound to the world while home is held apart, mixing the many musics of the country into one and proving the [Rainbow Nation](position_rainbow_nation.md) in song before the law had made it real. By blowing the music of the mines and the townships across the earth, he keeps the home alive in the world's ear, and sounds the welcome the country will get when it returns.

## Shadow

The joy in the horn can let the world hear only the party and miss the grief underneath, the township sound enjoyed as a good time while the cost it came out of goes unheard. A nation can dance to him and forget the long exile in the music, the homesickness blown into every bright note.

## Tell

He plays the song of the mineworker calling for the train to bring him home, and a whole stadium of people who never went near a mine weep and dance at once, the grief and the joy of the country sounded through one horn.
