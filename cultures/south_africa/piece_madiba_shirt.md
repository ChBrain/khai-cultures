# Piece: the Madiba shirt

## Taxonomy

Parent group: pieces

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Place

On the shoulders of the father of the nation and then on the country he made: a loose, bright, open-necked shirt, patterned and printed, worn untucked and worn anywhere, to the office and the rally and the braai alike. Named for the man who made it his own, Madiba, who wore it to meet kings and presidents in place of the stiff suit they expected, the easy shirt of an easy, dignified country.

## Load Bearing

It stands for the new country's way of carrying itself, dignified without being stiff, proud without standing on ceremony, the very tone of a nation that would rather sort things out face to face than bow to rank. When Madiba wore a bright printed shirt to meet the powerful of the world, he dressed authority down and humanity up, and told the country it could be itself, warm and informal and sure, before anyone at all. The shirt became the everyday flag of that spirit, worn by ordinary people as a small daily claim on the same ease and the same dignity. Without this shirt the country's gentle, unbowed style would have no cloth to wear it in.

## Apparent

A loose collared shirt of printed cotton or silk, bright with pattern, buttoned partway and worn over the trousers, comfortable and bold. In itself it is just a comfortable shirt; its whole weight is in the man who wore it and the way of being he wore it for, the dignity that did not need a suit to prove itself.

## Yearbook

- Worn by Madiba through his presidency in place of formal dress, to meet the powerful of the world as himself.
- Taken up by ordinary South Africans as the everyday sign of the country's warm, unbowed style.
- Worn on Madiba's birthday and on days of national pride as a small claim on the spirit he carried.
