# South Africa: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md) is written from South Africa's profile on
Geert Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no
number. The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record
without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | South Africa score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ---------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 49                           |
| Individualism                 | 65                           |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 63                           |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 49                           |
| Long Term Orientation         | 34                           |
| Indulgence                    | 63                           |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group. South Africa's published scores
reflect a long-standing measurement note, and this staging reads the country from
within its own self-understanding as a strongly group-held, hospitable, and
indulgent people; the data is kept here on the record and never enters the prose.

## Historical sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn
from the public historical record. They are credited here and never claimed; the
staging is original work. The hardest part of the story - the era of being held
apart, the mining heritage, and the cost to the first peoples - is staged soberly
on the public record, with no atrocity detail and no political side, as the era
overcome and the triumph of reconciliation.

historical individual: a composite that carries ubuntu, the grandmother who holds
the family and the stranger's child alike and passes on the tongue and the stories
the young are losing. Hugh Masekela's culture file is [persona_masekela.md](persona_masekela.md),
the six-colour flag is [piece_the_flag.md](piece_the_flag.md), and the Madiba
