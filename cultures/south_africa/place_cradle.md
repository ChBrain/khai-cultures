# Place: the Cradle of Humankind

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Shown

The hills of dolomite and limestone north of Johannesburg, the highveld grass running to the horizon and the deep caves beneath it where our oldest grandmothers walked. From this ground came some of the earliest bones of the human family, so that the country can say, plainly and proudly, that here is one of the places where all of us began. The koppies and the thornveld, the long light over the grass, and beneath the surface the caves that hold the deepest root of the whole human story. The oldest of all our homes, on the country's own soil.

## Offers

A place where the country touches the deepest pedigree there is, older than any nation or border, the very beginning of humankind; a stage where ubuntu can claim its oldest root, the truth that we are all one family reaching back to a common Cradle, and where the country reminds itself and the world that the human story started here, at the foot of the continent.

## Withheld

The ease of an unbroken inheritance. The Cradle holds the beginning of all of us, yet the first peoples of that very ground, the San and the Khoe, were among those most pushed to the edges in the eras that followed, so the deepest pride of the country sits beside its oldest debt, and the place where humankind began also asks that those who came first be honoured at last.
