# Position: ubuntu

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The old, deep truth the country carries in its bones: umuntu ngumuntu ngabantu, a person is a person through other people. It holds that no one is whole on their own, that a human being is made and held by family, by clan, by community, and that what is owed to a neighbour is owed because we belong to one another. It is older than any nation, rooted in the very Cradle where humankind began, carried in isiZulu and isiXhosa and a dozen tongues besides, and it is the ground on which a country of many peoples can stand as one. Ubuntu says my humanity is bound up in yours, so to diminish you is to diminish myself, and to lift you is to lift us both.

## Orders

That a person is to be greeted, fed, and seen before he is questioned, the stranger welcomed as kin; that wrongs are best mended face to face and the broken thing between people made whole rather than left to fester; that the group comes before the lone self and the gogo and the elder are honoured; and that the worth of the country is measured by how it holds the least of its people, for a person is a person through other people.

## Loses

Ground when the circle of who counts as one of us is drawn too small, when belonging hardens into keeping others out, the very thing the era overcome was built on; and ground, too, when the fine word ubuntu is said often and lived seldom, recited at funerals and forgotten in the daily grind, so the truth that we are made by one another thins into a slogan on a poster.

## Drives

Lives in the gogo, the grandmother who holds the family and the clan together and feeds whoever comes to her door, the truth of ubuntu in one pair of hands. It drives South Africa to reckon that no one is whole alone, that the home is only a home if all of us have a place at it, and to keep mending what breaks between the peoples face to face, the way the old truth says it must be done.
