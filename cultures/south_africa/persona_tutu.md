# Persona: Desmond Tutu

## Taxonomy

[reconciliation](position_reconciliation.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The Arch, the small archbishop with the enormous laugh, who gave the country the very word Rainbow Nation and then sat at the head of the table where it told its own truth out loud. Holy and cheeky both, he could weep with a witness one minute and giggle like a schoolboy the next, and he carried the conviction that no wound is healed in the dark, only in the open and only by forgiving. His mother tongue is isiXhosa, with the Setswana of his Klerksdorp boyhood close beside it: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [hears](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) in his mother tongue, the tongue of the prayers and the praise. [English](position_language_en_za.md) he [carries as the tongue of the church and the world](process_speaking_carried.md), preaching and [writing](process_writing_drafted.md) in it for the whole earth to hear, and the Afrikaans around him he [follows by ear](process_hearing_followed.md) without trouble. He told the nation's truth and asked its forgiveness across all its tongues at once, holding a hearing where a man could testify in isiZulu and be answered in English and wept over in Afrikaans, so the gap at the limit of the tongue was the very thing his table was built to cross, ukuxolelana, the making of peace between people who do not share a single word.

## Action

In [the long walk to reconciliation](plot_04_reconciliation.md) he leads the country in telling its truth and chooses [reconciliation](position_reconciliation.md) as the way an era is overcome, naming the new nation the Rainbow people of God. By insisting that the truth be told before the forgiving, he gives South Africa a way to heal its wound in the open rather than bury it, and makes mercy the country's founding act.

## Shadow

The forgiving he asked for can be turned into a way to close the book too soon, the truth told once and the changing left undone, so the Rainbow he named becomes a pretty word over gaps still standing. A nation can love the laugh and skip the hard demand behind it, treating reconciliation as a thing finished rather than a thing it must keep choosing.

## Tell

He stands before a hall of people who did terrible things to one another and asks them to face it and forgive it, and then breaks into a laugh that says, even here, especially here, there is hope for us yet.
