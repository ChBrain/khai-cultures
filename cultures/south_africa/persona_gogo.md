# Persona: the Gogo

## Taxonomy

[ubuntu](position_ubuntu.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The grandmother who holds the whole family on her back, the gogo, the makhulu, the ouma, whatever tongue you call her in, the one whose pot is always on and whose door is never shut. She raised her own and is raising her children's children too, sends the pension to whoever needs it, and feeds every child who comes past, hers or not, because in her house a child is everyone's child. Tough as veld grass and soft as mealie pap, slow to anger and quick to laugh, she is ubuntu standing in a doorway, the truth that a person is a person through other people worn in one apron. Her mother tongue is the deep one, isiZulu or isiXhosa or Sesotho or Setswana, the tongue of the clan praises and the bedtime stories: she [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks in her mother tongue](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md), and she [carries the old stories in it](process_speaking_carried.md), the proverbs and the praise-names the young ones half-forget. [English](position_language_en_za.md) she [follows by ear](process_hearing_followed.md) only in part, a word here and there off the radio and the grandchildren, and answers in her own tongue when they answer her in theirs. She tells the old stories in a tongue her grandchildren are losing, and they answer her in an English she only half-follows, so the love crosses the table even where the words no longer fully meet, and the gap at the limit of the tongue runs right between her and the children she is raising, ulwimi oludlulayo, the tongue that is passing.

## Action

In [the Cradle of Humankind](plot_01_cradle.md) she stands at the far end of the oldest human story there is, the keeper of [ubuntu](position_ubuntu.md) carried down from the first grandmothers who walked that ground, the one who feeds and holds and raises the next of us. She carries the deepest truth of the country forward by living it plainly, holding the family and the clan and the stranger's child all in one pair of hands.

## Shadow

The whole weight of the family resting on her can let the rest of the country lean on the gogo and call it ubuntu, the old woman carrying what a fairer home would share, her endless giving mistaken for a thing that costs her nothing. A nation can honour the grandmother in song and leave her to hold the world up alone.

## Tell

She sets another plate for a child who is not hers without being asked and without a second thought, because in her house no child eats alone, and that, more than any speech, is what umuntu ngumuntu ngabantu means.
