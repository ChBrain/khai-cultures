# Process: the braai

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

Anyone with a fire, some meat, and people to feed: a family on a Sunday, mates on a public holiday, a neighbourhood on Heritage Day when half the country fires up the coals at once. It takes only a grid over the flames, the boerewors and the chops and the chicken sosaties laid out, a few cold ones in the cooler box, and the call to come round. The fire is lit early, the meat goes on late, and nobody is in a hurry, because the braai is the gathering as much as the meal.

## Direction

Toward belonging and the mending of things between people: the fire where the whole country, every people and every tongue, does the same thing on the same day, gathered around the coals. The boerewors and the bobotie, the bunny chow brought by one family and the chakalaka by another, the pap and the chops, all of it shared, the feast of the Rainbow laid out on one table. It is not just cooking but a rite of being together, the place where the real talk happens, where a quarrel is settled and a stranger made a friend, all of it sorted out face to face over the flames.

## Lever

It is driven by the deep South African truth that things are best put right between people in person, around a fire, with food in hand, and by the long custom that no one who comes to the braai goes away hungry. It holds against the long week and the things that pull the peoples apart; against it stand the divisions that a single fire cannot mend on its own and the work still left when the coals go cold.

## Echo

The braai makes audible what drives [ubuntu](position_culture_ubuntu.md): that a person is a person through other people, and that what is broken between people is mended face to face, around a shared fire. To this fire [the Gogo](persona_gogo.md) brings her pot and feeds whoever comes, hers or not, the many made one around the coals.
