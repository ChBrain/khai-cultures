# Plot: the Cradle of Humankind

## Taxonomy

## Owner

## Cue

Long before there were nations or borders or names, the highveld north of where Johannesburg now stands held the caves where our oldest grandmothers walked. From the dolomite of those hills came some of the earliest of our human kin, so that the story of this ground runs deeper than any country, all the way down to the beginning of us.

## Action

In the limestone caves of the Cradle, generation upon generation of early humankind lived and died, leaving the bones that would one day tell the world that here, at the foot of the continent, the human family got going. Across the same wide land the first peoples settled, the San reading the game and the stars and painting the rock shelters, the Khoe herding their cattle across the veld, and after them the Bantu-speaking nations spreading south with their iron and their grain. Long before the word ubuntu was spoken, the truth of it was already being lived on this ground, the oldest of all our homes.

## Tension

The country can claim the deepest pedigree of all, the very Cradle of humankind, and yet the oldest peoples of that ground, the San and the Khoe who were here first, were among those most pushed to the edges in the eras that followed. The land that holds the beginning of all of us also holds the long task of honouring those who came first, so the pride of the Cradle and the debt to the first peoples lie together in the same ancient soil.
