# Position: South African English

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The authority of English as one of South Africa's eleven official tongues, and the working one that crosses between them, the language that holds among the isiZulu and the isiXhosa, the Sesotho and the Setswana, the Afrikaans and the many mother tongues of the house without belonging wholly to any of them. It is the tongue of the courtroom and the parliament, the newspaper and the boardroom, carried in by the colony and turned, in the long struggle against apartheid, into the shared speech of a liberation that had to reach every people at once. It belongs to no single group and so can be borrowed by all, the neutral ground on which a nation of many peoples meets, while the deepest things are still spoken in the tongue of the clan praises and the grandmother's story.

## Orders

What the nation conducts its public life across its peoples in: the statute, the ruling, the broadcast and the printed page. It orders the common ground where communities that carry different mother tongues can meet on equal terms, none made to speak inside another's language, and it gives a nation with no single shared tongue a working one in which to address itself and the world. It is the language in which the new constitution was written and the truth was told at the hearings, the speech that had to hold true across every tongue in the room at once.

## Loses

Ground to the mother tongues that live in the home and the heart, the isiXhosa and the isiZulu, the Sesotho and the Setswana in which the people pray, mourn and praise, and to the clan names and the proverbs the working tongue never wholly holds. It loses, too, to Afrikaans, the other settler tongue with its own long claim, and it loses most when it becomes the language of advancement and the screen, so that the grandchildren come to do their thinking in English and answer the grandmother in a tongue she only half-follows, and the deep tongue thins toward silence across a single kitchen table.

## Drives

It drives a nation of many peoples to hold its shared public life on ground that erases none of them. It is carried, not born, by [Nelson Mandela](persona_mandela.md), who held the whole nation together in an English that belonged wholly to none of its peoples, the tongue that crosses; by [Desmond Tutu](persona_tutu.md), who gave it the word Rainbow Nation and preached and wrote in it for the whole earth to hear; by [Miriam Makeba](persona_makeba.md), who carried it to every stage on earth alongside the click-songs of home; and by [Hugh Masekela](persona_masekela.md), who took it as the working tongue of the music and the wide world. It reached [the gogo](persona_gogo.md) only at the edge of the ear, a word here and there off the radio and the grandchildren, while she tells the old stories in the deep tongue they are losing. So the working tongue shows who carries it and whom it merely reaches, and binds the country's peoples without erasing the mother tongues they were born to.
