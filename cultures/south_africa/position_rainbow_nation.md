# Position: the Rainbow Nation

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The picture the country gave itself when it became one: a nation of many peoples and many tongues held together not by being the same but by standing side by side, each colour its own and all of them one bow of light. Zulu and Xhosa, Afrikaner and Sotho, Tswana and Pedi, Venda and Tsonga, Ndebele and Swazi, the Cape Malay and the Indian community, and the San and Khoe who were here first, eleven official tongues on the books and a feast of music and food and faith, all of it held in equal dignity under one flag. It is the wager that difference need not divide, that a country can be made richer, not weaker, by being many, and that the home belongs equally to every one who lives in it.

## Orders

That every people and every tongue is to be held in equal dignity, none above and none beneath; that the many are a richness to be celebrated and not a problem to be solved, the languages spoken, the songs sung, the foods shared at one table; and that the work of the country is to make the equal dignity real in life and not just fine on paper, so the Rainbow is something lived and not only said.

## Loses

Ground when the colours start to pull apart again, when one people's gain is read as another's loss and the country sorts itself back into us and them; and ground, too, when the Rainbow becomes a postcard, a pretty word for the tourists hiding the gaps still left between the peoples, so the celebration of the many papers over the work of making them truly equal.

## Drives

Lives in [Miriam Makeba](persona_makeba.md), who carried the country's many-voiced song to the whole world, and in [Hugh Masekela](persona_masekela.md), whose horn mixed the townships and the world into one sound. It drives South Africa to hold its many peoples in equal dignity, to celebrate the feast of its difference, and to keep the one bow of light from breaking back into separate colours.
