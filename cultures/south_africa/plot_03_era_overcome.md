# Plot: the era overcome

## Taxonomy

[play_south_africa](play_south_africa.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

For a long, hard era through much of the twentieth century, the peoples of South Africa were kept apart by law, each held separate from the others, denied the equal place in one home that the country would later choose. It was the era the nation would have to overcome before it could become one, and it is remembered soberly, with dignity, as the wound that had to be healed.

## Action

Through those decades the country was divided against itself, its peoples held apart and held unequal, while many within it and many in exile worked and waited for the day it would be undone. The music carried the conscience the law would not allow: [Miriam Makeba](persona_makeba.md) sang the country's many-voiced song to the whole world from her long exile, and [Hugh Masekela](persona_masekela.md) blew the sound of the townships and the mines across the earth, both of them keeping the home alive in the world's ear when it could not be lived in freely. The pressure built within and without, the world turned its face away from the division, and slowly the era that had seemed unmovable began to give. What had been built to keep the peoples apart could not, in the end, hold against the country's deeper truth that a person is a person through other people.

## Stage

- [Miriam Makeba](persona_makeba.md): the voice that carried the country's song and conscience to the world through its hardest era.
- [Hugh Masekela](persona_masekela.md): the horn that blew the township and mine music across the earth and kept the home alive abroad.
- [the Rainbow Nation](position_rainbow_nation.md): the many peoples held apart in this era who would soon be held in one equal home.

## Tension

The era of being held apart is the country's deepest wound, and it is named here plainly and without dwelling, an era to be overcome and not relived. The hardness of it ran against the country's own oldest truth, that the peoples are made by one another, and the music that crossed every border in those years was the proof that the home could not finally be kept divided. The wound was real, and so was the song that outlasted it, and the nation faced the one in order to win the other.
