# Persona: Edith

## Taxonomy

[understatement](position_understatement.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

An older Englishwoman of the village, who keeps the parish church flowers, lays on the cricket teas, and meets every trouble with a 'mustn't grumble'. Reserved, dry, and quietly capable, she would no sooner make a fuss than make a scene, and she says least about the things that matter most. She [speaks her mother tongue](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) in the level, understated key of someone who lets the unsaid carry the weight.

## Action

She lives [understatement](position_understatement.md) without thinking about it, pours the tea at the [village cricket](process_cricket.md) match, and keeps the parish ticking over by quiet, uncredited work. She holds the green and ordered England of fields and the village steady, and reminds the young that a stiff upper lip is not the same as feeling nothing.

## Shadow

Her reserve can become a refusal to speak of anything real, so that grief and trouble go unattended behind the calm. The horror of making a fuss can leave the deepest things unsaid until it is too late to say them.

## Tell

She answers serious sympathy with 'mustn't grumble' and a fresh pot of tea, and lets the silence say the rest.
