# England: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the
pitch (kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources
behind the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md) is written from the Great Britain profile on
Geert Hofstede's model of national culture. England has no separate Hofstede
profile of its own; the data sits at the national (Great Britain) level, so the
national scores are the base from which the pitch is written, and it names no
dimension and carries no number. The scores and their source are recorded here so
the data is on the record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Great Britain score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ----------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 35                            |
| Individualism                 | 89                            |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 66                            |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 35                            |
| Long Term Orientation         | 51                            |
| Indulgence                    | 69                            |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published
by Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group. No sub-national (English)
sub-score is claimed; the national (Great Britain) profile is the documented base.

The two defining personas, [Edith](persona_edith.md) and [Liam](persona_liam.md),
are archetypes, not historical individuals: composites that carry the culture's
living tensions, the older village England of the parish and the cricket tea
(Edith, understatement and 'mustn't grumble') against the younger, plain-spoken
post-industrial north (Liam, the football club and the pub).
