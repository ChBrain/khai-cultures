# Persona: Liam

## Taxonomy

[fair play](position_fair_play.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A younger Englishman of the post-industrial north, raised where the mills used to be, who lives for his football club, his mates, and the Friday pint. Wry, plain-spoken, and quick with a joke, he wears his loyalty hard and his feeling lightly, and he can take any subject seriously except, in so many words, himself. He [speaks, hears, and thinks in](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) his mother tongue in the dry, deadpan key of the post-industrial north, the broad vowels that [the King's English](position_language_en_gb_eng.md) of the south still hears as 'less', and he wears them with hard pride. The global English of the screen [he carries without a thought](process_speaking_worn.md), for the world's tongue is his by birth: there is the gap, the language that rules the earth is his own, yet his own accent can still place him below in his own country, and he would rather get a laugh than admit it stings.

## Action

He lives [fair play](position_fair_play.md) at the match and in the [pub](process_the_pub.md), where the rules of the game and a fair shout are sacred and a cheat is the worst thing going. He carries the loyalty and the humour of the old working north into a town whose work has gone, holding to the club and the round when the rest has changed.

## Shadow

His armour of irony can leave him with no language for the things that hurt, so that the loss of the work, the place, and the future goes unspoken under the banter. The joke that protects him can also keep him from ever saying what is wrong.

## Tell

He deflects every real feeling with a wind-up and a one-liner, and shows he cares only by who he stands beside.
