# Process: village cricket

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

Anyone who rolls a square, finds eleven willing players, and puts the kettle on for the tea. Village cricket needs only a green, a long summer afternoon, and a set of unwritten rules that everyone agrees to keep.

## Direction

Toward a slow, sociable ritual that may well end in a draw and matters more for the playing than the result: overs bowled, tea taken at the interval, applause for the other side's good shot. It is less a contest than a shared observance of fair play and good form.

## Lever

The love of the game for its own sake drives it, and the conviction that how you play is the point. Against it stands the wish to win at any cost, and that the game quietly refuses: a victory got by sharp practice is no victory at all.

## Echo

Village cricket makes [fair play](position_fair_play.md) visible on a summer green, and it gathers the parish around the boundary, where [Edith](persona_edith.md) pours the tea and the whole quiet, ordered England of the village shows itself for an afternoon.
