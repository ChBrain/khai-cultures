# Plot: The Industrial Revolution

## Taxonomy

[play_england](play_england.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

In the mill towns of the north, water and then steam are harnessed to spin and weave, and the country stands at the edge of a change that will remake how people live and work.

## Action

Between roughly 1760 and 1840, [Manchester](place_manchester.md) and the towns around it fill with mills, and England becomes the workshop of the world, the first country to be made over by the machine. The land empties into the cities, and the mills bring both vast new wealth and crowded, grinding poverty. [Charles Dickens](persona_dickens.md) writes the human cost of it into the national conscience, holding the smoke and the want up to the light.

## Stage

- [Manchester](place_manchester.md): the cotton city, the workshop of the world.
- [Charles Dickens](persona_dickens.md): the novelist who makes the country see the cost of its own machine.

## Tension

The country leads the world into the industrial age and grows rich on it, but at a price written in slums and child labour. Out of that contradiction comes a lasting English unease about wealth and fairness, and a green, pastoral self-image held up against the smoke as the country the machine took away.
