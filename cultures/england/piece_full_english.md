# Piece: the full English

## Owner

## Place

Served on a warm plate in cafes and kitchens across the country of a weekend morning, the cooked breakfast of bacon, eggs, sausage, beans, tomato, mushroom, and fried bread, the full English.

## Load Bearing

It stands for the plain, hearty, unfussy comfort the country likes best. Generous without being grand, the same on a workman's plate and a country-house sideboard, it is the food of the ordinary good morning, no ceremony about it. Without this honest, level plateful, the English liking for the solid and unpretentious would be only a saying; here it is breakfast.

## Apparent

A loaded plate of fried and grilled things, hot and filling, with a pot of strong tea beside it. Nothing about it is delicate or refined; it is meant to be tucked into, not admired, and to set a person up for the day.
