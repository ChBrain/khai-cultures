## Shown

The cotton city of the industrial north, the first great factory town, of mills and warehouses, canals and chimneys, rain and red brick. Plain-spoken and hard-working, proud of its work and its football, the workshop of the world that built much of the modern age in smoke and noise.

## Offers

The other England against the village green: the city the machine made, where the country's wealth and its want were both spun, and where the wry, loyal, plain-spoken temper of the working north took shape.

## Withheld

The honeyed calm of the pastoral idyll. Manchester refuses the soft, green picture of the country and wears its smoke and its graft without apology, the workshop set against the field.
