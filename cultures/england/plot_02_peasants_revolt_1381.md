# Plot: The Peasants' Revolt 1381

## Taxonomy

[play_england](play_england.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

A new poll tax falls hardest on the poorest, and the labourers of the countryside, fewer and more needed since the plague, have had enough of being bound and taxed by their betters.

## Action

Led by Wat Tyler, the common people march on the capital and force an audience with the boy king himself, demanding an end to serfdom and a country where, as the rebels' preacher put it, no man is born a master of another. Tyler is struck down and the promises are broken, but the rising lodges a thing that does not go away: the figure of the free-born Englishman who will answer back to power and stand on his rights.

## Stage

- [fair play](position_fair_play.md): the rebels' claim that the rules must bind the high as well as the low.
- [the Cotswolds](place_cotswolds.md): the country shires the labourers march out of, the England of fields and parishes.

## Tension

The revolt is crushed and its leaders killed, and serfdom does not end by decree. But the idea outlasts the defeat: that the ordinary Englishman is no man's property and may speak back to a king. It becomes part of the national grain, a stubborn dissent from below that fair play is owed to all.
