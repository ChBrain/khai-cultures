# Position: fair play

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The power of the rules of the game over the prize. Fair play holds that how you play matters more than whether you win, that the rules bind the strong as well as the weak, and that a cheat forfeits more by cheating than they could ever gain by it. It makes 'playing the game' a measure of a person and 'it isn't cricket' a verdict that needs no appeal.

## Orders

That you play by the rules even when breaking them would win, take your loss without complaint, and never kick a man when he is down. The umpire's word is final, the weaker side gets a fair shout, and a victory got by sharp practice is no victory at all.

## Loses

Ground where the appeal to fair play becomes a way to keep the well-placed comfortable and tell the losers to lose quietly, or where the form of the game matters more than any real justice. It loses when 'playing the game' means accepting a rigged one.

## Drives

It lives in [Liam](persona_liam.md), for whom a cheat is the worst thing going at the match, and it carries [Charles Dickens](persona_dickens.md), who pressed that fair play is owed to the pauper and the child, not only to the gentleman.
