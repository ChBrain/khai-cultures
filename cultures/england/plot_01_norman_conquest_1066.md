# Plot: The Norman Conquest 1066

## Taxonomy

[play_england](play_england.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

The old English king Harold has just won in the north when word comes that a duke has landed from across the Channel with an army and a claim. He marches his tired men south to the field by Hastings.

## Action

By the end of a single long day Harold is dead and the English line is broken. The duke is crowned king, and over the next twenty years the conquerors survey the whole country down to the last ox, raise stone castles over the towns, and set a French-speaking order above an English-speaking people. The old tongue goes underground and comes back changed; the country is made over from the top down.

## Stage

- [the Cotswolds](place_cotswolds.md): the green English shires that the survey counts and the new order takes over.
- [understatement](position_understatement.md): the habit, born here, of a conquered people learning to keep its feeling to itself.

## Tension

The country is taken whole, by force and from above, and an English world is buried under a foreign crown. Yet the loss does not erase the English; it drives them inward, where over centuries the buried tongue and the stubborn temper rise again and remake the conquerors into something English in their turn.
