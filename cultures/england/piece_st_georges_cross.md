# Piece: the St George's Cross

## Taxonomy

Parent group: pieces

## Owner

## Place

Painted on faces and draped over balconies at the football, flown from church towers on the saint's day, the plain red cross on a white ground that is England's own flag, distinct from the union of the four nations.

## Load Bearing

It stands for England as itself, apart from the wider union it long led. A simple red cross of the country's patron saint, it carries the awkward, hopeful question of what England is on its own, and it comes out most at the match, where the country cheers for itself by name. Without this plain English sign, England would have no image of itself separate from the union.

## Apparent

A red cross on a white field, with none of the layered union of the larger flag. Nothing about it is ornate; it belongs to the terrace and the church tower as much as to any ceremony, the country's own plain mark.
