# Position: the amateur

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The power of the gifted all-rounder over the narrow specialist. The cult of the amateur holds that the man who does a thing for love and makes it look easy is finer than the professional who grinds at it for a living, and that the well-rounded talent who can turn a hand to anything beats the expert who can do only the one thing. It makes effortlessness a virtue and trying too hard a small disgrace.

## Orders

That you wear your ability lightly, never seem to swot or strain, and treat your accomplishments as a happy knack rather than hard-won skill. The good loser, the dabbler in many arts, the captain who has never trained: these are the admired figures, not the drilled professional.

## Loses

Ground where the disdain for the professional becomes mere snobbery and an excuse not to prepare, so that charm and confidence are preferred to competence and the country is run by gifted amateurs who do not know their brief. It loses when looking easy matters more than being good.

## Drives

It reaches from the Renaissance king who was theologian, composer, and scholar at once and mastered each as a happy knack, into the lasting English preference for the all-rounder who makes it look easy.
