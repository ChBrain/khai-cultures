# Persona: Henry VIII

## Taxonomy

[the amateur](position_the_amateur.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The Renaissance king who could turn his hand to anything: theologian, composer, jouster, scholar of languages, a gifted all-rounder who wore his talents lightly and his will heavily. Vain, clever, and used to getting his way, he believed an English question was England's to settle. He [speaks, writes, and thinks in](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) his mother tongue, [the King's English](position_language_en_gb_eng.md), as the natural ground of a king who would answer to no foreign authority; the Latin of the Church, the French and Spanish of the courts, he [carries as a learned man's tools](process_speaking_carried.md). There is the gap: the scholar-king who knows the tongues of Europe makes an English question England's alone, and breaks the Latin grip of Rome to set the English word, in time, over the altar his people kneel at.

## Action

In the [English Reformation 1534](plot_03_reformation_1534.md) he breaks the church from Rome and makes himself its head, cutting the country loose to govern its own faith. What he does for his own reasons sets [the amateur](position_the_amateur.md) king, master of many arts and ruler over all of them, at the head of an English settlement.

## Shadow

The freedom he wins is taken from the top by force and for himself, and the wealth of the monasteries goes into his own hands. The all-rounder's confidence that he can master anything tips into a tyrant's certainty that he may do anything.

## Tell

He treats every art as one he can pick up and master, and every authority above him as one he need not obey.
