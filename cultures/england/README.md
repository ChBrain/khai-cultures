# England: a khai culture

England staged as a full khai play, ISO GB-ENG, language en. England is a
constituent nation of the United Kingdom, and it has no Hofstede profile of its
own: it inherits the Great Britain (national) base. The green and ironic country
as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its
temper encodes, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by
a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_england.md](play_england.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md), the Hofstede layer, written from the
  Great Britain profile (reserved, ironic, individualist, fair play and the
  amateur over the grind) and naming no dimension. England carries no sub-score of
  its own; the national base stands in.
- **Plots (history):** the Norman Conquest of 1066, the Peasants' Revolt of 1381,
  the English Reformation of 1534, and the Industrial Revolution (c. 1760-1840),
  each casting the elements it needs (a figure, a place, a position).
- **Personas:** Henry VIII and Charles Dickens as the historic cast, plus two
  defining archetypes, Edith (older, the village, the parish, understatement and
  'mustn't grumble') and Liam (younger, the post-industrial north, football and
  the pub). Each is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** fair play (the rules of the game over the prize), understatement
  (the stiff upper lip, saying less than you mean), the amateur (the gifted
  all-rounder over the professional).
- **Places:** the Cotswolds (the green and pleasant countryside, the village),
  Manchester (the industrial north, the workshop of the world).
- **Processes:** village cricket, the pub.
- **Pieces:** the St George's Cross (England's own flag), the full English
  (the cooked breakfast).
