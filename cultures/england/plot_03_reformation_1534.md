# Plot: The English Reformation 1534

## Taxonomy

[play_england](play_england.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

[Henry VIII](persona_henry_viii.md) wants his marriage annulled and an heir, and Rome will not grant it. The king decides that no foreign authority will rule on an English matter.

## Action

By act of his own parliament Henry is declared Supreme Head of the Church in England, and the country is cut loose from Rome. The monasteries are dissolved and their wealth taken, an English settlement of faith begins, and the Church of England comes into being as something the country governs for itself, in its own measured way, neither wholly old nor wholly new.

## Stage

- [Henry VIII](persona_henry_viii.md): the king who makes himself head of the church and breaks with Rome.
- [the amateur](position_the_amateur.md): the king as gifted all-rounder, theologian and musician, who turns his own wants into a national settlement.

## Tension

The break is made from the top, for the king's own reasons, yet it sets the country on its own course in faith as in temper: a national church that prefers the middle way and the quiet compromise to the absolute. What began as one man's want becomes a lasting habit of settling English questions by English means.
