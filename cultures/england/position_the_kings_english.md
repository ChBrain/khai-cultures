# Position: the King's English

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The prestige standard of the tongue: received pronunciation and the grammar of the public school and the BBC, with the King James Bible and Johnson's Dictionary behind it. It is the voice that places a person the moment they open their mouth, and the accent that opens the doors that matter.

## Orders

That one is heard as one's class before one is heard as oneself: that the received voice is "correct" and the regional voice a falling-short, and that the interview, the bench, and the drawing-room reward the right vowels. It makes how a person speaks matter as much as what they say.

## Loses

Ground as Estuary, regional, and multicultural London speech rise and received pronunciation itself softens; the old plummy voice becomes a liability as often as an asset, and the shame once pinned on the dropped aitch slowly eases.

## Drives

It lives in [Edith](persona_edith.md)'s level gentry key, worn without a thought; it is what [Liam](persona_liam.md) sets his broad northern vowels against with hard pride; and it is the polite voice [Dickens](persona_dickens.md) wrote the street against. It drives England to hear class in every vowel and to sort its people by how, not what, they speak.
