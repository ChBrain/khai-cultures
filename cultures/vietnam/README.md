# Vietnam: a khai culture

Vietnam staged as a full khai play, ISO VN, language vi. A civilization of the
rice deltas, bound by family and an enduring will to remain itself, set out as a
theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its
language and faith encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects,
all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_vietnam.md](play_vietnam.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_vi.md](pitch_vi.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Vietnam's profile (a collectivist, family- and village-centred society, marked
  by respect for hierarchy and elders, patience and thrift, and a long-term,
  enduring outlook) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the dragon-and-fairy origin lore and the Trung Sisters,
  the thousand years and the winning of independence, script and poetry, and
  reunification and renewal (Doi Moi) - each casting the elements it needs, and
  each staging the twentieth century soberly, taking no side.
- **Personas:** the Trung Sisters, Nguyen Trai, Nguyen Du, and Ho Xuan Huong as
  the historic cast, plus one defining archetype, the Mother (Nguoi Me), the
  keeper of the village home and the ancestral altar. Each is wired to the
  language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** the spirit of independence (the enduring will of a small nation
  to remain itself), the three teachings and ancestor veneration (Confucianism,
  Buddhism, Daoism with folk belief, crowned by the family altar), the village
  and wet rice (the cradle of Vietnamese life on the two deltas).
- **Places:** the Red River Delta (the northern cradle of the first state and the
  dynasties), the Mekong Delta (the southern rice basket and the face of the
  opening country).
- **Processes:** Tet (the lunar new year of reunion and ancestral return), water
  puppetry (the village turning its own rice pond into a stage).
- **Pieces:** the ao dai (the graceful national dress of Vietnamese women), pho
  (the slow-simmered noodle soup that became the face of Vietnamese cuisine).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; Vietnam's long history of occupation and resistance, and the wars,
partition, and reunification of the twentieth century, are treated soberly on the
public record, with no atrocity detail and taking no political side; the staging
and the architecture are original work.
