# Vietnam: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_vi.md](pitch_vi.md) is written from Vietnam's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record without
entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Vietnam score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ----------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 70                      |
| Individualism                 | 20                      |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 40                      |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 30                      |
| Long Term Orientation         | 57                      |
| Indulgence                    | 35                      |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; Vietnam scores low on Individualism
(a strongly collectivist, group-oriented society) and high on Power Distance.

## Historical sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn
from the public historical record. They are credited here and never claimed; the
staging is original work. The long history of occupation and resistance, and the
wars, partition, and reunification of the twentieth century, are staged soberly on
the public record, with no atrocity detail and taking no political side; the focus
is kept on the enduring culture, language, land, and people.
