# Persona: the Kiwi

## Taxonomy

[the social laboratory](position_the_social_laboratory.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The ordinary New Zealander: modest, dry, practical, good with their hands, who backs the underdog as a reflex, distrusts the show-off and the big-noter, and asks of the world only a fair go and to be left to get on with it. Equally at home on the farm and at the bach, at the rugby and the barbecue, the Kiwi fixes the broken thing with what is in the shed, takes pride in self-reliance, and is suspicious of anyone who puts on airs or talks themselves up. Warm under the reserve and quick to help a mate, slow to boast and quick to deflate. The mother tongue is [New Zealand English](position_language_en_nz.md) in its own register, dry and deadpan and understated: the Kiwi [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md), and [hears](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) in it, and through it now run the words of the other tongue, te reo Māori carried into everyday speech - kia ora, whānau, mana, kai, aroha - the bicultural cadence that has [borrowed](process_speaking_borrowed.md) what was once suppressed into the common voice. And there is the gap at the limit of the tongue: that the Kiwi greets in te reo and farewells in it and means it warmly, yet most often does not have the whole of it, the everyday words held while the deeper language stands just out of reach - the partnership spoken at the surface of the tongue while the full fluency, like the full Treaty, is still being learned. The Kiwi belongs to [New Zealand culture](position_culture_new_zealand_culture.md) as the ordinary citizen the fair go, the Treaty, and the make-do spirit are all held in trust for.

## Action

In [the Social Laboratory](plot_03_the_social_laboratory.md) the Kiwi is the citizen the fair society is built for and around, the ordinary person owed a level field and a floor beneath them, the keeper and the beneficiary of [the social laboratory](position_the_social_laboratory.md). By backing the underdog, distrusting rank, and asking for nothing but fairness, the Kiwi holds the egalitarian creed in the small daily way that keeps it real.

## Shadow

The modest everyperson is the nation's own shadow: the same instinct that distrusts the skite is the tall poppy syndrome that cuts down the one who rises, and the same easygoing "she'll be right" that makes the Kiwi unflappable can let the harder work - the Treaty, the inequality under the fair go - drift quietly unfinished while everyone agrees it will probably sort itself out.

## Tell

The Kiwi opens with kia ora and shrugs off a compliment, fixes the fence with a length of wire, and backs whoever is losing - asking only for a fair go, and faintly embarrassed by anyone who asks for more.
