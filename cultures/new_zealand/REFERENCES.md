# Aotearoa New Zealand: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-record historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md) is written from New Zealand's profile on
Geert Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no
number. The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record
without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | New Zealand score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | --------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 22                          |
| Individualism                 | 79                          |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 58                          |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 49                          |
| Long Term Orientation         | 33                          |
| Indulgence                    | 75                          |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; New Zealand's low Power Distance and
high Individualism together read as the egalitarian, informal, fair-go temper that
tunes the pitch.

## Historical sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn
from the public historical record. They are credited here and never claimed; the
staging is original work. The partnership at the heart of the story - the Treaty of
Waitangi and its breaches, the New Zealand Wars and the land confiscations, the
near-loss and the renaissance of te reo Māori, and biculturalism - is staged
soberly and respectfully on the public record.

The one defining persona drawn as an archetype, [the Kiwi](persona_the_kiwi.md),
is not a historical individual but a composite that carries the modest, practical,
dry, and egalitarian temper of the ordinary New Zealander - the fair go, the
backing of the underdog, the distrust of pretension, and the bicultural everyday
voice in which te reo Māori is woven into English.
