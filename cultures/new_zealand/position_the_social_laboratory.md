# Position: the social laboratory

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The conviction that the country is a level field where everyone gets a fair go, and that a small and young society can lead the world by trying first what the old countries dare not. New Zealand made itself the social laboratory of the world: the first self-governing nation to grant women the vote, in 1893; among the first to build old-age pensions; and the builder of one of the earliest and broadest welfare states, the promise that no one would be left to fall too far. Under it runs the egalitarian creed, the deep dislike of class and rank and side, the faith that no one is born your better and that the worth of a person is in the doing and the decency, not the birth or the bank.

## Orders

That the field should be level and the go should be fair, that the worker and the woman and the ordinary citizen are owed a floor beneath them and a share of the common wealth; that a small country can experiment, can be first, can prove a thing works before the larger world will risk it; and that the egalitarian temper is to be guarded as the country's own, the mark of a decent society.

## Loses

Ground when the fair go thins to a slogan while inequality widens beneath it, when the floor is let sag and the level field tilts; and ground, too, to the same egalitarian instinct turned sour as the tall poppy syndrome, the levelling that mistrusts excellence and pulls down the one who rises, so that the creed meant to lift everyone is used instead to keep everyone in the row.

## Drives

Lives in the suffragist who led the campaign that made New Zealand the first to give women the vote, and in the Kiwi, the modest, practical everyperson who backs the underdog and asks only for a fair go. It drives Aotearoa New Zealand to keep the field level and the floor beneath its people, and to keep being the country that tries the decent thing first.
