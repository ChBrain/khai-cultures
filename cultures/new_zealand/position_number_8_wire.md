# Position: number 8 wire

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The conviction that whatever needs doing can be done with what is to hand, by yourself, out here at the edge of the world. Number 8 wire is the fencing wire that ran the back-country farms and got bent to a thousand other uses, the byword for the practical, make-do, fix-it-yourself ingenuity of a people far from any factory or expert. "She'll be right" is its motto: get on with it, improvise, trust that it will hold. It is the spirit of the back shed and the farm, of the small nation that could not send away for the part and so made it - and it reaches all the way back to the first arrival, to the wayfinders who crossed the largest ocean on earth with no instrument but the read of the stars, the swell, and the flight of birds.

## Orders

That a person should be able to turn their hand to anything, fix what breaks and build what is needed without waiting for an expert or an excuse; that the smallness and distance of the country are not a handicap but a school of ingenuity, breeding self-reliance and invention; and that the practical doer who quietly gets it done is worth more than the credentialed one who explains why it cannot be.

## Loses

Ground when "she'll be right" slides from confidence into complacency, the make-do bodge standing in for the thing properly done, the corner cut once too often; and ground, too, when the pride in amateur ingenuity hardens into a suspicion of expertise and ambition, so that the country that improvises brilliantly will not invest in doing things to scale, and the wire that fixed everything becomes the excuse for never building better.

## Drives

Lives in [Sir Edmund Hillary](persona_hillary.md), the beekeeper who climbed the highest mountain on earth and shrugged that he had knocked the bastard off, and reaches back to [Kupe](persona_kupe.md), the navigator who found a land no instrument could have shown him. It drives Aotearoa New Zealand to make do, make new, and trust its own hands at the far edge of the world.
