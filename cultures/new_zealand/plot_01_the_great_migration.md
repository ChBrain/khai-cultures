# Plot: the Great Migration

## Owner

## Cue

Out across the vast emptiness of the South Pacific, the great age of Polynesian voyaging reaches its furthest south. The islands behind are settled and full; the navigators read in the stars and the swell and the birds the signs of land still further on. In tradition it is [Kupe](persona_kupe.md) who first steers his waka toward the last great land left empty on the face of the earth.

## Action

Around 1300 the voyagers make landfall on a long, cold, mountainous land at the bottom of the world, the last large habitable place humans would ever find. They had crossed thousands of miles of open ocean in double-hulled waka, navigating by the read of the sky and the sea with no instrument at all, the supreme feat of wayfinding in human history. Their descendants become Māori, and over the centuries they build a whole world upon the new land: iwi and hapū, the deep tie to whenua, the order of whakapapa and mana and tapu, the carving and the song and the spoken genealogy that carried everything. A people make themselves on an empty coast, and name it Aotearoa.

## Stage

- [Kupe](persona_kupe.md): the navigator of tradition who finds the land and lays the first word upon it.
- [number 8 wire](position_number_8_wire.md): the wayfinding ingenuity, the first and purest making-do, that crossed the largest ocean on earth.
- [Aoraki / Mount Cook](place_aoraki.md): the great peak the new people take as an ancestor, the land made kin.

## Tension

A whole world is found and built by skill alone - the most distant and difficult landfall in human history, achieved with nothing but knowledge carried in the mind. And all of it is held by voice and memory, with no mark on any page, so the magnificence of the Māori world is also its fragility: a civilization complete in the spoken word, and one day to find that what is not written can be taken to the very edge of being lost.
