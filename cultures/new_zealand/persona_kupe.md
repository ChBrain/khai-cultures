# Persona: Kupe

## Taxonomy

[number 8 wire](position_number_8_wire.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The navigator of tradition who found Aotearoa, the first voyager across the open Pacific to the last great land left empty on earth, the origin of the migration from which the Māori world descends. In the accounts handed down he reads the ocean as a book - the stars, the long swell, the run of the currents, the flight and the homing of birds - and steers his waka by them across thousands of miles of water no chart ever showed, the first and purest ingenuity of a people who would learn to make do with what the world to hand provides. His tongue is an ancestral Polynesian language, the root from which te reo Māori would grow: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md), and [hears](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) the world wholly in it, for in his time and his ocean there is no other and no European tongue yet exists anywhere in his world. He has no writing; his charts are memory and his record is the spoken word, the oral genealogy carried in the mind and recited down the generations. And he names the land as he finds it - headland and harbour and the cloud that marks the shore - so that the first act of language in Aotearoa is the naming, the word laid on the empty coast to make it known - the first names of a land that [New Zealand English](position_language_en_nz.md) would one day carry at the surface of its speech, long after his tongue had grown into te reo Māori. The gap at the limit of his tongue is not a colonial one but a deeper one still: a whole world found and held and passed on entirely by voice and memory, with no mark on any page, so that all he knew survives only as far as the spoken word could carry it across the centuries. He belongs to [New Zealand culture](position_culture_new_zealand_culture.md) as the wayfinder whose crossing is the first and purest act of the ingenuity the country would come to call its own.

## Action

In [the Great Migration](plot_01_the_great_migration.md) he crosses the largest ocean on earth and makes landfall on the last large land left for humans to find, the wayfinding the first and purest [number 8 wire](position_number_8_wire.md), reading a path across open water with nothing but the read of the sky and the sea. By finding the land and naming it, he opens the world the Māori will build, and lays the first word on Aotearoa.

## Shadow

The voyager who carries a whole world in memory and the spoken word is the nation's own shadow at its root: the knowledge held only by voice, magnificent and whole while it is spoken, but always one broken generation from silence - the warning that what is not set down can be lost, as te reo itself would later come so near to being.

## Tell

He steers by stars no instrument records and names a coast no map has drawn, carrying a continent's worth of knowing in his head - and leaves a land that knows him only by the words his descendants kept saying.
