# Plot: the Treaty of Waitangi (1840)

## Taxonomy

[play_new_zealand](play_new_zealand.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

By 1840 the European ships have been coming for decades - whalers, traders, missionaries - and the contact is growing unruly and the land is changing hands. The British Crown moves to formalize its presence, and in the Bay of Islands a treaty is drawn up to be put before the Māori rangatira. On 6 February the chiefs gather at Waitangi to debate and to sign.

## Action

On 6 February 1840, the Crown and a great number of Māori rangatira sign the Treaty of Waitangi, te Tiriti o Waitangi, the founding compact of the nation: a partnership between the indigenous people and the newcomers, promising the Crown's protection and the Māori the continued possession of their lands and treasures. But the treaty is written twice, and the two texts do not agree. The English version has the chiefs cede sovereignty; the te reo Māori version, the one most signed, reserves to them tino rangatiratanga, their full chieftainship over their own affairs. From that divergence between the two texts the long dispute is seeded - a nation founded on an agreement whose own words disagree, and a partnership the country will spend its history trying to read true.

## Stage

- [te Tiriti o Waitangi](position_the_treaty.md): the founding compact itself, the partnership of two peoples written twice.
- [Waitangi](place_waitangi.md): the ground where the treaty is signed, the birthplace of the nation.
- [Sir Apirana Ngata](persona_ngata.md): the later leader who will hold the Crown to its own signature and make the partnership a living claim.

## Tension

The nation is born of a partnership rather than a conquest, the rare founding in which the newcomers bound themselves by treaty to the people already there - and born already in contradiction, the two texts saying two different things, so the compact that promises partnership is the same compact that will be breached. The promise and the gap arrive together on the same day, and the whole story after is the long work of reading the treaty true and honouring what was signed.
