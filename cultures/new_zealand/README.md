# Aotearoa New Zealand: a khai culture

Aotearoa New Zealand staged as a full khai play, ISO NZ, language en. The last
great land settled by humans, a partnership of two peoples bound by a founding
treaty, as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the
roles its languages and faith encode, its places, its practices, and its defining
objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_new_zealand.md](play_new_zealand.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md), the Hofstede layer, written from New
  Zealand's profile (strong individualism softened by an egalitarian fair-go
  temper, low power distance, modesty and understatement, dry self-deprecation,
  and a practical, make-do self-reliance) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Great Migration, the Treaty of Waitangi (1840), the
  Social Laboratory, and the Renaissance and the Nuclear-Free Nation - each
  casting the elements it needs, and each staging the partnership and its
  breaches honestly.
- **Personas:** Kupe, Sir Apirana Ngata, Kate Sheppard, and Sir Edmund Hillary as
  the historic cast, plus one defining archetype, the Kiwi (modest, practical,
  dry, egalitarian, backing the underdog). Each is wired to the language engine by
  the language-crossing it holds, Māori figures carrying te reo and English, Pākehā
  figures carrying English.
- **Positions:** te Tiriti o Waitangi (the founding compact and bicultural
  partnership of two peoples, written twice and breached), the social laboratory
  (egalitarianism, the fair go, suffrage first in the world and the welfare state),
  number 8 wire (practical ingenuity and "she'll be right" at the edge of the
  world).
- **Places:** Waitangi (the Treaty grounds and the birthplace of the nation) and
  Aoraki / Mount Cook (the highest peak, an ancestor of Ngāi Tahu and the emblem
  of the land and kaitiakitanga).
- **Processes:** the hangi (the earth-oven feast, Māori in origin and now shared)
  and the haka (the national game and the All Blacks' challenge, a bicultural
  ritual carried by the whole nation).
- **Pieces:** the silver fern (the ponga frond and the koru, the nation's shared
  face) and pounamu (greenstone, a taonga of the South Island carrying mana and
  ancestry).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-record historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources on the public record are credited, never
claimed; the Treaty and its breaches, the New Zealand Wars and the land
confiscations, the near-loss and the renaissance of te reo Māori, and
biculturalism are treated soberly and respectfully; the staging and the
architecture are original work.
