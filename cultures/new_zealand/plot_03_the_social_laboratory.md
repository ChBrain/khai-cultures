# Plot: the Social Laboratory

## Taxonomy

[play_new_zealand](play_new_zealand.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

By the close of the nineteenth century the young colony, far from the old class structures of Europe and proud of its level field, is ready to try what the old countries dare not. Reformers press their case, and a movement led by [Kate Sheppard](persona_sheppard.md) gathers a petition of tens of thousands of women asking only to be counted.

## Action

In 1893 New Zealand becomes the first self-governing nation in the world to grant women the vote. It does not stop there: it builds among the earliest old-age pensions, pioneers industrial arbitration, and in the following decades raises one of the broadest welfare states on earth, the promise that no citizen would be left to fall too far. A small country at the edge of the world makes itself the social laboratory of the world, trying first the decent experiments the larger nations would only later risk, and writing the egalitarian creed of the fair go into law and into the national character.

## Stage

- [Kate Sheppard](persona_sheppard.md): the suffrage leader who wins women the vote and makes the country first in the world.
- [the Kiwi](persona_the_kiwi.md): the ordinary citizen the fair society is built for, owed a level field and a floor beneath them.
- [the social laboratory](position_the_social_laboratory.md): the egalitarian creed made law, the conviction that a small country can lead by trying the decent thing first.

## Tension

The country proves it can lead the world in fairness, first to give women the vote and early to guarantee a floor beneath its people - a genuine pride and a genuine achievement. And yet the egalitarian creed it builds carries its own shadow from the start: the same levelling instinct that lifts everyone can turn to the tall poppy that pulls down the one who rises, and being first to one fairness can let a country recite the achievement as if the work were finished while other fairnesses, the Treaty's chief among them, still wait.
