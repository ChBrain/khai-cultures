# Play: Aotearoa New Zealand

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the house that holds this staging and answers for it.

## Name

Aotearoa New Zealand, an island nation of two long islands and many smaller ones in the South Pacific, far to the southeast of Australia, alone at the bottom of the world. ISO NZ, language en, with English, te reo Māori, and New Zealand Sign Language all standing as official tongues. Aotearoa, the land of the long white cloud, is the Māori name carried alongside the English one, and the doubled name is the nation in miniature: two peoples, one country, named twice and meant to be held as one.

## Arc

The last great land settled by humans, found not by accident but by skill. Around 1300 Polynesian voyagers crossed the open Pacific in waka, navigating by stars and swell, and made landfall on the last large habitable land left empty on earth; their descendants became Māori, and built a world of iwi and hapū, of whakapapa and mana and the deep tie to whenua, the land. Then the European ships came, and in 1840 the Crown and the Māori rangatira bound themselves to each other by the Treaty of Waitangi, te Tiriti, the founding compact of two peoples - a partnership written twice, in English and in te reo, and the gap between those two texts is the seam the whole story runs along. The promise was broken almost at once: the New Zealand Wars, the confiscation of land, and the long suppression of te reo Māori, which came near to being lost. And yet on that same ground the nation built itself into a social laboratory for the world, the first self-governing country to grant women the vote in 1893, the welfare state, the fair go and the egalitarian creed. In the later century came the reckoning and the return: the Māori renaissance and the revival of te reo, the Waitangi Tribunal and the Treaty settlements, and an independent, nuclear-free voice that set a small country's own course in the world. Under it all runs one nerve: a modest, dry, practical people, proud of being small and far, who back the underdog, distrust the show-off, fix things with number 8 wire, and carry the unfinished partnership of te Tiriti at the heart of who they are.

## Stakes

Whether the partnership of [te Tiriti o Waitangi](position_the_treaty.md) can be honoured and the two peoples held as one nation - the breaches answered, the settlements completed, the doubled name made true rather than merely spoken; whether the fair and egalitarian society of [the social laboratory](position_the_social_laboratory.md) endures against the pull of inequality; and whether a small and distant nation keeps its own independent voice and guards its land, the kaitiakitanga that holds the whenua in trust for those still to come, or whether the easygoing surface settles over the unfinished work and lets it lapse.
