# Plan: honouring the Treaty

## Taxonomy

The open mandate the nation set itself on the lawn at Waitangi: the long work of honouring [te Tiriti o Waitangi](position_the_treaty.md) and completing the Treaty settlements, of holding the two peoples as one nation in partnership rather than letting one absorb the other, of keeping the fair and egalitarian society of [the social laboratory](position_the_social_laboratory.md) against the pull of inequality, and of guarding the land and the independent voice, the kaitiakitanga and the self-reliance of [number 8 wire](position_number_8_wire.md), that a small distant nation needs to keep its own course.

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Direction

Honour what was signed - read te Tiriti true, answer the breaches, and carry the settlements through to a partnership real in fact and not only in name; hold the two peoples as one nation, neither dissolving the difference nor letting it divide, the doubled name made whole; keep the fair go real, holding the level field and the floor beneath the people when inequality pulls them apart; and guard the land and the voice, keeping the whenua in trust for those still to come and the small nation's independent, nuclear-free course its own.

## Orders

- To the citizen like [the Kiwi](persona_the_kiwi.md): keep the fair go and back the underdog, learn the partnership beyond the surface of the tongue, and do not let "she'll be right" leave the harder work undone.
- To the leader like [Sir Apirana Ngata](persona_ngata.md): carry the mana of the people into the nation's institutions, hold the Crown to its own signature, and keep te reo and the partnership alive.
- To the nation: honour te Tiriti and complete the settlements, hold the two peoples as one, and guard the land and the independent voice, never mistaking the easy surface for the work.

## Implementation

A young nation, founded on a treaty and proud of its fairness, that has come far - reviving te reo from the edge of loss, establishing the Waitangi Tribunal, carrying through settlement after settlement, declaring itself nuclear-free and finding its own voice - and that has far still to go: settlements unfinished, the partnership still being read true, te reo revived but not yet whole, inequality widening beneath the fair go, and the standing question of every Waitangi Day whether the two peoples can truly be held as one.

## Targets

- [x] Founded on te Tiriti o Waitangi, the partnership of two peoples written into the nation's beginning.
- [x] First in the world to grant women the vote, and built into a broad and early welfare state.
- [x] Te reo Māori revived from the edge of loss and made an official language; the Waitangi Tribunal established to hear the breaches.
- [x] Declared nuclear-free in 1987, setting the small nation's own independent course in the world.
- [W] The Treaty settlements continue, answering the historical wrongs but not yet complete.
- [W] Inequality widens beneath the fair go, and the tall poppy strains the egalitarian creed.
- [ ] Hold the two peoples truly as one nation, the doubled name made whole and the partnership lived in fact.
- [ ] Guard the land and the independent voice, keeping the whenua in trust through kaitiakitanga for those still to come.
