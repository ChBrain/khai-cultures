# Peru: a khai culture

Peru staged as a full khai play, ISO PE, language es. A nation of all the bloods,
heir to a great Andean civilization and still closing the gap between its ancient
grandeur and its modern reality, as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its
people as the cast, the roles its society and language encode, its places, its
practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## A nation in Spanish, rooted in Quechua

The play and the whole cast are written in Castilian Spanish (`es`), the common
tongue of the nation. Behind the Spanish runs the living Andean reality: Quechua
and Aymara are official languages, and several personas carry Quechua as their
mother tongue with Spanish carried or borrowed. The personas are wired to the
language engine by the language-crossing each one holds, and the deepest of those
crossings is the Quechua of the Andes thinning into Spanish across a generation.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_peru.md](play_peru.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_es.md](pitch_es.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Peru's
  profile (courteous, formal, hierarchical, hospitable, regional, present- and
  group-oriented) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Tawantinsuyu (the Inca empire and its grandeur), the
  Conquest of 1532 (Pizarro, Atahualpa at Cajamarca, the Viceroyalty and Lima),
  Rebellion and Independence (Tupac Amaru II in 1780, then San Martin and Bolivar
  in 1821), and modern Peru (the great Andean migration, all the bloods, the
  cultural and culinary resurgence) - each staging the gap between the promise and
  the reality honestly.
- **Personas:** Pachacutec, Tupac Amaru II, Jose Maria Arguedas, and Cesar
  Vallejo as the historic cast, plus one defining archetype, el cholo (the Andean
  migrant who remade Lima). Each is wired to the language engine by the
  language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** lo andino (the living Inca, Quechua, and Andean heritage), todas
  las sangres (all the bloods, the fusion and the strata of the nation), Lima y
  las provincias (the centralist coastal capital against the deep provinces).
- **Places:** el Cusco (the Inca navel of the world and the grandeur in stone),
  Lima (the colonial City of Kings and the centralist capital remade by migration).
- **Processes:** el Inti Raymi (the revived Inca festival of the sun at Cusco),
  el Senor de los Milagros (the great purple October procession in Lima).
- **Pieces:** el cebiche (the national dish and pride of a celebrated cuisine),
  el quipu (the Inca knotted-cord record, the Andean memory).

## Provenance
