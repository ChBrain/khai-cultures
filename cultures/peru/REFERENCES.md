## Languages

The play and the whole cast are written in Castilian Spanish (`es`), the nation's
common tongue. Behind the Spanish runs the living Andean reality: Quechua and
Aymara are official languages of Peru, and several personas carry Quechua as their
mother tongue with Spanish carried or borrowed. The cast is wired to the language
engine by the language-crossing each persona holds, the deepest being the Quechua
of the Andes thinning into Spanish within a generation.

from the public historical record. They are credited here and never claimed; the
staging is original work. The contradiction at the heart of the story - the
Conquest, the colonial casta, and the deep social and racial strata that still
separate the official Peru from the deep Peru - is staged soberly on the public
record, as the gap between the nation's promise and its reality.

The defining persona [el cholo](persona_el_cholo.md) is an archetype, not a
historical individual: a composite that carries the culture's living tension - the
Andean migrant who came down from the sierra, remade Lima from its hills, and
turned the whole country mestizo, paying the language of the Andes for a place on
the coast. The cultural framing - lo andino, todas las sangres, and the centralist
tension of Lima and the provinces - is original work built on the public record.
