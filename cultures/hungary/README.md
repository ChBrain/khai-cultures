# Hungary: a khai culture

Hungary staged as a full khai play, ISO HU, language hu. A people alone in Europe
as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its
kinless language encodes, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all
tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_hungary.md](play_hungary.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_hu.md](pitch_hu.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Hungary's
  profile (pride and drive, individualism, a strong need for certainty, deep
  pessimism and restraint - the "rejoicing through tears") and naming no
  dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Conquest (c. 895), 1848, Trianon (1920), and 1956 - each
  casting the elements it needs.
- **Personas:** István Széchenyi, Sándor Petőfi, and Imre Nagy as the historic
  cast, plus two defining archetypes, Erzsi (older, the melancholy monolingual of
  a kinless tongue) and Bence (younger, the Transylvanian Magyar whose passport is
  Romanian and whose homeland is a language). Each is wired to the language engine
  by the language-crossing it holds - every one turning on the solitude of a tongue
  related to none of its neighbours.
- **Positions:** a magyar nyelv (the Finno-Ugric language kin to no neighbour),
  Trianon (the 1920 dismemberment and the Magyars beyond the borders), a
  szabadságharc (the tradition of heroic, doomed freedom-fights).
- **Places:** Budapest (the Danube capital of 1848 and 1956), Erdély (Transylvania,
  the lost heartland beyond the border).
- **Processes:** a mulatság (the music-feast of "rejoicing through tears"), a fürdő
  (the thermal-bath culture).
- **Pieces:** a paprika (the red spice of the national kitchen), a Szent Korona (the
  Holy Crown, sacred symbol of statehood and a nation whole in spite of its lost
  lands).
