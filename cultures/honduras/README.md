# Honduras: a khai culture

Honduras staged as a full khai play, ISO HN, language es. A nation long shaped
from outside, reaching for its own sovereignty and dignity, as a theatre: its
history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its languages and
faith encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a
single pitch. The prose is written in Spanish; only the structural headings are in
English.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_honduras.md](play_honduras.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_es.md](pitch_es.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Honduras' profile (warm, humble, hardworking, familial and devout, resilient and
  self-deprecating, marked by a history of external control and the ache of
  migration) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** Copán and the Peoples, Independence and the Federal Dream,
  the Banana Republic, and Catracho Honduras - each casting the elements it needs,
  and each staging the gap between promise and reality soberly, taking no side.
- **Personas:** Lempira (the Lenca war chief who resisted the conquest), José
  Cecilio del Valle (the sage who drafted the Central American act of
  independence), Francisco Morazán (the liberal hero of the federation), and Ramón
  Amaya Amador (the novelist of the banana plantations) as the historic cast, plus
  one defining archetype, el bananero (the banana-plantation worker of the north
  coast). Each is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** la resistencia lenca (the indigenous resistance and Copán's Maya
  past), el sueño de la unión (the dream of a single Central American homeland),
  la república bananera (the external domination of the plantation era and the
  instability it left).
- **Places:** Copán (the Maya city of carved stelae and the hieroglyphic
  stairway), la Costa Norte (the Caribbean banana lowlands, the Sula valley, La
  Ceiba, and the Garifuna coast).
- **Processes:** la Semana Santa de Comayagua (the sawdust carpets and processions
  of Holy Week in the old colonial capital), la punta (the Garifuna drum-and-dance
  of the coast).
- **Pieces:** la baleada (the folded flour tortilla with beans, the national food
  that levels everyone at the daily table), la estela de Copán (the carved Maya
  monument, the art of the ancestors and the proof of a grandeur of its own).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the conquest, the external domination of the banana era, and the long
instability are treated soberly as the gap between promise and reality, taking no
side and detailing no atrocity; the staging and the architecture are original work.
