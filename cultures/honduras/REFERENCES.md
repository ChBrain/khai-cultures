# Honduras: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical and cultural
sources behind the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_es.md](pitch_es.md) is written from Honduras' profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record without
entering the prose.

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; figures for Honduras are partial in
the published data and are given here only as the orientation behind the pitch, not
as precise measures.

## Historical and cultural sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn
from the public historical and cultural record. They are credited here and never
claimed; the staging is original work. The hard passages of the story - the Spanish
conquest and the Lenca resistance, the external domination of the banana era, and
the long instability and migration - are staged soberly on the public record, as
the gap between promise and reality, taking no side and detailing no atrocity.

The defining archetype, [el bananero](persona_el_bananero.md), is not a historical
individual but a composite that carries the life of the banana-plantation worker of
the north coast. The cultural framing - the catracho self-understanding, the baleada
as national food, Holy Week in Comayagua, Garifuna punta, and the Copan stela as the
art of the ancestors - is drawn from the public cultural record and staged from
within Honduras' own self-understanding.
