# Georgia: a khai culture

Georgia staged as a full khai play, ISO GE, language ka. An ancient Christian
land of the Caucasus, staged as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its
people as the cast, the roles its faith and language encode, its places, its
practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_georgia.md](play_georgia.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_ka.md](pitch_ka.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Georgia's profile (close family and in-group bonds, warmth and emotional
  openness, deep respect for elders and tradition, and a strong sense of honour
  and hospitality) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Baptism (4th century), the Golden Age (Queen Tamar and
  Shota Rustaveli), the National Revival (the 19th century and Ilia
  Chavchavadze), and the Return (the restoration of the modern state) - each
  casting the elements it needs.
- **Personas:** Saint Nino, Queen Tamar, Shota Rustaveli, and Ilia Chavchavadze
  as the historic cast, plus one defining archetype, the Tamada (the toastmaster
  who leads the supra). Each is wired to the language engine by the
  language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** the Orthodox faith (one of the world's first Christian nations,
  faith as the national backbone), the supra and the guest (the feast and the
  conviction that the guest is a gift from God), the language and the alphabet
  (the unique Georgian script and tongue).
- **Places:** Mtskheta (the ancient spiritual capital and Svetitskhoveli
  cathedral), Tbilisi (the living capital on the Mtkvari, the crossroads city).
- **Processes:** the supra (the feast led by the tamada, with toasts and
  polyphonic song), qvevri winemaking (the 8,000-year heritage of wine aged in
  buried clay vessels).
- **Pieces:** the qvevri (the buried clay wine vessel, a recognised heritage
  treasure), the Knight in the Panther's Skin (Rustaveli's epic, the summit of
  the Georgian tongue).
