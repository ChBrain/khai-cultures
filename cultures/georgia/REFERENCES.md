# Georgia: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical and cultural
sources behind the plots, personas, places, processes, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_ka.md](pitch_ka.md) is written from Georgia's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record without
entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Georgia score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ----------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 65                      |
| Individualism                 | 25                      |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 55                      |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 75                      |
| Long Term Orientation         | 38                      |
| Indulgence                    | 32                      |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; where a country's published values
vary across sources, the figures here are indicative of the collectivist,
hierarchical, and tradition-respecting profile that tunes the pitch.

here and never claimed; the staging is original work. The staging keeps to the
enduring culture, faith, wine, language, mountains, and people, and notes the
turns of history soberly and without taking any side.

The one defining persona, the [Tamada](persona_tamada.md), is an archetype, not a
historical individual: a composite of the toastmaster who leads the Georgian supra,
carrying the position of the feast and the sacred guest. The qvevri winemaking and
the polyphonic singing of the supra are recognised parts of the world's
intangible cultural heritage.
