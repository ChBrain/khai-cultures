# Place: the Pitons

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Shown

The twin volcanic peaks rising straight up out the sea above the old town of Soufriere, Gros Piton and Petit Piton, two green spires of rock that are the face the island show the world and the image on its own flag. Around them the sulphur springs steam in the island's volcanic heart, the rainforest climb the slopes, and the Caribbean open up at their feet. The most beautiful sight on an island they call beautiful enough to fight over, the peaks the empires want and the visitors now come to see.

## Holds

## Offers

A place that gather the whole island into one image: the volcanic land, the sea, the beauty that name the island the Helen of the West Indies, and the pride a small nation put on its own flag. The peaks are the island showing itself off to the world, the ground of its contest and now of its welcome, the face by which a tiny nation is known.

## Withheld

The innocence of mere beauty. The Pitons carry the weight of what beauty cost a small island: the peaks that draw the empires' wars now draw the visitor's money, and the same sight that is the island's pride is also the island's product, sold to a world that could choose to come or stay home, just so, the loveliness that was fought over now leaned on.
