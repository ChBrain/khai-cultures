# Position: the Saint Lucian tongue

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The standing of English as the official tongue of the island, the language of the schoolroom and the great books, the legislature, the law, the constitution, and the Crown that once held the island in a long tug-of-war. It is the tongue the empire taught and the tongue the nation was carried into being in, the polished English of the academy where a son of Castries wrote the theory of how poor nations grow, and the English of verse the world's largest stage would crown. It is the proposition that the island holds this English whole and turns it to its own use, from the careful prose of the journals to the sweet music of the epic, even as the Kweyol of the yard and the harbour runs beneath it as the undersong of the place.

## Orders

That English is the language of the record, the state, and the school: the tongue the law is written in, the price and the paper signed in, the theory and the poem set down in. It orders the ground where the island must meet the world that reads and buys and grades, the constitution and the contract and the standard the distant market writes, and it holds the register a Saint Lucian is schooled and examined and governed in.

## Loses

Ground to the Kweyol it has never fully displaced, the Creole the fishermen and the growers love in and pray in and name their very things in, the tongue that carries the island's soul while English carries its business. It loses too at its own edge, where even the island's greatest writer reached always for the Kweyol word the imperial tongue could not quite hold, so that the epic of the island is written in an English forever stretching after a language it cannot contain.

## Drives

It drives the island to answer the world in a tongue it holds whole while its heart beats in another. It gives [Arthur Lewis](persona_arthur_lewis.md) the polished English his development theory was cited the world over in, and [Derek Walcott](persona_derek_walcott.md) the mother English he turned into the verse a Nobel would crown; it gives [Harold Simmons](persona_harold_simmons.md) the English of scholarship he set the saved folklore down in, and [John Compton](persona_john_compton.md) the English of the constitution he led the island to its own flag in, both of them carrying the Kweyol of the people alongside. And it is the borrowed English [the banana grower](persona_the_banana_grower.md) reaches for just enough to read the price and sign the paper, the tongue the crop is sold in though the fruit is grown in Kweyol. So the one tongue shows who holds it as their own and who meets it as a borrowed thing, and how the island's business and its soul run in two languages at once.
