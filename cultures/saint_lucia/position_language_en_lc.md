# Position: the Saint Lucian tongue

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

English gives its speaker an analytic grammar that carries meaning mostly through word order and small function words rather than through endings, a vocabulary built from two deep layers, a Germanic core and a vast Latinate layer laid over it by centuries of French and Latin borrowing, that lets one idea be said in a plain word or a learned one, and phrasal verbs that build precise meaning by pairing a simple verb with a preposition (take up, give in, look after) rather than by inventing a new verb each time.

## Orders

It forces a fixed subject-verb-object order to carry grammatical roles that an inflected language would mark on the word itself, and it forces every verb to lean on an auxiliary ("do," "does," "did") to ask a question or deny a statement, a support no bare verb can supply alone. Spoken alongside Kweyol, it forces the speaker to hold English's word order against the creole's different one, translating the shape of a sentence and not only its words.

## Loses

Its thin verb morphology, only "-s," "-ed," and "-ing" to carry person, tense, and aspect, cannot mark the fine shades of completed, ongoing, and habitual action that a creole verbal system carries with a string of separate particles set before the verb, so an English sentence can flatten a distinction the Kweyol beneath it still keeps whole. And its spelling keeps sounds the spoken language has long since dropped or shifted, so the written word and the spoken word answer to two different rules.

## Drives

It drives whoever holds it to build meaning from order and small function words rather than from endings, listening for "the" and "a" and word position to carry work another language would put on the noun itself. It trains an ear split between two grammars, English's fixed order and the word order of Kweyol beneath it, and it makes borrowing and calquing between the two a constant, unremarked habit of speech rather than a rare event.
