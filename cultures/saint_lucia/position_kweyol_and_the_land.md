# Position: Kweyol and the land

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The Creole-Catholic soul of the island and the soil that fed it: Kweyol, the French-based Creole the African majority forged on the plantations, the tongue of the yard, the market, and the heart, carried under an English that ruled the school and the court; the Roman Catholic faith brought by France and held by nearly all, with its saints' days, its processions, and the flower festivals of La Rose and La Marguerite; and the land itself, worked first in sugar and then in bananas, the green gold of the river valleys on which a whole people's living was balanced. It is the inheritance that was not legislated but lived, the part of the island no empire designed and the island itself long undervalued.

## Orders

That the true Saint Lucia speaks Kweyol whatever the law writes in English, lives by the saint's day and the flower society, and stands on the land that fed it; that the Creole tongue and faith are not a lower thing beneath the English and the law but the living soul of the place; and that the dignity of the island rests on the field and the worker as much as on the laureate and the book.

## Loses

Ground when Kweyol is taken for broken French and a mark of the backward, schooled out of the child and left for the old, so the soul of the island thins into the English of the office; and ground, too, when the land is read only as the green gold that pays, a single crop to lean a whole life on, until one closed market can take what generations built, and the worker who grows the island's food is valued less than the fruit.

## Drives

Lives in [Harold Simmons](persona_harold_simmons.md), the painter and folklorist who gathered the Kweyol heritage others were taught to despise and gave it back to the island as worth, and in [the banana grower](persona_the_banana_grower.md), who works the green-gold valleys and carries the Creole tongue of the land. It drives Saint Lucia to value its Creole heart and the soil and the people who feed it.
