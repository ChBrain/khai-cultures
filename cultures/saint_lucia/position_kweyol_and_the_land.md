# Position: Kweyol and the land

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The Creole-Catholic soul of the island and the soil that fed it: Kweyol, the French-based Creole the African majority forge on the plantations, the tongue of the yard, the market, and the heart, carried under an English that rule the school and the court; the Roman Catholic faith France bring and nearly everyone hold, with its saints' days, its processions, and the flower festivals of La Rose and La Marguerite, the two flower societies the whole island divide between; and the land itself, worked first in sugar and then in bananas, the green gold of the river valleys on which a whole people's living was balanced. It is the inheritance that was not legislated but lived, the part of the island no empire ever design and the island itself long held cheap.

## Orders

That the true Saint Lucia talk Kweyol whatever the law write down in English, live by the saint's day and the flower society, and stand on the land that fed it; that the Creole tongue and faith are not some low thing beneath the English and the law but the living soul of the place itself; and that the dignity of the island rest on the field and the worker as much as on the laureate and the book.

## Loses

Ground when Kweyol is taken for broken French and a mark of the backward, schooled out of the child and left for the old people only, so the soul of the island thin away into the English of the office; and ground, too, when the land is read only as the green gold that pay, one single crop to lean a whole life on, until one closed market can take what generations build, and the worker who grow the island's food is valued less than the fruit itself.

## Drives

Lives in the painter and folklorist who gather up the Kweyol heritage others were taught to throw away and give it back to the island as worth, and in the banana grower, who work the green-gold valleys and carry the Creole tongue of the land. It drive Saint Lucia to love its Creole heart and the soil and the people who feed it.
