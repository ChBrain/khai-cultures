# Persona: Harold Simmons

## Taxonomy

[Kweyol and the land](position_kweyol_and_the_land.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The painter and folklorist who gather up the island's Kweyol heritage in the very years the schools were teaching the children to be shamed of it, who put down the songs, the proverbs, the festivals, and the old ways of the people, and who raise a young Derek Walcott into the poet he would become. Generous, learned, devoted, and quiet but brave, he see worth in the Creole soul that the colonial mind wave off as backward, and spend himself saving it for an island that did not yet know what it had. English he have whole and use it for the record: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in his mother tongue, the language of his schooling and his scholarship, and he [writes](process_writing_drafted.md) it into the notes, the papers, and the records by which the folklore was saved, [the Saint Lucian tongue](position_language_en_lc.md) held whole and turned to the record. The Kweyol of the songs and the proverbs he [carries as his own](process_speaking_carried.md), going to the people in their own tongue to gather what they alone still hold, the living material his English would set down. He put down the soul of the island in the English of the empire that had taught its people to shame it, the tongue of scholarship and the school, while the Kweyol he was saving had as yet no settled spelling to be saved in, so that the gap at the limit of the tongue ran right through the very work of saving a tongue that had never once been written. He belongs to [The Land, The People, The Light](position_culture_the_land_the_people_the_light.md), gathering back the people's own soul that the school had taught them to throw away.

## Action

In [the Green-Gold Island](plot_02_the_green_gold_island.md) he gather up the Kweyol folklore the colonial school threw away and give it back to the island as worth, the keeper of [Kweyol and the land](position_kweyol_and_the_land.md). He save the Creole heritage from being schooled clean out of existence, and light in the young Walcott the love of the island that would one day become its epic.

## Shadow

The keeper who save the heritage can become the last one who hold it: the folklore saved up in a scholar's records instead of alive in the children's mouths, the soul of the island honored like a collection in a cabinet while the living tongue it come from thin away, saved as a record because it was lost as a life.

## Tell

He treat a fisherman's proverb and a planter's hymn like treasures worth a scholar's whole care, going to the people in Kweyol to gather what the school had taught them to throw away, and he give a boy named Walcott the eye that would make the island epic.
