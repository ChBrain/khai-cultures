# Position: two Nobels from a small island

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The wonder sitting right at the island's heart, the thing every Saint Lucian will tell you before long: that from a people that never reach two hundred thousand come two Nobel laureates, the highest count of Nobel prizes per head of any sovereign nation on God's earth. Sir Arthur Lewis take the prize in Economics for showing how poor nations grow, and Derek Walcott take it in Literature for a poetry that turn the small island and its sea into epic. Two laureates from one speck of land, in two fields as far apart as the science of wealth and the art of words, both of them born in the same small capital within a few years of one another. It is the proof that achievement is not the property of big and rich places only, and the standard the island hold itself to ever after.

## Orders

That smallness is no excuse and no destiny, since the smallest of places has reach the highest of marks; that learning is the island's truest export and its surest pride, the schoolroom and the book worth more than the size of the land; and that a people may measure itself not against its neighbors' bigness but against the height its own two laureates prove it could reach.

## Loses

Ground when the two Nobels are kept like a trophy instead of a charge, recited to flatter the island instead of to drive it, so the proof of what learning can do just sit in a frame while the schools that might make the next laureate go wanting; and ground, too, when the gift is read as a freak of luck instead of the fruit of a tradition of letters and learning the island could choose to feed.

## Drives

Lives in [Derek Walcott](persona_derek_walcott.md), who turn the island and its sea into epic and bring home the prize for Literature, and in [Sir Arthur Lewis](persona_arthur_lewis.md), who carry the science of how poor nations grow to the prize for Economics. It drive Saint Lucia to hold itself to the height its own laureates reach, and to keep faith with the learning that make them.
