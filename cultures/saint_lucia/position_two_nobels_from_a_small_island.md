# Position: two Nobels from a small island

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The astonishing fact at the island's heart: that from a population that never reached two hundred thousand came two Nobel laureates, the highest count of Nobel prizes per head of any sovereign nation on earth. Sir Arthur Lewis took the prize in Economics for showing how poor nations grow, and Derek Walcott took it in Literature for a poetry that made the small island and its sea into epic. Two laureates from one speck of land, in two fields as far apart as the science of wealth and the art of words, both born in the same small capital within a few years of each other. It is the proof that achievement is not the property of large and rich places, and the standard the island holds itself to ever after.

## Orders

That smallness is no excuse and no destiny, since the smallest of places has reached the highest of marks; that learning is the island's truest export and its surest pride, the schoolroom and the book worth more than the size of the land; and that a people may measure itself not against its neighbors' bigness but against the height its own two laureates proved it could reach.

## Loses

Ground when the two Nobels are kept as a trophy rather than a charge, recited to flatter the island instead of to drive it, so the proof of what learning can do sits in a frame while the schools that might make the next laureate go wanting; and ground, too, when the gift is read as a freak of luck rather than the fruit of a tradition of letters and learning the island could choose to feed.

## Drives

Lives in [Derek Walcott](persona_derek_walcott.md), who made the island and its sea into epic and brought home the prize for Literature, and in [Sir Arthur Lewis](persona_arthur_lewis.md), who carried the science of how poor nations grow to the prize for Economics. It drives Saint Lucia to hold itself to the height its own laureates reached, and to keep faith with the learning that made them.
