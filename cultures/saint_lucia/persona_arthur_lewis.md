# Persona: Sir Arthur Lewis

## Taxonomy

[two Nobels from a small island](position_two_nobels_from_a_small_island.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The economist born in Castries who carried the question of how poor nations grow to the Nobel Prize in Economics, the first Black laureate in a field outside peace and literature, a scholar of development whose work shaped how the world thought about the rise of small and poor economies. Precise, principled, tireless, and quietly determined, he left a tiny island to study at the heart of the empire and made himself one of the great economists of his century, never forgetting the poor places his theory was for. English he has whole and bends to argument: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in his mother tongue, the language of his schooling and his science, and he [writes](process_writing_drafted.md) it into the careful prose of the theory that would be cited around the world. The literature of economics in its other tongues he [deciphers on the page](process_reading_deciphered.md) where his work required it, reaching past English for the data and the doctrine of a wider field. He carried the science of how poor nations grow in the polished English of the country that had ruled his own, the tongue of the academy where the theory of development was written, while the Kweyol of the island his work was meant to lift had no word in the journals at all, so that the gap at the limit of the tongue ran between the language of the prize and the language of the people it was for.

## Action

In [the Island of Two Nobels](plot_03_the_island_of_two_nobels.md) he wins the prize in Economics and proves that the science of wealth could be advanced by a son of the poorest of places, the first of [two Nobels from a small island](position_two_nobels_from_a_small_island.md). He gives the small island its first claim on the world's highest learning, and lends his own life as the argument that smallness is no limit on the mind.

## Shadow

The laureate of how poor nations grow leaves a homeland still leaning on a single crop: the theory honored abroad and the lesson half-heard at home, the proof that learning can lift a place hung on the wall while the place stays bound to the green gold his own science warned against.

## Tell

He spends his life on the question of how the poorest places might rise, and answers it as a man from one of them - never letting the height of the prize loosen his hold on the smallness it came from.
