# Persona: Sir Arthur Lewis

## Taxonomy

[two Nobels from a small island](position_two_nobels_from_a_small_island.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The economist born in Castries who carry the question of how poor nations grow all the way to the Nobel Prize in Economics, the first Black laureate in a field outside peace and literature, a scholar of development whose work shape how the whole world think about the rise of small and poor economies. Precise, principled, tireless, and quiet but determined, he leave a tiny island to study at the heart of the empire and make himself one of the great economists of his century, and never once forget the poor places his theory was for. English he have whole and bend to argument: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in his mother tongue, the language of his schooling and his science, and he [writes](process_writing_drafted.md) it into the careful prose of the theory that would be cited the world over, [the Saint Lucian tongue](position_language_en_lc.md) held whole and bent to argument. The literature of economics in its other tongues he [deciphers on the page](process_reading_deciphered.md) where his work require it, reaching past English for the data and the doctrine of a wider field. He carry the science of how poor nations grow in the polished English of the country that had ruled his own, the tongue of the academy where the theory of development was written, while the Kweyol of the island his work was meant to lift had no word in the journals at all, so that the gap at the limit of the tongue ran between the language of the prize and the language of the people it was for.

## Action

In [the Island of Two Nobels](plot_03_the_island_of_two_nobels.md) he take the prize in Economics and prove that the science of wealth could be carried forward by a son of the poorest of places, the first of [two Nobels from a small island](position_two_nobels_from_a_small_island.md). He give the small island its first claim on the world's highest learning, and lend his own life as the argument that smallness is no limit on the mind.

## Shadow

The laureate of how poor nations grow leave a homeland still leaning on one single crop: the theory honored abroad and the lesson half-heard at home, the proof that learning can lift a place hung up on the wall while the place stay bound to the green gold his own science warn against.

## Tell

He spend his whole life on the question of how the poorest places might rise, and answer it as a man from one of them, never once letting the height of the prize loosen his hold on the smallness it come from.
