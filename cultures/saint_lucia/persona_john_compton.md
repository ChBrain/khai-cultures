# Persona: Sir John Compton

## Taxonomy

[the Helen of the West Indies](position_the_helen_of_the_west_indies.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The father of the nation and its first Prime Minister, who led the long-contested island out of empire to its own flag in 1979 and shaped its modern politics across decades, the man who took the Helen of the West Indies out of the tug-of-war at last. Shrewd, patient, durable, and devoted to the small farmer and the green gold, he built his career on the banana and his standing on having steadied a young nation into being. English he has whole and uses for the work of state: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in his mother tongue, the language of the legislature, the law, and the negotiation that won independence, and he [drafts](process_writing_drafted.md) it into the speeches and the policy that carried the island to its own name. The Kweyol of the farmer and the market he [carries as his own](process_speaking_carried.md), turning to the people's tongue on the platform and at the roadside to reach the grower whose vote and whose crop he built his power on. He led the island to its own flag in the English of the empire that had held it, the tongue of the constitution and the Crown, while the Kweyol he campaigned in had no place in the documents of the state he founded, so that the gap at the limit of the tongue ran between the language of the nation's law and the language of its people.

## Action

In [the Island of Two Nobels](plot_03_the_island_of_two_nobels.md) he leads Saint Lucia along the road to independence in 1979, taking [the Helen of the West Indies](position_the_helen_of_the_west_indies.md) out of the long contest and into its own statehood. He turns the island that was always someone's prize into a nation that answers for itself, and gives the small island its own flag to gather around.

## Shadow

The father of the nation who built it on the banana can bind it to the one crop he rose on: the green gold made the ground of power as well as living, so the dependence the island most needed to outgrow was the very thing its founding leader had every reason to keep.

## Tell

He speaks the law of the new nation in English and works the roadside in Kweyol, courting in the people's tongue the grower he would govern in the empire's - and stakes the young country's living, as he staked his own career, on the green gold of the valleys.
