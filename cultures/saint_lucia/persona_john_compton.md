# Persona: Sir John Compton

## Taxonomy

[the Helen of the West Indies](position_the_helen_of_the_west_indies.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The father of the nation and its first Prime Minister, who lead the long-contested island out of empire to its own flag in 1979 and shape its modern politics across the decades, the man who take the Helen of the West Indies out of the tug-of-war at last. Shrewd, patient, hard-wearing, and devoted to the small farmer and the green gold, he build his career on the banana and his standing on having steadied a young nation into being. English he have whole and use it for the work of state: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in his mother tongue, the language of the legislature, the law, and the negotiation that won independence, and he [drafts](process_writing_drafted.md) it into the speeches and the policy that carried the island to its own name, [the Saint Lucian tongue](position_language_en_lc.md) held whole and turned to the work of state. The Kweyol of the farmer and the market he [carries as his own](process_speaking_carried.md), turning to the people's own tongue on the platform and at the roadside to reach the grower whose vote and whose crop he build his power on. He lead the island to its own flag in the English of the empire that had held it, the tongue of the constitution and the Crown, while the Kweyol he campaign in had no place at all in the documents of the state he found, so that the gap at the limit of the tongue ran between the language of the nation's law and the language of its people.

## Action

In [the Island of Two Nobels](plot_03_the_island_of_two_nobels.md) he lead Saint Lucia along the road to independence in 1979, taking [the Helen of the West Indies](position_the_helen_of_the_west_indies.md) out of the long contest and into its own statehood. He turn the island that was always somebody's prize into a nation that answer for itself, and give the small island its own flag to gather around.

## Shadow

The father of the nation who build it on the banana can bind it to the one crop he rise on: the green gold made the ground of power as well as the ground of living, so the dependence the island most needed to outgrow was the very thing its founding leader had every reason to keep.

## Tell

He speak the law of the new nation in English and work the roadside in Kweyol, courting in the people's tongue the grower he would govern in the empire's, and he stake the young country's living, just as he stake his own career, on the green gold of the valleys.
