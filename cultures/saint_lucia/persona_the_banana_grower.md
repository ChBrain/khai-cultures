# Persona: the banana grower

## Taxonomy

[Kweyol and the land](position_kweyol_and_the_land.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A small grower of the green-gold valleys, working a few acres of banana on the slopes above a river, who cuts and washes and boxes the fruit for the boat that carries the island's living to the world. Hardy, devout, plainspoken, and bound to the land, the saint's day kept and the field worked, the grower is the island's living balanced on a single crop, the one whose harvest is the nation's wage. Kweyol is the grower's whole inheritance and first tongue: the grower [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md), and [hears](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) in the mother tongue of the yard and the field, the Creole of the proverb, the prayer, and the market, the only tongue the work of the land has ever needed. The English of the school, the form, and the buyer the grower [carries as a borrowed tongue](process_speaking_borrowed.md), enough to read the price and sign the paper, [following the buyer's words](process_hearing_followed.md) when the market sets the terms. The grower lives in the Kweyol of the land and meets the world that buys the crop in a borrowed English, the tongue of the contract and the standard the distant market writes, so that the very price of the green gold is set in words not the grower's own, the gap at the limit of the tongue running between the language the fruit is grown in and the language it is sold in.

## Action

In [the Green-Gold Island](plot_02_the_green_gold_island.md) the grower works the banana that is the island's living, the human face of [Kweyol and the land](position_kweyol_and_the_land.md), the Creole worker on whom the single crop and the whole economy rest. The grower carries the island's dependence in one body, feeding the nation from a few acres while the price is fixed an ocean away.

## Shadow

The living balanced on the one crop can fall with it: the grower bound to the green gold has no second field to turn to when the market closes or the preference ends, so a whole life and a whole island's wage hang on a fruit whose price is set by buyers who owe the grower nothing.

## Tell

The grower cuts and boxes the banana with a saint's-day prayer in Kweyol and signs the buyer's form in a borrowed English - and watches a price set across an ocean decide whether a year of the land's work will feed the family or not.
