# Position: the Helen of the West Indies

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The island as the contested prize, fought over until it change hands fourteen times between France and Britain, more times than any other in all the Caribbean, and named the Helen of the West Indies for the war they wage over her beauty. From the Kalinago who held the island first, through the French who plant and name it and bring the faith and the Creole, to the British who take it last and give it the English and the law, the island was less a possession than a battlefield, its harbors and its heights worth dying for. The contest leave behind no single inheritance but a doubled one, and a people who learn to be the ground of other people's wars before they could ever be their own.

## Orders

That the island is shaped by the tug-of-war, English and French both pressed into it, Catholic faith and British law laid one over the other, so the Saint Lucian is heir to two empires and the property of neither one; that what was fought over so long must be worth the holding; and that a people made on contested ground learn to take what each comer leave behind and make of it something all their own.

## Loses

Ground when the contest is remembered only as a wound, the long humiliation of being the prize and never the player, so the island read its past as a loss instead of an inheritance; and ground, too, when the doubled heritage is taken for confusion instead of wealth, the French faith and the English law and the Kweyol tongue set to quarrel in one small place instead of being held together as the one thing no single empire could ever have given.

## Drives

Lives in [Sir John Compton](persona_john_compton.md), who take the long-contested island out of the contest at last and lead it to its own flag and its own name, the father of the nation that the Helen of the West Indies finally become. It drive Saint Lucia to carry the long tug-of-war as heritage and not as wound alone, and to make one island out of all the hands that ever held it.
