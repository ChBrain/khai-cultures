# Process: the Gros Islet Jump-Up

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

The fishing village of Gros Islet on a Friday night, when the street closes to traffic and opens to the whole island: the speakers stacked at the corners, the grills smoking with fish and chicken, the rum and the beer flowing, and the locals and the visitors alike pouring into the road to dance until late. It takes only a street, a sound system, and the coming of the weekend, and the village turns into the island's open dance floor.

## Direction

Toward release and welcome: the weekly street party where the island lets go together and lets the world join in. It is the gentle, devout island at play, the work of the week set down for the music and the company, the soca and the dancehall and the old calypso in the warm night, the village street made a meeting place where the grower and the tourist dance to the same beat and a stranger is a friend not yet met.

## Lever

It is driven by a people's love of company and song and the long conversation, and by a fishing village's gift for turning a Friday night into the island's heartbeat. It holds against the week's labor and the worry the sweetness covers, the long balance on the single crop and the season; against it stands the risk that the welcome becomes only a show for the visitor, the village's own night sold to the wider world.

## Echo

The Gros Islet Jump-Up makes visible what [the Saint Lucian tone](pitch_en.md) carries: a people gentle and warm and fond of company, who greet the world with music and let a stranger into the dance. To this street [the banana grower](persona_the_banana_grower.md) comes when the week's cutting is done, the work of the green gold set down for a night in the village's open road.
