# Process: the Gros Islet Jump-Up

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

The fishing village of Gros Islet on a Friday night, when the street close to traffic and open to the whole island: the speakers stack up at the corners, the grills smoking with fish and chicken, the rum and the beer running, and the locals and the visitors alike pouring out into the road to dance till late. Sa ka fet, a man call to his friend through the crowd, and the whole street take it up. It take only a street, a sound system, and the coming of the weekend, and the village turn into the island's own open dance floor.

## Direction

Toward release and welcome: the weekly street party where the island let go together and let the world come and join in. It is the gentle, devout island at play, limin sweet in the warm night, the work of the week set down for the music and the company, the soca and the dancehall and the old calypso, the village street turned into a meeting place where the grower and the tourist dance to the same beat and a stranger is just a friend you have not met yet.

## Lever

It is driven by a people's love of company and song and the long limin conversation, and by a fishing village's gift for turning a Friday night into the island's heartbeat. It hold against the week's labor and the worry the sweetness cover, the long balance on the single crop and the season; against it stand the risk that the welcome become only a show for the visitor, the village's own night sold off to the wider world.

## Echo

The Gros Islet Jump-Up make visible what [the Saint Lucian tone](pitch_en.md) carry: a people gentle and warm and fond of company, who greet the world with music and let a stranger into the dance. To this street [the banana grower](persona_the_banana_grower.md) come when the week's cutting is done, the work of the green gold set down for one night in the village's open road.
