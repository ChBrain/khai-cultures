## Place

On the Sunday table, the festival plate, and the everyday pot: the national dish of Saint Lucia, green fig and saltfish, boiled green banana with salted cod sauteed in onion, garlic, sweet pepper, and the island's herbs. The "fig" is the green banana of the green-gold valleys, the "saltfish" the salt cod that came with the trade, the two married on every Saint Lucian stove, eaten at Jounen Kweyol and at home, the dish the island knows itself by.

## Load Bearing

It stands for the island's whole inheritance laid on one plate. The green fig is the banana the island's living was balanced on, the green gold of the valleys served as the people's own food; the saltfish is the salt cod of the old Atlantic trade, the colonial larder turned into the national taste. Together they are the Creole table itself, African hands and French seasoning and the island's own crop made one dish, the food the colonial school once ranked beneath the imported and that Jounen Kweyol holds up as worth. Without this plate the island would have no table to gather its soul around, the green gold and the Creole kitchen made one thing the whole nation eats.

## Apparent

A plate of boiled green banana, firm and pale, with flaked salt cod cooked down in onion, garlic, tomato, sweet pepper, and thyme, a little oil and a squeeze of lime. Plain and humble on the plate, a poor people's food made of what the land and the trade gave; its weight is in the whole island it carries, the green-gold crop and the Creole kitchen served as one.
