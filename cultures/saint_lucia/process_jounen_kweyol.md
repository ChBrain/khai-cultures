# Process: Jounen Kweyol

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

The whole island on the last Sunday of October and the days around it: villages dressing in the madras, the cooks firing the bouyon and the green fig and saltfish, the old people teaching the proverbs, and everyone, from the schoolchild to the minister, speaking Kweyol on purpose for a day. It takes only a village square, a pot, and the will to wear the Creole soul out loud, and the whole island keeps Creole Day together.

## Direction

Toward pride and reclamation: the day the island holds up the Kweyol tongue, the Creole food, and the madras dress as worth, the very things the colonial school once taught the children to be ashamed of. It is not only a festival but an act of repair, the nation choosing the soul it was schooled to despise, speaking in the market's tongue and dressing in the field's cloth and eating the people's food, and saying with one voice that this is who we are.

## Lever

It is driven by a small island's need to value its own and to keep the Creole heart from thinning into English alone, and by the long memory of a tongue handed down and held against shame. It holds against the pull of the wider world and the slow loss of Kweyol from the young; against it stands the risk that the soul becomes a costume, lived for a day and laid aside, the reclamation reduced to a show.

## Echo

Jounen Kweyol makes audible what [Kweyol and the land](position_kweyol_and_the_land.md) holds: that the Creole tongue and the Creole table are the island's living soul and worth keeping out loud. On this day the heritage [Harold Simmons](persona_harold_simmons.md) gathered when the school despised it is sung and worn and eaten by the whole island, the worth he saw at last seen by all.
