# Process: Jounen Kweyol

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

The whole island on the last Sunday of October and the days around it: the villages dressing up in the madras, the cooks firing the bouyon and the green fig and saltfish, the old people teaching out the proverbs, and everybody, from the schoolchild to the minister, talking Kweyol on purpose for the day. Sa ka fet, they call out one to another, and the answer come back in Kweyol too. It take only a village square, a pot, and the mind to wear the Creole soul out loud, and the whole island keep Creole Day together.

## Direction

Toward pride and reclamation: the day the island hold up the Kweyol tongue, the Creole food, and the madras dress as worth, the very things the colonial school once taught the children to shame. It is not only a festival but an act of repair, the nation choosing the soul it was schooled to throw away, talking in the market's tongue and dressing in the field's cloth and eating the people's food, and saying with one voice, sa se nou, this is who we are.

## Lever

It is driven by a small island's need to love its own and to keep the Creole heart from thinning away into English alone, and by the long memory of a tongue handed down and held against shame. It hold against the pull of the wider world and the slow loss of Kweyol from the young; against it stand the risk that the soul become a costume, lived for one day and laid aside, the reclamation cut down to a show.

## Echo

Jounen Kweyol make audible what [Kweyol and the land](position_kweyol_and_the_land.md) hold: that the Creole tongue and the Creole table are the island's living soul and worth keeping out loud. On this day the heritage [Harold Simmons](persona_harold_simmons.md) gather up when the school threw it away is sung and worn and eaten by the whole island, the worth he see at last seen by all.
