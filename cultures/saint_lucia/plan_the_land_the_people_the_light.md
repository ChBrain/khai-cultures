# Plan: The Land, The People, The Light

## Taxonomy

The charge the island set itself in its own national motto, the land, the people, the light: to build on the Nobel legacy of learning that [two Nobels from a small island](position_two_nobels_from_a_small_island.md) proved was within its reach, to value the [Kweyol and the land](position_kweyol_and_the_land.md) soul it was once schooled to be ashamed of, to find a future for the land beyond the single crop of the green gold, and to carry the long contest of [the Helen of the West Indies](position_the_helen_of_the_west_indies.md) as heritage rather than wound, so that the smallest of islands keeps the wide future its own laureates showed it could have.

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Direction

Feed the light - make the learning that gave the island two Nobels a living tradition and not a trophy, putting the means into the schools that might make the next laureate; honor the people - hold the Kweyol tongue, the Creole table, and the faith as worth, not shame, and value the worker who feeds the island as much as the fruit; renew the land - diversify the living beyond the single crop, so a closed market cannot take a whole nation's wage; and carry the contest as heritage, making the French faith, the English law, and the Kweyol soul one island's wealth rather than its old wound.

## Orders

- To the worker like [the banana grower](persona_the_banana_grower.md): grow the green gold and demand a living that does not hang on one fruit alone, and carry the Kweyol soul of the land as the island's worth.
- To the leader like [Sir John Compton](persona_john_compton.md): take the island past the single crop it was founded leaning on, and turn the freedom won into a future built.
- To the nation: build on the Nobel legacy of learning, value the Creole soul, and find the wide future a small island has every right to.

## Implementation

A young nation independent since 1979, the smallest place on earth to give the world two Nobel laureates, that has reclaimed the Kweyol soul its colonial school once shamed - Creole Day kept, the dish and the dress held up as worth - and that has begun to turn from the single crop, the banana's preference gone and tourism risen beneath the Pitons, while the work goes on still: to feed the schools that made the laureates, to widen a living that still leans hard on the crop and the cruise ship, and to keep the Creole tongue alive in the young against the pull of the wider world.
