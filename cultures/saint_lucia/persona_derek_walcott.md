# Persona: Derek Walcott

## Taxonomy

[two Nobels from a small island](position_two_nobels_from_a_small_island.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The poet of Castries who made his small island into epic and won the Nobel Prize in Literature, author of "Omeros," who set the fishermen of his harbor beside the heroes of Homer and proved that a speck in the Caribbean was world enough for the grandest poetry. Lyrical, exacting, divided, and devoted to the place that made him, he took the doubled inheritance of the island - the English of the empire and the Kweyol of the yard, the saltfish and the sea - and forged from it a tongue all his own. English he has whole and turns into music: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in his mother tongue, the language of the schoolroom and the great books, and he [writes](process_writing_mother_tongue.md) it into verse that the world's largest stage would crown. The Kweyol of the harbor and the market he [catches always on the ear](process_reading_caught.md) and [hears as the undersong of the place](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md), the tongue of the fishermen he made his Achilles and his Hector, threaded through his English without ever ruling it. He wrote his island into Homer's measure in the conqueror's English while the Kweyol that named its things had no Homer and barely a spelling, so that even as he gave the small island its epic he was always reaching for the words the imperial tongue could not quite hold, the gap at the limit of the tongue between the language he wrote in and the one his people loved in.

## Action

In [the Pitons and the Future](plot_04_the_pitons_and_the_future.md) he wins the second Nobel and makes the island epic, lifting Saint Lucia into world literature and showing [two Nobels from a small island](position_two_nobels_from_a_small_island.md) to be no accident but a height the place could reach. He turns the small island's sea and people into myth, and gives a small nation a face the whole world would learn to read.

## Shadow

The laureate who makes the island epic can become the island's excuse to stop: the prize quoted and the square renamed while the schools that might make the next poet go wanting, the achievement honored as a relic rather than fed as a tradition, the comfort of having produced a Walcott mistaken for the work of producing another.

## Tell

He sets a Castries fisherman beside Homer's heroes and means it wholly, finding in a small island's harbor all the world a poet ever needed - and writes its epic in the empire's English, reaching always for the Kweyol word the line could not hold.
