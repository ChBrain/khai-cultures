# Persona: Derek Walcott

## Taxonomy

[two Nobels from a small island](position_two_nobels_from_a_small_island.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The poet of Castries who turn his small island into epic and take the Nobel Prize in Literature, author of "Omeros," who set the fishermen of his own harbor beside the heroes of Homer and proved that a speck in the Caribbean was world enough for the grandest poetry there is. Sweet-tongued, exacting, divided down the middle, and devoted to the place that make him, he take the doubled inheritance of the island, the English of the empire and the Kweyol of the yard, the green fig and the saltfish and the sea, and forge from it a tongue all his own. English he have whole and turn into music: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in his mother tongue, the language of the schoolroom and the great books, and he [writes](process_writing_mother_tongue.md) it into verse the world's largest stage would crown, [the Saint Lucian tongue](position_language_en_lc.md) turned into music in his hand. The Kweyol of the harbor and the market he [catches always on the ear](process_reading_caught.md) and [hears as the undersong of the place](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md), the tongue of the fishermen he make his Achilles and his Hector, threaded through his English without ever ruling it. He write his island into Homer's measure in the conqueror's English while the Kweyol that name its very things had no Homer and barely a spelling, so that even as he give the small island its epic he was always reaching for the word the imperial tongue could not quite hold, the gap at the limit of the tongue between the language he write in and the one his people love in. He belongs to [The Land, The People, The Light](position_culture_the_land_the_people_the_light.md), turning its land, its people, and its Kweyol soul into a myth the whole world would learn to read.

## Action

In [the Pitons and the Future](plot_04_the_pitons_and_the_future.md) he take the second Nobel and make the island epic, lifting Saint Lucia into world literature and showing [two Nobels from a small island](position_two_nobels_from_a_small_island.md) to be no accident at all but a height the place could reach. He turn the small island's sea and people into myth, and give a small nation a face the whole world would learn to read.

## Shadow

The laureate who make the island epic can turn into the island's excuse to stop: the prize quoted and the square renamed while the schools that might make the next poet go wanting, the achievement honored like a relic instead of fed like a tradition, the comfort of having produced one Walcott mistaken for the work of producing another.

## Tell

He set a Castries fisherman beside Homer's heroes and mean it with his whole heart, finding in one small island's harbor all the world a poet ever need, and he write its epic in the empire's English, reaching always for the Kweyol word the line could not hold.
