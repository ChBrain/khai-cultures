# Plot: the Green-Gold Island

## Taxonomy

[play_saint_lucia](play_saint_lucia.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

With the wars settled at last, the island turn to the land. The plantations want a crop and a people to work it, and on the soil of the river valleys a Creole society take shape, African in its majority and its tongue, Catholic in its faith, balanced first on the cane and then on a fruit they will come to call the green gold.

## Action

The island build itself on the land. Sugar come first, worked by the enslaved Africans whose freedom was long withheld and whose descendants would make the island's living after; and when the sugar fade the banana rise up in its place, the green gold of the valleys, one single crop carrying a whole people's wage to the boats and the world. On that land a Creole-Catholic society grow, Kweyol in the yard and the market, the saint's day kept and the flower societies of La Rose and La Marguerite singing one against the other, the soul of the island lived out in a tongue the colonial school taught the children to throw away. Against that shame [Harold Simmons](persona_harold_simmons.md) gather up the folklore, the songs and proverbs and festivals, and give the Kweyol heritage back to the island as worth, while [the banana grower](persona_the_banana_grower.md) work the fruit on which it all lean.

## Stage

- [Harold Simmons](persona_harold_simmons.md): the folklorist who save the Kweyol heritage the school threw away and give it back as worth.
- [the banana grower](persona_the_banana_grower.md): the Creole worker of the green-gold valleys on whom the island's living rest.
- [Kweyol and the land](position_kweyol_and_the_land.md): the Creole-Catholic soul and the soil of sugar and banana that fed it.

## Tension

The island find its living and its soul on the same land and is bound by the both of them. The green gold pay a whole people's wage and balance it on one single fruit whose price is set an ocean away, and the Kweyol soul that is the island's truest self is the very thing its schools teach it to shame, so the work of the green-gold years is to find a living that does not hang on one crop and a pride that does not throw away its own tongue.
