# Position: The Land, The People, The Light

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The national motto and the standing it names: the belonging to a small volcanic island fought over fourteen times between France and Britain, a French-British-Kweyol soul that no single flag ever quite settled. It carries [the Helen of the West Indies](position_the_helen_of_the_west_indies.md), the standing of the most contested ground in the Caribbean, heir to two empires and the property of neither; it carries [Kweyol and the land](position_kweyol_and_the_land.md), the Creole-Catholic soul the African majority forged on the plantations and the green gold of the banana valleys that fed a whole economy; and it carries [two Nobels from a small island](position_two_nobels_from_a_small_island.md), the wonder of a population under two hundred thousand giving the world a laureate in Economics and a laureate in Literature both. It speaks [the Saint Lucian tongue](position_language_en_lc.md), the English of the law and the school held alongside Kweyol, the tongue of the yard and the market. It has, without ever choosing it, the Pitons rising over Soufriere as the face it shows the world, and a gentle, devout, sweet-mouthed manner that knows itself small and has proved itself not.

## Orders

That the true Saint Lucia talk Kweyol whatever the law writes down in English, and stand on the land and the worker who feed it as much as on the laureate and the book; that smallness be no excuse and no destiny, since the smallest of places has reached the highest of marks, and the schoolroom be honoured as the island's truest export; and that the long contest of empires be carried as heritage owned rather than a wound denied, the French faith, the English law, and the Kweyol soul held together as one island's wealth and not set to quarrel.

## Loses

Ground when Kweyol is taken for broken French and schooled out of the child, so the soul of the island thins away into the English of the office alone; ground when the two Nobels are kept like a trophy recited to flatter the island instead of a charge that feeds the next generation of schools; and ground when the single crop, first sugar and then the green gold of the banana, is left to carry a whole nation's wage, so a closed market an ocean away can take what generations built. It loses ground, too, when the long tug-of-war is remembered only as humiliation, a people who were the ground of other people's wars before they could be their own.

## Drives

Lives in the folklorist who gathered the Kweyol heritage others were taught to throw away and gave it back to the island as worth, and in the grower who works the green-gold valleys carrying the Creole tongue of the land. It drives whoever is raised in it to hold the height its own two laureates proved was within reach as a standard rather than a relic, to love the Kweyol heart it was once schooled to shame, and to make one island out of every hand that ever held it, English and French and Kweyol together, rather than let the doubled inheritance be read as loss.
