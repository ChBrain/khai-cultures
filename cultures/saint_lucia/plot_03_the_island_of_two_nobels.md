# Plot: the Island of Two Nobels

## Taxonomy

[play_saint_lucia](play_saint_lucia.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

In the small capital of Castries, in the years between the wars and after, a generation of children go to school on a tiny island the wider world has barely even heard of. From that same town and that same schooling will come a son who carry its learning to the highest prize on earth, and a leader who carry the island itself to its own flag.

## Action

The small island reach the world's highest marks. [Sir Arthur Lewis](persona_arthur_lewis.md), born in Castries, carry the question of how poor nations grow all the way to the Nobel Prize in Economics, the first of two laureates a population under two hundred thousand will give the world. And on the political road, [Sir John Compton](persona_john_compton.md), building his power on the small farmer and the green gold, lead the long-contested island step by step out of empire to independence in 1979, taking the Helen of the West Indies out of the tug-of-war at last and giving it its own name and its own flag. The island that was always somebody's prize become a nation that answer for itself, and the island that was thought too small for anything at all reach the highest learning there is.

## Stage

- [Sir Arthur Lewis](persona_arthur_lewis.md): the economist who carry the science of how poor nations grow to the Nobel Prize.
- [Sir John Compton](persona_john_compton.md): the father of the nation who lead the island to independence in 1979.
- [Castries](place_castries.md): the small capital that school the laureate and seat the new nation's government.

## Tension

The small island prove the same time that it can govern itself and that it can reach the world's highest learning, and is judged by whether it will build on the one or the other. The first Nobel show that smallness is no limit on the mind, and independence show it is no limit on self-rule, yet the new nation is founded still leaning on the single crop its own laureate's science warn against, so the work after is to turn the proof of what learning and freedom can do into a living and a future, and not just a pride.
