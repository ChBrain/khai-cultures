# Plot: the Island of Two Nobels

## Taxonomy

[play_saint_lucia](play_saint_lucia.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

In the small capital of Castries, in the years between the wars and after, a generation of children goes to school on a tiny island that the wider world has barely heard of. From that same town and that same schooling will come a son who carries its learning to the highest prize on earth, and a leader who carries the island itself to its own flag.

## Action

The small island reaches the world's highest marks. [Sir Arthur Lewis](persona_arthur_lewis.md), born in Castries, carries the question of how poor nations grow to the Nobel Prize in Economics, the first of two laureates a population under two hundred thousand will give the world. And on the political road, [Sir John Compton](persona_john_compton.md), building his power on the small farmer and the green gold, leads the long-contested island step by step out of empire to independence in 1979, taking the Helen of the West Indies out of the tug-of-war at last and giving it its own name and its own flag. The island that was always someone's prize becomes a nation that answers for itself, and the island that was thought too small for anything reaches the highest learning there is.

## Stage

- [Sir Arthur Lewis](persona_arthur_lewis.md): the economist who carries the science of how poor nations grow to the Nobel Prize.
- [Sir John Compton](persona_john_compton.md): the father of the nation who leads the island to independence in 1979.
- [Castries](place_castries.md): the small capital that schooled the laureate and seated the new nation's government.

## Tension

The small island proves at once that it can govern itself and that it can reach the world's highest learning, and is judged by whether it will build on either. The first Nobel shows that smallness is no limit on the mind, and independence shows it is no limit on self-rule, yet the new nation is founded still leaning on the single crop its own laureate's science warned against, so the work after is to make the proof of what learning and freedom can do into a living and a future, and not only a pride.
