# Paraguay: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind the
plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_es.md](pitch_es.md) is written from Paraguay's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record without
entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Paraguay score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------ |
| Power Distance                | 70                       |
| Individualism                 | 12                       |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 40                       |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 85                       |
| Long Term Orientation         | 20                       |
| Indulgence                    | 56                       |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; Paraguay's Individualism score is
among the lowest in the published data, marking a deeply collective, familial
culture.

## Historical sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn
from the public historical record. They are credited here and never claimed; the
staging is original work. The hardest passages of the national story - Dr. Francia's
founding dictatorship, the War of the Triple Alliance (1864-1870), and the Chaco War

- are staged soberly on the public record, as the nation's great wound and its
  resilience, without atrocity detail or blame.

The defining persona [la residenta](persona_la_residenta.md) is an archetype, not a
historical individual: a composite that carries the figure of the Paraguayan woman
who survived the War of the Triple Alliance and reconstituted the nation, sustaining
the land, the family, and the Guarani mother tongue she passed to the next
generation.
