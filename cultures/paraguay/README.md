# Paraguay: a khai culture

Paraguay staged as a full khai play, ISO PY, language es. A landlocked, bilingual
nation forged in isolation and near-annihilation, as a theatre: its history as the
plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its two languages encode, its places,
its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch. The prose is
written in Spanish, the language of the nation, with its Guarani soul woven through
the jopara of the page; only the headings are in English.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_paraguay.md](play_paraguay.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_es.md](pitch_es.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Paraguay's profile (warm, courteous, soft-spoken, deeply familial and collective,
  reserved and patient, bilingual to the core) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Guarani Reductions, the Isolated Homeland (independence
  and Dr. Francia), the Great War (the War of the Triple Alliance, 1864-1870), and
  the Paraguay That Was Reborn - each casting the elements it needs, and each
  staging the nation's founding dictatorship and its great wound soberly on the
  public record, without atrocity detail or blame.
- **Personas:** Roque Gonzalez de Santa Cruz, Jose Gaspar Rodriguez de Francia,
  Francisco Solano Lopez, and Augusto Roa Bastos as the historic cast, plus one
  defining archetype, la residenta (the Paraguayan woman who survived the war and
  reconstituted the nation). Each is wired to the language engine by the
  Spanish/Guarani crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** la lengua guarani (the bilingual Guarani soul, the jopara, the one
  Latin American nation whose indigenous tongue the majority speaks), las
  reducciones (the singular Jesuit-Guarani civilization and the deep mestizaje), and
  la patria aislada y herida (the isolated, wounded, resilient nation).
- **Places:** Asuncion (the river capital, "madre de ciudades," seat of the
  republic), las Misiones (the ruins of the Jesuit-Guarani reductions, Trinidad and
  Jesus).
- **Processes:** el terere (the shared cold yerba mate, the constant Paraguayan
  ritual), San Juan (the June festival of fire games and Guarani-Catholic
  tradition).
- **Pieces:** el nanduti (the radial "spiderweb" lace that weaves the two
  heritages), el arpa paraguaya (the diatonic harp, voice of the nation's music).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; Dr. Francia's founding dictatorship, the War of the Triple Alliance, and
the Chaco War are treated soberly on the public record, as the nation's great wound
and its resilience, without atrocity detail or blame; the staging and the
architecture are original work.
