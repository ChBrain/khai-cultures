# Position: New Jersey English

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The Mid-Atlantic English of the Garden State, split between the fast, hard-edged metropolitan speech of the northern turnpike counties that lean toward the New York City ear and the softer South Jersey talk of the Pine Barrens and the shore that leans toward Philadelphia. It holds a dense, direct plainness that both the diner counter and the pine forest carry.

## Orders

Where it passes as native ground: the Newark diner, the North Jersey highway, the Pine Barrens fire road, the shore boardwalk. It orders which vowels and turns of phrase mark a speaker as Jersey and sets the northern metropolitan ear against the southern pine-and-shore one.

## Loses

Ground to General American, the flat national broadcast standard the screen and the corporate board carry, which trims the metropolitan edge off the young and stamps the Jersey accent as a punchline. The commercial highway register also crowds the older Pine Barrens speech toward the margin.

## Drives

It carries [Tony](persona_tony.md), whose rapid metropolitan cadence is the working English of the Newark diner and the turnpike corridor, and [Gabriella](persona_gabriella.md), whose articulate Mid-Atlantic cadence holds the quieter English of the southern Pine Barrens. So the one Jersey speech shows who turns toward the industrial corridor and who keeps the forest ground.
