# Palau: a khai culture

Palau staged as a full khai play, ISO PW, language pau (the Palauan tongue,
the tekoi er a Belau). A Micronesian nation of matrilineal clans and the bai,
of bead money and the rock islands, as a theatre: its history as the plot line,
its people as the cast, the roles its clans and its conservation ethic encode,
its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single
pitch. The prose body is written in Palauan; only the structural headings are in
English.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_palau.md](play_palau.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_pau.md](pitch_pau.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Palau's profile (clan-rooted and collective, matrilineal in authority,
  consensus-seeking, proud, and conservation-minded) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the clans and the bai (ancient Palau and the matrilineal
  order), the four flags (Spain, Germany, Japan, and the United States, and the
  WWII battle of Peleliu staged soberly), the nuclear-free republic (the world's
  first nuclear-free constitution, the Compact of Free Association, and Remeliik),
  and the reef and the pledge (modern conservation, the Palau Pledge, the bul, and
  Remengesau) - each casting the elements it needs.
- **Personas:** Haruo Remeliik (the first president and the nuclear-free
  constitution) and Tommy Remengesau Jr. (the conservation president of the Palau
  Pledge) as the historic cast, plus three defining archetypes: a rubak (the
  paramount chief and the council of chiefs), a ourrot (the senior clan mother and
  the matrilineal authority), and a meselang (the storyboard carver and keeper of
  legends). Each is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds,
  Palauan the mother tongue with English or Japanese carried where apt.
- **Positions:** the clans and the bai (the matrilineal clans, women's authority,
  and the men's meeting house), the rock islands and the reef (the conservation
  ethic), the nuclear-free republic (sovereignty and free association).
- **Places:** Koror (the main town, the bai, and the rock islands offshore) and
  Babeldaob (the great island, Melekeok the capital, and the ancient terraces and
  monoliths).
- **Processes:** the ngasech (the first-birth ceremony honoring a first-time
  mother) and the bul (the chiefs' conservation moratorium on a reef or species).
- **Pieces:** the udoud (the traditional bead and bracelet money) and the itabori
  (the carved storyboard of legends).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public historical record behind the
plots, personas, places, and pieces are recorded in
[REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the pitch is its
invisible hand. Sources on the public record are credited, never claimed; the four
colonial administrations and the WWII battle of Peleliu are treated soberly, with
no atrocity detail and no side taken. The staging and the architecture are
original work.
