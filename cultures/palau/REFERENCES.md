# Palau: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public historical record behind the plots,
personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_pau.md](pitch_pau.md) is written from Palau's profile on the
model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number. Palau is
not separately published in Geert Hofstede's country tables; the profile is read
from the well-documented traits of Palauan society - matrilineal clan authority,
collective and consensus-seeking decision-making through the council of chiefs, a
strong respect for rank and elders, and a conservation ethic rooted in the bul.
The orientation is recorded here so the reading is on the record without entering
the prose.

| Dimension                     | Palau reading (qualitative) |
| ----------------------------- | --------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | high (rank, chiefs, elders) |
| Individualism                 | low (clan and collective)   |
| Motivation toward Achievement | moderate                    |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | moderate                    |
| Long Term Orientation         | high (the bul, the reef)    |
| Indulgence                    | moderate                    |

Source: read qualitatively from the public ethnographic and documentary record of
Palauan society, in the absence of separately published Hofstede scores for Palau.
The reading is approximate and serves only to tune the pitch.

The three defining personas, [a rubak](persona_a_rubak.md),
[a ourrot](persona_a_ourrot.md), and [a meselang](persona_a_meselang.md), are

her authority) and the keeping of the legends (the storyboard carver).
