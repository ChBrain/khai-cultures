# México: a khai culture

Mexico staged as a full khai play, ISO MX, language es. A nation born of the
violent meeting of two worlds and made into a third, as a theatre: its history as
the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its language and faith encode,
its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_mexico.md](play_mexico.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_es.md](pitch_es.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Mexico's
  profile (deep collectivism and family, high power distance and respect for
  hierarchy, a strong avoidance of uncertainty, warmth and ceremony, and an
  indulgent, festive register) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Conquest (1519-1521), the Virgin and the Colony,
  Independence (1810), and the Revolution (1910) - each casting the elements it
  needs, and each staging the gap between the promise and the reality honestly.
- **Personas:** La Malinche, Miguel Hidalgo, Emiliano Zapata, and Frida Kahlo as
  the historic cast, plus one defining archetype, el mestizo (the child of two
  worlds, the cosmic race, neither fully indigenous nor Spanish). Each is wired to
  the language engine by the language-crossing it holds, with La Malinche, la
  lengua, the tongue itself, at the center of the staging of language.
- **Positions:** mestizaje (the fusion of the indigenous and the Spanish into a
  new people, and the defining gap between the celebration of the mixture and the
  conditions beneath it), tierra y libertad (the agrarian-democratic claim from
  the Grito to the Revolution), and the shadow of the north (mexicanidad asserted
  beside the United States).
- **Places:** Mexico City (the capital built atop Tenochtitlan, the conquest
  written into the pavement) and Teotihuacan (the vast pre-Aztec pyramid city, the
  deep indigenous past in stone).
- **Processes:** the Day of the Dead (the dead welcomed home, the Mexican intimacy
  with death) and the fiesta (the communal release where, per Paz, the masked
  people pour out).
- **Pieces:** the Virgin of Guadalupe (the brown-skinned, syncretic mother of the
  nation) and maize (the sacred staple, "people of corn," the unbroken thread to
  the deep indigenous past).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the Conquest, the colonial caste system, and modern violence are treated
soberly on the public record, as the gap between promise and reality, never
luridly; the staging and the architecture are original work.
