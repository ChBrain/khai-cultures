# Plan: Each Endeavouring, All Achieving

## Taxonomy

The open mandate the nation set itself in its own motto: that when each one endeavours, all achieve - the charge to share the wealth the beaches earn beyond the lucky few of [a small place, a wide reach](position_a_small_place_a_wide_reach.md), to honour the labour and the emancipation that made the country in [the labour nation](position_the_labour_nation.md), to keep the colonial past of [sugar and the dockyard](position_sugar_and_the_dockyard.md) plain and not smoothed away, and to turn the small nation's wide voice back toward the smallest of we own.

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Direction

Share the wealth the visitors bring, so the resorts do not run right past the yards that staff them and the freedom from want the labour movement promised is kept in fact; rebuild and keep faith with Barbuda, the sister isle's common land and frigate-bird sanctuary made whole again after the storm and not left to rebuild alone; honour the labour and the emancipation that made the nation - in the wages, the schools, and the share, not only the holiday and the song; and carry the small place's wide voice in the world, in cricket and letters and song, while turning that reach back across the channel and into the island's own homes.

## Orders

- To the islander like [the Barbudan](persona_the_barbudan.md): hold the common land and the sanctuary, rebuild the sister isle, and hold the nation to reaching back across the water to we.
- To the heirs of [Vere Bird](persona_vere_bird.md) and the line of [Vivian Richards](persona_vivian_richards.md): honour the labour that won the country and carry its name into the world, and turn that power and that fame right back toward the people who made them.
- To the nation: share the wealth of the beaches, rebuild Barbuda whole, and keep the past plain, never once mistaking the welcome it sells the world for the freedom from want it owes its own.

## Implementation

A young nation, free since 1981, that has turned a sugar colony into a tourism economy and carried a likkle island's name far into the world - and that still must close the distance between the wealth its beaches earn and the yards that earn it, must finish the long rebuilding of storm-struck Barbuda and settle the standing of its common land, and must keep its colonial past staged plain rather than sold smooth, so that the freedom of 1834 and 1981 reaches all the way across the channel and into every single home.
