# Persona: the Barbudan

## Taxonomy

[a small place, a wide reach](position_a_small_place_a_wide_reach.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

An islander of Barbuda, the lower and farther sister isle, who holds land not by private deed but in common with all Barbudan people, who knows every frigate bird in the sanctuary on Codrington Lagoon and every lobster on the reef, and who rode out and rebuilt after Hurricane Irma emptied the whole island in 2017 - the keeper of a way of holding land and place that the wider nation does not share. Rooted, communal, proud, and keeping one eye always on the capital across the water, this is the islander who carries the smallest and the farthest of we own. English is the mother tongue, in the broad Barbudan measure: this islander [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [hears](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) it whole, and the Creole of the sister isle, close kin to Antigua's and yet the island's own, is [carried as the home tongue](process_speaking_carried.md), the speech of the common land and the standpipe. The official English of the documents and the deeds the capital sends across the water this islander [reads carefully and follows](process_reading_followed.md), watching the words that would turn common land to private title. The Barbudan speaks the nation's English and yet holds a way of belonging the nation's law has no name for - common land, held by all, owned by none - so the gap at the limit of the tongue is the distance between the title-deed English of the capital and a way of holding place that has no word in it at all, a people whose belonging the official language just cannot quite say.

## Action

In [the Beaches and Barbuda](plot_04_the_beaches_and_barbuda.md) this islander keeps the sister isle's common land and its frigate-bird sanctuary right through the modern tourism nation and right through the storm, the proof of [a small place, a wide reach](position_a_small_place_a_wide_reach.md) turned back toward home. By holding the common land and rebuilding after Irma, the Barbudan keeps the smallest and farthest of the nation in the count, and asks the wide reach to reach back across the channel to we.

## Shadow

The islander who holds the common land can be left to hold it all alone - the sister isle remembered by the capital only after the storm pass through, the common way of belonging pressed hard by the developer and the deed, the wide-reaching nation spending itself on its beaches and its famous sons while the farthest of its own rebuild with no help at all. The shadow of the small place is the smaller place left and forgotten.

## Tell

This islander holds the land in common with every other Barbudan and counts the frigate birds on Codrington Lagoon as family - and keeps watching the capital across the water to see whether the nation that reaches all the way to the wide world will trouble itself to reach the sister isle.
