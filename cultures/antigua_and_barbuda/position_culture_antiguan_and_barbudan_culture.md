# Position: Antiguan and Barbudan culture

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

Belonging to a two-island nation that came up out of sugar and bondage and made itself free, and that knows exactly what that freedom cost. It carries [sugar and the dockyard](position_sugar_and_the_dockyard.md) as the wound at its root and the 1736 rising as its true first date; it carries [the labour nation](position_the_labour_nation.md), the union that turned emancipated subjects into citizens; and it carries [a small place, a wide reach](position_a_small_place_a_wide_reach.md), the pride of a likkle country heard the whole world over. Its tongue is [Antiguan English](position_language_en_ag.md), sweet with the Creole talked in every yard. It has the warmth of a small island where everybody know everybody, the standpipe and the corner lime, and a hospitality that stretches the pot for one more mouth without ever being asked twice.

## Orders

That a stranger is greeted like family until him prove otherwise, and that the door and the pot stay open; that the elder and the ancestor who refused bondage are honoured in more than a holiday and a song, in the wages and the schools and the share of the wealth actually given; that cricket pride runs through everything, a son who can bat or bowl the highest thing the island can offer the wide world; and that the smaller and the farther, Barbuda across the channel and its common land, is carried with all the rest and not left to hold itself alone.

## Loses

Ground when the beach-paradise sold to the visitor becomes the whole story, the island made a backdrop and its own people made the help, so that the warmth given to the world is not returned in the wealth the world pays for it; ground when the memory of the cane and the chains gets smoothed away behind the postcard, and the resistance that the nation dates itself from is forgotten for the heritage tour alone; and ground at the channel between Antigua and Barbuda, the sister isle's common land and its storm-struck rebuilding easy to forget from the capital, so the wide reach that touches the whole world does not always reach all the way home.

## Drives

Drives its people to lime and laugh exactly because they remember what it cost to be free enough to do so, an ease that is hard-won and never taken for granted. It sends the small island out into the world, on the cricket field, on the page, in the song of Carnival, to make the wide world hear a place it had barely noticed; and it turns that same reach back toward home, asking that the sister isle and the working many who organised for the country also share in what it earns. It measures a free people by whether their freedom, from the chains and from want both, is kept in fact and not only in name.
