# Piece: fungie and pepperpot

## Taxonomy

Parent group: pieces

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Place

On the Sunday table and at the family gathering, in the home pot and the local kitchen: fungie, a smooth cornmeal-and-okra dumpling worked stiff and turned by hand, served beside pepperpot, the long-simmered stew of salt meat, greens, and ground provisions, with saltfish to the side and a piece of sweet ducana too if the cook feeling generous - the national dish of Antigua and Barbuda, the food the island claims as its very own.

## Load Bearing

It carries the truth of [the labour nation](position_the_labour_nation.md): that the nation's own dish was made by working people out of what the cane island gave and what the freed people grew. Cornmeal and okra, salt meat and the greens and roots of the provision ground, simmered long and stretched easy to feed a whole family - it is the food of those who had to make plenty out of little, the table of the freed and their children, the everyday dignity of the working home turned into the dish a whole nation calls its own. Without this pot the island's claim to a cooking of its own, made by its own people out of its own ground, would have no centre to it.

## Apparent

A bowl of stiff yellow cornmeal beside a dark stew of meat and greens. In itself it is a humble plate, the food of the home kitchen and not the resort menu at all; its weight is entirely in what the island has poured into it, the labour and the making-do and the family table of a people who fed themselves free.
