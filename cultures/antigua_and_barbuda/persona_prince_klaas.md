# Persona: Prince Klaas

## Taxonomy

[sugar and the dockyard](position_sugar_and_the_dockyard.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The enslaved man they called King Court, an African of standing held on an Antiguan sugar estate here on Wadadli, who in 1736 stood at the head of a planned island-wide rising to throw off slavery and make the enslaved free, and who is honoured today as a national hero - the first to give the islands the refusal we would build a nation on. Proud, grave, and a leader of his people even in bondage, he carried a kingship the cane could not take from him. His mother tongue was Akan, the language of the Asante homeland from which he was carried: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md), and [hears](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) in the tongue of his ancestors, and it was in that tongue and its kin, among the many African languages of the enslaved, that the rising was planned quiet-quiet, beyond the planters' ears. The English of his captors, [Antiguan English](position_language_en_ag.md), he [carries only as a borrowed tongue](process_speaking_borrowed.md), the speech of the estate and the overseer, never freely his and never chosen. He led his people toward freedom in the languages they had carried across the sea, while the masters' English pressed in around them as the tongue of the whip and the ledger - so the gap at the limit of the tongue was the line of his resistance itself, the free word kept in the old tongue the masters could not follow at all, at all. He belongs to [Antiguan and Barbudan culture](position_culture_antiguan_and_barbudan_culture.md) as the refusal it dates its whole story from.

## Action

In [Sugar and the Dockyard](plot_01_sugar_and_the_dockyard.md) he stands at the head of the planned 1736 rising, the proof that the enslaved never gave their consent to the bondage the colony was built on. By saying no, and by leading others to say no, he gives the nation its first hero and its founding act - the resistance, not the cane, that Antigua and Barbuda chooses to date its whole story from.

## Shadow

The hero made into a name and a statue can be honoured right into safety - the rising remembered as one brave man's deed and not as the whole people's refusal, the resistance carved in bronze and so hushed, the comfort of the monument mistaken for the keeping of what he stood up for. To honour him true-true is to honour the freedom he reached for, and not only the courage of the reaching.

## Tell

He carried a king's bearing on a slave estate and planned a whole island's freedom in a tongue the masters could not hear one word of - and is fi him name, not theirs, that the free nation coming after carries to this day.
