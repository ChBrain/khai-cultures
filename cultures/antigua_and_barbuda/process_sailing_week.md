# Process: Antigua Sailing Week

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

The yachts and their crews from all across the world, the race committees and the shore crews, the island that hosts them: the great regatta sailed each spring out of English Harbour and Nelson's Dockyard, the boats running the courses round the coast by day and the harbour filling up with sail and celebration by night. It needs the trade winds, the deep old harbour, and the fleet that comes for one of the Caribbean's premier regattas.

## Direction

Toward the island's wide reach turned out to the sea. Sailing Week carries Antigua's name onto the world's water, the old naval harbour become the start line of a great international regatta, the small island drawing the world's sailors right to its coast. It is the colonial dockyard turned to the free nation's own purpose, the deep harbour that once careened warships now hosting the world at play, and the island's warm welcome and its steady winds shown to all who come.

## Lever

It is driven by the island's matchless harbour and steady trade winds and by the wish to draw the wide world to the small place's shore. It holds against the season and the competition of every other Caribbean port by the dockyard's heritage and the island's own warm hospitality; against it stand the pull of the regatta toward the visitor and the yacht set, and the question of how far its wealth really reaches past the harbour and into the island's own yards.

## Echo

Sailing Week makes audible what drives [a small place, a wide reach](position_a_small_place_a_wide_reach.md): that a likkle island can draw the whole world to its coast and hold its own among the great. Out of the same Wadadli pride that [Vivian Richards](persona_vivian_richards.md) carried into the world by the bat, the island carries its name onto the water, the small place reaching far - and asked, here as everywhere, to turn the welcome and the wealth back home again.
