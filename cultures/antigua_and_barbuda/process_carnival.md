# Process: Carnival

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

The whole island, every summer: the mas troupes and the steel bands, the calypso and soca singers competing for the crown, the costume makers and the revellers, the families lining the road for J'ouvert before dawn and the bands that play till they can play no more. It needs only the season to come round and the island to take to the streets in feathers and sequins and sound.

## Direction

Toward the dancing memory of freedom. Antigua's Carnival is rooted in the celebration of the 1834 emancipation, the festival that carries the day the chains were struck off forward into joy - the freed people's claim to the streets and to delight, renewed each year. It is not only spectacle but remembrance turned to celebration, the island dancing the fact of its freedom and stretching the welcome of it to the visitor who comes for the show.

## Lever

It is driven by the deep need to mark and keep the memory of emancipation, and by the island's own love of band, mas, and the open street. It holds against forgetting, against the smoothing of the past into a tourist spectacle, by anchoring the joy in the day it remembers; against it stand the pull of the show toward the visitor's eye and the slow loosening of the festival from the freedom it began in.

## Echo

Carnival makes audible what drives [the labour nation](position_the_labour_nation.md): that the freedom won out of bondage is the island's to keep and to celebrate, the streets claimed by the freed and their children as their own. In its band and its mas the whole nation dances the day [Vere Bird](persona_vere_bird.md) and the movement carried forward - the freedom from the chains and the freedom from want both - and keeps the count of what the dancing is for.
