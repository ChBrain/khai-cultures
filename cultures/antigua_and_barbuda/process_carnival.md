# Process: Carnival

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

The whole island, every summer: the mas troupes and the steel bands, the calypso and benna and soca singers fighting for the crown, the costume makers and the revellers, the Moko Jumbie stilt-dancers stepping tall above the crowd like jumbie come to play, the families lining the road for J'ouvert before day clean and the bands that play till they just can't play no more. It needs only the season to come round and the whole island to tek to the streets in feathers and sequins and pure sound.

## Direction

Toward the dancing memory of freedom. The Antigua Carnival is rooted in the celebration of the 1834 emancipation, the festival that carries the day the chains were struck off forward into pure joy - the freed people's claim to the streets and to delight, renewed every single year. It is not only spectacle but remembrance turned right into celebration, the island dancing the fact of its own freedom and stretching the welcome of it wide to the visitor who comes for the show.

## Lever

It is driven by the deep need to mark and keep the memory of emancipation, and by the island's own love of the band, the mas, and the open street. It holds against forgetting, against the smoothing of the past into a tourist spectacle, by anchoring the joy hard in the day it remembers; against it stand the pull of the show toward the visitor's eye and the slow loosening of the festival from the freedom it began in.

## Echo

Carnival makes audible what drives [the labour nation](position_the_labour_nation.md): that the freedom won out of bondage is the island's own to keep and to celebrate, the streets claimed by the freed and their children as their very own. In its band and its mas the whole nation dances the day [Vere Bird](persona_vere_bird.md) and the movement carried forward - the freedom from the chains and the freedom from want both - and never lose the count of what all the dancing is really for.
