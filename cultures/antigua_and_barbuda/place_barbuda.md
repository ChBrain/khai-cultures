# Place: Barbuda

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Shown

The lower, flatter sister isle to the north of Antigua, a long reach of pink-white sand and lagoon, far quieter and far less peopled than the big sister across the water - home of the great frigate-bird sanctuary on Codrington Lagoon, where thousands upon thousands of the birds nest in the mangroves, and of a way of life held on land owned in common by all Barbudan people rather than by any private deed. Its one town, Codrington, the sweet lobster on its reefs, and the wide empty beaches that drew the few who came; the sister isle that holds the nation's oldest way of belonging to a place.

## Holds

## Offers

A place where the nation keeps a way of holding land that the wider country does not share - common land, owned by all and none, and a sanctuary held in trust for the frigate birds; a quieter, communal life across the channel, and a test of whether the wide-reaching nation will trouble itself to reach back to the smallest and farthest of we own and rebuild the sister isle whole.

## Withheld

The ease of being remembered. Barbuda carries the gap between the nation's wide reach and its own neglect - the sister isle that can feel clean forgotten by the capital across the water, the common land pressed hard by the developer and the deed, the storm that emptied it and the long rebuilding still not finish. It withholds the comfort of the paradise image, holding the nation to whether its freedom and its wealth reach all the way across the channel to we.
