# Persona: Vivian Richards

## Taxonomy

[a small place, a wide reach](position_a_small_place_a_wide_reach.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

Sir Isaac Vivian Alexander Richards, the Master Blaster, Antigua's own pride and the master batsman of the great West Indies side, among the most feared and most loved players the game of cricket has ever known, who walked to the crease with no helmet, chewing him gum easy as you please, and carried a small island's name onto every ground on earth - a national hero, and the man who showed the world just what a small place could send out. Proud, fearless, and dead sure of himself, he batted as a free man before a watching world. English, [Antiguan English](position_language_en_ag.md), is his mother tongue, in the Antiguan measure: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in it whole, and the broad Antiguan Creole of the yard and the pitch he [carries as his own](process_speaking_carried.md), the talk he came up in on Wadadli and never left behind. He could [follow](process_reading_followed.md) all the press of the wide world that crowded round him and answer it back in his own plain words. He carried the island's name in the masters' old English across the very grounds of the empire that had ruled it, and made that English say, in his bat far more than his mouth, that the small place was nobody's lesser - so the gap at the limit of the tongue was crossed not in words at all but in the language of the game, where a man from a likkle island spoke to the whole world and was understood. He belongs to [Antiguan and Barbudan culture](position_culture_antiguan_and_barbudan_culture.md) as the proof that a small place is nobody's lesser.

## Action

In [Independence and the Field](plot_03_independence_and_the_field.md) he carries the newly free nation's name onto the world's cricket grounds and masters every one of them, the proof of [a small place, a wide reach](position_a_small_place_a_wide_reach.md). He turns the field into the island's own stage and bats its pride straight into the world's notice, a likkle country made large by sheer mastery.

## Shadow

The hero who carries the small place to the wide world can come to be the only thing the world sees of it - the island shrunk down to its famous son, the nation known abroad for one great bat and not for the people and the yard that raised him, the whole wide reach spent on a single name. To honour him is to honour the small place that made him, not to let one man stand in for the whole.

## Tell

He walked to the crease with no helmet at all, just a man from a likkle island facing down the fastest the world could bowl, cool as the breeze off the reef, and made the whole earth sit and watch a place it had barely heard of bat as a free nation.
