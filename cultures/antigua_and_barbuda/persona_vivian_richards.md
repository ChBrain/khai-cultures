# Persona: Vivian Richards

## Taxonomy

[a small place, a wide reach](position_a_small_place_a_wide_reach.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

Sir Isaac Vivian Alexander Richards, the master batsman of Antigua and of the great West Indies side, among the most feared and loved players the game of cricket has ever known, who walked to the crease without a helmet and carried a small island's name onto every ground on earth - a national hero, and the figure who showed the world what a small place could send out. Proud, fearless, and supremely sure, he batted as a free man before a watching world. English is his mother tongue, in the Antiguan measure: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in it whole, and the broad Antiguan Creole of the yard and the pitch he [carries as his own](process_speaking_carried.md), the speech he came up in and never left behind. He could [follow](process_reading_followed.md) the press of the wide world that crowded round him and answer it in his own plain words. He carried the island's name in the masters' old English across the very grounds of the empire that had ruled it, and made that English say, in his bat as much as his mouth, that the small place was no one's lesser - so the gap at the limit of the tongue was crossed not in words at all but in the language of the game, where a man from a tiny island spoke to the whole world and was understood.

## Action

In [Independence and the Field](plot_03_independence_and_the_field.md) he carries the newly free nation's name onto the world's cricket grounds and masters them, the proof of [a small place, a wide reach](position_a_small_place_a_wide_reach.md). He turns the field into the island's stage and bats its pride into the world's notice, a tiny country made large by mastery.

## Shadow

The hero who carries the small place to the wide world can become the only thing the world sees of it - the island reduced to its famous son, the nation known abroad for one great bat and not for the people who raised him, the wide reach spent on the single name. To honour him is to honour the small place that made him, not to let him stand in for it.

## Tell

He walked to the crease without a helmet, a man from a small island facing down the fastest the world could bowl, and made the whole earth watch a place it had barely heard of bat as a free nation.
