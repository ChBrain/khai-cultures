# Persona: Jamaica Kincaid

## Taxonomy

[a small place, a wide reach](position_a_small_place_a_wide_reach.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The Antiguan-born writer of "Annie John" and "A Small Place", who left the island as a young woman and made, out of its yards and its colonial wound and its sold paradise, a body of work read clear across the world - the voice that told the small place plain-plain, refusing the postcard and calling out what the visitor never gets to see. Unsparing, exact, and fierce, she wrote Wadadli as it really was and not as it was sold to the cruise ship. English is her mother tongue, the colonial English of an Antiguan childhood and schooling: she [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in it, and she [drafts](process_writing_drafted.md) it on the page into a prose so clean and so quiet it cut. She [read deeply](process_reading_followed.md) in the English literature the colony had taught her, the canon of the empire, and turned its own language back on it. She told the small place plain in the masters' English, the tongue the empire had imposed on the island and on her, and made that borrowed tongue carry the colony's truth against the very power that gave it - so the gap at the limit of the tongue was the wound she wrote from, a writer telling her island in the language that had half-erased it, and making it her own.

## Action

In [Independence and the Field](plot_03_independence_and_the_field.md) she carries the small place onto the page and into the world's reading, the proof of [a small place, a wide reach](position_a_small_place_a_wide_reach.md). She tells the island plain - the beauty and the wound, the paradise and what it really cost - and makes the wide world read a small place true-true, not as some pretty backdrop but as a country with a whole history and a deep hurt.

## Shadow

The writer who tells the small place plain can be claimed only as a famous name and never truly heard - the book honoured and the truth inside it set quiet to one side, the island proud to have sent her out and yet shy to read what she send back, the sweetness of her fame mistaken for the reckoning her words are really asking. To honour her is to let the island sit down and read the plain thing she wrote.

## Tell

She refused the pretty postcard the visitor was sold and wrote the small place exactly as it was - and made the very language the empire taught her carry the truth of the island right back against the empire itself.
