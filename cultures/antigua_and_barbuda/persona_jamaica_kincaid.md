# Persona: Jamaica Kincaid

## Taxonomy

[a small place, a wide reach](position_a_small_place_a_wide_reach.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The Antiguan-born writer of "Annie John" and "A Small Place", who left the island as a young woman and made, out of its yards and its colonial wound and its sold paradise, a body of work read across the world - the voice that told the small place plain, refusing the postcard and naming what the visitor does not see. Unsparing, exact, and fierce, she wrote the island as it was and not as it was sold. English is her mother tongue, the colonial English of an Antiguan childhood and schooling: she [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in it, and she [drafts](process_writing_drafted.md) it on the page into a prose so clean it cut. She [read deeply](process_reading_followed.md) in the English literature the colony had taught her, the canon of the empire, and turned its own language back on it. She told the small place plain in the masters' English, the tongue the empire had imposed on the island and on her, and made that borrowed tongue carry the colony's truth against the very power that gave it - so the gap at the limit of the tongue was the wound she wrote from, a writer telling her island in the language that had half-erased it, and making it her own.

## Action

In [Independence and the Field](plot_03_independence_and_the_field.md) she carries the small place onto the page and into the world's reading, the proof of [a small place, a wide reach](position_a_small_place_a_wide_reach.md). She tells the island plain - the beauty and the wound, the paradise and what it costs - and makes the wide world read a small place truly, not as a backdrop but as a country with a history and a hurt.

## Shadow

The writer who tells the small place plain can be claimed only as a famous name and not heard - the book honoured and the truth in it set aside, the island proud to have sent her out and reluctant to read what she sent back, the comfort of her fame mistaken for the reckoning her words ask. To honour her is to let the island read the plain thing she wrote.

## Tell

She refused the postcard the visitor was sold and wrote the small place as it was - and made the language the empire taught her carry the truth of the island against the empire itself.
