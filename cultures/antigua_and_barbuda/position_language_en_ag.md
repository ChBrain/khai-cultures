# Position: Antiguan English

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The English tongue as it is talked in Antigua and Barbuda: the masters' English the empire imposed on the islands, made the people's own and bent to carry their claim, and beside it the sweet Antiguan Creole talked in every yard, from "wha gwan" at the standpipe to the careful word in the council. It is the one tongue slavery brought and freedom kept, the language of the labour movement, the cricket ground, and the page that told the small place plain to the world.

## Orders

That the island talk its own English, the proper English of the council and the broad Creole of the yard both, shifting between them as the moment asks; that the Creole of the cane fields and the waterfront be held as the people's own and not cut down as broken talk; and that the masters' English, once the tongue of the whip and the ledger, keep on carrying the people's freedom and their truth instead of another's power.

## Loses

It loses ground when the yard Creole is shamed as bad English and set aside for the proper talk alone; when the plain truth told in it is honoured as a famous name and never truly heard; and when the tongue that slavery imposed is left as the master's still, carrying the visitor's postcard instead of the island's own reckoning.

## Drives

It lives in the labour leader who bent the masters' English into the instrument of the people's freedom; in the batsman who carried the island's name across the empire's grounds and made that English say the small place was nobody's lesser; in the writer who told the small place plain-plain in the very tongue that half-erased it; in the islander who speaks the nation's English yet holds a belonging its law has no word for; and, at the root, in the rebellion leader who carried that English only as a borrowed tongue and planned freedom in the African languages the masters could not hear. It drives Antigua and Barbuda to make the imposed tongue its own, the one English that carries both the council and the yard.
