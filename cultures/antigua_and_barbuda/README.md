# Antigua and Barbuda: a khai culture

Antigua and Barbuda staged as a full khai play, ISO AG, language en. Two small
islands that came up out of a sugar colony and made themselves a free nation with
a wide reach, as a theatre: their history as the plot line, their people as the
cast, the roles their language and faith encode, their places, their practices,
and their defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_antigua_and_barbuda.md](play_antigua_and_barbuda.md), the
  culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md), the Hofstede layer, written from the
  islands' profile (warm island sociability, cricket pride, an easy pace and an
  open door, faith and family close, and the long shadow of the cane carried under
  the smile) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** Sugar and the Dockyard, Emancipation and Labour,
  Independence and the Field, and the Beaches and Barbuda - each casting the
  elements it needs, and each staging the gap between the paradise sold and the
  freedom and labour that made the nation honestly.
- **Personas:** Prince Klaas (King Court, leader of the planned 1736 rising and
  national hero), Sir Vere Bird (the labour leader and father of the nation), Sir
  Vivian Richards (the master batsman), and Jamaica Kincaid (the writer of "A
  Small Place") as the historic cast, plus one defining archetype, the Barbudan
  (the islander of the sister isle's common land and frigate-bird sanctuary). Each
  is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** sugar and the dockyard (the colonial foundation of cane and navy,
  built on bondage and refused from within), the labour nation (the trade-union
  movement and the road to nationhood, freedom won twice), a small place, a wide
  reach (cricket, letters, the beaches, and Barbuda, the small nation's voice in
  the world).
- **Places:** Nelson's Dockyard (the Georgian naval base at English Harbour, a
  UNESCO site, the colonial base become a face of the free nation), Barbuda (the
  sister isle of common land and frigate birds, storm-struck and rebuilding).
- **Processes:** Carnival (the festival that dances the memory of the 1834
  emancipation), Antigua Sailing Week (the great regatta out of the old dockyard).
- **Pieces:** fungie and pepperpot (the national dish made by working people out
  of the island's own ground), the Antigua black pineapple (the sweet national
  fruit, the small place's mastery in a fruit).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-record historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources on the public record are credited, never
claimed; slavery, the plantation, and the colonial past are treated soberly, with
resistance and emancipation at the centre; the staging and the architecture are
original work.
