# Antigua and Barbuda: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-record historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md) is written from a Caribbean cultural profile
on Geert Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries
no number. Antigua and Barbuda is not separately scored in the published data, so
the pitch is tuned from the West African and Caribbean regional profile (the
nearest published proxy) and from the islands' own self-understanding; the values
are recorded here so the basis is on the record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Caribbean/West African profile (approx.)        |
| ----------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | high                                            |
| Individualism                 | low (collective, family- and community-centred) |
| Motivation toward Achievement | moderate                                        |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | moderate                                        |
| Long Term Orientation         | low (present- and tradition-oriented)           |
| Indulgence                    | high                                            |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the six-dimensions model of national
culture and the West Africa regional grouping used as the nearest published proxy
for the eastern Caribbean). Antigua and Barbuda has no separate published score;
the profile is approximate and used only to set the pitch's register.

The one defining archetype, [the Barbudan](persona_the_barbudan.md), is not a
historical individual but a composite: the islander of the sister isle who holds
land in common, keeps the frigate-bird sanctuary, and carries the question of
whether the wide-reaching nation reaches back across the channel.
