# Plot: the Beaches and Barbuda

## Cue

The free nation, no longer a colony of cane, must make its living a new way. Antigua counts three hundred and sixty-five beaches, one for every day of the year, and the world wants them; tourism becomes the nation's livelihood, the resorts rising along the shore and the visitors arriving by the planeload and the cruise ship.

## Action

The modern nation lives on its beaches, its harbours, and its visitors, and trades the welcome of a warm and sociable island for the wealth that sustains it - while the question runs underneath of who shares in that wealth, the resorts often running past the yards that staff them. Across the channel the sister isle, Barbuda, keeps its own way: land held in common by all Barbudans, the great frigate-bird sanctuary on the lagoon, a quieter and a poorer life. In 2017 Hurricane Irma strikes Barbuda head-on and empties it, the whole population carried to Antigua, and the long work of rebuilding begins - the common land, the houses, the sanctuary - with the sister isle watching to see whether the wide-reaching nation will reach back across the water to the smallest of its own.

## Stage

- [Barbuda](place_barbuda.md): the lower sister isle of common land and frigate birds, emptied and battered by Hurricane Irma and rebuilding.
- [a small place, a wide reach](position_a_small_place_a_wide_reach.md): the wide-reaching small nation, asked to turn its reach back toward home.

## Tension

The nation sells the world a paradise of beaches and lives by it, and the same image that earns its keep can erase the people who live and work behind it and run the wealth past their yards. And the wide reach that carries the island's name across the world can spend itself entirely outward while the sister isle, lower and farther and storm-struck, rebuilds its common land alone - the small place's deepest test being whether it will remember the smaller place that is its own.
