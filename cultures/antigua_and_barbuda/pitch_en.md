# Pitch: the Antiguan tone

## Taxonomy

The key in which the staging of [Antigua and Barbuda](play_antigua_and_barbuda.md) is played. It changes not one thing of what happens - not the sugar estates, not the rising, not emancipation, not independence, not the storm. It sets only how it sounds, the register and the lilt in which all of it is given.

## Tenor

Wha gwan? Come, sit down, no rush. Played warm, easy, and sociable, in the lilt of a small island where everybody know everybody, and everybody know everybody business too. A people quick to laugh and quick to talk, who lime on the corner and at the standpipe and let the day run at the island's own pace while the visitor hurry alone. Cricket-proud above all, for a son who can bat or bowl is the highest thing this island can give the wide world, and that pride run through every single thing. The proverb is always close to hand - "one one full basket", little by little the work get done - the greeting open, the door open, and the pot of fungie and pepperpot stretch easy for one more mouth. Faith is plain and the church full come Sunday, the band loud as thunder come Carnival, and a stranger get greeted like family until him prove otherwise.

## Undertow

Under the ease run the long shadow of the cane: a people who know their warmth was bought hard from a history of bondage, and who carry the memory quiet under the smile they give the visitor. There is the strain of the beach-paradise image sold to the world, the island made a backdrop and its own people made the help, the wealth of the resorts running past the same yards that earn it. And there is the quiet hurt of the sister isle, Barbuda smaller and lower and farther off, that can feel forgotten by the capital across the water - the easy island warmth not always reaching all the way across the channel to we who hold the land in common and bore the storm.

## Nerve

It strikes the chord of a hard-won ease: the pride of a people who came up out of slavery and made a free and friendly nation, who can lime and laugh exactly because they remember what it cost to be able to. It plays on the joy of Carnival and the roar of the cricket ground, on the warmth of the small place where nobody stay a stranger for long, and on the deep island knowing that the warmth itself is the victory - that to be easy and free on your own ground is the very thing the ancestors never had and won for we.

## Echo

What is left is the image of a warm, proud, easygoing people on a string of beaches, who carry a heavy past with a light step and would sooner greet you than grieve at you. A nation that runs on the sociable ease of a small island and the fierce pride of a small place with a wide reach - and that, under all the welcome it gives the world, still keeps the count of what its freedom and its friendliness are worth, and to whom.
