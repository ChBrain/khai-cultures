# Pitch: the Antiguan tone

## Taxonomy

The key in which the staging of [Antigua and Barbuda](play_antigua_and_barbuda.md) is played. It changes nothing of what happens - not the sugar estates, not the rising, not emancipation, not independence, not the storm. It sets only how it sounds, the register in which all of it is given.

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Tenor

Played warm, easy, and sociable, in the rhythm of a small island where everybody knows everybody. A people quick to laugh and quick to talk, who lime on the corner and at the standpipe, who run the day at the island's own pace and let the visitor hurry alone. Cricket-proud above all, raising a son who can bat or bowl as the highest thing the island can give the world, and carrying that pride into everything. The Creole runs thick and bright in every yard, the proverb close to hand, the greeting open, the door open, the pot stretched for one more. Faith is plain and church is full, the band is loud at Carnival, and a stranger is greeted like family until proven otherwise.

## Undertow

Under the ease runs the long shadow of the cane: a people who know their warmth was bought from a history of bondage, and who carry the memory under the smile they give the visitor. There is the strain of the beach-paradise image sold to the world, the island made a backdrop and its people made the help, the wealth of the resorts running past the yards that earn it. And there is the quiet hurt of the sister isle, Barbuda smaller and lower and farther off, that can feel forgotten by the capital across the water - the easy island sociability not always reaching across the channel to the people who hold their land in common and bore the storm.

## Nerve

It strikes the chord of a hard-won ease: the pride of a people who came up out of slavery and made a free and friendly nation, who can lime and laugh because they remember what it cost to be able to. It plays on the joy of Carnival and the roar of the cricket ground, on the warmth of the small place where no one is a stranger for long, and on the deep island knowledge that the warmth is the victory - that to be easy and free on your own ground is the thing the ancestors did not have and won.

## Echo

What is left is the image of a warm, proud, easygoing people on a string of beaches, who carry a heavy past with a light step and would rather greet you than grieve at you. A nation that runs on the sociable ease of a small island and the fierce pride of a small place with a wide reach - and that, under the welcome it gives the world, keeps the count of what its freedom and its friendliness are worth, and to whom.
