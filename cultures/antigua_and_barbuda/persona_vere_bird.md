# Persona: Vere Bird

## Taxonomy

[the labour nation](position_the_labour_nation.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

Sir Vere Cornwall Bird, the labour leader the people called the father of the nation, who came up through the Antigua Trades and Labour Union to give working people their first real power, led the islands through self-government, and became the first Prime Minister of independent Antigua and Barbuda in 1981. A powerful speaker and a tireless organiser, come up from the people and answering to them, he carried the freed people's long road from emancipation to nationhood. English, [Antiguan English](position_language_en_ag.md), is his mother tongue, in the Antiguan measure: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in it whole, and the broad Antiguan Creole of the cane fields and the waterfront he [carries as his own](process_speaking_carried.md), able to shift between the proper English of the colonial council and the people's own talk just as the moment asked - one minute "wha gwan" at the standpipe, the next a careful word to the Governor. He could [follow](process_reading_followed.md) the law and the constitution on the page and turn it back into plain talk a labourer could act on, so a man with no schooling knew exactly wha gwan with him own rights. He spoke for the working people in the masters' own English, the tongue slavery had imposed and made the island's, and bent it into the instrument of their freedom - so the gap at the limit of the tongue was the distance he closed between the language of power and the people it had ruled, made to carry their claim at last.

## Action

In [Emancipation and Labour](plot_02_emancipation_and_labour.md) he turns the freed people's want and powerlessness into organised strength, leading the union and then the nation from emancipation's half-kept promise right through to independence. He wins the second freedom the movement was made for - the freedom from want and powerlessness that outlived the chains - and carries [the labour nation](position_the_labour_nation.md) into being.

## Shadow

The leader who comes up from the people to win their freedom can sit too long at its head, the liberator turn into a fixture, the union turn into a machine, the man who once answered to the people grown too used to being answered to. The shadow of the labour nation is the big man who forgets that the power was the people's own and only lent to him.

## Tell

He could stand in a colonial council in the Englishman's English and then turn round to the cane cutter and meet him in the cane cutter's own Creole - "we deh ya, man, we deh ya" - and made the one tongue carry the other's claim, the father of the nation talking the people straight into power.
