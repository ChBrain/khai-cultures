# Iceland: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_is.md](pitch_is.md) is written from Iceland's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no
number. The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the
record without entering the prose. Iceland's Hofstede profile is less fully
established than that of larger countries, so these figures are approximate and
indicative rather than definitive.

| Dimension                     | Iceland score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ----------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 30                      |
| Individualism                 | 60                      |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 10                      |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 50                      |
| Long Term Orientation         | 28                      |
| Indulgence                    | 67                      |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published
by Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group, and Iceland's profile is less
fully established than that of larger countries.

The two defining personas, [Guðrún](persona_gudrun.md) and
[Birna](persona_birna.md), are archetypes, not historical individuals: composites
that carry the tension between the older generation ("þetta reddast", community,
the pools) and the younger, connected one (the global, open world).
