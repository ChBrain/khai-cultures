# Iceland: a khai culture

Iceland staged as a full khai play, ISO IS, language is. A small, tough island
nation as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the
roles its language encodes, its places, its practices, and its defining objects,
all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_iceland.md](play_iceland.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_is.md](pitch_is.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Iceland's profile (resilient, egalitarian, "þetta reddast", a tiny tight-knit
  nation, an old and living tongue) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the founding of the Alþingi at Þingvellir (930), the
  peaceful adoption of Christianity (1000), the republic (1944), and the crash
  and recovery (2008) - each casting the elements it needs.
- **Personas:** Snorri Sturluson and Jón Sigurðsson as the historic cast, plus
  two defining archetypes, Guðrún (older, "þetta reddast" and community) and
  Birna (younger, the open world). Each is wired to the language engine by the
  language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** þetta reddast (it will sort itself out), jafnréttið (equality),
  Alþingisarfurinn (the parliamentary heritage).
- **Places:** Reykjavík (the capital), Þingvellir (the founding ground).
- **Processes:** sundlaugin (the social life of the pools), réttirnar (the autumn
  sheep round-up).
- **Pieces:** lopapeysan (the wool sweater), handritin (the saga manuscripts).
