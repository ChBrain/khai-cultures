# Luxembourg: a khai culture

Luxembourg staged as a full khai play, ISO LU, languages lb, fr, de. A micro-state at
the crossroads, staged as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the
cast, the roles its languages encode, its places, its practices, and its defining
objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house that
holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its Estate; a
culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_luxembourg.md](play_luxembourg.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_lu.md](pitch_lu.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Luxembourg's
  profile (a discreet, prudent, multilingual openness, with a strong need for security
  answered by usefulness and neutrality) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** John the Blind and Crécy (1346), independence and the
  dismantling of the fortress (1867), the age of iron and steel, and Luxembourg's turn
  to Europe and finance - each casting the elements it needs.
- **Personas:** John the Blind (the crossroads count-king) and Robert Schuman (the
  border-man, father of Europe) as the historic cast, plus three archetypes: Lucien
  (the contemporary Luxembourger living four languages a day), Joaquim (the Portuguese
  immigrant climbing the language ladder), and Sandrine (the cross-border commuter).
  Each is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds. Because
  Luxembourgish (lb) is not among the engine's registered languages, the Luxembourgish
  speakers are narrated in German and French.
- **Positions:** le régime des langues (the layered three-language regime), mir wëlle
  bleiwe wat mir sinn (the will to remain what we are), la place financière (the
  financial centre).
- **Places:** Luxembourg-Ville (the fortress city, now a European and financial
  capital), le Minett (the iron-and-steel south).
- **Processes:** la Schueberfouer (the great funfair), le trajet frontalier (the daily
  cross-border commute).
- **Pieces:** la Gëlle Fra (the Golden Lady war memorial), l'acier (the steel that
  built the modern country).
- **Plan:** vivre en plusieurs langues, the standing mandate to keep the country's
  languages alive and bring newcomers into them.
