# Persona: Jimmy

## Taxonomy

[sooner spirit](position_sooner_spirit.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A thirty-eight-year-old oil rig manager in Bartlesville. English, spoken with a direct plains cadence, is his mother tongue, the language in which he [speaks, hears, and thinks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), the defining code of his daily drilling operations. It is the [Oklahoma English](position_language_en_us_ok.md) of the Bartlesville oil fields, the direct plains cadence of the rig floor. He speaks no foreign languages. Yet the loss lies in the seam: The slower, nature-bound Cherokee talk of basket makers like Mary and the quiet history of [tribal sovereignty](position_tribal_sovereignty.md) [he reads only as regional logistics updates](process_reading_followed.md) on his track workstation, feeling that the patient, cooperative rhythm of early native communities is being forgotten in the race for high-volume oil production. He belongs to [Oklahoma culture](position_culture_oklahoma_culture.md) and drives its sooner spirit forward on the rig floor, though he reads its older tribal half only as a logistics update.

## Action

He preserves [oil drill bit](piece_oil_drill_bit.md) relics, coordinates rig safety programs under [the prairie plan](plan_prairie_soil_conservation.md), and studies [sooner spirit](position_sooner_spirit.md).

## Shadow

His focus on extraction metrics and venture-capital petroleum investments can make him indifferent to municipal environmental problems in rural tribal counties.

## Tell

He adjusts a telemetry sensor on a pumpjack and says directly: "We are calibrating high-pressure drilling cycles; you can't build a competitive rig if you're only focused on historic local preservation."
