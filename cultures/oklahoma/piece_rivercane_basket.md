## Place

Maintained in tribal homes and displayed in the Cherokee National Museum in Tahlequah.

## Load Bearing

It represents the preservation of traditional Cherokee weaving, the connection to native river basin ecosystems, and the resilience of tribal culture.

## Apparent

A hand-woven, double-walled basket constructed of split rivercane, dyed with natural walnut and bloodroot in geometric patterns.
