# Process: rivercane weaving

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

Cherokee basket makers and traditional apprentices, conducted year-round in tribal cultural centers.

## Direction

In the weaving workshop. Gathering native rivercane stalks, splitting the cane into thin ribbons, scraping the inner pith, dying the fibers with walnut or bloodroot, and hand-weaving double-walled baskets.

## Lever

The traditional craft process that preserves Native American heritage and tribal self-reliance. It supports local artist micro-trade and cultural tourism, though it is limited by manual production times and reduced access to native rivercane beds.
