# Persona: Mary

## Taxonomy

[tribal sovereignty](position_tribal_sovereignty.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A fifty-two-year-old Cherokee basket maker in Tahlequah. English, spoken with a warm drawl, is her mother tongue, the language in which she [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and writes her daily community logs. It is the [Oklahoma English](position_language_en_us_ok.md) of the Cherokee country, the warm drawl of the Tahlequah basket benches. She speaks no foreign languages. What she cannot square is the pace of it: the technology-driven talk of oil rig managers like Jimmy and the digital demands of Oklahoma City boards reach her [only like a distant echo](process_hearing_followed.md), and she feels the patient, season-dependent rhythm of agricultural soil preservation treated as secondary to corporate petroleum and speedway entertainment. Her claim on [Oklahoma culture](position_culture_oklahoma_culture.md) is tribal sovereignty, the ground she guards against a sooner spirit that would drill straight through it.

## Action

She preserves [rivercane basket](piece_rivercane_basket.md) records, coordinates field conservation under [the prairie plan](plan_prairie_soil_conservation.md), and studies [sooner spirit](position_sooner_spirit.md).

## Shadow

Her pride in traditional tribal land sovereignty and independent cultural rights can make her suspicious of state-wide zoning and environmental agricultural mandates, fearing they will restrict family property use.

## Tell

She looks out over the windbreak trees and says thoughtfully: "The land has its own treaties; you can't protect prairie productivity if you're only focused on digital speed and petroleum profit metrics."
