# Plot: The Dust Bowl Migration 1930

## Cue

In the 1930s, severe drought and wind storms trigger massive soil erosion across the western plains.

## Action

The dust storms prompt national conservation reforms, developing [the prairie plan](plan_prairie_soil_conservation.md). Mary implements tree windbreak programs on family farms, while Jimmy audits oil field soil stabilization projects.

## Stage

- [tribal sovereignty](position_tribal_sovereignty.md) : the indigenous land stewardship, cultural preservation, and resilience.
- [rivercane weaving](process_rivercane_weaving.md) : the traditional basket weaving process that uses native river basin grasses.
- [the Cherokee Cultural Center](place_cherokee_cultural_center.md) : the heritage facility that preserved agricultural records during the crisis.

## Tension

Agricultural displacement forces thousands of tenant families to migrate westward under extremely impoverished conditions.
