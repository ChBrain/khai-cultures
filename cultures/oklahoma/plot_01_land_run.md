## Cue

In April 1889, the federal government opens unassigned lands, prompting thousands of settlers to race for claims.

## Action

The run establishes settlement infrastructure, developing [sooner spirit](position_sooner_spirit.md). The [rivercane basket](piece_rivercane_basket.md) represents this historical era. Jimmy coordinates lease logistics for regional oil fields, while Mary preserves traditional Cherokee migration narratives.

## Stage

- [sooner spirit](position_sooner_spirit.md) : the pioneer determination, oil rush ambition, and land pride.
- [rivercane basket](piece_rivercane_basket.md) : the traditional Cherokee basket representing agricultural heritage.
- [the Cherokee Cultural Center](place_cherokee_cultural_center.md) : the tribal heritage museum and preservation complex.

## Tension

The land opening violates prior treaties and displaces Native American nations from designated territorial lands.
