# Position: Oklahoma culture

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The belonging to a land claimed overnight in the Land Run of 1889, where cities rose in a single afternoon, and to the older, sovereign nations who held this ground long before the settlers raced across it. It carries [sooner spirit](position_sooner_spirit.md), the pioneer determination and drilling wealth of the oil fields, alongside [tribal sovereignty](position_tribal_sovereignty.md), the ancestral land stewardship and self-determination of the Cherokee and the other native nations who govern their own ground within the state's. It carries, too, the memory of the Dust Bowl, when the very soil that made the state rich blew away, and the discipline of the prairie plan built to keep that memory from repeating.

## Orders

That one act with resourcefulness and private property rights on the derrick as much as on the farm, and that one honor tribal treaties and self-determination as a standing older than the state itself. It orders coordination between drilling regulation and agricultural conservation, participation in weaving circles and oral history as much as in rig safety programs, and a stewardship of the prairie soil learned the hard way, once, by watching it vanish.

## Loses

Ground at the seam between the fast, extraction-driven talk of the oil fields and the slower, cooperative rhythm of tribal and agricultural life, each hearing the other only as a distant echo across the plains. It loses ground when resource speculation degrades the water and soil that both oil worker and tribal steward depend on, and it loses ground whenever state-wide development is allowed to override sovereign treaties, forgetting that some of this land was never the state's alone to give away.

## Drives

It drives the sooner spirit to keep drilling and building even against the standing memory of the Dust Bowl, and it drives the state's native nations to keep their languages, their weaving, and their self-governance alive alongside the derricks. It carries a land run in a single day toward a slower reckoning with what the soil and the treaties both demand, and it drives Oklahomans, oil worker and tribal artisan alike, toward a resilience neither could hold on its own. Its English is the direct plains cadence and the warm Cherokee-country drawl of [Oklahoma English](position_language_en_us_ok.md), shared however unevenly across derrick and basket bench.
