# Thailand: a khai culture

Thailand staged as a full khai play, ISO TH, language th. The never-colonized
land of the smile, anchored in Theravada Buddhism and held together by warmth,
as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its
language and faith encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects,
all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_thailand.md](play_thailand.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_th.md](pitch_th.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Thailand's profile (deference to hierarchy and age, a collective warmth centred
  on family and community, the avoidance of open conflict and the keeping of face,
  and a present-focused enjoyment of life) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** Sukhothai, Ayutthaya, the keeping of independence, and
  modern Thailand, each casting the elements it needs, and each staging the
  nation's turbulence soberly and without taking a side.
- **Personas:** King Ramkhamhaeng the Great (credited with the Thai script), King
  Chulalongkorn / Rama V (modernization and the keeping of independence), the poet
  Sunthorn Phu, and the Buddhist teacher Buddhadasa Bhikkhu, as the historic cast,
  plus one archetype, the rice farmer (chao na), the backbone of the land. Each is
  wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** Theravada Buddhism (the faith at the heart of daily life), the
  giving heart (nam chai, with the wai and the value of sanuk), and independence
  (the never-colonized land and its diplomatic adaptability).
- **Places:** Bangkok (the river capital and modern metropolis, heir of
  Ayutthaya) and Sukhothai (the first capital, where the script and the ideal of
  Thainess were born).
- **Processes:** Songkran (the water festival and traditional new year) and Loy
  Krathong (the festival of floating lights and thanks to the rivers).
- **Pieces:** Muay Thai (the martial art that holds reverence and the fighter in
  one body) and rice (khao, the grain that carries the life of the nation).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the monarchy is staged respectfully as a cultural and unifying heritage,
and the nation's modern political turbulence is treated soberly and neutrally,
taking no side. The staging and the architecture are original work.
