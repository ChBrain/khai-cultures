# Yemen: a khai culture

Yemen staged as a full khai play, ISO YE, language ar (Modern Standard Arabic in
a distinctly Yemeni voice). The ancient south of the Arabian peninsula, "Arabia
Felix," staged as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the
cast, the roles its language and faith encode, its places, its practices, and its
defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_yemen.md](play_yemen.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_ar.md](pitch_ar.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Yemen's
  profile (a deeply collective and tribal society, a high respect for age and
  authority, dignity and restraint, endurance under scarcity, and a measured,
  rooted sense of time) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the kingdom of Saba and the Marib dam, the coming of Islam
  and the great Yemeni scholars of Arabic, the tower-house architecture of old
  Sanaa and Shibam, and Mocha and the birth of the coffee trade - each casting
  the elements it needs.
- **Personas:** Bilqis the Queen of Sheba, the scholar-geographer al-Hasan
  al-Hamdani, the poet Abdullah al-Baradduni, and the Himyarite scholar Nashwan
  al-Himyari as the historic cast, plus one archetype, the coffee farmer of the
  terraced highlands. Each is wired to the language engine by the
  language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** Arabia Felix (Yemen as ancient root and the true meaning of "the
  happy"), the incense road (the caravan trade that built the ancient kingdoms),
  hospitality and tribe (the bond of kin, the guest as a covenant, and the
  jambiya as its emblem).
- **Places:** Old Sanaa (the tower-house city, among the oldest inhabited on
  earth) and Marib (the Sabaean capital and the great dam).
- **Processes:** growing and brewing coffee (the gift Yemen gave the world, kept
  as the work of the hand and the welcome of the house) and building the Sanaa
  house (the tower-house craft and its carved facades).
- **Pieces:** the jambiya (the curved dagger worn as the emblem of manhood and
  honor) and qishr (the coffee-husk drink poured first for the guest).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical and
heritage sources are recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never
enters the prose; the pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain
are credited, never claimed; the staging and the architecture are original work.
This culture is staged strictly on enduring cultural and historical ground -
Arabia Felix, Sheba, the incense road, ancient Sanaa and Shibam, coffee, the
mountains, poetry, and faith - and does not touch contemporary politics.
