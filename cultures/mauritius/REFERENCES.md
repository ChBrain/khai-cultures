# Mauritius: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain sources behind the plots,
personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_mfe.md](pitch_mfe.md) is written from Mauritius' profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record without
entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Mauritius score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 78                        |
| Individualism                 | 28                        |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 39                        |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 67                        |
| Long Term Orientation         | 43                        |
| Indulgence                    | 53                        |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; Mauritius reads as a
relationship-first, hierarchy-respecting society rather than an individualist one.

## Historical and cultural sources

The plots, the real personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn from
the public record. They are credited here and never claimed; the staging is
original work. The hard passages of the story - slavery and the indentured labour
system - are staged soberly and with dignity, on the public record, without
atrocity detail.

The one archetype persona, [the ravanne player](persona_ravann_zwer.md), is not a
historical individual: a composite who carries the living reality of lalit ansam,
the player whose drum makes every community - Hindu, Creole, Sino-Mauritian,
Franco-Mauritian - dance together as one.
