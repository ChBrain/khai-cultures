# Position: Tar Heel

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The belonging to the Tar Heel State, a land that stretches from the wind-scoured dunes of the Outer Banks, where the Roanoke colonists vanished and left only the word Croatoan carved on a post, and the Wright brothers first proved that a machine could hold the air, to the old ridgelines of the Appalachians, where [Appalachian heritage](position_appalachian_heritage.md) keeps the loom, the ballad, and the porch conversation alive. It carries, without ever choosing it, both that mountain patience and craft and the newer pride of [Research Triangle](position_research_triangle.md), the university towns of the Piedmont turned into one of the world's dense clusters of biotech and research. It is a state that holds a frontier mystery, a first flight, and a research park inside the same identity.

## Orders

That one speaks in a thoughtful, polite, and articulate register whether in the lab or on the mountain porch, that education and technical competence are honoured alongside inherited craft and local history, and that rural community autonomy and hospitality are respected even as the Piedmont's research economy expands. It asks that the state's two halves, the fast-growing research cities and the older mountain counties, be held together in one polite and structured conversation rather than let drift apart.

## Loses

Its balance when the rapidly expanding, diverse research hubs of the Piedmont and the conservative, rural mountain communities of the west read each other only through the zoning fight and the funding argument, urban growth met by rural protective instinct and rural tradition read as a brake on progress. It loses ground, too, when corporate pharmaceutical interest prioritizes patent profit over the regional healthcare access the research was supposed to serve, and when the hand-woven, patient rhythm of the mountains is treated as a quaint leftover rather than a living craft.

## Drives

Lives in the biotech scientist of Chapel Hill who advances target-specific therapeutics and reads the mountain's weaving traditions only as historical record on a terminal, and in the hand-weaver of Asheville who preserves the loom and the community forum and hears the Piedmont's digital urgency only as a distant echo. It drives North Carolina to keep the lab and the porch, the Research Triangle and Appalachian heritage, the Lost Colony's mystery and Kitty Hawk's first flight, inside one state that plans its forests and its future without giving up either half of what it is.
