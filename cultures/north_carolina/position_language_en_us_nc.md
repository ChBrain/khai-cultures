# Position: North Carolina English

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The Southern English of the Tar Heel State, split between the softening Piedmont speech of the Research Triangle cities and the deeper, older Appalachian mountain drawl of the western peaks. It holds a courteous, unhurried Upper South plainness that both the biotech campus and the mountain loom carry.

## Orders

Where it passes as native ground: the Chapel Hill lab, the Piedmont town, the Asheville craft studio, the mountain holler. It orders which vowels and turns of phrase mark a speaker as a Carolinian and sets the Piedmont ear against the mountain one.

## Loses

Ground to General American, the flat national broadcast standard the screen and the corporate board carry, which trims the drawl off the young Piedmont professional and stamps the mountain speech as backward. The high-tech research register also crowds the older mountain speech toward the margin.

## Drives

It carries [Ethan](persona_ethan.md), whose articulate Piedmont cadence is the working English of the Chapel Hill biotech labs, and [Sarah](persona_sarah.md), whose expressive mountain drawl holds the Appalachian English of the Asheville craft studios. So the one Carolinian speech shows who turns toward the research campus and who keeps the mountain ground.
