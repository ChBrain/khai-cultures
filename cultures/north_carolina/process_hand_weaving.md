# Process: hand weaving

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

Appalachian traditional weavers and craft coordinators, conducted year-round in mountain workshops and heritage centers.

## Direction

At the wooden hand loom. Preparing the warp threads, winding the bobbin, throwing the shuttle through the shed, and beating the weft to create traditional textile patterns.

## Lever

The traditional craft process that reinforces Appalachian cultural identity and heritage tourism. It supports local craftspeople and cottage trade, though it is limited by manual production times and reduced supply of local wool.

## Echo

The weaving patterns preserve historical narratives. The process defines the daily work of [Sarah](persona_sarah.md) in Asheville, while scientist [Ethan](persona_ethan.md) values the craft as an early model of structured execution.
