# Persona: Ethan

## Taxonomy

[Research Triangle](position_research_triangle.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A thirty-six-year-old biotech scientist in Chapel Hill. English, spoken with an articulate Piedmont cadence, is his mother tongue, the language in which he [speaks, hears, and thinks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), the defining code of his professional life. It is the [North Carolina English](position_language_en_us_nc.md) of the Piedmont, the articulate Southern cadence of the Chapel Hill research campus. He speaks no foreign languages. Yet the loss lies in the seam: The slower, tradition-bound mountain talk of weavers like Sarah and the quiet history of [Appalachian heritage](position_appalachian_heritage.md) [he reads only as historical records](process_reading_followed.md) on his terminal, feeling that the patient, manual traditions of the region are being replaced by high-speed corporate research. He belongs to [Tar Heel](position_culture_tar_heel.md) as the researcher's half of a state that holds the lab and the mountain porch inside one identity.

## Action

He preserves [biotech vial](piece_biotech_vial.md) samples, coordinates data under [the forest preservation plan](plan_forest_preservation.md), and studies [Research Triangle](position_research_triangle.md).

## Shadow

His focus on pharmaceutical metrics and biotech innovation can make him indifferent to the economic struggles and loss of manufacturing jobs in rural mountain counties.

## Tell

He looks at a chromatography display and says directly: "We are developing target-specific therapeutics; you can't advance public health if you're only focused on preserving traditional rural ways."
