# Chad: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_ar.md](pitch_ar.md) is written from Chad's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
Where a published score for Chad is not available, the West African cluster that
Hofstede surveyed is used as the nearest proxy; the values are recorded here so
the data is on the record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Chad score (approx., West African proxy) |
| ----------------------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 77                                       |
| Individualism                 | 20                                       |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 46                                       |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 54                                       |
| Long Term Orientation         | 9                                        |
| Indulgence                    | 78                                       |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the six-dimensions model of national
culture and the Country Comparison tool). Scores are approximate; absent a
dedicated Chad score, the West African values published by Hofstede Insights / The
Culture Factor Group are used as the nearest proxy, with the collectivist,
high-power-distance reading carried into the pitch.

from the public historical record. They are credited here and never claimed; the
staging is original work. Chad's many peoples and faiths are staged with equal
dignity, and the country's civil wars and recent conflicts are out of scope.

The defining persona, [the Herder](persona_the_herder.md), is an archetype, not a
historical individual: a composite that carries the oldest calling on this land,
the herder who crosses the long pasture behind the rain as his forebears did when
the Sahara was green.
