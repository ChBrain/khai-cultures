# Seychelles: a khai culture

Seychelles staged as a full khai play, ISO SC, language crs (Seychellois Creole,
Seselwa). A Creole nation woven from many peoples on the oldest oceanic islands,
as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its
language and faith encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects,
all tuned by a single pitch. All prose bodies are written in Seychellois Creole,
the first national language and the mother tongue of nearly all Seychellois.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_seychelles.md](play_seychelles.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_crs.md](pitch_crs.md), the Hofstede layer, written from a
  warm, close, slow-living small-island profile (collective and family-centred,
  low on distance between people, present- and life-enjoying, conscious of being
  small and far on a great sea) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the first people (1770), the birth of a Creole people, a
  new nation (1976), and Creole made official - each casting the elements it
  needs, and each staging the hardship of the beginning soberly and with dignity.
- **Personas:** Antoine Abel (the father of Seychellois letters), Danielle de St
  Jorre (the champion who gave Creole its orthography and dictionary), Pierre
  Poivre (the French administrator whose spice plan opened the islands, handled
  soberly), and Patrick Victor (the sega and moutya master) as the cast, plus one
  archetype, the fisherman (sa peser), the Seychellois woven from many roots whose
  life is the sea. Each is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing
  it holds.
- **Positions:** Lasosyasyon (the weaving of African, Indian, Chinese, French, and
  English strands into one Seselwa people), Kreol (the island-born language made
  the first official tongue), Lanmer (the sea that surrounds and feeds a small
  island nation).
- **Places:** Victoria (the small capital with its clock tower and market) and the
  Vallee de Mai (the ancient palm forest of the coco de mer on Praslin).
- **Processes:** Festival Kreol (the yearly celebration that lifts the language and
  culture) and the moutya (the deep drum-dance of the ancestors, born in hardship,
  treated with dignity).
- **Pieces:** the coco de mer (the great double coconut found nowhere else) and the
  giant tortoise (the Aldabra tortoise, among the oldest life on the oldest
  islands).

## Provenance

The Hofstede orientation behind the pitch and the public-domain historical and
natural sources are recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never
enters the prose; the pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain
are credited, never claimed; the hardship of settlement, the period of slavery,
and the colonial past are treated soberly on the public record, taking no
political side; the staging and the architecture are original work.
