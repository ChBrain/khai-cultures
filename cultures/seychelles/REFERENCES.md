# Seychelles: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede orientation that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical and natural
sources behind the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_crs.md](pitch_crs.md) is written from a cultural orientation in
the spirit of Geert Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension
and carries no number. Seychelles is a very small island state and is not
individually scored in the main published Hofstede country set; the orientation
below is read soberly from the small-island, family-centred, Creole-society
profile and from regional Indian Ocean and Sub-Saharan comparisons, and is
recorded here so the orientation is on the record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Seychelles orientation (qualitative) |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------------------ |
| Power Distance                | moderate                             |
| Individualism                 | low (collective, family-centred)     |
| Motivation toward Achievement | low (quality of life over winning)   |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | moderate                             |
| Long Term Orientation         | low to moderate                      |
| Indulgence                    | high (life-enjoying, relaxed)        |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the six-dimensions model of national
culture and the Country Comparison tool). Where Seychelles is not individually
published, the orientation is inferred qualitatively from the model and from
neighbouring profiles; it is approximate and used only to set the pitch.

## Historical and natural sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn
from the public record. They are credited here and never claimed; the staging is
original work. The hardship of settlement, the period of slavery in the early
plantation society, and the colonial past are staged soberly on the public
record, taking no political side and dwelling on no atrocity.

The one archetype persona, [the fisherman](persona_sa_peser.md), is not a
historical individual but a composite: the ordinary Seychellois woven from many
roots whose life is the sea, carrying the islands' reliance on the ocean and on
the Creole mother tongue.
