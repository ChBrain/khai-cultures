# Indonesia: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_id.md](pitch_id.md) is written from Indonesia's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record without
entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Indonesia score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 78                        |
| Individualism                 | 14                        |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 46                        |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 48                        |
| Long Term Orientation         | 62                        |
| Indulgence                    | 38                        |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; Indonesia's low Individualism and
high Power Distance scores are among the markers of a strongly collectivist and
hierarchical profile.

## Historical sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn
from the public historical record. They are credited here and never claimed; the
staging is original work. The colonial period, the independence struggle, and the
twentieth-century transitions are staged soberly on the public record, with no
political side and no graphic detail, keeping the focus on the enduring culture,
the unifying language, the faiths, the islands, and the people.

The one defining archetype, [Petani](persona_petani.md), is not a historical
individual but a composite: the farmer of the rice terraces who carries gotong
royong, the communal mutual aid of the village, from the oldest times to the
present, and whose heart beats in a local mother tongue while the nation speaks in
the chosen unifying language.
