# Indonesia: a khai culture

Indonesia staged as a full khai play, ISO ID, language id. An archipelago of
thousands of islands made one nation by a chosen tongue and a shared creed, as a
theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its
language and faith encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects,
all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_indonesia.md](play_indonesia.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_id.md](pitch_id.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Indonesia's profile (strong collectivism and group harmony, deference to age
  and authority, decisions sought through deliberation toward consensus, restraint
  and patience, and a long, accommodating view) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** Srivijaya and Majapahit, the spice islands with the
  arrival of the faiths and the long colonial period, the Proclamation of 1945,
  and the building of the republic, each casting the elements it needs and each
  treated soberly and without taking a side.
- **Personas:** Sukarno (proclaimer of independence and articulator of
  Pancasila), Mohammad Hatta (co-proclaimer and nation-builder), Raden Ajeng
  Kartini (pioneer of women's education and letters), and Pramoedya Ananta Toer
  (the great novelist) as the historic cast, plus one defining archetype, Petani
  (the farmer of the rice terraces, the backbone of mutual aid). Each is wired to
  the language engine by the language-crossing it holds, between a local mother
  tongue and the chosen national language.
- **Positions:** Pancasila (the five-principle creed that is the basis of the
  state), gotong royong (mutual aid, beban dipikul bersama), and Bhinneka Tunggal
  Ika (unity in diversity, the national motto from the Majapahit era).
- **Places:** Borobudur and Yogyakarta (the great temples and the heart of
  Javanese heritage, where batik, gamelan, and wayang live), and Jakarta (the
  capital where the republic was proclaimed and is built).
- **Processes:** musyawarah untuk mufakat (deliberation toward consensus) and
  panen di sawah (the communal rice harvest).
- **Pieces:** batik (the diversity of the nation woven into one cloth) and
  gamelan (mutual aid made into music).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the colonial period, the independence struggle, and the twentieth-century
transitions are treated soberly on the public record, with no political side and
no graphic detail; the staging and the architecture are original work.
