# Eritrea: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical and cultural
sources behind the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_ti.md](pitch_ti.md) is written from Eritrea's profile on the
model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number. Eritrea is
not separately published in the core Hofstede country tables; the orientation used
here follows the regional pattern for the Horn of Africa and East Africa
(strongly collective, high power distance, a high value on endurance), and is
recorded here so the orientation is on the record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Eritrea orientation (regional, approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | --------------------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | high                                    |
| Individualism                 | low (strongly collective)               |
| Motivation toward Achievement | moderate                                |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | moderate                                |
| Long Term Orientation         | moderate to high                        |
| Indulgence                    | low (restrained)                        |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the six-dimensions model of national
culture) and the East Africa regional cluster. Values are an approximate regional
orientation, not a published national score; they are used only to set the pitch.

## Historical and cultural sources

The plots, the real personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn from
the public historical and cultural record. They are credited here and never
claimed; the staging is original work. The long road to independence and
self-reliance is staged soberly on the public record, taking no political side and
with no atrocity detail; recent politics is out of scope.
