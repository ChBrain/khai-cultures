# Eritrea: a khai culture

Eritrea staged as a full khai play, ISO ER, language ti (Tigrinya, written in the
Ge'ez script). A Red Sea nation of nine peoples and two faiths, held as one by
hard-won self-reliance, staged as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its
people as the cast, the roles its languages and faiths encode, its places, its
practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_eritrea.md](play_eritrea.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_ti.md](pitch_ti.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Eritrea's profile (a strongly collective and community-minded society, a
  respect for age and authority, a deep value of endurance and self-reliance, and
  a measured, unhurried register) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the trade of Adulis, the making of Asmara, the long road
  to self-reliance, and nine peoples, one nation - each casting the elements it
  needs, and each staging the nation soberly from within its own self-understanding.
- **Personas:** Alemseged Tesfai (the writer-historian), Bereket Mengisteab (the
  master of the krar), Ras Alula (the nineteenth-century figure of endurance), and
  Reesom Haile (the Tigrinya poet) as the real cast, plus one archetype, the
  coffee-ceremony host (the woman who receives the guest over the bun). Each is
  wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** resilience and self-reliance (the hard-won endurance at the root
  of the national self), the nine nationalities (Tigrinya, Tigre, Saho, Afar,
  Bilen, Hedareb, Kunama, Nara, and Rashaida, held in equal dignity), and two
  faiths side by side (Orthodox Tewahedo Christianity and Islam living together).
- **Places:** Asmara (the modernist Art Deco capital on the highland plateau, a
  UNESCO World Heritage city of cinemas and cafes), Massawa (the ancient Red Sea
  port and the gateway to the Dahlak islands).
- **Processes:** the coffee ceremony (bun, the unhurried rite of hospitality and
  gathering), the evening promenade (the cafe-and-street life of Asmara).
- **Pieces:** injera (the shared bread eaten by hand from one plate), the krar
  (the lyre that carries the music of all the peoples).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the long road to independence and self-reliance is treated soberly on the
public record, taking no political side; recent politics is out of scope. The
staging and the architecture are original work.
