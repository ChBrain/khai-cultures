# eSwatini: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_ss.md](pitch_ss.md) is written from Eswatini's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
Eswatini does not have a full published score on every dimension; the pitch is
written from the regional Southern African / West African profile that Hofstede
groups it within, recorded here so the basis is on the record without entering the
prose.

| Dimension                     | Regional profile (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | -------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | high                       |
| Individualism                 | low (strongly collective)  |
| Motivation toward Achievement | moderate                   |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | moderate                   |
| Long Term Orientation         | low to moderate            |
| Indulgence                    | moderate                   |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Where a country lacks a full published
score, the regional Southern African profile is used, as published by Hofstede
Insights / The Culture Factor Group; the strongly collective, high-power-distance
shape is characteristic of the region.

from the public historical record. They are credited here and never claimed; the
staging is original work. The nation is staged from within its own
self-understanding and on the public record, taking no political position on the
modern monarchy or governance.
