# eSwatini: a khai culture

Eswatini staged as a full khai play, ISO SZ, language ss (siSwati). One of
Africa's last kingdoms staged as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its
people as the cast, the roles its language and ceremonies encode, its places, its
practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_eswatini.md](play_eswatini.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_ss.md](pitch_ss.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Eswatini's profile (a deep respect for hierarchy and the dual monarchy, a
  strongly collective sense of self bound to family, regiment, and nation, and a
  reverence for community over the lone individual) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** King Mswati II and the making of the nation, the nation
  forged and settled, Queen Mother Labotsibeni and the schooling of the nation,
  and independence and the living kingdom - each casting the elements it needs,
  and each staged soberly from within the nation's own self-understanding.
- **Personas:** King Sobhuza II (the long-reigning unifying figure and architect
  of independence), King Mswati II (after whom the nation is named), Queen Mother
  Labotsibeni Gwamile (who steadied the nation and championed its schooling), and
  J.S.M. Matsebula (the celebrated siSwati poet and chronicler) as the historic
  cast, plus one archetype, the reed-carrying maiden of the Umhlanga. Each is
  wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** the kingship heritage (the dual monarchy of the Ngwenyama and
  the Ndlovukati, where king and nation are one), community and the regiments
  (the collective self and the emabutfo), cattle and the kraal (cattle as wealth
  and the sibaya as the sacred heart of the home).
- **Places:** Lobamba (the royal and spiritual capital, Ludzidzini, and the seat
  of the dual monarchy), the Ezulwini Valley (the landscapes from Highveld to
  Lowveld and the Sibebe rock).
- **Processes:** the Incwala (the sacred kingship ceremony of the first fruits,
  the nation's most important ritual), the Umhlanga (the Reed Dance, the great
  gathering of the maidens before the Queen Mother).
- **Pieces:** the beehive grass hut (the woven home of the nation), emasi and
  sishwala (the sour milk and porridge of the kitchen, drawn from the cattle of
  the kraal).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed. The nation is staged from within its own self-understanding, taking no
political position on the modern monarchy or governance; the staging and the
architecture are original work.
