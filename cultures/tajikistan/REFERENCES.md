# Tajikistan: References

The provenance behind this culture: the cultural source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Cultural source data

The pitch [pitch_tg.md](pitch_tg.md) is written from Tajikistan's regional
cultural profile and names no dimension and carries no number. Tajikistan is not
separately scored in the most widely published editions of Geert Hofstede's model
of national culture; the pitch is therefore drawn from the broadly documented
cultural traits of the region and is rendered qualitatively rather than from a
numeric profile, so that the orientation is on the record without entering the
prose.

| Trait (qualitative)          | Tajikistan (regional profile)                         |
| ---------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------- |
| Group and family closeness   | Strong; the family, village, and elder come first     |
| Respect for authority/elder  | High; age and the spoken word carry weight            |
| Hospitality                  | Central; the guest is honored as a gift               |
| Rootedness in language/faith | Deep; the Persian word and Islam are core to identity |

Source: the qualitative cultural orientation is drawn from the broadly documented
traits of the wider Central Asian and Persianate cultural region; Tajikistan is
not a separately published country in the standard Hofstede Insights / The Culture
Factor Group country comparison data, and no numeric scores are claimed for it.

language passed down by ear.
