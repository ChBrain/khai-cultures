# Tajikistan: a khai culture

Tajikistan staged as a full khai play, ISO TJ, language tg (Tajik, the Tajik
form of Persian, written in Cyrillic script). The Persian heart of Central Asia
as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its
language and faith encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects,
all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_tajikistan.md](play_tajikistan.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_tg.md](pitch_tg.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Tajikistan's regional cultural profile (warmth and respect for elders, the
  closeness of family and village, the value placed on hospitality and the spoken
  word, and a deep rootedness in language and faith) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Samanid golden age, the mountains and faith, the new
  awakening, and independence and healing - each casting the elements it needs.
  The hard twentieth century and the difficult years that followed independence
  are acknowledged soberly and with dignity, with no detail and taking no side.
- **Personas:** Abu Abdullah Rudaki (the father of Persian poetry), Ismail Samani
  (Ismoili Somoni, founder of the Samanid state and the nation's father-figure),
  Sadriddin Ayni (founder of modern Tajik literature), and Mirzo Tursunzoda (the
  poet) as the historic cast, plus one archetype, the highland farmer (the
  dehqon). Each is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** the Persian word (the language and the deep Persian poetic
  heritage), the mountains (the Pamirs and the high valleys that kept the nation
  whole), hospitality (the open dastarkhan and respect for the guest and the
  elder).
- **Places:** the Pamirs (the Roof of the World, the high valleys and the
  Ismailis), Dushanbe (the bazaar grown into the capital, the seat of letters and
  the new state).
- **Processes:** Navruz (the ancient Persian new-year and the renewal of spring),
  the osh gathering (palav cooked for the feast, the guest, and the community).
- **Pieces:** atlas and chakan (the bright silk and the hand embroidery of Tajik
  dress), the rubab (the wooden stringed instrument that carries the old poetry
  and the falak music of the Pamirs).
