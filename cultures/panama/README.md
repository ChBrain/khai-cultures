# Panama: a khai culture

Panama staged as a full khai play, ISO PA, language es. A nation that is above all
a passage, the isthmus and the crossroads of the world, a crucible fighting to make
its own crossing its own, as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as
the cast, the roles its language encodes, its places, its practices, and its
defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house that
holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its Estate; a
culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_panama.md](play_panama.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_es.md](pitch_es.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Panama's
  profile (warm, plural and cosmopolitan hospitality, a quick and entrepreneurial
  "juega vivo" wit, a coastal Caribbean indulgence, a person-before-matter
  closeness, and an outlook shaped by being the world's crossing) and naming no
  dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Crossing of the Seas, the Railroad and the Separation,
  the Struggle for the Canal, and the Global Crucible - each casting the elements it
  needs, and each staging the fortune and the wound of the crossroads honestly.
- **Personas:** Vasco Nunez de Balboa, Victoriano Lorenzo, Omar Torrijos, and Ruben
  Blades as the historic cast, plus one defining archetype, el canalero (the
  Afro-Antillean and local labor that dug the canal and runs it). Each is wired to
  the language engine by the language-crossing it holds - Spanish the mother tongue
  for most, an indigenous mother tongue carried under Spanish for Victoriano
  Lorenzo, English carried to the world by Ruben Blades, and West Indian Creole
  English the mother tongue of el canalero, with Spanish carried.
- **Positions:** the crossroads of the world (the isthmus and the transit, the
  bridge of the world), sovereignty and the canal (the long fight for the canal and
  the Zone, carried by the way of the treaties to the 1999 handover), the crucible
  of the isthmus (the plural, transit-made society and the global Panamanian).
- **Places:** the Panama Canal (the locks and the cut that join the two oceans, the
  heart of the crossing), Panama City (the ruins, the Casco Antiguo, and the
  gleaming skyline at the mouth of the canal).
- **Processes:** Carnival (the pre-Lenten festival of the pollera, tamborito and
  murga, the plural nation set dancing), the Congos of Portobelo (the Afro-colonial
  Congo tradition of the Caribbean coast, the memory of freedom kept alive).
- **Pieces:** the pollera (the elaborate embroidered national dress of the
  interior), the sombrero pintao (the hand-woven painted hat, the campesino's
  crown).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the 1903 separation, the US Canal Zone and the long struggle for
sovereignty, the 1977 treaties and the 1999 handover, and the events of 1989 are
named soberly on the public record, without atrocity detail and taking no political
side; the staging and the architecture are original work.
