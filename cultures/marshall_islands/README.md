# the Marshall Islands: a khai culture

The Marshall Islands staged as a full khai play, ISO MH, language mah (Kajin
Majol). A Micronesian nation of master navigators, scarred by the bomb and now on
the front line of the rising sea, as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its
people as the cast, the roles its language and faith encode, its places, its
practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch. The prose is
written in Marshallese; only the English headings are kept.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_marshall_islands.md](play_marshall_islands.md), the culture
  itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_mah.md](pitch_mah.md), the Hofstede layer, written from the
  Marshallese profile (sea-bound and clan-rooted, collective and family-bound,
  faith-filled, resilient, with the navigator's patience and the community's bond)
  and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** ri-jerakrok (settlement, wave-piloting, and the matrilineal
  clans), baam im pata (the German, Japanese, and US administration, WWII, and the
  nuclear testing named soberly), republic im compact (Amata Kabua, the republic,
  the Compact, and the fight for nuclear justice), and dan ej wanlontak (the rising
  sea, the climate front line, deBrum and Keju, and the diaspora) - each casting
  the elements it needs.
- **Personas:** Amata Kabua (the first president and paramount chief), Tony deBrum
  (the statesman of nuclear justice and climate diplomacy), and Darlene Keju (the
  public-health and anti-nuclear advocate) as the historic cast, plus two defining
  archetypes, the ri-meto (the master navigator and wave-pilot) and the lerooj (the
  chiefly clan-mother). Each is wired to the language engine by the
  language-crossing it holds, Marshallese held as the mother tongue and English
  carried.
- **Positions:** the navigators and the sea (wave-piloting, the stick chart, the
  canoe, and the matrilineal clans), the nuclear wound (Bikini and Enewetak,
  displacement, and the fight for justice), the atolls and the Compact (the climate
  front line and free association).
- **Places:** Majuro (the capital atoll, the crowded ring of islets) and Bikini
  Atoll (the nuclear-test wound, the displaced people's lost home).
- **Processes:** jerakrok (wave-piloting, voyaging by reading the swells), kemem
  (the great first-birthday feast that binds the clan and community).
- **Pieces:** the stick chart (the navigator's chart of sticks and shells), the
  outrigger canoe (the great sailing walap that binds the atolls).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the nuclear testing at Bikini and Enewetak, the displacement, and the
long fight for justice are treated soberly on the public record, with dignity and
no graphic detail; the staging and the architecture are original work.
