# the Marshall Islands: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind the
plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_mah.md](pitch_mah.md) is written from the Marshallese profile on
Geert Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no
number. Published Hofstede scores for the Marshall Islands are not available in the
standard dataset; the pitch is written from the well-documented cultural profile of
Pacific island and Micronesian societies (strong collectivism and family and clan
bonds, a long communal time horizon, and a high regard for community and faith over
the individual). The model and its source are recorded here so the data is on the
record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Marshallese profile (qualitative) |
| ----------------------------- | --------------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | high (chiefly, iroij authority)   |
| Individualism                 | low (clan- and family-bound)      |
| Motivation toward Achievement | low (cooperative, communal)       |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | moderate                          |
| Long Term Orientation         | high (lineage and inheritance)    |
| Indulgence                    | moderate                          |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the six-dimensions model of national
culture), read together with the published cultural anthropology of Micronesia. No
official numeric scores are published for the Marshall Islands; the profile above is
qualitative and approximate.

## Historical sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn
from the public historical record. They are credited here and never claimed; the
staging is original work. The nuclear testing at Bikini and Enewetak, the
displacement of the affected communities, and the long fight for justice are staged
soberly on the public record, with dignity and no graphic detail and taking no side.

The two defining personas, [the ri-meto](persona_ri_meto.md) and
[the lerooj](persona_lerooj.md), are archetypes, not historical individuals:
composites that carry the navigator's reading of the sea and the chiefly
clan-mother's keeping of the lineage. Darlene Keju ([persona_darlene_keju.md](persona_darlene_keju.md))
is credited on the public record for her public-health and anti-nuclear advocacy.
