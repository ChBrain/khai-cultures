# Ecuador: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_es.md](pitch_es.md) is written from Ecuador's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record without
entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Ecuador score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ----------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 78                      |
| Individualism                 | 8                       |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 63                      |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 67                      |
| Long Term Orientation         | n/a                     |
| Indulgence                    | n/a                     |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; Ecuador's Individualism score is
among the lowest in the published data, and two later dimensions are not published
for Ecuador.

## Languages

The play and the whole cast are written in Spanish (`es`), with only the
structural headings in English. The Kichwa-rooted persona
[persona_dolores_cacuango.md](persona_dolores_cacuango.md) carries Kichwa as her
mother tongue and Spanish as a carried tongue; Kichwa terms used in the culture
(Inti Raymi, sumak kawsay) are offered in good faith and best reviewed by a native
speaker.

## Historical sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn
from the public historical record. They are credited here and never claimed; the
staging is original work. The contradictions at the heart of the story - the
conquest, the social strata it left, the sierra and coast divide, and the long
wait of the indigenous nations for their place - are staged soberly on the public
record and without lurid detail, the gap between promise and reality.

The defining persona [el montubio](persona_el_montubio.md) is an archetype, not a
historical individual: a composite that carries the culture's living tension
between the courteous, hierarchical sierra of Quito and the open, mercantile coast
of Guayaquil, the coastal mestizo farmer of cacao and banana who shares the
nation's language with the highlander and yet talks past him.
