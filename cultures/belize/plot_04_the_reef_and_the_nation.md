## Action

The modern nation finds its living in its land and its sea. The barrier reef, the cayes, and the rainforest the world prizes, the jewel, become the country's draw, and tourism rises beside the older work of fishing and farming, the nation learning to live off the natural treasure it must also guard. Zee Edgell gives the plural country a literature, writing Belize into the world's hands and letting all a we read weself. CARICOM ties bind it to the Caribbean, the Guatemalan claim hangs on unresolved, and the country weighs how to hold its many peoples as one, secure its borders, and keep the reef and the forest whole against a warming world and a hungry market. A small nation, late-born and many-peopled, takes the measure of itself.

## Tension

The young nation reaches the measure of itself and finds it must guard weh it has on every side: the reef and the forest the world treasures, the jewel, loved by outsiders for weh they can take and worn by the loving; the plural society it must hold as one without flattening any of its peoples; and the security of a small country under a claim that will not lift. The treasure that draws the world and the smallness that must watch the world are weighed together, and the nation's whole task is to keep weh makes it, the many, the reef, the forest, while living off the very things it must protect.
