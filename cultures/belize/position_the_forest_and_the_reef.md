# Position: the forest and the reef

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The natural heritage that made the land and still defines it: the rainforest that the Maya knew and the British came to cut, and the sea before it. From the forest came logwood for the dye trade and then mahogany for the empire's furniture, the timber that built the colony and gave it its first economy - and gave it, too, the slavery on which that cutting was done, the wealth and the wound bound in the same wood. Before the coast lies the Belize Barrier Reef, the second-largest on earth, with its cayes, its atolls, and the deep blue eye of the Blue Hole. The land is small but rich beyond its size, a treasure of timber and coral and creature that the world has always wanted - and that the nation must now guard as carefully as it once spent.

## Orders

That the forest and the reef are the nation's true wealth and its trust, to be guarded and not merely sold; that the mahogany legacy is both pride and warning, the wood on the flag a reminder of what the cutting was built on; and that a small nation living off its natural beauty must keep it whole, because what draws the world to its shores can also be taken from them.

## Loses

Ground when the heritage is spent rather than kept, the forest cut and the reef worn for the quick return, so the treasure that drew the world is consumed to please it; ground, too, when the mahogany pride forgets the labor it was wrung from, the legacy honored without the reckoning, the wood on the flag emptied of the truth of how it was felled.

## Drives

Lives in [the mahogany cutter](persona_the_mahogany_cutter.md), the enslaved African and the Baymen whose work in the forest made the colony and carried its first deep wound. It drives Belize to guard the forest and the reef as the whole of what it has, and to hold the wealth and the wound of the wood in the same hand.
