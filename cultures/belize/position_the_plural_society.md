# Position: the plural society

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The reality of many peoples living side by side in one small land: Creole, the English- and Kriol-speaking descendants of the Baymen and the enslaved Africans; Garifuna, who landed on the southern shore with their own tongue, drum, and faith; Mestizo, who crossed from the north speaking Spanish; the Maya, who held the forest from before any colony; and the Mennonites, who came to farm in Plautdietsch and their own plain ways. Belize Kriol is the lingua franca that crosses every line, English the official tongue, and no single people the whole. It is a plurality grown over an old plantation order of color and origin, the hierarchy softened but not erased, the many living together more easily than almost anywhere - and never quite as equals.

## Orders

That the nation is the sum of its peoples and not the property of any one of them, so the Creole, the Garifuna, the Mestizo, the Maya, and the Mennonite are each fully Belizean; that difference is to be lived beside, not melted away, each people keeping its tongue, its faith, and its day; and that the small land's whole genius is the holding of the many as one without flattening any of them.

## Loses

Ground when the old hierarchy hardens back, when one people's Belize is taken for the only Belize and the others are made to feel guests in their own country; ground, too, when the easy plurality is mistaken for solved, the gap between the peoples' standing left unaddressed because they get along, so the warmth becomes a cover for the order beneath it.

## Drives

Lives in [Thomas Vincent Ramos](persona_thomas_vincent_ramos.md), who gave the Garifuna a day of arrival and remembrance and taught a people to be seen in the whole, and in [Zee Edgell](persona_zee_edgell.md), whose novels gave the plural nation a voice and held its many lives on one page. It drives Belize to keep being the place where the many are one without ceasing to be many.
