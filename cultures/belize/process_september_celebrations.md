# Process: the September Celebrations

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

The whole nation through the month of September: the flags going up, the streets dressed in red, white, and blue, the bunting and the bands, the carnival road march and the long patriotic season that runs from St. George's Caye Day on the tenth to Independence Day on the twenty-first. It takes only a calendar and a country proud of itself, and from the schoolchild's parade to the official ceremony the month becomes one long celebration of the nation.

## Direction

Toward patriotism and belonging: the season that binds the many peoples into one Belizean year. St. George's Caye Day recalls the 1798 battle that settled the coast as the Baymen's, and Independence Day marks the nation's late birth in 1981, the two together carrying the colony's founding and the country's freedom in a single month. It is the time the plural nation gathers as one people, every tongue and origin under the same flag.

## Lever

It is driven by a young, small nation's need to feel and show itself as one, and by the pride of a people who came late to nationhood and hold it dear. It holds against the smallness, the territorial claim, and the many divisions a plural country carries; against it stand the harder histories beneath the founding - the slavery behind the famous battle, and the question of whose celebration it most is.

## Echo

The September Celebrations make visible what [the Caribbean nation](position_the_caribbean_nation.md) needs: a season for a late-born, many-peopled country to stand up as one and claim itself. Through this month the work of [George Price](persona_george_price.md), who carried the colony to the independence the twenty-first now marks, is honored, the nation he named celebrating the freedom he won.
