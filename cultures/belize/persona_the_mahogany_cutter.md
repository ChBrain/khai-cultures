# Persona: the mahogany cutter

## Taxonomy

[the forest and the reef](position_the_forest_and_the_reef.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The woodcutter of the colony's first economy, the figure in whom Baymen and enslaved African are bound together at the saw: the man sent upriver in the rainy season to find and fell the great trees, to cut the logwood for the dye and the mahogany for the empire's tables, the labor that made British Honduras and the slavery that did the cutting. Skilled, endangered, and unfree, he knew the forest the Maya knew and worked it for masters across the sea. The ancestral African tongues are his mother tongues, the languages of the peoples carried across the Atlantic in bondage: in them he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md), and [hears](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) the home that was taken from him, the words of a continent the cutting was meant to make him forget. The English of the Baymen and the masters he [carries because he must](process_speaking_carried.md), the orders given in it and the work named in it, and from the meeting of his many African tongues with that imposed English the Creole of Belize is forged. The mahogany he felled crossed the sea to be read as the empire's wealth, while the tongues he carried were never written and were meant to die in his mouth - and so out of that gap at the limit of the tongue, the home languages lost and the master's language imposed, a wholly new tongue, Belize Kriol, was made by the cutters and the slaves themselves.

## Action

In [the Maya and the Baymen](plot_01_the_maya_and_the_baymen.md) he is the labor on which the colony is built, the enslaved and the Baymen cutting the logwood and mahogany that gave British Honduras its economy and its first deep wound. By his work in the forest he makes [the forest and the reef](position_the_forest_and_the_reef.md) the colony's whole wealth, and by his unfreedom he lays its hardest debt.

## Shadow

The figure who made the colony's wealth in chains is the nation's own shadow: the temptation to celebrate the mahogany and the Baymen and the famous battle while passing over the slavery that did the cutting, to put the tree on the flag and leave the cutter off it, honoring the wood and forgetting the hand.

## Tell

He goes upriver to fell a tree taller than a house for a table he will never sit at, in a forest his masters claim and his Maya neighbors knew first - and out of the African words he was forbidden and the English he was forced, he and his fellows make a new language entirely their own.
