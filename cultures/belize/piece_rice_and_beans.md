# Piece: rice and beans

## Taxonomy

Parent group: pieces

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Place

On every Belizean table from the city to the village, on the Sunday plate and the roadside stall: red kidney beans cooked into the rice with coconut milk, served with stew chicken, a slice of fried plantain, and a spoon of coleslaw, the dish that means home to a Belizean anywhere on earth. The national plate, distinct from the rice-and-beans-apart of the neighbors, the beans cooked in with the rice the Belizean way.

## Load Bearing

It stands for the plural nation gathered at one plate. In a country of Creole, Garifuna, Mestizo, Maya, and Mennonite, where each people keeps its own kitchen, rice and beans with stew chicken is the meal nearly all of them share, the common Sunday table of [the plural society](position_the_plural_society.md). Born of the coconut, the bean, and the rice the many peoples brought and grew, cooked in the Creole way that became everyone's way, it is the dish that crosses every line a small plural country draws, the food a Belizean abroad aches for and a Belizean at home expects. Without this plate the many peoples would have no common table to point to as theirs together.

## Apparent

A plate of rice cooked through with red beans and coconut milk, browned and savory, a piece of stewed chicken in its dark gravy beside it, plantain and slaw to the side. Plain, hearty, everyday food, cooked in every household its own slight way; its weight is in its ubiquity, the one dish a whole plural nation calls its own.
