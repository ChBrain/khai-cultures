# Plan: Under the Shade We Flourish

## Taxonomy

The open mandate the nation set itself in the motto beneath the mahogany tree on its arms, "Sub Umbra Floreo," to flourish under the shade: the standing work of holding the many peoples of [the plural society](position_the_plural_society.md) as one without flattening any of them, of securing [the Caribbean nation](position_the_caribbean_nation.md) late-born and small against the long claim at its border, and of guarding [the forest and the reef](position_the_forest_and_the_reef.md) the world prizes while the nation lives off them.

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Direction

Hold the many as one - keep the easy plurality from hardening back into the old hierarchy, so Creole, Garifuna, Mestizo, Maya, and Mennonite are each fully Belizean and none is made a guest; secure the nation, defending the late-won independence and the borders against the unlifted claim while keeping the peace with the neighbor; guard the forest and the reef, keeping the natural treasure whole against the warming sea and the hungry market, living off it without spending it; and reckon honestly with the wound beneath the wealth, honoring the mahogany without forgetting the hand that felled it.

## Orders

- To [the mahogany cutter](persona_the_mahogany_cutter.md): be remembered, not passed over - let the nation honor the labor and the slavery the colony was built on, and not the wood alone.
- To the voice like [Zee Edgell](persona_zee_edgell.md): keep writing the many lives of the nation, so the plural country can see and read itself and be seen by the world.
- To the nation: hold the many as one, secure what is small, and guard the reef and the forest, flourishing under the shade without spending the tree.

## Implementation

A young nation, independent only since 1981, that has held its many peoples in an uncommon peace and built a Caribbean identity in a Hispanic isthmus - and that still carries the Guatemalan claim unresolved, still works to make the easy plurality an equal one, and still must guard the reef and the forest against a warming world and a market that wants its piece, the smallness that is both its charm and its exposure never far from view.

## Targets

- [x] Won independence in 1981, the last British mainland territory to become a nation, English-speaking and Caribbean and its own.
- [x] Held the many peoples - Creole, Garifuna, Mestizo, Maya, and Mennonite - in an uncommon plural peace, Kriol the shared tongue.
- [x] Joined CARICOM and the Commonwealth and took its place in the Caribbean world.
- [W] The Guatemalan territorial claim hangs on unresolved, the small nation's security still to be settled.
- [W] The easy plurality is held but not yet made equal, the old hierarchy softened beneath it not gone.
- [ ] Guard the barrier reef and the rainforest whole against the warming sea and the appetite of the market the nation lives by.
- [ ] Reckon fully with the wound beneath the wealth, honoring the labor and the slavery the colony's mahogany was cut in.
