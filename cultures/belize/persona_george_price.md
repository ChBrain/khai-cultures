# Persona: George Price

## Taxonomy

[the Caribbean nation](position_the_caribbean_nation.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The father of the nation and its first Prime Minister, the quiet, ascetic, deeply Catholic son of Belize City who led British Honduras from colony to country and gave the late-born nation its own voice. Plainspoken, frugal, and famously private, walking the streets he governed and living without show, he built a movement, named a people Belizean, and carried them to the independence of 1981. English he has whole and uses with care: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in the mother tongue of the colony, the Kriol of the streets under the English of the lectern, and [writes](process_writing_drafted.md) the platforms and pleas of a nation being born in it. Spanish, of his Mestizo heritage and the isthmus around him, he [carries almost as his own](process_speaking_carried.md) and [follows on the page](process_reading_followed.md), a man of the English Caribbean equally at ease in the tongue of the mainland. He gave a Caribbean people their nationhood in the English of the empire he was leaving, and named them in the Kriol they actually spoke - and yet the new nation he made held Garifuna and Maya tongues he could not himself answer in, so that even in the founder's mouth there remained a gap at the limit of the tongue, the one Belize no single Belizean wholly speaks.

## Action

In [the Road to Independence](plot_03_the_road_to_independence.md) he leads the long passage from colony to nation, builds the movement that names a people Belizean, and brings the country to independence in 1981 as [the Caribbean nation](position_the_caribbean_nation.md) it had become. By giving the late-born country its own name and its own seat, he makes a nation where there had been only a colony, and leaves it to hold what he won.

## Shadow

The patient founder who builds a nation slowly carries the shadow of the slow road: the independence won late and under the unlifted claim, the compromises a small nation must make to be born at all, and the temptation to mistake the quiet getting-along of the many for the harder work of making them equal, leaving the next generation the part he could not finish.

## Tell

He walks the streets of the capital he governs without a guard, lives like a monk in a small room, and refuses the trappings of the office - the father of the nation who wanted the nation, not the throne.
