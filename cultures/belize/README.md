# Belize: a khai culture

Belize staged as a full khai play, ISO BZ, language en. The only English-speaking
nation of Central America, a small, late-born, many-peopled country between forest
and reef, as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the
roles its many tongues and faiths encode, its places, its practices, and its
defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_belize.md](play_belize.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Belize's profile (warmth and ease, a strong collective and family pull, a
  relaxed relation to authority and a high indulgence, set against the smallness
  and the plurality) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Maya and the Baymen, the Many Peoples, the Road to
  Independence, and the Reef and the Nation - each casting the elements it needs,
  and each staging the gap between the colony's wealth and its wound, and the
  promise and the reality of the plural land, honestly.
- **Personas:** George Price, Philip Goldson, Thomas Vincent Ramos, and Zee
  Edgell as the historic cast, plus one defining archetype, the mahogany cutter
  (the Baymen and the enslaved African whose labor in the forest made the colony
  and carried its first wound). Each is wired to the language engine by the
  language-crossing it holds - English and Kriol for most, the Garifuna tongue
  carried for Ramos, the ancestral African tongues carried from slavery for the
  cutter.
- **Positions:** the plural society (Creole, Garifuna, Mestizo, Maya, and
  Mennonite living side by side over an old hierarchy), the Caribbean nation (the
  English-speaking, late-born nation in a Hispanic isthmus, in CARICOM, under the
  Guatemalan claim), the forest and the reef (the natural heritage, the mahogany
  legacy and the barrier reef).
- **Places:** Belize City (the old colonial port and the crucible of the nation),
  the Barrier Reef (the second-largest reef on earth, the cayes, and the Blue
  Hole).
- **Processes:** Garifuna Settlement Day (the November reenactment of the Garifuna
  landing), the September Celebrations (St. George's Caye Day and Independence Day,
  the patriotic season).
- **Pieces:** rice and beans (the national plate, the common table of the plural
  society), the mahogany tree (the national tree on the flag, the wealth and the
  wound of the colony in one emblem).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-record historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources on the public record are credited, never
claimed; slavery, the logwood and mahogany economy, the plural society, and the
Guatemalan territorial claim are treated soberly and from within Belize's own
self-understanding; the staging and the architecture are original work.
