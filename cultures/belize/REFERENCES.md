# Belize: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-record historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md) is written from Belize's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
Belize is not always separately published in the Country Comparison tool; where it
is absent, the profile is read from the Caribbean and the close regional
neighbors. The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the
record without entering the prose.

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Belize is read from the published
Caribbean and regional profile where a separate Belize score is not available;
values are approximate and directional, as published by Hofstede Insights / The
Culture Factor Group.

## Historical sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn
from the public historical record. They are credited here and never claimed; the
staging is original work. The harder histories - slavery, the logwood and mahogany
economy, the plural society grown over an old hierarchy, and the long Guatemalan
territorial claim - are staged soberly, from within Belize's own self-understanding,
on the public record and taking no side.

The single defining archetype, [the mahogany cutter](persona_the_mahogany_cutter.md),
is not a historical individual but a composite: the Baymen and the enslaved African
bound together at the colony's first labor, carrying the wealth and the wound of
the wood and the making of Belize Kriol out of the African tongues and the imposed
English.
