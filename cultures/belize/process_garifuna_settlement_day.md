# Process: Garifuna Settlement Day

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

The Garifuna of the southern towns, Dangriga and Punta Gorda and Hopkins, on the nineteenth of November: the dugus rowed ashore at dawn with cassava and plantain and the drums going, the reenactment of the 1832 landing, and the day turned to drumming, dancing, feasting, and the long night of remembrance. It takes a people who will not be forgotten and a date set down by Thomas Vincent Ramos, and a whole nation now marks the morning the Garifuna came.

## Direction

Toward remembrance and recognition: the reenactment of the Garifuna arrival that makes a people's coming a nation's holiday. It carries the memory of a long passage from the Caribbean islands to the Belizean shore, keeps the Garifuna tongue, the punta drum, and the dugu rite alive against the pull of the years, and asserts before the whole plural country that the Garifuna are here, are Belizean, and arrived in their own way and their own time.

## Lever

It is driven by a small people's need to be seen and to hold their own, and by the day Ramos won them on the nation's calendar. It holds against the thinning of the mother tongue and the forgetting that comes for every small culture; against it stand the danger of the one-day festival, the rite kept for an afternoon while the language fades the rest of the year.

## Echo

Garifuna Settlement Day makes audible what [the plural society](position_the_plural_society.md) needs: a way for one of the many to be remembered and recognized without disappearing into the whole. On this morning [Thomas Vincent Ramos](persona_thomas_vincent_ramos.md) rows his people ashore again, the landing he set as a holiday repeated so the nation must watch the Garifuna arrive.
