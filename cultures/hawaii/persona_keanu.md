# Persona: Keanu

## Taxonomy

[Aloha spirit](position_aloha_spirit.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A forty-eight-year-old outrigger navigator on Kauai. English, the local [Hawaii English](position_language_en_us_hi.md) of the islands, is his mother tongue, the language in which he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and writes his daily navigation logs. Hawaiian [he wears as an overgarment](process_speaking_worn.md) during traditional canoe carving ceremonies, keeping alive the language of his ancestors. Yet the loss lies in the seam: The fast, academic talk of historians like Leilani and the digital planning under [the native forest plan](plan_native_forest_restoration.md) [he hears only like a distant echo](process_hearing_followed.md), feeling that the physical, sea-based wisdom of navigation is treated as secondary to museum archives.

## Action

He manages [outrigger carving](process_outrigger_carving.md), coordinates resource data under [the native forest plan](plan_native_forest_restoration.md), and studies [Hawaiian sovereignty](position_hawaiian_sovereignty.md).

## Shadow

His pride in traditional navigation and island independence can make him suspicious of state-wide zoning and tourist boating regulations, fearing they will restrict access to native forests and shoreline fishing grounds.

## Tell

He pulls a steering paddle out of the water and says directly: "The ocean doesn't look at political maps; you have to read the stars and waves to know where you are, no matter what boundary lines they draw."
