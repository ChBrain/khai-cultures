# Persona: Leilani

## Taxonomy

[Hawaiian sovereignty](position_hawaiian_sovereignty.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A fifty-two-year-old cultural historian in Honolulu. English, the local [Hawaii English](position_language_en_us_hi.md) of the islands, is her mother tongue, the language in which she [speaks, hears, and thinks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), the defining code of her professional life. Hawaiian [she wears as an overgarment](process_speaking_worn.md) during archival research, keeping alive the language of her ancestors. Yet the loss lies in the seam: The slower, nature-bound sea talk of outrigger navigators like Keanu and the environmental planning under [the native forest plan](plan_native_forest_restoration.md) [she reads only as historic manuscripts](process_reading_followed.md) on her terminal, feeling that the physical, active legacy of the islands is being replaced by commercialized tourism and museum displays.

## Action

She preserves [traditional lei](piece_traditional_lei.md) archives, coordinates community history forums under [the native forest plan](plan_native_forest_restoration.md), and studies [Aloha spirit](position_aloha_spirit.md).

## Shadow

Her focus on academic research and royal archives can make her elitist, overlooking the immediate material struggles of working-class families outside the historic centers.

## Tell

She looks at a historic royal decree and says thoughtfully: "Sovereignty isn't just about political borders; it's a commitment to preserve the stories and language that define the community's unique cultural identity."
