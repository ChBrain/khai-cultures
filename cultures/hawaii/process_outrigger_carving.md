# Process: outrigger carving

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

Hawaiian outrigger canoe builders, traditional navigators, and community apprentices, conducted year-round in coastal workshops.

## Direction

In the carving shed. Selecting native koa timber, rough-shaping the hull with adzes, sanding the wood to a smooth finish, and joining the outrigger float (_ama_) with lashed crossbeams (_iako_).

## Lever

The traditional woodworking process that preserves ancient voyaging technology and cultural identity. It supports local craftsmen and youth programs, though it is limited by the scarcity of mature native koa logs.

## Echo

The canoe carving requires spiritual focus and ceremony. It drives the daily work of [Keanu](persona_keanu.md) on Kauai, while cultural historian [Leilani](persona_leilani.md) values the craft as a preservation of ancient engineering.
