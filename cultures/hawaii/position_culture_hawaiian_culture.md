# Position: Hawaiian culture

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

Descent from the Polynesian navigators who crossed thousands of miles of open ocean by star and swell to settle these islands, and with it a bond to the land, the _ʻāina_, that outsiders learn only slowly and clumsily. It carries the [Aloha spirit](position_aloha_spirit.md), the communal hospitality and ecological stewardship built into island life, alongside the harder memory of [Hawaiian sovereignty](position_hawaiian_sovereignty.md), the kingdom overthrown by American sugar interests in 1893 and never fully restored. It speaks [Hawaii English](position_language_en_us_hi.md) as its everyday tongue, while carrying the Hawaiian language and its concepts, _ʻohana_, _mālama_, as an inheritance kept alive through ceremony and revival rather than daily habit for most.

## Orders

That one shares resources and welcomes the stranger with genuine kindness, not performance; that one protects sacred sites, native forests, and shoreline waters as kin rather than as scenery; that one supports the revival of the Hawaiian language and the return of ancestral lands; and that community harmony and stewardship of the land are weighed as heavily as any individual gain.

## Loses

Its footing when commercialized tourism reduces aloha to a marketing gimmick, pricing out the very residents it claims to welcome; ground between the physical, sea-based wisdom of navigators and the archival, academic register of historians, each hearing the other's work only as a distant echo; and unity when factionalism within the sovereignty movement distracts from the shared goals of land return and environmental protection.

## Drives

It drives the reading of stars and currents that once carried voyaging canoes across the open Pacific, and it drives today's advocates to protect sacred land, revive the native tongue, and press for the kingdom's story to be told in full. It shapes a people who measure resilience not by conquest but by care, holding fast to ancestral custom even as the islands are reshaped again and again by outside arrivals.
