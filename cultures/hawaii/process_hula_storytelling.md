# Process: hula storytelling

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

Hula instructors (_kumu hula_), dancers, and chanter groups, conducted year-round in schools (_hālau_) and community assemblies.

## Direction

On the dance platform (_kahua_). Memorizing genealogical chants (_oli_), executing precise hand and foot gestures that depict natural elements or historic events, and dancing to rhythmic percussion.

## Lever

The sacred performance process that preserves native history, genealogy, and connection to the land. It supports cultural tourism and community education, though it faces commercial distortion in tourist shows.

## Echo

The hula chants preserve historical records. The process defines the public presentations coordinated by [Leilani](persona_leilani.md) in Honolulu, while navigator [Keanu](persona_keanu.md) respects the storytelling as a validation of ancestral lines.
