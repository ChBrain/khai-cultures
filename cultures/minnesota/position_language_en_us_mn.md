# Position: Minnesotan English

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The flat, friendly Upper Midwest speech of the Land of 10,000 Lakes, the raised monophthong of the long "o", the clipped "you betcha" and "oh, for sure" of the porch and the ice house. It carries the Scandinavian and German settlement cadence of the Northwoods, a mild, understated diction that leans away from confrontation and into the polite reserve of Minnesota nice.

## Orders

Where it passes as home ground: the Boundary Waters outfitter counter, the Iron Range union hall, the Twin Cities lake-district block, the church basement hotdish supper. It orders which vowels and turns of phrase mark a speaker as of this north country and sets the mild, cooperative register as the local measure.

## Loses

Ground to General American, the unmarked national standard the screen and the corporate cleanroom carry, which levels the raised vowels and the folksy tags toward the flat national middle. The fast, tech-oriented speech of the medical-alley boardroom also crowds the slow Northwoods drawl toward the margin, treated as quaint rather than current.

## Drives

It carries [Lars](persona_lars.md), whose slow, flat midwestern drawl is the working English of the wilderness safety briefing, and [Samantha](persona_samantha.md), whose articulate, fast-paced cadence holds the same native tongue on the Minneapolis cleanroom floor. So the one north-country speech shows who keeps the season-bound wilderness and who turns toward the technical breakthrough.
