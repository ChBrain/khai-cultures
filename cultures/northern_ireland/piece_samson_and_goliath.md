# Piece: Samson and Goliath

## Taxonomy

Parent group: pieces

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Place

Standing over the Harland and Wolff shipyard at the head of Belfast Lough, the two great yellow gantry cranes that can be seen from across the city and the hills behind it, named Samson and Goliath after the giants.

## Load Bearing

They stand for the shipbuilding pride of the whole city, a civic landmark that belongs across both communities rather than to one. Raised in the 1970s on the slipways that built the great liners of [the shipyards and the Titanic](plot_03_titanic.md), they mark the place where Belfast made the finest ships in the world. Without this shared, non-sectarian emblem the city's common pride would lack its plainest sign.

## Apparent

Two enormous yellow box-girder cranes on the skyline, lettered with their names, visible for miles. There is nothing of one tradition or the other about them; they are simply the city's, a working landmark that the whole place claims.

## Yearbook

- Raised in the early 1970s over the Harland and Wolff yard that built the great liners.
- Named Samson and Goliath, after the giants, and visible across the whole of Belfast.
- Kept and listed as a shared civic landmark and emblem of the city's industrial pride.
