# Process: Gaelic games

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

The Gaelic Athletic Association and the nationalist, Catholic community, who each week gather around the club for Gaelic football, hurling, and camogie. It needs only the parish club, the pitch, and the volunteers who keep it to carry its own tradition.

## Direction

Toward the communal sporting life of one tradition: the matches of a Sunday, the club at the heart of the parish, the county colours, and the bringing-up of the young in the games and often the language of their community. It is, for those who keep it, a knot of belonging, place, and identity.

## Lever

The wish of the [nationalist](position_nationalism.md) community to hold and to live its own tradition drives it, carried by the parish club and the amateur volunteer. Against it stands the question of how a deeply rooted communal game is read by the other tradition, and the day is best kept when its welcome is open and its pride is its own rather than a banner against a neighbour.

## Echo

Gaelic games make the nationalist tradition visible to itself and to its neighbours, the sporting and communal life of [Niamh](persona_niamh.md) and her community, a tradition that is at its finest when it is a celebration of one people's own and not a marker set against the other.
