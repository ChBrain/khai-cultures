# Play: Northern Ireland

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the house that holds this production and answers for it.

## Name

Northern Ireland, the small north-eastern corner of the island of Ireland, where the green of the hills meets the grey of the lough, a place of two cities, two flags, and two traditions. ISO GB-NIR, language en, with Irish and Ulster-Scots in its cast.

## Arc

A small place shaped by two peoples who share one ground and have long read its story in two ways: a unionist, Protestant, British tradition that holds to the union, the Crown, and the marching season, and a nationalist, Catholic, Irish tradition that holds to an Irish identity, the language, and the games. Out of plantation, partition, and the long grief of the Troubles, neither community could make the other go away, and so, at last, they did the harder thing: they sat down together. The 1998 Agreement did not end the difference; it gave the difference a way to live without the gun. Beneath it all runs one nerve - that two traditions can be held in one small place with equal dignity, and that the wee country can be home to both at once.

## Triggers

**[Plot 1: The Plantation of Ulster 1609](plot_01_plantation_1609.md)**

The Crown settles Scots and English Protestants on the lands of Ulster, planting in [Derry/Londonderry](place_derry_londonderry.md) - whose newer name comes from the London livery companies - the two peoples whose long story becomes both [unionism](position_unionism.md) and [nationalism](position_nationalism.md).

**[Plot 2: Partition and the founding of Northern Ireland 1921](plot_02_partition_1921.md)**

Ireland is partitioned; a new state is founded with its seat at [Belfast](place_belfast.md), built to secure the [unionist](position_unionism.md) majority, while a large nationalist minority is left within the new border.

**[Plot 3: The shipyards and the Titanic](plot_03_titanic.md)**

At the height of its industry [Belfast](place_belfast.md) builds the greatest ships in the world at Harland and Wolff, and the [great cranes](piece_samson_and_goliath.md) rise over a civic pride shared across both communities.

**[Plot 4: The Troubles and the Good Friday Agreement 1998](plot_04_good_friday_1998.md)**

After three decades of the Troubles, [John Hume](persona_hume.md) and [David Trimble](persona_trimble.md) lead their traditions to the table, and [the peace](position_the_peace.md) of the Good Friday, or Belfast, Agreement is settled by consent of both communities.

## Stakes

Whether two traditions that share one small place - unionist and nationalist, British and Irish, Protestant and Catholic - can hold their difference with equal dignity and keep faith with the hard-won settlement they made together, so that the wee country stays home to both at once.
