# Plan: parity of esteem

## Taxonomy

A mandate: the deliberate effort the peace set in motion to give both traditions' tongues official standing, so that language might stop being a weapon and the [two tongues](position_the_two_tongues.md) become culture rather than colours.

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Direction

To make good on the peace's promise of parity of esteem in language: Irish and Ulster-Scots both recognised, funded, and let to live, so that neither community has to win at the other's expense, and the tongues join rather than divide.

## Orders

- To the Assembly: legislate for and fund both Irish and Ulster-Scots, and hold the parity the agreements promised.
- To both communities: let the other's tongue live without reading it as a threat.
- To the state: deliver the recognition that [the peace](position_the_peace.md) pledged, not as a favour to one side but as a right of both.

## Implementation

The Good Friday and St Andrews commitments to language; the long-stalled Irish Language Act; the New Decade New Approach deal of 2020; and the Irish and Ulster-Scots bodies, with their rival fundings and contested signage.

## Targets

- [x] The peace settlements promised parity of esteem and recognition for both traditions.
- [W] The Irish Language Act stalled for years and helped collapse the Assembly: language made a weapon, not a bridge.
- [x] New Decade New Approach (2020) at last legislated for both Irish and Ulster-Scots.
- [ ] Let both tongues live as culture rather than serve as flags, so language joins where it has long divided.
