# Northern Ireland: a khai culture

Northern Ireland staged as a full khai play, ISO GB-NIR, language en. A small
place of two traditions as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as
the cast, the roles its languages encode, its places, its practices, and its
defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch. It is a constituent nation culture
of the United Kingdom, staged with deliberate, even-handed balance between its two
communities.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_northern_ireland.md](play_northern_ireland.md), the culture
  itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_nir.md](pitch_nir.md), the Hofstede layer. Northern Ireland
  has no separate Hofstede profile, so the pitch is written from the Great Britain
  (UK) base it inherits (wit and warmth over a hard history, resilience, and a
  fragile, hard-won peace) and names no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Plantation of Ulster (1609), partition and the founding
  of Northern Ireland (1921), the shipyards and the Titanic, and the Troubles and
  the Good Friday Agreement (1998) - each casting the elements it needs, with the
  two communities given equal weight throughout.
- **Personas:** John Hume and David Trimble as the historic cast, the nationalist
  and the unionist leaders honoured jointly with the 1998 Nobel Peace Prize, plus
  two defining archetypes, Niamh (the nationalist, Catholic tradition) and Davy
  (the unionist, Protestant tradition). The cast is written across English, Irish,
  and Ulster-Scots, and each persona is wired to the language engine by the
  language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** unionism (the union, the Crown, the marching tradition),
  nationalism (an Irish identity, the language, the GAA), and the peace (the 1998
  power-sharing settlement made by consent), with unionism and nationalism given
  equal and equally respectful framing.
- **Places:** Belfast (the capital and the shipyards) and Derry/Londonderry (the
  second city, named in both traditions' tongues at once).
- **Processes:** the Twelfth (the Orange Order parades of the unionist community)
  and Gaelic games (the GAA life of the nationalist community), framed evenly.
- **Pieces:** Samson and Goliath (the shipyard cranes) and the Ulster fry, both
  shared, civic, and non-sectarian.

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch (the inherited Great Britain base, with no
Northern Irish sub-score claimed) and the public-domain historical sources are

staged with even-handed balance between its two traditions.
