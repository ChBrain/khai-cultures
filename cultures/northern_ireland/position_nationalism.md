# Position: nationalism

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The standing of the tradition that holds Northern Ireland's place to belong with the rest of Ireland. Descended from the Gaelic Irish of the land, mostly Catholic, it keeps to an Irish identity, the hope of a united Ireland reached by the consent of the people, the Irish language, and the Gaelic games of the GAA. It is one of the two peoples who share this ground, and it carries an identity that is Irish as surely as the other is British.

## Orders

That one keep faith with an Irish identity, hold the language and the games in honour, and work for a united Ireland by agreement rather than by force. Belonging to the Irish nation and to one's own community come together, and the link with the rest of the island is to be cherished as the ground of who one is.

## Loses

Ground where the hope for unity narrows into a refusal of the other tradition, where the games and the language are turned into a banner against a neighbour rather than one community's own life, and where past grievance forgets the dignity owed to the people one would share a future with. Nationalism loses when it pursues its aim at the cost of the consent and respect that any shared ground requires.

## Drives

It is carried by [John Hume](persona_hume.md), who held that its aim must be reached by argument and consent, and by [Niamh](persona_niamh.md), who keeps the Irish language and the Gaelic games alive in her community.
