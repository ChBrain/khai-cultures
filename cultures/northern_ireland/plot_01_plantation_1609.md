# Plot: The Plantation of Ulster 1609

## Taxonomy

[play_northern_ireland](play_northern_ireland.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

After the flight of the old Gaelic earls, the lands of Ulster lie open to the Crown. A scheme is drawn up to settle Protestant colonists from Scotland and England on the ground, alongside the Gaelic Irish who already live there.

## Action

From 1609 the Plantation brings Scots and English Protestant settlers into Ulster, while the Gaelic Irish, mostly Catholic, remain on and around the land. In [Derry/Londonderry](place_derry_londonderry.md) the London livery companies fund the building of the walled city and give it the newer of its two names. From this single ground grow the two long traditions that this place still holds: the settler and his descendants, who become [unionism](position_unionism.md), and the native and his, who become [nationalism](position_nationalism.md).

## Stage

- [Derry/Londonderry](place_derry_londonderry.md): the walled city of the Plantation, named by the London companies.
- [unionism](position_unionism.md): the tradition descended from the Protestant settlers of Ulster.
- [nationalism](position_nationalism.md): the tradition descended from the Gaelic Irish of the land.

## Tension

Two peoples are now planted on one ground, each with its own faith, name, and memory of how it came to be there. The Plantation does not erase either; it lays down, side by side, the origin of both communities, and with it the long question of how the two are to share a single place.
