# Persona: David Trimble

## Taxonomy

[unionism](position_unionism.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The unionist leader and lawyer who took the harder risk of his career for peace: a sharp, sometimes prickly defender of the union who came to believe that the way to secure it was not to refuse the table but to sit at it. Rigorous and combative in argument, he led the largest unionist party of his day to a settlement many of his own community feared. He [speaks, hears, and thinks in](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) the plain mother tongue of his people, the public English of unionist Ulster, sharp and combative in argument. The harder risk of his life was to turn that arguing tongue from refusal to negotiation, to [weigh and concede words](process_writing_drafted.md) at a table many of his own feared: there is the gap, the same English that rallied his people to say No he bends, against his own grain, to a guarded Yes, and pays for every word with their trust.

## Action

In [the Good Friday Agreement 1998](plot_04_good_friday_1998.md) he carries [unionism](position_unionism.md) to meet constitutional nationalism, accepting power-sharing and the principle of consent in return for the union resting on the will of the majority. He becomes the first First Minister of the new power-sharing government, the institutions of [the peace](position_the_peace.md), and shares the Nobel Peace Prize with [John Hume](persona_hume.md), the two honoured together for a peace neither tradition could have made alone.

## Shadow

The risk he took split his own ground beneath him: many unionists felt he had conceded too much, his party was steadily eroded by harder-line rivals, and the leader who helped make the peace was, within a few years, turned out by the very community he had tried to bring with him. The price of the settlement, for him, was his own political base.

## Tell

He argues every point to the last and then, when it matters most, chooses the settlement over the refusal, securing the union by consent rather than by saying no.
