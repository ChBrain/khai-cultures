# Position: unionism

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The standing of the tradition that holds Northern Ireland's place within the United Kingdom. Descended from the Protestant settlers of Ulster, it keeps to the union with Britain, the Crown, the Covenant signed against home rule, and the marching tradition of the Orange Order. It is one of the two peoples who share this ground, and it carries an identity that is British as surely as the other is Irish.

## Orders

That one stand by the union, keep faith with the Crown and the reformed faith, and hold the marching season and its memory in honour. Loyalty to Britain and to one's own community come together, and the link with the rest of the United Kingdom is to be defended as the ground of who one is.

## Loses

Ground where loyalty narrows into a refusal of the other tradition, where the marching season is read as triumph over a neighbour rather than as one community's own day, and where the certainty of the majority forgets the dignity owed to the minority beside it. Unionism loses when it secures its place at the cost of the respect that lets two peoples share one ground.

## Drives

It is carried by the leadership that risked its standing to secure the union by consent, and by the everyday keeping of the Ulster-Scots tongue and the marching tradition alive within the community.
