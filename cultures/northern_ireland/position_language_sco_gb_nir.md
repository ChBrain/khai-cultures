# Position: Ulster-Scotch

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The pouer o the braid Ulster-Scotch, the leid the Lawland planters brocht ower the watter an that yet leeves in the kintra pairts o Antrim an Doun: the sister o the Scots o Scotland, wi its ain words an its ain souch, hauden for the merk o the Protestant, unionist fowk. It cairries the warmth o the ferm, the kirk an the marchin band, the tongue a bodie spaeks amang their ain kin.

## Orders

Whit passes for guid Ulster-Scotch amang the planter fowk, the auld words an the gate o speakin brocht ower frae the Lawlands lang syne, an the claim that it staunds as a leid in its ain richt an no as bad English or a ploy for siller. It orders that the tongue be kent for a badge o the unionist tradeetion, an says, in the same breith as the Erse forenenst it, whilk side o the hoose a body cams frae.

## Loses

Grund tae the ilkaday English that baith fowk speak, an tae the pairty stramash that maks a fitba o the leid, the rival fundin an the argie ower whether it's a hale language ava. It dwines doun tae a wheen words an a register, hauden mair for a token o wha ye are nor spak as a hale tongue, an it tholes the jaloosin o thae that tak it for naething but politics riggit oot in an auld coat.

## Drives

It lives in [Davy](persona_davy.md), the aulder unionist man that hauds the braid Ulster-Scotch for the grun unner aathing else an cairries it, wi pride an nae ill wull, as the merk o his ain fowk. It drives the province tae read a side intae ilka word, sae that een the tongue a bodie spaeks is a takin o pairts atween the twa tradeetions.
