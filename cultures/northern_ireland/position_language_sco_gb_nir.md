# Position: Ulster-Scotch

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The tongue haes its ain wittins for things Inglis haes tint the auld wurds for, bairn for a bairn, ken for tae ken, thole for tae thole an bide wi somethin sair, a hantle o wurds wi the auld Germanic ring that Inglis chynged awa lang syne. It cairries forbye the byordinar wee, that can be hung on near ony noun tae mak it couthie or smaa.

## Orders

It bids the speaker fasten the negative straucht ontae the verb insteid o a saeparate wurd, cannae, dinnae, wisnae, sae the no is aye pairt o the verb itsel an no a wurd a bodie can drap; an it haads the auld Scots vowel-law, whaur the lenth o a vowel is set by the soond that follaes it, sae the same letter can soond lang or short dependin on whit comes efter.

## Loses

It canna aye haud its ain agin the ilkaday English that dwines the auld wurds oot o the young anes' mooths, an the fecht ower whether it's a hale leid or juist a wheen auld wurds taks awa the mense a fu grammar wad gie it. The couthie wee an the auld fastened negatives dwine tae token bits o colour raither nor pairts o a leevin tongue.

## Drives

The habit o fastenin the no ontae the verb gars the speaker haud negation an action thegither in ae braith insteid o twa; an the auld, hamely wurds gar the speaker cairry a fund o kin-words that mark ilka sayin as frae the same rist. It gars the ane that speaks it hae an ear for the auld soonds athin the new.
