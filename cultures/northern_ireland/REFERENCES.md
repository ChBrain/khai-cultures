# Northern Ireland: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_nir.md](pitch_nir.md) is written from Great Britain's profile on
Geert Hofstede's model of national culture - Northern Ireland is a constituent
nation of the United Kingdom and has no separate Hofstede profile, so the national
(Great Britain) scores are the documented base - and it names no dimension and
carries no number. The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on
the record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Great Britain score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ----------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 35                            |
| Individualism                 | 89                            |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 66                            |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 35                            |
| Long Term Orientation         | 51                            |
| Indulgence                    | 69                            |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published
by Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group. No Northern Irish sub-score is
claimed; the national (Great Britain) profile is the documented base.

## Languages

The play and most of the cast are written in English (`en`). Two personas are
authored in their community's own tongue: [persona_niamh.md](persona_niamh.md) in
Irish (`ga`) and [persona_davy.md](persona_davy.md) in Ulster-Scots (`sco`). The
Irish of Niamh and the Ulster-Scots of Davy are the authors' own composition,
offered in good faith and best reviewed by a native speaker.

The two defining personas [Niamh](persona_niamh.md) and [Davy](persona_davy.md)
are archetypes, not historical individuals: composites that carry the two living
traditions - the younger nationalist, Catholic woman of the Irish language and the
Gaelic games (Niamh) and the older unionist, Protestant man of the Ulster-Scots
tongue and the marching tradition (Davy).

This culture is staged with deliberate, even-handed balance between its two
traditions, unionist and nationalist, British and Irish, Protestant and Catholic,
giving each equal dignity and equal space, endorsing neither, and presenting the
1998 settlement as the shared achievement it was.
