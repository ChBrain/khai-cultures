# Plot: Partition and the Founding of Northern Ireland 1921

## Taxonomy

[play_northern_ireland](play_northern_ireland.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

As Ireland moves toward independence, the question of Ulster cannot be settled to the satisfaction of both traditions at once. A border is drawn, and the island is partitioned.

## Action

In 1921 Northern Ireland is founded as a self-governing region within the United Kingdom, with its parliament and seat at [Belfast](place_belfast.md), and a Protestant and [unionist](position_unionism.md) majority secured within its border. The same border leaves a large Catholic and nationalist minority on the northern side, inside a state to which many of them do not feel they belong. Partition gives the unionist tradition the settlement it sought and leaves the nationalist tradition a grievance it will carry for generations.

## Stage

- [Belfast](place_belfast.md): the seat of the new region's parliament and government.
- [unionism](position_unionism.md): the tradition for whom the new border secures the union.

## Tension

The border answers one tradition's hope and another's fear at the same time. It makes Northern Ireland a place with a built-in majority and a substantial minority, each measuring the new state against a different idea of where it ought to belong - the unresolved question that the later decades will inherit.
