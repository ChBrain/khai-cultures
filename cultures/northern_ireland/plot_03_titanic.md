# Plot: The Shipyards and the Titanic

## Taxonomy

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

By the turn of the twentieth century [Belfast](place_belfast.md) is one of the great industrial cities of the world, and the Harland and Wolff shipyard on Queen's Island is among the largest anywhere.

## Action

The yard builds the finest liners afloat, and in 1911 it launches the Titanic, the largest moving object made by human hands to that date. Thousands of workers fit her out under the cranes of the lough; the ship is lost on her maiden voyage in 1912, but the skill that made her is real, and the [great gantry cranes](piece_samson_and_goliath.md) that later stand on the spot become the city's emblem. The shipyards are a civic pride that reaches, for a moment, across both communities, even as the workforce reflects the divisions of the wider place.

## Stage

- [Belfast](place_belfast.md): the industrial city and the lough where the great ships are built.
- [Samson and Goliath](piece_samson_and_goliath.md): the gantry cranes that stand for the city's shipbuilding pride.

## Tension

The shipyards show the place at its most confident and most shared: a city that built the world's greatest ship. Yet the work is woven into the same fault line as everything else, so that even the proudest chapter carries, beneath its civic glory, the question of who built it and who got the work.
