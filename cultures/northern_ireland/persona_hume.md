# Persona: John Hume

## Taxonomy

[nationalism](position_nationalism.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The schoolteacher from the Bogside who became the patient architect of peace: a constitutional nationalist from [Derry/Londonderry](place_derry_londonderry.md) who held, through three decades of violence, that the difference between the two traditions was to be resolved by argument and consent, never by the gun. Steady, dogged, and unglamorous, he repeated the same case until it was heard. He carries the words and the cause of his people across to the other tradition and to the world, a tongue [carried](process_speaking_carried.md) to where it needs to go.

## Action

In the long road to [the Good Friday Agreement 1998](plot_04_good_friday_1998.md) he leads constitutional [nationalism](position_nationalism.md) to the table, talks to those others would not talk to, and stakes his standing on [the peace](position_the_peace.md), a settlement made by agreement of both communities. For this work he shares the Nobel Peace Prize with [David Trimble](persona_trimble.md), the two of them honoured together for a peace neither tradition could have made alone.

## Shadow

The patience that made the peace possible cost him dearly: the talks he held with those still tied to violence drew bitter criticism, his own health and party were worn down by the effort, and the slow, principled path looked to many, for years, like weakness. The man the world later honoured was, in his own day, often attacked from every side.

## Tell

He says the same thing, calmly, until the violence wears itself out against it, and chooses the agreement that both can live with over the victory that only one could claim.
