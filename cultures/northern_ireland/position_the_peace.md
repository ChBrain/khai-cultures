# Position: the peace

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The standing of the settlement made in 1998, the shared achievement that belongs to neither tradition alone. Built on power-sharing between unionist and nationalist, on the principle that the constitutional question rests on the consent of the people, and on cross-community reconciliation, it is the ground on which the two peoples agreed to govern together rather than to fight. It holds the difference between the traditions without asking either to surrender it.

## Orders

That the two traditions share power and govern together, that change come only by consent, and that each community grant the other the dignity it claims for itself. The settlement asks for a daily, deliberate choice to keep faith with it, and for the patience to hold a rival hope by agreement rather than by force.

## Loses

Ground where the parties withdraw from sharing power, where old grievance is allowed the last word, and where either tradition treats the peace as a defeat to be undone rather than a settlement to be kept. The peace loses whenever the harder work of governing together is abandoned for the easier comfort of standing apart.

## Drives

It is carried together by [John Hume](persona_hume.md) and [David Trimble](persona_trimble.md), the nationalist and the unionist who led their communities to the table and were honoured, jointly, for the settlement neither tradition could have made alone.
