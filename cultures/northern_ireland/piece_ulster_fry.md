# Piece: the Ulster fry

## Taxonomy

Parent group: pieces

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Place

On the plate in every kitchen and cafe across the place, the cooked breakfast of bacon, egg, and sausage with its two local breads, the soda farl and the potato bread, fried golden on the griddle.

## Load Bearing

It stands for the everyday life that both communities share without a thought. The fry knows nothing of the line that runs through the place; it is eaten the same in every home and every greasy-spoon, and the soda and potato bread that mark it out are made the same on both sides. Without this plain, shared thing the culture would lack its picture of the ordinary ground the two traditions hold in common.

## Apparent

A heaped plate of bacon, sausage, egg, and the two fried breads, with maybe black or white pudding and a fried tomato. There is nothing of one tradition or the other about it; it is simply what breakfast is here, in any house you walk into.

## Yearbook

- The cooked breakfast of the whole place, marked out by the soda farl and the potato bread.
- Made and eaten the same across both communities, in home and cafe alike.
- Carried as the plainest emblem of the everyday life the two traditions share.
