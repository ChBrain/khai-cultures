# Place: Derry/Londonderry

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Shown

The second city, on the banks of the Foyle, ringed by the great Plantation walls that have never been breached. It carries two names at once - Derry from the old Irish for the oak grove, and Londonderry from the London livery companies that built it - and the double name is itself the emblem of the two traditions that share the place. It is the home city of [John Hume](persona_hume.md).

## Holds

- [the Plantation of Ulster 1609](plot_01_plantation_1609.md): here the walled city is built and named by the London companies.

## Offers

The clearest sign that this is a place of two peoples and two namings, where the same ground is held and remembered in two tongues at once, and where, in its walls and its civil-rights memory, the whole story can be read in miniature.

## Withheld

A single, agreed name. The city refuses to be only Derry or only Londonderry, and in holding both it withholds the comfort of pretending that one tradition's word can stand for the place alone.
