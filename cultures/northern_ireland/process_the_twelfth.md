# Process: the Twelfth

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

The Orange Order and the unionist, Protestant community, who each year on the Twelfth of July mark the day with parades, banners, flute bands, and the bonfires of the night before. It needs only the lodges, the bands, and the streets of a community gathering to keep its own tradition.

## Direction

Toward the public expression of one tradition's history and identity: the marching of the lodges, the music of the bands, the colour of the banners, and the gathering of a community around its own calendar. It is, for those who keep it, a day of belonging, faith, and remembrance.

## Lever

The wish of the [unionist](position_unionism.md) community to hold and to show its own tradition drives it, carried by the long custom of the lodges. Against it stands the question of route and respect, since a parade through shared ground touches the dignity of the other tradition, and the day is best kept when that is held in mind.

## Echo

The Twelfth makes the unionist tradition visible to itself and to its neighbours, the marching season of [Davy](persona_davy.md) and his community, a day that is at its finest when it is a celebration of one people's own and not a triumph over the other.
