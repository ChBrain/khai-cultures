# Afghanistan: a khai culture

Afghanistan staged as a full khai play, ISO AF, language fa (Dari Persian). The
ancient crossroads of Asia at the heart of the Silk Road, staged as a theatre:
its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its language and
faith encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a
single pitch. It is staged strictly from within its enduring cultural
self-understanding: the Silk Road heritage, Persian poetry and Sufism, the great
cities, hospitality, the mountains, music, craft, and the peoples.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_afghanistan.md](play_afghanistan.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_fa.md](pitch_fa.md), the tonal layer, written from
  Afghanistan's enduring cultural profile (the warmth and duty of hospitality,
  the honour code, deep reverence for elders and the word, a poetic and Sufi
  sensibility, and the patient hardiness of mountain life) and naming no
  dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Silk Road crossroads (ancient Balkh and the caravan
  routes), the flowering of Persian poetry and Sufism (Rumi of Balkh and Ansari
  of Herat), the founding of modern Afghanistan (the gathering of the peoples
  under one name in 1747), and the age of poetry and resilience (the living
  tradition carried into the modern day). History is staged soberly; any upheaval
  is acknowledged neutrally and with dignity, with no detail.
- **Personas:** Jalaluddin Rumi (born in Balkh), Khwaja Abdullah Ansari (the sage
  of Herat), Ahmad Shah Durrani (the founder of the modern state, who was also a
  poet), and Khalilullah Khalili (the great modern Dari poet) as the historic
  cast, plus one archetype, the Host (the mehman-dost of any of the peoples, who
  keeps the door open to every traveller). Each is wired to the language engine by
  the relationship to the mother tongue it holds.
- **Positions:** hospitality (melmastia, the guest as a gift of God and the open
  door), poetry and Sufism (the poetic soul of the land, from Rumi and Ansari
  onward), and the mountains (the hardiness, freedom, and endurance the Hindu Kush
  shapes).
- **Places:** Balkh (the mother of cities and the cradle on the Silk Road) and
  Herat (the cradle of art, calligraphy, and the garden).
- **Processes:** buzkashi (the national horseback game of the highlands) and tea
  and the guest (the welcoming of the traveller with naan and green tea).
- **Pieces:** the carpet (the hand-knotted craft and Silk Road good) and the
  rubab (the national instrument of the country's music).

## Provenance

The public-domain heritage sources behind the plots, personas, places, pieces,
and the cultural profile behind the pitch are recorded in
[REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the pitch is its
invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never claimed; the
culture is staged strictly on enduring ground and takes no political side, with
no contemporary politics or conflict in scope; the staging and the architecture
are original work.
