# Afghanistan: References

The provenance behind this culture: the enduring cultural profile that tunes the
pitch (kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain heritage sources
behind the plots, personas, places, and pieces. The culture is staged strictly
from within Afghanistan's enduring cultural self-understanding, on the public
record, taking no political side; contemporary politics and conflict are out of
scope and appear nowhere.

## Cultural profile behind the pitch

The pitch [pitch_fa.md](pitch_fa.md) is written from Afghanistan's enduring
cultural profile and names no dimension and carries no number. The profile is
recorded here so the basis is on the record without entering the prose.

| Trait                  | Afghan cultural profile (qualitative)                                                 |
| ---------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Hospitality and honour | Very high; the guest is a gift of God, the open door and the honour code (melmastia). |
| Kinship and elders     | Strong; family, tribe, and the word of elders carry great weight.                     |
| Poetic sensibility     | Deep; poetry and Sufism are woven into everyday speech and feeling.                   |
| Endurance              | High; the patient hardiness and freedom of mountain life.                             |
| Plurality of peoples   | Many peoples (Pashtun, Tajik, Hazara, Uzbek, and others) under one name.              |

Source: the broadly documented, public-record cultural traditions of Afghanistan
(the code of hospitality and honour, the Persian and Dari literary heritage, and
the customs of its peoples), as described in standard reference and ethnographic
literature. The profile is qualitative and the prose never names it.

The one archetype persona, [the Host](persona_mizban.md), is not a historical
individual but a composite that carries the enduring custom of melmastia: the
mehman-dost of any of Afghanistan's peoples, who keeps the door open to every
traveller and treats the guest as a gift of God.
