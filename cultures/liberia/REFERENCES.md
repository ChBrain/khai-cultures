# Liberia: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md) is written from Liberia's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record without
entering the prose. Where Liberia is not separately published, the profile draws on
Hofstede's West African regional estimates.

| Dimension                     | Liberia / West Africa score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 77                                    |
| Individualism                 | 20                                    |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 46                                    |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 54                                    |
| Long Term Orientation         | 16                                    |
| Indulgence                    | 78                                    |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; where Liberia is not separately
listed, the regional West Africa estimates stand in, showing a strongly collective,
hierarchical, and warm profile.

## Historical sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn
from the public historical record. They are credited here and never claimed; the
staging is original work. The two heritages, the freed and free-born Black American
settlers and the older indigenous peoples, are given equal dignity, and the secret
societies and their masks are treated with respect.

The one defining archetype, [the market woman](persona_market_woman.md), is not a
historical individual but a composite that carries the warmth, strength, and deep
heritage of the country side as it comes into the city, and the everyday work of
binding the two heritages around the pot and the market table.
