# Position: the societies

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The deep institutions of the older peoples that raise a child into a person: the Poro for the men and the Sande for the women, the schools of the bush that have shaped the Kpelle, the Vai, the Gola, the Mende and their neighbours for longer than any record. In them the young are taught the law, the craft, the medicine, the dance, and the carving, brought through their growing under the eye of the elders, and given the masks that carry the spirits of the land, the great wooden faces treated with honour and never as mere show. They are the wisdom of the country side, the way one generation hands the next its name and its worth.

## Orders

That the indigenous heritage carries its own deep law and its own way of making a person, owed the same dignity as any school or church; that the elders hold the knowledge and pass it down, the old before the young, the way the country has always run; that the masks and the medicine are sacred things, not curiosities, and are spoken of with respect; and that the country side and its societies are a glory of the nation, not a thing to be left behind for the coast.

## Loses

Ground when the indigenous institutions are looked down on as backward by those who came later, when the bush school is counted as less than the book school, and the great heritage of the interior is treated as a thing to grow out of rather than a thing to honour. It loses, too, whenever the dignity of the masks and the elders is forgotten and the deep things of the country side are made into mere spectacle.

## Drives

Lives in the script that a Vai inventor set down to give his people's deep tongue writing of their own, and in the market woman, the daughter of the soil who carries the everyday wisdom and warmth of the older heritage to the city. It drives Liberia to honour the societies, the elders, and the deep law of the country side as a full and equal part of the one nation.
