# Persona: George Weah

## Taxonomy

[the love of liberty](position_love_of_liberty.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The boy from the Monrovia waterside who kicked a ball on the dust until his feet carried him to the biggest fields in Europe, the only African ever named the best footballer in the whole world, and who then came home and rose to the highest office in the land. A son of the soil out of a poor quarter, raised by his grandmother, he carried the name of Liberia high before the eyes of the world when his small country needed a thing to be proud of, my people. English he has whole, and the Liberian Kolokwa with it: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in his mother tongue, the easy warm [Liberian English](position_language_en_lr.md) of the waterside, the talk of the street and the stadium. The [Italian](../italy/position_language_it_it.md) and the [French](../france/position_language_fr_fr.md) of the great clubs where he made his name he [carried as working tongues](process_speaking_carried.md), enough to play, to lead, to live in those far cities, and [followed the press in them](process_reading_followed.md) as a stranger learning to read a new place. He spoke the world's game in his feet, where every tongue understands a goal, and answered in three languages on three fields far from home, yet it was always in his mother tongue that he spoke to his own people, and at the edge of every borrowed tongue stood the gap that only the home talk closes, the place where you are finally, fully, understood. He belongs to [the Liberian way](position_culture_the_liberian_way.md) as the son of the waterside who carried the Lone Star's pride to the whole world and brought it home again.

## Action

In [one country, one people](plot_04_one_country_one_people.md) he carries the name of Liberia before the whole world and stands for [the love of liberty](position_love_of_liberty.md) and the dignity of a small Black nation, a son of the soil risen as high as a Liberian can go. By making the world cheer for a poor boy from the waterside, he hands his country a pride that belongs to every quarter of it, settler and son of the soil alike.

## Shadow

The boy who rose from nothing to everything is the dream of a country that has known hard times, and the danger in that dream is to wait for one great man to lift the whole nation, rather than to do the slow work, small small, that lifts a people together; the star can shine so bright the common road is left dark behind it.

## Tell

He won the world's highest honour in the game on fields far from home and brought the cheer back to a dusty Monrovia quarter, the son of the soil who made the whole earth say the name of Liberia out loud.
