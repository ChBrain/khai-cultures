# Liberia: a khai culture

Liberia staged as a full khai play, ISO LR, language en. The nation that two
peoples built together, the freed and free-born Black Americans who came back for
liberty and the older peoples who were always here, staged as a theatre: its
history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its languages and its
faith encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a
single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house that
holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its Estate;
a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_liberia.md](play_liberia.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Liberia's profile (warm and collective, people before paper, family and town
  before the self, deep respect for elders and faith, and an unhurried, small
  small patience) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the older homeland, the settlers come back, the republic of
  1847, and one country one people, each casting the elements it needs, and each
  giving the settler and the indigenous heritage equal dignity.
- **Personas:** Joseph Jenkins Roberts, Edward Wilmot Blyden, Momolu Duwalu
  Bukele, and George Weah as the historic cast, plus one defining archetype, the
  market woman, the daughter of the soil who carries the warmth of the interior to
  the city. Each is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** the love of liberty (freedom as the reason of the place and the
  motto on the seal), one nation from two (the long stitching of settler and son
  of the soil), the societies (the Poro and the Sande and the deep heritage of the
  country side, treated with dignity).
- **Places:** Monrovia (the capital and the meeting of the two heritages), Cape
  Mount (the Vai homeland and the older heritage of the land).
- **Processes:** cooking the pot (the kitchen that binds the many into one),
  the football match (the common joy that makes the whole nation one crowd).
- **Pieces:** the Lone Star (the flag of the first free Black republic), the Vai
  script (one of Africa's own writings and the dignity of the indigenous heritage).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the settler and the indigenous heritages are given equal dignity, the
secret societies and their masks treated with respect, and the staging and the
architecture are original work.
