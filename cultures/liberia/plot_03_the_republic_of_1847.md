# Plot: the republic of 1847

## Cue

By the eighteen forties the settlement on the cape has grown into a standing community with its trade, its schools, and its churches, but it has no recognized state of its own, and the powers along the coast deal with it as they please. The settlers decide the time has come to stand the place up as a nation in its own right, ya hear.

## Action

In 1847 the settlers declare Liberia a free and independent republic, the first one the African continent ever knew, with a constitution, a seal that reads the love of liberty brought us here, and the Lone Star raised over it all. [Joseph Jenkins Roberts](persona_roberts.md) is made the first president and carries himself before the courts of Europe so that they must deal with a free Black nation as an equal. A free African state stands on its feet for the world to see, a light the whole continent looks toward, while at home the long work of [one nation from two](position_one_nation_from_two.md) is only just begun, the freedom proclaimed for all not yet shared in full with every son of the soil.

## Stage

- [Joseph Jenkins Roberts](persona_roberts.md): the first president who raises the Lone Star and stands the republic up.
- [the love of liberty](position_love_of_liberty.md): the motto cut into the seal, the reason of the state made law.
- [Monrovia](place_monrovia.md): the capital of the new republic and seat of its government.

## Tension

A free Black republic stands up where none had before, the great pride of the continent, and yet the freedom it proclaims is, in the early going, lived more fully by some of its peoples than by others. The republic is born both as a glory and as an unfinished promise, carrying from its founding the task of making the love of liberty belong, in full, to settler and son of the soil alike.
