# Persona: Joseph Jenkins Roberts

## Taxonomy

[the love of liberty](position_love_of_liberty.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The trader from Virginia who crossed the water as a young free-born Black man and rose to be the first president of the first republic Africa ever stood up, the man who raised the Lone Star in 1847 over a free Black nation. Dignified, careful, a merchant who knew the ledger and a statesman who knew the wider world, he carried himself so that the powers of Europe would have to deal with a free African state as an equal, my people. English he has whole and shaping: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in his mother tongue, the settlers' American English that would become [Liberian English](position_language_en_lr.md), and [writes](process_writing_mother_tongue.md) the proclamations and the letters of a young state in it, the very tongue carried back across the same ocean his people had been carried over the other way. The tongues of the older peoples whose land the new republic touched, the Vai and the Bassa and the Dei, he [catches only by ear](process_reading_caught.md), a greeting here, a name there, and never makes his own. He raised a free Black nation on the words of liberty in the language of the country his people had fled, while the older nations all around him spoke deep tongues he could not enter, and so even at the founding of the free land the gap stood there at the limit of the tongue, between the man on the ship and the man of the soil. He belongs to [the Liberian way](position_culture_the_liberian_way.md) as the man who gave the love of liberty a state to stand in, my people, even before the state stood equally for all.

## Action

In [the republic of 1847](plot_03_the_republic_of_1847.md) he becomes the first president and raises the Lone Star, giving [the love of liberty](position_love_of_liberty.md) a state to stand in and proving to the world that a Black nation could govern itself. By standing the first African republic on its feet, he hands Liberia both its great pride and the unfinished work of making the freedom he proclaimed belong to every one of its peoples.

## Shadow

The free state he founded was first a state of the settlers, and the dignity he won abroad did not yet reach every son of the soil at home: the love of liberty proclaimed for all was, in the early going, lived more fully by some than by others, the gap between the motto and the daily fact there from the founding.

## Tell

He stood before the courts of Europe as the head of a free Black republic and made them deal with him as an equal, and in the same breath left his country the long task of making the man of the interior as free as the man who came on the ship.
