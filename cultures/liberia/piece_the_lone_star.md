# Piece: the Lone Star

## Taxonomy

Parent group: pieces

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Place

Over the government house and the schoolyard, on the football shirt and the coffin, in the harbour and on the seal of the state: eleven red and white stripes for the men who signed the declaration, and a single white star on a blue field in the corner, the lone star of the first free Black republic on the African continent. It flies over Monrovia, it travels on every ship that ever sailed under it, and it is saluted, raised, and loved as the very face of the nation.

## Load Bearing

It stands for the whole nation and the reason it was made: a free Black state, the one light on the continent when no other was standing, the love of liberty turned into a flag. In a country woven from two heritages and many tongues, the Lone Star is the one body all can point to, settler and son of the soil alike, the proof that a Black nation governed itself and held its head up before the world. It carries the pride of being Africa's first republic and the long hope that the freedom it promises will belong, in full, to every one of its peoples. Without this banner the nation made of many would have no single face to gather under.

## Apparent

A rectangle of dyed cloth, red, white, and blue, stripes and a single star sewn or printed, run up a pole or stitched to a shirt. In itself it is just fabric; its whole weight is in what a people who crossed an ocean for freedom have poured into it, and in the hands that raise it over the first free republic the continent ever knew.

## Yearbook

- Raised in 1847 when Liberia declared itself a free and independent republic, the first on the African continent.
- Made the single lone star for a single free Black nation, the eleven stripes for the men who signed the declaration of independence.
- Carried on ships the world over and flown over every hard time the country has known, the emblem a people salute when, whatever falls, Liberia gets up and carries on.
