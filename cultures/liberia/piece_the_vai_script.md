# Piece: the Vai script

## Taxonomy

Parent group: pieces

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Place

In the old letters and ledgers of Cape Mount, in the books of the Vai elders, and on the printed pages that now carry it to the world: a syllabary of many signs, each one a sound of the Vai tongue, devised by Vai hands in the early eighteen hundreds, one of the few writings the African continent ever made for itself from the ground up. It lives where the Vai keep their own record, the deep tongue of a people of the soil set down in letters that owe nobody.

## Load Bearing

It stands for the full dignity of the older heritage and the genius of the country side: the proof that an African people, with no borrowed alphabet, could devise its own letters and keep its own knowledge in its own hand. In a country where the settlers' English would spread widest, the Vai script is the standing answer that the deep tongues of the soil are no less worthy, that the man of the interior has a glory of his own. It carries the honour of the indigenous heritage as a full and equal part of the one nation, and the truth that Liberia was lettered before the ship as well as after.

## Apparent

Rows of small, distinct signs, curves and strokes and dots, each one a syllable of the Vai language, written by hand or set in type. In itself it is just marks on a page; its whole weight is in what it proves, that a people of the soil reached into the gap where a deep tongue had no letters and set its own writing there.

## Yearbook

- Devised by the Vai people of Cape Mount in the early eighteen hundreds, the signs said to have come to its maker in a dream and worked out town by town.
- Made one of the very few scripts the African continent ever created for itself from the ground up, a full syllabary for the Vai tongue.
- Carried still by the Vai elders and now studied the world over, the country side's own answer to how a people keeps its word.
