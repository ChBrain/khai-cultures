# Position: the love of liberty

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The very reason the place was made, cut into the seal of the state: the love of liberty brought us here. It is the conviction that freedom is the whole point of Liberia, that the settlers crossed the water for it and that the country exists to hold it. It is the name itself, Liberia, the land of the free, and the single white star on the blue, the lone star of a free Black nation lit when no other on the continent was standing. And it is a love founded in a long road, freedom claimed by a people whose grandfathers had been carried the other way across the same ocean.

## Orders

That liberty is the measure of the place, the thing every Liberian is owed and the thing the country answers for; that a free man is a free man whether his people came on the ship or were here from before; that the stranger is welcomed and the weak are not trampled, because a land made for freedom cannot make any of its own less than free; and that the Lone Star stands for the dignity of a Black nation governing itself, a light the whole continent once looked toward.

## Loses

Ground when the love of liberty is kept for some and not for all, when the freedom the settlers came for is not fully shared with the countryman who was always here; and ground, too, when the motto becomes only words on a seal, recited at the ceremony and forgotten in the living, so that the great name of the place outruns the daily fact of it.

## Drives

Lives in the first president, who raised the Lone Star over Africa's first republic and gave the love of liberty a state to stand in, and in the son of the soil who carried the name of Liberia high before the whole world and stood for the dignity of his people. It drives Liberia to hold freedom as the reason for the place and to keep the promise of the motto bigger than the seal.
