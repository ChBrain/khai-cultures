# Place: Cape Mount

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Shown

The headland in the west where the green hill stands over the wide lagoon and the long beach runs to the sea, the homeland of the Vai people who have lived by the water there longer than any record. A country of fishing and farming and old towns, where the elders keep the Poro and the Sande, where the masks are carved with honour, and where, in the early eighteen hundreds, a people gave the world one of Africa's own scripts.

## Holds

- [the older homeland](plot_01_the_older_homeland.md): the Vai homeland by the lagoon where the script is made and the older peoples hold the land.
- [one country, one people](plot_04_one_country_one_people.md): one of the many towns and tongues woven, small small, into the one nation.

## Offers

A place that shows the deep, older heritage of the land in full dignity: the societies that raise a child into a person, the masks that carry the spirits, and above all the Vai script, the proof that an African people could devise its own letters and keep its own record. It offers the country side as a glory of the nation, equal and whole, not a thing to be left behind.

## Withheld

The simple story that the nation began with the ship. Cape Mount holds the truth that the land was long home before any settler came, that the son of the soil has a claim as old as the hills, and that the one nation can only ever be made by giving the older heritage its full and equal place. It withholds the easy telling and offers the honest one.
