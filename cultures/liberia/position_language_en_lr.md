# Position: Liberian English

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The English carried back across the water and made Liberia's own. The settlers brought their American English over the same ocean their grandfathers had been carried across the other way, and on its words of liberty they stood a republic, the first on the continent. Laid over the many tongues of the older peoples, the Kpelle and the Bassa, the Vai and the Grebo and the rest, it spread as the one tongue that belonged to no single people and so could be shared by all, until it bent into Kolokwa, the warm Liberian talk of the waterside and the market. It is the tongue of the proclamation and the ledger and the tongue of the stall and the stump, the bridge on which the man from the ship and the man of the soil could at last meet and be one country.

## Orders

That the English of Liberia be a bridge and not a wall, the shared tongue that lets many peoples be one nation with none made a guest for the tongue he was born to; that it carry the name of Liberia out into the world with dignity, and still be spoken warm and easy at home in the Kolokwa of the street; and that it stay the living talk of a free people and not harden into the paper tongue of the seal, recited at the ceremony and cold in the mouth.

## Loses

Ground when the settlers' English is kept for some and set as a fence against the man of the interior, so the bridge tongue divides where it was meant to join; ground when the deep country tongues thin in the city Kolokwa until a grandchild cannot fully answer a grandmother, and the whole warmth of a mother town waits at the limit of the tongue; and ground when it is left only the language of the office and the record, the great words of liberty outrunning the daily fact of the place.

## Drives

Lives in [Joseph Jenkins Roberts](persona_roberts.md), the first president, who raised the republic on the words of liberty in the settlers' English, and in [Edward Wilmot Blyden](persona_blyden.md), the greatest mind of the young nation, who gave it a scholar's standing and yet reached only across a distance for the deep tongues of the soil. It is carried into the world by [George Weah](persona_weah.md), the son of the waterside born to its warm Kolokwa, and carried as a selling tongue by [the market woman](persona_market_woman.md), whose own mother tongue of the interior stays at home while the market talk feeds the city. And it stood as the wider written tongue that spread past the Vai signs of [Momolu Duwalu Bukele](persona_bukele.md), who held to the script born for his own mother tongue and would not take it up. So the one bridge tongue shows who was born to it, who carries it, and whose own deep tongue it never reached.
