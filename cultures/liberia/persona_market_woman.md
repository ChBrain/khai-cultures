# Persona: the market woman

## Taxonomy

[the societies](position_the_societies.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The woman at the stall in the Monrovia market, the ma with the basin on her head and the baby on her back, who came from the country side to the city and feeds half the town from her little table of greens and pepper and dry fish. Strong past telling, warm, loud, quick with a joke and quicker with a blessing, she is the backbone nobody thanks and everybody needs, raised in the Sande, carrying the wisdom of the elders and the warmth of the soil straight into the heart of the capital, my people. Her mother tongue of the interior, the Kpelle or the Bassa or the Lorma of her town, she still has whole: she [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [hears](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) it with her own people, the tongue of her ma and her Sande sisters. The [Liberian English](position_language_en_lr.md), the Kolokwa, she [carries as her selling tongue](process_speaking_carried.md), the talk of the market where every people meets, and the Mende and the Vai and the Gola of her neighbours at the next stall she [catches by ear](process_hearing_followed.md), enough to bargain and to laugh. She feeds the whole city in a tongue not her first, and the deep words of her mother town she will not pass whole to a child raised in the Monrovia talk, so even at her own busy table stands the gap at the limit of the tongue, the home language thinning as the country comes to the coast.

## Action

In [one country, one people](plot_04_one_country_one_people.md) she carries [the societies](position_the_societies.md) and the warmth of the soil into the city market, the daughter of the interior feeding the capital and making, stall by stall, the one nation out of the many. She carries the country into the coast in her basin and her talk, and binds the two heritages with the plainest thing there is, the food on the table.

## Shadow

The strong woman who holds up the market and the home gets the least of the country's fair share, and the deep mother tongue she carried from her town thins in the city talk until a grandchild cannot fully answer a grandmother; the warmth that feeds everyone can be the thing the country leans on and never lifts.

## Tell

She blesses you in her mother tongue, prices the pepper in Kolokwa, and bargains with the next stall in a third, three tongues across one little table, and dreams of her town in the deep one she is slowly losing.
