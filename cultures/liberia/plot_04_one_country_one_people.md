# Plot: one country, one people

## Owner

## Cue

The republic stands, but the nation is not yet one. Across the years that follow, Liberia must do the slow, patient work of binding the settler and the son of the soil into a single people, and of carrying the name of a small African nation out into a wide world that did not always look its way.

## Action

The scholar [Edward Wilmot Blyden](persona_blyden.md) teaches the whole African world that the African heritage is a glory to be claimed, and argues that settler and son of the soil are one people of one country, giving [one nation from two](position_one_nation_from_two.md) its mind and its argument. The interior and the coast grow toward each other, small small, in the kitchen and the market and the football field, the country tongues and the coast English mixing into the warm Kolokwa all share. And a boy from a poor Monrovia quarter, [George Weah](persona_weah.md), kicks his way to being the best footballer in the whole world and carries the name of Liberia high before the eyes of the earth, a son of the soil whose pride belongs to every quarter of the land. The two heritages keep stitching into one, my people, the work never finished but always carried on.

## Stage

- [Edward Wilmot Blyden](persona_blyden.md): the scholar who argues the settler and the son of the soil are one people.
- [George Weah](persona_weah.md): the son of the soil who carries the name of Liberia before the whole world.
- [one nation from two](position_one_nation_from_two.md): the long stitching of the two heritages into one people.
- [Cape Mount](place_cape_mount.md): the Vai homeland, one of the many towns and tongues woven into the one nation.

## Tension

The one nation is made not at a stroke but small small, across the kitchen, the market, the field, and the long argument that both heritages are equal and whole. The work is never quite finished, the seam never wholly closed, and the whole hope of the place rests on a warm, tough people carrying it on, holding that Liberia, whatever comes, will get up and remain one country and one people.
