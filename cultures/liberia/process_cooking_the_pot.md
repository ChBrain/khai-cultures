# Process: cooking the pot

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

Any Liberian woman with a fire, a big pot, and people to feed, my people: the ma starting the rice before light, the market woman setting up her greens and pepper, the family gathering for the Sunday pot of palm butter or cassava leaf or check rice and jollof, with the dry fish and the pepper and the palm oil going in, and a place at the table for whoever passes by hungry.

## Direction

Toward warmth, belonging, and the binding of the two heritages into one: the kitchen is where the country side and the coast meet on one plate, where the rice and the palm and the cassava of the older peoples sit beside the dishes the settlers brought, and where the stranger is fed before he is even asked his business. It is not just eating but the daily proof that one nation can be made out of many, small small, around a single pot.

## Lever

It is driven by the deep Liberian law that people come before paper and the table is the heart of the home, and by the warmth that cannot let a person go hungry in the house. It holds against hard times and lean seasons, the big pot stretched to feed one more; against it stand the burden carried mostly by the women, the strong ma who feeds everyone and is thanked by no one.

## Echo

Cooking the pot makes audible what [one nation from two](position_one_nation_from_two.md) needs: a daily place where settler and son of the soil sit at one table and the many become one. Over this pot [the market woman](persona_market_woman.md) carries the warmth of the soil into the heart of the city, feeding the whole town from her little table.
