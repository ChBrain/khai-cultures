# Position: one nation from two

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The long work that makes Liberia one country out of two great heritages: the settlers, the freed and free-born Black Americans who sailed back across the water, and the older peoples who were always here, the Kpelle, Bassa, Gio, Mano, Kru, Vai, Grebo and the rest, each with its own tongue and town and law. It is the truth that the nation was not one to start, that the man on the ship and the man of the soil came to it from different roads, and that the whole story of the place is the patient stitching of the two into a single people who share one flag, one kitchen, and increasingly one way of talking.

## Orders

That both heritages carry equal dignity, the settler who came for liberty and the countryman who was always home, neither counted above the other; that the country belongs as much to the interior as to the coast, as much to the Vai by the water as to the families of Monrovia; and that being Liberian is the thing all share, made out of many tongues and many towns, the one growing small small from the two until you cannot say where the seam runs.

## Loses

Ground when one heritage is held above the other, when the settler line is treated as more Liberian than the son of the soil, or the country tongue counted as less than the coast English; and ground when the seam is denied instead of stitched, the old difference papered over rather than honestly worked, so that the wound is hidden but not healed.

## Drives

Lives in [Edward Wilmot Blyden](persona_blyden.md), the scholar who taught that the African heritage was a glory to be claimed and not a thing to be left behind, and who bound the settler and the African back into one. It drives Liberia to keep weaving the two heritages into one people, sharing the pot, the field, and the flag, until the nation is whole.
