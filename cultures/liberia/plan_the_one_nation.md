# Plan: the one nation

## Taxonomy

The open mandate the country set itself from its founding: the long work of weaving the settler and the son of the soil into [one nation from two](position_one_nation_from_two.md), of keeping [the love of liberty](position_love_of_liberty.md) the living reason of the place and not just words on a seal, and of honouring [the societies](position_the_societies.md) and the deep heritage of the country side as a full and equal part of the whole.

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Direction

Stitch the two heritages into one people, giving the settler and the son of the soil the same dignity and the same fair share; keep the love of liberty real in the daily living and not only on the ceremony; honour the indigenous heritage, the tongues, the societies, and the Vai script, as a glory of the nation and not a thing to be left behind; and carry the name and pride of Africa's first republic on, so that whatever falls, Liberia gets up and remains one country and one people, ya hear.

## Orders

- To the son of the soil like [the market woman](persona_market_woman.md): carry the warmth and wisdom of the country side into the whole nation, and claim your full and equal place in it.
- To the one risen high like [George Weah](persona_weah.md): carry the name of Liberia with pride, and pull not only yourself but the whole people up, small small.
- To the nation: keep stitching the two heritages into one, keep the love of liberty bigger than the seal, and honour every tongue and town of the land as wholly Liberian.

## Implementation

A republic founded in 1847, the first the African continent ever knew, that has carried the Lone Star through pride and through hard times and remains one country still. It has done great work, a free Black nation standing, the two heritages mixing in the kitchen and the market and the field, the indigenous heritage honoured and studied, and it leaves the work open still, in the old seam between settler and son of the soil, in the fair share not yet wholly shared, and in the long task of making the love of liberty belong, in full, to every people of the land.
