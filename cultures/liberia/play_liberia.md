# Play: Liberia

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the house that holds this production and answers for it.

## Name

Liberia, a small country on the Atlantic coast of West Africa, sitting between Sierra Leone, Guinea, and Cote d'Ivoire, with Monrovia for its capital and the Lone Star flying over it. ISO LR, language en, the English of the settlers laid over the Kpelle, Bassa, Gio, Mano, Kru, Vai, Grebo and the other peoples who were here long before. A land of thick forest and long beach, the first republic on the African continent, with a name that means freedom and a motto that says plain why anybody came at all.

## Arc

Liberia is the nation that two peoples built together, my people, and it carries both in its name. Long before any ship, the country was already home: the Kpelle and the Bassa in the inland forest, the Kru and the Grebo working the surf, the Vai by Cape Mount with a writing all their own, the Gio and the Mano in the highlands, each people with its own tongue, its own town, its own Poro and its own Sande to raise its children right. Then in the early eighteen hundreds came the others, freed and free-born Black men and women out of America, sailing back across the same water their grandfathers had been carried over, looking for a place to be free. They landed on this shore, they named the cape Mesurado and the town Monrovia, and in 1847 they stood up and declared a republic, the first one Africa ever knew, with a single white star on a blue field and a motto cut into the seal: the love of liberty brought us here. The settler and the countryman did not always pull the same way, that is the honest truth, but small small the one nation grew from the two, the kitchen and the language and the football field mixing them together until you cannot tell where the one heritage ends and the other starts. Under it all runs one nerve, ya hear: a stubborn, warm, God-fearing people who hold that liberty is the whole point, that the stranger is fed before he is asked his business, and that whatever falls down, Liberia get up again and carry on.

**[Plot 4: one country, one people](plot_04_one_country_one_people.md)**

Across the years that follow, scholars like [Edward Wilmot Blyden](persona_blyden.md) and sons of the soil like [George Weah](persona_weah.md) carry Liberia out into the world, while at home the settler and the countryman keep growing, small small, into [one nation from two](position_one_nation_from_two.md).

## Stakes

Whether a small country that two peoples built together, the freed who came back for liberty and the older nations who were always here, can keep weaving the settler heritage and the indigenous heritage into [one nation from two](position_one_nation_from_two.md) without either side losing its dignity; whether it can keep [the love of liberty](position_love_of_liberty.md) the living reason of the place and not just a motto on a seal; and whether, after every hard time, Liberia can get up, dust off, and carry the Lone Star on, ya hear.
