# Maryland: a khai culture

Maryland staged as a full khai play, ISO US-MD, language en. The Old Line State: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its Toleration Act, Star-Spangled Banner defense, and Chesapeake Bay clean water policies encodes, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house that holds this culture and answers for it.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_maryland.md](play_maryland.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_maryland.md](pitch_maryland.md), the Maryland key.
- **Plots (history):** the Toleration Act of 1649, the Battle of Baltimore in 1814, and the Chesapeake Bay Clean Water Act of 1983.
- **Personas:** Ann (Chesapeake Bay waterman) and David (biomedical vaccine scientist), written in English.
- **Positions:** chesapeake stewardship (estuary customs/coastal protection), biotech innovation (medical excellence/academic discovery).
- **Places:** Fort McHenry, Chesapeake Bay.
- **Processes:** crab potting (traditional crab trapping), vaccine synthesis (cleanroom biochemistry).
- **Pieces:** a crab pot, a laboratory pipette.
- **Plan:** the oyster plan.
