# Persona: David

## Taxonomy

[biotech innovation](position_biotech_innovation.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A thirty-six-year-old vaccine scientist in Baltimore. English, spoken with an articulate, fast-paced academic cadence, is his mother tongue, the language in which he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and writes his research reports. What he speaks is [Maryland English](position_language_en_us_md.md), the quick urban register of the Baltimore research corridor. He speaks no foreign languages. Yet the two Marylands sit right next to each other and barely talk: the slower, nature-dependent water talk of crabbers like Ann and the traditional water quality histories of the Eastern Shore [he hears only like a distant echo](process_hearing_followed.md), while the physical legacy of the estuary gets treated as a secondary constraint next to the push for the next medical breakthrough. He holds [the Old Line State](position_culture_old_line_state.md) in the hand that still runs an assay in the cleanroom.

## Action

He manages cleanroom plans, utilizes data under [the oyster plan](plan_chesapeake_oyster_restoration.md), and studies [biotech innovation](position_biotech_innovation.md).

## Shadow

His focus on research grant funding and cleanroom productivity metrics can make him indifferent to the municipal wastewater infrastructure disinvestment in outlying coastal counties.

## Tell

He looks at a laboratory monitor and says directly: "We are designing micro-scale vaccine delivery systems to protect global health; you can't optimize regional medical research if you're only focused on traditional shoreline preservation."
