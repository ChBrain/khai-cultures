# Uruguay: a khai culture

Uruguay staged as a full khai play, ISO UY, language es. A small, secular, modest
republic that guards a rare equality and decency and punches far above its size,
as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its
society encodes, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by
a single pitch. The prose is in Spanish; only the structural headings are in
English.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_uruguay.md](play_uruguay.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_es.md](pitch_es.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Uruguay's
  profile (understated, secular, ironic, modest, equal and unpretentious, warm but
  reserved) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** la Banda Oriental (the contested land, Artigas's federal
  revolution, and the independence of 1828), el Uruguay Batllista (the early
  twentieth-century reforms and the secular welfare state), la Garra y la Gloria
  (the football epics, the first World Cup of 1930 and the Maracanazo of 1950), and
  el Pais Pequeno y Grande (the modern small secular nation) - each casting the
  elements it needs.
- **Personas:** Jose Gervasio Artigas, Jose Batlle y Ordonez, Obdulio Varela, and
  Eduardo Galeano as the historic cast, plus one defining archetype, el oriental
  (the secular, modest everyman, mate in hand on the rambla). Each is wired to the
  language engine by the language-crossing it holds; the Afro-Uruguayan dimension
  is carried in the candombe drum, the ancestral African tongue the word lost and
  the drumhead kept.
- **Positions:** el Estado laico y solidario (the secular welfare state and
  equality, "la Suiza de America"), la garra charrua (the fighting spirit beyond
  size, football pride), la patria oriental (Artigas's federal-agrarian founding
  ideal and the continental conscience).
- **Places:** Montevideo (the rambla and the port capital that holds half the
  country), la pradera (the rolling interior of estancias and gauchos).
- **Processes:** el mate (the gourd carried everywhere and shared all day), las
  Llamadas (the Afro-Uruguayan candombe drum parade of Carnival).
- **Pieces:** el tambor de candombe (the chico, repique, and piano drums of African
  heritage), la celeste (the sky-blue jersey, emblem of the football epics).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-record historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources on the public record are credited, never
claimed; Uruguay is staged from within its own self-understanding, taking no side,
and the twentieth-century dictatorship, where touched, is named soberly via memory
alone. The staging and the architecture are original work.
