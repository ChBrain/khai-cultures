# Albania: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_sq.md](pitch_sq.md) is written from Albania's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no
number. The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the
record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Albania score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ----------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 90                      |
| Individualism                 | 20                      |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 80                      |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 70                      |
| Long Term Orientation         | 61                      |
| Indulgence                    | 15                      |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Albania's Hofstede data is approximate
and limited, as the country is not part of the original survey set and its scores
are estimated and less firmly established than for most countries; the figures
above are published estimates by Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group and
are used only to tune the pitch.

The two defining personas, [Drita](persona_drita.md) and [Ardit](persona_ardit.md),
are archetypes, not historical individuals: composites that carry the tension
between the older generation (the highland kanun and besa, sacred hospitality,
religious harmony, the mother tongue) and the younger one (the post-1991 boats to
Italy and Greece and the pull of those tongues on a language alone on its branch).
