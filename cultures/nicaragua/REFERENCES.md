# Nicaragua: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_es.md](pitch_es.md) is written from Nicaragua's cultural profile
and names no dimension and carries no number. Nicaragua is not separately scored
in the most widely published Hofstede dataset; the pitch is drawn from the broad
Latin American and Central American profile (warm and relationship-first,
present-living and expressive, with a marked pride in letters and wit particular
to Nicaragua) rather than from a single published national figure. Any scores
consulted are recorded here so the framing is on the record without entering the
prose.

| Dimension                     | Profile (approx., Central American / Latin American) |
| ----------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | high                                                 |
| Individualism                 | low (collectivist)                                   |
| Motivation toward Achievement | moderate                                             |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | high                                                 |
| Long Term Orientation         | low (short-term)                                     |
| Indulgence                    | moderate to high                                     |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the six-dimensions model of national
culture). Values are approximate and regional, as published by Hofstede Insights
/ The Culture Factor Group; Nicaragua's own pride in poetry and humor is a
national trait added to the regional profile, not a Hofstede dimension.

## Languages

The play and the whole cast are written in Spanish (`es`), Nicaragua's national
tongue. The Caribbean coast's own languages, Miskito and Creole English, are
named within the prose (most of all in [persona_el_costeno_miskito.md](persona_el_costeno_miskito.md))
as the mother tongues of the other Nicaragua, carried alongside the Spanish of
the centre.

## Historical sources

The plots, the historic personas they cast, the places, and the pieces are drawn
from the public historical record. They are credited here and never claimed; the
staging is original work. Foreign intervention (the filibuster Walker), the
Somoza era, and the 1979 revolution are named soberly on the public record,
without atrocity detail and without taking a political side.

The one defining persona, [el costeno miskito](persona_el_costeno_miskito.md), is
an archetype, not a historical individual: a composite that carries the living
tension of the two coasts, the Miskito and Creole Caribbean against the Hispanic
Pacific, the other Nicaragua that the centre tends to forget.
