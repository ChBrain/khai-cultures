# Position: Delawarean English

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The Mid-Atlantic speech of the Delaware valley, the Delawarean English that sits on the seam between the Northern and Southern sounds, carrying a soft coastal drawl in the lower counties and a flatter city cadence around Wilmington. It holds the sound of a small state that speaks with a warmth measured against the law.

## Orders

What passes here as how folks talk, the mixed Mid-Atlantic voice, where the same register serves the filing report and the powder-mill demonstration. It orders which sound reads as native, the border cadence against which the newcomer is heard.

## Loses

Ground to General American, the broadcast standard that erases the region's border color, and to the sharper Philadelphia sound pressing down from the north and the Southern drawl rising from below. It loses too to the neutral corporate register of the boardrooms that fill its incorporation rolls.

## Drives

It carries the state's habit of sounding warm, careful, and plain. It gives [Pierre](persona_pierre.md) the Mid-Atlantic cadence of his demonstration checks, the defining code he speaks and thinks in, and it carries the warm drawl of [Irene](persona_irene.md), the mother tongue of her preservation reports. So the one valley sound shows who speaks it as home.
