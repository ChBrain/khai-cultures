# Persona: Irene

## Taxonomy

[corporate chancery](position_corporate_chancery.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

Fifty-two years old, a legal archivist in Wilmington who has read more chancery decrees than most judges. English - [Delawarean English](position_language_en_us_de.md), Mid-Atlantic and unhurried - is her mother tongue, the language she [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) at the filing counter and writes into her daily preservation reports. She has no foreign languages, and doesn't need one: everything she needs to argue is already on the record in Delaware. What she can't shake is the sense of two clocks running at different speeds in the same building - the fast, digital churn of chemical specialists like Pierre and the corporate boards [she takes in only secondhand, like word passed down from upstairs](process_hearing_followed.md), against the patient rhythm of a court where precedent is still the whole argument. She belongs to [the First State spirit](position_culture_first_state_spirit.md) as the one who keeps the registry credible to businesses that never once have to think about her.

## Action

She keeps [corporate seal](piece_corporate_seal.md) records in order, coordinates the field conservation crews working under [the Brandywine plan](plan_brandywine_river_protection.md), and keeps close study of [first state charter](position_first_state_charter.md).

## Shadow

Her pride in the county courts and local jurisdiction runs deep enough that she eyes state-wide zoning and environmental mandates with real suspicion, half convinced Dover is one signature away from telling a family what it can do with its own land.

## Tell

She holds up a century-old court decree and says, without raising her voice: "The Chancery's got its own precedents, and they're older than anybody's spreadsheet. You want corporate stability, you don't get it by outrunning the paperwork - you get it by keeping it straight."
