# Plan: Datganoli

## Taxonomy

A production directive: the long national scheme to win and widen Welsh self-government, and so to reach by ballot and statute the Welsh institutions that [Owain Glyndwr](persona_glyndwr.md) once sought by the sword.

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Direction

To move Wales from a country governed wholly from London to one that governs itself: a parliament of its own, the power to make its own law, and a settled place in the constitution. The same hope that ran through the rising of 1400, pursued now by referendum and Act rather than by arms.

## Orders

- To the electorate: settle the question at the ballot box, twice over, since a self-government built on consent is the only kind that holds.
- To the parliament in Cardiff: take up the powers won and govern with them, in [solidariaeth](position_solidariaeth.md) with the communities of [the Valleys](place_the_valleys.md) and the country beyond.
- To the parties and the public bodies: treat devolution as a settlement to be deepened, not a concession to be managed.

## Implementation

The 1997 referendum and the Government of Wales Acts as the founding floor; the 2011 referendum for primary law-making powers; the renaming of the institution as Senedd Cymru; and the slow accretion of fields, from health and education to tax, that turn an assembly into a parliament.

## Targets

- [x] A National Assembly for Wales created by referendum (1997, by the narrowest of margins)
- [x] Primary law-making powers granted by referendum (2011)
- [x] The institution recognised as a parliament, Senedd Cymru (2020)
- [ ] A stable, settled constitutional place for Welsh self-government
- [ ] The powers matched by the means to use them well
- [W] The early hope that a modest assembly would settle the question once and for all
