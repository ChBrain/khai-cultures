# Position: y capel

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The power of the Nonconformist chapel, the Welsh house of worship that for generations was the heart of the community. Egalitarian and self-governing, it answered to no bishop and no squire; it taught hymn-singing, self-improvement, and the conviction that before God one man is no better than another. The chapel made a literate, singing, debating people out of a poor one.

## Orders

That the community gather, sing, and govern itself, that learning and the word be prized, and that show and rank be distrusted. It asks plainness, sobriety, and mutual care, and it teaches the gifted poor to stand level with the great through the pulpit, the choir, and the recitation.

## Loses

Ground where its rigour hardens into joyless respectability, the chapel turning narrow, censorious, and afraid of the world. And it loses, too, simply by emptying: as the congregations age and the doors close, the egalitarian, singing culture it carried is left without its house.

## Drives

It lives wherever the hymns and the chapel tradition are kept alive and the conviction holds that before God none stands above another, and from it grew the levelling, self-improving temper that runs through the whole of Welsh life.
