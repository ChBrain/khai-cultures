# Process: rugby

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

Any town or valley with a club and a field, and a whole nation when the side plays. Rugby needs only fifteen players, a crowd, and the hymns that the crowd brings with it.

## Direction

Toward the gathering of a people into one voice and one feeling. It is the national game, rooted in the clubs of [the Valleys](place_the_valleys.md), where the working town tested itself against the world; and on the great days it lifts a whole country, the terraces singing the old chapel hymns until the ground itself seems to roar.

## Lever

The longing to stand level with the great and to belong to something larger drives it, and the [solidariaeth](position_solidariaeth.md) of the club and the town. Against it stand the thin years and the closing grounds, but the hymn and the roar answer by making the stadium a chapel of another kind.

## Echo

Rugby carries the chapel feeling into a secular age: the same hymns, the same massed singing, the same belonging, now lifted on the terraces. It is where [Rhys](persona_rhys.md) sings the old hymns with the whole crowd, and it shows a people keeping its song even as its chapels empty.
