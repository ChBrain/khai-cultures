# Persona: Aneurin Bevan

## Taxonomy

[solidariaeth](position_solidariaeth.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A collier's son from Tredegar in the coalfield, who went down the pit at fourteen and taught himself in the miners' institute library and the debating club. Fiery, eloquent, and unbowed, he believed to his marrow that working people must stand together and that no one should be left to fall. He [speaks, reads, and writes the English of the coalfield as his mother tongue](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), every channel of it his own; what he carries onto the national stage is not another language but the cadence and the class of the Valleys in the one he was born to. The Welsh his grandparents prayed in reaches him only as [the sound of the chapel](process_hearing_caught.md), a music he can catch but never answer: the tongue his valley had already set down a generation before him.

## Action

He lives the [solidariaeth](position_solidariaeth.md) of the pit and the lodge, learned in the chapels and institutes of [the Valleys](place_the_valleys.md) in the years of coal. He carries the federation's creed - that the strong owe the weak, that the community comes before the man - from a miner's cottage to the front rank of national politics, arguing always from the bedrock conviction that working people are nobody's inferiors.

## Shadow

The same fierce loyalty that made him a tribune could make him a divider, cutting and tribal toward those outside the fold. The solidarity he preached, turned sour, can become a suspicion of anyone who rises out or speaks differently, and a grievance that nurses old wounds.

## Tell

He keeps the accent and the bite of the coalfield in every word, and never lets the powerful forget where he came from or whom he speaks for.
