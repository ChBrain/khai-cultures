# Play: Wales

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the house that holds this production and answers for it.

## Name

Wales, Cymru, the western country of mountain and valley, the old Brythonic land held against the tide from the east. ISO GB-WLS, language en, with Welsh in its cast.

## Arc

A small nation that lost its last native prince to the conqueror, saw its land ringed with castles and its rule broken, and rose once more under Glyndwr only to be broken again - and yet held on to the one thing no army could take, the oldest living tongue in these islands. A Bible in Welsh saved and standardised that language and made a literate, chapel-going, hymn-singing people; coal then drew them down into the southern valleys, where the pit, the chapel, and the choir forged a fierce working-class solidarity that carried a miner's son to the national stage. Beneath it all runs one nerve: hiraeth, the deep longing for a land and a belonging half-lost, and the stubborn pride of the square mile that refuses to be spoken for.

## Triggers

**[Plot 1: The Death of Llywelyn 1282](plot_01_llywelyn_1282.md)**

Llywelyn ap Gruffudd, the last native Prince of Wales, falls in 1282; Edward I rings [Eryri](place_eryri.md) with castles and breaks native rule, but cannot break [yr iaith](position_yr_iaith.md), the tongue of the conquered.

**[Plot 2: The Rising of Glyndwr 1400](plot_02_glyndwr_1400.md)**

[Owain Glyndwr](persona_glyndwr.md) raises the last great Welsh rebellion from the fastness of [Eryri](place_eryri.md), holds a parliament at Machynlleth, and is crowned Prince of Wales before the rising is ground down.

**[Plot 3: The Welsh Bible 1588](plot_03_welsh_bible_1588.md)**

William Morgan gives Wales [the Bible](piece_welsh_bible.md) in its own tongue, saving and standardising [yr iaith](position_yr_iaith.md) and laying the root of Welsh literacy and Nonconformity.

**[Plot 4: Coal and Bevan](plot_04_coal_and_bevan.md)**

Coal draws a people into [the Valleys](place_the_valleys.md), where the pit and the chapel forge a working-class solidarity that carries [Aneurin Bevan](persona_bevan.md) from a miner's cottage to the national stage.

## Stakes

Whether a small nation that lost its princes and its rule, kept its ancient tongue and its song, and forged a fierce solidarity underground can hold its language, its chapels, and its belonging to the square mile together as one people, even as the pits close, the chapels empty, and the young are drawn outward.
