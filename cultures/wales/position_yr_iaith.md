# Position: yr iaith

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The power of the oldest living tongue in these islands, the keystone of Welsh identity. Where the princes fell and the state was lost, the language remained, and to speak it is itself an act of belonging. Yr iaith holds what no army could take: a people defined not by a border but by a tongue.

## Orders

That the language be carried, taught, and used, against the constant pull of the larger tongue to the east. It asks that children be raised in it, that it be honoured in chapel, school, and eisteddfod, and that to keep it alive is a duty owed to those who kept it before.

## Loses

Ground where the tongue becomes a relic to be mourned rather than a language to be lived, spoken only by the old and sung only on stage. Yr iaith loses when pride in it outlasts the daily using of it, and it shrinks to a symbol of a nation rather than the speech of one.

## Drives

It lives in [Owain Glyndwr](persona_glyndwr.md), who spoke it as the ground of his rising, and in [Gwen](persona_gwen.md), who answers in Welsh whatever tongue you address her in, and it is the keystone the whole nation is built upon.
