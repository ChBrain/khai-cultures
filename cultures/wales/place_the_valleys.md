# Place: the Valleys

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Shown

The South Wales Valleys: the coalfield where terraced rows climb the steep hillsides above the rivers, the pit-wheels once turned, and chapel, institute, and rugby club stood close together. A crowded, hard, intensely communal landscape, largely English-speaking, made and then unmade by coal.

## Holds

- [Coal and Bevan](plot_04_coal_and_bevan.md): the coalfield of pit and chapel that draws a people in and forges [Aneurin Bevan](persona_bevan.md).

## Offers

The crucible of [solidariaeth](position_solidariaeth.md): the place where the pit taught that no man comes up alone, the chapel and the institute taught hymn and debate, and a fierce working-class community was forged underground.

## Withheld

The permanence of the work that built it. The Valleys gave a singing, solidarity-bound culture, but the coal that made them ran out, leaving closed pits, emptying chapels, and a community that must outlast its own industry.
