# Wales: a khai culture

Wales staged as a full khai play, ISO GB-WLS. A small nation that lost its
princes and kept its language - a constituent nation of the United Kingdom -
held together across English and Welsh. Its history is the plot line, its people
the cast, the roles its society encodes, its places, its practices, and its
defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## Two tongues

The play and most of the cast are in English, but two personas are written in
Welsh, each with its own declared `language:`:

- **English** - Aneurin Bevan, Rhys
- **Welsh** (`cy`) - Owain Glyndwr, Gwen

The play and the shared instances are in English (the nation's common tongue);
Glyndwr and Gwen are authored in Welsh, each routed to its own language detector.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_wales.md](play_wales.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_wls.md](pitch_wls.md), the Hofstede layer, written from the
  Great Britain profile (Wales has no separate Hofstede profile and inherits the
  national base) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the death of Llywelyn (1282), the rising of Glyndwr
  (1400), the Welsh Bible (1588), and coal and Bevan (c. 1900-1948) - each
  casting the elements it needs (the Llywelyn plot casts a place and a position,
  no persona).
- **Personas:** Owain Glyndwr and Aneurin Bevan as the historic cast, plus two
  defining archetypes, Gwen (older, Welsh-speaking, the chapel and the square
  mile) and Rhys (younger, the largely English-speaking post-industrial Valleys).
  Each is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** yr iaith (the Welsh language, keystone of identity), y capel (the
  Nonconformist chapel and its egalitarian, hymn-singing tradition), solidariaeth
  (the working-class solidarity of the coalfield).
- **Places:** Eryri / Snowdonia (the northern mountains, the heartland of the
  language and of native rule), the Valleys (the South Wales coalfield).
- **Processes:** the eisteddfod, rugby.
- **Pieces:** Y Ddraig Goch (the red dragon, the flag), the Welsh Bible (William
  Morgan's 1588 translation).
