# Place: Eryri

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Shown

The northern mountains, Eryri, called Snowdonia in English: high peaks, deep glens, and hard upland country, the roof of Wales. This was the fastness of the native princes and the heartland where the old tongue held strongest against every tide from the east.

## Holds

- [The Death of Llywelyn 1282](plot_01_llywelyn_1282.md): the heartland that Edward I rings with castles to break native rule.
- [The Rising of Glyndwr 1400](plot_02_glyndwr_1400.md): the mountain country that shelters the last great rising and never gives Glyndwr up.

## Offers

The deep root of the nation: the seat of native rule, the stronghold of [yr iaith](position_yr_iaith.md), and the high, beautiful land that taught a people to hold out where it could not be easily reached.

## Withheld

The promise of a victory that lasts. Eryri sheltered princes and rebels alike, but it could not give them a state; it offers endurance and refuge, not triumph.
