# Position: Welsh English

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The authority of English as it is spoken across Wales, and above all in the industrial south: the tongue of the coalfield and the valley town, English in its grammar but Welsh in its lilt, its rhythm, and a good many of its words. It carries the sing of the chapel and the choir even where the older tongue has gone silent, the cwtch and the tidy and the by there, the everyday speech of the great majority of the nation, its own music laid over the language of the east.

## Orders

What passes for good English across Wales, the register of the school, the council, and the broadcaster, and the plain valleys idiom that marks the speech as Welsh and not the English of England. It orders the working tongue of the coalfield and the city, the language in which the labour movement argued its case and the nation now does most of its living, carrying into the wider world the cadence and the class of the Valleys in the one tongue their people were left with.

## Loses

Ground on two sides at once: to the flattening pull of a metropolitan English that hears its lilt as merely quaint, and to the memory of the Welsh it stands in the place of, for it is the tongue that filled the valleys as the older one was set down a generation before. It thins wherever its own music is smoothed away, and it carries, under the pride, the quiet knowledge that it belongs to a people who once spoke another tongue and gave it up.

## Drives

It drives Wales to speak its solidarity, its song, and its pride in an English all its own. It lives in [Aneurin Bevan](persona_bevan.md), who carried the cadence and the class of the coalfield from a miner's cottage to the national stage, and in [Rhys](persona_rhys.md), who owns the valley English whole while the Welsh reaches him only as the echo of a hymn. So the tongue the valleys were left with becomes the carrier of a fierce belonging the older language once held.
