# Process: the eisteddfod

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

Any community that gathers to compete and perform in poetry, song, and recitation, from the village hall to the great national festival. The eisteddfod needs only a people who prize the word and the voice, and a chair to be won.

## Direction

Toward the honouring of language and art as the common property of a whole people, not a few. It crowns the poet and the singer, sets cynghanedd and the strict metres of the bardic tradition beside the choir and the hymn, and lifts the gifted of any station to the chair before the nation.

## Lever

The deep prizing of [yr iaith](position_yr_iaith.md) and of song drives it, and the egalitarian conviction that the word belongs to all. Against it stands only the pull of the larger world and the louder tongue, but the eisteddfod answers by making the language a thing of pride and ceremony.

## Echo

The eisteddfod makes a small nation's love of its tongue and its song audible and visible. It is where [Gwen](persona_gwen.md) sings and where the old recitations are kept alive, and it shows that a people can carry its language not as a relic but as a living art.
