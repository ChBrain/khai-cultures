# Persona: Rhys

## Taxonomy

[solidariaeth](position_solidariaeth.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A younger man of the largely English-speaking Valleys, raised in a terraced town after the pits closed. Wry, warm, and quick with a joke, he carries the solidarity of the coalfield without the coal, and the pride without the work that built it. Rugby is half his religion; he has a few words of Welsh from school and the anthem, and he reaches for [his mother tongue](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) of the valley - the lilting, English-laced speech of the south - as the natural ground of who he is.

## Action

He lives [solidariaeth](position_solidariaeth.md) as belonging more than as struggle, standing by his town and his club through thin years. He fills the terraces for the [rugby](process_rugby.md) and sings the old hymns there with the whole crowd, carrying into the stadium the chapel feeling his grandparents kept, even as the chapels themselves stand empty.

## Shadow

His warmth can slide into resignation. With the work gone and the future drawn outward, the pride of the square mile can curdle into a wry fatalism, a sense that the best days are behind and that nothing here will change.

## Tell

He can lift the whole terrace into a hymn at a rugby match, and means every word of it, though he could not tell you when he last set foot in a chapel.
