# Piece: Y Ddraig Goch

## Taxonomy

Parent group: pieces

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Place

Flown over town hall and rugby ground, in classroom and on hillside across the country: the red dragon passant on a field of green and white, Y Ddraig Goch, the flag of Wales.

## Load Bearing

It stands for a nation that kept its identity when it had no state. The dragon is older than the conquest, a symbol carried down from the Brythonic past and borne by the princes; it says that Wales is a people and a name, not a possession of any crown. Without this ancient sign, the long survival of the nation through every loss would lack its banner.

## Apparent

A bold red dragon, claws raised and tongue out, striding across a banner half green, half white. There is nothing meek about it; it is a fighting beast, and a people that was conquered and never assimilated chose it for that.

## Yearbook

- Carried down from the old Brythonic and Arthurian past, older than the conquest itself.
- Borne by [Owain Glyndwr](persona_glyndwr.md) and the native princes as the sign of a free Wales.
- Flown today over ground and hillside as the banner of a nation that kept its name.
