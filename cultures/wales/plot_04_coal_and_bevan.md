# Plot: Coal and Bevan

## Taxonomy

[play_wales](play_wales.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

From the late nineteenth century coal draws hundreds of thousands of people into [the Valleys](place_the_valleys.md) of the south. Terraced rows climb the hillsides, the pit-wheels turn, and the chapels and miners' institutes rise alongside them.

## Action

The coalfield forges a new Wales: hard, crowded, and intensely communal. The pit teaches that no man comes up alone; the chapel and the institute teach hymn, debate, and self-improvement; the miners' federation teaches that working people must stand together. Out of this world comes [Aneurin Bevan](persona_bevan.md), a collier's son from Tredegar who carries the voice and the solidarity of the Valleys onto the national stage and never sheds the accent of the coalfield that made him.

## Stage

- [Aneurin Bevan](persona_bevan.md): the miner's son and tribune who carries the Valleys' solidarity outward.
- [the Valleys](place_the_valleys.md): the coalfield of pit, chapel, and institute that forms him.

## Tension

The coal builds a fierce, singing, solidarity-bound community - and the coal also ends, leaving the pits closed, the chapels emptying, and the institutes shut. What was forged underground must now find a way to outlast the industry that forged it.
