# Pakistan: a khai culture

Pakistan staged as a full khai play, ISO PK, language ur (Urdu, written in the
Nastaliq script). A nation of the Indus and of faith, rendered as a theatre: its
history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its language and faith
encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single
pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_pakistan.md](play_pakistan.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_ur.md](pitch_ur.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Pakistan's profile (a collective, family- and community-first society, warm and
  hospitable, with high deference to elders and authority, a strong present focus,
  and a deep religious and Sufi undercurrent) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Indus Valley civilization (Mohenjo-daro and Harappa),
  the coming of Islam and the Sufi saints, the founding of Pakistan in 1947, and
  the building of the nation - each casting the elements it needs, the founding
  staged soberly and with dignity, taking no side.
- **Personas:** Muhammad Ali Jinnah (the founder, Quaid-e-Azam), Allama Muhammad
  Iqbal (the national poet and philosopher), Bulleh Shah (the Sufi poet of the
  heritage), and Nusrat Fateh Ali Khan (the master of qawwali) as the historic
  cast, plus one archetype, the devotee (the farmer and shrine-goer who quietly
  keeps the heritage and the open door). Each is wired to the language engine by
  the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** Islam (the foundation of the national identity, lived as faith
  and as a Sufi tradition of love and unity), the Urdu language (the shared tongue
  and the great poetic tradition), and hospitality (the guest as a mercy, and the
  warm ties of family and community).
- **Places:** Lahore (the heart of Punjab, the city of culture, learning, and the
  shrines) and Mohenjo-daro (the ancient city on the Indus, the deepest root).
- **Processes:** qawwali (devotional music as a path to ecstasy) and the urs (the
  yearly festival at a Sufi saint's shrine).
- **Pieces:** truck art (the rolling folk art of the highways) and the shalwar
  kameez (the national dress that joins the many in one form).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical and cultural
sources are recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the
prose; the pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited,
never claimed. Pakistan is staged from within its own self-understanding, on the
public record, taking no political side; the founding of 1947 and the migration
that came with it are treated soberly and with dignity, without detail and without
taking a side. The staging and the architecture are original work.
