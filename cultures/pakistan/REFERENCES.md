# Pakistan: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical and cultural
sources behind the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_ur.md](pitch_ur.md) is written from Pakistan's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the record without
entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Pakistan score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------ |
| Power Distance                | 55                       |
| Individualism                 | 14                       |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 50                       |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 70                       |
| Long Term Orientation         | 50                       |
| Indulgence                    | 0                        |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published by
Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; Pakistan's low Individualism reflects
a strongly collective, family- and community-centred society.

The one archetype persona, [the devotee](persona_saalik.md), is not a historical
individual but a composite: the villager who is at once a farmer of the Indus
plains and a lover of the shrine, who keeps hospitality and the Sufi heritage alive
without saying so, and whose heart tongue is his mother language while the written
language of the state stays at his threshold.
