## Initiated by

The whole of St. Kitts at the turning of the year: the masquerade troupes and the steel pan and the soca and calypso, the bands and the revellers who fill up the streets of Basseterre from late December right into the new year for the National Carnival we call Sugar Mas, the J'ouvert before day clean and the costume parade in the heat of the day. Leh we jump up, the people say, leh we wine down the road.

## Direction

Toward joy claim right out of the very crop that bind the islands: the festival take its name from sugar, the word for the harvest that once held the people in the cane, and turn it into the name of we carnival, the masquerade dancing where the cane once stood. It is not only a fete but a taking-back, the free people's children making a celebration out of the season and the word of they bondage, the mask and the music answering the field.

## Lever

It is drive by the deep Caribbean genius for turning suffering into festival, and by a people who would sooner dance the cane's memory than stand silent before it. It hold against the long shadow of the monoculture and the smallness of the stage; against it stand the cost of the show and the risk that the carnival's roar drown out the history it is make from.
