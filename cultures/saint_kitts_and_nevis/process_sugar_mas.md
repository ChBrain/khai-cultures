# Process: Sugar Mas

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

The whole of St. Kitts at the turning of the year: the masquerade troupes and the steel pan and the soca and calypso, the bands and the revellers who fill the streets of Basseterre from late December into the new year for the National Carnival the islands call Sugar Mas, the J'ouvert before the dawn and the costumed parade in the heat of the day.

## Direction

Toward joy claimed out of the very crop that bound the islands: the festival takes its name from sugar, the word for the harvest that once held the people in the cane, and turns it into the name of their carnival, the masquerade dancing where the cane once stood. It is not only a party but a reclaiming, the freed people's descendants making a celebration out of the season and the word of their bondage, the mask and the music answering the field.

## Lever

It is driven by the deep Caribbean genius for turning suffering into festival, and by a people who would rather dance the cane's memory than be silent before it. It holds against the long shadow of the monoculture and the smallness of the stage; against it stand the cost of the show and the risk that the carnival's roar drowns the history it is made from.

## Echo

Sugar Mas makes audible what drives [the Mother Colony and the sugar cradle](position_the_mother_colony_and_the_sugar_cradle.md): that the islands carry the cane's history not by denial but by transformation, the word for bondage made the name of joy. In its masquerade the descendants of [the sugar worker](persona_the_sugar_worker.md) dance the harvest that once held them, free at last to make a festival of it.
