# Persona: Caryl Phillips

## Taxonomy

[small islands, wide sons](position_small_islands_wide_sons.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

Born on St. Kitts and carried to England as an infant, raised in the grey north of another country, who grew to write the Caribbean and its scattering into the literature of the English language, the author of "The Final Passage" and "A Distant Shore": the islands' wide son of letters, the small island present in the world's bookshelves through a son who left it before he could remember it. Thoughtful, displaced, exacting, a writer who made the diaspora and the in-between his subject and his home. English he has whole and shaping: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in his mother tongue, the English of the country that raised him, and he [writes](process_writing_mother_tongue.md) in it the novels that carry the islands' departures into literature. The warm Kittitian Creole of the island he was born on he [follows when he hears it](process_hearing_followed.md) and [reads when he meets it on the page](process_reading_deciphered.md), the home tongue he can recognize but was carried away from before he could hold it. He writes the Caribbean in the perfect English of an English childhood, yet the island that bore him gave him a Creole he must approach as a half-stranger, so that the writer who returns the islands to the world's letters returns to a mother tongue not quite his own - the gap at the limit of the tongue, where the writer of home can read its language but cannot say he grew up speaking it.

## Action

In [Beyond Sugar](plot_04_beyond_sugar.md) he carries the islands into the world's literature, writing the Caribbean and its scattering from the far side of the sea. By making the diaspora his subject, he proves [small islands, wide sons](position_small_islands_wide_sons.md): that the smallest island can reach the widest letters through a son it sent away.

## Shadow

The writer who returns the island to the page can become the shadow of the leaving itself: the diaspora that gives the islands their voice in the world is the same diaspora that emptied the villages, the wide son a measure of how many had to go. The home written most beautifully by the one who could not stay in it.

## Tell

He is the infant carried off the island who spent a life writing his way back to it on the page - the wide son who made the islands' departures into the literature the islands are known by.
