# Position: Country Above Self

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The national motto and the standing it names: the belonging to the smallest sovereign nation of the Americas, two islands tied by law and by a narrow channel of water, that no always of one mind but a country still. It carries [the Mother Colony and the sugar cradle](position_the_mother_colony_and_the_sugar_cradle.md), the standing of St. Kitts as the first English colony in the whole Caribbean and the cane monoculture worked under a slavery hard as any in the islands; it carries [the labour nation and the federation](position_the_labour_nation_and_the_federation.md), the standing of a nation born from the union hall and held together as two islands across a narrow strait that once near enough came apart; and it carries [small islands, wide sons](position_small_islands_wide_sons.md), the proof that a speck this size can be the cradle of a founder of another nation and of a novelist both. It speaks [the Kittitian-Nevisian tongue](position_language_en_kn.md), the warm creole of the yard held alongside the proper English of the platform and the law. It has, without ever choosing it, a people who slide between the two so easy you cyaan find the seam, and a memory of cane worked right down to the waterline that no independence has yet let go unspoken.

## Orders

That the worker and the labour movement be honoured as the founders of this nation, independence won by organizing the field and not handed down from above; that St. Kitts never take the union for granted and Nevis find her place within it rather than reach again for the door, country hold up above the pull of either isle; that the great fort and the ruin mills be kept as heritage owned sober-eyed, the wealth they made and the bondage that grew it carried in the one same hand; and that the islands claim their wide sons and scattered people with pride, no matter how far the sea has carried them.

## Loses

Ground when the federation forgets that it is two, St. Kitts talking as if it were the whole of it while Nevis is left feeling a colony of a colony; ground when the cradle is told as glory alone, the proud first colony and the mighty fort, with the slavery that made it all left unsaid; and ground when the wide sons are claimed abroad but forgotten at home, the diaspora that built the nation's name treated as mere walking-away instead of reach. It loses ground, too, now the last sugar harvest is cut and gone since 2005, in the hard search for a living past the cane that made the islands and bound them both.

## Drives

Lives in the worker who bore the cradle's history in the flesh and outlasted it, in the labour leader who carried the islands from colony to independence, and in the Nevisian voice that would not let the union forget it is two. It drives Saint Kitts and Nevis to hold country above the claim of either isle, to look the cradle's history dead in the eye rather than deny it, and to answer the wide world as an equal for all that it is small, sending its own people out to make their mark while never letting the yard at home be waved away as a mere footnote.
