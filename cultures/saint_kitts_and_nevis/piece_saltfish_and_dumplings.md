# Piece: stewed saltfish and dumplings

## Taxonomy

Parent group: pieces

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Place

On the Sunday table and the holiday plate cross both islands: salt cod stew down with onion, tomato, and pepper, dish up with boil flour dumplings and the islands' own spicy plantain and breadfruit, the national dish of Saint Kitts and Nevis, cook in the yard and carry to every gathering from a christening to Culturama. And no Sunday complete without a pot of goat water bubbling beside it.

## Load Bearing

It carry the whole history of the cradle on the one plate. The salt cod come as cheap salt-down protein ship in to feed the enslave on the sugar estates, the breadfruit bring to the islands for the same reason, and the people take the rations of bondage and make out of them a dish they love and claim as they own. To eat it is to taste the monoculture and the endurance of [the Mother Colony and the sugar cradle](position_the_mother_colony_and_the_sugar_cradle.md) at the one time, the food of the estate turn into the food of a free nation, the worker's table come the country's pride. Without this dish the islands would have no single plate that hold both the cane's history and the people's making of a life out of it.

## Apparent

A plate of flake-up salt fish in a red-brown stew with pale boil dumplings, slices of fry plantain, and a wedge of breadfruit, steam rising up in a hot kitchen. In itself it is a humble meal of salt fish and starch; its weight is all in the history it carry, the rations of the estate raise up by generations of cooks into the dish a nation call its own.
