# Piece: stewed saltfish and dumplings

## Taxonomy

Parent group: pieces

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Place

On the Sunday table and the holiday plate across both islands: salted cod stewed down with onion, tomato, and pepper, served with boiled flour dumplings and the islands' own spicy plantain and breadfruit, the national dish of Saint Kitts and Nevis, cooked in the yard and carried to every gathering from a christening to Culturama.

## Load Bearing

It carries the whole history of the cradle on one plate. The salt cod came as cheap preserved protein shipped to feed the enslaved of the sugar estates, the breadfruit brought to the islands for the same purpose, and the people took the rations of bondage and made of them a dish they love and claim as their own. To eat it is to taste the monoculture and the endurance of [the Mother Colony and the sugar cradle](position_the_mother_colony_and_the_sugar_cradle.md) at once, the food of the estate turned into the food of a free nation, the worker's table become the country's pride. Without this dish the islands would have no single plate that holds both the cane's history and the people's making of a life out of it.

## Apparent

A plate of flaked salt fish in a red-brown stew with pale boiled dumplings, slices of fried plantain, and a wedge of breadfruit, steam rising in a hot kitchen. In itself it is a humble meal of preserved fish and starch; its weight is entirely in the history it carries, the rations of the estate raised by generations of cooks into the dish a nation calls its own.
