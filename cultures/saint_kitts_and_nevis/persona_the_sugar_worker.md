# Persona: the sugar worker

## Taxonomy

[the Mother Colony and the sugar cradle](position_the_mother_colony_and_the_sugar_cradle.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The hand that worked the cane: enslaved on the estates of the Mother Colony, then freed in 1834 and bound still to the same field, the man and woman who cut and carried and boiled the sugar that made the islands rich and themselves poor, generation upon generation in the cane to the last harvest of 2005. Enduring, dignified, rooted to the land that held them, the worker in whom the whole monoculture is embodied, free in law and tied to the estate, who outlasted the cradle and made a people out of bondage. The ancestral African tongues their forebears [carried across the water from slavery](process_speaking_carried.md) they once [spoke and heard](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) among themselves in the rows and the yard, the mother tongues of the lands they were taken from, [held and passed in the only telling there was](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) when nothing could be written down. The planters' English they were made to [follow when it was spoken at them](process_hearing_followed.md), the tongue of the estate and the law, learned under the lash and turned, in time, into the warm Kittitian-Nevisian Creole that became the worker's own. The African mother tongues thinned and were lost in the generations of the cane, never written, surviving only as a word, a rhythm, a name half-remembered, so that the people who built the islands cannot fully hear the tongues their grandmothers were taken with - the gap at the limit of the tongue, where the language carried across the water is the very thing the crossing took.

## Action

In [Emancipation and the Cane](plot_02_emancipation_and_the_cane.md) the worker is freed in 1834 and stays in the field, the cane still king and the estate still owning the day, the gap between freedom in law and freedom in fact made flesh. By enduring the monoculture and outlasting it, the worker carries [the Mother Colony and the sugar cradle](position_the_mother_colony_and_the_sugar_cradle.md): the one who bore the cradle's history and made a free people from it.

## Shadow

The worker freed but not free is the cradle's own shadow: emancipation that changed the law and not the field, the promise of liberty undercut by the cane that still owned the days, a freedom on paper that the estate could quietly take back. The reminder that to be told one is free is not yet to be free.

## Tell

The worker is freed on a morning in 1834 and walks back into the same cane by afternoon, free and bound at once - and carries in the Creole on the tongue the worn-down trace of mother tongues the crossing took away.
