# Persona: the sugar worker

## Taxonomy

[the Mother Colony and the sugar cradle](position_the_mother_colony_and_the_sugar_cradle.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The hand that work the cane: enslave on the estates of the Mother Colony, then free in 1834 and tie still to the same field, the man and the woman who cut and carry and boil the sugar that make the islands rich and theyself poor, generation upon generation in the cane right down to the last harvest of 2005. Enduring, dignified, root deep in the land that hold them, the worker in whom the whole monoculture stand up, free by the law and tie to the estate, who outlast the cradle and make a people out of bondage. The old African tongues they foreparents [carried across the water from slavery](process_speaking_carried.md) they once [spoke and heard](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) among theyself in the cane row and the yard, the mother tongues of the lands they was take from, [held and passed in the only telling there was](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) when nothing could be write down. The planters' English they was force to [follow when it was spoken at them](process_hearing_followed.md), the tongue of the estate and the law, learn under the lash and turn, in time, into the warm Kittitian-Nevisian that come the worker's own, [the Kittitian-Nevisian tongue](position_language_en_kn.md) the worker carry as they own though it was not the mother tongue they was born to. The African mother tongues thin out and was lost in the generations of the cane, never write down, living on only as a word, a drum rhythm, a name half-remember, so the people who build these islands cyaan rightly hear the tongues they grandmother was take with. The cane remember nothing, we say, but the blood does; the gap at the limit of the tongue, where the language carry across the water is the very thing the crossing take away.

## Action

In [Emancipation and the Cane](plot_02_emancipation_and_the_cane.md) the worker is free in 1834 and stay right there in the field, the cane still king and the estate still owning the day, the gap between freedom by the law and freedom in truth make flesh. By enduring the monoculture and outlasting it, the worker carry [the Mother Colony and the sugar cradle](position_the_mother_colony_and_the_sugar_cradle.md): the one who bear the cradle's story and make a free people out of it.

## Shadow

The worker free but not free is the cradle's own shadow: emancipation that change the law and not the field, the promise of liberty cut down by the cane that still own the days, a freedom on paper the estate could quiet quiet take back. The reminder that to be tell you free is not yet to be free, no sah.

## Tell

The worker is free one morning in 1834 and walk right back into the same cane by the afternoon, free and bound at the one time, and carry in the Kittitian on the tongue the worn-down trace of mother tongues the crossing take away.
