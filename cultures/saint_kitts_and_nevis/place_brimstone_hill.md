# Place: Brimstone Hill

## Taxonomy

## Owner

## Shown

The great fort climbing a volcanic outcrop eight hundred foot up over the cane of St. Kitts, black-stone walls and bastions and the Citadel of Fort George rising right out the rock, the Gibraltar of the West Indies, raise up over generations to guard the Mother Colony and she sugar. Now a UNESCO World Heritage Site, the ramparts looking down over the green fields to the blue sea and the small sister Nevis beyond the strait. The might of empire set in stone, ah tell yuh, and down under it the cane that pay for every last block.

Brimstone Hill carry the gap between the mighty fort and the bondage that build it and feed it: the great stone walls raise up over a slavery near total, the Gibraltar of the West Indies standing guard over a monoculture of unfree hands, and it remind the islands that the cradle's glory and the cradle's wound is the one same hill.
