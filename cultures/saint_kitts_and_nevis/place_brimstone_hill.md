# Place: Brimstone Hill

## Taxonomy

Parent group: places

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Shown

The great fortress climbing a volcanic outcrop eight hundred feet above the cane of St. Kitts, black-stone walls and bastions and the Citadel of Fort George rising from the rock, the Gibraltar of the West Indies, raised over generations to guard the Mother Colony and its sugar. Now a UNESCO World Heritage Site, its ramparts looking down over the green fields to the blue sea and the sister isle of Nevis beyond the strait. The might of empire set in stone, and beneath it the cane that paid for every block.

## Offers

A place where the islands show themselves their own founding in stone: the engineering and the endurance of the fort a source of pride, the view from its walls the whole small kingdom of cane and sea at once, the heritage kept and shown to the world as proof that these small isles were the cradle of an empire's Caribbean.

## Withheld

The innocence of mere grandeur. Brimstone Hill carries the gap between the mighty fort and the bondage that built and fed it - the great stone walls raised over a near-total slavery, the Gibraltar of the West Indies standing guard over a monoculture of unfree hands, and it reminds the islands that the cradle's glory and the cradle's wound are the same hill.
