# Persona: Alexander Hamilton

## Taxonomy

[small islands, wide sons](position_small_islands_wide_sons.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

Born right here on Nevis under she cloud-ringed peak and raised in the Leeward isles, an orphan boy keeping a clerk's book, who teach himself the ledger and the law before the sea carry him north, and who rose to help found the United States, write the most of its founding argument, and build its treasury and its money: the islands' wide son before all the rest, and ah tell yuh, the pride of a country no bigger than a town that give the world a founder of another. Bright, restless, self-make, bound for far horizon, a small-island boy who answer the wide world as a equal. English he has whole and shaping: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in his mother tongue, and he [writes](process_writing_mother_tongue.md) in it with such force that as a boy on St. Croix he write a letter so vivid the island club together they money to send him be schooled abroad, and as a man he write the papers that argue a whole constitution into being. The merchant Dutch and the planters' French of the islands he [reads and follows](process_reading_followed.md) across the ledger and the wharf, the tongues of the trading sea he grow up beside. He write a nation into being in his mother tongue clear across a ocean, yet the island that bore him he leave as a boy and never lay eyes on again, so the home he carry in his English was a place his own words could never quite reach back to. As we say, the river run far from the spring but cyaan turn round and go home; the gap at the limit of the tongue, where the mother tongue reach everywhere except back to the small island that give it.

## Action

In [the Mother Colony](plot_01_the_mother_colony.md) he is the islands' wide son, the Nevis boy born under the great fort and the cane who cross the sea and help found another nation. By rising from the smallest of the Leeward isles to shape the United States, he prove [small islands, wide sons](position_small_islands_wide_sons.md): that a speck in the Atlantic can be the cradle of a founder, and that the islands' reach run far past they size. Small axe does fell big tree, the old people say.

## Shadow

The wide son who never come home is the islands' own shadow: the gift send out into the world and the world keep it, his face on a far nation's money while the small island that bore him hold only a plaque and a pride. The reach that is a leaving too, the brightest forever gone, till the islands come known abroad through the very sons who could not have stayed.

## Tell

He is the boy whose one letter buy him a passage off the island, ah tell yuh, and who spend the whole of his life building another country's foundation: the small island son make large by the very same sea that take him from it.
