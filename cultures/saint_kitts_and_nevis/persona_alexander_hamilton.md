# Persona: Alexander Hamilton

## Taxonomy

[small islands, wide sons](position_small_islands_wide_sons.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

Born on Nevis beneath her cloud-ringed peak and raised in the Leeward isles, an orphan clerk who taught himself the ledger and the law before the sea carried him north, and who rose to help found the United States, write much of its founding argument, and build its treasury and its money: the islands' wide son before all others, a point of national pride that a country no bigger than a town gave the world a founder of another. Brilliant, restless, self-made, and bound for far horizons, he was a small-island boy who answered the wide world as an equal. English he has whole and shaping: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in his mother tongue, and he [writes](process_writing_mother_tongue.md) in it with such force that as a boy on St. Croix he wrote a letter so vivid the island clubbed together to send him to be schooled abroad, and as a man he wrote the papers that argued a constitution into being. The merchant Dutch and the planters' French of the islands he [reads and follows](process_reading_followed.md) across the ledger and the wharf, the tongues of the trading sea he grew up beside. He wrote a nation into being in his mother tongue across an ocean, yet the island that bore him he left as a boy and never saw again, so that the home he carried in his English was a place his words could never quite return to - the gap at the limit of the tongue, where the mother tongue reaches everywhere except back to the small island that gave it.

## Action

In [the Mother Colony](plot_01_the_mother_colony.md) he is the islands' wide son, the boy born on Nevis under the great fort and the cane who crossed the sea and helped found another nation. By rising from the smallest of the Leeward isles to shape the United States, he proves [small islands, wide sons](position_small_islands_wide_sons.md): that a speck in the Atlantic can be the cradle of a founder, and that the islands' reach runs far beyond their size.

## Shadow

The wide son who never came home is the islands' own shadow: the gift sent out into the world and claimed by it, celebrated on a far nation's money while the small island that bore him keeps only a plaque and a pride. The reach that is also a leaving, the brightest forever gone, until the islands are known abroad through sons who could not have stayed.

## Tell

He is the boy whose letter bought him a passage off the island, and who spent his life building another country's foundations - the small island's son made large by the very sea that took him from it.
