# Persona: Sir Robert Bradshaw

## Taxonomy

[the labour nation and the federation](position_the_labour_nation_and_the_federation.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A factory man who rose up out of the sugar works to lead the cane worker, build the labour movement into a power, and carry St. Kitts toward self-government, honour as the father of the nation: the man in whom the whole labour founding stand up, the worker turn leader who take the field's struggle and make it a country. Forceful, proud, formal in how he carry himself, a labour chief who dress and talk with the dignity of a office he win from nothing at all. English he has whole and shaping: he [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and [thinks](process_thinking_mother_tongue.md) in his mother tongue, the crisp proper English of the platform and the legislature, and he [writes](process_writing_mother_tongue.md) in it the resolutions and the demands that organize a people, [the Kittitian-Nevisian tongue](position_language_en_kn.md) held in its proper platform register. But the tongue of the worker he lead was the warm Kittitian of the yard and the cane row, "leh we down tools, comrade," and that he [carries as his own](process_speaking_carried.md) and [hears](process_hearing_mother_tongue.md) in every meeting, the lilt the field truly talk in. He win a nation in the formal English of the empire he was leaving, yet the labour he raise it from talk a Kittitian the record seldom keep, so the platform tongue and the cane-row tongue never quite meet on the page. The hand that cut the cane is not always the hand that hold the pen; the gap at the limit of the tongue, where the language that win the nation is not always the language that suffer for it. He belongs to [Country Above Self](position_culture_country_above_self.md), and carries it as the labour founder who made the field itself the cradle of a nation.

## Action

In [Labour and Federation](plot_03_labour_and_federation.md) he lead the cane worker and the labour movement, building the power that carry the islands from colony toward independence. By organizing the field into a force, he found [the labour nation and the federation](position_the_labour_nation_and_the_federation.md): the worker make the nation, ah tell yuh, and the union hall is its true cradle.

## Shadow

The strong founder who hold the big island can come the big island's own shadow falling over the small one: the labour power centre on St. Kitts that leave Nevis feeling a partner nobody hearing, the very strength that win the nation straining the federation it was meant to hold together. The leader so sure where the centre of the union sit that the small sister reach for the door.

## Tell

He is the factory hand who wear the dignity of office like a tailor-make coat, and make a country out of a cane field: the worker who would answer the empire in its own crisp English, ah tell yuh, and win.
