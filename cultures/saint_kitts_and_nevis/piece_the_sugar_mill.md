# Piece: the sugar mill

## Taxonomy

Parent group: pieces

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Place

On near every hillside of both islands: the round stone towers of the old estate windmills, no roof and grow over with vine, that once turn the great rollers to crush the cane, scatter cross St. Kitts and Nevis among the fields, and beside them the rusting line of the old sugar railway that ring round St. Kitts to carry the cane to the central factory. The ruin mill is the most common monument the islands have, standing wherever there was once a estate, and the green vervet monkey now sit up on the broken wall where the overseer once stood.

## Load Bearing

It stand for the whole four century of the cane. The mill is where the harvest was crush and the wealth was make and the labour was spend, the still centre of the monoculture, and its ruin is the islands' truest record of the sugar age: the estate, the slavery, the emancipation, and the end. The railway that join them carry the cane to the last harvest of 2005, the last cane train pulling in, and now it carry the visitor round the island, the line of the industry come the line of the tour. Without these ruin towers the islands would have no standing memorial to the crop that make and bind them, no stone that say here was a estate and here the cane was crush.

## Apparent

A round tower of rough dark stone with no roof, open to the sky, vine climbing the walls where the sails once turn, and a thread of old narrow-gauge rail running through the cane fields gone to bush. In itself it is a broken-down thing of stone and iron; its weight is all in what it remember, the engine of the sugar age leave standing as its own gravestone cross two small islands.
