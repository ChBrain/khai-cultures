# Piece: the sugar mill

## Taxonomy

Parent group: pieces

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Place

On nearly every hillside of both islands: the round stone towers of the old estate windmills, roofless and ivy-grown, that once turned the great rollers to crush the cane, scattered across St. Kitts and Nevis among the fields, and beside them the rusting line of the old sugar railway that ringed St. Kitts to carry the cane to the central factory. The ruined mill is the most common monument the islands have, standing wherever there was once an estate.

## Load Bearing

It stands for the whole four centuries of the cane. The mill is where the harvest was crushed and the wealth was made and the labour was spent, the still centre of the monoculture, and its ruin is the islands' truest record of the sugar age: the estate, the slavery, the emancipation, and the end. The railway that joined them carried the cane to the last harvest of 2005 and now carries the visitor around the island, the line of the industry become the line of the tour. Without these ruined towers the islands would have no standing memorial to the crop that made and bound them, no stone that says here was an estate and here the cane was crushed.

## Apparent

A roofless round tower of rough dark stone, open to the sky, vines climbing the walls where the sails once turned, and a thread of old narrow-gauge rail running through the cane fields gone to scrub. In itself it is a derelict of stone and iron; its weight is entirely in what it remembers, the engine of the sugar age left standing as its own gravestone across two small islands.
