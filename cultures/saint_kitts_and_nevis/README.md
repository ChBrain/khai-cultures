# Saint Kitts and Nevis: a khai culture

Saint Kitts and Nevis staged as a full khai play, ISO KN, language en. The
smallest sovereign nation of the Americas, two volcanic isles that were the cradle
of the English sugar Caribbean, as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its
people as the cast, the roles its language and faith encode, its places, its
practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_saint_kitts_and_nevis.md](play_saint_kitts_and_nevis.md), the
  culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_en.md](pitch_en.md), the Hofstede layer, written from the
  Kittitian-Nevisian profile (a warm, courteous, close small-island culture, dual
  -island proud and dialect-rich, formal and familiar at once) and naming no
  dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Mother Colony, Emancipation and the Cane, Labour and
  Federation, and Beyond Sugar - each casting the elements it needs, and each
  staging the gap between the promise and the reality honestly.
- **Personas:** Alexander Hamilton, Sir Robert Bradshaw, Sir Simeon Daniel, and
  Caryl Phillips as the historic cast, plus one defining archetype, the sugar
  worker (the enslaved then freed hand of the cane in whom the whole monoculture is
  embodied). Each is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it
  holds.
- **Positions:** the Mother Colony and the sugar cradle (the first English colony,
  Brimstone Hill, and the sugar-slavery monoculture), the labour nation and the
  federation (the labour founding and the two-island union with Nevis's voice),
  small islands, wide sons (the tiny isles that sent figures to the world).
- **Places:** Brimstone Hill (the great fort, the Gibraltar of the West Indies,
  the cradle's glory and wound in one hill), Nevis (the sister isle, Hamilton's
  birthplace, the federation's other half).
- **Processes:** Sugar Mas (the National Carnival that makes a festival of the word
  for the harvest that bound the islands), Culturama (Nevis's own summer festival,
  the sister isle holding her distinct voice).
- **Pieces:** stewed saltfish and dumplings (the national dish, the rations of the
  estate made the food of a free nation), the sugar mill (the ruined estate
  windmills and the old railway, the islands' truest monument to the cane).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; slavery, the sugar monoculture, and the cradle's history are treated
soberly on the public record, with emancipation and labour at the centre; the
staging and the architecture are original work.
