# Plan: Country Above Self

## Taxonomy

The open mandate the nation set itself in its motto: to hold the two-island federation as one country above the claim of either isle, honoring the voice of Nevis without letting [the labour nation and the federation](position_the_labour_nation_and_the_federation.md) come apart at the strait; to find a future beyond the cane now that the cradle of [the Mother Colony and the sugar cradle](position_the_mother_colony_and_the_sugar_cradle.md) has cut its last harvest; and to carry the islands' reach in the world through their wide sons and scattered people, [small islands, wide sons](position_small_islands_wide_sons.md) answering the wide sea as an equal.

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Direction

Hold the federation whole, keeping St. Kitts and Nevis one country while Nevis's voice is heard and her standing kept, country held above either isle; build a future beyond sugar, remaking the smallest economy of the Americas on the sea, the visitor, and the islands' own people now that the cane is gone; and reckon soberly with the cradle, owning the slavery at the islands' foundation as the gap between the promise of freedom and the long reality of the field, carrying the history with dignity rather than denial.

## Orders

- To the descendants of [the sugar worker](persona_the_sugar_worker.md): carry the cradle's history whole, owning the bondage at the islands' foundation and the freedom made out of it, and dance the cane's memory rather than be silent before it.
- To the labour heirs of [Sir Robert Bradshaw](persona_robert_bradshaw.md) and the Nevisian voice of [Sir Simeon Daniel](persona_simeon_daniel.md): hold the federation as one country above either isle, the larger island never taking the union for granted and the sister isle finding her place within it rather than apart.
- To the nation: find a future beyond sugar without losing the federation or forgetting the cradle, and answer the wide world as an equal for all that it is small.

## Implementation

A federation four decades sovereign, the smallest nation in the Americas, that has held its two islands as one through a near-secession and kept the union whole; that cut its last sugar harvest in 2005 and turned to the sea and the visitor, the economy remade but not yet secure; and that carries the cradle's history in its forts and its mills and its festivals, the work of holding the union, finding the future, and reckoning with the past all standing open still.
