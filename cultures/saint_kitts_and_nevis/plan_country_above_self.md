# Plan: Country Above Self

## Taxonomy

The open mandate the nation set itself in its motto: to hold the two-island federation as one country above the claim of either isle, hearing the voice of Nevis proper without letting [the labour nation and the federation](position_the_labour_nation_and_the_federation.md) come apart at the strait; to find a future past the cane now the cradle of [the Mother Colony and the sugar cradle](position_the_mother_colony_and_the_sugar_cradle.md) cut its last harvest; and to carry the islands' reach in the world through they wide sons and scattered people, [small islands, wide sons](position_small_islands_wide_sons.md) answering the wide sea as a equal.

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Direction

Hold the federation whole, keeping St. Kitts and Nevis one country while Nevis's voice is heard and she standing kept, country hold up above either isle; build a future beyond sugar, remaking the smallest economy of the Americas on the sea, the visitor, and the islands' own people now the cane gone; and look the cradle dead in the eye, owning the slavery at the islands' foundation as the gap between the promise of freedom and the long truth of the field, carrying the history with dignity and no denial.

## Orders

- To the children of [the sugar worker](persona_the_sugar_worker.md): carry the cradle's history whole, owning the bondage at the islands' foundation and the freedom make out of it, and dance the cane's memory rather than stand silent before it.
- To the labour heirs of [Sir Robert Bradshaw](persona_robert_bradshaw.md) and the Nevisian voice of [Sir Simeon Daniel](persona_simeon_daniel.md): hold the federation as one country above either isle, the big island never taking the union for granted and the small sister finding she place within it rather than apart.
- To the nation: find a future beyond sugar without losing the federation or forgetting the cradle, and answer the wide world as a equal for all that it is small.

## Implementation

A federation four decade sovereign, the smallest nation in the Americas, that hold its two islands as one through a near-secession and keep the union whole; that cut its last sugar harvest in 2005 and turn to the sea and the visitor, the economy remake but not yet secure; and that carry the cradle's history in its forts and its mills and its festivals, the work of holding the union, finding the future, and squaring up to the past all standing open still.
