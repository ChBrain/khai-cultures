# Position: the labour nation and the federation

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The standing of a nation born not from the estate but from the field that worked it: the labour movement that rose out of the cane in the twentieth century, organized the worker, and carried the islands from colony to self-government to independence in 1983. It is the proposition that the worker made the nation, that the union hall is its true founding ground, and that the country is the labour nation. And it is, by the same act, the standing of a federation of two: St. Kitts and Nevis bound into one state across a narrow strait, the sister isle of Nevis a partner who has more than once felt herself the lesser and very nearly went alone, the union a thing held together by will against the pull of the two islands' difference.

## Orders

That the worker and the labour movement are the founders of this nation and are to be honored as such; that independence was won by organizing the field, not granted from above; and that the federation must be held as one while Nevis's voice is heard and kept, the larger island never taking the union for granted and the sister island finding her place within it rather than apart, country held above the pull of either isle alone.

## Loses

Ground when the federation forgets that it is two - when St. Kitts speaks as if it were the whole and Nevis is left to feel a colony of a colony, until the sister isle reaches again for the door; and ground, too, when the labour founding is forgotten, the union hall and the worker's struggle smoothed out of the nation's story in favour of a tidier birth.

## Drives

Lives in [Sir Robert Bradshaw](persona_robert_bradshaw.md), the labour leader and father of the nation who carried the islands toward independence, and in [Sir Simeon Daniel](persona_simeon_daniel.md), the first Premier of Nevis who held the sister isle's voice within the union. It drives Saint Kitts and Nevis to keep faith with its labour founding and to hold its two-island federation as one country above the claim of either isle.
