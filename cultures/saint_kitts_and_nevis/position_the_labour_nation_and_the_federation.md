# Position: the labour nation and the federation

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The standing of a nation born not from the estate but from the very field that work it: the labour movement that rose up out of the cane in the twentieth century, organize the worker, and carry the islands from colony to self-government to independence in 1983. It is the proposition that the worker make the nation, that the union hall is its true founding ground, and that this country is the labour nation. And it is, by the same act, the standing of a federation of two: St. Kitts and Nevis bound into one state across a narrow strait, the small sister Nevis a partner who more than once feel sheself the lesser and near enough went she own way alone, the union a thing hold together by sheer will against the pull of the two islands' difference.

## Orders

That the worker and the labour movement are the founders of this nation and are to be honour as such; that independence was win by organizing the field, not hand down from above; and that the federation must be hold as one while Nevis's voice is heard and kept, the big island never taking the union for granted and the small sister finding she place within it rather than apart, country hold up above the pull of either isle alone.

## Loses

Ground when the federation forget that it is two: when St. Kitts talk as if it was the whole of it and Nevis is leave to feel like a colony of a colony, till the small sister reach again for the door; and ground, too, when the labour founding is forget, the union hall and the worker's struggle smooth out of the nation's story for a tidier birth.

## Drives

Lives in [Sir Robert Bradshaw](persona_robert_bradshaw.md), the labour leader and father of the nation who carry the islands toward independence, and in [Sir Simeon Daniel](persona_simeon_daniel.md), the first Premier of Nevis who hold the small sister's voice within the union. It drive Saint Kitts and Nevis to keep faith with its labour founding and to hold its two-island federation as one country above the claim of either isle.
