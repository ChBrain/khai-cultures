# Plot: Beyond Sugar

## Taxonomy

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

For nearly four centuries the cane has been the islands' whole world, the one crop that made and bound them. By the new century the sugar runs at a loss, the world price gone against so small a grower, and the government faces the question no Kittitian had ever quite faced: what is St. Kitts without sugar.

## Action

In 2005 the last sugar harvest in the Caribbean is cut, the mills fall silent, and the railway that carried the cane is left to the rust and the visitor. The smallest nation of the Americas turns to the sea and the traveller for its future, the beaches and Brimstone Hill and the cloud-ringed peak of Nevis offered to the world that once bought its sugar. And the islands' reach runs on through their people: sons like Caryl Phillips carry the Caribbean and its scattering into the world's literature from the far side of the sea, the small isles present in great cities through the children they sent out. The cane is gone, and the islands begin again on what is left, the land and the water and the wide world.

## Tension

The end of sugar frees the islands from the crop that bound them and strips away the one thing they ever knew how to be: the cane is cut for the last time and there is relief in it and a hollow where four centuries stood. The smallest nation must remake itself on the sea and the visitor, holding its federation and its history together while it finds a future, the second chance of beyond-sugar shadowed by how little so small a country has to begin again with.
