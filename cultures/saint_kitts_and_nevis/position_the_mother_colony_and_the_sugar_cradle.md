# Position: the Mother Colony and the sugar cradle

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

The standing of St. Kitts as the first English colony in the whole Caribbean, plant in 1624 and make the seed-bed the English settle Antigua, Montserrat, Barbuda and beyond from, so the island earn she name as the Mother Colony of the West Indies. Up over the cane rose the great fort on Brimstone Hill, the Gibraltar of the West Indies, standing guard over the prize below. And the prize was sugar: a monoculture near total, cane plant right down to the very waterline, work under a slavery hard as any in the islands. It is a position found in contradiction, the cradle of a Caribbean build by the English and build on bondage, the mother of colonies and the mother of a unfree labour, the gap between the wealth it make and the cost it take present from the very first furrow.

## Orders

That these small islands are not no footnote but a foundation, the place where the English Caribbean start and spread out from; that the great fort and the ruin mills are a heritage to keep and not rub out; and that the cane and the bondage that grow it are to be carry sober-eyed, the cradle owning its history rather than hiding it, holding the wealth and the wound in the one same hand.

## Loses

Ground when the cradle is tell as glory alone, the proud first colony, the mighty fort, the rich estates, with the slavery that make it all left unsaid; and ground, too, when the islands are wave away as merely small, a speck in the sea, forgetting that this same speck was the mother of a whole colonial Caribbean and bore the weight of its founding.

## Drives

Lives in the sugar worker, the enslave and then free hand of the cane in whom the whole monoculture stand up, the one who bore the cradle's history in the very flesh and outlast it. It drive Saint Kitts and Nevis to keep the memory of the Mother Colony whole, the first colony, the great fort, and the bondage in the field, and to honour the worker who carry it.
