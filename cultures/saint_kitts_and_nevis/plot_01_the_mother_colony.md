# Plot: the Mother Colony

## Owner

## Cue

Long before the sail, the Kalinago hold the larger isle and call it Liamuiga, the fertile land. In 1624 an English party lands and plants the first lasting English colony in the whole Caribbean, the French soon sharing and fighting over the same small island, and the cane begins to climb from the shore.

## Action

St. Kitts becomes the Mother Colony of the West Indies, the seed-bed from which the English settle Antigua, Montserrat, Barbuda and beyond. The land turns almost wholly to sugar, cane to the waterline, worked by enslaved Africans carried across the sea, and high above the estates the great black-stone fortress rises on Brimstone Hill, the Gibraltar of the West Indies, guarding the prize below. Across the strait, on the sister isle of Nevis, beneath her cloud-ringed peak, a boy named Alexander Hamilton is born who will cross the sea and help found another nation. The English Caribbean begins here, on these two small isles, and begins already built on bondage.

## Tension

The islands are founded as the cradle of the whole English Caribbean, the proud first colony and the mighty fort - and founded already in contradiction, the wealth of the cane built on the bondage of the field, the mother of colonies and the mother of an unfree labour. The same small isles that sent a founder to another nation held thousands in slavery to grow their sugar, so the cradle carries both the founding and the wound from its first furrow.
