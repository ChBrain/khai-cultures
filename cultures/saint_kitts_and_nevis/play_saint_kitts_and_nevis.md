## Name

Leh we say it plain: the Federation of Saint Kitts and Nevis, two volcanic isles in the Leeward chain of the eastern Caribbean, the smallest sovereign state in all the Americas, by land and by people both. ISO KN, language en. We talk the proper English on the platform and in the law, and we talk the warm Kittitian-Nevisian in the yard, and ah tell yuh, we slide between the two so easy you cyaan find the seam. St. Kitts the big sister, the great green cone of Mount Liamuiga sitting up at she heart; Nevis the small sister, two mile across the strait, she peak forever ring round with cloud. A federation of two, tied by law and by a narrow channel of water, and we no always of one mind, no sah.

## Arc

Long before the sail come, the Kalinago held these isles and called the big one Liamuiga, the fertile land. The English land in 1624 and make St. Kitts they first colony in the whole Caribbean, the seed-bed they spread out from to settle Antigua, Montserrat, Barbuda and more, so the island earn she name as the Mother Colony of the West Indies. The French come share it and fight over it, and high up over the cane the great fort rose on Brimstone Hill, the Gibraltar of the West Indies, the black stone walls climbing right up the volcanic rock. The land turn almost whole to one crop: sugar, cane right down to the waterline, worked under a slavery hard as any in the islands, a monoculture near total that make a few man rich and hold the plenty in bondage. Emancipation come in 1834, but the cane stay king and the worker stay in the field, free by the law and tied to the estate still. Out of that same field, in time, the labour movement rose, and out of the labour movement a nation rose. Cross the strait, Nevis the small sister had give the world Alexander Hamilton, born under she peak; and she would give the federation its hardest question too, near enough seceding to go she own way alone. Independence come in 1983, two island make one country, the smallest in the whole hemisphere. The sugar run on till 2005, the last working sugar in all the Caribbean, and then the mills gone quiet and the last cane train pull in and the cane give way to the visitor and the sea. And through the whole of it run Sugar Mas at the turning of the year, the masquerade dancing down the street, a people who take the very word for the harvest that once held them and make it the name of they own carnival.

## Stakes

Whether the smallest sovereign nation of the Americas can hold its two-island federation as one across the narrow strait that part them, hearing Nevis proper without losing the union; whether it can find a future past the sugar that make it and bind it both, now the cane is cut for the last time and the sea is all that leave to sell; and whether it can carry the weight of being the cradle of the English sugar empire, looking the slavery at its foundation dead in the eye while building something free of it, or whether smallness, division, and the long shadow of the cane in the end outrun what so small a country can hold. When the two sister cyaan agree, the saying go, the strait grow wide; and ah tell yuh, the strait is only two mile.
