# Tonga: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-record historical sources behind the
plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_ton.md](pitch_ton.md) is written from Tonga's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
Where Tonga is not separately published, the values are read from the regional and
collectivist profile of the Pacific; the scores and their source are recorded here
so the data is on the record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Tonga profile (approx.)   |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | high                      |
| Individualism                 | low (strongly collective) |
| Motivation toward Achievement | moderate                  |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | moderate                  |
| Long Term Orientation         | low (tradition-honouring) |
| Indulgence                    | moderate                  |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the six-dimensions model of national
culture and The Culture Factor Group). Tonga is not always published as a separate
country; the profile is read as approximate from the regional and collectivist
Pacific data and is qualitative, not numeric.

The two defining personas, [the Tu'i Tonga](persona_tui_tonga.md) and
[e tu'a](persona_e_tua.md), are archetypes, not single historical individuals:
the ancient sacred paramount of the sea-empire, and the commoner, the tu'a, the
backbone of the kingdom who works the garden and the sea.
