# Tonga: a khai culture

Tonga staged as a full khai play, ISO TO, the prose in lea faka-Tonga and only the
headings in English. The only Pacific nation never colonized, staged as a theatre:
its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its language and
faith encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a
single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house that
holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its Estate;
a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_tonga.md](play_tonga.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_ton.md](pitch_ton.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Tonga's profile (deep respect and hierarchy of noble and commoner, a faith-filled
  and collective life, the Sabbath rest, and a proud sense of a kingdom never
  colonized) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** e Tu'i Tonga (the ancient sacred kingship and the maritime
  empire), fakatahataha mo e konisitutone (George Tupou I unifies and Christianizes
  the kingdom and grants the 1875 constitution), Kuini Salote mo e senituli (the
  beloved Queen Salote and the twentieth century), and e kalauni mo e kakai (the
  democratic turn, the 2006 unrest handled soberly, the diaspora and climate) -
  each casting the elements it needs.
- **Personas:** King George Tupou I, Queen Salote Tupou III, and 'Akilisi Pohiva
  as the historic cast, plus two defining archetypes, the Tu'i Tonga (the ancient
  sacred paramount of the sea-empire) and e tu'a (the commoner, the backbone of the
  kingdom). Each is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** pule ta'emotu'a (the unbroken crown of a kingdom never colonized),
  Tu'i Tonga mo e tahi (the ancient Tu'i Tonga and the maritime empire of voyaging),
  le'o 'o e kakai (the commoner's voice and the democratic turn, held within faith
  and the Sabbath).
- **Places:** Tongatapu (Nuku'alofa the capital and the royal palace), Lapaha (the
  ancient royal tombs, the langi, and the Ha'amonga 'a Maui trilithon).
- **Processes:** faikava (the kava circle of the kingdom), e Sapate (the nationwide
  Sunday rest).
- **Pieces:** the ta'ovala (the woven waist-mat of respect), the fangufangu (the
  Tongan nose flute).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-record historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources on the public record are credited, never
claimed; the 2006 unrest and the democratic transition are treated soberly and
take no side; the staging and the architecture are original work.
