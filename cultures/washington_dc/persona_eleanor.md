# Persona: Eleanor

## Taxonomy

[the national stage](position_national_stage.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A fifty-eight-year-old federal archivist in Washington, D.C. English, spoken with a formal midwestern accent, is her mother tongue, the language in which she [speaks, hears, and thinks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), the defining code of her professional life. It is the formal register of [Washington, D.C. English](position_language_en_us_dc.md), the transplant-standard cadence of the federal archive halls. She speaks no foreign languages. Yet the loss lies in the seam: The local go-go music of musicians like Marcus and the municipal struggles of [taxation without representation](position_taxation_without_representation.md) [she reads only as administrative reports](process_reading_followed.md) on her terminal, feeling that the community life of the District is being obscured by national political debates.

## Action

She archives legislative records, coordinates policy studies under [the D.C. statehood plan](plan_dc_statehood.md), and studies the history of [the national stage](position_national_stage.md).

## Shadow

Her focus on federal regulations and policy frameworks can make her elitist, ignoring the immediate material struggles of local neighborhoods that lack federal representation.

## Tell

She looks at a historic roll of legislative documents and says politely: "Our archives document the history of the entire nation, but they often ignore the voices of the citizens who live on the very streets outside these walls."
