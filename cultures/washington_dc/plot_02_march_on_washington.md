# Plot: The March on Washington 1963

## Taxonomy

[play_washington_dc](play_washington_dc.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

On August 28, 1963, over 250,000 civil rights demonstrators gather at the Lincoln Memorial for a massive demonstration.

## Action

The protest on [the National Mall](place_national_mall.md) projects the civil rights movement onto [the national stage](position_national_stage.md), culminating in Martin Luther King Jr.'s "I Have a Dream" speech. Marcus's parents participated in the march, inspiring his career in local community organizing.

## Stage

- [the National Mall](place_national_mall.md) : the monumental park that serves as the nation's primary stage for protest.
- [the national stage](position_national_stage.md) : the political and public arena where civil rights policies are debated.
- [Marcus](persona_marcus.md) : the local organizer who works to maintain the legacy of civil rights in D.C. neighborhoods.

## Tension

Despite the peaceful assembly, federal agencies monitor the organizers and local police prepare for riots, exposing the government's fear of civil unrest.
