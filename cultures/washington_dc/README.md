# Washington, D.C.: a khai culture

Washington, D.C. staged as a full khai play, ISO US-DC, language en. The federal capital: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its political, monumental, and local cultural life encodes, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house that holds this culture and answers for it.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_washington_dc.md](play_washington_dc.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_washington_dc.md](pitch_washington_dc.md), the Washington, D.C. key.
- **Plots (history):** the Residence Act & L'Enfant Plan of 1790, the March on Washington of 1963, and the Home Rule Act of 1973.
- **Personas:** Eleanor (federal archivist) and Marcus (local community organizer and musician), written in English.
- **Positions:** the national stage (power/monuments), taxation without representation (local struggle).
- **Places:** the National Mall, U Street.
- **Processes:** lobbying (policy advocacy), go-go jams (local music).
- **Pieces:** the Capitol Dome, the D.C. license plate.
- **Plan:** the D.C. statehood plan.
