# Position: D.C. culture

## Taxonomy

Parent group: positions

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Has

Belonging to a federal district built to be looked at rather than lived in, and lived in anyway. It carries [the national stage](position_national_stage.md), the grand diagonal avenues and marble monuments raised to project the whole country's unity, and it carries [taxation without representation](position_taxation_without_representation.md), the plain civic fact that the people who actually live among those monuments pay federal taxes and answer to a Congress in whose chamber they have no vote. It speaks [Washington, D.C. English](position_language_en_us_dc.md), the register of the transplant and the register of the native neighborhood both. What it hands to whoever grows up in it is a double vision: the capital's marble geometry as a backdrop to an ordinary life, and the ordinary life as a thing the backdrop keeps overshadowing.

## Orders

That the local, whether the go-go beat on a U Street corner or a neighborhood's own ballot for mayor and council, be granted the same seriousness as anything argued on the Hill; that a resident be permitted pride in the city's federal grandeur without letting that pride stand in for the vote the resident does not have; that the District's home rule, however partial, be defended and extended rather than taken as a settled compromise; and that whoever debates policy for a living, in an office or on a corner, treat the District's own residents as citizens of a place and not merely staff and spectators of a capital.

## Loses

Ground when the federal administrative view reads the District chiefly through legislative records and policy frameworks, treating the immediate material struggles of its neighborhoods as background noise to the national debate happening on the Mall; ground when development and federal money accelerate the displacement of the local communities, U Street among them, that gave the city its culture in the first place; and ground in the plain unresolved fact of the license plate's own slogan, a capital that can host a march for the whole nation's rights while its own residents wait, plot by plot, for the statehood that would grant them theirs.

## Drives

Drives the people of Washington, D.C. to hold the national and the local as one inheritance rather than two rival claims on what the city is for, the marble that speaks for the country and the go-go beat that speaks for the block answering to the same civic standard. It pushes residents to keep pressing the case for full representation, generation after generation, refusing to let the city's service to the nation's symbolism excuse the nation's failure to grant the city's own people a vote in the making of the laws they live under.
