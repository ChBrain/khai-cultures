# Plot: The Residence Act and L'Enfant Plan 1790

## Taxonomy

[play_washington_dc](play_washington_dc.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

In July 1790, the Residence Act establishes a federal district on the Potomac, and Pierre Charles L'Enfant designs its monumental layout.

## Action

The design establishes [the national stage](position_national_stage.md) of wide diagonal avenues and monumental public spaces, projecting the authority of the new republic. Eleanor curates early architectural plans in the federal archives, documenting L'Enfant's geometric layout.

## Stage

- [the national stage](position_national_stage.md) : the monumental public spaces, memorials, and corridors of federal power.
- [Capitol Dome](piece_capitol_dome.md) : the white cast-iron dome that stands as the center of the city's design.
- [Eleanor](persona_eleanor.md) : the federal archivist who preserves the city's historic blueprints.

## Tension

The construction relies on the labor of enslaved African Americans who cleared the malaria-prone marshes to build the federal city, a reality omitted from early celebratory descriptions.
