# Plot: The Home Rule Act 1973

## Taxonomy

[play_washington_dc](play_washington_dc.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

In December 1973, President Richard Nixon signs the D.C. Home Rule Act, establishing an elected Mayor and Council.

## Action

The act grants local self-governance, allowing D.C. residents to elect their own local leaders and assert [taxation without representation](position_taxation_without_representation.md) as a political protest. The D.C. [license plate](piece_dc_license_plate.md) is stamped with the slogan. Marcus coordinates local advocacy campaigns, while Eleanor archives the legislative histories.

## Stage

- [taxation without representation](position_taxation_without_representation.md) : the local protest against lack of voting representation in Congress.
- [license plate](piece_dc_license_plate.md) : the municipal plate bearing the protest slogan.
- [U Street](place_u_street.md) : the historic Black Broadway district that served as a center for local civil rights organizing.

## Tension

The act retains Congressional veto power over all local laws and budgets, limiting the District's autonomy and sparking ongoing debates over statehood.
