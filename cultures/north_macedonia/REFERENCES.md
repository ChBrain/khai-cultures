# North Macedonia: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_mk.md](pitch_mk.md) is written from North Macedonia's profile on
Geert Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no
number. The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the
record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | North Macedonia score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 90                              |
| Individualism                 | 22                              |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 45                              |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 87                              |
| Long Term Orientation         | 62                              |
| Indulgence                    | 35                              |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published
by Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group. Published data for North
Macedonia is limited and some dimensions are estimated or carried over from
former-Yugoslav and regional figures; the scores above should be read as
indicative rather than precise.

The two defining personas, [Lence](persona_lence.md) and [Stefan](persona_stefan.md),
are archetypes, not historical individuals: composites that carry the tension
between the older generation (Orthodox faith, the parish, the lake and the
churches of Ohrid, the embroidery, the Yugoslav-then-independent life) and the
younger one (the Prespa name-change, the EU path, emigration, and the Albanian
fellow citizens across the Vardar).
