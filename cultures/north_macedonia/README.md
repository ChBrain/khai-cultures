# North Macedonia: a khai culture

North Macedonia staged as a full khai play, ISO MK, language mk. A young nation
with deep roots, whose name, tongue, church and history each neighbour contests,
as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its
language and faith encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects,
all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_north_macedonia.md](play_north_macedonia.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_mk.md](pitch_mk.md), the Hofstede layer, written from North
  Macedonia's profile (deference to those above, family-bound and rooted in kin,
  anxious for certainty and for recognition, enduring, proud and a little wounded,
  hospitable) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Ohrid Literary School (9th-10th c.), the Ilinden
  Uprising (1903), the codification and Yugoslav Macedonia (1945), and the name
  dispute and the Prespa Agreement (2018) - each casting the elements it needs.
- **Personas:** Saint Naum of Ohrid, Goce Delcev, and Krste Misirkov as the
  historic cast, plus two defining archetypes, Lence (older, Orthodox faith, the
  lake and the churches of Ohrid) and Stefan (younger, the Prespa name-change, the
  EU path, emigration, the Albanian fellow citizens across the Vardar). Each is
  wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** the Macedonian language (South Slavic, Cyrillic, codified late
  and called a dialect by Bulgaria), the name dispute (Greece over the name,
  Bulgaria over the language, Serbia over the church; the Prespa renaming), Ohrid
  (the lake, the school of Clement and Naum, the cradle of Slavic letters).
- **Places:** Ohrid (the lake, the 365 churches, St Naum's monastery, the
  spiritual heart), Skopje (the capital, the 1963 earthquake and rebuild, the
  multiethnic city across the Vardar).
- **Processes:** Teshkoto (the solemn men's dance of endurance), Vodici (Epiphany
  and the diving for the cross in icy water).
- **Pieces:** the sun on the flag (the eight-rayed sun adopted after the Vergina
  sun was forced off), the Ohrid pearl (the lake's pearls made by a guarded family
  craft).
