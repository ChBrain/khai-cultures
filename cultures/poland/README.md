# Poland: a khai culture

Poland staged as a full khai play, ISO PL, language pl. A nation that outlived its
own erasure as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the
roles its language and faith encode, its places, its practices, and its defining
objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_poland.md](play_poland.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_pl.md](pitch_pl.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Poland's
  profile (romantic pride and martyrdom, a deep need for certainty, devotion to
  Church and mother tongue, wariness of powerful neighbours) and naming no
  dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Partitions (1772-1795), the Second World War
  (1939-1945), Solidarity (1980), and Free Poland (1989) - each casting the
  elements it needs.
- **Personas:** Adam Mickiewicz, John Paul II, and Lech Wałęsa as the historic
  cast, plus two defining archetypes, Halina (older, faith and the mother tongue
  kept through occupation and communism) and Kuba (younger, the post-2004
  emigrant whose Polish thins abroad). Each is wired to the language engine by the
  language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** język polski (the mother tongue that became the homeland), Kościół
  (the Church as the nation's backbone), martyrologia (the romantic myth of the
  martyr-nation).
- **Places:** Warszawa (the phoenix capital razed and rebuilt), Gdańsk (the Baltic
  port where Solidarity was born).
- **Processes:** Wigilia (the Christmas Eve supper with its empty place), pielgrzymka
  (the pilgrimage to Częstochowa).
- **Pieces:** Orzeł Biały (the White Eagle), pierogi (the dumplings folded by the
  whole family).
