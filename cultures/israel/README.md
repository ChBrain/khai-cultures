# Israel: a khai culture

Israel staged as a full khai play, ISO IL, language he. An ancient people of the
book that kept its faith, its festivals, and its sacred tongue through two
thousand years of dispersion and brought that tongue back to daily life, as a
theatre: its heritage as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its
language and faith encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects,
all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_israel.md](play_israel.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_he.md](pitch_he.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Israel's profile (warm and direct, family and community first, low formality
  and quick improvisation, set over a long memory of dispersion and the keeping
  of the book) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (heritage):** the covenant and the land, the keeping of the book in
  dispersion, the revival of Hebrew, and the ingathering and national renewal -
  each casting the elements it needs. The founding of modern national life is
  noted soberly as the ingathering and the renewal of national life, with
  dignity and no detail.
- **Personas:** Eliezer Ben-Yehuda (the reviver of spoken Hebrew), Hayim Nahman
  Bialik (the national poet), Shmuel Yosef Agnon (the Nobel laureate novelist),
  and David Ben-Gurion (the founding statesman, sober and on the public record)
  as the historic cast, plus the pioneer (he-halutz) as the defining archetype.
  Each is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** the revived tongue (the sacred language brought back to daily
  life), the people of the book (faith, text, and the study tradition), the
  ingathering (the communities gathered from across the world and the
  pioneering spirit).
- **Places:** Jerusalem (the holy city and the heart of memory), Tel Aviv (the
  new Hebrew city where the revived tongue is heard in the street).
- **Processes:** the Sabbath (the weekly day of rest and gathering), the
  Passover seder (the night the story is handed from generation to generation).
- **Pieces:** the Torah scroll (the portable home of the people of the book),
  the menorah (the ancient lamp and emblem of the kept light).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain heritage sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited, never
claimed; the culture is staged strictly from within its own enduring cultural
and religious self-understanding, taking no political side. Contemporary
political disputes are out of scope; the staging and the architecture are
original work.
