# Ireland: a khai culture

Ireland staged as a full khai play, ISO IE, first official language Irish (ga), daily
language English (en). A nation that lost its language and won a literature, staged as a
theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its languages
encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house that holds
this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its Estate; a culture
with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_ireland.md](play_ireland.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_ie.md](pitch_ie.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Ireland's
  profile (easy, witty, low on rank and on the need for rules, sociable and indulgent,
  with a long melancholy beneath) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Great Famine, the Easter Rising of 1916, the literary revival,
  and modern, Celtic-Tiger Ireland - each casting the elements it needs.
- **Personas:** Pádraig Pearse (who tied the Irish language to freedom) and James Joyce
  (who made English a world literature from exile) as the historic cast, plus three
  archetypes: Bríd (a Gaeltacht native speaker), Cian (a young Dubliner who owns Irish
  only as school scraps), and Mary (the rural, Catholic, emigration generation). Each is
  wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** an Ghaeilge (the first official language, revered and barely spoken),
  the Church (the fallen backbone of identity), emigration (the diaspora and the leaving).
- **Places:** Dublin (the capital of the Rising, of Ulysses, and of the tech boom), an
  Ghaeltacht (the Irish-speaking West).
- **Processes:** an seisiún (the traditional music session), the GAA (the amateur,
  parish-rooted Gaelic games).
- **Pieces:** the harp (the Gaelic national emblem), the pint (the pub, the craic, and
  its shadow).
- **Plan:** athbheochan na Gaeilge, the standing project to revive Irish as a living
  tongue.
