# Persona: Pádraig Pearse

## Taxonomy

[an Ghaeilge](position_an_ghaeilge.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The schoolmaster, poet, and revolutionary who made the Irish language the soul of the cause and his own death its seal. [He speaks, writes, and thinks in](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) English, the tongue of his Dublin childhood, but the Irish he had to learn as a young man he [forges with devotion](process_writing_drafted.md) into the language of his school and the heart of the nation. There is the gap: the revivalist's own first tongue is English, and he proclaims a Republic, and dies for it, in the coloniser's language, while giving his soul to [an Ghaeilge](position_an_ghaeilge.md), the tongue the nation he frees already barely speaks.

## Action

In [the Easter Rising of 1916](plot_02_easter_rising_1916.md) he reads the Proclamation on the steps of the GPO and gives himself to the blood-sacrifice, binding the freedom of Ireland to the revival of its language.

## Shadow

The sacrifice he glorifies can become a cult of the heroic dead, and the language he dies for, the freed nation will honour but barely speak: he hands it an ideal it cannot quite live up to, in a tongue it has already half lost.

## Tell

In his speeches he slips from English into Irish for the ringing phrase, and the crowd loves the sound of it without following the words.
