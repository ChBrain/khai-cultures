## Owner

## Shown

The capital above on the Liffey: Georgian streets and tenement ghosts, the General Post Office on O'Connell Street, the pubs and the Abbey, the city Joyce mapped out street by street in Ulysses, and now, would you believe it, the European headquarters of the world's tech giants.

## Holds

- [The Easter Rising, 1916](plot_02_easter_rising_1916.md): here the rebels seize the GPO and proclaim out the Republic.
- [The Literary Revival](plot_03_the_literary_revival.md): here is the very city Joyce turns into the century's novel.
- [Modern Ireland](plot_04_modern_ireland.md): here the Celtic Tiger and the tech multinationals go and remake a poor city into a rich one.

## Offers

The stage where Ireland argues away with itself and shows itself off to the world: rebellion, literature, and boom, a small city that has carried the whole of a nation's history and now houses a slice of the global economy as well.

## Withheld

The Irish language as the daily living. Dublin speaks English through and through, so it does; the tongue it honours on its signs and learns off in its schools it does not live in at all, and the city withholds from the language the one thing it's after needing, to be spoken.
