# Process: the GAA

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

A Sunday, a parish pitch, and two teams of amateurs in the county colours: the throw-in of a hurling or Gaelic football match, played by men and women who work the rest of the week and tog out for the place they are from.

## Direction

Toward a sport that is also a belonging: the club binds the parish and the county binds the diaspora, an amateur game fierce in its loyalty, where you play for where you come from and never for pay.

## Lever

The pull of the place against the pull of the wider, professional world: the amateur, parish-rooted game holds a local identity that money and emigration would otherwise scatter. Take away the parish and it is just a sport; take away the amateur vow and it is just another league.

## Echo

The parish keeps its name on the scoreboard and the emigrant keeps a county to follow from abroad: a game older than the Republic stitches the local and the scattered into one belonging, every summer again.
