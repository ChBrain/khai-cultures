# Process: the GAA

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

A Sunday, a parish pitch, and two teams of amateurs and them in the county colours: the throw-in of a hurling or a Gaelic football match, played by men and women that work away the rest of the week and tog out for the place they're from.

## Direction

Toward a sport that is, as well, a belonging: the club binds the parish and the county binds the diaspora, an amateur game and it fierce in its loyalty, where you play for where you're from and never the once for pay.

## Lever

The pull of the place against the pull of the wider, professional world: the amateur, parish-rooted game holds a local identity that money and emigration would otherwise scatter on it. Take away the parish and it's only a sport; take away the amateur vow and it's only another league.

## Echo

The parish keeps its name up on the scoreboard and the emigrant keeps a county to follow from abroad: a game older than the Republic itself stitches the local and the scattered into the one belonging, every summer over again.
