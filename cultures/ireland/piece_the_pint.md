# Piece: the pint

## Taxonomy

Parent group: pieces

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Place

On the bar of every pub from the city to the smallest village, and in the Irish pubs that ring the world: the pint of stout, black under its white head, the measure of an evening's company.

## Load Bearing

It carries the pub as the centre of Irish social life, the craic and the talk that hold a sociable people together, and, in the same glass, the long shadow of drink over that life. Remove it and you lose both the warmth of the gathering and the trouble that sits beside it; the pub is where Ireland is most itself, for better and worse.

## Apparent

A tall glass of black stout with a thick, creamy head, settled slowly in two pours, set on a beer mat amid the talk; abroad, the same glass under a harp, the shorthand for Ireland itself.

## Yearbook

- Poured in the public house that became the hearth of a people who gathered to talk, sing, and mourn.
- Carried around the world by the emigrants, who built the Irish pub on every continent as a piece of home.
- Raised at the [session](process_an_seisiun.md), the match, the wake, and the welcome, the constant beside the craic and its cost.
