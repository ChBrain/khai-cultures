# Piece: the pint

## Taxonomy

Parent group: pieces

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Place

On the bar of every pub from the city down to the smallest village, and in the Irish pubs that ring the whole world: the pint of stout, black under its white head, the measure of an evening's company.

## Load Bearing

It carries the pub as the heart of Irish social life, the craic and the talk that hold a sociable people the one together, and, in the very same glass, the long shadow of the drink over that life. Take it away and you'd lose both the warmth of the gathering and the trouble that sits in beside it; sure the pub is where Ireland is most itself, for better and for worse.

## Apparent

A tall glass of black stout with a thick, creamy head on it, settled slowly in the two pours, and it set on a beer mat in the middle of the talk; abroad, the very same glass under a harp, the shorthand for Ireland itself.

## Yearbook

- Poured in the public house that became the hearth of a people that gathered in to talk, to sing, and to mourn.
- Carried the world over by the emigrants, who built the Irish pub on every continent going as a bit of home.
- Raised at the [session](process_an_seisiun.md), the match, the wake, and the welcome, the constant in beside the craic and its cost.
