# Persona: Cian

## Taxonomy

[an Ghaeilge](position_language_ga_ie.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A young fella from Dublin, at home in a rich, open, English-speaking Ireland and working away in one of the tech multinationals. [He speaks, hears, and thinks in](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) [English](position_language_en_ie.md), the only tongue he has any real fluency in. The Irish of his thirteen years of school he [catches only in scraps](process_hearing_caught.md), a cúpla focal on the road signs and on TG4. There's the gap for you: he owns [an Ghaeilge](position_language_ga_ie.md) on paper, the first official language, his by right and by schooling, and yet he could barely put a sentence of it together; the national tongue is a fondness and a small guilt on him, not a voice at all, while the world's English he was born speaking costs him no bother whatever. He belongs, all the same, to [Irishness](position_culture_irishness.md), the nation that reveres a tongue it hardly speaks and lives easy in the one that replaced it.

## Action

He lives the whole of his working and social life in English, builds himself a career in the global tech firms that made Dublin rich, and feels the faint guilt of the Irish he was taught and let slip away on him.

## Shadow

His ease in the world's language hides the fact that he's a stranger to his own first official tongue, and he mistakes a warm fondness for it for actually having the thing at all; the loss feels like nobody's fault whatsoever, and so it goes unmended.

## Tell

He'd sing you the national anthem in Irish by the sound of it at a match, mouthing away at the words without quite knowing what it is they mean at all.
