# Persona: James Joyce

## Taxonomy

[emigration](position_emigration.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The exile who made the coloniser's language his own and turned a single Dublin day into the century's novel. [He writes, reads, and thinks in](process_writing_mother_tongue.md) English and bends it as no one before him, and the Italian, French, and German of his wandering life in Trieste, Zurich, and Paris he [wears as a vagabond's tools](process_speaking_worn.md). The Irish-language revival he leaves cold. There is the gap: Ireland's greatest gift to the world is written in the very tongue it had lost its own to, by a man who took the boat and never truly came home.

## Action

In [the Literary Revival](plot_03_the_literary_revival.md) he turns from the parochial, Irish-Ireland movement and, from [exile](position_emigration.md), writes Ulysses, proving that the nation's genius will speak to the world in English.

## Shadow

The exile can only possess Ireland by leaving it: he fixes the city forever as the place he fled, and the freedom he wins in language he buys by refusing to live among his own.

## Tell

For all his scorn of home, he writes of nothing but Dublin, down to the names of its shops and the turns of its streets, mapping in exile the one place he could not stay.
