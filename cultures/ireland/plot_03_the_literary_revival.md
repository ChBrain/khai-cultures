# Plot: The Literary Revival

## Taxonomy

[play_ireland](play_ireland.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

At the turn of the twentieth century, a generation sets out to give Ireland a national literature, some reaching back to Irish, others writing the country in English.

## Action

The lasting work proves to be in English: from [exile](position_emigration.md), [James Joyce](persona_joyce.md) turns a single Dublin day into Ulysses and remakes the novel, while Yeats and the Abbey build a theatre in English. Ireland's gift to the world arrives in the coloniser's tongue.

## Stage

- [James Joyce](persona_joyce.md): the exile who makes English the medium of the nation's genius.
- [Dublin](place_dublin.md): the city Joyce maps street by street from abroad.

## Tension

The nation finds its voice in the very language it lost its own to: the literary triumph is also the quiet proof that English has taken the daily ground the revival hoped to win back, and that the country's greatness and its loss are written in the same words.
