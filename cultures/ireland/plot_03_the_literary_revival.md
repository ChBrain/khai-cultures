# Plot: The Literary Revival

## Taxonomy

[play_ireland](play_ireland.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

At the turn of the twentieth century, a generation sets out to give Ireland a national literature of its own, some of them reaching back to Irish, more of them writing the country in English.

## Action

The lasting work proves to be in English: from [exile](position_emigration.md), [James Joyce](persona_joyce.md) turns the one single Dublin day into Ulysses and remakes the novel out of it, while Yeats and the Abbey build a theatre in English. Ireland's gift to the world arrives in the coloniser's own tongue.

## Stage

- [James Joyce](persona_joyce.md): the exile who makes English the medium of the nation's whole genius.
- [Dublin](place_dublin.md): the city Joyce maps out street by street and him abroad.

## Tension

The nation finds its voice in the very language it lost its own to: the literary triumph is, as well, the quiet proof that English is after taking the daily ground the revival hoped to win back, and that the country's greatness and its loss are written the both in the one set of words.
