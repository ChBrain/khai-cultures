## Cue

In the 1840s, hundreds of thousands of pioneers transit the Platte River valley, using geologic landmarks like Chimney Rock.

## Action

The transit establishes landmark pioneer trails, developing [pioneer_trail](position_pioneer_trail.md) routes. The [sod brick](piece_sod_brick.md) represents early building methods. Helen coordinates historical trail marking programs, while John preserves oral stories of early cattle drives along the river basin.

## Stage

- [pioneer trail](position_pioneer_trail.md) : the frontier navigation, homestead grit, and trail preservation.
- [sod brick](piece_sod_brick.md) : the grass sod building block representing pioneer housing.
- [place_chimney_rock](place_chimney_rock.md) : the geologic sandstone pillar in western Nebraska.

## Tension

The heavy pioneer traffic depletes local timber and grass resources, triggering conflicts with Native American nations.
