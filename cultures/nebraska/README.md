# Nebraska: a khai culture

Nebraska staged as a full khai play, ISO US-NE, language en. The Cornhusker State: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its Oregon Trail migration, Homestead Act sod claims, and Arbor Day inauguration encodes, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house that holds this culture and answers for it.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_nebraska.md](play_nebraska.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_nebraska.md](pitch_nebraska.md), the Nebraska key.
- **Plots (history):** the Oregon Trail Migration of the 1840s, the Homestead Act Claims of 1862, and the Arbor Day Inauguration of 1872.
- **Personas:** John (Sandhills rancher) and Helen (Arbor Day advisor), written in English.
- **Positions:** pioneer trail (frontier navigation/homestead grit), prairie forestry (tree windbreak planting/soil conservation).
- **Places:** Chimney Rock, Morton Arboretum.
- **Processes:** cattle branding (ranch identification branding), tree sapling planting (conservation tree planting).
- **Pieces:** a sod brick, a branding iron.
- **Plan:** the Nebraska windbreak plan.
