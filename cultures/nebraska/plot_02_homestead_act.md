# Plot: The Homestead Act Claims 1862

## Taxonomy

[play_nebraska](play_nebraska.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

In May 1862, President Abraham Lincoln signs the Homestead Act, allowing settlers to claim sod land for agricultural farms.

## Action

The act establishes prairie farming settlements, developing [cattle branding](process_cattle_branding.md). The [branding iron](piece_branding_iron.md) represents ranch tools. John coordinates cattle brand records on Sandhills ranches, while Helen manages safety databases for early agricultural land offices.

## Stage

- [pioneer trail](position_pioneer_trail.md) : the frontier settlement, homestead grit, and farm self-reliance.
- [branding iron](piece_branding_iron.md) : the steel cattle brand stamp representing ranching.
- [place_chimney_rock](place_chimney_rock.md) : the geologic trail marker serving as a homestead boundary landmark.

## Tension

Rapid sod cultivation removes native prairie grasses, leaving the topsoil vulnerable to severe wind and water erosion.
