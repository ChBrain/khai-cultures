# Persona: John

## Taxonomy

[pioneer trail](position_pioneer_trail.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A rancher in the Sandhills, thirty-eight and not much for talk until the herd's counted. English, in a prairie cadence, is his mother tongue, the language he [speaks, hears, and thinks in](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md), the code his daily ranching runs on. It is the [Nebraskan English](position_language_en_us_ne.md) of the Sandhills ranch, the even cadence of cattle country. He speaks no foreign languages. Where it thins is with the conservationists: the slower, tradition-bound talk of Helen and the quiet history of [prairie forestry](position_prairie_forestry.md), he [reads only as routine paperwork](process_reading_followed.md) on his workstation, sensing that the patient, resource-conserving rhythm of early windbreak planting counts for less against the push for more cattle, faster. He holds [Cornhusker culture](position_culture_cornhusker_culture.md) too, for the fence line and the trail he rides are the same pioneer grit that culture was founded on.

## Action

He preserves [branding iron](piece_branding_iron.md) tools, coordinates windbreak planting under [the Nebraska windbreak plan](plan_nebraska_windbreak_afforestation.md), and studies [pioneer trail](position_pioneer_trail.md).

## Shadow

His intense focus on livestock metrics and commercial beef trade can make him indifferent to municipal environmental zoning problems in urban counties.

## Tell

He adjusts a telemetry sensor on a water tank and says directly: "We are calibrating high-volume livestock watering systems; you can't run a profitable ranch if you're only focused on historic local preservation."
