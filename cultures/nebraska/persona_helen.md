# Persona: Helen

## Taxonomy

[prairie forestry](position_prairie_forestry.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

A conservation advisor in Nebraska City, fifty-two and still measuring the year by what's been proven to grow. English, in a prairie drawl, is her mother tongue, the language she [speaks](process_speaking_mother_tongue.md) and writes her daily tree-planting reports in. It is the [Nebraskan English](position_language_en_us_ne.md) of the Nebraska City arbor plot, the drawl of the conservation office. She speaks no foreign languages. Where her patience runs out is with the ranchers: the quick, technology-oriented talk of men like John and the digital demands of agricultural boards, she [hears only as background static](process_hearing_followed.md), sensing that the slower, law-protecting rhythm of municipal forest stewardship counts for less next to livestock feeding and speedway entertainment. She holds [Cornhusker culture](position_culture_cornhusker_culture.md) too, for the windbreak she plants row by row is the same Arbor Day inheritance that culture was founded on.

## Action

She preserves [sod brick](piece_sod_brick.md) records, coordinates field conservation under [the Nebraska windbreak plan](plan_nebraska_windbreak_afforestation.md), and studies [pioneer trail](position_pioneer_trail.md).

## Shadow

Her pride in traditional property rights and local county independence can make her suspicious of state-wide zoning and environmental agricultural mandates, fearing they will restrict family property use.

## Tell

She looks at a historic forestry report and says thoughtfully: "The forest has its own cycles; you can't protect prairie topsoil if you're only focused on digital speed and livestock profit metrics."
