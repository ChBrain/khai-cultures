# Louisiana: a khai culture

Louisiana staged as a full khai play, ISO US-LA, language en. The Pelican State: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its French/Spanish Creole, Cajun, and Mississippi delta water life encodes, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house that holds this culture and answers for it.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_louisiana.md](play_louisiana.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_louisiana.md](pitch_louisiana.md), the Louisiana key.
- **Plots (history):** the Louisiana Purchase of 1803, the Battle of New Orleans in 1815, and the Great Mississippi Flood of 1927.
- **Personas:** Yvette (New Orleans preservationist and jazz historian) and Jean (Cajun crawfish fisherman and swamp guide), written in English.
- **Positions:** Creole pluralism (cultural synthesis), bayou resilience (water adaptation).
- **Places:** the French Quarter, Bayou Teche.
- **Processes:** crawfish boils (communal cooking), jazz funerals (musical celebration).
- **Pieces:** a crawfish trap, a brass trumpet.
- **Plan:** the coastal master plan.
