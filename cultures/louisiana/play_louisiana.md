## Name

Louisiana, the Pelican State in the Gulf Coast region of the United States. ISO US-LA, language en.

## Arc

The gumbo pot got here first: French and Spanish colonial rule poured in, and Creole and Cajun came out. The Louisiana Purchase of 1803 signed the territory over, and the mixed coalition of Creoles, free men of color, pirates, and militia who beat the British at New Orleans in 1815 proved this place holds together when it counts. The Great Mississippi Flood of 1927 broke the levees and rewrote how the country handles a river. Louisiana keeps the pot on - gumbo, the horn line, the second line - even while the coast washes out from under the Delta, wetland by wetland.

The United States purchases the territory from France, doubling the nation's size and introducing a complex civil law system.

**[Plot 2: The Battle of New Orleans 1815](plot_02_battle_new_orleans.md)**

General Andrew Jackson coordinates a diverse force of Creoles, free Black soldiers, pirates, and militia to defeat a British invasion.

The Mississippi River breaks its levees, flooding millions of acres, displacing populations, and prompting the federal government to assume control of river management.

## Stakes

Whether a state sitting on the delta of the country's biggest river can push through the water diversion and engineering it needs to hold what's left of its vanishing coastal wetlands.
