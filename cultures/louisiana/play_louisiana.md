## Name

Louisiana, the Pelican State in the Gulf Coast region of the United States. ISO US-LA, language en.

## Arc

A land of cultural synthesis and water-bound adaptation, defined by its unique French and Spanish colonial roots. From the Louisiana Purchase of 1803 and the diverse coalition that fought the Battle of New Orleans, to the devastating Great Mississippi Flood of 1927 that reshaped national flood control. A culture that prioritizes indulgence, culinary arts, and musical celebration, even as it faces coastal erosion and the disappearing wetlands of the Mississippi Delta.

**[Plot 1: The Louisiana Purchase 1803](plot_01_louisiana_purchase.md)**

The United States purchases the territory from France, doubling the nation's size and introducing a complex civil law system.

**[Plot 2: The Battle of New Orleans 1815](plot_02_battle_new_orleans.md)**

General Andrew Jackson coordinates a diverse force of Creoles, free Black soldiers, pirates, and militia to defeat a British invasion.

The Mississippi River breaks its levees, flooding millions of acres, displacing populations, and prompting the federal government to assume control of river management.

## Stakes

Whether a state built on the delta of the nation's largest river system can successfully implement massive water diversion and engineering to restore its disappearing coastal wetlands.
