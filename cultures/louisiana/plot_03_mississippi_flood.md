# Plot: The Great Mississippi Flood 1927

## Taxonomy

[play_louisiana](play_louisiana.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

In the spring of 1927, the Mississippi River breaks its levee systems, flooding 27,000 square miles and displacing hundreds of thousands of residents.

## Action

The disaster prompts the federal government to assume control of river management, establishing [bayou resilience](position_bayou_resilience.md) as a key regional survival strategy. The [crawfish trap](piece_crawfish_trap.md) represents the water-based livelihood maintained during seasonal floods. Jean's family relocated to the high ground of Acadiana during this displacement, while Yvette documents the flood's impact on delta jazz music.

## Stage

- [bayou resilience](position_bayou_resilience.md) : the self-reliance and community coordination in response to rising waters.
- [crawfish trap](piece_crawfish_trap.md) : the wire trap used to harvest crawfish in the flooded basin.
- [crawfish boils](process_crawfish_boil.md) : the communal cooking gatherings that brought displaced families together.

## Tension

To protect New Orleans, city officials dynamite the levee at Caernarvon, intentionally flooding poor rural parishes and causing massive displacement.
