# Plot: The Louisiana Purchase 1803

## Taxonomy

[play_louisiana](play_louisiana.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Cue

In April 1803, the United States purchases the Louisiana Territory from France for fifteen million dollars, doubling the nation's land area.

## Action

The acquisition establishes [Creole pluralism](position_creole_pluralism.md) within the expanding republic, incorporating French civil law and Catholic parishes. Yvette preserves early French-language land deeds in New Orleans, presenting the complex history of the transfer.

## Stage

- [Creole pluralism](position_creole_pluralism.md) : the mixture of French, Spanish, African, and Native heritages that defined early Louisiana.
- [the French Quarter](place_french_quarter.md) : the historic heart of New Orleans where the transfer documents were proclaimed.
- [Yvette](persona_yvette.md) : the New Orleans archivist who preserves the colonial records and legal agreements.

## Tension

The purchase incorporates a population that did not speak English and practiced a different legal system, leading to decades of political tension over Americanization.
