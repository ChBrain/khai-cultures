# Process: crawfish boils

## Taxonomy

Parent group: processes

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Initiated by

Cajun families, community organizations, and restaurant chefs, conducted throughout the spring and early summer harvest season in Acadiana.

## Direction

Around the outdoor boiling rig. Purging live crawfish, boiling them in a large seasoned pot with cayenne pepper, corn, and potatoes, and dumping the mixture onto newspaper-covered tables.

## Lever

The communal culinary practice that reinforces family and regional ties. It supports local fishermen and seafood markets, though it is highly dependent on seasonal catches and water levels in the basin.

## Echo

The boil provides a space for community gathering. It defines the family weekends of [Jean](persona_jean.md) in Acadiana, while New Orleans historian [Yvette](persona_yvette.md) values the practice as a preservation of traditional Cajun cooking.
