# Greenland (Kalaallit Nunaat): a khai culture

Greenland staged as a full khai play, ISO GL (an autonomous country of the Kingdom of Denmark), language kl. The largest island in the world as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its Arctic and hunting life encodes, its places, its practices, and its defining objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_greenland.md](play_greenland.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_greenland.md](pitch_greenland.md), the Hofstede layer, written from Greenland's profile inflected by its harsh Arctic environment and hunting roots, naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the Norse settlement and disappearance, Hans Egede's colonization, and the achievement of Home Rule — each casting the elements that event needs.
- **Personas:** Nuka (traditional hunter archetype) and Pipaluk (modern Nuuk youth), written with Kalaallisut/Greenlandic language reads.
- **Positions:** das Jagdrecht (the hunting tradition), Kalaallisut (the language variety), der Eisschutz (the ice connection).
- **Places:** Nuuk (the capital), Ilulissat (the icefjord).
- **Processes:** das Qajaq (kayaking).
- **Pieces:** der Tupilak (the spirit figure), das Ulo (the traditional knife).
