# the Democratic Republic of the Congo: a khai culture

The Democratic Republic of the Congo staged as a full khai play, ISO CD, official
language French, with four national languages (Lingala, Swahili, Kikongo,
Tshiluba). A subcontinent of a country joined by its river and its music, staged
as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the roles its
languages and arts encode, its places, its practices, and its defining objects,
all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_dr_congo.md](play_dr_congo.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_fr.md](pitch_fr.md), the Hofstede layer, written from the
  DRC's profile (a strongly collective, group-and-family orientation, deference to
  elders and authority held with distance, and a warm, present-focused way of
  living together) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the kingdoms (Kongo, Luba, Kuba), the river that unites,
  the nation standing, and the rumba that conquers the world - each casting the
  elements it needs, staged through the country's grandeur, culture, and music.
- **Personas:** Franco Luambo Makiadi, Tabu Ley Rochereau, and Papa Wemba as the
  musicians who carried the rumba to the world, Patrice Lumumba honoured soberly
  as a national figure, plus one archetype, the sapeur (elegance as dignity).
  Each is wired to the language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** the river (the country held by its great waterway), the rumba
  (the nation that speaks to the world through its music), the country-continent
  (a plural land the size of a subcontinent, four national languages under
  French).
- **Places:** Kinshasa (Kinshasa la belle, capital and music capital of the
  continent), the Congo River (the second river in the world and the spine of the
  country).
- **Processes:** the rumba (joy and sorrow danced together) and the sape
  (elegance worn as dignity).
- **Pieces:** the seben guitar (the voice of Congolese music) and the Kuba
  textile (the raphia velvet of the kingdoms' arts).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-domain cultural and historical
sources are recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the
prose; the pitch is its invisible hand. Sources in the public domain are credited,
never claimed. The staging is original work and treats the nation soberly, through
its grandeur, its peoples, its arts, and its music, taking no political side.
