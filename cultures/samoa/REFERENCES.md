# Samoa: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-record historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_smo.md](pitch_smo.md) is written from Samoa's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no number.
Samoa does not appear among the countries individually scored in the published
Hofstede data; the pitch is written from the regional Polynesian and Pacific
profile and from Samoa's own self-understanding, so the orientation is recorded
here qualitatively, without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Samoa orientation (qualitative) |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------------- |
| Power Distance                | high (the matai and the fono)   |
| Individualism                 | low (the aiga before the self)  |
| Motivation toward Achievement | moderate                        |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | moderate                        |
| Long Term Orientation         | low to moderate                 |
| Indulgence                    | moderate                        |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the six-dimensions model of national
culture). As Samoa is not separately scored in the published data, the values
above are a qualitative reading from the regional Pacific profile and from the
faaSamoa itself, which reads as strongly communal, respectful, and hierarchical
under the matai.

The defining persona [le matai](persona_le_matai.md) is an archetype, not a
historical individual: a composite of the chief and head of the aiga who carries
the faaSamoa, the title, the land, and the voice in the fono, and who holds the
Samoan mother tongue whole while the English of school and the wider world is only
carried.
