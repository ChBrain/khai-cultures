# Samoa: a khai culture

Samoa staged as a full khai play, ISO WS, language smo. The heart of Polynesia,
the Navigators' Islands, a nation held together by the faaSamoa, the matai, and
the aiga, as a theatre: its history as the plot line, its people as the cast, the
roles its language and faith encode, its places, its practices, and its defining
objects, all tuned by a single pitch.

## Estate

[khai-cultures](https://github.com/ChBrain/khai-cultures): the Cultures house
that holds this culture and answers for it. This culture logs the house as its
Estate; a culture with no Estate is not yet a production.

## The production

- **Anchor:** [play_samoa.md](play_samoa.md), the culture itself.
- **Pitch:** [pitch_smo.md](pitch_smo.md), the Hofstede layer, written from
  Samoa's profile (deeply communal and respectful, hierarchical under the matai,
  faith-filled and oratorical, the aiga before the self and the measured speech of
  the chief) and naming no dimension.
- **Plots (history):** the navigators and the Polynesian settlement of the
  Navigators' Islands, German and New Zealand administration and the Mau, the
  independence of 1962, and the faaSamoa today of the diaspora, the word, rugby,
  and the climate - each casting the elements it needs, and each staging the
  colonial era and the Mau soberly and with dignity.
- **Personas:** Tupua Tamasese Lealofi III, Olaf Frederick Nelson, Malietoa
  Tanumafili II, and Albert Wendt as the historic cast, plus the matai as the
  defining archetype of the chief and head of the aiga. Each is wired to the
  language engine by the language-crossing it holds.
- **Positions:** the faaSamoa (the matai system and the aiga, the Samoan way of
  life), the Mau (the nonviolent resistance and the claim to self-rule), the
  voyage and the word (the navigators' heritage and the Pacific literary voice).
- **Places:** Upolu (Apia the capital, Vailima, the ground of the Mau and of
  independence), Savai'i (the great volcanic island, the heartland of the old
  faaSamoa).
- **Processes:** the ava ceremony (the kava ceremony of the chiefs), the tatau
  (the tattooing of the pe'a and the malu).
- **Pieces:** the fine mat (the ie toga, the most precious Samoan treasure), siapo
  (the tapa bark cloth of ceremony).

## Provenance

The Hofstede scores behind the pitch and the public-record historical sources are
recorded in [REFERENCES.md](REFERENCES.md). The data never enters the prose; the
pitch is its invisible hand. Sources on the public record are credited, never
claimed; the colonial era and the Mau are treated soberly, from within Samoa's own
self-understanding, taking no side; the staging and the architecture are original
work.
