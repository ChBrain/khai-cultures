# Estonia: References

The provenance behind this culture: the Hofstede source data that tunes the pitch
(kept here, never in the prose) and the public-domain historical sources behind
the plots, personas, places, and pieces.

## Hofstede source data

The pitch [pitch_et.md](pitch_et.md) is written from Estonia's profile on Geert
Hofstede's model of national culture and names no dimension and carries no
number. The scores and their source are recorded here so the data is on the
record without entering the prose.

| Dimension                     | Estonia score (approx.) |
| ----------------------------- | ----------------------- |
| Power Distance                | 40                      |
| Individualism                 | 60                      |
| Motivation toward Achievement | 30                      |
| Uncertainty Avoidance         | 60                      |
| Long Term Orientation         | 82                      |
| Indulgence                    | 16                      |

Source: Geert Hofstede, Hofstede Insights (the Country Comparison tool and the
six-dimensions model of national culture). Scores are approximate, as published
by Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group; Estonia's very high Long Term
Orientation and very low Indulgence are notable in the published data.

The two defining personas, [Aino](persona_aino.md) and
[Kristjan](persona_kristjan.md), are archetypes, not historical individuals:
composites that carry the tension between the older generation (the Soviet
decades, the mother tongue kept whole, Finnish TV across the gulf as the window
to the West) and the younger one (the post-2004 work migration to Finland and the
pull of global English on a small Finnic language).
