# Instructions: Cultures

The law of the content: how a culture is staged and how its personas hold their
tongues. It is the solution layer, declared once and read system-wide. It carries
no coding rules - branching, versioning, and the gates live in `CLAUDE.md` and
`GEMINI.md` - and nothing of the management voice. The chapters spell HACKS:
Human, Agent, Collaboration, Knowledge, System.

## Human

- Names the culture, its defining event, and the tongues it is staged in.
- Sets the read each persona must field, and where the loss should bite.
- Reviews and approves the staging before it lands.

## Agent

- Inhabits the culture and stages it whole, in its own tongue, fielding every
  khai type the canon requires.
- Gives each persona its language read and enacts the **gap** in the scene - what
  is heard is not what is understood, what is understood is not what is said -
  showing it in the first beat, never naming the width.
- Executes the canon; does not narrate it.

## Collaboration

- A culture casts within its own play; every cast must resolve to a real owned
  file.
- Cross-culture meaning resolves by the communication ladder (below) and is staged
  in **groups**, where a group casts member-culture personas into one encounter.
- A no-shared-language pair crosses only through a **named proxy** who shares a
  tongue with each.

## Knowledge

- **The engine law.** A persona uses language through the ladder rooted at [Using
  Language](process_using_language.md): five channels - speaking and writing
  outward, hearing and reading inward, thinking underneath - each held **per
  language, per channel, at its true width**, from the tongue gripped as a foreign
  object to the tongue that needs no grip because it is already the body. The gap
  between what the persona means and what the tongue gives is where a culture is
  lost at the limits of a language.
- **Communication is emergent,** read off two personas' reads and never authored
  pairwise. Whether meaning crosses, and how much, resolves by a ladder:
  1. Same mother tongue and variety -> it crosses whole, culture and all.
  2. Same language, different variety -> the words cross, idiom and connotation
     slip.
  3. Only the worn common tongue -> the transaction crosses, the culture does
     not.
  4. No shared tongue -> a proxy relays it twice-worn, stripped at each hop, and
     the proxy holds the power.
  5. No shared tongue and no proxy -> silence; it does not cross.
- **A language variety is a `position`** (Has / Orders / Loses / Drives), held and
  shared by a culture's personas; `pitch` stays reserved for the single Hofstede
  tone, never used for varieties.
- `README.md` is the house's Estate identity: every culture and group logs the
  house in its Estate, and the link must resolve.

## System

- Every persona fields a language read: per language, per channel, at its true
  width. A read that names no width, or no tongue, is not a read.
- Every multilingual culture fields at least one no-shared-language pair and a
  named proxy; the gap is shown, never narrated.
- The minor version IS the culture count; the registry build is its single
  writer. A plan is a non-count enrichment, listed in the play's Company.
