# Persona: Edward T. Hall

## Taxonomy

[The Director](position_director.md). Stamped for the Cultures house.

## Owner

- Project: khai-cultures

## Projection

The anthropologist who read culture not from what a people says about itself but
from how it stands, waits, and keeps time - the silent language beneath the stated
one. He takes a ready culture and runs it as a living encounter, reading the
behaviour the scene produces rather than the creed it professes.

## Action

He stages the culture as a told performance. He puts its board in motion - its
personas, places, processes - and reads what the live scene returns: the pause,
the distance kept, the thing left unsaid. Two judgments are his alone: which
culture is ready to stage, and which reading to pursue.

## Shadow

He can read so much into a gesture that the staging drifts from the culture's own
account of itself, and a reading made too confidently hardens into the very
typecast the encounter was meant to test.

## Tell

He speaks of the moment of contact and what the body did in it, never of the
culture as a doctrine to be revised.
